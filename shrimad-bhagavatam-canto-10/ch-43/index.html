Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 43

The elephant Kuvalayapida killed by Krishna

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

कुवलयापीडवधः नाम त्रिचत्वारिंशः अध्यायः

(38 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

अथ कृष्णश्च रामश्च कृतशौचौ परन्तप ।

मल्लदुन्दुभिनिर्घोषं श्रुत्वा द्रष्टुमुपेयतुः ॥10.43.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अथ then, कृष्णः Krishna, च and, रामः Balarama, च and, कृत-शौचौ having performed their morning purification, परन्तप O chastiser of the enemies (King Parikshit), मल्ल-दुन्दुभि-निर्घोषम् the resounding sound of the wrestlers kettledrums, श्रुत्वा hearing, द्रष्टुम् to see, उपेयतुः they both approached

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — परन्तप ! अथ कृतशौचौ कृष्णः च रामः च मल्लदुन्दुभिनिर्घोषं श्रुत्वा द्रष्टुम् उपेयतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, परन्तप O chastiser of the enemies, अथ then, कृतशौचौ having performed their purification, कृष्णः च रामः च Krishna and Balarama, मल्लदुन्दुभिनिर्घोषं श्रुत्वा hearing the resounding sound of the wrestlers kettledrums, द्रष्टुम् उपेयतुः approached to see.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: O King Parikshit, chastiser of enemies, after performing their necessary morning purifications, Krishna and Balarama heard the resounding boom of the wrestlers kettledrums. Eager to see the arena, they both went toward it.

रङ्गद्वारं समासाद्य तस्मिन् नागमवस्थितम् ।

अपश्यत् कुवलयापीडं कृष्णोऽम्बष्ठप्रचोदितम् ॥10.43.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: रङ्ग-द्वारम् the entrance of the arena, समासाद्य reaching, तस्मिन् there, नागम् the elephant, अवस्थितम् stationed, अपश्यत् saw, कुवलयापीडम् Kuvalayapida, कृष्णः Krishna, अम्बष्ठ-प्रचोदितम् driven by its mahout

Anvaya: कृष्णः रङ्गद्वारं समासाद्य तस्मिन् अम्बष्ठप्रचोदितम् अवस्थितं कुवलयापीडं नाम नागम् अपश्यत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णः Krishna, रङ्गद्वारं समासाद्य reaching the entrance of the arena, तस्मिन् there, अम्बष्ठप्रचोदितम् driven by its keeper, अवस्थितं stationed, कुवलयापीडं नाम नागम् अपश्यत् saw the elephant named Kuvalayapida.

Simple Meaning: Reaching the entrance of the wrestling arena, Krishna saw the massive elephant Kuvalayapida stationed there. The elephant was being goaded and directed by its keeper.

बद्ध्वा परिकरं शौरिः समुह्य कुटिलालकान् ।

उवाच हस्तिपं वाचा मेघनादगभीरया ॥10.43.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: बद्ध्वा binding, परिकरम् his belt, शौरिः Krishna (the descendant of Shura), समुह्य gathering together, कुटिल-अलकान् his curly locks of hair, उवाच spoke, हस्ति-पम् to the elephant keeper, वाचा with words, मेघ-नाद-गभीरया as grave as the roaring of a cloud

Anvaya: शौरिः परिकरं बद्ध्वा कुटिलालकान् समुह्य मेघनादगभीरया वाचा हस्तिपम् उवाच।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: शौरिः Krishna, परिकरं बद्ध्वा binding his belt, कुटिलालकान् समुह्य gathering together his curly locks of hair, मेघनादगभीरया वाचा with words as grave as the roaring of a cloud, हस्तिपम् उवाच spoke to the elephant keeper.

Simple Meaning: Tying his belt tightly and gathering his scattered curly locks of hair, Krishna addressed the elephant keeper in a voice as deep and resounding as the rumbling of a thundercloud.

अम्बष्ठाम्बष्ठ मार्गं नौ देह्यपक्रम मा चिरम् ।

नो चेत् सकुञ्जरम् त्वाद्य नयामि यमसादनम् ॥10.43.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: अम्बष्ठ O elephant keeper, अम्बष्ठ O elephant keeper, मार्गम् the path, नौ to us, देहि give, अपक्रम clear out, मा चिरम् do not delay, नो चेत् otherwise, स-कुञ्जरम् along with the elephant, त्वा you, अद्य today, नयामि I shall send, यम-सादनम् to the abode of Yamaraja (the lord of death)

Anvaya: अम्बष्ठ अम्बष्ठ ! नौ मार्गं देहि, मा चिरम् अपक्रम, नो चेत् अद्य त्वा सकुञ्जरं यमसादनं नयामि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अम्बष्ठ अम्बष्ठ O elephant keeper, O elephant keeper, नौ मार्गं देहि give the path to us, मा चिरम् अपक्रम clear out without delay, नो चेत् otherwise, अद्य today, त्वा you, सकुञ्जरं along with the elephant, यमसादनं नयामि I shall send to the abode of Yamaraja.

Simple Meaning: O elephant keeper, clear the way for us and move aside immediately! Do not delay. If you do not, today I will send you along with your elephant to the abode of Yamaraja, the lord of death.

एवं निर्भर्त्सितोऽम्बष्ठः कुपितः कोपितं गजम् ।

चोदयामास कृष्णाय कालान्तकयमोपमम् ॥10.43.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, निर्भर्त्सितः insulted/threatened, अम्बष्ठः the elephant keeper, कुपितः became angry, कोपितम् angered, गजम् the elephant, चोदयामास driven towards/instigated, कृष्णाय towards Krishna, काल-अन्तक-यम-उपमम् who resembled the lord of death, Yamaraja, at the time of universal destruction

Anvaya: एवं निर्भर्त्सितः अम्बष्ठः कुपितः (सन्) कोपितं कालान्तकयमोपमं गजं कृष्णाय चोदयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं निर्भर्त्सितः In this way insulted, अम्बष्ठः the elephant keeper, कुपितः became angry, कोपितं the angered, कालान्तकयमोपमं who resembled Yamaraja at the time of destruction, गजं elephant, कृष्णाय towards Krishna, चोदयामास drove forward.

Simple Meaning: Being insulted and threatened in this manner, the elephant keeper became furious. He goaded his already angered elephant, who resembled Yamaraja (the lord of death) at the time of universal destruction, and drove him directly toward Krishna.

करीन्द्रस्तमभिद्रुत्य करेण तरसाग्रहीत् ।

कराद् विगलितः सोऽमुं निहत्याङ्घ्रिष्वलीयत ॥10.43.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: करीन्द्रः the king of elephants (Kuvalayapida), तम् Him (Krishna), अभिद्रुत्य rushing towards, करेण with his trunk, तरसा swiftly/forcefully, अग्रहीत् seized, करात् from the trunk, विगलितः slipping away/escaping, सः He (Krishna), अमुम् him (the elephant), निहत्य striking, अङ्घ्रिषु among his legs, अलीयत hid/disappeared

Anvaya: करीन्द्रः तम् अभिद्रुत्य करेण तरसा अग्रहीत्, करात् विगलितः सः अमुं निहत्य अङ्घ्रिषु अलीयत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: करीन्द्रः The king of elephants, तम् अभिद्रुत्य rushing towards Him, करेण तरसा अग्रहीत् swiftly seized with his trunk, करात् विगलितः escaping from the trunk, सः He, अमुं निहत्य striking him, अङ्घ्रिषु अलीयत hid among his legs.

Simple Meaning: The mighty elephant rushed forward and swiftly seized Krishna with his trunk. However, Krishna slipped away from the trunk's grasp, struck the beast, and then hid out of sight right between the elephant's massive legs.

संक्रुद्धस्तमचक्षाणो घ्राणदृष्टिः स केशवम् ।

परामृशत् पुश्करेण स प्रसह्य विनिर्गतः ॥10.43.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: संक्रुद्धः completely enraged, तम् Him (Krishna), अचक्षाणः not seeing, घ्राण-दृष्टिः whose sight is guided by the sense of smell, सः he (the elephant), केशवम् Kesava (Krishna), परामृशत् searched for/groped after, पुष्करेण with the tip of his trunk, सः He (Krishna), प्रसह्य forcefully/violently, विनिर्गतः escaped

Anvaya: तम् अचक्षाणः संक्रुद्धः घ्राणदृष्टिः सः पुष्करेण केशवं परामृशत्, सः प्रसह्य विनिर्गतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तम् अचक्षाणः Not seeing Him, संक्रुद्धः completely enraged, घ्राणदृष्टिः whose sight is guided by smell, सः he, पुष्करेण with the tip of his trunk, केशवं परामृशत् searched for Kesava, सः He, प्रसह्य विनिर्गतः forcefully escaped.

Simple Meaning: Completely enraged at losing sight of Krishna, the elephant used his keen sense of smell to locate Him and began groping around with the tip of his trunk. But Krishna forcefully broke free from his grasp and escaped once again.

पुच्छे प्रगृह्यातिबलं धनुषः पञ्चविंशतिम् ।

विचकर्ष यथा नागं सुपर्ण इव लीलया ॥10.43.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुच्छे by the tail, प्रगृह्य grabbing/holding tightly, अति-बलम् the extremely powerful (elephant), धनुषः of bow-lengths, पञ्चविंशतिम् twenty-five, विचकर्ष dragged, यथा just as, नागम् a snake, सुपर्णः Garuda (the king of birds), इव like, लीलया effortlessly/playfully

Anvaya: अतिबलं पुच्छे प्रगृह्य धनुषः पञ्चविंशतिं विचकर्ष, यथा सुपर्णः नागम् इव लीलया।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अतिबलं The extremely powerful elephant, पुच्छे प्रगृह्य grabbing by the tail, धनुषः पञ्चविंशतिं twenty-five bow-lengths, विचकर्ष dragged, यथा just as, सुपर्णः Garuda, नागम् इव like a snake, लीलया effortlessly.

Simple Meaning: Grabbing the extremely powerful elephant by its tail, Krishna effortlessly dragged the beast backwards for a distance of twenty-five bow-lengths, just as Garuda effortlessly drags a snake.

स पर्यावर्तमानेन सव्यदक्षिणतोऽच्युतः ।

बभ्राम भ्राम्यमाणेन गोवत्सेनेव बालकः ॥10.43.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः He, पर्यावर्तमानेन who was turning around, सव्य-दक्षिणतः to the left and to the right, अच्युतः Achyuta (Krishna), बभ्राम moved around, भ्राम्यमाणेन who was being whirled around, गो-वत्सेन इव like a young calf, बालकः a young boy

Anvaya: सव्यदक्षिणतः पर्यावर्तमानेन भ्राम्यमाणेन (गजेन) सः अच्युतः, गोवत्सेन बालकः इव बभ्राम।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सव्यदक्षिणतः पर्यावर्तमानेन Turning around to the left and right, भ्राम्यमाणेन being whirled around, सः अच्युतः He, the infallible Lord, गोवत्सेन बालकः इव like a young boy with a calf, बभ्राम moved around.

Simple Meaning: As the elephant tried to turn around frantically to the left and to the right to catch Him, Achyuta moved along with it just like a playful young boy pulling a young calf around by its tail.

ततोऽभिमुखमभ्येत्य पाणिनाऽऽहत्य वारणम् ।

प्राद्रवन् पातयामास स्पृश्यमानः पदे पदे ॥10.43.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, अभिमुखम् face-to-face, अभ्येत्य coming forward, पाणिना with His hand, आहत्य striking, वारणम् the elephant, प्राद्रवन् running away, पातयामास caused to fall, स्पृश्यमानः being touched/reached, पदे पदे at every step

Anvaya: ततः अभिमुखम् अभ्येत्य पाणिना वारणम् आहत्य प्राद्रवन् पदे पदे स्पृश्यमानः (सन् तम्) पातयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, अभिमुखम् अभ्येत्य coming face-to-face, पाणिना वारणम् आहत्य striking the elephant with His hand, प्राद्रवन् running away, पदे पदे स्पृश्यमानः being touched at every step, पातयामास caused him to fall.

Simple Meaning: Then, Krishna came right in front of the elephant and struck it with His bare hand. As He ran away, making the elephant chase Him so closely that it seemed to touch Him at every step, Krishna tricked the beast and caused it to trip and fall down.

स धावन् क्रीडया भूमौ पतित्वा सहसोत्थितः।

तं मत्वा पतितं क्रुद्धो दन्ताभ्यां सोऽहनत्क्षितिम् ॥10.43.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः He (Krishna), धावन् while running, क्रीडया playfully, भूमौ on the ground, पतित्वा falling down, सहसा suddenly, उत्थितः got up, तम् Him, मत्वा thinking, पतितम् to have fallen, क्रुद्धः enraged, दन्ताभ्याम् with his two tusks, सः he (the elephant), अहनत् struck, क्षितिम् the earth

Anvaya: सः धावन् क्रीडया भूमौ पतित्वा सहसा उत्थितः। सः क्रुद्धः तं पतितं मत्वा दन्ताभ्यां क्षितिम् अहनत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः धावन् He (Krishna) while running, क्रीडया भूमौ पतित्वा playfully falling on the ground, सहसा उत्थितः suddenly got up. सः क्रुद्धः He (the elephant) being enraged, तं पतितं मत्वा thinking Him (Krishna) to have fallen, दन्ताभ्यां क्षितिम् अहनत् struck the earth with his two tusks.

Simple Meaning: While running, Krishna playfully staged a fall onto the ground and sprang right back up. The enraged elephant, falsely thinking Krishna was down, violently struck the earth with his massive tusks instead.

स्वविक्रमे प्रतिहते कुञ्जरेन्द्रोऽत्यमर्षितः ।

चोद्यमानो महामात्रैः कृष्णमभ्यद्रवद् रुषा ॥10.43.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्व-विक्रमे when his own prowess, प्रतिहते was thwarted/frustrated, कुञ्जर-इन्द्रः the king of elephants, अत्यमर्षितः became extremely angry, चोद्यमानः being goaded/urged on, महामात्रैः by the elephant keepers/mahouts, कृष्णम् towards Krishna, अभ्यद्रवत् rushed blindly, रुषा with intense fury

Anvaya: स्वविक्रमे प्रतिहते (सति) अत्यमर्षितः कुञ्जरेन्द्रः महामात्रैः चोद्यमानः (सन्) रुषा कृष्णम् अभ्यद्रवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्वविक्रमे प्रतिहते When his own prowess was thwarted, अत्यमर्षितः became extremely angry, कुञ्जरेन्द्रः the king of elephants, महामात्रैः चोद्यमानः being goaded by the keepers, रुषा कृष्णम् अभ्यद्रवत् rushed towards Krishna with intense fury.

Simple Meaning: His immense strength and prowess having been completely thwarted, the king of elephants became violently frustrated and angry. Urged on by his keepers, the beast charged blindly ahead with intense fury toward Krishna.

तमापतन्तमासाद्य भगवान् मधुसूदनः।

निगृह्य पाणिना हस्तं पातयामास भूतले ॥10.43.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (the elephant), आपतन्तम् charging forward, आसाद्य confronting, भगवान् the Lord, मधुसूदनः Madhusudana (the slayer of the asura Madhu), निगृह्य catching hold of, पाणिना with His hand, हस्तम् the trunk, पातयामास threw down/felled, भूतले onto the ground

Anvaya: भगवान् मधुसूदनः आपतन्तम् तम् आसाद्य पाणिना हस्तं निगृह्य भूतले पातयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवान् मधुसूदनः Lord Madhusudana, आपतन्तम् तम् आसाद्य confronting him as he charged forward, पाणिना हस्तं निगृह्य catching hold of the trunk with His hand, भूतले पातयामास threw him down onto the ground.

Simple Meaning: As the furious elephant charged right at Him, Lord Madhusudana confronted the beast head-on. Seizing its trunk firmly in His hand, He single-handedly tripped the elephant and slammed him violently down onto the ground.

पतितस्य पदाऽऽक्रम्य मृगेन्द्र इव लीलया ।

दन्तमुत्पाट्य तेनेभं हस्तिपांश्चाहनद् हरिः ॥10.43.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: पतितस्य of the fallen (elephant), पदा with His foot, आक्रम्य stepping upon, मृगेन्द्रः the king of beasts (a lion), इव like, लीलया effortlessly, दन्तम् the tusk, उत्पाट्य pulling out, तेन with that (tusk), इभम् the elephant, हस्तिपान् च and the elephant keepers, अहनत् killed/struck down, हरिः Hari (Krishna)

Anvaya: हरिः पतितस्य पदा आक्रम्य मृगेन्द्रः इव लीलया दन्तम् उत्पाट्य तेन इभं हस्तिपान् च अहनत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हरिः Lord Hari, पतितस्य पदा आक्रम्य stepping upon the fallen elephant with His foot, मृगेन्द्रः इव लीलया effortlessly like a lion, दन्तम् उत्पाट्य pulling out the tusk, तेन इभं हस्तिपान् च अहनत् with that tusk struck down the elephant and the elephant keepers.

Simple Meaning: Holding down the fallen beast with His foot, Lord Hari effortlessly ripped out one of its massive tusks, just like a lion overpowering its prey. He then used that very tusk to kill the elephant and dispatch its wicked keepers.

मृतकं द्विपमुत्सृज्य दन्तपाणिः समाविशत् ।

अंसन्यस्तविषाणोऽसृङ्मदबिन्दुभिरङ्कितः ।

विरूढस्वेदकणिकावदनाम्बुरुहो बभौ ॥10.43.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: मृतकम् the dead, द्विपम् elephant, उत्सृज्य leaving aside, दन्त-पाणिः holding the tusk in His hand, समाविशत् entered (the wrestling arena), अंस-न्यस्त-विषाणः with the tusk resting on His shoulder, असृक्-मद-बिन्दुभिः with drops of blood and sweat/elephant-secretion, अङ्कितः smeared/decorated, विरूढ-स्वेद-कणिका-वदन-अम्बुरुहः His lotus-like face covered with fine drops of perspiration, बभौ shone brilliantly

Anvaya: मृतकं द्विपम् उत्सृज्य दन्तपाणिः अंसन्यस्तविषाणः असृङ्मदबिन्दुभिः अङ्कितः विरूढस्वेदकणिकावदनाम्बुरुहः (सन्) समाविशत् बभौ च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मृतकं द्विपम् उत्सृज्य leaving aside the dead elephant, दन्तपाणिः holding the tusk in His hand, अंसन्यस्तविषाणः with the tusk resting on His shoulder, असृङ्मदबिन्दुभिः अङ्कितः smeared with drops of blood and sweat, विरूढस्वेदकणिकावदनाम्बुरुहः His lotus face covered with fine drops of perspiration, समाविशत् entered, बभौ च and shone brilliantly.

Simple Meaning: Leaving the dead elephant behind, Lord Krishna entered the wrestling arena carrying the tusk in His hand. With the massive tusk resting lightly on His shoulder, His body sprinkled with drops of blood and elephant secretion, and His beautiful lotus face adorned with fine beads of perspiration, He looked absolutely magnificent.

वृतौ गोपैः कतिपयैर्बलदेवजनार्दनौ ।

रङ्गं विविशतू राजन् गजदन्तवरायुधौ ॥10.43.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: वृतौ surrounded, गोपैः by the cowherd boys, कतिपयैः by a few, बलदेव-जनार्दनौ Baladeva and Janardana (Krishna), रङ्गम् the arena, विविशतू the two of them entered, राजन् O King (Parikshit), गज-दन्त-वर-आयुधौ holding the choice weapons of the elephant tusks

Anvaya: राजन् ! कतिपयैः गोपैः वृतौ गजदन्तवरायुधौ बलदेवजनार्दनौ रङ्गं विविशतू।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King, कतिपयैः गोपैः वृतौ surrounded by a few cowherd boys, गजदन्तवरायुधौ holding the choice weapons of the elephant tusks, बलदेवजनार्दनौ Baladeva and Janardana, रङ्गं विविशतू entered the arena.

Simple Meaning: O King Parikshit, surrounded by a few of their cowherd friends, Baladeva and Krishna entered the wrestling arena, each carrying one of those powerful elephant tusks as their supreme weapon.

मल्लानामशनिर्नृणां नरवरः स्त्रीणां स्मरो मूर्तिमान् गोपानां स्वजनोऽसतां क्षितिभुजां शास्ता स्वपित्रोः शिशुः ।

मृत्युर्भोजपतेर्विराडविदुषां तत्त्वं परं योगिनां वृष्णीनां परदेवतेति विदितो रङ्गं गतः साग्रजः ॥10.43.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: मल्लानाम् for the wrestlers, अशनिः a lightning bolt, नृणाम् for the common people, नर-वरः the best of men, स्त्रीणाम् for the women, स्मरः Cupid (the god of love), मूर्तिमान् personified, गोपानाम् for the cowherds, स्व-जनः their own kinsman/friend, असताम् for the wicked, क्षिति-भुजाम् for the kings, शास्ता a punisher/ruler, स्व-पित्रोः for His own parents, शिशुः a child, मृत्युः death, भोज-पतेः for the king of the Bhoja dynasty (Kamsa), विराट् the cosmic form/an overwhelming force, अविदुषाम् for the ignorant, तत्वम् the ultimate reality, परम् supreme, योगिनाम् for the yogis, वृष्णीनाम् for the Vrishnis, पर-देवता the supreme deity, इति thus, विदितः perceived/understood, रङ्गम् the arena, गतः entered, स-अग्रजः along with His elder brother (Baladeva)

Anvaya: साग्रजः (कृष्णः) रङ्गं गतः (सन्) मल्लानाम् अशनिः, नृणां नरवरः, स्त्रीणां मूर्तिमान् स्मरः, गोपानां स्वजनः, असतां क्षितिभुजां शास्ता, स्वपित्रोः शिशुः, भोजपतेः मृत्युः, अविदुषां विराट्, योगिनां परं तत्त्वं, वृष्णीनां परदेवता इति विदितः (बभूव)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: साग्रजः Along with His elder brother, रङ्गं गतः entered the arena, मल्लानाम् अशनिः for the wrestlers a lightning bolt, नृणां नरवरः for the common people the best of men, स्त्रीणां मूर्तिमान् स्मरः for the women Cupid personified, गोपानां स्वजनः for the cowherds their own kinsman, असतां क्षितिभुजां शास्ता for the wicked kings a punisher, स्वपित्रोः शिशुः for His parents a child, भोजपतेः मृत्युः for Kamsa death, अविदुषां विराट् for the ignorant an overwhelming force, योगिनां परं तत्त्वं for the yogis the supreme reality, वृष्णीनां परदेवता इति विदितः for the Vrishnis the supreme deity thus perceived.

Simple Meaning: When Krishna entered the arena alongside His elder brother Balarama, He was perceived differently by each onlooker according to their own mindset. To the wrestlers He appeared as a deadly lightning bolt; to the common men as the noblest of heroes; to the women as Cupid personified; to the cowherds as their dear friend; to the wicked kings as a stern punisher; to His parents as a child; to Kamsa, the king of the Bhojas, as death itself; to the ignorant as an overwhelming cosmic force; to the yogis as the Absolute Truth; and to the Vrishnis as their supreme worshipable Deity.

हतं कुवलयापीडं दृष्ट्वा तावपि दुर्जयौ ।

कंसो मनस्व्यपि तदा भृशमुद्विविजे नृप ॥10.43.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: हतम् killed, कुवलयापीडम् the elephant Kuvalayapida, दृष्ट्वा seeing, तौ those two (Krishna and Balarama), अपि also, दुर्जयौ unconquerable, कंसः Kamsa, मनस्वी अपि although broad-minded/resolute, तदा then, भृशम् extremely, उद्विविजे became anxious/disturbed, नृप O King (Parikshit)

Anvaya: नृप ! हतं कुवलयापीडं तौ दुर्जयौ अपि दृष्ट्वा तदा मनस्वी अपि कंसः भृशम् उद्विविजे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, हतं कुवलयापीडं the killed Kuvalayapida, तौ दुर्जयौ अपि and those two unconquerable ones also, दृष्ट्वा seeing, तदा then, मनस्वी अपि although resolute, कंसः Kamsa, भृशम् उद्विविजे became extremely anxious.

Simple Meaning: O King Parikshit, seeing that his mighty elephant Kuvalayapida had been killed and that the two brothers were completely unconquerable, Kamsa, despite being otherwise resolute and strong-minded, became deeply anxious and disturbed.

तौ रेजतू रङ्गगतौ महाभुजौ विचित्रवेषाभरणस्रगम्बरौ ।

यथा नटावुत्तमवेषधारिणौ मनः क्षिपन्तौ प्रभया निरीक्षताम् ॥10.43.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two (Krishna and Balarama), रेजतू shone brilliantly, रङ्ग-गतौ having entered the arena, महा-भुजौ possessing mighty arms, विचित्र-वेष-आभरण-स्रक्-अम्बरौ adorned with wonderful outfits, ornaments, garlands, and garments, यथा just like, नटौ two actors, उत्तम-वेष-धारिणौ wearing exquisite costumes, मनः the minds, क्षिपन्तौ captivating/stealing, प्रभया by their effulgence, निरीक्षताम् of those who were looking at them

Anvaya: रङ्गगतौ विचित्रवेषाभरणस्रगम्बरौ महाभुजौ तौ उत्तमवेषधारिणौ नटौ यथा प्रभया निरीक्षतां मनः क्षिपन्तौ रेजतू।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: रङ्गगतौ Having entered the arena, विचित्रवेषाभरणस्रगम्बरौ adorned with wonderful outfits, ornaments, garlands, and garments, महाभुजौ possessing mighty arms, तौ those two, उत्तमवेषधारिणौ wearing exquisite costumes, नटौ यथा just like two actors, प्रभया by their effulgence, निरीक्षतां मनः क्षिपन्तौ captivating the minds of those looking at them, रेजतू shone brilliantly.

Simple Meaning: Having entered the arena, the mighty-armed brothers shone brilliantly. Adorned in wonderful garments, ornaments, and flower garlands, they looked like two master actors dressed in exquisite costumes, completely captivating the minds of everyone in the audience with their stunning effulgence.

निरीक्ष्य तावुत्तमपूरुषौ जना मञ्चस्थिता नागरराष्ट्रका नृप ।

प्रहर्षवेगोत्कलितेक्षणाननाः पपुर्न तृप्ता नयनैस्तदाननम् ॥10.43.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: निरीक्ष्य beholding, तौ those two, उत्तम-पूरुषौ Supreme Personalities, जनाः the people, मञ्च-स्थिताः seated on the viewing platforms/benches, नागर-राष्ट्रकाः the citizens of the city and the inhabitants of the provinces, नृप O King (Parikshit), प्रहर्ष-वेग-उत्कलित-ईक्षण-आननाः whose eyes and faces expanded due to the force of intense joy, पपुः they drank, न not, तृप्ताः satisfied, नयनैः with their eyes, तत्-आननम् Their faces

Anvaya: नृप ! मञ्चस्थिताः नागरराष्ट्रकाः जनाः तौ उत्तमपूरुषौ निरीक्ष्य प्रहर्षवेगोत्कलितेक्षणाननाः (सन्तः) नयनैः तदाननं पपुः, न तृप्ताः (च बभूवुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, मञ्चस्थिताः नागरराष्ट्रकाः जनाः the people from the city and the provinces seated on the platforms, तौ उत्तमपूरुषौ निरीक्ष्य beholding those two Supreme Personalities, प्रहर्षवेगोत्कलितेक्षणाननाः whose eyes and faces expanded due to the force of intense joy, नयनैः तदाननं पपुः with their eyes drank Their faces, न तृप्ताः and were not satisfied.

Simple Meaning: O King Parikshit, as the citizens of Mathura and the people from the provinces sitting on the viewing platforms beheld those two Supreme Personalities, their eyes and faces blossomed with intense joy. They eagerly drank in the beauty of the brothers' faces through their eyes, yet they could never feel fully satisfied.

पिबन्त इव चक्षुर्भ्यां लिहन्त इव जिह्वया ।

जिघ्रन्त इव नासाभ्यां श्लिष्यन्त इव बाहुभिः ॥10.43.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: पिबन्तः इव as if drinking, चक्षुर्भ्याम् with their two eyes, लिहन्तः इव as if licking, जिह्वया with their tongue, जिघ्रन्तः इव as if smelling, नासाभ्याम् with their two nostrils, श्लिष्यन्तः इव as if embracing, बाहुभिः with their arms

Anvaya: (ते जनाः) चक्षुर्भ्यां पिबन्तः इव, जिह्वया लिहन्तः इव, नासाभ्यां जिघ्रन्तः इव, बाहुभिः श्लिष्यन्तः इव (स्थिताः बभूवुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते जनाः Those people, चक्षुर्भ्यां पिबन्तः इव as if drinking with their two eyes, जिह्वया लिहन्तः इव as if licking with their tongue, नासाभ्यां जिघ्रन्तः इव as if smelling with their two nostrils, बाहुभिः श्लिष्यन्तः इव as if embracing with their arms.

Simple Meaning: It appeared as if the onlookers were drinking the two brothers with their eyes, licking them with their tongues, smelling them with their nostrils, and tightly locking them in an embrace with their arms.

ऊचुः परस्परं ते वै यथा दृष्टं यथा श्रुतम् ।

तद्रूपगुणमाधुर्यप्रागल्भ्यस्मारिता इव ॥10.42.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: ऊचुः they spoke, परस्परम् to one another, ते they, वै indeed, यथा दृष्टम् according to what they saw, यथा श्रुतम् according to what they had heard, तत्-रूप-गुण-माधुर्य-प्रागल्भ्य-स्मारिताः reminded of Their beauty, qualities, sweetness, and boldness, इव as if

Anvaya: ते वै तद्रूपगुणमाधुर्यप्रागल्भ्यस्मारिताः इव यथा दृष्टं यथा श्रुतं परस्परम् ऊचुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते वै They indeed, तद्रूपगुणमाधुर्यप्रागल्भ्यस्मारिताः इव as if reminded of Their beauty, qualities, sweetness, and boldness, यथा दृष्टं यथा श्रुतं according to what they saw and heard, परस्परम् ऊचुः spoke to one another.

Simple Meaning: Deeply moved as they were reminded of the extraordinary beauty, qualities, sweetness, and boldness of the two brothers, the onlookers began talking to one another, eagerly sharing everything they had seen and heard about them.

एतौ भगवतः साक्षाद्धरेर्नारायणस्य हि ।

अवतीर्णाविहांशेन वसुदेवस्य वेश्मनि ॥10.43.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: एतौ these two, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, साक्षात् directly, हरेः of Hari, नारायणस्य of Narayana, हि indeed, अवतीर्णौ have descended, इह here, अंशेन with His expansion, वसुदेवस्य of Vasudeva, वेश्मनि in the home

Anvaya: एतौ साक्षात् भगवतः हरेः नारायणस्य हि अंशेन इह वसुदेवस्य वेश्मनि अवतीर्णौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एतौ These two, साक्षात् भगवतः हरेः नारायणस्य हि indeed directly of the Supreme Lord Hari Narayana, अंशेन with His expansion, इह वसुदेवस्य वेश्मनि here in the home of Vasudeva, अवतीर्णौ have descended.

Simple Meaning: These two are directly the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Lord Hari Narayana Himself, who has descended into this world with His expansion, appearing here in the home of Vasudeva.

एष वै किल देवक्यां जातो नीतश्च गोकुलम् ।

कालमेतं वसन् गूढो ववृधे नन्दवेश्मनि ॥10.43.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: एषः this one (Krishna), वै indeed, किल it is said, देवक्याम् from Devaki, जातः was born, नीतः was taken, च and, गोकुलम् to Gokula, कालम् time, एतम् this, वसन् living, गूढः secretly, ववृधे grew up, नन्द-वेश्मनि in the house of Nanda

Anvaya: एषः वै किल देवक्यां जातः गोकुलं नीतः च एतं कालं गूढः वसन् नन्दवेश्मनि ववृधे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एषः वै किल This one indeed, देवक्यां जातः was born from Devaki, गोकुलं नीतः च and was taken to Gokula, एतं कालं गूढः वसन् living secretly for all this time, नन्दवेश्मनि ववृधे grew up in the house of Nanda.

Simple Meaning: They continued, "This very youth was born from Devaki and then taken away to Gokula. Living there secretly all this time, He grew up in the house of Nanda."

पूतनानेन नीतान्तं चक्रवातश्च दानवः ।

अर्जुनौ गुह्यकः केशी धेनुकोऽन्ये च तद्विधाः ॥10.43.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: पूतना Putana, अनेन by Him (Krishna), नीता अन्तम् was brought to her end (killed), चक्रवातः च​ and the whirlwind (Trinavarta), दानवः the danava, अर्जुनौ the twin Arjuna trees, गुह्यकः the Guhyaka (Shankhachuda/Vyomasura), केशी Kesi, धेनुकः Dhenuka, अन्ये others, च and, तत्-विधाः of that kind

Anvaya: अनेन पूतना अन्तं नीता, चक्रवातः दानवः च अर्जुनौ गुह्यकः केशी धेनुकः अन्ये च तद्विधाः (च हताः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अनेन By Him, पूतना Putana, अन्तं नीता were brought to her end, चक्रवातः दानवः च and the danava whirlwind, अर्जुनौ गुह्यकः केशी धेनुकः अन्ये च तद्विधाः the twin Arjuna trees, the Guhyaka, Kesi, Dhenuka, and others of that kind (were destroyed/killed).

Simple Meaning: "By Him, Putana was brought to her end. He is the one who killed the whirlwind danava Trinavarta, uprooted the twin Arjuna trees and killed the Guhyaka (Shankhachuda), Kesi, Dhenuka, and many other asuras of their kind."

गावः सपाला एतेन दावाग्नेः परिमोचिताः ।

कालियो दमितः सर्प इन्द्रश्च विमदः कृतः ॥10.43.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: गावः the cows, स-पालाः along with the cowherd boys, एतेन by Him, दावाग्नेः from the forest fire, परिमोचिताः were completely delivered, कालियः Kaliya, दमितः was subdued, सर्पः the serpent, इन्द्रः Indra, च and, विमदः stripped of his pride, कृतः was made

Anvaya: एतेन गावः सपालाः दावाग्नेः परिमोचिताः, कालियः सर्पः दमितः, इन्द्रः च विमदः कृतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एतेन By Him, गावः सपालाः the cows along with the cowherd boys, दावाग्नेः परिमोचिताः were completely delivered from the forest fire, कालियः सर्पः दमितः Kaliya the serpent was subdued, इन्द्रः च विमदः कृतः and Indra was made stripped of his pride.

Simple Meaning: "He completely delivered the cows and the cowherd boys from the deadly forest fire. He subdued the venomous serpent Kaliya, and He crushed the false pride of Indra, the king of heaven."

सप्ताहमेकहस्तेन धृतोऽद्रिप्रवरोऽमुना ।

वर्षवाताशनिभ्यश्च परित्रातं च गोकुलम् ॥10.43.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: सप्ताहम् for seven days, एक-हस्तेन with one hand, धृतः was held, अद्रि-प्रवरः the best of mountains (Govardhana), अमुना by Him, वर्ष-वात-अशनिभ्यः from rain, wind, and thunderbolts, च and, परित्रातम् was completely protected, च and, गोकुलम् the village of Gokula

Anvaya: अमुना सप्ताहम् एकहस्तेन अद्रिप्रवरः धृतः, वर्षवाताशनिभ्यः गोकुलं परित्रातं च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अमुना By Him, सप्ताहम् एकहस्तेन for seven days with one hand, अद्रिप्रवरः धृतः the best of mountains was held, वर्षवाताशनिभ्यः गोकुलं परित्रातं च and from rain, wind, and thunderbolts, Gokula was completely protected.

Simple Meaning: "He held up the supreme Govardhana Mountain with just one hand for seven consecutive days, completely protecting the entire village of Gokula from the devastating rain, fierce winds, and terrifying thunderbolts."

गोप्योऽस्य नित्यमुदितहसितप्रेक्षणं मुखम् ।

पश्यन्त्यो विविधांस्तापांस्तरन्ति स्माश्रमं मुदा ॥10.43.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः the gopis, अस्य His, नित्य-मुदित-हसित-प्रेक्षणम् always beaming, smiling, and full of glances, मुखम् face, पश्यन्त्यः beholding, विविधान् various, तापान् miseries, तरन्ति स्म they crossed over, श्रमम् fatigue, मुदा with joy

Anvaya: अस्य नित्यमुदितहसितप्रेक्षणं मुखं पश्यन्त्यः गोप्यः विविधान् तापान् श्रमं च मुदा तरन्ति स्म।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अस्य His, नित्यमुदितहसितप्रेक्षणं मुखं face which is always beaming, smiling, and full of glances, पश्यन्त्यः beholding, गोप्यः the gopis, विविधान् तापान् various miseries, श्रमं च and fatigue, मुदा तरन्ति स्म crossed over with joy.

Simple Meaning: "By constantly beholding His beautiful face—which is always beaming, smiling, and full of loving glances—the gopis joyfully cross over all kinds of material miseries and physical fatigue."

वदन्त्यनेन वंशोऽयं यदोः सुबहुविश्रुतः ।

श्रियं यशो महत्त्वं च लप्स्यते परिरक्षितः ॥10.43.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: वदन्ति they say, अनेन by Him, वंशः अयम् this dynasty, यदोः of Yadu, सुबहु-विश्रुतः highly celebrated/famous, श्रियम् fortune/prosperity, यशः fame, महत्त्वम् greatness, च and, लप्स्यते will achieve, परिरक्षितः protected

Anvaya: (जनाः) वदन्ति अनेन परिरक्षितः अयं यदोः वंशः सुबहुविश्रुतः (सन्) श्रियं यशः महत्त्वं च लप्स्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (जनाः) वदन्ति The people say, अनेन परिरक्षितः protected by Him, अयं यदोः वंशः this dynasty of Yadu, सुबहुविश्रुतः highly celebrated, श्रियं यशः महत्त्वं च लप्स्यते will achieve fortune, fame, and greatness.

Simple Meaning: "People say that under His protection, this dynasty of Yadu will become highly celebrated and achieve supreme fortune, fame, and greatness."

अयं चास्याग्रजः श्रीमान् रामः कमललोचनः ।

प्रलम्बो निहतो येन वत्सको ये बकादयः ॥10.43.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: अयम् this, च and, अस्य His (Krishna's), अग्रजः elder brother, श्रीमान् glorious/opulent, रामः Rama (Balarama), कमल-लोचनः lotus-eyed, प्रलम्बः Pralamba, निहतः was killed, येन by whom, वत्स्यकः Vatsaka (Vatsasura), ये those, बक-आदयः Baka and others

Anvaya: अस्य अग्रजः श्रीमान् कमललोचनः अयं रामः च (अस्ति), येन प्रलम्बः निहतः, ये वत्स्यकः बकादयः च (हताः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अस्य अग्रजः His elder brother, श्रीमान् कमललोचनः अयं रामः च अस्ति and this is the glorious, lotus-eyed Balarama, येन प्रलम्बः निहतः by whom Pralamba was killed, ये वत्स्यकः बकादयः च and those like Vatsasura, Bakasura, and others (were also destroyed).

Simple Meaning: "And this is His glorious, lotus-eyed elder brother, Balarama. It is He who killed the asura Pralamba, as well as Vatsasura, Bakasura, and many others."

Note: In fact two of the asuras mentioned here were killed by Krishna, not Balarama. The reason for the mistake is that as news of Krishna’s exploits spread among ordinary people, the facts became somewhat muddled.

जनेष्वेवं ब्रुवाणेषु तूर्येषु निनदत्सु च ।

कृष्णरामौ समाभाष्य चाणूरो वाक्यमब्रवीत् ॥10.43.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: जनेषु while the people, एवम् thus, ब्रुवाणेषु were speaking, तूर्येषु the musical instruments/drums, निनदत्सु च and were resounding, कृष्ण-रामौ to Krishna and Balarama, समाभाष्य addressing, चाणूरः Chanura, वाक्यम् words, अब्रवीत् spoke

Anvaya: जनेषु एवं ब्रुवाणेषु तूर्येषु च निनदत्सु (सत्सु), चाणूरः कृष्णरामौ समाभाष्य वाक्यम् अब्रवीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जनेषु एवं ब्रुवाणेषु While the people were thus speaking, तूर्येषु च निनदत्सु and while the musical instruments were resounding, चाणूरः Chanura, कृष्णरामौ समाभाष्य addressing Krishna and Balarama, वाक्यम् अब्रवीत् spoke these words.

Simple Meaning: While the people were speaking in this way and the musical instruments were loudly resounding in the arena, the wrestler Chanura stepped forward, addressed Krishna and Balarama, and spoke the following words.

हे नन्दसूनो हे राम भवन्तौ वीरसंमतौ ।

नियुद्धकुशलौ श्रुत्वा राज्ञाऽऽहूतौ दिदृक्षुणा ॥10.43.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: हे नन्द-सूनो O son of Nanda (Krishna), हे राम O Rama (Balarama), भवन्तौ the two of you, वीर-संमतौ honored as heroes, नियुद्ध-कुशलौ skillful at wrestling, श्रुत्वा having heard, राज्ञा by the King (Kamsa), आहूतौ have been called, दिदृक्षुणा desiring to see

नियुद्ध (नपुंसकलिङ्गः) बाहुयुद्धम् src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: हे नन्दसूनो ! हे राम ! भवन्तौ वीरसंमतौ नियुद्धकुशलौ (च इति) श्रुत्वा दिदृक्षुणा राज्ञा आहूतौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हे नन्दसूनो O son of Nanda, हे राम O Rama, भवन्तौ नियुद्धकुशलौ वीरसंमतौ (च इति) श्रुत्वा having heard that the two of you are skillful at wrestling and honored as heroes, दिदृक्षुणा राज्ञा आहूतौ you have been called by the King, who desires to see (your skills).

Simple Meaning: Chanura said, "O son of Nanda, O Balarama! Hearing that the two of you are highly skillful at wrestling and well-respected as great heroes, the King has summoned you here out of a desire to witness your prowess.

प्रियं राज्ञः प्रकुर्वन्त्यः श्रेयो विन्दन्ति वै प्रजाः ।

मनसा कर्मणा वाचा विपरीतमतोऽन्यथा ॥10.43.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रियम् what is pleasing, राज्ञः to the King, प्रकुर्वन्त्यः doing, श्रेयः auspiciousness/well-being, विन्दन्ति achieve, वै indeed, प्रजाः the subjects, मनसा with the mind, कर्मणा with deeds, वाचा with words, विपरीतम् the opposite, अतः than this, अन्यथा otherwise

Anvaya: मनसा कर्मणा वाचा राज्ञः प्रियं प्रकुर्वन्त्यः प्रजाः वै श्रेयः विन्दन्ति, अतः विपरीतं (कुर्वन्त्यः) अन्यथा (विपरीतं फलम् एव लभन्ते)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मनसा कर्मणा वाचा With their mind, deeds, and words, राज्ञः प्रियं प्रकुर्वन्त्यः प्रजाः वै subjects who do what is pleasing to the King indeed, श्रेयः विन्दन्ति achieve auspiciousness. अतः विपरीतम् (कुर्वन्त्यः) Those who do the opposite of this, अन्यथा (विपरीतं फलम् एव लभन्ते) otherwise achieve the opposite result.

Simple Meaning: "Subjects who do what is pleasing to the king through their thoughts, words, or actions surely achieve true well-being. But those who do the opposite reap exactly the opposite result.

नित्यं प्रमुदिता गोपा वत्सपाला यथा स्फुटम् ।

वनेषु मल्लयुद्धेन क्रीडन्तश्चारयन्ति गाः ॥10.43.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: नित्यम् always, प्रमुदिताः very happy, गोपाः the cowherd boys, वत्स-पालाः keepers of the calves, यथा as, स्फुटम् clearly, वनेषु in the forests, मल्ल-युद्धेन with wrestling matches, क्रीडन्तः playing, चारयन्ति they tend, गाः the cows

Anvaya: यथा स्फुटं, वत्सपालाः गोपाः नित्यं प्रमुदिताः (सन्तः) वनेषु मल्लयुद्धेन क्रीडन्तः गाः चारयन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यथा स्फुटं As it is evident, वत्सपालाः गोपाः the cowherd boys who tend the calves, नित्यं प्रमुदिताः (सन्तः) being always very happy, वनेषु मल्लयुद्धेन क्रीडन्तः playing with wrestling matches in the forests, गाः चारयन्ति tend the cows.

Simple Meaning: "It is well known that you cowherd boys and caretakers of calves are always very happy. While herding your cows in the forest, you regularly play by engaging in wrestling matches with one another."

तस्मात् राज्ञः प्रियं यूयं वयं च करवामहे ।

भूतानि नः प्रसीदन्ति सर्वभूतमयो नृपः ॥10.43.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मात् therefore, राज्ञः of the king, प्रियम् what is pleasing, यूयम् you, वयम् we, च and, करवामहे let us do, भूतानि all living beings, नः with us, प्रसीदन्ति become pleased, सर्व-भूत-मयः the embodiment of all living entities, नृपः the king

Anvaya: तस्मात् यूयं वयं च राज्ञः प्रियं करवामहे, (येन) भूतानि नः प्रसीदन्ति, (यतः) नृपः सर्वभूतमयः (भवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्मात् Therefore, यूयं वयं च you and we, राज्ञः प्रियं करवामहे let us do what is pleasing to the king, येन भूतानि नः प्रसीदन्ति by which all living beings will be pleased with us, (यतः) नृपः सर्वभूतमयः भवति because the king is the embodiment of all living entities.

Simple Meaning: "Therefore, let both you and us do what is pleasing to the king. By satisfying the king, all living beings will become pleased with us, for the king embodies all living entities."

तन्निशम्याब्रवीत् कृष्णो देशकालोचितं वचः ।

नियुद्धमात्मनोऽभीष्टं मन्यमानोऽभिनन्द्य च ॥10.43.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, निशम्य hearing, अब्रवीत् spoke, कृष्णः Krishna, देश-काल-उचितम् appropriate for the time and place, वचः words, नियुद्धम् wrestling match, आत्मनः of Himself, अभीष्टम् desired, मन्यमानः considering, अभिनन्द्य च and welcoming/praising

Anvaya: तत् निशम्य आत्मनः अभीष्टं नियुद्धं मन्यमानः अभिनन्द्य च कृष्णः देशकालोचितं वचः अब्रवीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् निशम्य Hearing that, आत्मनः अभीष्टं नियुद्धं मन्यमानः considering the wrestling match to be desired by Himself, अभिनन्द्य च and welcoming it, कृष्णः देशकालोचितं वचः अब्रवीत् Krishna spoke words appropriate for the time and place.

Simple Meaning: Hearing Chanura's words, Lord Krishna, who actually welcomed the opportunity to wrestle because it perfectly suited His own desire, praised his statement and spoke words appropriate for the time and place.

प्रजा भोजपतेरस्य वयं चापि वनेचराः ।

करवाम प्रियं नित्यं तन्नः परमनुग्रहः ॥10.43.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रजाः subjects, भोज-पतेः of the Lord of the Bhojas (Kamsa), अस्य of this, वयम् we, च and, अपि also, वने-चराः forest-dwellers, करवाम let us do, प्रियम् what is pleasing, नित्यम् always, तत् that, नः for us, परम-अनुग्रहः a supreme favor/blessing

Anvaya: (हे चानूर!) वनेचराः वयम् अपि अस्य भोजपतेः प्रजाः च, (अतः वयम् अस्य) नित्यं प्रियं करवाम, तत् नः परम अनुग्रहः (भविष्यति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (O Chanura!) वनेचराः वयम् अपि अस्य भोजपतेः प्रजाः च We forest-dwellers, are also the subjects of this King of the Bhojas, (अतः वयम् अस्य) नित्यं प्रियं करवाम therefore let us always do what is pleasing to him, तत् नः परम अनुग्रहः भविष्यति that will be a supreme blessing for us.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna replied, "O Chanura, we forest-dwellers, are also the subjects of the King of the Bhojas. Therefore, we must always do whatever pleases him. To perform such service would truly be a supreme blessing for us.

बाला वयं तुल्यबलैः क्रीडिष्यामो यथोचितम् ।

भवेन्नियुद्धं माधर्मः स्पृशेन्मल्ल सभासदः ॥10.43.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: बालाः young boys, वयम् we, तुल्य-बलैः with those of equal strength, क्रीडिष्यामः we shall play/engage, यथा उचितम् as appropriate, भवेत् should be, नियुद्धम् a wrestling match, मा not, अधर्मः injustice, स्पृशेत् should touch, मल्ल-सभा-सदः the members of this wrestling assembly

सदः सदस्, सकारान्तः, नपुंसकलिङ्गः, असन्तः, द्वितीयैकवचनम् src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: वयं बालाः (स्मः, अतः) तुल्यबलैः (सह) यथा उचितं क्रीडिष्यामः। नियुद्धं (तथा) भवेत् (येन) अधर्मः मल्लसभासदः मा स्पृशेत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वयं बालाः (स्मः) We are young boys, अतः तुल्यबलैः (सह) therefore with those of equal strength, यथा उचितं क्रीडिष्यामः we shall properly play. नियुद्धं (तथा) भवेत् The wrestling match should be conducted in such a way, (येन) अधर्मः मल्लसभासदः मा स्पृशेत् by which injustice does not touch the members of this wrestling assembly.

Simple Meaning: "However, we are just young boys, so we should properly play and wrestle only with those of equal strength. The match must be fair and balanced, so that no injustice or wrongdoing touches the members of this respectable assembly."

चाणूर उवाच

न बालो न किशोरस्त्वं बलश्च बलिनां वरः ।

लीलयेभो हतो येन सहस्रद्विपसत्त्वभृत् ॥10.43.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: चाणूरः उवाच Chanura said, न not, बालः a child, न not, किशोरः a youth / adolescent, त्वम् you, बलः Balarama, च and, बलिनाम् of the strong, वरः the foremost / best, लीलया playfully, इभः the elephant (Kuvalayapida), हतः was killed, येन by whom, सहस्र-द्विप-सत्त्व-भृत् bearing the strength of a thousand elephants

Anvaya: चाणूरः उवाच— येन सहस्रद्विपसत्त्वभृत् इभः लीलया हतः त्वं बालः न (असि), किशोरः न (असि), बलः च बलिनां वरः (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: चाणूरः उवाच Chanura said— येन By whom, सहस्रद्विपसत्त्वभृत् bearing the power of a thousand elephants, इभः the elephant, लीलया हतः was playfully killed, त्वं बालः न (असि) You are not a child, किशोरः न (असि) you are not a mere youth. बलः च बलिनां वरः (अस्ति) And Balarama is the foremost of the strong.

Simple Meaning: Chanura said: "You who have playfully killed the mighty elephant Kuvalayapida, who possessed the immense strength of a thousand ordinary elephants, are neither a child nor a mere youth. And Balarama, is the strongest of the strong.

तस्माद् भवद्भ्यां बलिभिर्योद्धव्यं नानयोऽत्र वै ।

मयि विक्रम वार्ष्णेय बलेन सह मुष्टिकः ॥10.43.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मात् therefore, भवद्भ्याम् by the two of you, बलिभिः with strong opponents, योद्धव्यम् must fight, न no, अनयः injustice/impropriety, अत्र here, वै indeed, मयि against me, विक्रम exhibit your prowess, वार्ष्णेय O descendant of Vrishni (Krishna), बलेन सह with Balarama, मुष्टिकः Mushtika (shall fight)

Anvaya: तस्मात् भवद्भ्यां बलिभिः (सह) योद्धव्यम्, अत्र अनयः वै न (अस्ति)। हे वार्ष्णेय! मयि विक्रम, बलेन सह मुष्टिकः (योत्स्यति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्मात् Therefore, भवद्भ्यां बलिभिः सह योद्धव्यम् by the two of you, a fight must be waged with strong opponents. अत्र अनयः वै न अस्ति Here there is indeed no injustice. हे वार्ष्णेय O descendant of Vrishni, मयि विक्रम exhibit your prowess against me, बलेन सह मुष्टिकः and Mushtika will fight with Balarama.

Simple Meaning: Chanura said: "Therefore, both of you must fight with powerful opponents like us; there is absolutely no unfairness or injustice in this. O Krishna, show your prowess against me, and let Mushtika duel with Balarama!"

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे “कुवलयापीडवधः नाम” त्रिचत्वारिंशः अध्यायः ॥43॥

Thus ends the forty-third discourse named “The elephant Kuvalayapida killed by Krishna” in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
