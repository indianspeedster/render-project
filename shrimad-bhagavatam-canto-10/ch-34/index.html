Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 34

Deliverance of Sudarshana and slaying of Shankhachuda by the Lord

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

सुदर्शनमोक्षः तथा रासक्रीडायां शङ्खचूडवधो नाम चतुस्त्रिंशः अध्यायः

(32 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

एकदा देवयात्रायां गोपाला जातकौतुकाः ।

अनोभिरनडुद्युक्तैः प्रययुस्तेम्बिकावनम् ॥10.34.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एकदा once, देव-यात्रायाम् for a pilgrimage to a deity, गोपालाः the cowherd men, जात-कौतुकाः being very eager/excited, अनोभिः by bullock carts, अनडुत्-युक्तैः yoked with oxen, प्रययुः went, ते they, अम्बिका-वनम् to the forest of Ambikavana

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — एकदा जातकौतुकाः ते गोपालाः अनडुद्युक्तैः अनोभिः देवयात्रायाम् अम्बिकावनं प्रययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एकदा once, जातकौतुकाः being full of eagerness, ते गोपालाः those cowherd men, अनडुद्युक्तैः अनोभिः with carts yoked with oxen, देवयात्रायां for a religious pilgrimage, अम्बिकावनं प्रययुः went to Ambikavana.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: O King, once, filled with great eagerness, the cowherd men headed by Nanda traveled by ox-drawn carts to the holy forest of Ambikavana for a pilgrimage to worship the deities.

तत्र स्नात्वा सरस्वत्यां देवं पशुपतिं विभुम् ।

आनर्चुरर्हणैर्भक्त्या देवीं च नृपतेऽम्बिकाम् ॥10.34.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there, स्नात्वा having bathed, सरस्वत्याम् in the river Saraswati, देवम् the lord, पशुपतिम् Pashupati (Shiva), विभुम् the powerful/all-pervading, आनर्चुः they worshipped, अर्हणैः with various offerings/paraphernalia, भक्त्या with devotion, देवीम् the goddess, च and, नृपते O King, अम्बिकाम् Ambika (Parvati)

Anvaya: नृपते! तत्र सरस्वत्यां स्नात्वा विभुं देवं पशुपतिं देवीं अम्बिकां च भक्त्या अर्हणैः आनर्चुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृपते O King, तत्र there, सरस्वत्यां in the Saraswati, स्नात्वा having bathed, विभुं देवं पशुपतिं powerful Lord Pashupati, देवीं अम्बिकां च and Goddess Ambika, भक्त्या with devotion, अर्हणैः with offerings, आनर्चुः they worshipped.

Simple Meaning: O King, after arriving there, the cowherds bathed in the river Saraswati and then devotedly worshipped the powerful Lord Pashupati and Goddess Ambika with various ritual offerings.

गावो हिरण्यं वासांसि मधु मध्वन्नमादृताः ।

ब्राह्मणेभ्यो ददुः सर्वे देवो नः प्रीयतामिति ॥10.34.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: गावः cows, हिरण्यम् gold, वासांसि clothes, मधु honey, मधु-अन्नम् grains mixed with honey/sweet food, आदृताः with great respect, ब्राह्मणेभ्यः unto the brahmanas, ददुः gave, सर्वे all of them, देवः the Lord, नः our, प्रीयताम् may be pleased, इति thus (praying)

Anvaya: सर्वे आदृताः गावः हिरण्यं वासांसि मधु मध्वन्नं च "नः देवः प्रीयताम्" इति ब्राह्मणेभ्यः ददुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वे All of them, आदृताः with great respect, गावः हिरण्यं वासांसि मधु मध्वन्नं च cows, gold, clothing, honey, and sweet food, "नः देवः प्रीयताम् May the Lord be pleased with us", इति thus, ब्राह्मणेभ्यः to the brahmanas, ददुः gave.

Simple Meaning: With great respect, all the cowherds gave gifts of cows, gold, clothing, and sweet food mixed with honey to the brahmanas, praying, "May the Lord be pleased with us."

ऊषुः सरस्वतीतीरे जलं प्राश्य धृतव्रताः ।

रजनीं तां महाभागा नन्दसुनन्दकादयः ॥10.34.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: ऊषुः they stayed/passed the time, सरस्वती-तीरे on the bank of the river Saraswati, जलम् water, प्राश्य having drunk, धृत-व्रताः observing their vows, रजनीम् the night, ताम् that, महा-भागाः the greatly fortunate ones, नन्द-सुनन्दक-आदयः Nanda, Sunandaka, and others

Anvaya: महाभागाः नन्दसुनन्दकादयः धृतव्रताः जलं प्राश्य तां रजनीं सरस्वतीतीरे ऊषुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महाभागाः नन्दसुनन्दकादयः The greatly fortunate ones, Nanda, Sunanda, and others, धृतव्रताः observing their vows, जलं प्राश्य having drunk the water, तां रजनीं that night, सरस्वतीतीरे on the bank of the Saraswati, ऊषुः stayed.

Simple Meaning: The greatly fortunate Nanda, Sunanda, and the other cowherds spent that night on the bank of the Saraswati. Strictly observing their vows, they fasted, taking only water.

कश्चिन्महानहिस्तस्मिन् विपिनेऽतिबुभुक्षितः।

यदृच्छयाऽऽगतो नन्दं शयानमुरगोऽग्रसीत् ॥10.34.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: कश्चित् a certain, महान् large, अहिः snake, तस्मिन् in that, विपिने forest, अति-बुभुक्षितः very hungry, यदृच्छया by chance, आगतः arriving, नन्दम् Nanda, शयानम् who was sleeping, उरगः the serpent, अग्रसीत् swallowed

Anvaya: तस्मिन् विपिने अतिबुभुक्षितः कश्चित् महान् अहिः यदृच्छया आगतः। उरगः शयानं नन्दम् अग्रसीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्मिन् विपिने In that forest, अतिबुभुक्षितः very hungry, कश्चित् महान् अहिः a certain large snake, यदृच्छया आगतः by chance, arrived. उरगः शयानं नन्दम् अग्रसीत् The serpent swallowed Nanda who was sleeping.

Simple Meaning: In that forest, a very hungry and huge serpent happened to arrive at the spot. This snake then began to swallow Nanda while he lay asleep.

स चुक्रोशाहिना ग्रस्तः कृष्ण कृष्ण महानयम् ।

सर्पो मां ग्रसते तात प्रपन्नं परिमोचय ॥10.34.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Nanda), चुक्रोश cried out, अहिना by the snake, ग्रस्तः seized/swallowed, कृष्ण Krishna, कृष्ण Krishna, महान् huge, अयम् this, सर्पः serpent, माम् me, ग्रसते is swallowing, तात O dear son, प्रपन्नम् who has surrendered, परिमोचय please deliver

Anvaya: अहिना ग्रस्तः सः "कृष्ण कृष्ण! तात! अयं महान् सर्पः मां ग्रसते, प्रपन्नं परिमोचय" इति चुक्रोश।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहिना ग्रस्तः Seized by the snake, सः he, "कृष्ण कृष्ण Krishna Krishna, तात dear son, अयं महान् सर्पः this huge serpent, मां ग्रसते is swallowing me, प्रपन्नं surrendered one, परिमोचय deliver", इति चुक्रोश thus, cried out.

Simple Meaning: Being seized by the snake, Nanda cried out, "Krishna! Krishna! My dear son, this huge serpent is swallowing me! Please deliver me who has surrendered to You."

तस्य चाक्रन्दितं श्रुत्वा गोपालाः सहसोत्थिताः ।

ग्रस्तं च दृष्ट्वा विभ्रान्ताः सर्पं विव्यधुरुल्मुकैः ॥10.34.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य his (Nanda's), च and, आक्रन्दितम् cries for help, श्रुत्वा hearing, गोपालाः the cowherd men, सहसा suddenly, उत्थिताः stood up/awoke, ग्रस्तम् seized, च and, दृष्ट्वा seeing, विभ्रान्ताः greatly bewildered/disturbed, सर्पम् the serpent, विव्यधुः they struck, उल्मुकैः with firebrands/burning logs

Anvaya: तस्य आक्रन्दितं च श्रुत्वा गोपालाः सहसा उत्थिताः, ग्रस्तं च दृष्ट्वा विभ्रान्ताः सर्पम् उल्मुकैः विव्यधुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्य आक्रन्दितं च श्रुत्वा Hearing his cries, गोपालाः the cowherd men, सहसा उत्थिताः woke up suddenly, ग्रस्तं च दृष्ट्वा and seeing him seized, विभ्रान्ताः bewildered, सर्पम् the serpent, उल्मुकैः विव्यधुः struck with firebrands.

Simple Meaning: Hearing Nanda's cries for help, the cowherds woke up suddenly. Seeing that he was being swallowed, they became greatly distressed and began to strike the serpent with burning torches to make it release him.

अलातैर्दह्यमानोऽपि नामुञ्चत्तमुरङ्गमः ।

तमस्पृशत् पदाभ्येत्य भगवान् सात्वतां पतिः ॥10.34.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: अलातैः by the firebrands, दह्यमानः being burned, अपि even though, न not, अमुञ्चत् released, तम् him (Nanda), उरङ्गमः the serpent, तम् that (serpent), अस्पृशत् touched, पदा with His foot, अभ्येत्य coming near, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, सात्वताम् of the devotees, पतिः the master

Anvaya: अलातैः दह्यमानः अपि उरङ्गमः तं न अमुञ्चत्। सात्वतां पतिः भगवान् अभ्येत्य पदा तम् अस्पृशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अलातैः दह्यमानः अपि Even after being burned by the firebrands, उरङ्गमः the serpent, तं न अमुञ्चत् did not release him. सात्वतां पतिः The master of the devotees, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अभ्येत्य coming near, पदा with His foot, तम् अस्पृशत् touched that (serpent).

Simple Meaning: Even though the serpent was being burned by the firebrands, it would not release Nanda. Then the Supreme Lord Krishna, the master of all devotees, approached the serpent and touched it with His foot.

स वै भगवतः श्रीमत्पादस्पर्शहताशुभः ।

भेजे सर्पवपुर्हित्वा रूपं विद्याधरार्चितम् ॥10.34.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (the serpent), वै indeed, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, श्रीमत्-पाद-स्पर्श-हत-अशुभः whose sinful reactions were destroyed by the touch of the divine foot, भेजे attained, सर्प-वपुः the serpent body, हित्वा giving up, रूपम् a form, विद्याधर-अर्चितम् worshipped by the Vidyadharas

Anvaya: भगवतः श्रीमत्पादस्पर्शहताशुभः सः वै सर्पवपुः हित्वा विद्याधरार्चितं रूपं भेजे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवतः श्रीमत्पादस्पर्शहताशुभः Whose sins were destroyed by the touch of the divine foot of the Lord, सः he, वै indeed, सर्पवपुः हित्वा giving up the serpent body, विद्याधरार्चितं रूपं form which was worshipped by Vidyadharas, भेजे attained.

Simple Meaning: Upon being touched by the lotus foot of the Supreme Lord, the serpent was immediately freed from all his sins. Casting aside his reptilian body, he regained his original form which was worshipped by the Vidyadharas.

तमपृच्छद्धृषीकेशः प्रणतं समुपस्थितम् ।

दीप्यमानेन वपुषा पुरुषं हेममालिनम् ॥10.34.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, अपृच्छत् asked, हृषीकेशः Lord Hrishikesha (Krishna), प्रणतम् who was bowing down, समुपस्थितम् standing before Him, दीप्यमानेन with a glowing/effulgent, वपुषा body, पुरुषम् the person, हेम-मालिनम् wearing a golden necklace

Anvaya: हृषीकेशः दीप्यमानेन वपुषा समुपस्थितं हेममालिनं प्रणतं तं पुरुषम् अपृच्छत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हृषीकेशः Lord Krishna, दीप्यमानेन वपुषा with a glowing body, समुपस्थितं standing before Him, हेममालिनं प्रणतं तं पुरुषम् to the person who was wearing a golden necklace and bowing down, अपृच्छत् asked.

Simple Meaning: Lord Hrishikesha then questioned that personality, who was standing before Him with a glowing body, wearing a golden necklace, and bowing down in respect.

को भवान् परया लक्ष्म्या रोचतेऽद्भुतदर्शनः ।

कथं जुगुप्सितामेतां गतिं वा प्रापितोऽवशः ॥10.34.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: कः who, भवान् you, परया with supreme, लक्ष्म्या beauty/effulgence, रोचते shine, अद्भुत-दर्शनः of wonderful appearance, कथम् how, जुगुप्सिताम् abominable, एताम् this, गतिम् state/destination, वा or, प्रापितः were made to reach, अवशः helplessly

Anvaya: अद्भुतदर्शनः भवान् कः? परया लक्ष्म्या रोचते। अवशः एतां जुगुप्सितां गतिं कथं वा प्रापितः?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अद्भुतदर्शनः Of wonderful appearance, भवान् कः who are you?, परया लक्ष्म्या रोचते You shine with supreme beauty. अवशः Helplessly, एतां जुगुप्सितां गतिं this abominable state, कथं how, वा or, प्रापितः were made to reach.

Simple Meaning: Who are you that shine so wonderfully with such supreme beauty? How was it that you were helplessly forced to assume such an abominable form as that of a serpent?

सर्प उवाच

अहं विद्याधरः कश्चिद् सुदर्शन इति श्रुतः ।

श्रिया स्वरूपसम्पत्त्या विमानेनाचरं दिशः ॥10.34.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: सर्पः उवाच the serpent said, अहम् I, विद्याधरः a Vidyadhara, कश्चित् a certain, सुदर्शनः Sudarshana, इति known as, श्रुतः famous, श्रिया with opulence, स्वरूप-सम्पत्त्या with the asset of my personal beauty, विमानेन by airplane, अचरम् I traveled, दिशः in all directions

Anvaya: सर्पः उवाच — अहं सुदर्शनः इति श्रुतः कश्चित् विद्याधरः। श्रिया स्वरूपसम्पत्त्या च विमानेन दिशः अचरम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्पः उवाच The serpent said, अहं सुदर्शनः इति श्रुतः I am known as Sudarshana, कश्चित् विद्याधरः a certain Vidyadhara. श्रिया स्वरूपसम्पत्त्या च With opulence and with beauty of form, विमानेन by my airplane, दिशः through the directions, अचरम् traveled.

Simple Meaning: The serpent spoke: I am the well-known Vidyadhara named Sudarshana. I used to be very opulent and possessed great personal beauty. I would travel in all directions in my celestial airplane.

ऋषीन् विरूपानङ्गिरसः प्राहसं रूपदर्पितः ।

तैरिमां प्रापितो योनिं प्रलब्धैः स्वेन पाप्मना ॥10.34.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: ऋषीन् the sages, विरूपान् deformed/unattractive, अङ्गिरसः of the lineage of Angira, प्राहसम् I laughed at, रूप-दर्पितः proud of my beauty, तैः by them, इमाम् this, प्रापितः made to attain, योनिम् birth/species, प्रलब्धैः who were mocked, स्वेन by my own, पाप्मना sin

Anvaya: रूपदर्पितः अहं विरूपान् अङ्गिरसः ऋषीन् प्राहसम्। प्रलब्धैः तैः स्वेन पाप्मना इमां योनिं प्रापितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: रूपदर्पितः Proud of my beauty, अहं I, विरूपान् अङ्गिरसः deformed-looking sages of the Angira line, प्राहसम् laughed at. प्रलब्धैः तैः By them who were mocked, स्वेन पाप्मना by my own, sin, इमां योनिं this birth, प्रापितः was made to reach.

Simple Meaning: Proud of my own physical beauty, I ridiculed the ugly appearance of the holy sages of the lineage of Angira. Because of my sin of mocking them, they cursed me to assume this low form of life.

शापो मेऽनुग्रहायैव कृतस्तैः करुणात्मभिः ।

यदहं लोकगुरुणा पदा स्पृष्टो हताशुभः ॥10.34.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: शापः curse, मे my, अनुग्रहाय for the benefit/grace, एव indeed, कृतः was made, तैः by them, करुणा-आत्मभिः who are merciful by nature, यत् because, अहम् I, लोक-गुरुणा by the spiritual master of the universe, पदा with the foot, स्पृष्टः touched, हत-अशुभः having all sins destroyed

Anvaya: करुणात्मभिः तैः मे शापः अनुग्रहाय एव कृतः, यत् लोकगुरुणा पदा स्पृष्टः अहं हताशुभः (अभवम्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: करुणात्मभिः तैः By them who were merciful, मे शापः curse to me, अनुग्रहाय एव कृतः was given as a favor, indeed, यत् since, लोकगुरुणा पदा स्पृष्टः अहं I, being touched by the foot of the teacher of the universe, हताशुभः sins have been destroyed.

Simple Meaning: That curse was actually meant for my benefit, as it was given by those merciful sages. Because of that curse, I have now been touched by the foot of the spiritual master of the entire universe, and all my sins have been destroyed.

तं त्वाहं भवभीतानां प्रपन्नानां भयापहम् ।

आपृच्छे शापनिर्मुक्तः पादस्पर्शादमीवहन् ॥10.34.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् that (person), त्वाम् You, अहम् I, भव-भीतानाम् of those afraid of material existence, प्रपन्नानाम् for those surrendered, भय-अपहम् the remover of fear, आपृच्छे take leave/beg permission, शाप-निर्मुक्तः freed from the curse, पाद-स्पर्शात् from the touch of Your foot, अमीव-हन् O destroyer of sins

Anvaya: अमीवहन्! भवभीतानां प्रपन्नानां भयापहं तं त्वां पादस्पर्शात् शापनिर्मुक्तः अहम् आपृच्छे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अमीवहन् O destroyer of sins, भवभीतानां for those afraid of material life, प्रपन्नानां of the surrendered, भयापहं remover of fear, तं You, त्वां पादस्पर्शात् शापनिर्मुक्तः freed from the curse by the touch of Your foot, अहम् I, आपृच्छे take leave.

Simple Meaning: O destroyer of sins, You are the remover of fear for all surrendered souls who dread material existence. Now that I have been freed from my curse by the touch of Your lotus foot, I beg Your permission to depart.

प्रपन्नोऽस्मि महायोगिन् महापुरुष सत्पते ।

अनुजानीहि मां देव सर्वलोकेश्वरेश्वर ॥10.34.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रपन्नः surrendered, अस्मि I am, महा-योगिन् O greatest of yogis, महा-पुरुष O Supreme Person, सत्-पते O master of the devotees, अनुजानीहि please give permission, माम् to me, देव O Lord, सर्व-लोक-ईश्वर-ईश्वर O controller of the controllers of all worlds

Anvaya: महायोगिन्! महापुरुष! सत्पते! देव! सर्वलोकेश्वरेश्वर! अहं प्रपन्नः अस्मि, माम् अनुजानीहि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महायोगिन् O greatest yogi, महापुरुष O Supreme Person, सत्पते O master of the righteous, देव O Lord, सर्वलोकेश्वरेश्वर O Lord of the lords of all worlds, अहं प्रपन्नः अस्मि I have surrendered, माम् अनुजानीहि please permit me.

Simple Meaning: O greatest of all yogis, O Supreme Personality, O master of the devotees! O Lord, You are the controller of all the controllers of the universe. I am a soul surrendered unto You, please grant me permission to return to my own world.

ब्रह्मदण्डाद् विमुक्तोऽहं सद्यस्तेऽच्युत दर्शनात् ।

यन्नाम गृह्णन्नखिलान् श्रोतृनात्मानमेव च

सद्यः पुनाति किं भूयस्तस्य स्पृष्टः पदा हि ते ॥10.34.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: ब्रह्म-दण्डात् from the curse of the brahmanas, विमुक्तः freed, अहम् I, सद्यः immediately, ते Your, अच्युत O infallible Lord, दर्शनात् by the sight, यत्-नाम whose name, गृह्णन् chanting, अखिलान् all, श्रोतृन् the hearers, आत्मानम् himself, एव certainly, च and, सद्यः immediately, पुनाति purifies, किम् what (to speak), भूयः more, तस्य of Him (that person), स्पृष्टः touched, पदा by the foot, हि indeed, ते Your

Anvaya: अच्युत! ते दर्शनात् अहं सद्यः ब्रह्मदण्डात् विमुक्तः। यन्नाम गृह्णन् अखिलान् श्रोतृन् आत्मानं च सद्यः पुनाति, तस्य ते पदा स्पृष्टः (अहं) भूयः किं (पुनाति) हि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अच्युत O infallible Lord, ते दर्शनात् By Your sight, अहं सद्यः ब्रह्मदण्डात् विमुक्तः I am immediately freed from the curse of the brahmanas. यन्नाम Whose name, गृह्णन् one who chants, अखिलान् श्रोतृन् आत्मानं च all the hearers and himself, सद्यः immediately, पुनाति purifies, तस्य of Him, ते Your, पदा by the foot, स्पृष्टः touched, भूयः more, किम् what to speak, हि indeed.

Simple Meaning: O infallible Lord, simply by seeing You I have been immediately freed from the brahmana's curse. One who chants Your holy name purifies himself and everyone who hears him. What, then, is to be said of the purification of one like me who has been directly touched by Your lotus foot?

इत्यनुज्ञाप्य दाशार्हं परिक्रम्याभिवन्द्य च ।

सुदर्शनो दिवं यातः कृच्छ्रान्नन्दश्च मोचितः ॥10.34.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, अनुज्ञाप्य taking permission, दाशार्हम् from Krishna (the descendant of Dasharha), परिक्रम्य circumambulating, अभिवन्द्य offering obeisances, च and, सुदर्शनः Sudarshana, दिवम् to heaven, यातः went, कृच्छ्रात् from the dangerous situation, नन्दः Nanda, च and, मोचितः was delivered

Anvaya: इति दाशार्हम् अनुज्ञाप्य परिक्रम्य अभिवन्द्य च सुदर्शनः दिवं यातः, नन्दः च कृच्छ्रात् मोचितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति Thus, दाशार्हम् अनुज्ञाप्य having obtained permission from Krishna, परिक्रम्य अभिवन्द्य च circumambulating and offering respects, सुदर्शनः Sudarshana, दिवं to the celestial planets, यातः went. नन्दः च And Nanda, कृच्छ्रात् मोचितः was freed from the ordeal.

Simple Meaning: Having thus obtained permission from Lord Krishna, Sudarshana circumambulated Him, bowed down to offer his respects, and then departed for his celestial abode. Nanda was thus delivered from the terrible danger of the serpent.

निशाम्य कृष्णस्य तदात्मवैभवं व्रजौकसो विस्मितचेतसस्ततः ।

समाप्य तस्मिन् नियमं पुनर्व्रजं नृपाययुस्तत् कथयन्त आदृताः ॥10.34.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: निशाम्य observing/witnessing, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, तत्-आत्म-वैभवम् that personal power/glory, व्रज-ओकसः the residents of Vraja, विस्मित-चेतसः their minds struck with wonder, ततः then, समाप्य completing, तस्मिन् in that (place), नियमम् their vow/ritual, पुनः again, व्रजम् to Vraja, नृप O King (Parikshit), आययुः they returned, तत् that (incident), कथयन्तः describing, आदृताः with great reverence

Anvaya: नृप! तदा कृष्णस्य तदात्मवैभवं निशाम्य विस्मितचेतसः व्रजौकसः तस्मिन् नियमं समाप्य तत् आदृताः कथयन्तः ततः पुनः व्रजम् आययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, तदा then, कृष्णस्य तदात्मवैभवं निशाम्य having heard of Krishna's personal glory/power, विस्मितचेतसः with amazed minds, व्रजौकसः the inhabitants of Vraja, तस्मिन् in that place, नियमं समाप्य completing their religious vow, तत् that incident, आदृताः कथयन्तः respectfully recounting, ततः from there, पुनः again, व्रजम् आययुः returned to Vraja.

Simple Meaning: O King Parikshit, after hearing this manifestation of Krishna's divine power, the residents of Vraja were completely amazed. They finished their religious observances at that holy place and returned to Vraja, respectfully recounting the wonderful incident of Nanda's rescue along the way.

कदाचिदथ गोविन्दो रामश्चाद्भुतविक्रमः ।

विजह्रतुर्वने रात्र्यां मध्यगौ व्रजयोषिताम् ॥10.34.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: कदाचित् once, अथ then, गोविन्दः Lord Krishna, रामः Balarama, च and, अद्भुत-विक्रमः of amazing prowess, विजह्रतुः they both sported/wandered, वने in the forest, रात्र्याम् at night, मध्य-गौ being in the midst, व्रज-योषिताम् of the women of Vraja (the gopis)

Anvaya: अथ कदाचित् अद्भुतविक्रमः गोविन्दः रामः च व्रजयोषितां मध्यगौ रात्र्यां वने विजह्रतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, कदाचित् once, अद्भुतविक्रमः of amazing power, गोविन्दः Govinda, रामः Balarama, च and, व्रजयोषितां मध्यगौ in the midst of the women of Vraja, रात्र्यां at night, वने in the forest, विजह्रतुः sported/roamed.

Simple Meaning: Once, the amazingly powerful Krishna and Balarama were enjoying themselves in the forest at night, surrounded by the young women of Vraja.

उपगीयमानौ ललितं स्त्रीजनैर्बद्धसौहृदैः ।

स्वलङ्कृतानुलिप्ताङ्गौ स्रग्वीणौ विरजोऽम्बरौ ॥10.34.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: उपगीयमानौ being sung about, ललितम् charmingly/sweetly, स्त्री-जनैः by the women, बद्ध-सौहृदैः whose hearts were bound in friendship/love, सु-अलङ्कृत-अनुलिप्त-अङ्गौ whose bodies were beautifully decorated and smeared with ointments, स्रग्विनौ wearing flower garlands, विरजः-अम्बरौ wearing spotless garments

Anvaya: बद्धसौहृदैः स्त्रीजनैः ललितं उपगीयमानौ स्वलङ्कृतानुलिप्ताङ्गौ स्रग्वीणौ विरजोऽम्बरौ (तौ विजह्रतुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बद्धसौहृदैः By those whose love was bound to Them, स्त्रीजनैः by the women, ललितं sweetly, उपगीयमानौ being sung about, स्वलङ्कृतानुलिप्ताङ्गौ with bodies beautifully decorated and anointed, स्रग्वीणौ wearing garlands, विरजोऽम्बरौ wearing spotless clothes (the two of Them sported).

Simple Meaning: The two of Them (Krishna and Balarama) were beautifully adorned, their bodies anointed with fragrant pastes, wearing fresh flower garlands and spotless garments. As they moved through the forest, the women of Vraja, whose hearts were deeply bound to Them in love, sang sweetly and charmingly about Their glories.

निशामुखं मानयन्तावुदितोडुपतारकम् ।

मल्लिकागन्धमत्तालिजुष्टं कुमुदवायुना ॥10.34.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: निशा-मुखम् the beginning of the night (twilight), मानयन्तौ honoring/enhancing the beauty of, उदित-उडुप-तारकम् in which the lord of the stars (the moon) and the stars had risen, मल्लिका-गन्ध-मत्त-अलि-जुष्टम् served by bees intoxicated by the fragrance of jasmine, कुमुद-वायुना by a breeze carrying the scent of night-blooming lotuses

Anvaya: उदितोडुपतारकं मल्लिकागन्धमत्तालिजुष्टं कुमुदवायुना (युक्तं) निशामुखं मानयन्तौ (तौ विजह्रतुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: उदितोडुपतारकं where the moon and stars had risen, मल्लिकागन्धमत्तालिजुष्टं served by bees drunk on the scent of jasmine, कुमुदवायुना with a breeze from the water-lilies, निशामुखं the face of the night, मानयन्तौ honoring/beautifying (the two of Them sported).

Simple Meaning: They sported during the early hours of the night, when the moon and stars had just risen. The evening was made beautiful by a breeze scented with night-blooming lotuses and was filled with the humming of bees intoxicated by the fragrance of jasmine flowers. Krishna and Balarama added to the glory of the twilight as They wandered through the forest.

जगतुः सर्वभूतानां मनःश्रवणमङ्गलम् ।

तौ कल्पयन्तौ युगपत् स्वरमण्डलमूर्च्छितम् ॥10.34.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: जगतुः they both sang, सर्व-भूतानाम् of all living beings, मनः-श्रवण-मङ्गलम् providing auspiciousness to the mind and ears, तौ they two, कल्पयन्तौ producing, युगपत् simultaneously, स्वर-मण्डल-मूर्च्छितम् the full scale of musical notes and melodies

Anvaya: तौ सर्वभूतानां मनःश्रवणमङ्गलं (यथा स्यात् तथा) युगपत् स्वरमण्डलमूर्च्छितं कल्पयन्तौ जगतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तौ They two, सर्वभूतानां for all living beings, मनःश्रवणमङ्गलं auspicious for the mind and ears, युगपत् simultaneously, स्वरमण्डलमूर्च्छितं the various musical scales and melodies, कल्पयन्तौ creating/arranging, जगतुः sang.

Simple Meaning: Krishna and Balarama began to sing, creating a melody that was supremely auspicious for the ears and minds of all living beings. Together, They perfectly executed the entire range of musical scales and complex melodic structures.

गोप्यस्तद्गीतमाकर्ण्य मूर्च्छिता नाविदन् नृप ।

स्रंसद्दुकूलमात्मानं स्रस्तकेशस्रजं ततः ॥10.34.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः the gopis, तत्-गीतम् that singing, आकर्ण्य hearing, मूर्च्छिताः stunned/entranced, न अविदन् did not know, नृप O King (Parikshit), स्रंसत्-दुकूलम् (of) their slipping garments, आत्मानम् themselves, स्रस्त-केश-स्रजम् (of) their loosened hair and garlands, ततः then

Anvaya: नृप! तत् गीतम् आकर्ण्य मूर्च्छिताः गोप्यः ततः स्रंसद्दुकूलं स्रस्तकेशस्रजं आत्मानं न अविदन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, तत् गीतम् आकर्ण्य hearing that song, मूर्च्छिताः stunned/fainted (in ecstasy), गोप्यः the gopis, ततः then, स्रंसद्दुकूलं with slipping clothes, स्रस्तकेशस्रजं with loosened hair and flower-garlands, आत्मानं themselves, न अविदन् did not notice.

Simple Meaning: O King, when the gopis heard that singing, they became so totally entranced that they were no longer aware of themselves. They did not even notice that their silk garments were slipping or that the flowers were falling from their loosened hair.

एवं विक्रीडतोः स्वैरं गायतोः सम्प्रमत्तवत् ।

शङ्खचूड इति ख्यातो धनदानुचरोऽभ्यगात् ॥10.34.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, विक्रीडतोः while they were playing, स्वैरम् at will/freely, गायतोः while they were singing, सम्प्रमत्त-वत् as if completely absorbed/intoxicated, शङ्खचूडः Shankhachuda, इति as, ख्यातः known, धनद-अनुचरः a servant of Kubera (the giver of wealth), अभ्यगात् arrived

Anvaya: सम्प्रमत्तवत् स्वैरम् एवं विक्रीडतोः गायतोः (तयोः अन्तिकम्) शङ्खचूडः इति ख्यातः धनदानुचरः अभ्यगात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सम्प्रमत्तवत् Like those completely intoxicated (with joy), स्वैरम् freely, एवं विक्रीडतोः in this way, while sporting, गायतोः (and) while singing, (near Them), शङ्खचूडः इति ख्यातः known as Shankhachuda, धनदानुचरः the follower of Kubera, अभ्यगात् arrived.

Simple Meaning: While Krishna and Balarama were thus playing and singing at will, as if completely absorbed in the joy of the moment, a servant of Kubera named Shankhachuda arrived on the scene.

Context Note: This marks a shift in the narrative. After the peaceful and divine atmosphere of the singing in the forest, the appearance of Shankhachuda (a follower of the treasurer of the gods) introduces a new conflict. Despite his high connection to Kubera, his actions in the following verses reveal his asuri nature as he attempts to interfere with the Gopis.

तयोर्निरीक्षतो राजंस्तन्नाथं प्रमदाजनम् ।

क्रोशन्तं कालयामास दिश्युदीच्यामशङ्कितः ॥10.34.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: तयोः while the two (Krishna and Balarama), निरीक्षतोः were looking on, राजन् O King (Parikshit), तत्-नाथम् those who had Them as their protector, प्रमदा-जनम् the women (gopis), क्रोशन्तम् crying out, कालयामास he drove them away, दिशि in the direction, उदीच्याम् North, अशङ्कितः without any fear/hesitation

Anvaya: राजन्! तयोः निरीक्षतोः (सति) तत्नाथं क्रोशन्तं प्रमदाजनम् उदीच्यां दिशि अशङ्कितः कालयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King, तयोः while the two (Krishna and Balarama), निरीक्षतोः were watching, तत्नाथं whose master was Krishna, क्रोशन्तं crying out, प्रमदाजनम् the women, उदीच्यां दिशि in the northern direction, अशङ्कितः without fear, कालयामास he drove away/kidnapped.

Simple Meaning: O King, right before the eyes of Krishna and Balarama, he began driving the women off toward the north. The gopis who had the Lords as their Protectors, cried out in distress as he kidnapped them without any hesitation.

क्रोशन्तं कृष्ण रामेति विलोक्य स्वपरिग्रहम् ।

यथा गा दस्युना ग्रस्ता भ्रातरावन्वधावताम् ॥10.34.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्रोशन्तम् crying out, कृष्ण 'O Krishna', राम 'O Rama', इति thus, विलोक्य seeing, स्व-परिग्रहम् their own dependents/property (the gopis), यथा just as, गाः cows, दस्युना by a thief, ग्रस्ताः seized, भ्रातरौ the two brothers, अन्वधावताम् ran after/pursued

Anvaya: 'कृष्ण राम' इति क्रोशन्तं स्वपरिग्रहं विलोक्य, दस्युना ग्रस्ताः गाः यथा, भ्रातरौ अन्वधावताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya:'कृष्ण राम' इति क्रोशन्तं स्वपरिग्रहं विलोक्य 'O Krishna! O Rama!' Thus crying out, seeing their own dependents, दस्युना ग्रस्ताः गाः यथा like cows seized by a thief, भ्रातरौ अन्वधावताम् the two brothers pursued (Shankhachuda).

Simple Meaning: Hearing the gopis crying out "O Krishna! O Rama!" and seeing that their own devotees were being carried off, the two brothers immediately gave chase. They ran after Shankachuda just as owners would rush to rescue their cows being stolen by a thief.

मा भैष्टेत्यभयारावौ शालहस्तौ तरस्विनौ ।

आसेदतुस्तं तरसा त्वरितं गुह्यकाधमम् ॥10.34.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: मा 'do not', भैष्ट 'fear', इति thus, अभय-आरावौ shouting words of fearlessness, शाल-हस्तौ with Shala trees in their hands, तरस्विनौ very powerful/swift, आसेदतुः they reached/confronted, तम् him (Shankhachuda), तरसा with speed, त्वरितम् quickly, गुह्यक-अधमम् the lowest of the Yakshas (Shankhachuda).

Anvaya: 'मा भैष्ट' इति अभयारावौ शालहस्तौ तरस्विनौ (तौ) त्वरितं तरसा तं गुह्यकाधमं आसेदतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: 'मा भैष्ट' इति अभयारावौ "Do not fear!", thus shouting words of reassurance, शालहस्तौ तरस्विनौ (तौ) with Shala trees in Their hands, the two powerful ones quickly and with great speed, तं गुह्यकाधमं आसेदतुः reached that lowest of the Guhyakas.

Simple Meaning: Shouting "Do not be afraid!", to reassure the gopis, Krishna and Balarama—the two incredibly powerful brothers—picked up Shala trees as weapons and swiftly charged after that wretched Shankhachuda, catching up to him in no time.

स वीक्ष्य तावनुप्राप्तौ कालमृत्यू इवोद्विजन् ।

विसृज्य स्त्रीजनं मूढः प्राद्रवज्जीवितेच्छया ॥10.34.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Shankhachuda), वीक्ष्य seeing, तौ those two (Krishna and Balarama), अनुप्राप्तौ arrived/approaching, काल-मृत्यू Death and Time, इव like, उद्विजन् trembling/agitated, विसृज्य abandoning, स्त्री-जनम् the women (the gopis), मूढः the fool, प्राद्रवत् fled, जीवितेच्छया with a desire to live

Anvaya: कालमृत्यू इव अनुप्राप्तौ तौ वीक्ष्य सः उद्विजन् मूढः स्त्रीजनं विसृज्य जीवितेच्छया प्राद्रवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कालमृत्यू इव अनुप्राप्तौ तौ वीक्ष्य Seeing those two arrived like Time and Death personified, सः उद्विजन् मूढः that trembling fool, स्त्रीजनं विसृज्य abandoning the women, जीवितेच्छया प्राद्रवत् fled out of a desire to save his life.

Simple Meaning: When the foolish Shankhachuda saw Krishna and Balarama bearing down on him like personified Time and Death, he was struck with terror. He immediately abandoned the gopis and ran for his life.

तमन्वधावद् गोविन्दो यत्र यत्र स धावति ।

जिहीर्षुस्तच्छिरोरत्नं तस्थौ रक्षन् स्त्रियो बलः ॥10.34.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, अन्वधावत् pursued, गोविन्दः Lord Krishna, यत्र यत्र wherever, सः he, धावति runs, जिहीर्षुः desiring to take, तत्-शिरः-रत्नम् the jewel on his head, तस्थौ stayed behind, रक्षन् protecting, स्त्रियः the women, बलः Balarama

Anvaya: सः यत्र यत्र धावति, गोविन्दः तच्छिरोरत्नं जिहीर्षुः तम् अन्वधावत्। बलः स्त्रियः रक्षन् तस्थौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः यत्र यत्र धावति Wherever he ran, गोविन्दः तच्छिरोरत्नं जिहीर्षुः Govinda, desiring to take the jewel of his head, तम् अन्वधावत् pursued him. बलः Balarama, स्त्रियः रक्षन् तस्थौ protecting the women, stayed behind.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna chased Shankhachuda wherever he tried to flee, intent on taking the crest jewel from his head. Meanwhile, Balarama stayed behind to remain with the gopis and ensure their protection.

अविदूर इवाभ्येत्य शिरस्तस्य दुरात्मनः ।

जहार मुष्टिनैवाङ्ग सहचूडामणिं विभुः ॥10.34.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: अविदूरे not very far, इव as if, अभ्येत्य having approached, शिरः the head, तस्य of him, दुरात्मनः the wicked-souled (Shankhachuda), जहार took/severed, मुष्टिना with his fist, एव indeed, अङ्ग O King (Parikshit), सह-चूडामणिम् along with the crest jewel, विभुः the Almighty Lord

Anvaya: अङ्ग! विभुः अविदूरे इव अभ्येत्य मुष्टिना एव तस्य दुरात्मनः सहचूडामणिं शिरः जहार।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग! O King, विभुः the Almighty Lord, अविदूरे इव अभ्येत्य having approached him as if he were not far at all, मुष्टिना एव with just a blow of His fist, तस्य दुरात्मनः of that wicked-souled one, सहचूडामणिं शिरः जहार removed the head along with its crest jewel.

Simple Meaning: O King, Lord Krishna quickly overtook him. With a single, powerful blow of His fist, the Almighty Lord severed the head of that wicked Shankhachuda, seizing it along with the brilliant jewel on his crest.

शङ्खचूडं निहत्यैवं मणिमादाय भास्वरम् ।

अग्रजायाददात् प्रीत्या पश्यन्तीनां च योषिताम् ॥10.34.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: शङ्खचूडम् Shankhachuda, निहत्य having killed, एवम् in this way, मणिम् the jewel, आदाय taking, भास्वरम् brilliant, अग्रजाय to His elder brother (Balarama), अददात् He gave, प्रीत्या with affection, पश्यन्तीनाम् who were watching, च and, योषिताम् the women (gopis)

Anvaya: एवं शङ्खचूडं निहत्य भास्वरं मणिम् आदाय पश्यन्तीनां योषितां (पुरतः) प्रीत्या अग्रजाय अददात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं In this way, शङ्खचूडं निहत्य having killed Shankhachuda and, भास्वरं मणिम् आदाय taking the brilliant jewel, पश्यन्तीनां योषितां (पुरतः) while the women were watching, प्रीत्या अग्रजाय अददात् He gave it with affection to His elder brother.

Simple Meaning: After killing Shankhachuda in this manner, Krishna took the shining jewel and, with great love and affection, presented it to His elder brother, Balarama, right in front of all the gopis as they looked on.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहम्स्यां संहितायां दशमे स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “सुदर्शनमोक्षः तथा रासक्रीडायां शङ्खचूडवधो नाम” चतुस्त्रिंशः अध्यायः ॥34॥

Thus ends the thirty-fourth discourse named “Deliverance of Sudarshana and slaying of Shankhachuda by the Lord”, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
