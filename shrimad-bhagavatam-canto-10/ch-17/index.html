Chapter 17

History of Kaliya and The Lord rescues the inhabitants of Vraja from a wild fire

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

दावाग्निमोचनं नाम सप्तदशः अध्यायः

(25 Shlokas)

राजा उवाच

नागालयं रमणकं कस्मात्तत्याज कालियः ।

कृतं किं वा सुपर्णस्य तेनैकेनासमञ्जसम् ॥10.17.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: राजा उवाच King (Parikshit) said, नाग-आलयम् the abode of the serpents, रमणकम् Ramanaka, कस्मात् why, तत्याज abandoned, कालियः Kaliya, कृतम् performed, किम् what, वा or, सुपर्णस्य of Garuda, तेन by him, एकेन alone, असमञ्जसम् offense/misdeed

Anvaya: राजा उवाच—कालियः नागालयं रमणकं कस्मात् तत्याज? तेन एकेन सुपर्णस्य किं वा असमञ्जसं कृतम्?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजा उवाच King Parikshit said— कालियः Kaliya, नागालयं रमणकं the serpents' residence Ramanaka, कस्मात् तत्याज for what reason, did abandon? तेन एकेन By him alone, सुपर्णस्य of Garuda, किं वा what indeed, असमञ्जसं कृतम् offense was committed?

Simple meaning: King Parikshit asked: Why did Kaliya leave Ramanaka Island, the abode of the serpents? And what offense did he alone commit against Garuda to make him so fearful?

श्री शुक उवाच

उपहार्यैः सर्पजनैर्मासि मासीह यो बलिः ।

वानस्पत्यो महाबाहो नागानां प्राङ् निरूपितः ॥10.17.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, उपहार्यैः by those who are to offer gifts, सर्प-जनैः by the serpent people, मासि मासि month after month, इह here, यः which, बलिः offering, वानस्पत्यः consisting of the fruits of the forest/trees, महा-बाहो O mighty-armed one, नागानाम् of the serpents, प्राक् previously, निरूपितः was assigned

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच—महाबाहो! इह उपहार्यैः सर्पजनैः यः वानस्पत्यः बलिः मासि मासी नागानां प्राङ् निरूपितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said — महाबाहो O mighty-armed one!, इह In this world, उपहार्यैः सर्पजनैः by the serpent people who were to offer, यः वानस्पत्यः बलिः that offering of forest products, मासि मासी every month, नागानां for the the serpents, प्राङ् निरूपितः previously, was settled.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: O mighty-armed King! It was previously settled that every month the serpent community would offer a gift of forest products as tribute.

Note: In a commentary on Srimad Bhagavata we read how Garuda—who bore a standing and inveterate grudge against the Serpent race for the cruelty perpetrated on his mother Vinata by the latter's mother, Kadru—used to devour indiscriminately whatever serpent he could lay his hands on. Perceiving the imminent extermination of their race, the Nagas sought protection of Brahma, who summoned Garuda and brought about peace between the two parties by laying it down—with mutual consent—that whatever was brought as an offering to the Nagas at the foot of a tree in the realm of the Nagas every Amavasya should be collected and offered as food to Garuda, who should satisfy his hunger with those offerings and desist from killing the snakes:—

अमायां वृक्षमूले तु नागलोकेषु यद् भवेत्। एकस्मिन् दिवसे तावदेकत्र स्थापयन्तु हि॥

ततो हि गरुडो तस्मिंस्तद् भुक्त्वा नैव पीडयेत् । इति व्यवस्थायां सर्पा मासि मासि बलं ददुः॥

स्वं स्वं भागं प्रयच्छन्ति नागाः पर्वणि पर्वणि ।

गोपीथायात्मनः सर्वे सुपर्णाय महात्मने ॥10.17.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्वम् स्वम् each their own, भागम् share/portion, प्रयच्छन्ति they would deliver, नागाः the serpents, पर्वणि पर्वणि on every new moon day, गोपीथाय for the sake of protection, आत्मनः of themselves, सर्वे all, सुपर्णाय to Garuda, महात्मने the great soul

Anvaya: सर्वे नागाः आत्मनः गोपीथाय पर्वणि पर्वणि महात्मने सुपर्णाय स्वं स्वं भागं प्रयच्छन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वे नागाः All the serpents, आत्मनः गोपीथाय for their own protection, पर्वणि पर्वणि on every new moon day, महात्मने सुपर्णाय to the great soul Garuda, स्वं स्वं भागं each their own share, प्रयच्छन्ति would give.

Simple meaning: To ensure their own protection, all the serpents would faithfully deliver their respective shares of the offering to the great Garuda on every new moon day.

विषवीर्यमदाविष्टः काद्रवेयस्तु कालियः ।

कदर्थीकृत्य गरुडं स्वयं तं बुभुजे बलिम् ॥10.17.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: विष-वीर्य-मद-आविष्टः possessed by the pride of his poison and strength, काद्रवेयः the son of Kadru, तु but, कालियः Kaliya, कदर्थी-कृत्य disregarding/disrespecting, गरुडम् Garuda, स्वयम् himself, तम् that, बुभुजे ate/consumed, बलिम् offering

Anvaya: विषवीर्यमदाविष्टः काद्रवेयः कालियः तु गरुडं कदर्थीकृत्य स्वयं तं बलिं बुभुजे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विषवीर्यमदाविष्टः Overpowered by the arrogance of his poison and power, काद्रवेयः कालियः तु but the son of Kadru, Kaliya, गरुडं कदर्थीकृत्य Garuda, showing contempt, स्वयं himself, तं बलिं बुभुजे ate that offering.

Simple meaning: However, Kaliya, the son of Kadru, became so puffed up by the pride of his own strength and poisonous power that he grew contemptuous of Garuda. He began to personally consume the offerings that were meant for Garuda.

तच्छ्रुत्वा कुपितो राजन् भगवान् भगवत्प्रियः।

विजिघांसुर्महावेगः कालियं समुपाद्रवत् ॥10.17.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् श्रुत्वा having heard that, कुपितः angered, राजन् O King (Parikshit), भगवान् the powerful one (Garuda), भगवत्-प्रियः the beloved devotee of the Supreme Lord, विजिघांसुः desiring to kill, महा-वेगः possessing great speed, कालियम् towards Kaliya, समुपाद्रवत् rushed/attacked

Anvaya: राजन्! भगवत्प्रियः भगवान् तत् श्रुत्वा कुपितः (सन्) विजिघांसुः महावेगः कालियं समुपाद्रवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King!, भगवत्प्रियः The beloved of the Lord, भगवान् the powerful (Garuda), तत् श्रुत्वा having heard that, कुपितः became angry, विजिघांसुः desiring to kill, महावेगः with great speed, कालियं towards Kaliya, समुपाद्रवत् rushed.

Simple meaning: O King! When the powerful Garuda, the beloved devotee of the Lord, heard about this, he became furious. Intent on killing the serpent, he rushed toward Kaliya with incredible speed.

तमापतन्तं तरसा विषायुधः प्रत्यभ्ययादुच्छ्रितनैकमस्तकः ।

दद्भिः सुपर्णं व्यदशद् ददायुधः करालजिह्वोच्छ्वसितोग्रलोचनः ॥10.17.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (Garuda), आपतन्तम् attacking/rushing, तरसा with great force, विष-आयुधः he whose weapon is poison (Kaliya), प्रत्यभ्ययात् went against/counter-attacked, उच्छ्रित-नैक-मस्तकः having raised his many heads, दद्भिः with his fangs, सुपर्णम् Garuda, व्यदशत् bit, दद-आयुधः he whose weapons are his fangs, कराल-जिह्व-उच्छ्वसित-उग्र-लोचनः with terrifying tongues, heavy breathing, and fierce eyes

Anvaya: विषायुधः ददायुधः करालजिह्वोच्छ्वसितोग्रलोचनः उच्छ्रितनैकमस्तकः (कालियः) तरसा आपतन्तं तं सुपर्णं प्रत्यभ्ययात्, दद्भिः व्यदशत् च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विषायुधः He whose weapon is poison, ददायुधः whose weapons are fangs, करालजिह्वोच्छ्वसितोग्रलोचनः with terrifying tongues, hissing breath, and fierce eyes, उच्छ्रितनैकमस्तकः having raised his many heads, (Kaliya) तरसा with force, आपतन्तं who was rushing, तं सुपर्णं that Garuda, प्रत्यभ्ययात् countered, दद्भिः with fangs, व्यदशत् and bit (him).

Simple meaning: Armed with deadly poison and fangs, Kaliya—his many heads raised, his tongues flickering terrifyingly, and his eyes burning with rage—charged at the fast-approaching Garuda and bit him with his sharp fangs.

तं तार्क्ष्यपुत्रः स निरस्य मन्युमान् प्रचण्डवेगो मधुसूदनासनः ।

पक्षेण सव्येन हिरण्यरोचिषा जघान कद्रुसुतमुग्रविक्रमः ॥10.17.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (Kaliya), तार्क्ष्य-पुत्रः the son of Kashyapa, सः he, निरस्य casting aside/repelling, मन्युमान् full of anger, प्रचण्ड-वेगः of terrible speed, मधुसूदन-आसनः the seat (carrier) of Lord Madhusudana, पक्षेण with the wing, सव्येन the left, हिरण्य-रोचिषा glowing like gold, जघान struck, कद्रु-सुतम् the son of Kadru (Kaliya), उग्र-विक्रमः possessing fierce prowess

तार्क्ष for °क्ष्य (N. of a Garuaa, of Kashyapa, and of a tree) src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: मन्युमान् प्रचण्डवेगः मधुसूदनासनः उग्रविक्रमः सः तार्क्ष्यपुत्रः तं निरस्य हिरण्यरोचिषा सव्येन पक्षेण कद्रुसुतं जघान।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मन्युमान् Full of anger, प्रचण्डवेगः with terrible speed, मधुसूदनासनः the carrier of Lord Krishna, उग्रविक्रमः of fierce power, सः तार्क्ष्यपुत्रः that son of Kashyapa, तं निरस्य repelling him (Kaliya's attack), हिरण्यरोचिषा with the golden-glowing, सव्येन पक्षेण left wing, कद्रुसुतं the son of Kadru, जघान struck.

Simple meaning: Garuda, the son of Tarkshya and the powerful vehicle of Lord Madhusudana, was filled with rage. With incredible speed and fierce prowess, he easily repelled Kaliya’s attack and struck the son of Kadru with his left wing, which shone like brilliant gold.

सुपर्णपक्षाभिहतः कालियोऽतीव विह्वलः ।

ह्रदं विवेश कालिन्द्यास्तदगम्यं दुरासदम् ॥10.17.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: सुपर्ण-पक्ष-अभिहतः struck by the wing of Garuda, कालियः Kaliya, अतीव extremely, विह्वलः distressed/agitated, ह्रदम् the lake/pool, विवेश entered, कालिन्द्याः of the Yamuna (daughter of Mount Kalinda), तत्-अगम्यम् inaccessible to him (Garuda), दुरासदम् difficult to approach

Anvaya: सुपर्णपक्षाभिहतः कालियः अतीव विह्वलः (सन्) कालिन्द्याः तदगम्यं दुरासदं ह्रदं विवेश।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सुपर्णपक्षाभिहतः Struck by the wing of Garuda, कालियः Kaliya, अतीव विह्वलः became extremely distressed, कालिन्द्याः of the Yamuna, तदगम्यं inaccessible to Garuda, दुरासदं ह्रदं difficult to reach lake, विवेश entered.

Simple meaning: Struck by Garuda's powerful wing, Kaliya became extremely distressed and overwhelmed. Seeking safety, he dove into a lake in the Yamuna River that was inaccessible to Garuda.

Note: Following shlokas describe why that lake was inaccessible to Garuda.

तत्रैकदा जलचरं गरुडो भक्ष्यमीप्सितम् ।

निवारितः सौभरिणा प्रसह्य क्षुधितोऽहरत् ॥10.17.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there (in that lake), एकदा once, जल-चरम् a creature living in the water (a fish), गरुडः Garuda, भक्ष्यम् food, ईप्सितम् desired, निवारितः being forbidden, सौभरिणा by the sage Saubhari, प्रसह्य by force/violently, क्षुधितः hungry, अहरत् took away/seized

Anvaya: एकदा तत्र क्षुधितः गरुडः सौभरिणा निवारितः (अपि) ईप्सितं भक्ष्यं जलचरं प्रसह्य अहरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एकदा Once, तत्र there, क्षुधितः गरुडः hungry Garuda, सौभरिणा निवारितः although forbidden by Saubhari, ईप्सितं भक्ष्यं desired food, जलचरं a fish, प्रसह्य अहरत् by force, seized.

Simple meaning: Once, at that very lake, a hungry Garuda seized a fish he desired for food. He did this by force, even though the sage Saubhari had forbidden him.

मीनान् सुदुःखितान् दृष्ट्वा दीनान् मीनपतौ हते ।

कृपया सौभरिः प्राह तत्रत्यक्षेममाचरन् ॥10.17.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: मीनान् the fish, सु-दुःखितान् greatly distressed, दृष्ट्वा having seen, दीनान् miserable/helpless, मीन-पतौ (their) leader of the fish (the king of the fishes), हते having been killed, कृपया out of compassion, सौभरिः the sage Saubhari, प्राह spoke, तत्रत्य-क्षेमम्-आचरन् desiring the welfare of those living there

Anvaya: मीनपतौ हते दीनान् सुदुःखितान् मीनान् दृष्ट्वा, सौभरिः कृपया तत्रत्यक्षेमम् आचरन् (एवम्) प्राह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मीनपतौ हते When the leader of the fish was killed, दीनान् सुदुःखितान् मीनान् दृष्ट्वा seeing the fishes helpless and very sorrowful, सौभरिः Saubhari, कृपया out of pity, तत्रत्यक्षेमम् आचरन् acting for the welfare of those remaining there, प्राह spoke (as follows).

Simple meaning: When the king of the fish was killed, the other fish were left helpless and overwhelmed with grief. Seeing their miserable condition, the sage Saubhari was moved by compassion and, seeking to ensure the future safety of the creatures living in that lake, spoke the following words.

अत्र प्रविश्य गरुडो यदि मत्स्यान् स खादति ।

सद्यः प्राणैर्वियुज्येत सत्यमेतद् ब्रवीम्यहम् ॥10.17.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: अत्र here (in this lake), प्रविश्य having entered, गरुडः Garuda, यदि if, मत्स्यान् the fish, सः he, खादति eats, सद्यः immediately, प्राणैः of his life, वियुज्येत he shall be deprived, सत्यम् the truth, एतत् this, ब्रवीमि speak, अहम् I

Anvaya: यदि गरुडः अत्र प्रविश्य मत्स्यान् खादति, (तर्हि) सः सद्यः प्राणैः वियुज्येत; अहम् एतत् सत्यं ब्रवीमि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यदि गरुडः If Garuda, अत्र प्रविश्य having entered here, मत्स्यान् खादति eats the fish, (then) सः he, सद्यः immediately, प्राणैः of life, वियुज्येत will be separated; अहम् एतत् सत्यं ब्रवीमि I speak this truth.

Simple meaning: "If Garuda ever enters this lake again to eat the fish, he will immediately lose his life. I say this as the absolute truth."

तं कालियः परं वेद नान्यः कश्चन लेलिहः ।

अवात्सीद् गरुडाद् भीतः कृष्णेन च विवासितः ॥10.17.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् that (curse), कालियः Kaliya, परम् alone/only, वेद knew, न अन्यः no one else, कश्चन anyone, लेलिहः serpent (lit. one who licks), अवात्सीत् lived/dwelt, गरुडात् from Garuda, भीतः afraid, कृष्णेन by Krishna, च and, विवासितः sent away/banished

Anvaya: तं (शापम्) कालियः परं वेद, अन्यः कश्चन लेलिहः न (वेद)। (अतः सः) गरुडात् भीतः (तत्र) अवात्सीत्, कृष्णेन च विवासितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तं That (curse), कालियः Kaliya, परं वेद only he knew, अन्यः कश्चन लेलिहः any other serpent, न did not (know). (Therefore), गरुडात् भीतः fearing Garuda, (there) अवात्सीत् he lived, कृष्णेन च and by Krishna, विवासितः he was banished.

Simple meaning: Only Kaliya was aware of this curse; no other serpent knew of it. Afraid of Garuda, he chose to live in that specific lake for protection, until he was finally banished from there by Lord Krishna.

कृष्णं ह्रदात् विनिष्क्रान्तं दिव्यस्रग्गन्धवाससम् ।

महामणिगणाकीर्णं जाम्बूनदपरिष्कृतम् ॥10.17.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णम् Lord Krishna, ह्रदात् from the lake, विनिष्क्रान्तम् emerging/coming out, दिव्य-स्रग्-गन्ध-वाससम् wearing divine garlands, fragrant scents, and clothing, महा-मणि-गण-आकीर्णम् covered with many great jewels, जाम्बूनद-परिष्कृतम् adorned with fine gold

Anvaya: (गोपाः ददृशुः) ह्रदात् विनिष्क्रान्तं दिव्यस्रग्गन्धवाससं महामणिगणाकीर्णं जाम्बूनदपरिष्कृतं कृष्णम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (The cowherds saw) ह्रदात् From the lake, विनिष्क्रान्तं emerging, दिव्यस्रग्गन्धवाससं wearing celestial garlands, fragrances, and clothes, महामणिगणाकीर्णं covered with clusters of precious gems, जाम्बूनदपरिष्कृतं adorned with ornaments of pure gold, कृष्णम् Krishna.

Simple meaning: The cowherds saw Krishna emerging from the lake. He was a magnificent sight—arrayed in divine garlands, fragrant pastes, and exquisite clothing, and adorned with clusters of priceless jewels and ornaments of fine gold.

उपलभ्योत्थिताः सर्वे लब्धप्राणा इवासवः ।

प्रमोदनिभृतात्मानो गोपाः प्रीत्याभिरेभिरे ॥10.17.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: उपलभ्य having regained, उत्थिताः stood up, सर्वे all, लब्ध-प्राणाः having regained their life air, इव as if, असवः the senses/vital breaths, प्रमोद-निभृत-आत्मानः their souls filled with joy, गोपाः the cowherds, प्रीत्या with great affection, अभिरेभिरे embraced (Him)

Anvaya: लब्धप्राणाः असवः इव सर्वे गोपाः (कृष्णम्) उपलभ्य उत्थिताः, प्रमोदनिभृतात्मानः (ते) प्रीत्या अभिरेभिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: लब्धप्राणाः Having regained their life, असवः इव like the vital breaths, सर्वे गोपाः all the cowherds, उपलभ्य having found (Krishna), उत्थिताः stood up; प्रमोदनिभृतात्मानः their hearts filled with ecstasy, (they) प्रीत्या with love, अभिरेभिरे embraced (Him).

Simple meaning: Just as the vital senses return to life when the life-breath is restored, all the cowherds sprang to their feet the moment they saw Krishna. Their hearts overflowed with joy, and they rushed to embrace Him with deep affection.

यशोदा रोहिणी नन्दो गोप्यो गोपाश्च कौरव ।

कृष्णं समेत्य लब्धेहा आसँल्लब्धमनोरथाः ॥10.17.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: यशोदा Yashoda, रोहिणी Rohini, नन्दः Nanda, गोप्यः the gopis, गोपाः the cowherds, च and, कौरव O descendant of Kuru (Parikshit), कृष्णम् Krishna, समेत्य having met, लब्ध-ईहाः regaining their physical functions/senses, आसन् became, लब्ध-मनोरथाः having their desires fulfilled

Anvaya: कौरव! यशोदा रोहिणी नन्दः गोप्यः गोपाः च कृष्णं समेत्य लब्धेहाः लब्धमनोरथाः आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कौरव O Parikshit!, यशोदा रोहिणी नन्दः Yashoda, Rohini, Nanda, गोप्यः the cowherd women, गोपाः च and the cowherd men, कृष्णं समेत्य having reached Krishna, लब्धेहाः regained their life-functions (became conscious/active), लब्धमनोरथाः (and) having their greatest desire fulfilled, आसन् they became.

Simple meaning: O King Parikshit! When Yashoda, Rohini, Nanda, and all the cowherd men and women finally reached Krishna, their vital functions returned and they came back to their senses. Having their greatest desire fulfilled by His safety, they felt completely rejuvenated.

रामश्चाच्युतमालिङ्ग्य जहासास्यानुभाववित् ।

नगा गावो वृषा वत्सा लेभिरे परमां मुदम् ॥10.17.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: रामः Balarama, च and, अच्युतम् the infallible Lord (Krishna), आलिङ्ग्य having embraced, जहास laughed, अस्य His (Krishna's), अनुभाव-वित् knowing the prowess/power, नगाः the trees, गावः the cows, वृषाः the bulls, वत्साः the calves, लेभिरे obtained, परमाम् supreme, मुदम् joy

Anvaya: अस्य अनुभाववित् रामः च अच्युतम् आलिङ्ग्य जहास; नगाः गावः वृषाः वत्साः (च) परमां मुदं लेभिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अस्य अनुभाववित् Knowing His (Krishna's) power, रामः Balarama, च and, अच्युतम् आलिङ्ग्य the infallible Lord, having embraced, जहास laughed; नगाः गावः वृषाः वत्साः (च) the trees, the cows, the bulls, and the calves, परमां मुदं supreme joy, लेभिरे obtained.

Simple meaning: Lord Balarama, who was fully aware of Krishna’s divine power, embraced the infallible Lord and laughed with joy. Even the trees, cows, bulls, and calves were filled with the highest state of happiness upon seeing Krishna safe.

नन्दं विप्राः समागत्य गुरवः सकलत्रकाः ।

ऊचुस्ते कालियग्रस्तो दिष्ट्या मुक्तस्तवात्मजः ॥10.17.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्दम् Nanda, विप्राः the learned brahmins, समागत्य coming together/approaching, गुरवः the elders, स-कलत्रकाः accompanied by their wives, ऊचुः said, ते they, कालिय-ग्रस्तः seized/swallowed by Kaliya, दिष्ट्या by good fortune, मुक्तः released/saved, तव your, आत्मजः son

Anvaya: सकलत्रकाः गुरवः विप्राः च नन्दं समागत्य ते ऊचुः — कालियग्रस्तः तव आत्मजः दिष्ट्या मुक्तः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सकलत्रकाः Accompanied by their wives, गुरवः the elders, विप्राः च and the brahmins, नन्दं समागत्य having approached Nanda, ते ऊचुः they said— कालियग्रस्तः तव आत्मजः Your son was seized by Kaliya, दिष्ट्या by providential good fortune, मुक्तः has been released.

Simple meaning: The elder cowherds and learned brahmins, along with their wives, approached Nanda and said, "Your son was seized by the serpent Kaliya, by great good fortune, He is released."

देहि दानं द्विजातीनां कृष्णनिर्मुक्तिहेतवे ।

नन्दः प्रीतमना राजन् गाः सुवर्णं तदादिशत् ॥10.17.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: देहि give, दानम् charity, द्वि-जातीनाम् to the twice-born (brahmins), कृष्ण-निर्मुक्ति-हेतवे because of Krishna’s safe release, नन्दः Nanda, प्रीत-मनाः with a joyful mind, राजन् O King (Parikshit), गाः cows, सुवर्णम् gold, तदा then, आदिशत् ordered/donated

Anvaya: (विप्राः ऊचुः) कृष्णनिर्मुक्तिहेतवे द्विजातीनां दानं देहि। राजन्! तदा प्रीतमानाः नन्दः गाः सुवर्णं च आदिशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (The brahmins said) कृष्णनिर्मुक्तिहेतवे Because of Krishna’s deliverance, द्विजातीनां दानं देहि give charity to the brahmins. राजन् O King! तदा प्रीतमानाः नन्दः Then with a heart full of joy, Nanda, गाः सुवर्णं च आदिशत् ordered (the distribution of) cows and gold.

Simple meaning: The brahmins told Nanda, "You should give charity to the brahmins in gratitude for Krishna's safe return." O King Parikshit! Nanda, his heart overflowing with happiness, then donated cows and gold to them.

यशोदापि महाभागा नष्टलब्धप्रजा सती ।

परिष्वज्याङ्कमारोप्य मुमोचाश्रुकलां मुहुः॥10.17.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: यशोदा Yashoda, अपि also, महा-भागा the greatly fortunate, नष्ट-लब्ध-प्रजा she who had lost and then regained her child, सती being, परिष्वज्य having embraced, अङ्कम् आरोप्य placing Him on her lap, मुमोच shed/released, अश्रु-कलाम् a stream of tears, मुहुः repeatedly

Anvaya: महाभागा यशोदा अपि नष्टलब्धप्रजा सती (कृष्णं) परिष्वज्य अङ्कम् आरोप्य मुहुः अश्रुकलां मुमोच।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महाभागा यशोदा अपि The greatly fortunate Yashoda, also, नष्टलब्धप्रजा सती being one who had lost and then regained her child, (Krishna) परिष्वज्य having embraced, अङ्कम् आरोप्य having placed on her lap, मुहुः repeatedly, अश्रुकलां मुमोच shed a stream of tears.

Simple meaning: The most fortunate Yasoda, who had lost her child and miraculously found Him again, pulled Krishna to her, embraced Him tightly, and placed Him on her lap. Overwhelmed with emotion, she wept tears of joy and relief again and again.

तां रात्रिं तत्र राजेन्द्र क्षुत्तड्भ्यां श्रमकर्शिताः ।

ऊषुर्व्रजौकसो गावः कालिन्द्या उपकूलतः ॥10.17.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताम् that, रात्रिम् night, तत्र there, राजेन्द्र O best of kings (Parikshit), क्षुत्-तड्भ्याम् by hunger and thirst, श्रम-कर्शिताः exhausted by fatigue, ऊषुः stayed/dwelt, व्रज-ओकसः the residents of Vraja, गावः the cows, कालिन्द्याः of the Yamuna, उप-कूलतः near the bank

Anvaya: राजेन्द्र! क्षुत्तड्भ्यां श्रमकर्शिताः व्रजौकसः गावः च तां रात्रिं कालिन्द्याः उपकूलतः तत्र ऊषुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजेन्द्र O best of kings!, क्षुत्तड्भ्यां By hunger and thirst, श्रमकर्शिताः weakened by exhaustion, व्रजौकसः the residents of Vraja, गावः च and the cows, तां रात्रिं that night, कालिन्द्याः of the Yamuna, उपकूलतः near the bank, तत्र there, ऊषुः stayed.

Simple meaning: O best of kings! Because they were exhausted by fatigue and weakened by hunger and thirst, the residents of Vraja and their cows remained there for the night, staying right on the banks of the Yamuna.

तदा शुचिवनोद्भूतो दावाग्निः सर्वतो व्रजम् ।

सुप्तं निशीथ आवृत्य प्रदग्धुमुपचक्रमे ॥10.17.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: तदा then, शुचि-वन-उद्भूतः arising from the dry summer forest, दावाग्निः a forest fire, सर्वतः from all sides, व्रजम् the people of Vraja, सुप्तम् sleeping, निशीथे in the middle of the night, आवृत्य surrounding/encircling, प्रदग्धुम् to burn, उपचक्रमे began

Anvaya: तदा निशीथे शुचिवनोद्भूतः दावाग्निः सुप्तं व्रजं सर्वतः आवृत्य प्रदग्धुम् उपचक्रमे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तदा Then, निशीथे in the middle of the night, शुचिवनोद्भूतः arising from the summer-dried forest, दावाग्निः a forest fire, सुप्तं व्रजं the sleeping people of Vraja, सर्वतः from all sides, आवृत्य having encircled, प्रदग्धुम् to burn them, उपचक्रमे began.

Simple meaning: Then, in the middle of the night while everyone was fast asleep, a forest fire—ignited by the dry summer heat—broke out. It quickly encircled the sleeping residents of Vraja from all directions and began to consume everything in its path.

तत उत्थाय सम्भ्रान्ता दह्यमाना व्रजौकसः ।

कृष्णं ययुस्ते शरणं मायामनुजमीश्वरम् ॥10.17.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, उत्थाय getting up/awakening, सम्भ्रान्ताः bewildered/panicked, दह्यमानाः being scorched, व्रज-ओकसः the residents of Vraja, कृष्णम् to Krishna, ययुः went, ते they, शरणम् for shelter, माया-मनुजम् appearing as a human by His internal potency, ईश्वरम् the Supreme Controller

Anvaya: ततः दह्यमानाः सम्भ्रान्ताः व्रजौकसः उत्थाय मायामनुजम् ईश्वरं तं कृष्णं शरणं ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, दह्यमानाः being scorched (by the heat), सम्भ्रान्ताः panicked, व्रजौकसः the residents of Vraja, उत्थाय having woken up, मायामनुजम् who appeared as a human through His potency, ईश्वरं the Supreme Lord, तं कृष्णं that Krishna, शरणं ययुः went to for shelter.

Simple meaning: Awakened by the searing heat and gripped by panic, the people of Vraja rose up and rushed for shelter to Krishna—the Supreme Lord who, through His divine potency, was playing the role of a human being among them.

कृष्ण कृष्ण महाभाग हे रामामितविक्रम ।

एष घोरतमो वह्निस्तावकान् ग्रसते हि नः ॥10.17.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्ण कृष्ण O Krishna, Krishna, महा-भाग O greatly fortunate one, हे राम O Balarama, अमित-विक्रम O You of limitless prowess, एषः this, घोर-तमः most terrible, वह्निः fire, तावकान् who belong to You, ग्रसते is devouring/swallowing, हि indeed, नः us

Anvaya: हे कृष्ण! कृष्ण! महाभाग! हे राम! अमितविक्रम! एषः घोरतमः वह्निः तावकान् नः ग्रसते हि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्ण! कृष्ण! O Krishna! Krishna! महाभाग! O greatly fortunate one! हे राम! O Balarama! अमितविक्रम! of limitless power! एषः घोरतमः वह्निः This most fearsome fire, तावकान् नः us who are Yours, ग्रसते हि is surely devouring.

Simple meaning: O Krishna, O Krishna! O most fortunate one! O Balarama of limitless prowess! This most terrible fire is devouring us, who belong to You.

सुदुस्तरान्नः स्वान् पाहि कालाग्नेः सुहृदः प्रभो ।

न शक्नुमस्त्वच्चरणं संत्यक्तुमकुतोभयम् ॥10.17.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: सु-दुस्तरात् from the insurmountable/impossible to cross, नः us, स्वान् Your own (people), पाहि please protect, काल-अग्नेः from the fire of death (the forest fire), सुहृदः (Your) friends/well-wishers, प्रभो O Lord, न not, शक्नुमः we are able, त्वत्-चरणम् Your feet, संत्यक्तुम् to give up/leave, अकुतः-भयम् no fear from anywhere

अकुतः अकुतः adv. [न कुतः न. त.] Not from anywhere src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: प्रभो! सुदुस्तरात् कालाग्नेः स्वान् सुहृदः नः पाहि। (वयं) अकुतोभयं त्वच्चरणं संत्यक्तुं न शक्नुमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रभो O Lord! सुदुस्तरात् From this insurmountable, कालाग्नेः fire of death, स्वान् सुहृदः Your own friends, नः पाहि protect us. (वयं) We, अकुतोभयं which grant fearlessness, त्वच्चरणं Your feet, संत्यक्तुं to abandon, न शक्नुमः are not able.

Simple meaning: O Lord, please protect us! We are Your own friends and devotees, and this insurmountable fire is like the fire of death itself. We are simply unable to leave Your lotus feet, which are the only source of absolute fearlessness.

इत्थं स्वजनवैक्लव्यं निरीक्ष्य जगदीश्वरः।

तमग्निमपिबत्तीव्रमनन्तोऽनन्तशक्तिधृक् ॥10.17.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: इत्थम् in this way, स्व-जन-वैक्लव्यम् the distress of His own people, निरीक्ष्य seeing/observing, जगत्-ईश्वरः the Lord of the Universe, तम् that, अग्निम् fire, अपिबत् drank/swallowed, तीव्रम् intense/terrible, अनन्तः the Infinite Lord, अनन्त-शक्ति-धृक् the possessor of infinite power

Anvaya: इत्थं स्वजनवैक्लव्यं निरीक्ष्य, अनन्तशक्तिधृक् अनन्तः जगदीश्वरः तं तीव्रम् अग्निम् अपिबत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इत्थं In this way, स्वजनवैक्लव्यं the suffering of His own people, निरीक्ष्य having seen, अनन्तशक्तिधृक् the wielder of limitless power, अनन्तः the infinite one, जगदीश्वरः the Lord of the universe, तं तीव्रम् अग्निम् that fierce fire, अपिबत् drank.

Simple meaning: Seeing His own people in such distress, the infinite Lord of the universe, who possesses limitless power, simply swallowed the fierce forest fire, instantly rescuing the residents of Vraja.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे दावाग्निमोचनं नाम सप्तदशः अध्यायः ॥17॥

Thus ends the seventeenth discourse named, 'The Lord rescues the inhabitants of Vraja from a wild fire' in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
