Chapter 24

The Lord stops the sacrifice to Indra

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

गोवर्धन पूजा नाम​ चतुर्विंशः अध्यायः

(38 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

भगवानपि तत्रैव बलदेवेन संयुतः ।

अपश्यन्निवसन् गोपानिन्द्रयागकृतोद्यमान् ॥10.24.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, भगवान् the Supreme Lord (Krishna), अपि also, तत्र there (in Vraja), एव certainly, बलदेवेन with Balarama, संयुतः accompanied by, अपश्यत् saw, निवसन् residing, गोपान् the cowherd men, इन्द्र-याग-कृत-उद्यमान् who were making great preparations for a sacrifice to Indra

Anvaya: भगवान् अपि बलदेवेन संयुतः तत्र एव निवसन् इन्द्रयागकृतोद्यमान् गोपान् अपश्यत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, भगवान् अपि The Supreme Lord also, बलदेवेन संयुतः accompanied by Balarama, तत्र एव निवसन् residing right there (in Vraja), इन्द्रयागकृतोद्यमान् making efforts for the Indra-yajna, गोपान् the cowherds, अपश्यत् saw.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: While residing in Vraja with His brother Balarama, the Supreme Lord Krishna saw the cowherd men, busily making elaborate preparations for a sacrifice intended to worship Indra.

तदभिज्ञोऽपि भगवान् सर्वात्मा सर्वदर्शनः ।

प्रश्रयावनतोऽपृच्छद् वृद्धान् नन्दपुरोगमान् ॥10.24.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तद्-अभिज्ञः aware of that (the purpose of the sacrifice), अपि although, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, सर्वात्मा the soul of all, सर्व-दर्शनः the all-seeing (witness), प्रश्रय-अवनतः bowing down with humility/modesty, अपृच्छत् he asked, वृद्धान् the elders, नन्द-पुरोगमान् headed by Nanda Maharaja

Anvaya: सर्वात्मा सर्वदर्शनः भगवान् तदभिज्ञः अपि प्रश्रयावनतः (सन्) नन्दपुरोगमान् वृद्धान् अपृच्छत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वात्मा The Soul of everyone, सर्वदर्शनः the Witness of everything, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, तदभिज्ञः अपि although knowing all about it, प्रश्रयावनतः bowing with humility, नन्दपुरोगमान् led by Nanda, वृद्धान् the elders, अपृच्छत् questioned.

Simple meaning: Although Lord Krishna is the soul of all beings and the all-seeing witness who already knew exactly what the cowherds were doing, He approached the elders, led by His father Nanda Maharaja, and with great humility and respect, He began to question them.

कथ्यतां मे पितः कोऽयं सम्भ्रमो व उपागतः ।

किं फलं कस्य चोद्देशः केन वा साध्यते मखः ॥10.24.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: कथ्यताम् please tell, मे to me, पितः O father, कः what, अयम् this, सम्भ्रमः great endeavor/bustle, वः of yours, उपागतः has arrived, किम् फलम् what is the result, कस्य for whom, च and, उद्देशः the purpose/intended person, केन by what means, वा or, साध्यते is being accomplished, मखः the sacrifice

Anvaya: पितः! वः उपागतः अयं कः सम्भ्रमः? (अस्य) किं फलम्? कस्य च उद्देशः? केन वा अयं मखः साध्यते? मे कथ्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पितः O Father! वः उपागतः That has come upon you, अयं कः सम्भ्रमः what is this great flurry of activity? किं फलम् What is the fruit? कस्य च उद्देशः And for whom is it intended? केन वा And by what means/procedure, अयं मखः this sacrifice, साध्यते is being carried out? मे कथ्यताम् Please tell me.

Simple meaning: Krishna said: "O Father, please tell me, what is this great commotion and preparation all about? What is the expected result of this sacrifice? For whose pleasure is it intended, and by what specific rituals is it being performed?"

एतद् ब्रूहि महान् कामो मह्यं शुश्रुषवे पितः ।

न हि गोप्यं हि साधूनां कृत्यं सर्वात्मनामिह ॥10.24.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: एतत् this, ब्रूहि please tell, महान् great, कामः desire (curiosity), मह्यम् to me, शुश्रुषवे who am very eager to hear, पितः O father, न not, हि certainly, गोप्यम् to be kept secret, हि indeed, साधूनाम् of saintly persons, कृत्यम् the activities, सर्वात्मनाम् who see everyone as themselves (impartial), इह in this world

Anvaya: पितः! शुश्रुषवे मह्यम् एतत् ब्रूहि (यतः) मह्यं महान् कामः (अस्ति)। इह सर्वात्मनां साधूनां कृत्यं हि गोप्यं न हि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पितः O Father! शुश्रुषवे मह्यम् To me, who am eager to listen, एतत् ब्रूहि tell this, महान् कामः (for I have) a great desire. इह In this world, सर्वात्मनां साधूनां of saintly people who are equal to all, कृत्यं the actions, गोप्यं न हि are not secret.

Simple meaning: Krishna continued: "My dear father, I am very eager to hear about this, so please explain it to me. After all, for saintly persons who see everyone as their own self, there should be nothing to keep secret regarding their activities."

अस्त्यस्वपरदृष्टीनाममित्रोदास्तविद्विषाम् ।

उदासीनोऽरिवद् वर्ज्य आत्मवत् सुहृदुच्यते ॥10.24.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: अस्ति there is, अ-स्व-पर-दृष्टीनाम् for those who distinguish between "mine" and "thine" (or "self" and "other"), अ-मित्र-उदास्त-विद्विषाम् those who have enemies, neutral parties, and envious rivals, उदासीनः a neutral person, अरि-वत् like an enemy, वर्ज्यः should be avoided, आत्म-वत् like one's own self, सुहृत् a friend, उच्यते is said (to be treated)

Anvaya: अस्वपरदृष्टीनाम् अमित्रोदास्तविद्विषाम् (जनानां कृते) उदासीनः अरिवत् वर्ज्यः (अस्ति), सुहृत् (च) आत्मवत् (ग्राह्यः) उच्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अस्वपरदृष्टीनाम् For those who see distinctions between "self" and "others," and, अमित्रोदास्तविद्विषाम् who recognize enemies, neutrals, and rivals: उदासीनः अरिवत् वर्ज्यः a neutral person is to be avoided just like an enemy, सुहृत् (च) आत्मवत् उच्यते while a friend is said to be treated as one's own self.

Simple meaning: Krishna continues His logic: People who still see the world in terms of 'mine' and 'yours' usually categorize others as friends, enemies, or neutral parties. To such people, a neutral person is as good as an enemy and should be avoided, whereas a friend should be cherished as dearly as one's own life. (Since I am your son and your dearest friend, you should not hide anything from me.)

ज्ञात्वाज्ञात्वा च कर्माणि जनोऽयमनुतिष्ठति ।

विदुषः कर्मसिद्धिः स्यात्तथा नाविदुषो भवेत् ॥10.24.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: ज्ञात्वा having known, अज्ञात्वा without knowing, च and, कर्माणि prescribed duties, जनः a person, अयम् this, अनुतिष्ठति performs, विदुषः for one who is wise, कर्म-सिद्धिः success in work, स्यात् becomes, तथा in that way, न not, अविदुषः for the ignorant, भवेत् it may be

Anvaya: अयं जनः कर्माणि ज्ञात्वा अज्ञात्वा च अनुतिष्ठति, (किन्तु) विदुषः कर्मसिद्धिः स्यात्, तथा अविदुषः (सिद्धिः) न भवेत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अयं जनः This world of people, कर्माणि अनुतिष्ठति performs activities, ज्ञात्वा अज्ञात्वा च both with knowledge and without knowledge; (किन्तु) विदुषः however, for the wise, कर्मसिद्धिः स्यात् success in work is achieved, तथा अविदुषः but for the ignorant, न भवेत् it is not so.

Simple meaning: Krishna argues that while everyone in this world performs some kind of work—some understanding the underlying principles and others acting blindly—it is only the work done with knowledge that leads to true success. One who acts in ignorance does not achieve the same result. Therefore, he urges Nanda to explain the actual reason behind the Indra-yajna.

तत्र तावत् क्रियायोगो भवतां किं विचारितः ।

अथवा लौकिकस्तन्मे पृच्छतः साधु भण्यताम् ॥10.24.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र in that regard, तावत् then, क्रिया-योगः this performance of ritual, भवताम् by you, किम् whether, विचारितः is based on scriptural conclusion (deliberated upon), अथवा or, लौकिकः a traditional social custom, तत् that, मे to Me, पृच्छतः who am asking, साधु clearly, भण्यताम् please be told

Anvaya: तत्र तावत् भवताम् अयं क्रियायोगः किं विचारितः अथवा लौकिकः? पृच्छतः मे तत् साधु भण्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र तावत् In that regard, भवताम् by you, अयं क्रियायोगः this ritualistic performance, किं विचारितः is it based on careful deliberation of scriptures, अथवा लौकिकः or is it just a worldly tradition? पृच्छतः मे To Me who am asking, तत् साधु भण्यताम् please explain that clearly.

Simple meaning: Krishna questions His father further: "I want to know about this ceremony you are preparing for. Is it based on the injunctions of the scriptures (Vedic science), or is it simply a customary tradition followed by the local people? Since I am asking with sincere interest, please explain the matter clearly to Me."

नन्द उवाच

पर्जन्यो भगवानिन्द्रो मेघास्तस्यात्ममूर्तयः ।

तेऽभिवर्षन्ति भूतानां प्रीणनं जीवनं पयः ॥10.24.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्दः उवाच Nanda said, पर्जन्यः the rain-god, भगवान् the great lord, इन्द्रः Indra, मेघाः the clouds, तस्य his, आत्म-मूर्तयः personal representations (forms), ते they, अभिवर्षन्ति rain down, भूतानाम् for all living entities, प्रीणनम् delight, जीवनम् the source of life, पयः water

Anvaya: नन्दः उवाच — भगवान् इन्द्रः पर्जन्यः (अस्ति), मेघाः तस्य आत्ममूर्तयः (सन्ति), ते भूतानां प्रीणनं जीवनं पयः अभिवर्षन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्दः उवाच Nanda said, भगवान् इन्द्रः The great lord Indra, पर्जन्यः is the rain-god; मेघाः the clouds, तस्य आत्ममूर्तयः are his personal forms; ते they, भूतानां for all living beings, प्रीणनं delighting, जीवनं life-giving, पयः water, अभिवर्षन्ति rain down.

Simple meaning: Nanda said: "The great lord Indra is the master of the rain. The clouds are his personal representatives, and they pour down the water that brings satisfaction and life to all living entities. (Therefore, we must worship him as he provides the very basis of our survival.)"

तं तात वयमन्ये च वार्मुचां पतिमीश्वरम् ।

द्रव्यैस्तद्रेतसा सिद्धैर्यजन्ते क्रतुभिर्नराः ॥10.24.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् that, तात O dear, वयम् we, अन्ये others, च and, वार्मुचाम् of the clouds, पतिम् the master, ईश्वरम् the lord, द्रव्यैः with various ingredients, तत्-रेतसा by that which is produced by his seed (the rain), सिद्धैः which are accomplished, यजन्ते worship, क्रतुभिः with sacrifices, नराः humans

Anvaya: तात! वयम् अन्ये च नराः वार्मुचां पतिं तम् ईश्वरं, तद्रेतसा सिद्धैः द्रव्यैः क्रतुभिः यजन्ते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तात O dear son, वयम् we, अन्ये च नराः and other men, वार्मुचां पतिं the master of the clouds, तम् ईश्वरं that lord, तद्रेतसा by his "seed" (the rainwater), सिद्धैः which are produced, द्रव्यैः using those ingredients, क्रतुभिः with sacrifices, यजन्ते worship.

Simple meaning: "My dear boy, we and other men worship that great lord Indra, who is the master of the clouds. We perform these sacrifices using the very grains and materials that are produced from the water he sends down. Since he provides the means for our worship and our very existence, we offer these sacrifices back to him.

तच्छेषेणोपजीवन्ति त्रिवर्गफलहेतवे ।

पुंसां पुरुषकाराणां पर्जन्यः फलभावनः ॥10.24.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-शेषेण by the remnants of that (sacrifice), उपजीवन्ति they sustain their lives, त्रिवर्ग-फल-हेतवे for the purpose of achieving the three goals of human life (dharma, artha, and kama), पुंसाम् for men, पुरुष-काराणाम् of those who exert human effort, पर्जन्यः the rain-god, फल-भावनः is the provider of the results

Anvaya: त्रिवर्गफलहेतवे तत्-शेषेण उपजीवन्ति, पुरुषकाराणां पुंसां पर्जन्यः फलभावनः (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: त्रिवर्गफलहेतवे For the purpose of attaining the results of dharma, artha, and kama, तत्-शेषेण by the remnants of that sacrifice, उपजीवन्ति they sustain their lives; पुरुषकाराणां पुंसां for men who perform human efforts, पर्जन्यः the rain-god, फलभावनः is the provider of the results.

Simple meaning: "People sustain their lives by using the remnants of these sacrifices to achieve the three goals of life—religiosity, economic development, and sense gratification. For any man who puts in hard work and effort, it is ultimately the rain-god Indra who provides the successful results of that labor."

य एवं विसृजेद् धर्मं पारम्पर्यागतं नरः ।

कामाल्लोभाद् भयाद् द्वेषात् स वै नाप्नोति शोभनम् ॥10.24.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: यः who, एवम् in this way, विसृजेत् should give up, धर्मम् the religious principles, पारम्पर्य-आगतम् received through tradition (parampara), नरः a man, कामात् out of lust, लोभात् out of greed, भयात् out of fear, द्वेषात् out of enmity, सः he, वै indeed, न not, आप्नोति achieves, शोभनम् auspiciousness

Anvaya: यः नरः कामात् लोभात् भयात् द्वेषात् (वा) पारम्पर्यागतं धर्मम् एवं विसृजेत्, सः वै शोभनं न आप्नोति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः नरः A man who, कामात् out of lust, लोभात् greed, भयात् fear, द्वेषात् or enmity, पारम्पर्यागतं धर्मम् the religious principles received through tradition, एवं विसृजेत् should in this way give up, सः वै he indeed, शोभनं auspiciousness, न आप्नोति does not achieve.

Simple meaning: Nanda Maharaja warns: "A person who abandons the religious traditions handed down through the generations—whether out of desire, greed, fear, or ill will—will never achieve anything auspicious in life. (Nanda is emphasizing that following the established parampara is essential for the well-being of the community.)"

श्री शुक उवाच

वचो निशम्य नन्दस्य तथान्येषां व्रजौकसाम् ।

इन्द्राय मन्युं जनयन् पितरं प्राह केशवः॥10.24.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, वचः the words, निशम्य hearing, नन्दस्य of Nanda, तथा and, अन्येषाम् of others, व्रज-ओकसाम् of the inhabitants of Vraja, इन्द्राय for Indra, मन्युम् anger, जनयन् provoking, पितरम् to His father, प्राह spoke, केशवः Lord Keshava (Krishna)

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच—नन्दस्य तथा अन्येषां व्रजौकसां वचः निशम्य, इन्द्राय मन्युं जनयन् केशवः पितरं प्राह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said: नन्दस्य of Nanda, तथा and, अन्येषां व्रजौकसां of other inhabitants of Vraja, वचः निशम्य the words having heard, इन्द्राय for Indra, मन्युं anger, जनयन् provoking, केशवः Lord Keshava, पितरं प्राह spoke to His father.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: After hearing the words of Nanda Maharaja and the other residents of Vraja, Lord Krishna—intending to provoke Indra's anger (to shatter his pride)—spoke to His father in the following way.

श्री भगवान् उवाच

कर्मणा जायते जन्तुः कर्मणैव विलीयते ।

सुखं दुःखं भयं क्षेमं कर्मणैवाभिपद्यते ॥10.24.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री भगवान् उवाच the Supreme Lord said, कर्मणा by karma (action), जायते is born, जन्तुः a living being, कर्मणा by karma, एव alone, विलीयते dies (is dissolved), सुखम् happiness, दुःखम् misery, भयम् fear, क्षेमम् security, कर्मणा by karma, एव alone, अभिपद्यते is attained

Anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच—जन्तुः कर्मणा जायते, कर्मणा एव विलीयते, सुखं दुःखं भयं क्षेमं (च) कर्मणा एव अभिपद्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच The Supreme Lord said: जन्तुः A living being, कर्मणा जायते is born by karma, कर्मणा एव विलीयते by karma alone is dissolved; सुखं दुःखं भयं क्षेमं happiness, misery, fear, and security, कर्मणा एव by karma alone, अभिपद्यते is attained.

Simple meaning: Lord Krishna replied: "A living entity is born by the force of his past actions (karma) and passes away solely because of them. Likewise, it is only through one's own karma that one experiences happiness, distress, fear, and security. (Krishna is introducing the philosophy of Karma-mimamsa to challenge the necessity of worshipping Indra.)"

अस्ति चेदीश्वरः कश्चित् फलरूप्यन्यकर्मणाम् ।

कर्तारं भजते सोऽपि न ह्यकर्तुः प्रभुर्हि सः ॥10.24.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: अस्ति there is, चेत् if, ईश्वरः a lord (controller), कश्चित् someone, फल-रूपी the giver of results, अन्य-कर्मणाम् of the actions of others, कर्तारम् the performer of action, भजते favors/depends upon, सः he, अपि also, न not, हि indeed, अकर्तुः of one who performs no action, प्रभुः the lord/master, हि certainly, सः he

Anvaya: कश्चित् ईश्वरः अन्यकर्मणां फलरूपी अस्ति चेत्, सः अपि कर्तारं भजते, हि सः अकर्तुः प्रभुः न (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कश्चित् ईश्वरः If some lord, अन्यकर्मणां फलरूपी as the giver of results of the actions of others, अस्ति चेत् exists, सः अपि he also, कर्तारं the performer of action, भजते favors (depends upon); हि for, सः he, अकर्तुः of one who does not perform work, प्रभुः the master, न is not.

Simple meaning: Krishna continues His argument: "Even if there is a supreme controller who awards the results of everyone's work, that controller still depends on the person performing the work. He can only reward those who actually engage in action; He has no power to grant results to someone who does nothing. (Therefore, it is the action itself that is primary, and Indra is merely a secondary factor.)"

किमिन्द्रेणेह भूतानां स्वस्वकर्मानुवर्तिनाम् ।

अनीशेनान्यथा कर्तुं स्वभावविहितं नृणाम् ॥10.24.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: किम् what use, इन्द्रेण with Indra, इह in this world, भूतानाम् for living beings, स्व-स्व-कर्म-अनुवर्तिनाम् who are following the results of their own respective actions, अनीशेन who is unable, अन्यथा otherwise, कर्तुम् to do, स्वभाव-विहितम् that which is ordained by one's own nature, नृणाम् for men

Anvaya: इह स्वस्वकर्मानुवर्तिनां भूतानां नृणां स्वभावविहितम् अन्यथा कर्तुम् अनीशेन इन्द्रेण किम्?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इह In this world, स्वस्वकर्मानुवर्तिनां भूतानां for living beings following their own respective karma, नृणां स्वभावविहितम् what is ordained by one's own nature for men, अन्यथा कर्तुम् to act otherwise (to change), अनीशेन इन्द्रेण किम् what is the use of Indra who is incapable?

Simple meaning: Krishna concludes this part of His logic: "Since all living beings in this world simply follow the results of their own past actions, what is the use of Indra? He is powerless to change the destiny that is already ordained for men by their own natures and karma. (Therefore, we should focus on our duties rather than worrying about pleasing Indra.)"

स्वभावतन्त्रो हि जनः स्वभावमनुवर्तते ।

स्वभावस्थमिदं सर्वं सदेवासुरमानुषम् ॥10.24.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्वभाव-तन्त्रः under the control of one's own nature, हि indeed, जनः a person, स्वभावम् nature, अनुवर्तते follows, स्वभाव-स्थम् situated in nature, इदम् this, सर्वम् all, स-देव-असुर-मानुषम् including the demigods, asuras, and human beings

Anvaya: जनः हि स्वभावतन्त्रः (अस्ति), स्वभावम् अनुवर्तते। सदेवासुरमानुषम् इदं सर्वं स्वभावस्थम् (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जनः A person, हि indeed, स्वभावतन्त्रः is under the control of his own nature. स्वभावम् अनुवर्तते And follows that nature; सदेवासुरमानुषम् Including the demigods, asuras, and humans, इदं सर्वं all this (the whole world), स्वभावस्थम् is situated in nature.

Simple meaning: Krishna continues His philosophical challenge: "Every living being is under the thumb of their own nature and simply acts according to those inherent tendencies. In fact, the entire universe—including the demigods like Indra, the asuras, and all human beings—is established in and driven by this same natural force of karma."

देहानुच्चावचाञ्जन्तुः प्राप्योत्सृजति कर्मणा ।

शत्रुर्मित्रमुदासीनः कर्मैव गुरुरीश्वरः ॥10.24.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: देहान् bodies, उच्च-अवचान् high and low, जन्तुः a living entity, प्राप्य having obtained, उत्सृजति gives them up, कर्मणा by the force of karma, शत्रुः an enemy, मित्रम् a friend, उदासीनः a neutral party, कर्म karma, एव alone, गुरुः the spiritual master, ईश्वरः the supreme controller

Anvaya: जन्तुः कर्मणा उच्चावचान् देहान् प्राप्य उत्सृजति (च); कर्म एव शत्रुः मित्रम् उदासीनः गुरुः ईश्वरः (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जन्तुः A living entity, कर्मणा by the force of karma, उच्चावचान् देहान् high and low bodies, प्राप्य having obtained, उत्सृजति gives them up; कर्म एव karma alone, शत्रुः is the enemy, मित्रम् the friend, उदासीनः the neutral party, गुरुः the teacher, ईश्वरः and the supreme lord.

Simple meaning: Krishna emphasizes the absolute power of karma: "It is solely through karma that a living being takes birth in higher or lower species and eventually leaves those bodies. One's own karma is actually the true friend, the true enemy, the neutral party, the teacher, and the supreme controller. (By this, He suggests that there is no need to look toward Indra, as one's own actions dictate every relationship and circumstance.)"

Note: Good karma - good results , so like a friend. Bad karma - bad results, so like an enemy.

तस्मात् सम्पूजयेत् कर्म स्वभावस्थः स्वकर्मकृत् ।

अञ्जसा येन वर्तेत तदेवास्य हि दैवतम् ॥10.24.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मात् therefore, सम्पूजयेत् one should fully worship, कर्म action (work), स्वभाव-स्थः remaining true to one's own nature, स्व-कर्म-कृत् performing one's own duty, अञ्जसा easily/honestly, येन by which, वर्तेत one maintains his livelihood, तत् that, एव indeed, अस्य for him, हि certainly, दैवतम् worshipable deity

Anvaya: तस्मात् स्वभावस्थः स्वकर्मकृत् (सन्) कर्म सम्पूजयेत्। येन अञ्जसा वर्तेत, तत् एव हि अस्य दैवतम् (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्मात् Therefore, स्वभावस्थः staying true to one's nature, स्वकर्मकृत् performing one's own duty, कर्म सम्पूजयेत् one should worship work. येन By which, अञ्जसा easily (one's livelihood), वर्तेत is maintained, तत् एव हि that indeed, अस्य for him, दैवतम् is the deity.

Simple meaning: Krishna gives His conclusion: "Therefore, a person should stay true to their own nature and simply focus on performing their prescribed duties. One should practically worship their work, because the very occupation that sustains a person's life and provides their livelihood is, in fact, their true worshipable deity."

आजीव्यैकतरं भावं यस्त्वन्यमुपजीवति ।

न तस्मात् विन्दते क्षेमं जारं नार्यसती यथा ॥10.24.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: आजीव्य living upon/gaining livelihood from, एकतरम् one, भावम् entity/source, यः who, तु indeed, अन्यम् another, उपजीवति worships/serves for livelihood, न not, तस्मात् from that, विन्दते obtains, क्षेमम् auspiciousness/security, जारम् a paramour, नारी a woman, असती unchaste, यथा just as

Anvaya: यः एकतरं भावम् आजीव्य अन्यं तु उपजीवति, (सः) तस्मात् क्षेमं न विन्दते, यथा असती नारी जारम् (उपजीवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः One who, एकतरं भावम् आजीव्य gaining livelihood from one source, अन्यं तु उपजीवति but another serves/worships, (सः) he, तस्मात् from that, क्षेमं न विन्दते security/benefit does not obtain, यथा just as, असती नारी an unchaste woman, जारम् (follows) a paramour.

Simple meaning: Krishna uses a strong analogy: "If someone gains their livelihood from one source but gives their devotion and worship to another, they will never receive any real benefit. Just as an unchaste woman cannot find true auspiciousness or security by turning away from her husband to serve a paramour, we should not ignore the source of our own livelihood to worship someone else."

वर्तेत ब्रह्मणा विप्रो राजन्यो रक्षया भुवः ।

वैश्यस्तु वार्तया जीवेच्छूद्रस्तु द्विजसेवया ॥10.24.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: वर्तेत should live, ब्रह्मणा by the Vedas (teaching and priesthood), विप्रः a brahmana, राजन्यः a kshatriya, रक्षया by protection, भुवः of the earth, वैश्यः a vaishya, तु but, वार्तया by agriculture and trade, जीवेत् should live, शूद्रः a shudra, तु indeed, द्विज-सेवया by serving the twice-born classes

Anvaya: विप्रः ब्रह्मणा वर्तेत, राजन्यः भुवः रक्षया (वर्तेत), वैश्यः तु वार्तया जीवेत्, शूद्रः तु द्विजसेवया (जीवेत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विप्रः ब्रह्मणा वर्तेत A brahmana, by the Vedas, should maintain himself; राजन्यः भुवः रक्षया (वर्तेत) a kshatriya, by protecting the earth, should live; वैश्यः तु वार्तया जीवेत् but a vaishya, by commerce and agriculture, should live; शूद्रः तु द्विजसेवया (जीवेत्) and a shudra, by serving the twice-born, should live.

Simple meaning: Krishna outlines the duties of the four social orders: "A brahmana should sustain himself by teaching and studying the Vedas; a kshatriya by protecting the land and its people; a vaishya by engaging in agriculture, trade, and cow protection; and a shudra by serving the other three classes. Each should be loyal to the occupation that supports them."

कृषिवाणिज्यगोरक्षा कुसीदं तुर्यमुच्यते ।

वार्ता चतुर्विधा तत्र वयं गोवृत्तयोऽनिशम् ॥10.24.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृषि-वाणिज्य-गोरक्षा agriculture, trade, and cow protection, कुसीदम् lending money (interest), तुर्यम् the fourth, उच्यते is said, वार्ता occupational science, चतुर्-विधा of four kinds, तत्र among those, वयम् we, गो-वृत्तयः those whose livelihood depends on cows, अनिशम् always

Anvaya: कृषिवाणिज्यगोरक्षा कुसीदम् (इति) वार्ता चतुर्विधा उच्यते। तत्र वयम् अनिशं गोवृत्तयः (स्मः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृषिवाणिज्यगोरक्षा Agriculture, commerce, and cow protection, (and) कुसीदम् money-lending, चतुर्विधा वार्ता उच्यते are said to be the fourfold occupational science. तत्र Among those, वयम् we, अनिशं always, गोवृत्तयः (स्मः) are those whose livelihood depends on cows.

Simple meaning: Krishna specifies the occupation of the community in Vraja: "The science of Varta (livelihood) is described as fourfold: farming, trade, cow protection, and money-lending. Among these four, we belong to the category that is permanently dedicated to the protection and service of the cows."

सत्त्वं रजस्तम इति स्थित्युत्पत्त्यन्तहेतवः ।

रजसोत्पद्यते विश्वमन्योन्यं विविधं जगत् ॥10.24.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: सत्त्वम् goodness, रजः passion, तमः ignorance, इति thus, स्थिति-उत्पत्ति-अन्त-हेतवः the causes of maintenance, creation, and destruction, रजसा by the mode of passion, उत्पद्यते is generated, विश्वम् the universe, अन्योन्यम् by mutual combination, विविधम् variegated, जगत् the world

Anvaya: सत्त्वं रजः तमः इति स्थित्युत्पत्त्यन्तहेतवः (सन्ति)। रजसा अन्योन्यं विविधं विश्वं जगत् उत्पद्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सत्त्वं रजः तमः इति Goodness, passion, and ignorance, स्थित्युत्पत्त्यन्तहेतवः are the causes of maintenance, creation, and destruction; रजसा By the mode of passion, अन्योन्यं through mutual combination, विविधं variegated, विश्वं जगत् the entire universe and world, उत्पद्यते is generated.

Simple meaning: Krishna explains the mechanics of nature: "The three modes of material nature—goodness, passion, and ignorance—are responsible for the maintenance, creation, and destruction of this world. Specifically, it is the mode of passion (rajo-guna) that, through the mutual combination of elements, brings about the birth and variety of this entire universe."

रजसा चोदिता मेघा वर्षन्त्यम्बूनि सर्वतः।

प्रजास्तैरेव सिद्ध्यन्ति महेन्द्र किं करिष्यति ॥10.24.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: रजसा by the mode of passion, चोदिताः impelled, मेघाः the clouds, वर्षन्ति rain down, अम्बूनि waters, सर्वतः everywhere, प्रजाः the living beings, तैः by those (waters), एव certainly, सिद्ध्यन्ति thrive/attain success, महेन्द्रः the great Indra, किम् what, करिष्यति will he do

Anvaya: रजसा चोदिताः मेघाः सर्वतः अम्बूनि वर्षन्ति, तैः एव प्रजाः सिद्ध्यन्ति; (तत्र) महेन्द्रः किं करिष्यति?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: रजसा चोदिताः Impelled by the mode of passion, मेघाः the clouds, सर्वतः everywhere, अम्बूनि वर्षन्ति rain down waters; तैः एव by those (waters) certainly, प्रजाः the living beings, सिद्ध्यन्ति thrive; (तत्र) in this matter, महेन्द्रः the great Indra, किं करिष्यति what will he do?

Simple meaning: Krishna continues His logic: "It is the mode of passion that drives the clouds to pour rain everywhere. It is by that very water that all living beings survive and thrive. Since this is a natural process of the modes of nature, what is the need for Indra? What can he actually do for us?"

न नः पुरो जनपदा न ग्रामा न गृहा वयम् ।

नित्यं वनौकसस्तात वनशैलनिवासिनः ॥10.24.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, नः our, पुरः cities, जनपदाः inhabited territories, न not, ग्रामाः villages, न not, गृहाः houses, वयम् we, नित्यम् always, वन-ओकसः forest-dwellers, तात O dear father, वन-शैल-निवासिनः inhabitants of the forests and mountains

Anvaya: तात! नः पुरः न, जनपदाः न, ग्रामाः न, गृहाः न (सन्ति); वयं नित्यं वनौकसः वनशैलनिवासिनः (स्मः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तात O dear father!, नः Our, पुरः cities, न are not, जनपदाः territories, न are not, ग्रामाः villages, न are not, गृहाः houses, न are not; वयं नित्यं we always, वनौकसः वनशैलनिवासिनः are forest-dwellers, inhabitants of the forests and mountains.

Simple meaning: Krishna reminds Nanda of their natural lifestyle: "My dear father, we are not city-dwellers, nor do we belong to vast territories, villages, or permanent houses. We are people of the wilderness, forever living in the forests and on the hills. (Therefore, we should honor the nature that surrounds us rather than a distant deity like Indra.)"

तस्माद् गवां ब्राह्मणानामद्रेश्चारभ्यतां मखः ।

य इन्द्रयागसम्भारास्तैरयं साध्यतां मखः ॥10.24.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मात् therefore, गवाम् for the cows, ब्राह्मणानाम् for the brahmanas, अद्रेः and for the hill (Govardhana), आरभ्यताम् let it be started, मखः a sacrifice, ये which, इन्द्र-याग-सम्भाराः ingredients collected for the Indra-yajna, तैः with those, अयम् this, साध्यताम् let it be performed, मखः sacrifice

Anvaya: तस्मात् गवां ब्राह्मणानाम् अद्रेः च मखः आरभ्यताम्; ये इन्द्रयागसम्भाराः (सन्ति), तैः अयं मखः साध्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्मात् Therefore, गवां for the cows, ब्राह्मणानाम् for the brahmanas, अद्रेः च and for the hill, मखः a sacrifice, आरभ्यताम् should be started; ये इन्द्रयागसम्भाराः those materials meant for the Indra-yajna, तैः with them, अयं मखः this sacrifice, साध्यताम् should be accomplished.

Simple meaning: Krishna gives His final recommendation: "Therefore, let us begin a sacrifice for the benefit of the cows, the brahmanas, and Govardhana Hill. All the ingredients and preparations you have gathered for the sacrifice to Indra should instead be used to conduct this worship for the mountain and our community."

पच्यन्तां विविधाः पाकाः सूपान्ताः पायसादयः ।

संयावापूपशष्कुल्यः सर्वदोहश्च गृह्यताम् ॥10.24.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: पच्यन्ताम् let there be cooked, विविधाः various kinds of, पाकाः preparations of food, सूप-अन्ताः ending with (including) soups/dals, पायस-आदयः sweet rice and so on, संयाव-अपूप-शष्कुल्यः fried sweet cakes, steamed cakes, and large salty cakes, सर्व-दोहः the entire yield of milk, च and, गृह्यताम् let it be collected

Anvaya: पायसादयः सूपान्ताः विविधाः पाकाः पच्यन्ताम्। संयावापूपशष्कुल्यः (पच्यन्ताम्), सर्वदोहः च गृह्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पायसादयः Sweet rice and other items, सूपान्ताः including various dals, विविधाः पाकाः various food preparations, पच्यन्ताम् should be cooked. संयावापूपशष्कुल्यः Wheat cakes, fried cakes, and large doughnuts (should be cooked), सर्वदोहः च and the entire stock of milk, गृह्यताम् should be gathered.

Simple meaning: Krishna describes the feast for the Govardhana Puja: "Let many different kinds of food be prepared, starting with sweet rice and including various dals and soups. Prepare special cakes like samyava, apupa, and shashkuli, and make sure the entire collection of milk from the cows is gathered for this occasion."

हूयन्तामग्नयः सम्यग् ब्राह्मणैर्ब्रह्मवादिभिः ।

अन्नं बहुविधं तेभ्यो देयं वो धेनुदक्षिणाः ॥10.24.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: हूयन्ताम् let them be offered oblations, अग्नयः the sacrificial fires, सम्यक् properly, ब्राह्मणैः by the brahmanas, ब्रह्म-वादिभिः who are learned in the Vedas, अन्नम् food, बहु-विधम् of many varieties, तेभ्यः to them, देयम् should be given, वः by you, धेनु-दक्षिणाः cows as gifts (dakshina)

Anvaya: ब्रह्मवादिभिः ब्राह्मणैः अग्नयः सम्यग् हूयन्ताम्; वः तेभ्यः बहुविधम् अन्नं धेनुदक्षिणाः च देयम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ब्रह्मवादिभिः ब्राह्मणैः By brahmanas learned in the Vedas, अग्नयः the sacrificial fires, सम्यग् हूयन्ताम् should be properly offered oblations; वः By you all, तेभ्यः to them, बहुविधम् अन्नं many varieties of food, धेनुदक्षिणाः च and cows as sacrificial gifts, देयम् should be given.

Simple meaning: Krishna continues the instructions for the festival: "Let the learned brahmanas properly offer oblations into the sacrificial fires. You should then feed these brahmanas a vast variety of delicious food and present them with cows as traditional gifts of gratitude (dakshina)."

अन्येभ्यश्चाश्वचाण्डालपतितेभ्यो यथार्हतः ।

यवसं च गवां दत्त्वा गिरये दीयतां बलिः ॥10.24.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्येभ्यः to others, च and, आ-श्व-चाण्डाल-पतितेभ्यः including the dogs, the outcaste candalas, and the fallen, यथा-अर्हतः according to their merit/appropriately, यवसम् grass, च and, गवाम् to the cows, दत्त्वा having given, गिरये unto the hill (Govardhana), दीयताम् let it be given, बलिः an offering

Anvaya: आश्वचाण्डालपतितेभ्यः अन्येभ्यः च यथार्हतः (अन्नं दत्त्वा), गवां यवसं च दत्त्वा, गिरये बलिः दीयताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आश्वचाण्डालपतितेभ्यः Including even the dogs, the outcaste candalas, and the fallen, अन्येभ्यः च and to all others, यथार्हतः appropriately (having given food), गवां to the cows, यवसं च and grass, दत्त्वा having given, गिरये बलिः दीयताम् unto the hill, an offering, should be given.

Simple meaning: Krishna emphasizes the inclusive nature of the festival: "Food should be distributed appropriately to everyone, including the dogs, the social outcasts, and even the most fallen souls. After providing fresh grass to the cows, a grand offering (bali) should be presented to Govardhana Hill."

स्वलङ्कृता भुक्तवन्तः स्वनुलिप्ताः सुवाससः ।

प्रदक्षिणं च कुरुत गोविप्रानलपर्वतान् ॥10.24.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: सु-अलङ्कृताः well-adorned, भुक्तवन्तः having eaten (the prasadam), सु-अनुलिप्ताः nicely smeared with sandalwood paste, सु-वाससः dressed in fine clothes, प्रदक्षिणम् circumambulation (clockwise), च and, कुरुत perform, गो-विप्र-अनल-पर्वतान् the cows, the brahmanas, the sacrificial fire, and the mountain

Anvaya: (यूयं) स्वलङ्कृताः भुक्तवन्तः स्वनुलिप्ताः सुवाससः (च भूत्वा) गोविप्रानलपर्वतान् प्रदक्षिणं कुरुत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (यूयं) You all, स्वलङ्कृताः being well-decorated, भुक्तवन्तः having eaten, स्वनुलिप्ताः being well-anointed with sandalwood, सुवाससः and wearing fine clothes, गोविप्रानलपर्वतान् around the cows, the brahmanas, the fire, and the mountain, प्रदक्षिणं कुरुत perform circumambulation.

Simple meaning: Krishna concludes the ritual instructions: "After you have all dressed in your finest clothes, adorned yourselves with jewelry, anointed your bodies with sandalwood paste, and partaken of the feast, you should then perform a clockwise circumambulation (pradakshina) around the cows, the brahmanas, the sacred fire, and Govardhana Hill."

एतन्मम मतं तात क्रियतां यदि रोचते ।

अयं गोब्राह्मणाद्रीणां मह्यं च दयितो मखः ॥10.24.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: एतत् this, मम My, मतम् opinion/desire, तात O dear father, क्रियताम् let it be done, यदि if, रोचते it is pleasing to you, अयम् this, गो-ब्राह्मण-अद्रीणाम् for the cows, the brahmanas, and the hill, मह्यम् to Me, च and, दयितः very dear, मखः sacrifice

Anvaya: तात! एतत् मम मतम् (अस्ति); यदि रोचते (तर्हि) क्रियताम्। अयं गोब्राह्मणाद्रीणां मखः मह्यं च दयितः (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तात O dear father, एतत् this, मम मतम् is My opinion. यदि रोचते If it pleases you, (तर्हि) then, क्रियताम् let it be done. अयं गोब्राह्मणाद्रीणां मखः This sacrifice for the cows, the brahmanas, and the mountain, मह्यं च and to Me, दयितः is very dear.

Simple meaning: Krishna concludes His proposal with grace and affection: "My dear father, this is My suggestion. If it seems right and pleasing to you, then please carry it out. This festival in honor of the cows, the brahmanas, and the mountain is very, very dear to Me."

श्री शुक उवाच

कालात्मना भगवता शक्रदर्पं जिघांसता ।

प्रोक्तं निशम्य नन्दाद्याः साध्वगृह्णन्त तद्वचः ॥10.24.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, काल-आत्मना who is the personification of Time, भगवता by the Supreme Lord, शक्र-दर्पम् the pride of Indra, जिघांसता desiring to destroy, प्रोक्तम् what was spoke, निशम्य having heard, नन्द-आद्याः Nanda and the others, साधु as excellent, अगृह्णन्त accepted, तत्-वचः those words

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच— शक्रदर्पं जिघांसता कालात्मना भगवता प्रोक्तं निशम्य नन्दाद्याः तत् वचः साधु अगृह्णन्त।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— शक्रदर्पं जिघांसता By the one who desired to destroy the pride of Indra, कालात्मना भगवता प्रोक्तं निशम्य having heard what was spoken by the Supreme Lord, who is the soul of Time, नन्दाद्याः Nanda and the others, तत् वचः साधु अगृह्णन्त accepted those words as excellent.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: "O King, Lord Krishna is the personification of eternal Time, and He spoke these words with the specific intent to shatter the pride of Indra. When Nanda Maharaja and the other elders of Vraja heard His brilliant proposal, they were completely convinced and accepted His words as the best course of action."

तथा च व्यदधुः सर्वं यथाऽऽह मधुसूदनः ।

वाचयित्वा स्वस्त्ययनं तद् द्रव्येण गिरिद्विजान् ॥10.24.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: तथा in that way, च and, व्यदधुः they performed, सर्वम् everything, यथा as, आह spoke, मधुसूदनः Lord Krishna (the killer of Madhu), वाचयित्वा having caused to be recited, स्वस्ति-अयनम् auspicious mantras/prayers, तत्-द्रव्येण with those very materials (collected for Indra), गिरि-द्विजान् the mountain and the brahmanas

Anvaya: मधुसूदनः यथा आह, तथा च (ते) सर्वं व्यदधुः। तत् द्रव्येण स्वस्त्ययनं वाचयित्वा गिरिद्विजान् (सम्पूजयामासुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मधुसूदनः As Krishna spoke, तथा च in that way, (ते) सर्वं व्यदधुः they performed everything. तत् द्रव्येण With those materials, स्वस्त्ययनं वाचयित्वा having caused auspicious hymns to be recited, गिरिद्विजान् (the residents of Vraja worshipped) the hill and the brahmanas.

Simple meaning: Following Krishna’s advice, Nanda and the cowherd men did exactly as He had suggested. Using all the materials they had originally gathered for Indra's sacrifice, they engaged the brahmanas in chanting auspicious Vedic hymns and performed the worship of Govardhana Hill and the priestly community.

उपहृत्य बलीन् सर्वानादृता यवसं गवाम् ।

गोधनानि पुरस्कृत्य गिरिं चक्रुः प्रदक्षिणम् ॥10.24.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: उपहृत्य having offered, बलीन् presentations/offerings, सर्वान् all, आदृताः with great respect, यवसम् grass, गवाम् to the cows, गो-धनानि the wealth of cows, पुरस्कृत्य placing in front, गिरिम् the hill, चक्रुः they performed, प्रदक्षिणम् circumambulation

Anvaya: (ते) सर्वान् बलीन् उपहृत्य, गवाम् आदृताः यवसं (दत्त्वा), गोधनानि पुरस्कृत्य गिरिं प्रदक्षिणं चक्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ते) They, सर्वान् बलीन् all the offerings, उपहृत्य having presented, गवाम् to the cows, आदृताः यवसं (दत्त्वा) with great respect having given grass, गोधनानि placing the wealth of cows, पुरस्कृत्य in front, गिरिं around the hill, प्रदक्षिणं चक्रुः performed circumambulation.

Simple meaning: After presenting all the food offerings and respectfully feeding fresh grass to the cows, the residents of Vraja placed their herds of cows in the front and began to circumambulate Govardhana Hill.

अनांस्यनडुद्युक्तानि ते चारुह्य स्वलङ्कृताः ।

गोप्यश्च कृष्णवीर्याणि गायन्त्यः सद्विजाशिषः ॥10.24.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: अनांसि wagons (carts), अनडुत्-युक्तानि yoked with bulls/oxen, ते they (the cowherds), च and, आरुह्य having mounted, स्व-अलङ्कृताः well-decorated, गोप्यः the cowherd women (gopis), च and, कृष्ण-वीर्याणि the heroic deeds of Krishna, गायन्त्यः singing, स-द्विज-आशिषः along with the blessings of the brahmanas

Anvaya: सद्विजाशिषः ते गोप्यः च स्वलङ्कृताः (सन्तः) अनडुद्युक्तानि अनांसि आरुह्य, कृष्णवीर्याणि गायन्त्यः (चक्रुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सद्विजाशिषः Taking the blessings of the Brahmanas, स्वलङ्कृताः beautifully adorned, ते गोप्यः च the gopis, अनडुद्युक्तानि अनांसि आरुह्य having climbed onto wagons yoked with oxen, कृष्णवीर्याणि the pastimes/prowess of Krishna, गायन्त्यः singing, (चक्रुः) (traveled).

Simple meaning: Taking the blessings of the brahmanas, the elegantly dressed gopis, mounted their bullock carts, riding in the wagons and singing the glories of Krishna’s heroic deeds, they went.

कृष्णस्त्वन्यतमं रूपं गोपविश्रम्भनं गतः ।

शैलोस्मीति ब्रुवन् भूरि बलिमादद् बृहद्वपुः ॥10.24.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णः Krishna, तु but, अन्यतमम् another, रूपम् form, गोप-विश्रम्भणम् to generate confidence in the cowherds, गतः assumed; शैलः अस्मि I am the mountain, इति thus, ब्रुवन् saying, भूरि massive, बलिम् offerings, आदत् ate/accepted, बृहत्-वपुः (having) a giant body

Anvaya: कृष्णः तु गोपविश्रम्भणं (कर्तुम्) अन्यतमं रूपं गतः, 'शैलः अस्मि' इति ब्रुवन् बृहद्वपुः (सन्) भूरि बलिम् आदत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णः तु Krishna, however, गोपविश्रम्भणं to instill faith in the cowherds, अन्यतमं रूपं गतः assumed another form; 'शैलः अस्मि' "I am the mountain," इति ब्रुवन् thus saying, बृहद्वपुः possessing a gigantic body, भूरि बलिम् the abundant offerings, आदत् ate.

Simple meaning: To instill complete faith in the cowherds, Lord Krishna assumed a second, gigantic form. This massive expansion of Himself stood atop the hill and declared, 'I am the mountain!' and consumed the vast quantities of food offered by the devotees.

तस्मै नमो व्रजजनैः सह चक्रेऽऽत्मनाऽऽत्मने ।

अहो पश्यत शैलोऽसौ रूपी नोऽनुग्रहं व्यधात् ॥10.24.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मै unto Him (the mountain-form), नमः obeisances, व्रज-जनैः सह along with the people of Vraja, चक्रे performed, आत्मना by Himself (the original Krishna), आत्मने unto Himself (the giant expansion); अहो oh!, पश्यत look!, शैलः mountain, असौ this, रूपी personified, नः unto us, अनुग्रहम् mercy/favor, व्यधात् has bestowed

Anvaya: (कृष्णः) आत्मना आत्मने तस्मै व्रजजनैः सह नमः चक्रे; (सः उवाच—) अहो पश्यत, असौ शैलः रूपी (सन्) नः अनुग्रहं व्यधात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (कृष्णः) Krishna, आत्मना through His original self, आत्मने unto His own (gigantic) self, तस्मै to Him, व्रजजनैः सह along with the people of Vraja, नमः चक्रे offered obeisances; (सः उवाच—) (He said—) अहो पश्यत Oh, look!, असौ शैलः this mountain, रूपी (सन्) being personified, नः unto us, अनुग्रहं mercy, व्यधात् has shown.

Simple meaning: In a beautiful display of His divine play, Krishna (in His original boy form) bowed down to His own gigantic expansion along with all the residents of Vraja. He then exclaimed to the cowherds, "Oh, look! This mountain has appeared in person to accept our offerings and has truly bestowed his mercy upon us!"

एषोऽवजानतो मर्त्यान् कामरूपी वनौकसः ।

हन्ति ह्यस्मै नमस्यामः शर्मणे आत्मनो गवाम् ॥10.24.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: एषः this (mountain), अवजानतः those who disrespect, मर्त्यान् mortals, काम-रूपी assuming any form at will, वन-ओकसः the forest-dwellers, हन्ति kills, हि indeed, अस्मै unto him, नमस्यामः let us offer obeisances, शर्मणे for the welfare, आत्मनः of ourselves, गवाम् (and) of the cows

Anvaya: एषः कामरूपी (शैलः) अवजानतः मर्त्यान् हन्ति; (सः) वनौकसः (अपि हन्ति)। हि अस्मै आत्मनः गवां च शर्मणे नमस्यामः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एषः कामरूपी (शैलः) This mountain who can take any form at will, अवजानतः those who disrespect (him), मर्त्यान् mortals, हन्ति kills; (सः) He (the mountain), वनौकसः (अपि हन्ति) the forest-dwellers (who offend him) (also kills). हि Indeed, अस्मै unto him, आत्मनः गवां च of ourselves and of the cows, शर्मणे for the welfare, नमस्यामः let us bow down.

Simple meaning: Krishna warns the cowherds to show proper respect: "This mountain-god can assume any form he desires and can destroy those mortals who disrespect him. Even we forest-dwellers must be careful. Therefore, for our own well-being and for the protection of our cows, let us offer our humble obeisances to him."

इत्यद्रिगोद्विजमखं वासुदेवप्रणोदिताः ।

यथा विधाय ते गोपाः सहकृष्णा व्रजं ययुः ॥10.24.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, अद्रि-गो-द्विज-मखम् the sacrifice to the mountain, cows, and brahmanas, वासुदेव-प्रणोदिताः inspired/directed by Krishna (Vasudeva), यथा as, विधाय having performed, ते those, गोपाः cowherds, सह-कृष्णाः along with Krishna, व्रजम् to Vraja (their settlement), ययुः went

Anvaya: वासुदेवप्रणोदिताः ते गोपाः इति अद्रिगोद्विजमखं यथा (निर्दिष्टं तथा) विधाय सहकृष्णाः व्रजं ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वासुदेवप्रणोदिताः Directed by Vasudeva (Krishna), ते गोपाः those cowherds, इति thus, अद्रिगोद्विजमखं the sacrifice to the hill, cows, and brahmanas, यथा as (directed), विधाय having performed, सहकृष्णाः together with Krishna, व्रजं ययुः went back to Vraja.

Simple meaning: Having been inspired and directed by Lord Krishna, the cowherds completed the sacrifice to the hill, the cows, and the brahmanas exactly as prescribed. Then, accompanied by Krishna, they all returned to their homes in Vraja.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे गोवर्धन पूजा नाम​ चतुर्विंशः अध्यायः ॥24॥

Thus ends the twenty-fourth discourse named “The Lord stops the sacrifice to Indra” in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.

Insights on the Verse 24.5

अस्त्यस्वपरदृष्टीनाममित्रोदास्तविद्विषाम् ।

उदासीनोऽरिवद् वर्ज्य आत्मवत् सुहृदुच्यते ॥10.24.5॥

This verse presents an interesting philosophical insight into human relationships and the way we perceive others. Krishna is essentially making a distinction between different attitudes toward relationships and guiding us toward an ideal vision.

Deeper Understanding:

  1. Those Who See No Distinction Between Self and Others

    • The first part of the verse describes a person who does not distinguish between “one’s own” (sva) and “another’s” (para).

    • Such a person does not categorize people as friends (amitra), neutral (udasina), or enemies (vidvisha)—they see everyone as equal.

    • This reflects the non-dual (advaitic) or spiritually elevated vision, where one recognizes the Supreme Self (Paramatma) in all beings.

  2. The Danger of Neutrality

    • Krishna warns that a neutral person (udasinah) should be avoided like an enemy (arivat varjya).

    • Why? Because neutrality often implies apathy and detachment from righteousness.

    • In the Mahabharata, Krishna repeatedly tells Arjuna that not taking a side in Dharma is as bad as siding with Adharma.

    • A person who remains indifferent to good and evil, justice and injustice, cannot be trusted in times of need.

  3. The Definition of a True Friend

    • A suhrt (true well-wisher) is someone who treats others as their own self (atma-vat).

    • Such a person does not differentiate between people based on external labels but cares deeply for the well-being of others.

    • Unlike the indifferent (udsinah), a true friend actively supports and uplifts.

Spiritual Message:

  • Krishna is urging us to rise above superficial classifications and see unity among all beings.

  • However, he also warns against the danger of detached neutrality in matters of Dharma.

  • Instead of remaining indifferent, one should be a true well-wisher (suhrt) who actively cares for others, just as one cares for oneself.

This aligns with Bhakti Yoga’s core principle—seeing all as Krishna’s own, yet actively engaging in Dharma with love and responsibility.
