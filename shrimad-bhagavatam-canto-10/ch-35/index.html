Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 35

The Gopis' song in pairs of verses

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

वृन्दावनक्रीडायां गोपिकायुगलगीतं नाम पञ्चत्रिंशः अध्यायः

(26 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

गोप्यः कृष्णे वनं याते तमनुद्रुतचेतसः ।

कृष्णलीलाः प्रगायन्त्यो निन्युर्दुःखेन वासरान् ॥10.35.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, गोप्यः the gopis, कृष्णे when Krishna, वनम् to the forest, याते had gone, तम्-अनुद्रुत-चेतसः their minds having followed Him, कृष्ण-लीलाः the pastimes of Krishna, प्रगायन्त्यः loudly singing, निन्युः they passed, दुःखेन with great sorrow, वासरान् the days

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — कृष्णे वनं याते (सति) तमनुद्रुतचेतसः गोप्यः कृष्णलीलाः प्रगायन्त्यो दुःखेन वासरान् निन्युः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, कृष्णे वनं याते when Krishna had gone to the forest, तमनुद्रुतचेतसः their hearts having followed Him, गोप्यः the gopis, कृष्णलीलाः प्रगायन्त्यः singing the pastimes of Krishna, दुःखेन वासरान् निन्युः passed their days with sadness.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: When Krishna had departed for the forest, the gopis, whose minds and hearts had followed Him, spent their days in great sorrow, singing loudly of His divine pastimes.

Note: From the following verses, the meaning of two verses together complete the meaning.

गोप्यः ऊचुः

वामबाहुकृतवामकपोलो वल्गितभ्रुरधरार्पितवेणुम् ।

कोमलाङ्गुलिभिराश्रितमार्गं गोप्य ईरयति यत्र मुकुन्दः ॥10.35.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः ऊचुः the gopis said, वाम-बाहु-कृत-वाम-कपोलः resting His left cheek on His left arm, वल्गित-भ्रुः with moving eyebrows, अधर-अर्पित-वेणुम् with the flute placed upon His lips, कोमल-अङ्गुलिभिः with His delicate fingers, आश्रित-मार्गम् following the holes of the flute, गोप्यः O gopis, ईरयति plays/vibrates, यत्र where, मुकुन्दः Mukunda (Krishna)

Anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः — हे गोप्यः! यत्र वामबाहुकृतवामकपोलः वल्गितभ्रुः मुकुन्दः अधरार्पितवेणुं कोमलाङ्गुलिभिः आश्रितमार्गम् ईरयति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः The gopis said, हे गोप्यः O gopis, यत्र where, वामबाहुकृतवामकपोलः having placed His left cheek on His left arm, वल्गितभ्रुः with moving eyebrows, मुकुन्दः Mukunda, अधरार्पितवेणुं with the flute placed on His lips, कोमलाङ्गुलिभिः with His tender fingers, आश्रितमार्गं covering the holes of the flute, ईरयति vibrates the flute.

Simple Meaning: The gopis said: O gopis, when Mukunda rests His left cheek on His left arm, moving His eyebrows while placing the flute to His lips, He vibrates the instrument with His delicate fingers covering its holes to create various melodies.

व्योमयानवनिताः सह सिद्धैर्विस्मितास्तदुपधार्य सलज्जाः ।

काममार्गणसमर्पितचित्ताः कश्मलं ययुरपस्मृतनीव्यः ॥10.35.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्योम-यान-वनिताः the women traveling in airplanes, सह with, सिद्धैः the Siddha demigods, विस्मिताः amazed, तत्-उपधार्य hearing that (music), स-लज्जाः becoming embarrassed, काम-मार्गण-समर्पित-चित्ताः their hearts being surrendered to the arrows of Cupid, कश्मलम् ययुः they became bewildered/fainted, अपस्मृत-नीव्यः forgetting that their belts had loosened

Anvaya: व्योमयानवनिताः सिद्धैः सह तत् उपधार्य विस्मिताः सलज्जाः (च सत्यः) काममार्गणसमर्पितचित्ताः अपस्मृतनीव्यः (सत्यः) कश्मलं ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: व्योमयानवनिताः The women in the celestial airplanes, सिद्धैः सह along with the Siddhas, तत् उपधार्य hearing that (flute music), विस्मिताः were amazed, सलज्जाः and felt bashful, काममार्गणसमर्पितचित्ताः their hearts were offered to the arrows of Kamadeva, अपस्मृतनीव्यः becoming unaware of their loosened garments, कश्मलं ययुः they fell into a state of bewilderment.

Simple Meaning: When the celestial women traveling in airplanes with the Siddhas heard that flute music, they were struck with wonder. Their hearts were so overcome by the arrows of Cupid that they became embarrassed and fainted in a daze, not even noticing that the knots of their garments had come loose.

हन्त चित्रमबलाः शृणुतेदं हारहास उरसि स्थिरविद्युत् ।

नन्दसूनुरयमार्तजनानां नर्मदो यर्हि कूजितवेणूः ॥10.35.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: हन्त oh!, चित्रम् wonderful, अबलाः O women/gopis, शृणुत hear, इदम् this, हार-हासः whose smile is like a pearl necklace, उरसि on His chest, स्थिर-विद्युत् constant lightning (the Srivatsa mark or Goddess Lakshmi), नन्द-सूनुः the son of Nanda, अयम् this, आर्त-जनानाम् for the distressed people, नर्म-दः giving pleasure, यर्हि when, कूजित-वेणुः vibrates His flute

Anvaya: हन्त अबलाः! इदं चित्रं शृणुत—यर्हि हारहासः उरसि स्थिरविद्युत् अयं नन्दसूनुः कूजितवेणुः (सन्) आर्तजनानां नर्मदः (भवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हन्त अबलाः Oh gopis!, इदं चित्रं शृणुत hear this wonderful thing—यर्हि when, हारहासः He whose smile is like a string of pearls, उरसि स्थिरविद्युत् with steady lightning on His chest, अयं नन्दसूनुः this son of Nanda, कूजितवेणुः vibrating His flute, आर्तजनानां नर्मदः becomes the giver of joy to distressed people.

Simple Meaning: Oh gopis, just listen to this wonderful thing! When this son of Nanda, whose smile shines like a necklace of pearls and whose chest bears the mark of Goddess Lakshmi like a stationary flash of lightning, vibrates His flute, He brings immense joy to all those who are distressed.

वृन्दशो व्रजवृषा मृगगावो वेणुवाद्यहृतचेतस आरात् ।

दन्तदष्टकवला धृतकर्णा निद्रिता लिखितचित्रमिवासन् ॥10.35.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: वृन्दशः in groups, व्रज-वृषाः the bulls of Vraja, मृग-गावः the deer and the cows, वेणु-वाद्य-हृत-चेतसः their minds stolen by the playing of the flute, आरात् from a distance, दन्त-दष्ट-कवलाः with mouthfuls of grass gripped between their teeth, धृत-कर्णाः with ears pricked up, निद्रिताः asleep, लिखित-चित्रम् इव like a painted picture, आसन् became

Anvaya: वेणुवाद्यहृतचेतसः वृन्दशः व्रजवृषाः मृगगावः आरात् (एव) दन्तदष्टकवलाः धृतकर्णाः निद्रिताः लिखितचित्रम् इव आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वेणुवाद्यहृतचेतसः Their hearts being captivated by the sound of the flute, वृन्दशः in groups, व्रजवृषाः the bulls of Vraja, मृगगावः the deer and the cows, आरात् even from a distance, दन्तदष्टकवलाः holding mouthfuls of grass in their teeth, धृतकर्णाः with their ears raised, निद्रिताः as if asleep, लिखितचित्रम् इव आसन् appeared like a painted picture.

Simple Meaning: When the groups of bulls, deer, and cows of Vraja hear the sound of the flute from a distance, their minds are completely captivated. They stand still with grass remaining in their mouths and their ears pricked up; they appear as if they are asleep or like figures in a motionless painting.

बर्हिणस्तबकधातुपलाशैर्बद्धमल्लपरिबर्हविडम्बः ।

कर्हिचित् सबल आलि स गोपैर्गाः समाह्वयति यत्र मुकुन्दः ॥10.35.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: बर्हिण-स्तबक-धातु-पलाशैः with peacock feathers, flower bouquets, colored minerals, and young leaves, बद्ध-मल्ल-परिबर्ह-विडम्बः appearing like a wrestler dressed in colorful garments, कर्हिचित् sometimes, स-बलः along with Balarama, आलि O friend, सः He, गोपैः with the cowherd boys, गाः the cows, समाह्वयति calls out, यत्र when, मुकुन्दः Mukunda

Anvaya: आलि! यत्र बर्हिणस्तबकधातुपलाशैः बद्धमल्लपरिबर्हविडम्बः सः मुकुन्दः सबलः गोपैः (सह) गाः समाह्वयति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आलि O friend, यत्र when, बर्हिणस्तबकधातुपलाशैः with peacock feathers, bouquets, minerals, and leaves, बद्धमल्लपरिबर्हविडम्बः dressed like a prize wrestler, सः मुकुन्दः that Mukunda, सबलः with Balarama, गोपैः with the cowherd boys, गाः the cows, समाह्वयति calls out.

Simple Meaning: O friend, when Mukunda, decorated with peacock feathers, flower bouquets, colorful minerals, and fresh leaves, appears just like a dressed-up wrestler, He wanders with Balarama and the other cowherd boys, calling out to the cows.

तर्हि भग्नगतयः सरितो वै तत्पादाम्बुजरजोऽनिलनीतम् ।

स्पृहयतीर्वयमिवाबहुपुण्याः प्रेमवेपितभुजाः स्तिमितापः ॥10.35.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: तर्हि then, भग्न-गतयः their flow being broken/arrested, सरितः the rivers, वै indeed, तत्-पाद-अम्बुज-रजः the dust of His lotus feet, अनिल-नीतम् carried by the wind, स्पृहयतीः desiring, वयम् इव like us, अबहु-पुण्याः without much pious merit, प्रेम-वेपित-भुजाः whose arms (waves) trembling due to love, स्तिमित-आपः (become) with their water stilled

Anvaya: तर्हि भग्नगतयः सरितः वै अनिलनीतं तत्पादाम्बुजरजः स्पृहयतीः अबहुपुण्याः वयम् इव प्रेमवेपितभुजाः स्तिमितापः (भवन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तर्हि Then, भग्नगतयः their movement stopped, सरितः the rivers, वै indeed, अनिलनीतं carried by the breeze, तत्पादाम्बुजरजः the dust of His lotus feet, स्पृहयतीः desiring, अबहुपुण्याः lacking sufficient piety, वयम् इव like us, प्रेमवेपितभुजाः (the rivers with waves -) arms trembling with love, स्तिमितापः become motionless with their waters still.

Simple Meaning: At that time, the flow of the rivers is interrupted as they stop to listen. The rivers long for the dust of His lotus feet carried by the wind. But being like us gopis who lack sufficient merit to stay with Him, those rivers whose waves were trembling, become motionless with their water still because of the inability to obtain the dust of Lord’s feet.

अनुचरैः समनुवर्णितवीर्यं आदिपूरुष इवाचलभूतिः ।

वनचरो गिरितटेषु चरन्तीर्वेणुनाऽऽह्वयति गाः स यदा हि ॥10.35.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: अनुचरैः by His followers, समनुवर्णित-वीर्यम् whose glories are being fully described, आदि-पूरुषः इव like the Original Personality (Narayana), अचल-भूतिः whose opulence is eternal/unmoving, वन-चरः wandering in the forest, गिरि-तटेषु on the mountain slopes, चरन्तीः grazing, वेणुना with His flute, आह्वयति calls, गाः the cows, सः He, यदा when, हि indeed

Anvaya: यदा हि अचलभूतिः आदिपूरुषः इव अनुचरैः समनुवर्णितवीर्यः वनचरः सः गिरितटेषु चरन्तीः गाः वेणुना आह्वयति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यदा हि When indeed, अचलभूतिः with eternal opulence, आदिपूरुषः इव like the Original Lord, अनुचरैः समनुवर्णितवीर्यः His prowess being praised by His companions, वनचरः wandering in the forest, सः He, गिरितटेषु चरन्तीः grazing on the hillsides, गाः the cows, वेणुना आह्वयति calls by His flute.

Simple Meaning: When Krishna wanders through the forest, His companions follow Him chanting His glories. With His eternal opulence, He appears like the Original Lord Himself. As He calls the cows grazing on the mountain slopes by playing His flute…

वनलतास्तरव आत्मनि विष्णुं व्यञ्जयन्त्य इव पुष्पफलाढ्याः ।

प्रणतभारविटपा मधुधाराः प्रेमहृष्टतनवः ससृजुः स्म ॥10.35.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: वन-लताः the forest creepers, तरवः and the trees, आत्मनि in themselves, विष्णुम् Lord Vishnu, व्यञ्जयन्त्यः manifesting, इव as if, पुष्प-फल-आढ्याः rich with flowers and fruits, प्रणत-भार-विटपाः their branches bowed down by the weight, मधु-धाराः streams of honey (or sweet sap), प्रेम-हृष्ट-तनवः their bodies (trunks/vines) erupting with joy out of love, ससृजुः स्म poured forth

Anvaya: पुष्पफलाढ्याः वनलताः तरवः च आत्मनि विष्णुं व्यञ्जयन्त्यः इव प्रणतभारविटपाः (सत्यः) प्रेमहृष्टतनवः मधुधाराः ससृजुः स्म।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पुष्पफलाढ्याः Abundant with flowers and fruits, वनलताः तरवः च the forest creepers and trees, आत्मनि विष्णुं व्यञ्जयन्त्यः इव as if revealing Lord Vishnu within themselves, प्रणतभारविटपाः with branches bent low under the weight, प्रेमहृष्टतनवः their bodies standing on end in ecstasy, मधुधाराः ससृजुः स्म poured out streams of honey.

Simple Meaning: The forest creepers and trees, loaded with an abundance of fruits and flowers, appeared to manifest the Presence of Lord Vishnu within their hearts. Their branches bowed low in prostration, and as if their bodies were erupting with hair-standing ecstasy out of love, they poured forth torrential streams of sweet honey.

दर्शनीयतिलको वनमालादिव्यगन्धतुलसीमधुमत्तैः ।

अलिकुलैरलघुगीतमभीष्टमाद्रियन् यर्हि सन्धितवेणुः ॥10.35.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: दर्शनीय-तिलकः wearing a beautiful tilaka, वन-माला-दिव्य-गन्ध-तुलसी-मधु-मत्तैः intoxicated by the honey of the Tulasi flowers in His divine forest garland, अलि-कुलैः by groups of bees, अलघु-गीत-अभीष्टम् the loud and desired singing, आद्रियन् honoring/welcoming, यर्हि when, सन्धित-वेणुः placing the flute to His lips

Anvaya: यर्हि दर्शनीयतिलकः (सः) वनमालादिव्यगन्धतुलसीमधुमत्तैः अलिकुलैः अभीष्टम् अलघुगीतम् आद्रियन् सन्धितवेणुः (भवति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यर्हि When, दर्शनीयतिलकः He who wears a beautiful tilaka, वनमालादिव्यगन्धतुलसीमधुमत्तैः by the bees intoxicated by the honey of the Tulasi in His forest garland, अभीष्टम् अलघुगीतम् the desired loud singing, आद्रियन् acknowledging with respect, सन्धितवेणुः placing the flute to His lips.

Simple Meaning: When Krishna, adorned with a beautiful tilaka, places His flute to His lips, He graciously acknowledges the loud, buzzing song of the bees. These bees, intoxicated by the sweet honey of the Tulasi flowers in His divine forest garland, follow Him everywhere, singing His praises.

सरसि सारहंसविहङ्गाश्चारुगीतहृतचेतस एत्य ।

हरिमुपासत ते यतचित्ता हन्त मीलितदृशो धृतमौनाः ॥10.35.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: सरसि in the lake, सार-हंस-विहङ्गाः the cranes, swans, and other birds, चारु-गीत-हृत-चेतसः their minds stolen by the sweet song, एत्य coming near, हरिम् Lord Hari (Krishna), उपासत they worship, ते they, यत-चित्ताः with controlled minds, हन्त oh!, मीलित-दृशः with closed eyes, धृत-मौनाः maintaining silence

Anvaya: हन्त! सरसि सारहंसविहङ्गाः चारुगीतहृतचेतसः एत्य यतचित्ताः मीलितदृशः धृतमौनाः (सन्तः) ते हरिम् उपासत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हन्त Oh!, सरसि in the lake, सारहंसविहङ्गाः the cranes, swans, and birds, चारुगीतहृतचेतसः their hearts captivated by the beautiful song, एत्य having approached, यतचित्ताः with their minds fixed, मीलितदृशः with eyes closed, धृतमौनाः observing silence, ते they, हरिम् उपासत worshiped Hari.

Simple Meaning: Oh, look! The swans, cranes, and other birds in the lake have had their minds completely stolen by the beautiful song of the flute. They have approached Krishna and, with their minds fixed and eyes closed in meditation, they are worshiping Him in absolute silence.

सहबलः स्रगवतंसविलासः सानुषु क्षितिभृतो व्रजदेव्यः ।

हर्षयन् यर्हि वेणुरवेण जातहर्ष उपरम्भति विश्वम् ॥10.35.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: सह-बलः with Balarama, स्रग्-अवतंस-विलासः appearing beautiful with a flower garland and earrings, सानुषु on the peaks, क्षिति-भृतः of the mountain (Govardhana), व्रज-देव्यः O goddesses of Vraja, हर्षयन् giving joy, यर्हि when, वेणु-रवेण by the sound of the flute, जात-हर्षः becoming joyful, उपरम्भति He enlivens/embraces, विश्वम् the universe

Anvaya: व्रजदेव्यः! यर्हि सानुषु क्षितिभृतः सहबलः स्रगवतंसविलासः जातहर्षः (सः) वेणुरवेण विश्वं हर्षयन् उपरम्भति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: व्रजदेव्यः O goddesses of Vraja, यर्हि when, क्षितिभृतः सानुषु standing on the mountain peaks, सहबलः along with Balarama, स्रगवतंसविलासः looking beautiful with His flower garland and ornaments, जातहर्षः being filled with joy, वेणुरवेण विश्वं हर्षयन् gladdening the entire universe with the sound of His flute, उपरम्भति He vibrates His flute/enlivens all.

Simple Meaning: O goddesses of Vraja, when Krishna, stands upon the peaks of the mountain, accompanied by Balarama and looking magnificent with His flower garlands and ornaments, He becomes filled with joy. By vibrating His flute, He enlivens the entire universe and fills every living being with transcendental pleasure.

महदतिक्रमणशङ्कितचेता मन्दमन्दमनुगर्जति मेघः ।

सुहृदमभ्यवर्षत् सुमनोभिश्छायया च विदधत् प्रतपत्रम् ॥10.35.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: महत्-अतिक्रमण-शङ्कित-चेताः his mind afraid of committing a great offense, मन्द-मन्दम् very softly, अनुगर्जति thunders in response, मेघः the cloud, सुहृदम् his friend (Krishna), अभ्यवर्षत् rained upon, सुमनोभिः with flowers (droplets of water), छायया with his shadow, च and, विदधत् acting as, प्रतपत्रम् an umbrella

Anvaya: महदतिक्रमणशङ्कितचेताः मेघः मन्दमन्दम् अनुगर्जति, सुमनोभिः सुहृदम् अभ्यवर्षत्, छायया च प्रतपत्रं विदधत् (अतिष्ठत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महदतिक्रमणशङ्कितचेताः His heart fearful of committing a great transgression, मेघः the cloud, मन्दमन्दम् अनुगर्जति thunders very gently, सुमनोभिः सुहृदम् अभ्यवर्षत् showered his friend with flowers, छायया च and with his shadow, प्रतपत्रं विदधत् acting as an umbrella.

Simple Meaning: Fearful of committing an offense against a great personality, the cloud thunders very softly in response to the flute. This cloud, acting as a friend to Krishna, showers Him with fine droplets like flowers and spreads his own shadow over Him to serve as an umbrella against the sun.

विविधगोपचरणेषु विदग्धो वेणुवाद्य उरुधा निजशिक्षाः ।

तव सुतः सति यदाधरबिम्बे दत्तवेणुरनयत् स्वरजातीः ॥10.35.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: विविध-गोप-चरणेषु in the various styles of cowherd pastimes, विदग्धः expert, वेणु-वाद्ये in playing the flute, उरुधा in many ways, निज-शिक्षाः His own instructions/lessons, तव your, सुतः son, सति O pious lady (Yashoda), यदा when, अधर-बिम्बे upon His bimba-red lips, दत्त-वेणुः placing the flute, अनयत् produced/evolved, स्वर-जातीः the various categories of musical notes

Anvaya: सति! यदा विविधगोपचरणेषु वेणुवाद्ये च विदग्धः तव सुतः अधरबिम्बे दत्तवेणुः (सन्) उरुधा निजशिक्षाः स्वरजातीः अनयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सति O pious lady, यदा when, विविधगोपचरणेषु in various cowherd activities, वेणुवाद्ये च and in flute playing, विदग्धः expert, तव सुतः your son, अधरबिम्बे दत्तवेणुः placing the flute on His red lips, उरुधा निजशिक्षाः His many learned lessons, स्वरजातीः अनयत् produced the scales of musical notes.

Simple Meaning: O pious Yashoda, your son is an expert in the various pastimes of the cowherds and in the art of playing the flute. When He places the flute to His bimba-red lips, He plays many unique and self-taught musical arrangements, producing a vast range of harmonic scales and melodies.

सवनशस्तदुपधार्य सुरेशाः शक्रशर्वपरमेष्टिपुरोगाः ।

कवय आनतकन्धरचित्ताः कश्मलं ययुरनिश्चिततत्त्वाः ॥10.35.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: सवनशः with a variety of low, high and middle pitches, तत्-उपधार्य hearing that (flute music), सुर-ईशाः the chief demigods, शक्र-शर्व-परमेष्टि-पुरोगाः headed by Indra, Shiva, and Brahma, कवयः the great scholars/sages, आनत-कन्धर-चित्ताः their necks and minds bowed down, कश्मलम् ययुः they became bewildered, अनिश्चित-तत्त्वाः unable to ascertain the essence of the music

Anvaya: सवनशः तत् उपधार्य शक्रशर्वपरमेष्टिपुरोगाः सुरेशाः कवयः च आनतकन्धरचित्ताः अनिश्चिततत्त्वाः (सन्तः) कश्मलं ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सवनशः With a variety of low, high and middle pitches, तत् उपधार्य hearing that music, शक्रशर्वपरमेष्टिपुरोगाः सुरेशाः the lords of the demigods headed by Indra, Shiva, and Brahma, कवयः च and the wise sages, आनतकन्धरचित्ताः with their necks and hearts bowed, अनिश्चिततत्त्वाः unable to grasp the underlying truth of the sound, कश्मलं ययुः fell into a state of stunned bewilderment.

Simple Meaning: When the master demigods, including Indra, Shiva, and Brahma, along with the great sages, hear that music with a variety of low, high and middle pitches, they bow their heads and hearts in deep concentration. Even these great thinkers become completely bewildered, unable to fathom the complex technicalities or the divine essence of the melodies Krishna creates.

निजपदाब्जदलैर्ध्वजवज्रनीरजाङ्कुशविचित्रललामैः ।

व्रजभुवः शमयन् खुरतोदं वर्ष्मधुर्यगतिरीडितवेणुः ॥10.35.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: निज-पद-अब्ज-दलैः with the soles of His lotus feet, ध्वज-वज्र-नीरज-अङ्कुश-विचित्र-ललामैः marked with the wonderful symbols of the flag, thunderbolt, lotus, and elephant goad, व्रज-भुवः of the land of Vraja, शमयन् relieving, खुर-तोदम् the pain caused by the hooves of the cows, वर्ष्म-धुर्य-गतिः having a graceful gait like a leading elephant, ईडित-वेणुः His flute being glorified

Anvaya: वर्ष्मधुर्यगतिः ईडितवेणुः (सः) ध्वजवज्रनीरजाङ्कुशविचित्रललामैः निजपदाब्जदलैः व्रजभुवः खुरतोदं शमयन् (गच्छति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वर्ष्मधुर्यगतिः Moving with the graceful gait of a majestic elephant, ईडितवेणुः His flute playing being praised, ध्वजवज्रनीरजाङ्कुशविचित्रललामैः with the wonderful marks of the flag, thunderbolt, lotus, and elephant goad, निजपदाब्जदलैः with the soles of His own lotus feet, व्रजभुवः खुरतोदं शमयन् relieving the pain of the earth of Vraja caused by the hooves.

Simple Meaning: Walking with the graceful, majestic gait of an elephant and playing His celebrated flute, Krishna relieves the pain the earth of Vraja suffers from the hooves of the cows. He does this by marking the ground with the soles of His lotus feet, which bear the wonderful symbols of the flag, thunderbolt, lotus, and elephant goad.

व्रजति तेन वयं सविलासवीक्षणार्पितमनोभववेगाः ।

कुजगतिं गमिता न विदामः कश्मलेन कबरं वसनं वा ॥10.35.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्रजति while He walks, तेन by that (gait), वयम् we, स-विलास-वीक्षण-अर्पित-मनोभव-वेगाः having the impulses of Cupid triggered by His playful glances, कुज-गतिम् the state of motionless trees, गमिताः brought to, न विदामः we do not know, कश्मलेन due to our bewilderment, कबरम् our hair, वसनम् our clothing, वा or

Anvaya: तेन (तस्य गत्या) व्रजति (सति) सविलासवीक्षणार्पितमनोभववेगाः वयं कुजगतिं गमिताः कश्मलेन कबरं वसनं वा न विदामः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तेन व्रजति When He walks with that gait, सविलासवीक्षणार्पितमनोभववेगाः our desires being aroused by His playful glances, वयं we, कुजगतिं गमिताः were reduced to the state of trees, कश्मलेन due to our stunned state, कबरं वसनं वा न विदामः do not notice our hair or our garments.

Simple Meaning: When Krishna walks with such a playful gait, His charming glances ignite the fires of love within our hearts. We become so stunned that we stand motionless like trees, completely unaware in our bewilderment whether our hair has come loose or our clothes have slipped.

मणिधरः क्वचिदागणयन् गा मालया दयितगन्धतुलस्याः ।

प्रणयिनोऽनुचरस्य कदांसे प्रक्षिपन् भुजमगायत यत्र ॥10.35.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: मणि-धरः wearing a necklace of jewels, क्वचित् sometimes, आगणयन् counting, गाः the cows, मालया with a garland, दयित-गन्ध-तुलस्याः of favorite scented Tulasi, प्रणयिनः affectionate, अनुचरस्य of a companion/follower, कदा-अंसे on the shoulder, प्रक्षिपन् throwing, भुजम् His arm, अगायत He sang, यत्र when

Anvaya: यत्र दयितगन्धतुलस्याः मालया (युक्तः) मणिधरः गाः आगणयन् कदा (चित्) प्रणयिनः अनुचरस्य अंसे भुजं प्रक्षिपन् अगायत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र When, दयितगन्धतुलस्याः मालया with a garland of dearly fragrant Tulasi, मणिधरः wearing a jeweled necklace, गाः आगणयन् counting the cows, कदाचित् sometimes, प्रणयिनः अनुचरस्य अंसे on the shoulder of a loving companion, भुजं प्रक्षिपन् resting His arm, अगायत He sang.

Simple Meaning: Sometimes, wearing a necklace of jewels and a garland of fragrant Tulasi that He loves dearly, Krishna counts His cows. At that time, He rests His arm on the shoulder of an affectionate companion and begins to sing.

क्वणितवेणुरववञ्चितचित्ताः कृष्णमन्वसत कृष्णगृहिण्यः ।

गुणगणार्णमनुगत्य हरिण्यो गोपिका इव विमुक्तगृहाशाः ॥10.35.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्वणित-वेणु-रव-वञ्चित-चित्ताः their minds stolen by the ringing sound of the flute, कृष्णम् Krishna, अन्वसत sat near/followed, कृष्ण-गृहिण्यः the black-skinned does (wives of the black deer), गुण-गण-आर्णम् the ocean of transcendental qualities, अनुगत्य having followed, हरिण्यः the female deer, गोपिकाः इव like the gopis, विमुक्त-गृह-आशाः having given up all attachment for home

Anvaya: (तत्र) क्वणितवेणुरववञ्चितचित्ताः कृष्णगृहिण्यः हरिण्यः गुणगणार्णं कृष्णम् अनुगत्य विमुक्तगृहाशाः गोपिकाः इव अन्वसत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (तत्र) In that place, क्वणितवेणुरववञ्चितचित्ताः their hearts stolen by the ringing vibration of the flute, कृष्णगृहिण्यः हरिण्यः the black-skinned does (wives of the deer), गुणगणार्णं कृष्णम् अनुगत्य having followed Krishna—who is an ocean of unlimited qualities, विमुक्तगृहाशाः गोपिकाः इव just like the gopis who have abandoned all attachment to their homes, अन्वसत remained by His side.

Simple Meaning: When the black deer (the wives of the Krishna-sara deer) hear the ringing sound of the flute, their hearts are completely captured. They approach Krishna, the ocean of all virtues, and sit beside Him. Just like us gopis, who have cast aside all thoughts of home and hearth to be with Him, these female-deer are so entranced that they simply follow Him and remain in His divine company.

कुन्ददामकृतकौतुकवेषो गोपगोधनवृतो यमुनायाम् ।

नन्दसूनुरनघे तव वत्सो नर्मदः प्रणयिनां विजहार ॥10.35.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: कुन्द-दाम-कृत-कौतुक-वेषः whose festive dress is decorated with garlands of kunda flowers, गोप-गोधन-वृतः surrounded by the cowherd boys and the wealth of cows, यमुनायाम् by the Yamuna river, नन्द-सूनुः the son of Nanda, अनघे O sinless lady (Yashoda), तव your, वत्सः darling child, नर्म-दः source of pleasure/playfulness, प्रणयिनाम् for His dear friends, विजहार performed pastimes

Anvaya: अनघे! कुन्ददामकृतकौतुकवेषः गोपगोधनवृतः तव वत्सः नन्दसूनुः यमुनायां प्रणयिनां नर्मदः (सन्) विजहार।

O sinless lady! कुन्ददामकृतकौतुकवेषः Decorated in festive attire with jasmine garlands, गोपगोधनवृतः surrounded by the cowherd boys and the cows, तव वत्सः नन्दसूनुः your darling son, the son of Nanda, यमुनायां प्रणयिनां नर्मदः (सन्) विजहार performed pastimes by the Yamuna, bringing great joy to His loving companions.

Simple Meaning: O sinless Yashoda, your darling son has dressed Himself in a festive way with beautiful garlands of jasmine. Surrounded by His cows and cowherd friends, He wanders along the banks of the Yamuna, sporting playfully and bringing immense delight to everyone who loves Him.

मन्दवायुरुपवात्यनुकूलं मानयन् मलयजस्पर्शेन ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मलयजस्पर्शेन मानयन् Honoring Him with the cool touch of sandalwood, अनुकूलं मन्दवायुः उपवाति a gentle, favorable breeze blows; ये उपदेवगणाः वन्दिनः and those groups of demigods who act as His bards, ते वाद्यगीतबलिभिः तं परिवव्रुः have surrounded Him with offerings of music and song.

Meanwhile, the Gandharvas and other celestial musicians act like court bards, surrounding Krishna and offering Him their finest music and songs as a tribute to His divinity.

As He returns, the eminent and distinguished people worship His lotus feet on the path. He plays His flute while His companions follow behind, loudly singing of His heroic deeds and fame.

Word-by-word meaning: उत्सवम् a festival, श्रम-रुचा with the beauty of perspiration (caused by fatigue), अपि even, दृशीनाम् for the eyes (of the gopis), उन्नयन् bringing about/lifting, खुर-रजः-छुरित-स्रक् His flower garland covered by the dust raised by the hooves, दित्सया with the desire to give, एति He comes, सुहृद-आशिषः the desires/blessings of His friends, एषः this, देवकी-जठर-भूः born from the womb of Devaki, उडु-राजः the king of the stars (the moon)

Anvaya: एषः देवकीजठरभूः उडुराजः खुररजश्छुरितस्रक् सुहृदाशिषः दित्सया (सन्) श्रमरुचा अपि दृशीनाम् उत्सवम् उन्नयन् एति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एषः देवकीजठरभूः उडुराजः This moon born from the womb of Devaki, खुररजश्छुरितस्रक् His garland covered in the dust raised by the cows' hooves, सुहृदाशिषः दित्सया comes with the desire to fulfill the blessings of His friends, श्रमरुचा अपि even while He is beautified by the glow of fatigue, उत्सवम् उन्नयन् एति bringing a festival to our eyes.

Simple Meaning: Here comes the moon-like Krishna, born from the womb of Devaki! Though His flower garland is coated with the dust kicked up by the cows and He appears tired from the day's work, that very perspiration only adds to His beauty. He returns with the desire to fulfill the hearts of His dear friends, providing a magnificent festival for our thirsty eyes.

मदविघूर्णितलोचन ईषन् मानदः स्वसुहृदां वनमाली ।

बदरपाण्डुवदनो मृदुगण्डं मण्डयन् कनककुण्डललक्ष्म्या ॥10.35.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: मद-विघूर्णित-लोचनः His eyes rolling as if intoxicated (with joy/love), ईषत् slightly, मान-दः offering respect/honor, स्व-सुहृदाम् to His own friends, वन-माली wearing a forest garland, बदर-पाण्डु-वदनः His face pale like a ripened jujube fruit (due to the dust and evening light), मृदु-गण्डम् His soft cheeks, मण्डयन् decorating, कनक-कुण्डल-लक्ष्म्या with the splendor of His golden earrings

Anvaya: (एषः) वनमाली ईषत् मदविघूर्णितलोचनः स्वसुहृदां मानदः बदरपाण्डुवदनः कनककुण्डललक्ष्म्या मृदुगण्डं मण्डयन् (आगच्छति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वनमाली This wearer of forest garlands, ईषत् मदविघूर्णितलोचनः His eyes slightly rolling in intoxication, स्वसुहृदां मानदः offering honor to His friends, बदरपाण्डुवदनः His face pale like a yellow berry, कनककुण्डललक्ष्म्या मृदुगण्डं मण्डयन् His soft cheeks decorated with the beauty of His golden earrings.

Simple Meaning: Here He comes, wearing His beautiful forest garland! His eyes roll slightly as if He is intoxicated. He honours each of His friends. His face has taken on a pale, golden hue like a ripe fruit, and the shimmering light from His golden earrings dances upon His soft cheeks, making Him look absolutely enchanting.

यदुपतिर्द्विरदराजविहारो यामिनीपतिरिवैष दिनान्ते ।

मुदितवक्त्र उपयाति दुरन्तं मोचयन् व्रजगवां दिनतापम् ॥10.35.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: यदुपतिः the Lord of the Yadus, द्विरद-राज-विहारः having the graceful gait of a kingly elephant, यामिनी-पतिः the lord of the night (the moon), इव like, एषः this (Krishna), दिन-अन्ते at the end of the day, मुदित-वक्त्रः with a joyful face, उपयाति approaches, दुरन्तम् endless, मोचयन् relieving, व्रज-गवाम् of the cows (and the people) of Vraja, दिन-तापम् the heat/distress of the day

Anvaya: एषः द्विरदराजविहारः मुदितवक्त्रः यदुपतिः, व्रजगवां दुरन्तं दिनतापं मोचयन् दिनान्ते यामिनीपतिः इव उपयाति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एषः द्विरदराजविहारः मुदितवक्त्रः यदुपतिः This Lord of the Yadus, moving with the gait of a royal elephant and possessing a joyful face, व्रजगवां दुरन्तं दिनतापं मोचयन् relieving the endless distress of the day for the inhabitants of Vraja, दिनान्ते यामिनीपतिः इव उपयाति approaches at the end of the day like the moon, the lord of the night.

Simple Meaning: Like the rising moon that cools the earth after a scorching day, the Lord of the Yadus approaches Vraja at sunset. Walking with the majestic grace of an elephant and wearing a blissful smile, He instantly dispels the long, agonizing heat and the pain of separation that the cows and the people of Vraja have endured all day.

श्री शुक उवाच

एवं व्रजस्त्रियो राजन् कृष्णलीला नु गायतीः ।

रेमिरेऽहःसु तच्चित्तास्तन्मनस्का महोदयाः ॥10.35.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एवम् in this way, व्रज-स्त्रियः the women of Vraja, राजन् O King (Parikshita), कृष्ण-लीलाः the pastimes of Krishna, अनु-गायतीः singing together/in response to each other, रेमिरे they enjoyed, अहःसु during the days, तत्-चित्ताः their hearts fixed on Him, तत्-मनस्काः their minds absorbed in Him, महोदयाः very fortunate/elevated

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच – राजन्! एवं महोदयाः व्रजस्त्रियः तच्चित्ताः तन्मनस्काः (सत्यः) अहःसु कृष्णलीलाः अनुगायतीः रेमिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, राजन्! O King! एवं महोदयाः व्रजस्त्रियः In this way, the highly fortunate women of Vraja, तच्चित्ताः तन्मनस्काः (सत्यः) with their hearts and minds completely absorbed in Him, अहःसु कृष्णलीलाः अनुगायतीः रेमिरे spent their days in bliss, singing of the various pastimes of Krishna.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: O King Parikshita, in this manner the women of Vraja passed their days in transcendental ecstasy. With their hearts and minds fully immersed in Krishna, these most fortunate souls found their joy in constantly singing together about His divine pastimes.

Section Summary: With this verse, the chapter known as the Yugala-gita (The Song of the Pairs) concludes. It beautifully depicts:

  • The purva-ahna and apara-ahna (morning and afternoon) pastimes of Krishna.

  • The deep viraha (separation) felt by the Gopis while Krishna is in the forest.

  • The universal impact of Krishna’s flute, which stuns the animate and animates the inanimate.

  • The total absorption of the residents of Vraja in Krishna-bhakti.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “वृन्दावनक्रीडायां गोपिकायुगलगीतं नाम पञ्चत्रिंशः अध्यायः॥35॥

Thus ends the thirty-fifth discourse named “The Gopis' song in pairs of verses”, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
