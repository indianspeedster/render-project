Chapter 9

Grace showered on Gopi Yashoda

(Yashoda binds Krishna)

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

गोपीप्रसादः नाम नवमः अध्यायः

(23 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

एकदा गृहदासीषु यशोदा नन्दगेहिनी ।

कर्मान्तरनियुक्तासु निर्ममन्थ स्वयं दधि ॥10.9.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एकदा one day, गृह-दासीषु household maids, यशोदा Yashoda, नन्द-गेहिनी wife of Nanda, कर्म-अन्तर-नियुक्तासु engaged in other work, निर्ममन्थ churned, स्वयम् herself, दधि yogurt

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — एकदा नन्दगेहिनी यशोदा गृहदासीषु कर्मान्तरनियुक्तासु स्वयं दधि निर्ममन्थ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said — एकदा One day, नन्दगेहिनी यशोदा Yashoda the wife of Nanda, गृहदासीषु कर्मान्तरनियुक्तासु when the household maids were engaged in other work, स्वयं दधि निर्ममन्थ herself churned yogurt.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: One day, when the household maids were engaged in other duties, Yashoda, the wife of Nanda, herself churned yogurt.

यानि यानीह गीतानि तद्बालचरितानि च ।

दधि निर्मन्थने काले स्मरन्ती तान्यगायत ॥10.9.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: यानि यानि whatever, ह indeed, गीतानि songs, तत्-बाल-चरितानि pastimes of that child, च and, दधि yogurt, निर्मन्थने while churning, काले time, स्मरन्ती remembering, तानि them, अगायत sang

Anvaya: दधि निर्मन्थने काले तद्बालचरितानि यानि यानि गीतानि ह, तानि स्मरन्ती अगायत च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: दधि निर्मन्थने काले While churning yogurt at that time, तद्बालचरितानि यानि यानि गीतानि ह whatever songs indeed about the pastimes of that child, तानि स्मरन्ती अगायत च remembering them she sang.

Simple meaning: While churning yogurt, remembering and singing whatever songs there were about the childhood pastimes of that child, she continued her work.

क्षौमं वासः पृथुकटितटे बिभ्रती सूत्रनद्धं पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतकुचयुगं जातकम्पं च सुभ्रूः ।

रज्जावकर्षश्रमभुजचलत्कङ्कणौ कुण्डले च स्विन्नं वक्त्रं कबरविगलन्मालती निर्ममन्थ ॥10.9.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्षौमम् silken, वासः garment, पृथु-कटि-तटे on wide hips, बिभ्रती wearing, सूत्र-नद्धम् bound with belt, पुत्र-स्नेह-स्नुत-कुच-युगम् pair of breasts flowing with motherly affection, जात-कम्पम् trembling, च and, सु-भ्रूः beautiful eyebrows, रज्जु-आवकर्ष-श्रम-भुज-चलत्-कङ्कणौ bangles moving on arms from exertion of pulling rope, कुण्डले earrings, च and, स्विन्नम् perspiring, वक्त्रम् face, कबरि-विगलत्-मालती jasmine falling from hair, निर्ममन्थ churned

Anvaya: सुभ्रूः पृथुकटितटे सूत्रनद्धं क्षौमं वासः बिभ्रती, जातकम्पं पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतकुचयुगं च, रज्जावकर्षश्रमभुजचलत्कङ्कणौ कुण्डले च, कबरविगलन्मालती स्विन्नं वक्त्रं, निर्ममन्थ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सुभ्रूः The beautiful browed one, पृथुकटितटे सूत्रनद्धं क्षौमं वासः बिभ्रती wearing silken garment bound with belt on wide hips, पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतकुचयुगं जातकम्पं pair of breasts flowing with motherly affection and trembling, रज्जावकर्षश्रमभुजचलत्कङ्कणौ कुण्डले च bangles and earrings moving from exertion of pulling the churning rope, कबरविगलन्मालती स्विन्नं वक्त्रं face perspiring with jasmine flowers falling from her hair, निर्ममन्थ she churned.

Simple meaning: The beautiful-browed Yashoda, wearing a silken garment bound with a belt on her wide hips, her breasts flowing and trembling with motherly affection, her bangles and earrings moving from the exertion of pulling the churning rope, her face perspiring with jasmine flowers falling from her loosened hair—thus she churned the yogurt.

तां स्तन्यकाम आसाद्य मथ्नन्तीं जननीं हरिः ।

गृहीत्वा दधिमन्थानं न्यषेधत् प्रीतिमावहन् ॥10.9.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताम् her, स्तन्य-कामः desiring milk, आसाद्य approaching, मथ्नन्तीम् churning, जननीम् mother, हरिः Hari, गृहीत्वा seizing, दधि-मन्थानम् churning rod, न्यषेधत् stopped, प्रीतिम् pleasure, आवहन् bringing

Anvaya: स्तन्यकामः हरिः मथ्नन्तीं तां जननीम् आसाद्य दधिमन्थानं गृहीत्वा प्रीतिम् आवहन् न्यषेधत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्तन्यकामः हरिः Hari desiring milk, मथ्नन्तीं तां जननीम् आसाद्य approaching that mother who was churning, दधिमन्थानं गृहीत्वा seizing the churning rod, प्रीतिम् आवहन् bringing joy, न्यषेधत् stopped her.

Simple meaning: Lord Hari, desiring milk, approached His mother who was churning yogurt. Seizing the churning rod and bringing joy to her (by His loving gesture), He stopped her from churning.

तमङ्कमारूढमपाययत् स्तनं स्नेहस्नुतं सस्मितमीक्षती मुखम् ।

अतृप्तमुत्सृज्य जवेन सा ययावुत्सिच्यमाने पयसि त्वधिश्रिते ॥10.9.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him, अङ्कम् on lap, आरूढम् mounted, अपाययत् gave to drink, स्तनम् breast, स्नेह-स्नुतम् flowing with affection, स-स्मितम् with smile, ईक्षती gazing, मुखम् face, अतृप्तम् unsatisfied, उत्सृज्य abandoning, जवेन quickly, सा she, ययौ went, उत्सिच्यमाने overflowing, पयसि milk, तु indeed, अधिश्रिते on the stove

Anvaya: तम् अङ्कम् आरूढं सस्मितं मुखम् ईक्षती, स्नेहस्नुतं स्तनम् अपाययत्। अधिश्रिते पयसि तु उत्सिच्यमाने अतृप्तम् उत्सृज्य सा जवेन ययौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तम् अङ्कम् आरूढम् Having mounted Him on her lap, सस्मितं मुखम् ईक्षती gazing at His smiling face, स्नेहस्नुतं स्तनम् अपाययत् she gave Him her breast flowing with affection. अधिश्रिते पयसि तु उत्सिच्यमाने As the milk on the stove was indeed overflowing, अतृप्तम् उत्सृज्य abandoning Him unsatisfied, सा जवेन ययौ she quickly went.

Simple meaning: Having placed Him on her lap, gazing lovingly at His smiling face, she gave Him her breast flowing with motherly affection. But she suddenly left Him unsatisfied and quickly went away, as the milk on the stove was overflowing.

सञ्जातकोपः स्फुरितारुणाधरं संदश्य दद्भिर्दधिमन्थभाजनम् ।

भित्त्वा मृषाश्रुर्दृषदश्मना रहो जघास हैयङ्गवमन्तरं गतः ॥10.9.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: सञ्जात-कोपः having become angry, स्फुरित-अरुण-अधरम् trembling reddish lower lip, संदश्य biting, दद्भिः with teeth, दधि-मन्थ-भाजनम् yogurt churning pot, भित्त्वा having broken, मृषा-अश्रुः with false tears, दृषद्-अश्मना with a grinding stone (which was maybe lying closeby), रहः secretly, जघास ate, हैयङ्गवम् butter, अन्तरम् inside, गतः having gone

हैयङ्गवीन   n. clarified butter prepared from yesterday's milking, fresh butter, src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

दृषद् - A mill-stone, a flat stone for grinding condiments upon src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: सञ्जातकोपः स्फुरितारुणाधरं दद्भिः संदश्य, दृषदश्मना दधिमन्थभाजनं भित्त्वा, मृषाश्रुः अन्तरं गतः रहः हैयङ्गवं जघास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सञ्जातकोपः Having become angry, स्फुरितारुणाधरं दद्भिः संदश्य biting His trembling reddish lower lip with His teeth, दृषदश्मना with a stone piece, दधिमन्थभाजनं भित्त्वा having broken the yogurt churning pot, मृषाश्रुः with false tears, अन्तरं गतः having gone inside, रहः secretly, हैयङ्गवं जघास ate the butter.

Simple meaning: Having become angry and biting His trembling reddish lower lip with His teeth, He broke the yogurt churning pot using a stone piece. With false tears in His eyes, He went inside the house, and secretly, He ate the butter.

उत्तार्य गोपी सुशृतं पयः पुनः प्रविश्य संदृश्य च दध्यमत्रकम् ।

भग्नं विलोक्य स्वसुतस्य कर्म तज्जहास तं चापि न तत्र पश्यती ॥10.9.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: उत्तार्य having removed, गोपी the gopi, सु-शृतम् well cooked, पयः milk, पुनः again, प्रविश्य entering, संदृश्य seeing, च and, दधि-अमत्रकम् yogurt pot, भग्नम् broken, विलोक्य seeing, स्व-सुतस्य of her own son, कर्म deed, तत् that, जहास laughed, तम् Him, च and, अपि also, न not, तत्र there, पश्यती seeing

अमत्रक   अमत्रक n. a drinking vessel, vessel, src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: गोपी सुशृतं पयः उत्तार्य, पुनः प्रविश्य, दध्यमत्रकं भग्नं संदृश्य, तं च अपि तत्र न पश्यती स्वसुतस्य तत् कर्म च विलोक्य, जहास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपी The gopi, सुशृतं पयः उत्तार्य having removed the well cooked milk from the stove, पुनः प्रविश्य entering again, दध्यमत्रकं च भग्नं संदृश्य and seeing the yogurt pot broken, तं च अपि तत्र न पश्यती also not seeing Him there, स्वसुतस्य तत् कर्म विलोक्य understood that it was the work of her son, जहास and laughed.

Simple meaning: The gopi, having removed the well-cooked milk from the stove and entering the room again, saw the yogurt pot broken and not seeing Him there in the room, she understood that it is the mischievous deed of her son and laughed.

उलूखलाङ्घ्रेरुपरि व्यवस्थितं मर्काय कामं ददतं शिचि स्थितम् ।

हैयङ्गवं चौर्यविशङ्कितेक्षणं निरीक्ष्य पश्चात् सुतमागमच्छनैः ॥10.9.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: उलूखल-अङ्घ्रेः of the mortar (used as a step), उपरि on top, व्यवस्थितम् standing, मर्काय to the monkey, कामम् as much as desired, ददतम् giving, शिचि स्थितम् in the pot hanging from the net, हैयङ्गवम् freshly churned butter, चौर्य-विशङ्कित-ईक्षणम् with eyes fearful of being caught stealing, निरीक्ष्य having seen, पश्चात् from behind, सुतम् her son, आगमत् she approached, शनैः slowly/quietly

Anvaya: (सा गोपी) उलूखलाङ्घ्रेः उपरि व्यवस्थितं, शिचि स्थितं हैयङ्गवं मर्काय कामं ददतं, चौर्यविशङ्कितेक्षणं सुतं निरीक्ष्य, पश्चात् शनैः आगमत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सा गोपी) She the gopi, उलूखलाङ्घ्रेः उपरि व्यवस्थितं standing on top of the mortar used as a step, शिचि स्थितं हैयङ्गवं the freshly churned butter situated in the hanging pot, मर्काय कामं ददतं giving as much as desired to the monkey, चौर्यविशङ्कितेक्षणं with eyes casting fearful glances lest He be caught stealing, सुतं निरीक्ष्य having seen her son, पश्चात् शनैः आगमत् approached slowly from behind.

Simple meaning: Yashoda saw her son Krishna standing on top of the mortar which He had used as a step, distributing the freshly churned butter from the hanging pot to the monkeys to His heart's content, all the while casting nervous glances around fearful of being caught — and having seen this, she quietly crept up behind Him.

तामात्तयष्टिं प्रसमीक्ष्य सत्वरस्ततोऽवरुह्यापससार भीतवत् ।

गोप्यन्वधावन्न यमाप योगिनां क्षमं प्रवेष्टुं तपसेरितं मनः ॥10.9.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताम् her, आत्त-यष्टिम् carrying a stick, प्रसमीक्ष्य having seen, सत्वरः quickly, ततः from there, अवरुह्य climbing down, अपससार fled, भीत-वत् as if frightened, गोपी the gopi, अन्वधावत् ran after, न not, यम् whom, आप reach, योगिनाम् of the yogis, क्षमम् ability, प्रवेष्टुम् to enter, तपसा by austerity, ईरितम् directed, मनः mind

Anvaya: ताम् आत्त-यष्टिं प्रसमीक्ष्य, सत्वरः ततः अवरुह्य, भीतवत् अपससार। गोपी अन्वधावत्, यं योगिनां तपसा ईरितं मनः प्रवेष्टुं क्षमं न आप।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ताम् आत्त-यष्टिम् प्रसमीक्ष्य having seen her carrying a stick, सत्वरः quickly, ततः from there, अवरुह्य climbing down, भीतवत् अपससार fled as if frightened. गोपी अन्वधावत् The gopi ran after Him, यं unto Whom, योगिनां तपसा ईरितं मनः the minds of yogis directed by austerity, प्रवेष्टुं क्षमं ability to enter, न आप don't reach.

Simple meaning: Seeing Yashoda approaching with a stick in her hand, Krishna quickly climbed down from the mortar and fled as if frightened. Yashoda ran after Him — unto Whom, the minds of great yogis, propelled by intense austerity, do not reach the ability to enter, was now being chased by a simple gopi mother.

अन्वञ्चमाना जननी बृहच्चलच्छ्रोणीभराक्रान्तगतिः सुमध्यमा ।

जवेन विस्रंसितकेशबन्धनच्युतप्रसूनानुगतिः परामृशत् ॥10.9.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्वञ्चमाना following after, जननी the mother, बृहत्-चलत्-श्रोणी-भर-आक्रान्त-गतिः whose movement was hindered by the weight of her heavy swaying hips, सु-मध्यमा slender-waisted, जवेन by speed/haste, विस्रंसित-केश-बन्धन-च्युत-प्रसून-अनुगतिः with flowers falling from loosened hair-knot as she ran, परामृशत् caught/touched

Anvaya: बृहच्चलच्छ्रोणीभराक्रान्तगतिः सुमध्यमा जननी अन्वञ्चमाना, जवेन विस्रंसितकेशबन्धनच्युतप्रसूनानुगतिः (सती) परामृशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बृहच्चलच्छ्रोणीभराक्रान्तगतिः Whose gait was slowed by the weight of her large swaying hips, सुमध्यमा slender-waisted, जननी the mother, अन्वञ्चमाना following after Him, जवेन by the speed of running, विस्रंसितकेशबन्धनच्युतप्रसूनानुगतिः her hair-knot having become loosened, and flowers falling from it trailing behind her, परामृशत् (finally) caught Him.

Simple meaning: The slender-waisted mother Yashoda, whose gait was being slowed by the weight of her large swaying hips, ran after Krishna, and as she ran, her hair-knot loosened and flowers fell from it trailing behind her — and with great effort she finally managed to catch Him.

कृतागसं तं प्ररुदन्तमक्षिणी कषन्तमञ्जन्मषिणी स्वपाणिना ।

उद्वीक्षमाणं भयविह्वलेक्षणं हस्ते गृहीत्वा भिषयन्त्यवागुरत् ॥10.9.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृत-आगसम् who had committed an offense, तम् Him, प्ररुदन्तम् crying, अक्षिणी both eyes, कषन्तम् rubbing, अञ्जन्-मषिणी smeared with black kajal/collyrium, स्व-पाणिना with his own hand, उद्वीक्षमाणम् looking up, भय-विह्वल-ईक्षणम् with eyes bewildered by fear, हस्ते by the hand, गृहीत्वा having caught/seized, भिषयन्ती threatening, अवागुरत् scolded/reprimanded

Anvaya: (यशोदा) कृतागसं तं, अञ्जन्मषिणी अक्षिणी स्वपाणिना कषन्तं, प्ररुदन्तं, भयविह्वलेक्षणम् उद्वीक्षमाणं (कृष्णम्) हस्ते गृहीत्वा, भिषयन्ती अवागुरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (यशोदा) Yashoda, कृतागसं तं Him who had committed the offense, अञ्जन्मषिणी अक्षिणी स्वपाणिना कषन्तं rubbing both His eyes smeared with black kajal with His own hand, प्ररुदन्तं crying, भयविह्वलेक्षणम् with eyes bewildered by fear, उद्वीक्षमाणं looking up at her, हस्ते गृहीत्वा having caught Him by the hand, भिषयन्ती threatening Him, अवागुरत् scolded Him.

Simple meaning: Yashoda caught Krishna — who had committed the mischief — by the hand. He was crying, rubbing His eyes with His own hand, smearing the kajal all around, and looking up at her with eyes full of fear and bewilderment. Having seized Him, Yashoda threatened and scolded Him.

त्यक्त्वा यष्टिं सुतं भीतं विज्ञायार्भकवत्सला ।

इयेष किल तं बद्धुं दाम्नातद्वीर्यकोविदा ॥10.9.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्यक्त्वा having put aside, यष्टिम् the stick, सुतम् her son, भीतम् frightened, विज्ञाय knowing/seeing, अर्भक-वत्सला affectionate towards her child, इयेष desired/decided, किल indeed, तम् Him, बद्धुम् to bind, दाम्ना with a rope, अ-तत्-वीर्य-कोविदा not knowing the extent of His powers

अ-तत्-वीर्य-कोविदा - अ- not, तत्- His, वीर्य- power, कोविदा - knower

Anvaya: अर्भकवत्सला (यशोदा) यष्टिं त्यक्त्वा, सुतं भीतं विज्ञाय, तं दाम्ना बद्धुम् इयेष, (यतः सा) अतद्वीर्यकोविदा (आसीत्) किल।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अर्भकवत्सला The affectionate mother Yashoda, यष्टिं त्यक्त्वा having put aside the stick, सुतं भीतं विज्ञाय seeing that her son was frightened, तं दाम्ना बद्धुम् to bind Him with a rope, इयेष decided, (यतः सा) अतद्वीर्यकोविदा (आसीत्) for she was unaware of the extent of His powers. किल indeed.

Simple meaning: The affectionate mother Yashoda, seeing that her son was frightened, put aside her stick and decided instead to bind Him with a rope — for indeed she had no knowledge of the true extent of His limitless power.

न चान्तर्न बहिर्यस्य न पूर्वं नापि चापरम् ।

पूर्वापरं बहिश्चान्तर्जगतो यो जगच्च यः ॥10.9.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, च and, अन्तः inside, न not, बहिः outside, यस्य whose, न not, पूर्वम् before/beginning, न not, अपि also, च and, अपरम् after/end, पूर्व-अपरम् beginning and end, बहिः outside, च and, अन्तः inside, जगतः of the universe, यः who, जगत् the universe, च and, यः who

Anvaya: यस्य न अन्तः न बहिः, न पूर्वं न अपरं च, (यः) जगतः पूर्वापरं बहिः च अन्तः (अस्ति), यः जगत् च (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यस्य न अन्तः न बहिः Whose there is neither inside nor outside, न पूर्वं न अपरं च and no beginning nor end, (यः) जगतः पूर्वापरं बहिः च अन्तः (अस्ति) who is the beginning and end, the outside and inside of the universe, यः जगत् च (अस्ति) and who is the universe itself.

Simple meaning: He who has no inside nor outside, no beginning nor end — yet who is the beginning and end, the inside and outside of this entire universe — and who is indeed the universe itself, that Supreme Being was now standing before Yashoda as her little son.

तं मत्वाऽत्मजमव्यक्तं मर्त्यलिङ्गमधोक्षजम् ।

गोपिकोलूखले दाम्ना बबन्ध प्राकृतं यथा ॥10.9.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him, मत्वा considering, आत्मजम् as her own son, अव्यक्तम् the unmanifest, मर्त्य-लिङ्गम् having assumed a mortal form, अधोक्षजम् the transcendental one beyond sense perception, गोपिका the gopi Yashoda, उलूखले to the mortar, दाम्ना with a rope, बबन्ध bound, प्राकृतम् an ordinary child, यथा as if

Anvaya: गोपिका तम् अव्यक्तं मर्त्यलिङ्गम् अधोक्षजम् आत्मजं मत्वा, प्राकृतं यथा, उलूखले दाम्ना बबन्ध।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपिका the gopi Yashoda, तम् Him, अव्यक्तं who is the unmanifest, मर्त्यलिङ्गम् who has assumed a mortal form, अधोक्षजम् who is beyond all sense perception, आत्मजं मत्वा considering as her own son, प्राकृतं यथा just as one would treat an ordinary child, उलूखले दाम्ना बबन्ध bound Him to the mortar with a rope.

Simple meaning: Yashoda, considering Him — the unmanifest, the one beyond all sense perception who had merely assumed a mortal form — as nothing more than her own little son, bound Him to the mortar with a rope, just as one would bind an ordinary child.

तद् दाम बध्यमानस्य स्वार्भकस्य कृतागसः।

द्वयङ्गुलोनमभूत्तेन सन्दधेऽन्यच्च गोपिका ॥10.9.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, दाम rope, बध्यमानस्य of the one being bound, स्व-अर्भकस्य of her own child, कृत-आगसः who had committed the offense, द्वि-अङ्गुल-ऊनम् short by two fingers, अभूत् became, तेन with that, सन्दधे joined/added, अन्यत् another rope, च and, गोपिका the gopi Yashoda

Anvaya: कृतागसः स्वार्भकस्य बध्यमानस्य तत् दाम द्वयङ्गुलोनम् अभूत्। गोपिका तेन अन्यत् च सन्दधे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृतागसः स्वार्भकस्य बध्यमानस्य of her own child who had committed the offense while being bound, तत् दाम that rope, द्वयङ्गुलोनम् अभूत् fell short by two fingers, गोपिका Yashoda, तेन with that, अन्यत् च सन्दधे joined another rope to it.

Simple meaning: As Yashoda was trying to bind her mischievous son with the rope, the rope fell short by two fingers. So she joined another rope to make it longer.

यदाऽसीत्तदपि न्यूनं तेनान्यदपि सन्दधे ।

तदपि द्व्यङ्गुलं न्यूनं यद् यदादत्त बन्धनम् ॥10.9.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: यदा when, असीत् it was, तत् that, अपि also, न्यूनम् short, तेन with that, अन्यत् another, अपि also, सन्दधे she joined, तत् that, अपि also, द्वि-अङ्गुलम् by two fingers, न्यूनम् short, यत् यत् whatever, आदत्त she took up, बन्धनम् for binding

Anvaya: यदा तत् अपि न्यूनम् असीत्, तेन अन्यत् अपि सन्दधे। यत् यत् बन्धनम् आदत्त तत् अपि द्व्यङ्गुलं न्यूनम् (अभूत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यदा तत् अपि न्यूनम् असीत् When that also fell short, तेन अन्यत् अपि सन्दधे she joined yet another rope to it, यत् यत् बन्धनम् आदत्त whatever rope she took up for binding, तत् अपि द्व्यङ्गुलं न्यूनम् (अभूत्) that too fell short by two fingers.

Simple meaning: When that joined rope also fell short, she added yet another rope to it — but that too fell short by two fingers. Indeed whatever rope she picked up to bind Him with, it always fell short by two fingers.

एवं स्वगेहदामानि यशोदा सन्दधत्यपि ।

गोपीनां सुस्मयन्तीनां स्मयन्ती विस्मिताभवत् ॥10.9.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् thus, स्व-गेह-दामानि ropes from her own house, यशोदा Yashoda, सन्दधती joining one after another, अपि even then, गोपीनाम् of the gopis, सु-स्मयन्तीनाम् who were smiling/laughing, स्मयन्ती herself smiling, विस्मिता bewildered/astonished, अभवत् became

Anvaya: एवं यशोदा स्वगेहदामानि सन्दधती अपि, सुस्मयन्तीनां गोपीनां (मध्ये) स्मयन्ती विस्मिता अभवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं Thus, यशोदा Yashoda, स्वगेहदामानि सन्दधती अपि even while joining all the ropes from her own house one after another, सुस्मयन्तीनां गोपीनां (मध्ये) among the gopis who were smiling, स्मयन्ती herself smiling, विस्मिता अभवत् became bewildered and astonished.

Simple meaning: Thus Yashoda kept joining rope after rope from her entire household, and yet every rope fell short. All the while the other gopis who had gathered were smiling at the scene, and Yashoda herself smiled too — yet she was filled with wonder and astonishment at what was happening.

स्वमातुः स्विन्नगात्राया विस्रस्तकबरस्रजः ।

दृष्ट्वा परिश्रमं कृष्णः कृपयाऽसीत् स्वबन्धने ॥10.9.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्वमातुः of his own mother, स्विन्न-गात्रायाः whose body was perspiring, विस्रस्त-कबर-स्रजः whose hair braid and garland had become disheveled, दृष्ट्वा having seen, परिश्रमम् the exhaustion/effort, कृष्णः Krishna, कृपया out of compassion, आसीत् allowed, स्व-बन्धने in His own binding

Anvaya: कृष्णः स्वमातुः स्विन्नगात्रायाः विस्रस्तकबरस्रजः परिश्रमं दृष्ट्वा, कृपया स्वबन्धने आसीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णः Krishna, स्वमातुः of His own mother, स्विन्नगात्रायाः whose body had become drenched in perspiration, विस्रस्तकबरस्रजः whose hair braid and garland had become disheveled, परिश्रमं दृष्ट्वा having seen the exhaustion and effort, कृपया out of compassion, स्वबन्धने आसीत् allowed Himself to be bound.

Simple meaning: Krishna, seeing the exhaustion of His own mother — her body drenched in sweat and her hair braid and garland all disheveled from the effort — was moved by compassion and allowed Himself to be bound by her rope.

एवं संदर्शिता ह्यङ्ग हरिणा भृत्यवश्यता ।

स्ववशेनापि कृष्णेन यस्येदं सेश्वरं वशे ॥10.9.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् thus, संदर्शिता demonstrated/shown, हि indeed, अङ्ग O dear one (Parikshita), हरिणा by Hari, भृत्य-वश्यता submissiveness to His devotee/servant, स्व-वशेन by one who is under His own control, अपि even, कृष्णेन by Krishna, यस्य whose, इदम् this, स-ईश्वरम् along with its ruler, वशे under control

Anvaya: अङ्ग! एवं हरिणा भृत्यवश्यता संदर्शिता हि, (यः) कृष्णः स्ववशेन अपि (अस्ति), यस्य वशे इदं सेश्वरं (जगत् अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग O dear one (Parikshit), एवं thus, हरिणा by Hari, भृत्य-वश्यता संदर्शिता हि His submissiveness to His devotee was indeed demonstrated, (यः) कृष्णः स्ववशेन अपि (अस्ति) Krishna who is under His own control alone, यस्य वशे इदं सेश्वरं (जगत् अस्ति) under whose control is this entire universe along with its ruler Brahma.

Simple meaning: O dear Parikshit! Thus Hari demonstrated His quality of being completely submissive to His devotee — He who is under no one's control but His own, and under whose control rests this entire universe along with its ruler Brahma, willingly allowed Himself to be bound by the love of mother Yashoda.

नेमं विरिञ्चो न भवो न श्रीरप्यङ्गसंश्रया ।

प्रसादं लेभिरे गोपी यत्तत् प्राप विमुक्तिदात् ॥10.9.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, इमम् this, विरिञ्चः Brahma, न not, भवः Shiva, न not, श्रीः Lakshmi, अपि even, अङ्ग-संश्रया who resides on His chest, प्रसादम् grace/favor, लेभिरे obtained, गोपी the gopi Yashoda, यत् which, तत् that, प्राप obtained, विमुक्ति-दात् from Him who is the giver of liberation

Anvaya: इमं प्रसादं विरिञ्चः न लेभे, भवः न लेभे, अङ्गसंश्रया श्रीः अपि न लेभे — यत् (प्रसादं) गोपी विमुक्तिदात् (हरेः) प्राप।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इमं प्रसादं this grace/favor, विरिञ्चः न लेभे Brahma did not obtain, भवः न लेभे Shiva did not obtain, अङ्गसंश्रया श्रीः अपि न लेभे even Lakshmi who always resides on His chest did not obtain — यत् (प्रसादं) which grace, गोपी the gopi Yashoda, विमुक्तिदात् (हरेः) from Hari who is the giver of liberation, प्राप obtained.

Simple meaning: That special grace which the gopi Yashoda obtained from Hari — the very giver of liberation — was not obtained even by Brahma, nor by Shiva, nor even by Lakshmi who ever dwells upon His chest. Such was the unique and supreme blessing of mother Yashoda's pure maternal love.

नायं सुखापो भगवान् देहिनां गोपिकासुतः।

ज्ञानिनां चात्मभूतानां यथा भक्तिमतामिह ॥10.9.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, अयम् He, सुख-आपः easily obtainable, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, देहिनाम् for those with material bodies, गोपिका-सुतः the son of Yashoda the gopi, ज्ञानिनाम् for the jnanis/those with knowledge, च and, आत्म-भूतानाम् for those who have realized the Self/for yogis, यथा as, भक्तिमताम् for those endowed with devotion, इह here/in this world

Anvaya: अयं भगवान् गोपिकासुतः देहिनां ज्ञानिनां च आत्मभूतानां (मध्ये) सुखापः न (अस्ति), यथा इह भक्तिमतां (सुखापः अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अयं भगवान् गोपिकासुतः This Supreme Lord who became the son of Yashoda, देहिनां for those with material bodies, ज्ञानिनां च and for the jnanis, आत्मभूतानां for those who have realized the Self, सुखापः न (अस्ति) is not easily obtainable, यथा as, इह in this world, भक्तिमतां for those endowed with pure devotion, (सुखापः अस्ति) He is easily obtainable.

Simple meaning: This Supreme Lord who became the son of Yashoda is not easily attainable for those with material bodies, nor for the jnanis, nor even for the Self-realized yogis — but He is most easily attainable for those who are endowed with pure devotion. Such is the supreme power of bhakti.

कृष्णस्तु गृहकृत्येषु व्यग्रायां मातरि प्रभुः।

अद्राक्षीदर्जुनौ पूर्वं गुह्यकौ धनदात्मजौ ॥10.9.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णः Krishna, तु indeed, गृह-कृत्येषु in household duties, व्यग्रायाम् being busy/occupied, मातरि while the mother, प्रभुः the Lord, अद्राक्षीत् saw, अर्जुनौ the two arjuna trees, पूर्वम् formerly, गुह्यकौ two yaksha attendants, धनद-आत्मजौ sons of Kubera

Anvaya: तु मातरि गृहकृत्येषु व्यग्रायां (सत्यां), प्रभुः कृष्णः पूर्वं गुह्यकौ धनदात्मजौ अर्जुनौ अद्राक्षीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तु Indeed, मातरि गृहकृत्येषु व्यग्रायां (सत्यां) while the mother was busy occupied with household duties, प्रभुः कृष्णः the Lord Krishna, पूर्वं who were formerly, गुह्यकौ two yaksha attendants, धनदात्मजौ sons of Kubera, अर्जुनौ the two arjuna trees, अद्राक्षीत् saw/noticed.

Simple meaning: While mother Yashoda was busy with her household duties, the Lord Krishna noticed the two arjuna trees standing nearby — trees which had formerly been the two yaksha attendants, the sons of Kubera.

पुरा नारदशापेन वृक्षतां प्रापितौ मदात् ।

नलकूबरमणिग्रीवाविति ख्यातौ श्रियान्वितौ ॥10.9.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुरा formerly, नारद-शापेन by the curse of Narada, वृक्षताम् the state of being trees, प्रापितौ caused to obtain/reduced to, मदात् due to pride/intoxication, नलकूबर-मणिग्रीवौ Nalakubara and Manigriva, इति thus, ख्यातौ well known, श्रिया with wealth/opulence, अन्वितौ endowed with

Anvaya: पुरा मदात् नारदशापेन वृक्षतां प्रापितौ, नलकूबरमणिग्रीवौ इति ख्यातौ, श्रिया अन्वितौ (आस्ताम्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पुरा formerly, मदात् due to pride and intoxication, नारदशापेन by the curse of Narada, वृक्षतां प्रापितौ reduced to the state of trees, नलकूबरमणिग्रीवौ इति ख्यातौ well known as Nalakubara and Manigriva, श्रिया अन्वितौ (आस्ताम्) they were endowed with great wealth and opulence.

Simple meaning: These two trees were formerly the well-known sons of Kubera, Nalakubara and Manigriva, who were endowed with great wealth and opulence — but due to their pride and intoxication they had been cursed by Narada and reduced to the state of trees.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे

गोपीप्रसादः नाम नवमः अध्यायः ॥9॥

Thus ends the ninth discourse entitled Grace (showered) on the cowherd lady (Yashoda), in the first half of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
