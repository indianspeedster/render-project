Chapter 21

The Song Inspired by the Flute

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

वेणुगीतं नाम एकविंशः अध्यायः

(20 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

इत्थं शरत्स्वच्छजलं पद्माकरसुगन्धिना ।

न्यविशद् वायुना वातं सगोगोपालकोऽच्युतः ॥10.21.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, इत्थम् thus, शरत्-स्वच्छ-जलम् (which had) the clear water of autumn, पद्म-आकर-सुगन्धिना scattering the fragrance of the lotus-filled lakes, न्यविशत् entered, वायुना by the wind, वातम् fanned/blown, स-गो-गोपालकः along with the cows and cowherd boys, अच्युतः the infallible Lord (Krishna)

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — इत्थं सगोगोपालकः अच्युतः शरत्स्वच्छजलं पद्माकरसुगन्धिना वायुना वातं (वृन्दावनं) न्यविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, इत्थं In this way, सगोगोपालकः अच्युतः the infallible Lord Krishna with the cows and cowherd boys, शरत्स्वच्छजलं possessing the clear waters of autumn, पद्माकरसुगन्धिना वायुना वातं fanned by the breeze carrying the fragrance of the lotus lakes, न्यविशत् entered (the forest).

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: Thus, the infallible Lord Krishna, accompanied by His cows and cowherd boyfriends, entered the Vrindavana forest. The forest was cooled by gentle breezes scented by the fragrance of lotuses in the autumn-clear lakes.

कुसुमितवनराजिशुष्मिभृङ्गद्विजकुलघुष्टसरःसरिन्महीध्रम् ।

मधुपतिरवगाह्य चारयन् गाः सहपशुपालबलश्चुकूज वेणुम् ॥10.21.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: कुसुमित-वन-राजि-शुष्मि-भृङ्ग-द्विज-कुल-घुष्ट-सरः-सरित्-महीध्रम् (the forest where) the lakes, rivers, and mountains were filled with the sounds of swarms of intoxicated bees and flocks of birds amidst the rows of flowering trees, मधुपतिः the Lord of Madhu (Krishna), अवगाह्य entering/plunging into, चारयन् tending/grazing, गाः the cows, सह-पशु-पाल-बलः along with the cowherd boys and Balarama, चुकूज sounded/played, वेणुम् the flute

कुसुमित- blossomed, वन-राजि rows of trees, -शुष्मि- intoxicated, भृङ्ग- bees, द्विज- birds, कुल- flocks, घुष्ट- filled with, सरः- lakes, सरित्- rivers, महीध्रम् mountains

Anvaya: सहपशुपालबलः मधुपतिः कुसुमितवनराजिशुष्मिभृङ्गद्विजकुलघुष्टसरःसरिन्महीध्रम् (वृन्दावनम्) अवगाह्य गाः चारयन् वेणुं चुकूज।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सहपशुपालबलः मधुपतिः Together with the cowherd boys and Balarama, Lord Krishna, कुसुमितवनराजिशुष्मिभृङ्गद्विजकुलघुष्टसरःसरिन्महीध्रम् the forest where the lakes, rivers, and hills resounded with the humming of intoxicated bees and the chirping of birds in the flowering groves, अवगाह्य having entered, गाः चारयन् while tending the cows, वेणुं चुकूज played His flute.

Simple meaning: Entering the forest of Vrindavana, where the mountains, lakes, and rivers were vibrating with the songs of birds and the humming of bees intoxicated by the blooming trees, Lord Krishna, along with Balarama and the cowherd boys, began to play His flute while pasturing the cows.

तद् व्रजस्त्रिय आश्रुत्य वेणुगीतं स्मरोदयम् ।

काश्चित् परोक्षं कृष्णस्य स्वसखीभ्योऽन्ववर्णयन् ॥10.21.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, व्रज-स्त्रियः the women of Vraja, आश्रुत्य hearing, वेणु-गीतम् the music of the flute, स्मर-उदयम् which gives rise to Cupid (arouses love/desire), काश्चित् some of them, परोक्षम् in the absence, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, स्व-सखीभ्यः to their own girlfriends, अन्ववर्णयन् began to describe

Anvaya: काश्चित् व्रजस्त्रियः स्मरोदयं तत् वेणुगीतम् आश्रुत्य कृष्णस्य परोक्षं स्वसखीभ्यः अन्ववर्णयन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: काश्चित् व्रजस्त्रियः Some of the women of Vraja, स्मरोदयं तत् वेणुगीतम् that flute-song which arouses love, आश्रुत्य having heard, कृष्णस्य परोक्षं in the absence of Krishna, स्वसखीभ्यः to their own girlfriends, अन्ववर्णयन् described (it).

Simple meaning: When the young women of Vraja heard the sound of Krishna's flute, which awakens the heart's deepest desires, they began to describe its beauty to one another in the absence of Krishna, gathering in small groups to share their feelings.

तद् वर्णयितुमारब्धाः स्मरन्त्यः कृष्णचेष्टितम् ।

नाशकन् स्मरवेगेन विक्षिप्तमनसो नृप ॥10.21.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, वर्णयितुम् to describe, आरब्धाः beginning, स्मरन्त्यः remembering, कृष्ण-चेष्टितम् the activities of Krishna, न अशकन् they were not able, स्मर-वेगेन by the force of love (Cupid), विक्षिप्त-मनसः whose minds were agitated/disturbed, नृप O King (Parikshit)

Anvaya: नृप! कृष्णचेष्टितं स्मरन्त्यः तत् वर्णयितुम् आरब्धाः स्मरवेगेन विक्षिप्तमनसः (ताः) न अशकन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King! कृष्णचेष्टितं स्मरन्त्यः Remembering the activities of Krishna, तत् वर्णयितुम् आरब्धाः beginning to describe that, स्मरवेगेन विक्षिप्तमनसः their minds being agitated by the force of love, न अशकन् they were not able (to do so).

Simple meaning: O King Parikshit! As the Gopis began to describe the activities of Krishna, their hearts were so overcome by the force of their love for Him that their minds became agitated, and they were unable to continue their description.

ब​र्हापीडं नटवरवपुः कर्णयोः कर्णिकारं बिभ्रद् वासः कनककपिशं वैजयन्तीं च मालाम् ।

रन्ध्रान् वेणोरधरसुधया पूरयन् गोपवृन्दैर्वृन्दारण्यं स्वपदरमणं प्राविशद् गीतकीर्तिः ॥10.21.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: बर्हा-पीडम् a peacock-feather decoration upon His head, नट-वर-वपुः a body like that of the best of dancers, कर्णयोः on His ears, कर्णिकारम् the yellow karnikara flower, बिभ्रत् wearing, वासः garment, कनक-कपिशम् yellowish gold, वैजयन्तीम् च मालाम् and a Vaijayanti flower garland, रन्ध्रान् the holes, वेणोः of the flute, अधर-सुधया with the nectar of His lips, पूरयन् filling, गोप-वृन्दैः with the groups of cowherd boys, वृन्दा-अरण्यम् the forest of Vrindavana, स्व-पद-रमणम् which is made enchanting by His footprints, प्राविशत् He entered, गीत-कीर्तिः He whose glories were being sung

Anvaya: नटवरवपुः बर्हापीडं कर्णयोः कर्णिकारं कनककपिशं वासः वैजयन्तीं मालां च बिभ्रत् वेणोः रन्ध्रान् अधरसुधया पूरयन् गोपवृन्दैः गीतकीर्तिः स्वपदरमणं वृन्दारण्यं प्राविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नटवरवपुः Having the form of the best of dancers, बर्हापीडं a peacock-feather crown, कर्णयोः कर्णिकारं karnikara flowers on His ears, कनककपिशं वासः golden-yellow garments, वैजयन्तीं मालां च and a Vaijayanti garland, बिभ्रत् wearing, वेणोः रन्ध्रान् the holes of the flute, अधरसुधया with the nectar of His lips, पूरयन् filling, गोपवृन्दैः गीतकीर्तिः Krishna, whose glories were being sung by the groups of cowherd boys, स्वपदरमणं वृन्दारण्यं the forest of Vrindavana which was enchanted by His own footprints, प्राविशत् He entered.

Simple meaning: Wearing a peacock-feather ornament on His head, yellow karnikara flowers on His ears, a golden-yellow garment, and a Vaijayanti garland, Lord Krishna appeared like the greatest of dancers. As He filled the holes of His flute with the nectar of His lips, the cowherd boys sang His glories. Thus, He entered the forest of Vrindavana, which was made beautiful by the marks of His divine footprints.

इति वेणुरवं राजन् सर्वभूतमनोहरम् ।

श्रुत्वा व्रजस्त्रियः सर्वा वर्णयन्त्योऽभिरेभिरे ॥10.21.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, वेणु-रवम् the sound of the flute, राजन् O King, सर्व-भूत-मनः-हरम् which steals the hearts of all living beings, श्रुत्वा hearing, व्रज-स्त्रियः the women of Vraja, सर्वाः all, वर्णयन्त्यः describing, अभिरेभिरे began/embraced (the act of narrating)

Anvaya: राजन्! इति सर्वभूतमनोहरं वेणुरवं श्रुत्वा सर्वाः व्रजस्त्रियः वर्णयन्त्यः अभिरेभिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King! इति Thus, सर्वभूतमनोहरं वेणुरवं the sound of the flute which enchants the minds of all living beings, श्रुत्वा having heard, सर्वाः व्रजस्त्रियः all the women of Vraja, वर्णयन्त्यः describing (it), अभिरेभिरे began (their narration).

Simple meaning: O King Parikshit! Having heard that sound of the flute which captivates the hearts of all living entities, all the women of Vraja began to vividly describe Krishna's beauty and His music among themselves.

गोप्यः ऊचुः

अक्षण्वतां फलमिदं न परं विदामः सख्यः पशूननु विवेशयतोर्वयस्यैः ।

वक्त्रं व्रजेशसुतयोरनुवेणु जुष्टं यैर्वा निपीतमनुरक्तकटाक्षमोक्षम् ॥10.21.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः ऊचुः The gopis said, अक्षण्वताम् of those who possess eyes, फलम् the fruit, इदम् this, न not, परम् anything else, विदामः we know, सख्यः O friends, पशून् अनु after the cows, विवेशयतोः of the two who are making (them) enter (the forest), वयस्यैः with their friends (the cowherd boys), वक्त्रम् the faces, व्रजेश-सुतयोः of the two sons (Krishna and Balarama) of the King of Vraja (Nanda), अनुवेणु-जुष्टम् joined with their flutes, यैः by which, वा or, निपीतम् imbibed/drunk, अनुरक्त-कटाक्ष-मोक्षम् emitting side-long glances of love

Anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः — हे सख्यः! वयस्यैः पशून् अनु विवेशयतोः व्रजेशसुतयोः अनुवेणुजुष्टं अनुरक्तकटाक्षमोक्षं वक्त्रं यैः निपीतं, अक्षण्वताम् इदं (एव) फलं, परं न विदामः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः The gopis said, हे सख्यः O friends, वयस्यैः with their companions, पशून् अनु along with the cows, विवेशयतोः of the two who are entering (the forest), व्रजेशसुतयोः of the two sons of Nanda Maharaja, अनुवेणुजुष्टं joined to the flute, अनुरक्तकटाक्षमोक्षं emitting loving side-long glances, वक्त्रं the face, यैः by which (eyes), निपीतं is drunk, अक्षण्वताम् of those who have eyes, इदं एव फलं this is the only fruit, परं न विदामः we do not know any other.

Simple meaning: The gopis said: O friends, for those who possess eyes, we know of no greater benefit than to gaze upon the faces of the two sons of Nanda as they enter the forest with their cows and playmates. Their faces, held to their flutes, shower loving sidelong glances upon all, to drink in such a sight is the true perfection of sight itself.

चूतप्रवालबर्हस्तबकोत्पलाब्जमालानुपृक्तपरिधानविचित्रवेषौ ।

मध्ये विरेजतुरलं पशुपालगोष्ट्यां रङ्गे यथा नटवरौ क्व च गायमानौ ॥10.21.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: चूत-प्रवाल-बर्ह-स्तबक-उत्पल-अब्ज-माला-अनुपृक्त-परिधान-विचित्र-वेषौ (dressed) in wonderful attire with garments by garlands mixed with mango sprouts, peacock feathers, flower bunches, blue lilies, and lotuses, मध्ये in the midst, विरेजतुः they both shone, अलम् greatly, पशुपाल-गोष्ट्याम् in the assembly of the cowherd boys, रङ्गे on a stage, यथा just as, नट-वरौ two master dancers, क्व च and sometimes, गायमानौ singing

चूत-mango प्रवाल- leaves/sprouts, बर्ह- peacock feathers स्तबक- bunches of flowers, उत्पल- blue lily अब्ज- lotus माला- garland, अनुपृक्त- mixed with, परिधान- clothes, विचित्र- wonderful, वेषौ wearing

Anvaya: चूतप्रवालबर्हस्तबकोत्पलाब्जमालानुपृक्तपरिधानविचित्रवेषौ (तौ) गायमानौ क्व च रङ्गे नटवरौ यथा पशुपालगोष्ट्यां मध्ये अलं विरेजतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: चूतप्रवालबर्हस्तबकोत्पलाब्जमालानुपृक्तपरिधानविचित्रवेषौ Adorned in wonderful costumes with garlands decorated by mango sprouts, peacock feathers, bouquets, blue lilies, and lotus garlands, गायमानौ singing, क्व च and sometimes, रङ्गे नटवरौ यथा like two master dancers on a stage, पशुपालगोष्ट्यां मध्ये in the midst of the assembly of cowherd boys, अलं विरेजतुः they both shone greatly.

Simple meaning: Dressed in extraordinary attire decorated with mango sprouts, peacock feathers, bunches of flowers, and garlands of lilies and lotuses, Krishna and Balarama appeared like two master dancers performing on a stage. As they sang and moved in the midst of the assembly of cowherd boys, they looked resplendently beautiful.

गोप्यः किमाचरदयं कुशलं स्म वेणुर्दामोदराधरसुधामपि गोपिकानाम् ।

भुङ्क्ते स्वयं यदवशिष्टरसं ह्रदिन्यो हृष्यत्त्वचोऽश्रुमुमुचुस्तरवो यथाऽऽर्याः ॥10.21.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः O gopis, किम् what, आचरत् performed, अयम् this, कुशलम् pious/meritorious act, स्म indeed, वेणुः the flute, दामोदर-अधर-सुधाम् the nectar of the lips of Damodara, अपि even, गोपिकानाम् (which belongs) to the gopis, भुङ्क्ते enjoys, स्वयम् itself, यत्-अवशिष्ट-रसम् of which the remaining sap/taste, ह्रदिन्यः the rivers, हृष्यत्-त्वचः whose bodies (lotuses) are standing on end with joy, अश्रु tears, मुमुचुः are shedding, तरवः the trees, यथा as, आर्याः venerable elders

Anvaya: गोप्यः! अयं वेणुः किं कुशलम् आचरत्? यत् (सः) गोपिकानाम् अपि दामोदराधरसुधां स्वयं भुङ्क्ते। यदवशिष्टरसं (दृष्ट्वा) ह्रदिन्यः हृष्यत्त्वचः (सन्ति), तरवः आर्याः यथा अश्रु मुमुचुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोप्यः O gopis, अयं वेणुः This flute, किं कुशलम् आचरत् what pious activities has it performed, यत् that, गोपिकानाम् अपि even though it belongs to the gopis, दामोदराधरसुधां the nectar of the lips of Damodara, स्वयं भुङ्क्ते it enjoys by itself. यदवशिष्टरसं The remaining taste (sap) of that, ह्रदिन्यः the rivers (seeing this), हृष्यत्त्वचः have their hair standing on end (lotuses blooming), तरवः the trees, आर्याः यथा like venerable elders, अश्रु मुमुचुः are shedding tears (of honey).

Simple meaning: O gopis! What great pious activities has this flute performed to be able to enjoy all for itself the nectar of Damodara’s lips, which rightfully belongs to us? Upon seeing the flute’s good fortune, the rivers are displaying their joy by blooming lotuses, and the trees, like venerable elders in a family, are shedding tears of honey in ecstasy.

Note: The rivers by whose waters the Bamboo trees (by which the flute was made) were nourished appear with their hair standing on end in the form of the blooming lotuses through rapture, while the Bamboo trees are shedding tears of joy in the form of honey just as the elders of a family are delighted to find their child enjoying the grace of the Lord.

वृन्दावनं सखि भुवो वितनोति कीर्तिं यद् देवकीसुतपदाम्बुजलब्धलक्ष्मि ।

गोविन्दवेणुमनुमत्तमयूरनृत्यं प्रेक्ष्याद्रिसान्वपरतान्यसमस्तसत्त्वम् ॥10.21.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: वृन्दावनम् Vrindavana, सखि O friend, भुवः of the earth, वितनोति is spreading, कीर्तिम् the fame/glory, यत् because, देवकी-सुत-पद-अम्बुज-लब्ध-लक्ष्मि (which has) obtained the treasure of the lotus feet of the son of Devaki (Krishna), गोविन्द-वेणु-अनु-मत्त-मयूर-नृत्यम् (where) the peacocks dance madly to the sound of Govinda’s flute, प्रेक्ष्य seeing (this), अद्रि-सानु-अपरत-अन्य-समस्त-सत्त्वम् all other living beings on the mountain peaks become stunned/motionless

Anvaya: सखि! यत् देवकीसुतपदाम्बुजलब्धलक्ष्मि, गोविन्दवेणुमनुमत्तमयूरनृत्यं वृन्दावनं प्रेक्ष्य अद्रिसान्वपरतअन्यसमस्तसत्त्वम् (अस्ति, तत्) भुवः कीर्तिं वितनोति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सखि O friend! यत् Because, देवकीसुतपदाम्बुजलब्धलक्ष्मि having obtained the treasure of the lotus feet of Krishna (the son of Devaki), गोविन्दवेणुमनुमत्तमयूरनृत्यं the dancing of the peacocks who are maddened by the flute of Govinda, वृन्दावनं the land of Vrindavana, प्रेक्ष्य having seen, अद्रिसान्वपरतअन्यसमस्तसत्त्वं (where) all other living entities on the mountain peaks have become stunned, (that Vrindavana) भुवः कीर्तिं वितनोति is spreading the glory of the entire earth.

Simple meaning: O friend! The land of Vrindavana is spreading the glory of the earth because it has been blessed with the treasure of the lotus feet of Krishna. When the peacocks hear Govinda’s flute, they begin to dance in ecstasy, and seeing this wonderful sight, all the other creatures living on the peaks of Govardhan Hill become completely stunned and motionless in devotion.

Note: The word ‘Devaki' occurring in the above verse stands for Yashoda. In Vishnu Purana there is the following shloka to show that Yashoda was called Devaki also — द्वे नाम्नी नन्दजायया यशोदा देवकीत्यपि

Until the death of Kamsa, Krishna was known as the son of Yashoda and Nanda only.

धन्याः स्म मूढमतयोऽपि हरिण्य एता या नन्दनन्दनमुपात्तविचित्रवेषम् ।

आकर्ण्य वेणुरणितं सहकृष्णसाराः पूजां दधुर्विरचितां प्रणयावलोकैः ॥10.21.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: धन्याः fortunate/blessed, स्म indeed, मूढ-मतयः possessing dull intelligence, अपि even, हरिण्यः the female deer, एताः these, याः who, नन्द-नन्दनम् the son of Nanda (Krishna), उपात्त-विचित्र-वेषम् who has assumed a wonderful dress, आकर्ण्य hearing, वेणु-रणितम् the vibrating sound of the flute, सह-कृष्ण-साराः along with the black deer (their husbands), पूजाम् worship, दधुः they performed/offered, विरचिताम् consisting of, प्रणय-अवलोकैः with loving glances

Anvaya: एताः मूढमतयः अपि हरिण्यः धन्याः स्म, याः उपात्तविचित्रवेषं नन्दनन्दनं वेणुरणितं च आकर्ण्य सहकृष्णसाराः प्रणयावलोकैः विरचितां पूजां दधुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एताः मूढमतयः अपि These, even though having dull intelligence, हरिण्यः the female deer, धन्याः स्म are indeed blessed, याः who, उपात्तविचित्रवेषं who has assumed a wonderful dress, वेणुरणितं च and who is playing the flute, नन्दनन्दनं the son of Nanda, आकर्ण्य hearing, सहकृष्णसाराः along with the black deer, प्रणयावलोकैः with loving glances, विरचितां पूजां दधुः offered their worship.

Simple meaning: These female deer, although possessing the dull intelligence of animals, are truly blessed. Upon hearing the vibration of the flute played by the son of Nanda dressed so wonderfully, they approach Him with their husbands, the black deer, and offer their worship through their deep, loving glances.

कृष्णं निरीक्ष्य वनितोत्सवरूपशीलं श्रुत्वा च तत्क्वणितवेणुविचित्रगीतम् ।

देव्यो विमानगतयः स्मरनुन्नसारा भ्रश्यत्प्रसूनकबरा मुमुहुर्विनीव्यः ॥10.21.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णम् Krishna, निरीक्ष्य observing, वनिता-उत्सव-रूप-शीलम् whose beauty and character are a festival for women, श्रुत्वा hearing, च and, तत्-क्वणित-वेणु-विचित्र-गीतम् the wonderful music of His sounded flute, देव्यः the goddesses/wives of the demigods, विमान-गतयः traveling in their airplanes, स्मर-नुन्न-साराः whose hearts were agitated by Cupid, भ्रश्यत्-प्रसून-कबराः the flowers falling from their hair, मुमुहुः became bewildered/fainted, विनीव्यः their belts (dress) becoming loosened

Anvaya: वनितोत्सवरूपशीलं कृष्णं निरीक्ष्य, तत्क्वणितवेणुविचित्रगीतम् श्रुत्वा च, विमानगतयः देव्यः स्मरनुन्नसाराः भ्रश्यत्प्रसूनकबराः विनीव्यः (सत्यः) मुमुहुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वनितोत्सवरूपशीलं Whose beauty and character are a festival for women, कृष्णं निरीक्ष्य seeing Krishna, तत्क्वणितवेणुविचित्रगीतं the wonderful song of His playing flute, श्रुत्वा च and hearing, विमानगतयः देव्यः the goddesses traveling in their airplanes, स्मरनुन्नसाराः their hearts pierced by the arrows of love, भ्रश्यत्प्रसूनकबराः the flowers falling from their braids, विनीव्यः and their waist-knots loosening, मुमुहुः became bewildered.

Simple meaning: Upon seeing Krishna, whose beauty and character are a festival for all women, and hearing the wonderful melody of His flute, the wives of the demigods traveling in the sky were overcome. Their hearts were so agitated by love that the flowers fell from their hair and their clothing loosened as they lost all composure in a state of bewildered ecstasy.

गावश्च कृष्णमुखनिर्गतवेणुगीतपीयूषमुत्तभितकर्णपुटैः पिबन्त्यः ।

शावाः स्नुतस्तनपयःकवलाः स्म तस्थुर्गोविन्दमात्मनि दृशाश्रुकलाः स्पृशन्त्यः ॥10.21.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: गावः the cows, च and, कृष्ण-मुख-निर्गत-वेणु-गीत-पीयूषम् the nectar of the flute-song coming from the mouth of Krishna, उत्तभित-कर्ण-पुटैः with their ears raised like cups, पिबन्त्यः drinking, शावाः the calves, स्नुत-स्तन-पयः-कवलाः the milk flowing from the udders (of the cows), (and the calves having) mouthfuls of milk, स्म indeed, तस्थुः stood still, गोविन्दम् Lord Govinda, आत्मनि within their hearts, दृशा with their eyes, अश्रु-कलाः with a flow of tears, स्पृशन्त्यः touching

Anvaya: गावः च उत्तभितकर्णपुटैः कृष्णमुखनिर्गतवेणुगीतपीयूषं पिबन्त्यः, शावाः स्नुतस्तनपयःकवलाः (सन्तः) दृशा गोविन्दम् आत्मनि स्पृशन्त्यः अश्रुकलाः (सत्यः) तस्थुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गावः च And the cows, उत्तभितकर्णपुटैः with their ears raised up like cups, कृष्णमुखनिर्गतवेणुगीतपीयूषं the nectar of the flute-music exiting Krishna's mouth, पिबन्त्यः drinking, शावाः the calves, स्नुतस्तनपयःकवलाः with mouthfuls of milk that had flowed from the udders, दृशा with their eyes (and) गोविन्दम् आत्मनि स्पृशन्त्यः touching Govinda within their hearts, अश्रुकलाः with tears in their eyes, तस्थुः stood still.

Simple meaning: The cows drank the nectar of the flute-song flowing from Krishna’s mouth by using their raised ears as cups. Their calves, even with mouthfuls of milk from their mothers’ udders, stood perfectly still, unable to swallow. With tears of joy in their eyes, they all embraced Govinda within their hearts through their steady gaze.

प्रायो बताम्ब विहगा मुनयो वनेऽस्मिन् कृष्णेक्षितं तदुदितं कलवेणुगीतम् ।

आरुह्य ये द्रुमभुजान् रुचिरप्रवालान् शृण्वन्त्यमीलितदृशो विगतान्यवाचः ॥10.21.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रायः-बत most certainly, अम्ब O mother, विहगाः the birds, मुनयः sages, वने अस्मिन् in this forest, कृष्ण-ईक्षितम् directed toward Krishna, तत्-उदितम् which has risen, कल-वेणु-गीतम् the sweet song of the flute, आरुह्य having climbed, ये who, द्रुम-भुजान् the branches (arms) of the trees, रुचिर-प्रवालान् possessing beautiful sprouts, शृण्वन्ति they listen, अमीलित-दृशः with unblinking eyes, विगत-अन्य-वाचः having ceased all other sounds

Anvaya: अम्ब! अस्मिन् वने विहगाः प्रायः मुनयः (सन्ति), ये रुचिरप्रवालान् द्रुमभुजान् आरुह्य अमीलितदृशः विगतान्यवाचः (सन्तः) कृष्णेक्षितं तदुदितं कलवेणुगीतं शृण्वन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अम्ब O mother, अस्मिन् वने In this forest, विहगाः the birds, प्रायः मुनयः बत are most likely sages, ये who, रुचिरप्रवालान् द्रुमभुजान् the tree branches with beautiful sprouts, आरुह्य having climbed, अमीलितदृशः with eyes unblinking, विगतान्यवाचः having given up all other speech, कृष्णेक्षितं looking at Krishna, तदुदितं कलवेणुगीतं शृण्वन्ति listen to that sweet flute-song which has arisen.

Simple meaning: O mother! Most certainly, the birds in this forest of Vrindavana must be great sages. Having perched themselves on the beautiful, leafy branches of the trees, they sit with unblinking eyes and total silence, completely absorbed in looking at Krishna and listening to the sweet vibration of His flute.

Note: The friends address each other as mother or ma sometimes.

नद्यस्तदा तदुपधार्य मुकुन्दगीतमावर्तलक्षितमनोभवभग्नवेगाः ।

आलिङ्गनस्थगितमूर्मिभुजैर्मुरारेर्गृह्णन्ति पादयुगलं कमलोपहाराः ॥10.21.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: नद्यः the rivers, तदा then, तत्-उपधार्य hearing that, मुकुन्द-गीतम् the song of Mukunda (the flute-music), आवर्त-लक्षित-मनोभव-भग्न-वेगाः whose current was broken by the desire (love) manifest in their whirlpools, आलिङ्गन-स्थगितम् held in an embrace, ऊर्मि-भुजैः by the arms of their waves, मुरारेः of Murari (Krishna), गृह्णन्ति they seize/hold, पाद-युगलम् the two feet, कमल-उपहाराः offering lotuses

Anvaya: नद्यः तदा तत् मुकुन्दगीतम् उपधार्य आवर्तलक्षितमनोभवभग्नवेगाः (सत्यः) ऊर्मिभुजैः मुरारेः पादयुगलं गृह्णन्ति, कमलोपहाराः (च) आलिङ्गनस्थगितम् (कुर्वन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नद्यः The rivers, तदा then, तत् मुकुन्दगीतम् उपधार्य having heard that song of Mukunda, आवर्तलक्षितमनोभवभग्नवेगाः their currents broken by the surge of love manifest in their whirlpools, ऊर्मिभुजैः with the arms of their waves, मुरारेः पादयुगलं गृह्णन्ति grasp the two feet of Murari, कमलोपहाराः offering lotuses, आलिङ्गनस्थगितम् (and) hold Him in an embrace.

Simple meaning: Upon hearing the music of Mukunda’s flute, the rivers find their currents interrupted as they develop whirlpools born of their intense love for Him. Using the arms of their waves, they seize the lotus feet of Krishna to offer Him lotuses and hold Him in a steady embrace.

दृष्ट्वाऽऽतपे व्रजपशून् सह रामगोपैः सञ्चारयन्तमनुवेणुमुदीरयन्तम् ।

प्रेमप्रवृद्ध उदितः कुसुमावलीभिः सख्युर्व्यधात् स्ववपुषाम्बुद आतपत्रम् ॥10.21.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्ट्वा seeing, आतपे in the heat of the sun, व्रज-पशून् the animals of Vraja, सह with, राम-गोपैः Balarama and the cowherd boys, सञ्चारयन्तम् moving/wandering, अनुवेणुम् on His flute, उदीरयन्तम् vibrating/playing, प्रेम-प्रवृद्धः increased by love, उदितः arising, कुसुम-आवलीभिः with a multitude of flowers (small water droplets), सख्युः of His friend (Krishna), व्यधात् expanded/made, स्व-वपुषा with his own body, अम्बुदः the cloud, आतपत्रम् an umbrella

Anvaya: आतपे रामगोपैः सह व्रजपशून् सञ्चारयन्तम् अनुवेणुम् उदीरयन्तं (कृष्णं) दृष्ट्वा प्रेमप्रवृद्धः अम्बुदः उदितः (सन्) कुसुमावलीभिः स्ववपुषा सख्युः आतपत्रं व्यधात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आतपे In the heat of the sun, रामगोपैः सह along with Balarama and the cowherds, व्रजपशून् सञ्चारयन्तम् herding the animals of Vraja, अनुवेणुम् उदीरयन्तं vibrating His flute, (Krishna) दृष्ट्वा having seen, प्रेमप्रवृद्धः swollen with love, अम्बुदः the cloud, उदितः having arisen, कुसुमावलीभिः with its flower-like droplets, स्ववपुषा with its own body, सख्युः for his friend, आतपत्रं व्यधात् made an umbrella.

Simple meaning: Seeing Krishna wandering in the summer heat while herding the cows and playing His flute along with Balarama and the cowherd boys, a cloud rose up in the sky out of intense love. By expanding its own body and raining down tiny, flower-like droplets, the cloud served as a cooling umbrella for its dear friend.

पूर्णाः पुलिन्द्य उरुगायपदाब्जरागश्रीकुङ्कमेन दयितास्तनमण्डितेन ।

तद्दर्शनस्मररुजस्तृणरूषितेन लिम्पन्त्य आननकुचेषु जहुस्तदाधिम् ॥10.21.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: पूर्णाः fulfilled/successful, पुलिन्द्यः the women of the Pulinda tribe, उरुगाय-पद-अब्ज-राग-श्री-कुङ्कुमेन by the beautiful vermilion powder from the lotus feet of the one whose glories are sung by sages (Lord Krishna), दयिता-स्तन-मण्डितेन which had decorated the breasts of His beloveds, तत्-दर्शन-स्मर-रुजः whose hearts were pained by Cupid upon seeing Him, तृण-रूषितेन which was smeared on the blades of grass, लिम्पन्त्यः smearing, आनन-कुचेषु on their faces and breasts, जहुः they gave up, तद्-आधिम् that mental agony

उरुगाय उरुगाय वि. उरु-गाय - उत्तम व्यक्तियों द्वारा जिसका स्तुतिगान किया गया हो src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: पुलिन्द्यः दयितास्तनमण्डितेन तृणरूषितेन उरुगायपदाब्जरागश्रीकुङ्कुमेन (सिक्ताः सत्यः), तद्दर्शनस्मररुजः (ताः) आननकुचेषु लिम्पन्त्यः तदाधिं जहुः, (अतः ताः) पूर्णाः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पुलिन्द्यः The women of the Pulinda tribe, उरुगायपदाब्जरागश्रीकुङ्कुमेन with the beautiful red kunkuma from the lotus feet of Krishna, दयितास्तनमण्डितेन which had decorated the breasts of His beloveds, तृणरूषितेन (and was now) smeared upon the grass, तद्दर्शनस्मररुजः they whose hearts felt the pain of love upon seeing Him, आननकुचेषु लिम्पन्त्यः smearing (that powder) on their faces and breasts, तदाधिं जहुः gave up their mental distress, (अतः hence) पूर्णाः they became fulfilled.

Simple meaning: The women of the Pulinda tribe felt great transcendental desire upon seeing Krishna. When He wandered through the forest, the red kunkuma from His lotus feet—originally from the breasts of His beloveds—was left upon the blades of grass. By smearing that powder on their own faces and breasts, these women extinguished the fire of separation in their hearts and felt completely fulfilled.

हन्तायमद्रिरबला हरिदासवर्यो यद् रामकृष्णचरणस्पर्शप्रमोदः।

मानं तनोति सहगोगणयोस्तयोर्यत् पानीयसूयवसकन्दरकन्दमूलैः ॥10.21.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: हन्त Oh!, अयम् this, अद्रिः mountain (Govardhana), अबलाः O girls/friends, हरि-दास-वर्यः the best among the servants of Hari, यत् because, राम-कृष्ण-चरण-स्पर्श-प्रमोदः feeling jubilation from the touch of the lotus feet of Balarama and Krishna, मानम् respect/hospitality, तनोति extends/offers, सह-गो-गणयोः along with the cows and calves, तयोः for those two, यत् since, पानीय-सूयवस-कन्दर-कन्द-मूलैः with water, very soft grass, caves, and edible roots

Anvaya: अबलाः! अयम् अद्रिः हन्त हरिदासवर्यः, यत् रामकृष्णचरणस्पर्शप्रमोदः (सन्) सहगोगणयोः तयोः पानीयसूयवसकन्दरकन्दमूलैः मानं तनोति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अबलाः O girls!, अयम् अद्रिः This mountain (Govardhana), हन्त indeed, हरिदासवर्यः is the best of the servants of Lord Hari, यत् because, रामकृष्णचरणस्पर्शप्रमोदः feeling ecstatic joy from the touch of the feet of Balarama and Krishna, सहगोगणयोः तयोः for those two along with the cows and cowherd boys, पानीयसूयवसकन्दरकन्दमूलैः by providing water, lush grass, caves, and roots, मानं तनोति offers respectful hospitality.

Simple meaning: O friends! This Govardhana Hill is the best among the servants of Lord Hari! Being jubilant from the touch of the lotus feet of Krishna and Balarama, the mountain offers great respect to Them and Their cows and cowherd friends by providing pure water, soft grass, caves for resting, and delicious roots to eat.

गा गोपकैरनुवनं नयतोरुदारवेणुस्वनैः कलपदैस्तनुभृत्सु सख्यः ।

अस्पन्दनं गतिमतां पुलकस्तरूणां निर्योगपाशकृतलक्षणयोर्विचित्रम् ॥10.21.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: गाः the cows, गोपकैः with the cowherd boys, अनुवनम् from forest to forest, नयतोः of the two (Krishna and Balarama) who are leading, उदार-वेणु-स्वनैः by the resonant vibrations of the flute, कल-पदैः with sweet tones, तनु-भृत्सु among the living entities (the embodied), सख्यः O friends, अस्पन्दनम् lack of movement (stunned), गतिमताम् of those who possess the ability to move, पुलकः standing of hair on end (ecstasy), तरूणाम् of the trees, निर्योग-पाश-कृत-लक्षणयोः of the two who are decorated with the ropes for binding the rear legs of cows, विचित्रम् wonderful/extraordinary

Anvaya: सख्यः! निर्योगपाशकृतलक्षणयोः (तयोः) गोपकैः सह अनुवनं गाः नयतोः कलपदैः उदारवेणुस्वनैः (हेतुना) तनुभृत्सु गतिमताम् अस्पन्दनं तरूणां च पुलकः (भवति, एतत्) विचित्रम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सख्यः O friends!, निर्योगपाशकृतलक्षणयोः Of the two who are marked/decorated with ropes for tying the cows, गोपकैः सह with the cowherd boys, अनुवनं गाः नयतोः who are leading the cows through the various forests, कलपदैः उदारवेणुस्वनैः with sweet resonant flute-sounds, तनुभृत्सु among the living beings, गतिमताम् अस्पन्दनं the lack of movement of those who usually move, तरूणां च पुलकः and the hair-standing-on-end (ecstatic trembling) of the trees, विचित्रम् is truly wonderful.

Simple meaning: O friends! Along with the cowherd boys as Krishna and Balarama distinguished by carrying the ropes for binding the cows, lead the cows through the forests, they play sweet, resonant melodies on their flutes. It is truly extraordinary to see that, upon hearing this music, the moving creatures of the forest become completely still and stunned, while the stationary trees begin to tremble and "bristle" with ecstatic joy.

एवंविधा भगवतो या वृन्दावनचारिणः ।

वर्णयन्त्यो मिथो गोप्यः क्रीडास्तन्मयतां ययुः ॥10.21.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवं-विधाः of this kind, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, याः which, वृन्दावन-चारिणः who wanders in Vrindavana, वर्णयन्त्यः describing, मिथः among one another, गोप्यः the gopis, क्रीडाः the pastimes, तत्-मयताम् a state of absorption in Him, ययुः attained

Anvaya: वृन्दावनचारिणः भगवतः एवंविधाः याः क्रीडाः (सन्ति), ताः (क्रीडाः) मिथः वर्णयन्त्यः गोप्यः तन्मयतां ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वृन्दावनचारिणः Of the one who wandered in Vrindavana, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, एवंविधाः याः क्रीडाः such pastimes which exist, ताः मिथः वर्णयन्त्यः describing those among themselves, गोप्यः the gopis, तन्मयतां ययुः attained a state of being fully absorbed in Him.

Simple meaning: In this way, by describing to one another the various wonderful pastimes of the Supreme Lord as He wandered through the groves of Vrindavana, the gopis became so completely lost in their love for Him that they attained a state of total meditative absorption.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमे स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे वेणुगीतं नाम एकविंशः अध्यायः ॥21॥

Thus ends the twenty-first discourse named 'A song inspired by the (Lord's) Flute,' in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
