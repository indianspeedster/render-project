ॐ

॥ॐ॥

Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 28

Shri Krishna rescues Nanda from the realm of Varuna

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

कृष्णः वरुणलोकात् नन्दम् उद्धरति नाम अष्टाविंशः अध्यायः

(17 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

एकादश्यां निराहारः समभ्यर्च्य जनार्दनम् ।

स्नातुं नन्दस्तु कालिन्द्या द्वादश्यां जलमाविशत् ॥10.28.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एकादश्याम् on the eleventh day (of the lunar fortnight), निराहारः fasting (without food), समभ्यर्च्य having worshiped properly, जनार्दनम् Lord Janardana (Krishna/Vishnu), स्नातुम् for the purpose of bathing, नन्दः Nanda, तु then, कालिन्द्याः in the river Yamuna (Kalindi), द्वादश्याम् on the twelfth day, जलम् आविशत् entered the water

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच— एकादश्यां निराहारः जनार्दनं समभ्यर्च्य, द्वादश्यां नन्दः तु स्नातुं कालिन्द्याः जलम् आविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— एकादश्यां निराहारः Fasting on the Ekadashi day, जनार्दनं समभ्यर्च्य and having duly worshiped Lord Janardana, द्वादश्यां नन्दः तु then on the Dwadashi (the twelfth day), Nanda, स्नातुं कालिन्द्याः जलम् आविशत् entered the waters of the Yamuna to take his ritual bath.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Having observed a complete fast on the Ekadashi day and having worshiped Lord Janardana with great devotion, Nanda Maharaja entered the waters of the Yamuna early on the Dwadashi morning to take his ritual bath.

तं गृहीत्वानयद् भृत्यो वरुणस्यासुरोऽन्तिकम् ।

अविज्ञायासुरीं वेलां प्रविष्टमुदकं निशि ॥10.28.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him (Nanda Maharaja), गृहीत्वा having seized, अनयत् brought, भृत्यः a servant, वरुणस्य of Varuna, असुरः an asura, अन्तिकम् into the presence, अविज्ञाय not knowing, आसुरीम् inauspicious, वेलाम् the time, प्रविष्टम् who had entered, उदकम् the water, निशि at night

Anvaya: वरुणस्य भृत्यः असुरः, निशि आसुरीं वेलाम् अविज्ञाय उदकं प्रविष्टं तं गृहीत्वा (वरुणस्य) अन्तिकम् अनयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वरुणस्य भृत्यः असुरः A servant of Varuna, who was an asura, निशि आसुरीं वेलाम् अविज्ञाय not realizing it was the prohibited demoniac hour of the night, उदकं प्रविष्टं तं गृहीत्वा seized him (Nanda) who had entered the water, (वरुणस्य) अन्तिकम् अनयत् and brought him into the presence of Varuna.

Simple Meaning: A servant of Varuna, belonging to the asura class, seized Nanda while he was bathing in the river. Because Nanda had entered the water at an inauspicious hour of the night, the servant—unaware of Nanda's identity—captured him and took him to Lord Varuna.

चक्रुशुस्तमपश्यन्तः कृष्ण रामेति गोपकाः ।

भगवांस्तदुपश्रुत्य पितरं वरुणाहृतम् ।

तदन्तिकं गतो राजन् स्वानामभयदो विभुः ॥10.28.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: चक्रुशुः they cried out loudly, तम् Nanda, अपश्यन्तः not seeing, कृष्ण Krishna, राम Rama, इति thus, गोपकाः the cowherd men, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, तत् that (outcry), उपश्रुत्य hearing, पितरम् His father, वरुण-आहृतम् taken away by Varuna, तत्-अन्तिकम् into his (Varuna's) presence, गतः went, राजन् O King (Parikshit), स्वानाम् to His own devotees/people, अभय-दः the giver of fearlessness, विभुः the Almighty Lord

Anvaya: राजन्! तम् अपश्यन्तः गोपकाः 'कृष्ण राम' इति चक्रुशुः। स्वानाम् अभयदः विभुः भगवान् तत् उपश्रुत्य वरुणाहृतं पितरं (विज्ञाय) तदन्तिकं गतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King!, तम् अपश्यन्तः गोपकाः The cowherd men, not seeing him (Nanda), 'कृष्ण राम' इति चक्रुशुः cried out "O Krishna! O Rama!". स्वानाम् अभयदः विभुः भगवान् The Almighty Lord, the giver of fearlessness to His own people, तत् उपश्रुत्य hearing that outcry, वरुणाहृतं पितरं (विज्ञाय) (understanding) that His father had been taken by Varuna, तदन्तिकं गतः went into his (Varuna's) presence.

Simple Meaning: O King, when the cowherd men could no longer see Nanda Maharaja, they began to cry out loudly, "O Krishna! O Rama!" Lord Krishna, the Almighty protector who removes the fear of His devotees, heard their distressed cries. Realizing that His father had been taken away by Varuna, He immediately went to Varuna's abode.

प्राप्तं वीक्ष्य हृषीकेशं लोकपालः सपर्यया ।

महत्या पूजयित्वाऽऽह तद्दर्शनमहोत्सवः ॥10.28.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्राप्तम् having arrived, वीक्ष्य seeing, हृषीकेशम् Hrishikesha (Krishna, the Lord of the senses), लोक-पालः the guardian of the planet (Varuna), सपर्यया with offerings, महत्या great, पूजयित्वा having worshiped, आह said, तत्-दर्शन-महोत्सवः taking great pleasure as if in a festival by seeing Him

Anvaya: लोकपालः प्राप्तं हृषीकेशं वीक्ष्य महत्या सपर्यया पूजयित्वा तद्दर्शनमहोत्सवः (सन्) आह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: लोकपालः The guardian of the quarters (Varuna), प्राप्तं हृषीकेशं वीक्ष्य seeing that Lord Hrishikesha had arrived, महत्या सपर्यया पूजयित्वा and having worshiped Him with elaborate ritual offerings, तद्दर्शनमहोत्सवः (सन्) आह feeling as if he were celebrating a great festival upon seeing Him, spoke as follows.

Simple Meaning: Upon seeing that Lord Krishna had arrived, Varuna, the guardian of the seas, worshiped Him with great ceremony and magnificent offerings. Feeling the immense joy of a grand festival simply by seeing the Lord, Varuna spoke the following words.

वरुण उवाच

अद्य मे निभृतो देहोऽद्यैवार्थोऽधिगतः प्रभो ।

त्वत्पादभाजो भगवन्नवापुः पारमध्वनः ॥10.28.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: वरुणः उवाच Varuna said, अद्य today, मे my, निभृतः successful/fulfilled, देहः body, अद्य today, एव certainly, अर्थः the goal of life, अधिगतः attained, प्रभो O Lord, त्वत्-पाद-भाजः those who serve Your lotus feet, भगवन् O Supreme Lord, अवापुः have reached, पारम् the end/limit, अध्वनः of the path (of material existence)

Anvaya: प्रभो! अद्य मे देहः निभृतः, अद्य एव अर्थः अधिगतः। भगवन्! त्वत्पादभाजः अध्वनः पारम् अवापुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रभो O Lord!, अद्य मे देहः निभृतः Today my body has become fruitful, अद्य एव अर्थः अधिगतः today indeed the purpose of my life has been achieved. भगवन् O Supreme Lord!, त्वत्पादभाजः Those who are devoted to Your lotus feet, अध्वनः पारम् अवापुः have reached the end of the path of worldly wandering.

Simple Meaning: Varuna said: O Lord, today my birth has become truly successful and my life has reached its ultimate goal. O Supreme Lord, those who serve Your lotus feet have crossed to the other side of the path of material existence.

नमस्तुभ्यं भगवते ब्रह्मणे परमात्मने ।

न यत्र श्रूयते माया लोकसृष्टिविकल्पना ॥10.28.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, तुभ्यम् unto You, भगवते to the Supreme Personality of Godhead, ब्रह्मणे to the Absolute Truth, परमात्मने to the Supersoul, न not, यत्र where, श्रूयते is heard (seen/perceived), माया the illusory energy, लोक-सृष्टि-विकल्पना which creates the variegatedness of the material worlds

Anvaya: भगवते ब्रह्मणे परमात्मने तुभ्यं नमः, यत्र लोकसृष्टिविकल्पना माया न श्रूयते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवते ब्रह्मणे परमात्मने तुभ्यं नमः Obeisances unto You, the Supreme Lord, the Absolute Truth, and the Supersoul, यत्र लोकसृष्टिविकल्पना माया न श्रूयते in Whom the illusory energy—which creates the false transformations and variety of this world—is never heard of.

Simple Meaning: I offer my respectful obeisances unto You, the Supreme Lord, the Absolute Truth, and the Supersoul. You are entirely free from the influence of the illusory energy (Maya), which creates the diverse manifestations of the material universe.

अजानता मामकेन मूढेनाकार्यवेदिना ।

आनीतोऽयं तव पिता तद् भवान् क्षन्तुमर्हसि ॥10.28.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: अजानता by one who is ignorant, मामकेन by my (servant), मूढेन by the foolish one, अ-कार्य-वेदिना who does not know what should not be done, आनीतः brought, अयम् this, तव Your, पिता father, तत् that (act), भवान् You, क्षन्तुम् to forgive, अर्हसि should/ought

Anvaya: अजानता अकार्यवेदिना मूढेण मामकेन तव अयं पिता आनीतः, तत् भवान् क्षन्तुम् अर्हसि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अजानता अकार्यवेदिना By an ignorant person who does not know right from wrong, मूढेण मामकेन (namely) my foolish servant, तव अयं पिता आनीतः this father of Yours was brought here, तत् भवान् क्षन्तुम् अर्हसि therefore, You ought to forgive that act.

Simple Meaning: My foolish servant was ignorant of Your father's identity and did not understand that he was committing an improper act by bringing him here. Therefore, O Lord, please be merciful and forgive this mistake.

ममाप्यनुग्रहं कृष्ण कर्तुमर्हस्यशेषदृक् ।

गोविन्द नीयतामेष पिता ते पितृवत्सल ॥10.28.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: मम my, अपि also, अनुग्रहम् favor/mercy, कृष्ण O Krishna, कर्तुम् to do, अर्हसि You ought, अशेष-दृक् O Witness of everything (all-seeing), गोविन्द O Govinda, नीयताम् please take, एषः this, पिता father, ते Your, पितृ-वत्सल O You who are very affectionate to Your father

Anvaya: अशेषदृक् कृष्ण! मम अपि अनुग्रहं कर्तुम् अर्हसि। पितृवत्सल गोविन्द! ते एषः पिता नीयताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अशेषदृक् कृष्ण O all-seeing Krishna! मम अपि अनुग्रहं कर्तुम् अर्हसि You should also show Your mercy to me. पितृवत्सल गोविन्द O Govinda, who are so fond of Your parents! ते एषः पिता नीयताम् please take this father of Yours back with You.

Simple Meaning: O all-seeing Lord Krishna, please bestow Your grace upon me as well. O Govinda, You are so affectionately devoted to Your father; please take him back home now.

श्री शुक उवाच

एवं प्रसादितः कृष्णो भगवानीश्वरेश्वरः ।

आदायागात् स्वपितरं बन्धूनां चावहन् मुदम् ॥10.28.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एवम् in this way, प्रसादितः being propitiated/pleased, कृष्णः Krishna, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, ईश्वर-ईश्वरः the Lord of lords, आदाय taking, अगात् went/returned, स्व-पितरम् His own father, बन्धूनाम् of His relatives, च and, आवहन् bringing, मुदम् joy

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच— ईश्वरेश्वरः भगवान् कृष्णः एवं प्रसादितः (सन्) स्वपितरम् आदाय अगात्, बन्धूनां च मुदम् आवहन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— ईश्वरेश्वरः भगवान् कृष्णः Lord Krishna, the Supreme Lord and the Lord of all lords, एवं प्रसादितः having been thus satisfied (by Varuna's prayers), स्वपितरम् आदाय अगात् took His father and left, बन्धूनां च मुदम् आवहन् bringing great joy to His relatives.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Thus satisfied by the prayers of Varuna, Lord Krishna, the Supreme Lord and the Lord of all lords, took His father and returned home, bringing immense joy to all His relatives.

नन्दस्त्वतीन्द्रियं दृष्ट्वा लोकपालमहोदयम् ।

कृष्णे च सन्नतिं तेषां ज्ञातिभ्यो विस्मितोऽब्रवीत् ॥10.28.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्दः Nanda, तु indeed, अति-इन्द्रियम् beyond the reach of the senses (supernatural), दृष्ट्वा having seen, लोक-पाल-महा-उदयम् the great opulence of the guardian of the world (Varuna), कृष्णे unto Krishna, च and, सन्नतिम् the bowing down/prostration, तेषाम् of them (Varuna and his followers), ज्ञातिभ्यः to his kinsmen (the cowherd men), विस्मितः amazed, अब्रवीत् spoke

Anvaya: नन्दः तु अतीन्द्रियं लोकपालमहोदयं तेषां कृष्णे सन्नतिं च दृष्ट्वा विस्मितः (सन्) ज्ञातिभ्यः अब्रवीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्दः तु Nanda indeed, अतीन्द्रियं लोकपालमहोदयं the supernatural and grand opulence of the world-guardian (Varuna), तेषां कृष्णे सन्नतिं च दृष्ट्वा and seeing their humble prostrations before Krishna, विस्मितः (सन्) being struck with wonder, ज्ञातिभ्यः अब्रवीत् spoke to his relatives.

Simple Meaning: Nanda was truly amazed. Having witnessed the supernatural opulence of the planetary guardian Varuna and seeing how Varuna and his associates bowed down in deep respect before Krishna, he returned to his kinsmen and spoke to them about his incredible experience.

ते त्वौत्सुक्यधियो राजन् मत्वा गोपास्तमीश्वरम् ।

अपि नः स्वगतिं सूक्ष्मामुपाधास्यदधीश्वरः ॥10.28.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: ते they, तु indeed, औत्सुक्य-धियः their minds filled with eagerness, राजन् O King (Parikshit), मत्वा thinking/considering, तम् Him (Krishna), ईश्वरम् the Supreme Lord, अपि whether, नः to us, स्व-गतिम् His own abode/attainment, सूक्ष्माम‍् subtle (transcendental), उपाधास्यत् would award, अधीश्वरः the Supreme Controller

Anvaya: राजन्! ते गोपाः तु औत्सुक्यधियः तम् ईश्वरं मत्वा (चिन्तयामासुः)— 'अधीश्वरः नः सूक्ष्मां स्वगतिम् अपि उपाधास्यत्?'

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King! ते गोपाः तु those cowherd men indeed, औत्सुक्यधियः with minds full of anticipation, तम् ईश्वरं मत्वा considering Him to be the Supreme Lord, (thought)— अधीश्वरः will the Supreme Controller, नः सूक्ष्मां स्वगतिम् अपि उपाधास्यत् also award us His own subtle, transcendental destination?

Simple Meaning: O King, hearing Nanda’s account, the cowherd men became very eager. Thinking of Krishna as the Supreme Lord, they wondered among themselves whether the Almighty Controller would eventually grant them a vision of His own transcendental abode.

इति स्वानां स भगवान् विज्ञायाखिलदृक् स्वयम् ।

सङ्कल्पसिद्धये तेषां कृपयैतदचिन्तयत् ॥10.28.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, स्वानाम् of His own devotees, सः He, भगवान् the Supreme Personality of Godhead, विज्ञाय understanding, अखिल-दृक् the seer of everything, स्वयम् Himself, सङ्कल्प-सिद्धये for the fulfillment of the desire, तेषाम् of them, कृपया out of mercy, एतत् this, अचिन्तयत् thought

Anvaya: अखिलदृक् सः भगवान् स्वयं स्वानाम् इति विज्ञाय, तेषां सङ्कल्पसिद्धये कृपया एतत् अचिन्तयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अखिलदृक् सः भगवान् He, the Supreme Lord, who is the seer of everything, स्वयं स्वानाम् इति विज्ञाय Himself understanding thus the thoughts of His own people, तेषां सङ्कल्पसिद्धये for the fulfillment of their desires, कृपया एतत् अचिन्तयत् out of mercy thought as follows.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna, the all-seeing Supreme Lord, understood the inner desires of His devotees. Out of His natural compassion and wanting to fulfill their yearning, He began to consider how to show them His transcendental abode.

जनो वै लोक एतस्मिन्नविद्याकामकर्मभिः ।

उच्चावचासु गतिषु न वेद स्वां गतिं भ्रमन् ॥10.28.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: जनः people/living beings, वै indeed, लोके एतस्मिन् in this world, अविद्या-काम-कर्मभिः by ignorance, desires, and fruitive activities, उच्च-अवचासु in higher and lower, गतिषु in conditions of life/species, न वेद do not know, स्वाम् their own, गतिम् destination/true goal, भ्रमन् wandering

Anvaya: एतस्मिन् लोके जनः वै अविद्याकामकर्मभिः उच्चावचासु गतिषु भ्रमन् स्वां गतिं न वेद।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एतस्मिन् लोके जनः वै In this world, the living beings indeed, अविद्याकामकर्मभिः due to ignorance, lusty desires, and the results of their activities, उच्चावचासु गतिषु भ्रमन् wandering through higher and lower species of life, स्वां गतिं न वेद do not know their own true destination.

Simple Meaning: In this material world, people are driven by ignorance, material desires, and the consequences of their actions. As they wander through various higher and lower forms of life, they remain unaware of their true spiritual destination and the ultimate goal of their existence.

इति सञ्चिन्त्य भगवान् महाकारुणिको हरिः ।

दर्शयामास लोकं स्वं गोपानां तमसः परम् ॥10.28.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, सञ्चिन्त्य considering/thinking, भगवान् the Supreme Personality of Godhead, महा-कारुणिकः the most compassionate, हरिः Lord Hari, दर्शयामास showed, लोकम् the planet/abode, स्वम् His own, गोपानाम् to the cowherd men, तमसः darkness/ignorance, परम् beyond

Anvaya: इति सञ्चिन्त्य महाकारुणिकः भगवान् हरिः गोपानां तमसः परं स्वं लोकं दर्शयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति सञ्चिन्त्य Thinking thus, महाकारुणिकः भगवान् हरिः the supremely compassionate Lord Hari, गोपानां to the cowherd men, तमसः परं स्वं लोकं दर्शयामास showed His own abode, which is situated beyond the darkness of material ignorance.

Simple Meaning: Having considered the situation in this way, the supremely compassionate Lord Hari showed the cowherd men His own transcendental abode, which lies far beyond the reach of material darkness and ignorance.

सत्यं ज्ञानमनन्तं यद् ब्रह्म ज्योतिः सनातनम् ।

यद्धि पश्यन्ति मुनयो गुणापाये समाहिताः ॥10.28.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: सत्यम् truth/existence, ज्ञानम् knowledge, अनन्तम् infinite, यत् which, ब्रह्म the Absolute Truth (Brahman), ज्योतिः effulgence/light, सनातनम् eternal, यत् which, हि indeed, पश्यन्ति see, मुनयः sages, गुण-अपाये when the modes of nature (gunas) are removed, समाहिताः absorbed in trance

Anvaya: यत् सत्यं ज्ञानम् अनन्तं सनातनं ज्योतिः ब्रह्म (अस्ति), यत् हि मुनयः गुणापाये समाहिताः (सन्तः) पश्यन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत् सत्यं ज्ञानम् अनन्तं That which is Truth, Knowledge, and Infinite, सनातनं ज्योतिः ब्रह्म (अस्ति) the eternal effulgent Brahman, यत् हि मुनयः which indeed the sages, गुणापाये when the material modes have subsided, समाहिताः (सन्तः) पश्यन्ति see while absorbed in spiritual trance.

Simple Meaning: That Absolute Truth is eternal, infinite, and the very embodiment of knowledge and light. It is this supreme, effulgent reality that great sages perceive when they are free from the influence of the material modes of nature and fully absorbed in meditative trance.

ते तु ब्रह्मह्रदम् नीता मग्नाः कृष्णेन चोद्धृताः ।

ददृशुर्ब्रह्मणो लोकं यत्राक्रूरोऽध्यगात् पुरा ॥10.26.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: ते they (the cowherd men), तु indeed, ब्रह्म-ह्रदम् to the lake known as Brahma-hrada, नीताः led, मग्नाः submerged, कृष्णेन by Krishna, च and, उद्धृताः lifted out, ददृशुः they saw, ब्रह्मणः of the Absolute (Brahman), लोकम् the spiritual world/abode, यत्र where, अक्रूरः Akrura, अध्यगात् saw/attained, पुरा previously

Anvaya: ते तु कृष्णेन ब्रह्मह्रदं नीताः मग्नाः च (ततः) उद्धृताः ब्रह्मणः लोकं ददृशुः, यत्र पुरा अक्रूरः अध्यगात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते तु कृष्णेन ब्रह्मह्रदं नीताः They indeed were led by Krishna to the Brahma-hrada, मग्नाः च (ततः) उद्धृताः and after they had dipped into the water and were lifted out, ब्रह्मणः लोकं ददृशुः they saw the spiritual abode of the Absolute, यत्र पुरा अक्रूरः अध्यगात् which Akrura had previously seen.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna led the cowherd men to the lake known as Brahma-hrada. After they submerged themselves in the water and were lifted out by Him, they were granted a vision of the spiritual kingdom of the Absolute—the same divine abode that Akrura had been shown in the past.

नन्दादयस्तु तं दृष्ट्वा परमानन्दनिर्वृताः ।

कृष्णं च तत्रच्छन्दोभिः स्तूयमानं सुविस्मिताः ॥10.28.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्दादयः Nanda and the others, तु indeed, तम् Him (the spiritual abode), दृष्ट्वा having seen, परमानन्द-निर्वृताः filled with supreme bliss, कृष्णम् Krishna, च and, तत्र there (in that abode), छन्दोभिः by the personified Vedas/hymns, स्तूयमानम् being praised, सु-विस्मिताः greatly amazed

Anvaya: नन्दादयः तु तं दृष्ट्वा परमानन्दनिर्वृताः (बभूवुः), तत्र छन्दोभिः स्तूयमानं कृष्णं च (दृष्ट्वा) सुविस्मिताः (अभवन्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्दादयः तु Nanda and the others indeed, तं दृष्ट्वा having seen that (abode), परमानन्दनिर्वृताः (बभूवुः) became filled with supreme bliss, तत्र छन्दोभिः स्तूयमानं कृष्णं च (दृष्ट्वा) and seeing Krishna there being offered prayers by the personified Vedas, सुविस्मिताः (अभवन्) they were struck with great wonder.

Simple Meaning: Upon seeing that divine abode, Nanda and the cowherd men were overwhelmed with supreme spiritual bliss. They were profoundly amazed to see Krishna there, being glorified by the personified Vedas with sacred hymns.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशम स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “कृष्णः वरुणलोकात् नन्दम् उद्धरति नाम” अष्टाविंशः अध्यायः ॥28॥

Thus ends the twenty-eighth discourse named “Lord Krishna rescues Nanda from the realm of Varuna” in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
