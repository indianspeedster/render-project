ब्रह्मा उवाच

नौमीड्य तेऽभ्रवपुषे तडिदम्बराय गुञ्जावतंसपरिपिच्छलसन्मुखाय ।

वन्यस्रजे कवलवेत्रविषाणवेणुलक्ष्मश्रिये मृदुपदे पशुपाङ्गजाय ॥10.14.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: नौमि I bow/offer salutations, ईड्य O worshipful one, ते to You, अभ्र-वपुषे whose body is like a dark rain cloud, तडित्-अम्बराय whose garment is like lightning, गुञ्जा-अवतंस-परिपिच्छ-लसत्-मुखाय whose face is radiant with gunja berries as ornaments and a peacock feather, वन्य-स्रजे wearing a garland of forest flowers, कवल-वेत्र-विषाण-वेणु-लक्ष्म-श्रिये adorned with the marks of a morsel of food, a herding stick, a horn and a flute, मृदु-पदे whose feet are tender/soft, पशुप-अङ्गजाय who is born as the son of a cowherd

Anvaya: ईड्य! अभ्रवपुषे तडिदम्बराय गुञ्जावतंसपरिपिच्छलसन्मुखाय वन्यस्रजे कवलवेत्रविषाणवेणुलक्ष्मश्रिये मृदुपदे पशुपाङ्गजाय ते नौमि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ईड्य O most worshipful one!, अभ्रवपुषे Whose body is like a dark rain cloud, तडिदम्बराय whose garment shines like lightning, गुञ्जावतंसपरिपिच्छलसन्मुखाय whose face is radiant with gunja berries as ornaments and a peacock feather, वन्यस्रजे wearing a garland of forest flowers, कवलवेत्रविषाणवेणुलक्ष्मश्रिये adorned with the beauty of a morsel of food, a herding stick, a horn and a flute, मृदुपदे whose feet are most tender and soft, पशुपाङ्गजाय who has taken birth as the son of a cowherd — ते नौमि I bow to You.

Simple meaning: O most worshipful one — I bow to You, whose body is like a dark rain cloud, whose garment shines like lightning, whose face is radiant with gunja berry ornaments and a peacock feather, who wears a garland of forest flowers, who is adorned with a morsel of food, a herding stick, a horn and a flute, whose feet are most tender, and who has appeared as the son of a cowherd.

अस्यापि देव वपुषो मदनुग्रहस्य स्वेच्छामयस्य न तु भूतमयस्य कोऽपि ।

नेशे महि त्ववसितुं मनसाऽऽन्तरेण साक्षात्तवैव किमुतात्मसुखानुभूतेः ॥10.14.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: अस्य of this, अपि even, देव O Lord, वपुषः of the form/body, मत्-अनुग्रहस्य which is a grace/blessing upon me, स्व-इच्छा-मयस्य which is of Your own free will, न तु not indeed, भूत-मयस्य made of the material elements, कः अपि anyone at all, न ईशे is not capable, महि the greatness/glory, तु indeed, अवसितुम् to comprehend/fathom, मनसा by the mind, आन्तरेण by the inner faculty, साक्षात् directly, तव एव of You alone, किम् उत what to speak then, आत्म-सुख-अनुभूतेः of the experience of Your own self-bliss

Compound word breakdown:

Anvaya: देव! मदनुग्रहस्य भूतमयस्य न तु स्वेच्छामयस्य अस्य वपुषः अपि महि आन्तरेण मनसा अवसितुं कः अपि न ईशे — साक्षात् तव एव आत्मसुखानुभूतेः किमुत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देव O Lord!, मदनुग्रहस्य Which is a grace upon me, भूतमयस्य न तु स्वेच्छामयस्य not made of material elements but of Your own free will, अस्य वपुषः अपि of even this form of Yours, महि the greatness, आन्तरेण मनसा by the inner mind, अवसितुं to comprehend, कः अपि न ईशे no one at all is capable — साक्षात् तव एव आत्मसुखानुभूतेः किमुत what then to speak of directly experiencing Your own self-bliss.

Simple meaning: O Lord! No one at all is capable of comprehending by their inner mind even the greatness of this form of Yours, which is Your grace upon me, made not of material elements but of Your own free will — what then to speak of directly experiencing the bliss of Your own Self, which is known to You alone.

Philosophical commentary: Brahma draws a crucial distinction here — Krishna's form is not a product of prakriti or the five bhutas, but is entirely svecchamaya, manifested by His own sovereign will. This directly refutes the mayavada view that the Lord's form is material or illusory. Even the greatest of meditators cannot fathom this form through the antahkarana — the inner instrument of mind — let alone taste the Lord's own atmananda.

Key insight: There are two levels of the unknowable here — the Lord's form (which no one can fully comprehend) and the Lord's own self-bliss (which is infinitely beyond even that). Brahma humbly places himself before both.

ज्ञाने प्रयासमुदपास्य नमन्त एव जीवन्ति सन्मुखरितां भवदीयवार्ताम् ।

स्थाने स्थिताः श्रुतिगतां तनुवाङ्मनोभिर्ये प्रायशोऽजित जितोऽप्यसि तैस्त्रिलोक्याम् ॥10.14.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: ज्ञाने in knowledge/jnana, प्रयासम् effort/striving, उदपास्य having completely given up, नमन्तः bowing/surrendering, एव indeed, जीवन्ति live, सत्-मुखरिताम् proclaimed/sung by the saints, भवदीय-वार्ताम् the topics/glories relating to You, स्थाने स्थिताः remaining in their own position/station, श्रुति-गताम् that which goes to the ears, तनु-वाक्-मनोभिः with body, speech and mind, ये those who, प्रायशः for the most part/generally, अजित O unconquerable one, जितः conquered/won over, अपि even, असि You are, तैः by them, त्रिलोक्याम् in all the three worlds

Compound word breakdown:

Anvaya: ये ज्ञाने प्रयासम् उदपास्य नमन्तः एव स्थाने स्थिताः सन्मुखरितां श्रुतिगतां भवदीयवार्तां तनुवाङ्मनोभिः जीवन्ति — अजित! त्रिलोक्यां तैः प्रायशः जितः अपि असि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ये Those who, ज्ञाने प्रयासम् उदपास्य having completely abandoned the effort of pursuit of knowledge, नमन्तः एव simply surrendering, स्थाने स्थिताः remaining in their own station in life, सन्मुखरितां श्रुतिगतां भवदीयवार्तां simply by listening to the glories relating to You — sung by the saints, तनुवाङ्मनोभिः with body, speech and mind, जीवन्ति live by — अजित O unconquerable one!, त्रिलोक्यां In all the three worlds, तैः by them, प्रायशः जितः अपि असि You are for the most part even conquered.

Simple meaning: O Ajita — those who completely abandon the effort of pursuit of knowledge, and simply surrendering, remaining in their own station in life, live by listening to the glories relating to You — as proclaimed by the saints — with body, speech and mind: by such devotees, You, who are unconquerable, for the most part are conquered, even in all the three worlds.

Philosophical commentary: This verse contrasts the path of jnana-prayasa — laborious intellectual striving — with the effortless path of surrender and shravana of bhagavat-katha. The word उदपास्य (completely cast aside) is emphatic — it is not a partial giving up but a total renunciation of the pride of independent knowing. The Lord, though Ajita (never conquered), is paradoxically won over by such humble devotees.

Key insight: The supreme paradox of bhakti — the Unconquerable is conquered not by effort or knowledge, but by loving surrender and living immersed in His glories with every faculty of body, speech and mind.

श्रेयःस्रुतिं भक्तिमुदस्य ते विभो क्लिश्यन्ति ये केवलबोधलब्धये ।

तेषामसौ क्लेशल एव शिश्यते नान्यद्यथा स्थूलतुषावघातिनाम् ॥10.14.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रेयः-स्रुतिम् the stream of highest good/supreme benefit, भक्तिम् bhakti/devotion, उदस्य having cast aside, ते Your, विभो O all-pervading Lord, क्लिश्यन्ति struggle/toil/suffer, ये those who, केवल-बोध-लब्धये for the attainment of mere knowledge alone, तेषाम् for them, असौ that, क्लेशलः toil/trouble/suffering, एव alone, शिश्यते remains, न अन्यत् nothing else, यथा just as, स्थूल-तुष-अवघातिनाम् of those who pound empty/thick husks of grain

Compound word breakdown:

Anvaya: विभो! ये श्रेयःस्रुतिं भक्तिम् उदस्य केवलबोधलब्धये क्लिश्यन्ति — तेषाम् असौ क्लेशलः एव शिश्यते न अन्यत् — यथा स्थूलतुषावघातिनाम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विभो O all-pervading Lord!, ये Those who, श्रेयःस्रुतिं भक्तिम् उदस्य having cast aside bhakti which is the flowing stream of the highest good, केवलबोधलब्धये for the attainment of mere knowledge alone, क्लिश्यन्ति toil and struggle — तेषाम् for them, असौ क्लेशलः एव that toil and suffering alone, शिश्यते remains, न अन्यत् nothing else — यथा just as, स्थूलतुषावघातिनाम् for those who pound thick empty husks of grain.

Simple meaning: O all-pervading Lord — those who cast aside bhakti, which is the flowing stream of the highest good, and toil and struggle merely for the attainment of dry knowledge alone — for them nothing remains but that toil and suffering itself — just like those who pound thick empty husks of grain expecting to find something within.

Philosophical commentary: Brahma here uses the vivid image of pounding empty husks — all effort, no fruit. Kevala-bodha, knowledge pursued without bhakti, lacks the essential inner substance. Bhakti is called श्रेयःस्रुति — not merely a path but a living current or stream that naturally carries one toward the highest good, whereas jnana without it, is dry exertion that yields only exhaustion.

Key insight: Bhakti is not one path among many — it is the very stream of supreme good itself. To abandon it in pursuit of bare knowledge is like pounding empty husks — the labour is real but the grain is simply not there.

पुरेह भूमन् बहवोऽपि योगिनस्त्वदर्पितेहा निजकर्मलब्धया ।

विबुध्य भक्त्यैव कथोपनीतया प्रपेदिरेऽञ्जोऽच्युत ते गतिं पराम् ॥10.14.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुरा formerly/in the past, इह in this world, भूमन् O most expansive/infinite one, बहवः many, अपि even, योगिनः yogis/seekers, त्वत्-अर्पित-ईहाः whose endeavours were offered to You, निज-कर्म-लब्धया obtained through their own prescribed duties/karma, विबुध्य having awakened/realised, भक्त्या एव by bhakti alone, कथा-उपनीतया brought about/nourished by hearing Your glories/katha, प्रपेदिरे attained/reached, अञ्जः easily/effortlessly, अच्युत O infallible one, ते Your, गतिम् पराम् the supreme destination/highest state

Compound word breakdown:

Anvaya: भूमन्! अच्युत! इह पुरा बहवः अपि योगिनः त्वदर्पितेहाः निजकर्मलब्धया कथोपनीतया भक्त्या एव विबुध्य ते परां गतिम् अञ्जः प्रपेदिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भूमन् O most infinite one, अच्युत O infallible one, इह पुरा formerly in this world, बहवः अपि योगिनः many yogis even, त्वदर्पितेहाः whose every endeavour was offered to You, निजकर्मलब्धया obtained through their own prescribed duties, कथोपनीतया भक्त्या एव by bhakti alone — brought about by hearing Your glories, विबुध्य having awakened to the truth, ते परां गतिम् Your supreme destination, अञ्जः प्रपेदिरे attained effortlessly.

Simple meaning: O infinite and infallible Lord — in this world, in the past, many yogis who dedicated their every endeavour to You, and who through faithful performance of their own duties obtained that bhakti brought about by hearing Your glories — by that bhakti alone, having awakened to the truth, they attained Your supreme destination with great ease.

Philosophical commentary: Brahma here traces the natural progression — nishkama karma offered to the Lord purifies the heart, which then becomes fertile ground for bhagavat-katha, which in turn nourishes bhakti, which alone leads to para-gati. The word अञ्जः (effortlessly) is striking — what appears to be the highest and most difficult attainment becomes natural and easy when approached through this sequence rooted in bhakti.

Key insight: The path is beautifully layered — karma offered to the Lord → katha → bhakti → awakening → para-gati. And the crowning word is अञ्जः — effortlessly. Bhakti does not make the goal harder; it makes the seemingly impossible suddenly easy.

तथापि भूमन् महिमागुणस्य ते विबोद्धुमर्हत्यमलान्तरात्मभिः ।

अविक्रियात् स्वानुभवादरूपतो ह्यनन्यबोध्यात्मतया न चान्यथा ॥10.14.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: तथापि nevertheless/even so, भूमन् O infinite one, महिमा the glory/greatness, अगुणस्य of the one beyond all qualities/gunas, ते Your, विबोद्धुम् to know/comprehend fully, अर्हति is possible/worthy, अमल-अन्तर-आत्मभिः by those whose inner self is pure/stainless, अविक्रियात् from that which is without modification/changeless, स्व-अनुभवात् from direct self-experience/inner realisation, अरूपतः from that which is formless/beyond form, हि indeed, अनन्य-बोध्य-आत्मतया by the nature of being knowable only by itself/none other, न च अन्यथा and not otherwise/in no other way

Compound word breakdown:

Anvaya: भूमन्! तथापि अगुणस्य ते महिमा अमलान्तरात्मभिः अविक्रियात् अरूपतः स्वानुभवात् अनन्यबोध्यात्मतया हि विबोद्धुम् अर्हति — न च अन्यथा।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भूमन् O infinite one!, तथापि Even so, अगुणस्य ते of You who are beyond all gunas, महिमा the glory and greatness, अमलान्तरात्मभिः by those whose innermost self is pure and stainless, अविक्रियात् from that which is changeless and without modification, अरूपतः from that which is beyond all form, स्वानुभवात् from direct inner self-experience, अनन्यबोध्यात्मतया हि by the very nature of being knowable only by itself, विबोद्धुम् अर्हति can indeed be known — न च अन्यथा and in no other way whatsoever.

Simple meaning: Even so, O infinite one — Your glory, being beyond all gunas, can indeed be known by those whose innermost self is completely pure and stainless — through that which is changeless, formless, and directly experienced within — by the very nature of Your being knowable only by Yourself. And in no other way whatsoever.

गुणात्मनस्तेऽपि गुणान् विमातुं हितावतीर्णस्य क ईशिरेऽस्य ।

कालेन यैर्वा विमिताः सुकल्पैर्भूपांसवः खे मिहिका द्युभासः ॥10.14.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: गुण-आत्मनः of You who are the source of all gunas, ते Your, अपि even, गुणान् the qualities/glories, विमातुम् to measure/comprehend fully, हित-अवतीर्णस्य of You who have descended for the welfare of all, कः who, ईशिरे was capable/able, अस्य of this, कालेन by time, यैः by whom, वा or, विमिताः have been measured/counted, सु-कल्पैः by very precise/accurate means, भू-पांसवः the particles of dust of the earth, खे in the sky, मिहिकाः particles of mist/snowflakes, द्यु-भासः the rays of light of the sun

Anvaya: हितावतीर्णस्य गुणात्मनः अस्य ते गुणान् विमातुं कः ईशिरे — यैः वा सुकल्पैः कालेन भूपांसवः खे मिहिकाः द्युभासः विमिताः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हितावतीर्णस्य Of You who have descended for the welfare of all, गुणात्मनः whose very nature is the transcendent source of all gunas, अस्य ते of You, गुणान् the glories and qualities, विमातुं to measure and comprehend, कः ईशिरे who was ever capable — यैः वा सुकल्पैः By those by whichever precise means, कालेन over all of time, भूपांसवः the particles of dust of the earth, खे मिहिकाः the particles of mist in the sky, द्युभासः the rays of the sun, विमिताः have been counted and measured.

Simple meaning: Who was ever capable of measuring and comprehending the glories of You — whose very nature is the transcendent source of all gunas, and who has descended solely for the welfare of all? Even if by the most precise means over all of time one were to count every particle of dust on the earth, every particle of mist in the sky, and every single ray of the sun — even then Your glories remain utterly beyond all measure.

तत्तेऽनुकम्पां सुसमीक्षमाणो भुञ्जान एवात्मकृतं विपाकम् ।

हृद्वाग्वपुर्भिर्विदधन्नमस्ते जीवेत यो मुक्तिपदे स दायभाक् ॥10.14.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् therefore/that, ते Your, अनुकम्पाम् compassion/grace, सु-समीक्षमाणः looking upon with great care/awaiting with hope, भुञ्जानः experiencing/undergoing, एव indeed, आत्म-कृतम् done by oneself/self-created, विपाकम् the ripened fruit/consequences of one's own actions, हृत्-वाक्-वपुर्भिः with heart, speech and body, विदधन् offering/performing, नमः ते salutation to You, जीवेत lives, यः who, मुक्ति-पदे for the state of liberation, सः he, दाय-भाक् rightful heir/one who has a share by birthright

Anvaya: तत् यः ते अनुकम्पां सुसमीक्षमाणः आत्मकृतं विपाकम् एव भुञ्जानः हृद्वाग्वपुर्भिः ते नमः विदधन् जीवेत — सः मुक्तिपदे दायभाक्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् Therefore, यः one who, ते Your, अनुकम्पां compassion and grace, सुसमीक्षमाणः looking upon with great care and hopeful trust, आत्मकृतं विपाकम् एव भुञ्जानः undergoing and accepting the fully ripened consequences of his own past actions, हृद्वाग्वपुर्भिः with heart, speech and body, ते नमः विदधन् offering salutation to You, जीवेत lives — सः मुक्तिपदे दायभाक् he is a rightful heir to the state of liberation.

Simple meaning: One who lives thus — awaiting Your grace with hopeful and discerning trust, accepting and undergoing the consequences of his own past actions without resistance, and offering salutation to You with heart, speech and body — such a one is a rightful heir to liberation, as if by birthright.

Philosophical commentary: This verse presents the ideal of a devotee in the midst of suffering — not denying karma, not lamenting it, but accepting it as आत्मकृत (self-created) while simultaneously fixing the gaze on the Lord's अनुकम्पा. This dual awareness — owning one's karma and trusting the Lord's grace — is the hallmark of a mature devotee. The word दायभाक् is deeply significant — liberation is not earned through heroic effort but inherited naturally, as a son inherits from his father.

Key insight: The devotee's qualification for liberation is not the absence of suffering but the quality of his response to it — accepting karma without bitterness, awaiting grace without impatience, and offering oneself completely through हृत्-वाक्-वपुस्. Liberation then comes not as a reward but as a birthright.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यथा Just as, यस्य कुक्षौ in whose belly, इदं सर्वं all of this universe, सात्मं along with (the Supreme Lord) Yourself, भाति appears —तथा in the same way, तत् त्वयि अपि in that You also, इह here, तत् सर्वम् all of that same universe appears — इदं मायया विना किम् how is all of this possible without maya.

Simple meaning: Just as all of this universe along with Yourself shines within whose (Supreme Lord’s) belly, that same universe appears within You (in Krishna) here in this world as well — how can any of this be explained without maya?

Philosophical commentary: Brahma draws together two simultaneous visions — the universe within the Lord's belly (the cosmic Narayana) and the universe appearing within Krishna here in Vrindavana. Both are the same Lord, both contain the same universe, and both demonstrate the same truth — that creation is a display of the Lord's maya.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अद्यैव Just now today, मम to me, ते मायात्वम् आदर्शितम् Your nature of maya has been shown — त्वदृते अस्य किं न without You what is there of all this — प्रथमम् एकः असि At first, You were one alone — ततः then, व्रजसुहृद् वत्साः समस्ताः अपि all the cowherd boys and calves of Vraja, तावन्तः चतुर्भुजाः असि You became that many four-armed forms — तत् then, अखिलैः साकं मया उपासिताः were worshipped by (entire celestial bodies) all of them along with me— तावन्ति एव जगन्ति अभूः You became that many universes — तत् then, अमितम् ब्रह्माद्वयं the immeasurable non-dual Brahman alone, शिष्यते remains.

Simple meaning: Just now has the nature of Your maya been revealed to me — for without You, what is any of this? First You were one alone — then You became all the cowherd boys and calves of Vraja as so many four-armed forms — and You were worshipped by all the gods and demigods along with me — then You became that many universes — and then what remains is the immeasurable non-dual Brahman alone.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: त्वत्पदवीम् Your true nature/position, अजानताम् those who do not know, अनात्मनि in material world, आत्मना by Yourself, मायां वितत्य having spread the maya, आत्मा You, भासि appear — सृष्टौ अहम् इव in creation, as if, I (Brahma), जगतः विधाने त्वम् इव in the maintenance of the universe, as if you yourself, एषः अन्ते त्रिनेत्रः इव in destruction of this world, as if the three-eyed one (Shiva).

Simple meaning: To those who do not know your true nature, in the material world, spreading Your Maya, You appear as Brahma in creation, as Yourself in the maintenance of the universe, and as the three-eyed Shiva at the time of destruction of this world.

Note: It is Vishnu Himself who is present everywhere and everyone. But in the material world, those who don’t understand this truth, they are bewildered by Vishnu’s Maya and perceive Him to be Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva separately.

Anvaya: भवबन्धमोक्षौ द्वौ अज्ञानसंज्ञौ नाम — ऋतज्ञभावात् न अन्यौ स्तः। अजस्रचित्यात्मनि केवले परे विचार्यमाणे तरणौ इव अहनी।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भवबन्धमोक्षौ Bondage and liberation, द्वौ these two, अज्ञानसंज्ञौ नाम are merely names (given) in ignorance — ऋतज्ञभावात् from the standpoint of the knower of truth, न अन्यौ स्तः they both do not exist separately. अजस्रचित्यात्मनि In the eternal consciousness, केवले परे in the Supreme alone, विचार्यमाणे when properly inquired into, तरणौ a sun, इव like, अहनी day and night

Simple meaning: Bondage in samsara and liberation — these two exist only as names born of ignorance. From the standpoint of the knower of truth, they have no separate existence. When the pure, supreme Self of eternal consciousness is properly inquired into, bondage and liberation are like the day and night with respect to the Sun — distinctions that belong only to the position of the Sun, not to the Sun itself.

Core Idea of the Verse

From the perspective of ultimate reality (Krishna/pure consciousness):

Bondage and liberation are not real differences — they exist only from the standpoint of ignorance.

त्वामात्मानं परं मत्वा परमात्मानमेव च ।

आत्मा पुनर्बहिर्मृग्य अहोऽज्ञजनताज्ञता ॥10.14.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वाम् You, आत्मानम् the Self, परम् separate, मत्वा considering, परम-आत्मानम् the Supreme Self, एव indeed, च and, आत्मा the self, पुनः again, बहिः outside, मृग्यः to be searched for, अहो alas, अज्ञ-जनता of ignorant people, अज्ञता ignorance

Anvaya: अज्ञजनता त्वां परमात्मानम् आत्मानम् च परम् एव मत्वा, पुनः आत्मा बहिः मृग्यः—अहो अज्ञता।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अज्ञजनता Ignorant people, त्वां परमात्मानम् You, the Supreme Self, आत्मानम् च and the Self, परम् separate, एव मत्वा considering indeed, आत्मा the self, पुनः again, बहिः outside, मृग्यः to be searched.अहो अज्ञता Alas, ignorance!

Simple meaning: Alas! Ignorant people consider themselves to be separate from You, the Supreme Self. Then they search for the Self outside again—what great ignorance!

अन्तर्भवेऽनन्त भवन्तमेव ह्यतत्त्यजन्तो मृगयन्ति सन्तः।

असन्तमप्यन्त्यहिमन्तरेण सन्तं गुणं तं किमु यन्ति सन्तः ॥10.14.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्तः-भवे in worldly existence/in the cycle of birth and death, अनन्त O Infinite One, भवन्तम् You, एव alone/indeed, हि certainly, अ-तत्-त्यजन्तः abandoning that is separate from You, मृगयन्ति seek/search for, सन्तः the saintly ones, असन्तम् the unreal/non-existent, अपि even, अन्ति near/close, अहिम् the snake, अन्तरेण without/in the absence of, सन्तम् the real/existing, गुणम् quality/virtue, तम् that, किमु why then/what to speak of, यन्ति attain/go toward, सन्तः the saintly ones

Anvaya: अनन्त! अन्तर्भवे अतत्त्यजन्तः सन्तः भवन्तम् एव हि मृगयन्ति। सन्तः असन्तम् अपि अन्ति अहिम् अन्तरेण तं सन्तं गुणं किमु यन्ति?  

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अनन्त O Infinite One, अन्तर्भवे In worldly existence, अतत्त्यजन्तः abandoning that is separate from You, सन्तः the saintly ones, भवन्तम् एव हि मृगयन्ति indeed, seek You alone. सन्तः The saintly people, असन्तम् अपि even though non-existent, अन्ति अहिम् the snake which is nearby, अन्तरेण separate from, सन्तं तं गुणम् that real quality of being a rope, किमु यन्ति why then would the saintly ones go toward it?

Simple meaning: O Infinite Lord, the saintly ones never abandon their search for You even while living within the cycle of worldly existence — it is You alone they seek. And just as even a non-existent snake appears near when a rope lies close by, why then would the wise ever go toward that which is merely apparent and unreal?

अथापि ते देव पदाम्बुजद्वयप्रसादलेशानुगृहीत एव हि ।

जानाति तत्त्वं भगवन् महिम्नो न चान्य एकोऽपि चिरं विचिन्वन् ॥10.14.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ अपि even so/nevertheless, ते Your, देव O Lord, पद-अम्बुज-द्वय-प्रसाद-लेश-अनुगृहीतः one who has been blessed by even a particle of the grace of Your two lotus feet, एव indeed, हि certainly, जानाति knows, तत्त्वम् the truth/the reality, भगवन् O Bhagavan, महिम्नः of the glory/of the greatness, न not, च and, अन्यः another, एकः even one, अपि even, चिरम् for a long time, विचिन्वन् searching/contemplating

Anvaya: भगवन्! देव! अथापि ते पदाम्बुजद्वयप्रसादलेशानुगृहीतः एव हि महिम्नः तत्त्वं जानाति। चिरं विचिन्वन् अन्यः एकः अपि न (जानाति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवन् देव O Bhagavan, O Lord!, अथापि Even so, ते पदाम्बुजद्वयप्रसादलेशानुगृहीतः one blessed by even a particle of the grace of Your two lotus feet, एव हि indeed certainly, महिम्नः तत्त्वं the truth of Your glory, जानाति knows. चिरं विचिन्वन् Searching for a long time, अन्यः एकः अपि न not even one other, (जानाति) knows.

Simple meaning: O Bhagavan, O Lord, even so — only one who has been graced by even the smallest particle of the mercy of Your two lotus feet can truly know the reality of Your greatness. Not even a single other person, no matter how long they may search and contemplate, can come to know it.

तदस्तु मे नाथ स भूरिभागो भवेऽत्र वान्यत्र तु वा तिरश्चाम् ।

येनाहमेकोऽपि भवज्जनानां भूत्वा निषेवे तव पादपल्लवम् ॥10.15.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् therefore, अस्तु may it be, मे for me, नाथ O Lord/O Master, सः that, भूरि-भागः great fortune/highest blessing, भवे in existence/in birth, अत्र here, वा or, अन्यत्र elsewhere, तु indeed, वा or, तिरश्चाम् among animals, येन by which, अहम् I, एकः one, अपि even, भवत्-जनानाम् of Your devotees, भूत्वा having become, निषेवे may serve/worship, तव Your, पाद-पल्लवम् the tender lotus feet

Anvaya: नाथ! तत् सः भूरिभागः मे अस्तु — अत्र भवे वा अन्यत्र वा तिरश्चां तु वा — येन अहं भवज्जनानाम् एकः अपि भूत्वा तव पादपल्लवं निषेवे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नाथ O Lord!, तत् सः भूरिभागः मे अस्तु Therefore, may that great fortune be mine, अत्र भवे वा in this existence here or, अन्यत्र वा or elsewhere, तिरश्चां तु वा or even among animals, येन by which, अहं I, भवज्जनानाम् एकः अपि भूत्वा having become even one of Your devotees, तव पादपल्लवं Your tender lotus feet, निषेवे may serve.

Simple meaning: O Lord! Therefore, may that great fortune be mine — whether in this life or in another, or even in a birth among animals — by which I may become even one among Your devotees and serve Your tender lotus feet.

अहोऽतिधन्या व्रजगोरमण्यः स्तन्यामृतं पीतमतीव ते मुदा ।

यासां विभो वत्सतरात्मजात्मना यत्तृप्तयेऽद्यापि न चालमध्वराः ॥10.14.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो oh, अति-धन्याः most fortunate/greatly blessed, व्रज-गो-रमण्यः the cows and women of Vraja, स्तन्य-अमृतम् the nectar of their milk, पीतम् was drunk, अतीव very much/exceedingly, ते by You, मुदा with joy/with delight, यासाम् of whom, विभो O All-pervading Lord, वत्सतर-आत्मज-आत्मना in the form of their own calves and sons, यत्-तृप्तये for whose satisfaction, अद्य-अपि even today, न not, च and, अलम् sufficient/enough, अध्वराः the Vedic sacrifices/yajnas

Anvaya: अहो! विभो! व्रजगोरमण्यः अतिधन्याः — यासां स्तन्यामृतं ते वत्सतरात्मजात्मना अतीव मुदा पीतम्। यत्तृप्तये अद्यापि अध्वराः अलं न च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो Oh!, विभो O All-pervading Lord! व्रजगोरमण्यः अतिधन्याः The cows and women of Vraja are most fortunate —  यासां स्तन्यामृतं Of whom the nectar of their milk, ते वत्सतरात्मजात्मना by You, in the form of their very own calves and sons, अतीव मुदा पीतम् was drunk with great joy, यत्तृप्तये for whose (Your) satisfaction, अद्यापि even today, अध्वराः अलं न च the Vedic sacrifices are not even sufficient.

Simple meaning: Oh, how greatly blessed are the cows and women of Vraja! O All-pervading Lord, You drank their milk-nectar with such exceeding delight, having taken the form of their very own calves and sons. Even all the Vedic sacrifices performed to this day are not sufficient to satisfy You.

Note: The Lord was satisfied with the milk of the cows and the women of Vraja because they were very devoted to Krishna. The shloka says that love and devotion is enough to please the Lord. Krishna does not care for the elaborate rituals.

यन्मित्रं परमानन्दं पूर्णं ब्रह्म सनातनम् ॥10.14.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो oh/how wonderful, भाग्यम् fortune/blessed luck, अहो oh, भाग्यम् fortune/blessed luck, नन्द-गोप-व्रज-ओकसाम् of the residents of Vraja headed by Nanda and the cowherd men, यत् whose, मित्रम् friend/companion, परम-आनन्दम् the supreme bliss, पूर्णम् complete/full, ब्रह्म Brahman, सनातनम् eternal

Anvaya: नन्दगोपव्रजौकसाम् अहो भाग्यम् अहो भाग्यम् — यत् मित्रं परमानन्दं पूर्णं सनातनं ब्रह्म।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्दगोपव्रजौकसाम् Of the residents of Vraja headed by Nanda and the cowherd men, अहो भाग्यम् अहो भाग्यम् oh what fortune, oh what fortune — यत् मित्रं whose friend is, परमानन्दं पूर्णं सनातनं ब्रह्म the supreme bliss, complete, the eternal Brahman.

Simple meaning: Oh what fortune, oh what fortune is that of the residents of Vraja — Nanda, the cowherd men and all who dwell there! For their intimate friend is none other than the supreme bliss, the complete and eternal Brahman itself.

एषा तु भाग्यमहिमाच्युत तावदास्तामेकादशैव हि वयं बत भूरिभागाः ।

एतद्धृषीकचषकैरसकृत् पिबामः शर्वादयोऽङ्घ्र्युदजमध्वमृतासवं ते ॥10.14.33॥

तद् भूरिभाग्यमिह जन्म किमप्यटव्यां यद् गोकुलेऽपि कतमाङ्घ्रिरजोऽभिषेकम् ।

यज्जीवितं तु निखिलं भगवान् मुकुन्दस्त्वद्यापि यत्पदरजः श्रुतिमृग्यमेव ॥10.14.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, भूरि-भाग्यम् great fortune, इह here, जन्म birth, किम् अपि any/whatever, अटव्याम् in the forest, यत् which, गोकुले in Gokula, अपि even, कतम-अङ्घ्रि-रजः-अभिषेकम् the sprinkling of the dust of whichever feet (dust of the feet of residents of Vraja), यत् whose, जीवितम् life, तु indeed, निखिलम् entire/whole, भगवान् the Lord, मुकुन्दः Mukunda, तु indeed, अद्य अपि even today, यत्-पद-रजः whose foot-dust, श्रुति-मृग्यम् sought after by the Vedas/searched for by the scriptures, एव indeed

Anvaya: तत् भूरिभाग्यम् — इह अटव्यां गोकुले अपि किमपि जन्म यत् कतमाङ्घ्रिरजोऽभिषेकम्। यत् निखिलं जीवितं तु भगवान् मुकुन्दः — अद्य अपि यत्पदरजः श्रुतिमृग्यम् एव।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् भूरिभाग्यम् That is great fortune — इह अटव्यां Here in the forest, गोकुले अपि even in Gokula, किमपि जन्म whatever birth, यत् कतमाङ्घ्रिरजोऽभिषेकम् which results in the sprinkling of the dust of whichever feet. यत् निखिलं जीवितं तु Whose (the residents of Vraja) entire life, indeed, भगवान् मुकुन्दः is the Lord Mukunda — यत्पदरजः whose foot-dust, अद्यापि even today, श्रुतिमृग्यम् एव is still sought after by the Vedas.

Simple meaning: That is truly great fortune — to take any birth here in this forest, or even in Gokula, such that one receives the sprinkling of the dust of those feet. For those residents of Vraja whose entire life is the Lord Mukunda Himself — whose foot-dust is even today sought after by the Vedas — such a birth anywhere in this land is the highest blessing.

Note: Brahma says that taking birth in any form in the forest or in Gokula is a matter of great fortune. That way, he will be sprinkled with the dust of the feet of the residents - the residents for whom Lord Mukunda is their very life indeed. Even Vedas seek the foot dust of Lord Mukunda.

Brahma seeks the dust of the residents. Residents are sprinkled with the dust of Krishna. Even Vedas are seeking the dust of feet of Krishna.

एतत्ते सर्वमाख्यातं यत् पृष्टोऽहमिह त्वया ।

यत् कौमारे हरिकृतं पौगण्डे परिकीर्तितम् ॥10.14.59॥

Word-by-word meaning: एतत् this, ते to you, सर्वम् all/everything, आख्यातम् has been told/has been narrated, यत् what, पृष्टः questioned/asked, अहम् I, इह here, त्वया by you, यत् which, कौमारे in the kaumara age/in early childhood, हरि-कृतम् done by Hari/performed by Krishna, पौगण्डे in the pauganda age/in the later boyhood stage, परिकीर्तितम् celebrated/proclaimed/described

Anvaya: इह यत् त्वया अहं पृष्टः, एतत् सर्वं ते आख्यातम् — यत् कौमारे हरिकृतं पौगण्डे परिकीर्तितम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इह यत् त्वया अहं पृष्टः Here, whatever I was asked by You, एतत् सर्वं ते आख्यातम् all of this, to you, has been narrated — यत् कौमारे हरिकृतं which was done by Hari in the kaumara age, पौगण्डे परिकीर्तितम् in the pauganda age, is celebrated/proclaimed.

Simple meaning: All of this that you asked me here has now been narrated to you — that which was done by Hari in His kaumara (childhood) age and is now celebrated and proclaimed in His pauganda (boyhood) age.

एतत् सुहृद्भिश्चरितं मुरारेरघार्दनं शाद्वलजेमनं च ।

व्यक्तेतरद् रूपमजोर्वभिष्टवं शृण्वन् गृणन्नेति नरोऽखिलार्थान् ॥10.14.60॥

Word-by-word meaning: एतत् this, सुहृद्भिः with friends/in the company of friends, चरितम् pastime/conduct, मुरारेः of Murari/of Krishna, अघ-अर्दनम् the destruction of sin/the slaying of Aghasura, शाद्वल-जेमनम् the eating on the grassy ground/the meal on the meadow, च and, व्यक्त-इतरत् रूपम् the transcendent form/the form beyond the manifest, अज-उर्व-अभिष्टवम् the great hymn of praise/the extensive eulogy by Brahma, शृण्वन् hearing, गृणन् chanting/reciting, एति attains, नरः a person, अखिल-अर्थान् all goals/all purposes of life

Anvaya: नरः एतत् मुरारेः सुहृद्भिः चरितम् — अघार्दनं शाद्वलजेमनं च, अजोर्वभिष्टवं व्यक्तेतरत् रूपं च — शृण्वन् गृणन् अखिलार्थान् एति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नरः A person, एतत् मुरारेः सुहृद्भिः चरितम् this pastime of Murari with His friends — अघार्दनं the destruction of Aghasura/the slaying of sin, शाद्वलजेमनं च and the meal on the grassy meadow, अजोर्वभिष्टवं the great hymn of praise by Brahma, व्यक्तेतरत् रूपं च and the transcendent form beyond the manifest — शृण्वन् गृणन् hearing and chanting, अखिलार्थान् एति attains all goals of life.

Simple meaning: A person who hears and chants these pastimes of Murari with His friends — the slaying of Aghasura and the destruction of sin, the meal on the grassy meadow, the transcendent form of the unborn Krishna beyond all manifestation, and the great hymn of praise offered by Brahma — attains all the goals and purposes of life.

एवं विहारैः कौमारैः कौमारं जहतुर्व्रजे ।

निलायनैः सेतुबन्धैर्मर्कटोत्प्लवनादिभिः ॥10.14.61॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् thus/in this way, विहारैः with pastimes/with playful activities, कौमारैः of childhood/befitting early childhood, कौमारम् the kaumara age/early childhood, जहतुः they left behind/they passed, व्रजे in Vraja, निलायनैः with games of hiding, सेतु-बन्धैः with building of dams/bridges, मर्कट-उत्प्लवन-आदिभिः with leaping about like monkeys and such other activities

Anvaya: व्रजे एवं निलायनैः सेतुबन्धैः मर्कटोत्प्लवनादिभिः कौमारैः विहारैः कौमारं जहतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: व्रजे In Vraja, एवं thus, निलायनैः with games of hiding, सेतुबन्धैः with building of dams, मर्कटोत्प्लवनादिभिः with leaping about like monkeys and other such activities, कौमारैः विहारैः with childhood pastimes, कौमारं the kaumara age, जहतुः they left behind/passed through.

Simple meaning: Thus in Vraja, Krishna and Balarama passed through their kaumara childhood with pastimes befitting that tender age — games of hiding, building of dams, leaping about like monkeys and many other such playful activities.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमे स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे ब्रह्मस्तुतिर्नाम चतुर्दशः अध्यायः ॥14॥

Thus ends the fourteenth discourse named 'Brahma extols The Lord,'

 in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
