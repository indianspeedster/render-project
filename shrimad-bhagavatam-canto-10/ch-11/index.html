Story of Krishna gracing the fruit seller;

 The destruction of Vatsasura and Bakasura

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

वत्सबकवधो नाम एकादशः अध्यायः

(59 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

गोपा नन्दादयः श्रुत्वा द्रुमयोः पततो रवम् ।

तत्राजग्मुः कुरुश्रेष्ठ निर्घातभयशङ्किताः ॥10.11.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, गोपाः the gopas/cowherd men, नन्द-आदयः Nanda and the others, श्रुत्वा having heard, द्रुमयोः of the two trees, पततोः falling, रवम् the sound/crash, तत्र there, आजग्मुः came/arrived, कुरु-श्रेष्ठ O best of the Kurus, निर्घात-भय-शङ्किताः fearful and apprehensive of a thunderbolt/lightning strike

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच — कुरुश्रेष्ठ! नन्दादयः गोपाः पततोः द्रुमयोः रवं श्रुत्वा, निर्घातभयशङ्किताः (सन्तः) तत्र आजग्मुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said — कुरुश्रेष्ठ O best of the Kurus, नन्दादयः गोपाः Nanda and the other cowherd men, पततोः द्रुमयोः रवं the crashing sound of the two falling trees, श्रुत्वा having heard, निर्घातभयशङ्किताः fearful and apprehensive that it was a thunderbolt or lightning strike, तत्र आजग्मुः came rushing there.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said — O best of the Kurus, Nanda and the other cowherd men, having heard the tremendous crashing sound of the two falling trees and fearful that it might have been caused by a thunderbolt or lightning strike, came rushing to that spot.

भूम्यां निपतितौ तत्र ददृशुर्यमलार्जुनौ ।

बभ्रमुस्तदविज्ञाय लक्ष्यं पतनकारणम् ॥10.11.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: भूम्याम् on the ground, निपतितौ fallen, तत्र there, ददृशुः they saw, यमल-अर्जुनौ the twin arjuna trees, बभ्रमुः they were bewildered/confused, तत् that, अविज्ञाय not knowing/understanding, लक्ष्यम् the obvious/evident cause, पतन-कारणम् the reason for the falling

Anvaya: तत्र भूम्यां निपतितौ यमलार्जुनौ ददृशुः। पतनकारणं लक्ष्यं तत् अविज्ञाय बभ्रमुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र there, भूम्यां on the ground, निपतितौ fallen, यमलार्जुनौ the twin arjuna trees, ददृशुः they saw. पतनकारणं The reason for the falling, लक्ष्यं though evident, तत् that, अविज्ञाय not understanding, बभ्रमुः they were bewildered.

Simple meaning: There they saw the twin arjuna trees lying fallen on the ground. Though the cause of their falling was right before their eyes (that Krishna had caused the trees to fall by dragging the mortar through the trees), they could not understand it and were completely bewildered.

उलूखलं विकर्षन्तं दाम्ना बद्धं च बालकम् ।

कस्येदं कुत आश्चर्यमुत्पात इति कातराः ॥10.11.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: उलूखलम् the mortar, विकर्षन्तम् dragging, दाम्ना with a rope, बद्धम् bound, च and, बालकम् the child, कस्य whose, इदम् this, कुतः from where/how, आश्चर्यम् wonderful/amazing/strange, उत्पातः portent/calamity, इति thus, कातराः filled with anxiety/distressed

Anvaya: दाम्ना बद्धम् उलूखलं विकर्षन्तं बालकं (दृष्ट्वा), (ते) — कस्य इदम्? कुतः आश्चर्यम् उत्पातः इति कातराः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: दाम्ना बद्धम् Bound with a rope, उलूखलं विकर्षन्तं dragging the mortar, बालकं the child, (दृष्ट्वा) having seen, (ते) they — कस्य इदम् whose deed is this? कुतः How, आश्चर्यम् उत्पातः this strange portent, इति कातराः thus were filled with anxiety.

Simple meaning: Seeing the child bound with a rope and dragging the mortar, they were filled with anxiety and said among themselves — whose deed is this? How did this strange portent come about?

बाला ऊचुरनेनेति तिर्यग्गतमुलूखलम् ।

विकर्षता मध्यगेन पुरुषावप्यचक्ष्महि ॥10.11.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: बालाः the children, ऊचुः said, अनेन by this one/by Him, इति thus, तिर्यक्-गतम् gone sideways/crosswise, उलूखलम् the mortar, विकर्षता while dragging, मध्य-गेन going between/passing through the middle, पुरुषौ two persons/beings, अपि also, अचक्ष्महि we saw

Anvaya: बालाः इति ऊचुः — तिर्यग्गतम् उलूखलं मध्यगेन विकर्षता अनेन, पुरुषौ अपि अचक्ष्महि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बालाः इति ऊचुः The children said thus — तिर्यग्गतम् उलूखलं  the crosswise mortar, मध्यगेन विकर्षता dragging between the trees, अनेन by this one, पुरुषौ अपि two persons also, अचक्ष्महि we saw.

Simple meaning: The children said — It was done by this one! As He dragged the mortar which had gone crosswise between the trees, we also saw two persons emerge from there.

न ते तदुक्तं जगृहुर्न घटेतेति तस्य तत् ।

बालस्योत्पाटनं तर्वोः केचित् सन्दिग्धचेतसः ॥10.11.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, ते they, तत् that, उक्तम् said/spoken, जगृहुः accepted/believed, न not, घटेत could happen/be possible, इति thus, तस्य his, तत् that, बालस्य of the child, उत्पाटनम् uprooting, तर्वोः of the two trees, केचित् some, सन्दिग्ध-चेतसः with doubtful/uncertain minds

Anvaya: ते तस्य बालस्य तर्वोः उत्पाटनं न घटेत — इति तत् उक्तं न जगृहुः। केचित् सन्दिग्धचेतसः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते They, तस्य बालस्य तर्वोः उत्पाटनं the uprooting of the two trees by that child, न घटेत did not occur, — इति तत् उक्तं न जगृहुः thus, that what was said (by other children), did not believe. केचित् सन्दिग्धचेतसः Some were doubtful.

Simple meaning: The elders did not accept what the children said — thinking that it was simply not possible for a child to uproot two trees. Some among them were of doubtful and uncertain minds. (Because they had seen other deeds done by Krishna.)

उलूखलं विकर्षन्तं दाम्ना बद्धं स्वमात्मजम् ।

विलोक्य नन्दः प्रहसद्वदनो विमुमोच ह ॥10.11.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: उलूखलम् the mortar, विकर्षन्तम् dragging, दाम्ना with a rope, बद्धम् bound, स्वम् his own, आत्मजम् son, विलोक्य having seen, नन्दः Nanda, प्रहसत्-वदनः with a smiling/laughing face, विमुमोच untied/released, ह indeed

Anvaya: नन्दः दाम्ना बद्धम् उलूखलं विकर्षन्तं स्वम् आत्मजं विलोक्य, प्रहसद्वदनः (सन्) विमुमोच ह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्दः Nanda, दाम्ना बद्धम् bound with a rope, उलूखलं विकर्षन्तं dragging the mortar, स्वम् आत्मजं his own son, विलोक्य having seen, प्रहसद्वदनः with a smiling face, विमुमोच ह indeed untied Him.

Simple meaning: Nanda, seeing his own son bound with a rope and dragging the mortar, broke into a smile and released Him.

गोपीभिः स्तोभितोऽनृत्यद् भगवान् बालवत् क्वचित् ।

उद्गायति क्वचिन्मुग्धस्तद्वशो दारुयन्त्रवत् ॥10.11.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोपीभिः by the gopis, स्तोभितः being praised/encouraged with songs, अनृत्यत् danced, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, बाल-वत् like a child, क्वचित् sometimes, उद्गायति sings aloud, क्वचित् sometimes, मुग्धः charmed/captivated, तत्-वशः under their control, दारु-यन्त्र-वत् like a wooden puppet

स्तोभित  mfn. praised in successive exclamations or shouts, src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi src. Kosha

Anvaya: गोपीभिः स्तोभितः भगवान्, तद्वशः मुग्धः (सन्) दारुयन्त्रवत्, क्वचित् बालवत् अनृत्यत्, क्वचित् उद्गायति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपीभिः स्तोभितः Being encouraged and praised with songs by the gopis, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, तद्वशः under their control, मुग्धः charmed and captivated, दारुयन्त्रवत् like a wooden puppet, क्वचित् sometimes, बालवत् like a child, अनृत्यत् danced, क्वचित् उद्गायति sometimes sang aloud.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord, being encouraged and praised with songs by the gopis, completely charmed and under their control like a wooden puppet, would sometimes dance like a child and sometimes sing aloud. Such is the wonder of the Lord's divine play — the master of all the worlds becomes a willing puppet in the hands of His loving devotees.

बिभर्ति क्वचिदाज्ञप्तः पीठकोन्मानपादुकम् ।

बाहुक्षेपं च कुरुते स्वानां च प्रीतिमावहन् ॥10.11.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: बिभर्ति carries, क्वचित् sometimes, आज्ञप्तः being ordered/commanded, पीठक-उन्मान-पादुकम् a small stool, measuring rod, and sandals, बाहु-क्षेपम् waving of arms, च and, कुरुते does/performs, स्वानाम् of His own people/devotees, च and, प्रीतिम् pleasure/delight, आवहन् bringing/causing

Anvaya: क्वचित् आज्ञप्तः पीठकोन्मानपादुकं बिभर्ति, स्वानां प्रीतिम् आवहन् बाहुक्षेपं च कुरुते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: क्वचित् Sometimes, आज्ञप्तः being commanded, पीठकोन्मानपादुकं a small stool, measuring rod and sandals, बिभर्ति carries, स्वानां प्रीतिम् आवहन् bringing delight and pleasure to His own people, बाहुक्षेपं च कुरुते and waves His arms.

Simple meaning: Sometimes, being commanded by the gopis, He would carry a small stool, a measuring rod and sandals, and would wave His arms about — all the while bringing great delight and joy to His own beloved people.

दर्शयंस्तद्विदां लोक आत्मनो भृत्यवश्यताम् ।

व्रजस्योवाह वै हर्षं भगवान् बालचेष्टितैः ॥10.11.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: दर्शयन् showing/demonstrating, तद्-विदाम् to those who know/understand this, लोके in the world, आत्मनः His own, भृत्य-वश्यताम् submissiveness to His devotees/servants, व्रजस्य of Vraja, उवाह brought/carried, वै indeed, हर्षम् joy/delight, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, बाल-चेष्टितैः by His childlike activities/pastimes

Anvaya: भगवान् तद्विदां लोके आत्मनः भृत्यवश्यतां दर्शयन्, बालचेष्टितैः व्रजस्य हर्षं वै उवाह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवान् The Supreme Lord, तद्विदां लोके to those in the world who understand this, आत्मनः भृत्यवश्यतां दर्शयन् demonstrating His own submissiveness to His devotees, बालचेष्टितैः by His childlike activities and pastimes, व्रजस्य हर्षं the joy of all of Vraja, वै उवाह indeed brought and carried.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord, demonstrated to those who truly understand, His own quality of being completely submissive to the love of His devotees — and through His delightful childlike activities, brought immense joy to all of Vraja.

क्रीणीहि भोः फलानीति श्रुत्वा सत्वरमच्युतः ।

फलार्थी धान्यमादाय ययौ सर्वफलप्रदः ॥10.11.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्रीणीहि please buy, भोः O, फलानि fruits, इति thus, श्रुत्वा having heard, सत्वरम् quickly, अच्युतः Achyuta/Krishna, फल-अर्थी desirous of fruits, धान्यम् grains, आदाय taking, ययौ went, सर्व-फल-प्रदः the giver of all fruits/results

Anvaya: भोः! फलानि क्रीणीहि — इति श्रुत्वा, सर्वफलप्रदः अच्युतः फलार्थी सत्वरं धान्यम् आदाय ययौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भोः! फलानि क्रीणीहि — O! Please buy fruits, इति श्रुत्वा thus having heard, सर्वफलप्रदः the giver of all fruits and results, अच्युतः Achyuta, फलार्थी desirous of fruits, सत्वरं quickly, धान्यम् आदाय taking grains, ययौ went.

Simple meaning: He who is the giver of all fruits and results to the entire universe — that very Achyuta, upon hearing the call of a fruit seller saying "O please buy fruits!", eagerly ran out quickly with some grains in His hands to buy fruits.

फल विक्रयिणी तस्य च्युतधान्यं करद्वयम् ।

फलैरपूरयद् रत्नैः फलभाण्डमपूरि च ॥10.11.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: फल-विक्रयिणी the female fruit seller, तस्य his, च्युत-धान्यम् spilled/fallen grains, कर-द्वयम् both hands, फलैः with fruits, अपूरयत् filled, रत्नैः with jewels/gems, फल-भाण्डम् the basket of fruits, अपूरि च and was filled

अपूरि पूर्, कर्तरि लुङ्लकारः (आत्मनेपदम्) , प्रथमपुरुषः , एकवचनम्  src. Dhatupath, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: फलविक्रयिणी तस्य च्युतधान्यं करद्वयं फलैः अपूरयत्। (तस्याः) फलभाण्डं रत्नैः अपूरि च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: फलविक्रयिणी The fruit seller lady, तस्य his, च्युतधान्यं करद्वयं both hands from which the grains had spilled, फलैः with fruits, अपूरयत् filled. (तस्याः) Her, फलभाण्डं basket of fruits, रत्नैः with jewels and gems, अपूरि च was also filled.

Simple meaning: The female fruit seller filled both His little hands — from which the grains had spilled on the way — with fruits. And in return her basket of fruits was miraculously filled with precious jewels and gems.

Such is the Lord's grace — whoever gives to Him even a trifle receives infinite treasure in return.

सरित्तीरगतं कृष्णं भग्नार्जुनमथाह्वयत् ।

रामं च रोहिणी देवी क्रीडन्तं बालकैर्भृशम् ॥10.11.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: सरित्-तीर-गतम् gone to the riverbank, कृष्णम् Krishna, भग्न-अर्जुनम् the one who broke the arjuna trees, अथ then, आह्वयत् called, रामम् Rama/Balarama, च and, रोहिणी Rohini, देवी the goddess/lady, क्रीडन्तम् playing, बालकैः with children/boys, भृशम् intensely/very much

Anvaya: अथ रोहिणी देवी सरित्तीरगतं बालकैः भृशं क्रीडन्तं भग्नार्जुनं कृष्णं, रामं च आह्वयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Once, रोहिणी देवी mother Rohini, सरित्तीरगतं who had gone to the riverbank, बालकैः भृशं क्रीडन्तं who was playing intensely with the boys, भग्नार्जुनं the one who had broken the arjuna trees, कृष्णं Krishna, रामं च and Rama/Balarama, आह्वयत् called.

Simple meaning: Once Mother Rohini called both Krishna, the one who had broken the arjuna trees — and Balarama, who had gone to the riverbank and who were deeply absorbed in play with the other boys.

नोपेयातां यदाऽहूतौ क्रीडासङ्गेन पुत्रकौ ।

यशोदां प्रेषयामास रोहिणी पुत्रवत्सलाम् ॥10.11.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, उपेयाताम् came/approached, यदा when, आहूतौ being called, क्रीडा-सङ्गेन due to absorption in play, पुत्रकौ the two little sons, यशोदाम् to Yashoda, प्रेषयामास sent, रोहिणी Rohini, पुत्र-वत्सलाम् who is affectionate towards her sons

पुत्रक   पुत्रक/ m. a little son, boy, child (often used as a term of endearment; src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: यदा आहूतौ पुत्रकौ क्रीडासङ्गेन न उपेयातां, रोहिणी पुत्रवत्सलां यशोदां प्रेषयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यदा When, आहूतौ being called, पुत्रकौ the two little sons, क्रीडासङ्गेन due to absorption in play, न उपेयातां did not come, रोहिणी Rohini, पुत्रवत्सलां यशोदां to Yashoda who was affectionate towards her sons, प्रेषयामास sent.

Simple meaning: When the two little sons, being so deeply absorbed in their play, did not come even upon being called, Rohini sent mother Yashoda — who was so affectionate towards her sons — to fetch them.

क्रीडन्तं सा सुतं बालैरतिवेलं सहाग्रजम् ।

यशोदाजोहवीत् कृष्णं पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतस्तनी ॥10.11.14॥

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्थानागतं प्राणम् इव ऐन्द्रियः गणः इव Like the group of senses finding the life force returned to its place, रामादयः बालकाः Rama and the other boys, बकास्यात् मुक्तं freed from the mouth of Baka, तम् Him, उपलभ्य having found, परिरम्भ्य having embraced, निर्वृताः overjoyed and delighted, वत्सान् प्रणीय having led the calves, व्रजम् एत्य having arrived at Vraja, तत् that, जगुः related.

Simple meaning: Rama and the other boys, finding Krishna freed from the mouth of Baka — like the senses finding the life force returned to its place — embraced Him with great joy and delight. Then leading the calves back, they arrived at Vraja and related what had happened.

श्रुत्वा तद् विस्मिता गोपा गोप्यश्चातिप्रियादृताः ।

प्रेत्यागतमिवौत्सुक्यादैक्षन्त तृषितेक्षणाः ॥10.11.54॥

Anvaya: गोपाः गोप्यः च तत् श्रुत्वा विस्मिताः, अतिप्रियादृताः (कृष्णं) प्रेत्यागतम् इव औत्सुक्यात् तृषितेक्षणाः ऐक्षन्त।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोपाः गोप्यः च the cowherd men and the gopis, तत् श्रुत्वा having heard that, विस्मिताः astonished, अतिप्रियादृताः (कृष्णं) Krishna who was held most dear, प्रेत्यागतम् इव as if one returned from death, औत्सुक्यात् out of eagerness and longing, तृषितेक्षणाः with thirsty and eager eyes, ऐक्षन्त gazed upon.

Simple meaning: The cowherd men and the gopis, having heard what had happened, were filled with astonishment — and gazing upon Krishna who was held most dear to them all, with thirsty and eager eyes full of longing, they looked upon Him as if He had returned from death itself.

अहो बतास्य बालस्य बहवो मृत्यवोऽभवन् ।

अप्यासीद् विप्रियं तेषां कृतं पूर्वं यतो भयम् ॥10.11.55॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो alas/oh, बत indeed/truly, अस्य this, बालस्य of the child, बहवः many, मृत्यवः deaths/mortal dangers, अभवन् have come/occurred, अपि even, आसीत् was/is there, विप्रियम् displeasure/offense/harm done, तेषाम् their/of them, कृतम् done, पूर्वम् previously/in the past, यतः from which/wherefrom, भयम् fear/danger

Anvaya: अहो! बत! अस्य बालस्य बहवः मृत्यवः अभवन्। अपि पूर्वं यतः भयं कृतं तेषां विप्रियम् आसीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो बत Oh indeed!, अस्य बालस्य Of this child, बहवः मृत्यवः many mortal dangers, अभवन् have come. अपि But, पूर्वं यतः भयं कृतं earlier, from whom the fear was there, तेषां विप्रियम् आसीत् of them only harm is done.

Simple meaning: Oh indeed, so many mortal dangers have come upon this child! But whoever has previously tried to harm Krishna, evil has befallen on them only.

अथाप्यभिभवन्त्येनं नैव ते घोरदर्शनाः ।

जिघांसयैनमासाद्य नश्यन्त्यग्नौ पतङ्गवत् ॥10.11.56॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ yet/however, अपि even, अभिभवन्ति overcome/harm, एनम् Him, न एव not at all, ते they, घोर-दर्शनाः fearful/terrible in appearance, जिघांसया with the desire to kill, एनम् Him, आसाद्य having approached, नश्यन्ति perish/are destroyed, अग्नौ in fire, पतङ्गवत् like moths

Anvaya: अथ अपि घोरदर्शनाः ते एनं न एव अभिभवन्ति। जिघांसया एनम् आसाद्य अग्नौ पतङ्गवत् नश्यन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ अपि Yet even so, घोरदर्शनाः terrible in appearance, ते they, एनं Him, न एव अभिभवन्ति do not overcome Him at all. जिघांसया With the desire to kill, एनम् आसाद्य having approached Him, अग्नौ पतङ्गवत् like moths in fire, नश्यन्ति they perish.

Simple meaning: Yet even so, these rakshasas, terrible in appearance, do not overcome Him at all. Rather approaching Him with the desire to kill Him, they themselves perish like moths flying into a fire.

अहो ब्रह्मविदां वाचो नासत्याः सन्ति कर्हिचित् ।

गर्गो यदाह भगवानन्वभावि तथैव तत् ॥10.11.57॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो oh/truly, ब्रह्म-विदाम् of the knowers of Brahman, वाचः the words, न not, असत्याः false/untrue, सन्ति are, कर्हिचित् ever/at any time, गर्गः Garga, यत् what, आह said, भगवान् the revered one, अन्वभावि was experienced/came to pass, तथा in that way, एव indeed, तत् that

Anvaya: अहो! ब्रह्मविदां वाचः कर्हिचित् असत्याः न सन्ति। भगवान् गर्गः यत् आह तत् तथा एव अन्वभावि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो oh truly!, ब्रह्मविदां Of the knowers of Brahman, वाचः the words, कर्हिचित् ever, असत्याः न सन्ति are never false. भगवान् गर्गः The revered Garga, यत् आह what he said, तत् that, तथा एव in exactly that way, अन्वभावि came to pass and was experienced.

Simple meaning: Oh truly, the words of the knowers of Brahman are never false at any time. What the revered sage Garga had said — that came to pass in exactly that way.

इति नन्दादयो गोपा: कृष्णरामकथां मुदा ।

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, नन्द-आदयः Nanda and the others, गोपाः the cowherd men, कृष्ण-राम-कथाम् the stories/topics of Krishna and Rama, मुदा with joy, कुर्वन्तः doing/engaging in, रममाणाः च and taking delight, न not, अविन्दन् found/experienced, भव-वेदनाम् the pain/suffering of material existence

Anvaya: इति नन्दादयः गोपाः कृष्णरामकथां मुदा कुर्वन्तः रममाणाः च भववेदनां न अविन्दन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति Thus, नन्दादयः गोपाः Nanda and the other cowherd men, कृष्णरामकथां the stories and topics of Krishna and Rama, मुदा with joy, कुर्वन्तः engaging in, रममाणाः च and taking delight, भववेदनां the pain and suffering of material existence, न अविन्दन् did not find or experience.

Simple meaning: Thus Nanda and the other cowherd men, joyfully engaging in and delighting in the stories and topics of Krishna and Rama, did not experience the pain and suffering of material existence.

Such is the supreme power of krishna-katha — it liberates one completely from the suffering of this world.

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् thus, विहारैः with pastimes/sports, कौमारैः childhood, कौमारम् childhood, जहतुः passed/left behind, व्रजे in Vraja, निलायनैः with games of hiding, सेतु-बन्धैः with building of dams/bridges, मर्कट-उत्प्लवन-आदिभिः with leaping about like monkeys and other such activities

Simple meaning: Thus those two passed through their childhood in Vraja with all manner of childhood pastimes — games of hiding, building dams and bridges, leaping about like monkeys and many other such activities.
