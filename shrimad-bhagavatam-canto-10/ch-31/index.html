Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 31

The Gopis' Song (at the Lord's disappearance) during the Rasa Play

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

रासक्रीडायां गोपीगीतं नाम एकत्रिंशः अध्यायः

(19 Shlokas)

गोप्यः ऊचुः

जयति तेऽधिकं जन्मना व्रजः श्रयत इन्दिरा शश्वदत्र हि ।

दयित दृश्यतां दिक्षु तावकास्त्वयि धृतासवस्त्वां विचिन्वते ॥10.31.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः ऊचुः The gopis said, जयति is victorious/glorified, ते Your, अधिकम् exceedingly, जन्मना by the birth, व्रजः the land of Vraja, श्रयते takes shelter/resides, इन्दिरा Indira (the Goddess of Fortune), शश्वत् eternally, अत्र here, हि indeed, दयित O beloved, दृश्यताम् please be seen, दिक्षु in all directions, तावकाः Your own (devotees), त्वयि in You, धृत-आसवः those whose life breaths are placed, त्वाम् for You, विचिन्वते are searching

Anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः — (हे) दयित! ते जन्मना व्रजः अधिकं जयति। अत्र हि इन्दिरा शश्वत् श्रयते। त्वयि धृतासवः तावकाः त्वां दिक्षु विचिन्वते, (तस्मात्) दृश्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः The gopis said, (हे) दयित O beloved, ते जन्मना By Your birth, व्रजः अधिकं जयति the land of Vraja is exceedingly glorified, अत्र हि इन्दिरा शश्वत् श्रयते indeed here the Goddess of Fortune resides eternally, त्वयि धृतासवः तावकाः Your own people who have placed their very life breaths in You, त्वां दिक्षु विचिन्वते are searching for You in all directions, (तस्मात्) दृश्यताम् (therefore) please reveal Yourself.

Simple Meaning: The gopis said: O Beloved, by Your birth the land of Vraja has become transitionally glorious, so much so that Lakshmi Devi now resides here constantly. We are Your own, and our very lives are sustained only by You. We are searching for You in every direction; please reveal Yourself to us.

शरदुदाशये साधुजातसत्सरसिजोदरश्रीमुषा दृशा ।

सुरतनाथ तेऽशुल्कदासिका वरद निघ्नतो नेह किं वधः ॥10.31.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: शरत्-उदाशये in the lakes of autumn, साधु-जात-सत्-सरसिज-उदर-श्री-मुषा by that which steals the beauty of the interior of the perfectly bloomed, fine lotus, दृशा by Your glance/eye, सुरत-नाथ O Lord of love, ते Your, अशुल्क-दासिकाः unpaid maidservants, वरद O bestower of benedictions, निघ्नतः of the slayer, न not, इह here/in this world, किम् whether, वधः murder

Anvaya: (हे) सुरतनाथ! (हे) वरद! शरदुदाशये साधुजातसत्सरसिजोदरश्रीमुषा दृशा अशुल्कदासिकाः निघ्नतः ते इह वधः किं न?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) सुरतनाथ O Lord of love, (हे) वरद O granter of boons, शरदुदाशये in the autumnal lakes, साधुजातसत्सरसिजोदरश्रीमुषा by Your eyes which steal the beauty of the heart of the most exquisite, well-grown lotuses, दृशा by such a glance, अशुल्कदासिकाः us who are Your unpaid maidservants, निघ्नतः of You who are slaying, ते for You, इह वधः किं न is this not a murder in this world?

Simple Meaning: O Lord of love, O bestower of benedictions! Your eyes are so beautiful that they steal the luster of the hearts of perfectly bloomed lotuses in an autumn pond. We are Your dedicated, unpaid maidservants, yet You are killing us with Your absence after having pierced us with those glances. Is this not a form of murder?

विषजलाप्ययाद् व्यालराक्षसाद् वर्षमारुताद् वैद्युतानलात् ।

वृषमयात्मजाद् विश्वतोभयादृषभ ते वयं रक्षिता मुहुः ॥10.31.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: विष-जल-अप्ययात् from destruction by the poisonous water, व्याल-राक्षसात् from the demon in the form of a serpent (Aghasura), वर्ष-मारुतात् from the rain and wind, वैद्युत-अनलात् from the fire of lightning, वृष-मय-आत्मजात् from the son of bull (Vatsasura) and the son of Maya (Vyomasura), विश्वतः-भयात् from all kinds of fear, ऋषभ O greatest of all, ते by You, वयम् we, रक्षिताः have been protected, मुहुः repeatedly

Anvaya: (हे) ऋषभ! विषजलाप्ययात् व्यालराक्षसात् वर्षमारुतात् वैद्युतानलात् वृषमयात्मजात् विश्वतोभयात् (च) ते वयं मुहुः रक्षिताः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) ऋषभ O greatest of personalities, विषजलाप्ययात् from the poisonous water (of Yamuna), व्यालराक्षसात् from the predatory serpent asura, वर्षमारुतात् from the fierce rain and wind, वैद्युतानलात् from the fire of lightning, वृषमयात्मजात् from the son of the bull, Vatsasura and the son of Maya, Vyomasura विश्वतोभयात् (च) and from all other fears, ते by You, वयं we, मुहुः रक्षिताः have been protected repeatedly.

Simple Meaning: O Greatest of all, You have repeatedly saved us from all kinds of danger—from the poisonous water of the Yamuna, from the serpent Aghasura, from the devastating rains and winds of Indra, from the fire of lightning, and from asuras like Vatsa and Vyoma. You have always protected us from every source of fear.

Note: According to a learned and saintly commentator the incident relating to Vyomasura took place before the Rasa-Lila even though it is described by the sage Shri Shuka later.

न खलु गोपिकानन्दनो भवानखिलदेहिनामन्तरात्मदृक् ।

विखनसार्थितो विश्वगुप्तये सख उदेयिवान् सात्वतां कुले ॥10.31.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, खलु indeed, गोपिका-नन्दनः the son of the gopi (Yashoda), भवान् You, अखिल-देहिनाम् of all embodied beings, अन्तरात्म-दृक् the witness within the heart (the Supersoul), विखनस-अर्थितः prayed for by Brahma, विश्व-गुप्तये for the protection of the universe, सखे O friend, उदेयिवान् You have appeared, सात्वताम् कुले in the family of the Satvatas (the Yadu dynasty)

Anvaya: (हे) सखे! भवान् खलु गोपिकानन्दनः न, (अपि तु) अखिलदेहिनाम् अन्तरात्मदृक्। विखनसार्थितः (सन्) विश्वगुप्तये सात्वतां कुले उदेयिवान्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) सखे O friend, भवान् You, खलु indeed, गोपिकानन्दनः न are not merely the son of the gopi, (अपि तु) but rather, अखिलदेहिनाम् अन्तरात्मदृक् the witness of the soul within all living beings. विखनसार्थितः (सन्) Being prayed for by Brahma, विश्वगुप्तये for the protection of the universe, सात्वतां कुले in the dynasty of the Satvatas, उदेयिवान् You have appeared.

Simple Meaning: O dear Friend, You are not actually just the son of Mother Yashoda. You are the indwelling witness and the Soul within all living beings. Having been prayed for by Lord Brahma to protect the universe, You have now appeared in the Yadu dynasty.

विरचिताभयं वृष्णिधुर्य ते चरणमीयुषां संसृतेर्भयात् ।

करसरोरुहं कान्त कामदं शिरसि धेहि नः श्रीकरग्रहम् ॥10.31.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: विरचित-अभयम् which grants fearlessness, वृष्णि-धुर्य O best of the Vrishnis, ते Your, चरणम् feet, ईयुषाम् of those who have approached, संसृतेः भयात् from the fear of material existence, कर-सरोरुहम् lotus hand, कान्त O beloved, काम-दम् which fulfills all desires, शिरसि on the head, धेहि please place, नः our, श्री-कर-ग्रहम् which has held the hand of Lakshmi

Anvaya: (हे) वृष्णिधुर्य! (हे) कान्त! संसृतेः भयात् ते चरणम् ईयुषां विरचिताभयं कामदं श्रीकरग्रहं (तव) करसरोरुहं नः शिरसि धेहि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) वृष्णिधुर्य O best of the Vrishnis, (हे) कान्त O beloved, संसृतेः भयात् from the fear of material existence, ते चरणम् ईयुषां of those who have approached Your feet, विरचिताभयं which grants fearlessness, कामदं which fulfills all desires, श्रीकरग्रहं which has held the hand of Lakshmi, (तव) करसरोरुहं Your lotus-like hand, नः शिरसि on our heads, धेहि please place.

Simple Meaning: O best of the Vrishnis, O Beloved! Your lotus feet grant fearlessness to all those who approach them for shelter from the cycle of birth and death. O fulfiller of all desires, please place Your lotus hand—the very same hand that holds the hand of Goddess Lakshmi—upon our heads.

व्रजजनार्तिहन् वीर योषिताम् निजजनस्मयध्वंसनस्मित ।

भज सखे भवत्किङ्करीः स्म नो जलरुहाननं चारु दर्शय ॥10.31.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्रज-जन-आर्ति-हन् O destroyer of the suffering of the people of Vraja, वीर O hero, योषिताम् of women, निज-जन-स्मय-ध्वंसन-स्मित O You whose smile destroys the pride of Your own people, भज accept/favor, सखे O friend, भवत्-किङ्करीः Your maidservants, स्म indeed, नः us, जलरुह-आननम् lotus-like face, चारु beautiful, दर्शय please show

Anvaya: (हे) व्रजजनार्तिहन्! (हे) वीर! निजजनस्मयध्वंसनस्मित! (हे) सखे! भवत्किङ्करीः नः भज, (तव) चारु जलरुहाननं दर्शय स्म।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) व्रजजनार्तिहन् O destroyer of the distress of Vraja's people, (हे) वीर O hero of women, निजजनस्मयध्वंसनस्मित O You whose smile shatters the pride of Your devotees, (हे) सखे O friend, भवत्किङ्करीः नः accept us as Your maidservants, (तव) चारु जलरुहाननं Your beautiful lotus face, दर्शय स्म please show to us.

Simple Meaning: O destroyer of the suffering of the residents of Vraja! O Hero! Your smile is enough to shatter the pride of Your followers. O dear Friend, please accept us as Your humble maidservants and show us Your beautiful lotus-like face.

प्रणतदेहिनां पापकर्षनं तृणचरानुगं श्रीनिकेतनम् ।

फणिफणार्पितं ते पदाम्बुजं कृणु कुचेषु नः कृन्धि हृच्छयम् ॥10.31.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रणत-देहिनाम् of those who bow down, पाप-कर्षणम् that which removes sins, तृण-चर-अनुगम् which follows the animals grazing on grass, श्री-निकेतनम् the abode of Lakshmi, फणि-फण-अर्पितम् which was placed on the hoods of the serpent (Kaliya), ते Your, पद-अम्बुजम् lotus feet, कृणु please place, कुचेषु on the breasts, नः our, कृन्धि destroy, हृत्-क्षयम् the desire in the heart (pangs of separation)

कृणु कृन्व् , कर्तरि लोट्लकारः (परस्मैपदम्) , मध्यमपुरुषः , एकवचनम् src. Dhatupatha, Ashtadhyayi - to destroy, to kill

कृन्धि कृत् , कर्तरि लोट्लकारः (परस्मैपदम्) , मध्यमपुरुषः , एकवचनम् src. Dhatupatha, Ashtadhyayi - to surround, to cover

Anvaya: प्रणतदेहिनां पापकषर्णं तृणचरानुगं श्रीनिकेतनं फणिफणार्पितं ते पदाम्बुजं नः कुचेषु कृणु, (तथा च नः) हृच्छयं कृन्धि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रणतदेहिनां पापकषर्णं Which destroys the sins of those who bow down, तृणचरानुगं which follows the cows grazing on grass, श्रीनिकेतनं the abode of the Goddess of Fortune, फणिफणार्पितं which was placed upon the hoods of the Kaliya serpent, ते पदाम्बुजं Your lotus feet, नः कुचेषु कृणु please place on our breasts, (तथा च नः) हृच्छयं कृन्धि and destroy the burning longing in our hearts.

Simple Meaning: O Hero, Your lotus feet destroy the past sins of all who bow before them. Those same feet follow the cows through the forest and are the eternal resting place of Goddess Lakshmi. You once placed them upon the heads of the serpent Kaliya; now, please place them upon our hearts and extinguish the fire of desire and separation that consumes us.

मधुरया गिरा वल्गुवाक्यया बुधमनोज्ञया पुष्करेक्षण ।

विधिकरीरिमा वीर मुह्यतीरधरसीधुनाऽऽप्याययस्व नः ॥10.31.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: मधुरया with sweet, गिरा voices, वल्गु-वाक्यया with charming words, बुध-मनोज्ञया which are attractive to even the learned, पुष्कर-ईक्षण O lotus-eyed one, विधिकरीः maidservants, इमाः these, वीर O hero, मुह्यतीः who are becoming bewildered/fainting, अधर-सीधुना with the nectar of Your lips, आप्याययस्व please revive, नः us

Anvaya: (हे) पुष्करेक्षण! (हे) वीर! मधुरया वल्गुवाक्यया बुधमनोज्ञया गिरा (स्वकीयया) मुह्यतीः इमाः विधिकरीः नः अधरसीधुना आप्याययस्व।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) पुष्करेक्षण O lotus-eyed one, (हे) वीर O hero, मधुरया वल्गुवाक्यया with sweet and charming words, बुधमनोज्ञया गिरा which are delightful even to the wise, (स्वकीयया) by Your own (speech), मुह्यतीः इमाः विधिकरीः नः us, these maidservants who are fainting, अधरसीधुना with the nectar of Your lips, आप्याययस्व please revive.

Simple Meaning: O lotus-eyed one, O Hero! Your sweet, charming speech and delightful words fascinate even the most learned souls. We, Your humble maidservants, are becoming bewildered and losing consciousness in Your absence. Please revive us with the nectar of Your lips.

तव कथामृतं तप्तजीवनं कविभिरीडितं कल्मषापहम् ।

श्रवणमङ्गलं श्रीमदाततं भुवि गृणन्ति ते भूरिदा जनाः ॥10.31.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: तव Your, कथा-अमृतम् the nectar of topics/glories, तप्त-जीवनम् the life-force for those distressed, कविभिः by great poets/sages, ईडितम् glorified, कल्मष-अपहम् that which removes sins, श्रवण-मङ्गलम् auspicious to hear, श्रीमत्-आततम् filled with spiritual beauty and spread far and wide, भुवि on this earth, गृणन्ति chant/spread, ते they, भूरिदाः the most beneficent/great givers, जनाः persons

Anvaya: तव कथामृतं तप्तजीवनं कविभिः ईडितं कल्मषापहं श्रवणमङ्गलं श्रीमदाततं (च अस्ति)। भुवि ते (एतत्) गृणन्ति (ते) जनाः भूरिदाः (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तव कथामृतं The nectar of Your stories, तप्तजीवनं is the life of the distressed, कविभिः ईडितं is glorified by the wise, कल्मषापहं is the destroyer of sins, श्रवणमङ्गलं is auspicious to hear, श्रीमदाततं (च अस्ति) and is full of spiritual splendor and widely expanded. भुवि On this earth, ते (एतत्) गृणन्ति those who chant these glories, (ते) जनाः भूरिदाः (सन्ति) those persons are the most munificent givers.

Simple Meaning: The nectar of Your stories is the life-breath for those suffering in this material world. These glories, praised by great sages, destroy all sins and bestow every auspiciousness upon the listener. They are filled with divine beauty and spread everywhere. Those who chant and distribute Your glories on this earth are surely the most generous of all people.

प्रहसितं प्रिय प्रेमवीक्षणं विहरणं च ते ध्यानमङ्गलम् ।

रहसि संविदो या हृदिस्पृशः कुहक नो मनः क्षोभयन्ति हि ॥10.31.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रहसितम् the hearty laughter, प्रिय O dear one, प्रेम-वीक्षणम् loving glances, विहरणम् wanderings/pastimes, च and, ते Your, ध्यान-मङ्गलम् auspicious to meditate upon, रहसि in private, संविदः conversations, याः which, हृदि-स्पृशः touching the heart, कुहक O deceiver, नः our, मनः minds, क्षोभयन्ति agitate, हि indeed

Anvaya: (हे) प्रिय! ते प्रहसितं प्रेमवीक्षणं विहरणं च ध्यानमङ्गलम् (अस्ति)। रहसि याः हृदिस्पृशः संविदः (ताः), (हे) कुहक, नः मनः क्षोभयन्ति हि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) प्रिय O dear one, ते प्रहसितं Your laughter, प्रेमवीक्षणं loving glances, विहरणं च and pastimes, ध्यानमङ्गलम् are auspicious for meditation. रहसि In private, याः हृदिस्पृशः संविदः those heart-touching conversations which occurred, (हे) कुहक O deceiver, नः मनः our minds, क्षोभयन्ति हि indeed agitate.

Simple Meaning: O dear Krishna, Your laughter, Your loving glances, and Your pastimes are all most auspicious to meditate upon. But O Deceiver, those intimate, heart-touching conversations we shared with You in private now return to our memories and deeply agitate our minds in Your absence.

चलसि यद् व्रजाच्चारयन् पशून् नलिनसुन्दरं नाथ ते पदम् ।

शिलतृणाङ्कुरैः सीदतीति नः कलिलतां मनः कान्त गच्छति ॥10.31.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: चलसि You go, यत् when, व्रजात् from the village of Vraja, चारयन् tending, पशून् the animals, नलिन-सुन्दरम् beautiful as a lotus, नाथ O Lord, ते Your, पदम् feet, शिल-तृण-अङ्कुरैः by sharp stones, grass, and sprouts, सीदति suffer pain, इति thus, नः our, कलिलताम् distress/agitation, मनः minds, कान्त O beloved, गच्छति attain

Anvaya: (हे) नाथ! (हे) कान्त! यत् पशून् चारयन् व्रजात् चलसि, (तदा) ते नलिनसुन्दरं पदं शिलतृणाङ्कुरैः सीदति इति (विचिन्त्य) नः मनः कलिलतां गच्छति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) नाथ O Lord, (हे) कान्त O beloved, यत् पशून् चारयन् when tending the animals, व्रजात् चलसि You walk away from Vraja, (तदा) at that time, ते नलिनसुन्दरं पदं Your feet which are as beautiful as lotuses, शिलतृणाङ्कुरैः by sharp stones, blades of grass, and sprouts, सीदति suffer pain, इति (विचिन्त्य) thinking this, नः मनः our minds, कलिलतां गच्छति feel great distress.

Simple Meaning: O Lord, O Beloved! When You leave Vraja to herd the cows, we become filled with anxiety. We know that Your feet are as tender and beautiful as lotus flowers, and the thought of them being pricked by sharp stones, grass, and grain-stalks makes our minds wander in great pain.

दिनपरिक्षये नीलकुन्तलैर्वनरुहाननं बिभ्रदावृतम् ।

घनरजस्वलं दर्शयन् मुहुर्मनसि नः स्मरं वीर यच्छसि ॥10.31.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: दिन-परिक्षये at the end of the day, नील-कुन्तलैः by dark locks of hair, वनरुह-आननम् lotus-like face, बिभ्रत् carrying/holding, आवृतम् covered, घन-रजस्वलम् thick with dust, दर्शयन् showing, मुहुः repeatedly, मनसि in the mind, नः our, स्मरम् Cupid/desire, वीर O hero, यच्छसि You are placing

Anvaya: (हे) वीर! दिनपरिक्षये नीलकुन्तलैः आवृतं घनरजस्वलं वनरुहाननं बिभ्रत् (त्वं तत्) मुहुः दर्शयन् नः मनसि स्मरं यच्छसि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) वीर O hero, दिनपरिक्षये at the end of the day, नीलकुन्तलैः आवृतं covered by dark curls, घनरजस्वलं smeared with thick dust (from the cows), वनरुहाननं lotus-like face, बिभ्रत् carrying, (त्वं तत्) मुहुः दर्शयन् showing that face repeatedly, नः मनसि in our minds, स्मरं यच्छसि You are placing the fire of desire.

Simple Meaning: O Hero! At the end of the day, You return from the forest, Your lotus face partially covered by Your dark locks and smeared with the thick dust raised by the cows' hooves. By showing us Your beauty in this way again and again, You ignite the flames of longing within our hearts.

प्रणतकामदं पद्मजार्चितं धरणिमण्डनं ध्येयमापदि ।

चरणपङ्कजं शन्तमं च ते रमण नः स्तनेष्वर्पयाधिहन् ॥10.31.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रणत-काम-दम् fulfilling the desires of those who bow down, पद्मज-अर्चितम् worshipped by Brahma (born from the lotus), धरणि-मण्डनम् the ornament of the earth, ध्येयम् to be meditated upon, आपदि in times of distress, चरण-पङ्कजम् lotus feet, शन्-तमम् most auspicious/source of peace, च and, ते Your, रमण O lover, नः our, स्तनेषु on the breasts, अर्पय please place, आधि-हन् O destroyer of mental agony

Anvaya: (हे) रमण! (हे) आधिहन्! प्रणतकामदं पद्मजार्चितं धरणिमण्डनं आपदि ध्येयं शन्तमं च ते चरणपङ्कजं नः स्तनेषु अर्पय।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) रमण O lover, (हे) आधिहन् O destroyer of mental suffering, प्रणतकामदं which fulfills the desires of those who prostrate, पद्मजार्चितं which is worshipped by Lord Brahma, धरणिमण्डनम् which is the ornament of the earth, आपदि ध्येयं which is the object of meditation in times of trouble, शन्तमं च and which is most calming/auspicious, ते चरणपङ्कजं Your lotus feet, नः स्तनेषु अर्पय please place upon our breasts.

Simple Meaning: O Lover, O destroyer of our mental agony! Your lotus feet fulfill the desires of all who surrender to them. They are worshipped by Lord Brahma, they are the beauty of this earth, and they are the only object of meditation in times of distress. Please place those most auspicious feet upon our hearts to soothe our pain.

सुरतवर्धनं शोकनाशनं स्वरितवेणुना सुष्ठु चुम्बितम् ।

इतररागविस्मारणं नृणां वितर वीर नस्तेऽधरामृतम् ॥10.31.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: सुरत-वर्धनम् increasing the joy of love, शोक-नाशनम् destroying all grief, स्वरित-वेणुना by the vibrated flute, सुष्ठु touchingly/perfectly, चुम्बितम् kissed, इतर-राग-विस्मारणम् making one forget all other attachments, नृणाम् for men/people, वितर please distribute, वीर O hero, नः to us, ते Your, अधर-अमृतम् the nectar of Your lips

Anvaya: (हे) वीर! सुरतवर्धनं शोकनाशनं स्वरितवेणुना सुष्ठु चुम्बितं नृणाम् इतररागविस्मारणं ते अधरामृतं नः वितर।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) वीर O hero, सुरतवर्धनं which increases conjugal longing, शोकनाशनं which destroys all sorrow, स्वरितवेणुना सुष्ठु चुम्बितं which is perfectly kissed by the sounding flute, नृणाम् इतररागविस्मारणं which makes people forget all other mundane attachments, ते अधरामृतं the nectar of Your lips, नः वितर please grant to us.

Simple Meaning: O Hero, please grant us the nectar of Your lips! That nectar increases our love, destroys all grief, and is enjoyed by Your vibrating flute. It has the power to make anyone who tastes it forget all other worldly desires. Please bestow it upon us.

अटति यद् भवानह्नि काननं त्रुटिर्युगायते त्वामपश्यताम् ।

कुटिलकुन्तलं श्रीमुखं च ते जड उदीक्षतां पक्ष्मकृद् दृशाम् ॥10.31.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: अटति wander, यत् when, भवान् You, अह्नि during the day, काननम् the forest, त्रुटिः a fraction of a second, युगायते becomes like an age (Yuga), त्वाम् You, अपश्यताम् for those who do not see, कुटिल-कुन्तलम् with curly locks of hair, श्री-मुखम् beautiful face, च and, ते Your, जडः foolish, उदीक्षताम् for those who are looking, पक्ष्म-कृत् the creator of eyelids, दृशाम् of the eyes

Anvaya: (हे नाथ!) यत् भवान् अह्नि काननं अटति, (तदा) त्वाम् अपश्यतां (नः) त्रुटिः युगायते। कुटिलकुन्तलं ते श्रीमुखं च उदीक्षतां दृशाम् (अन्तैः) पक्ष्मकृत् जडः (इति अस्माभिः मन्यते)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे नाथ!) O Lord, यत् भवान् अह्नि काननं अटति when You wander into the forest during the day, (तदा) त्वाम् अपश्यतां (नः) for us who do not see You, त्रुटिः युगायते a mere moment feels like a Yuga. कुटिलकुन्तलं ते श्रीमुखं च And Your beautiful face framed by curly hair, उदीक्षतां for those of us watching, पक्ष्मकृत् the creator of eyelids, जडः is considered foolish.

Simple Meaning: O Lord, when You leave for the forest during the day, a single moment feels like an entire age to us because we cannot see You. And when You return, we consider the creator of the eyes (Brahma) to be foolish for having made eyelids, because they blink and interrupt our vision of Your beautiful face and curly locks.

पतिसुतान्वयभ्रातृबान्धवानतिविलङ्घ्य तेऽन्त्यच्युतागताः ।

गतिविदस्तवोद्गीतमोहिताः कितव योषितः कस्त्यजेन्निशि ॥10.31.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: पति-सुत-अन्वय-भ्रातृ-बान्धवान् husbands, sons, families, brothers, and relatives, अतिविलङ्घ्य completely abandoning/transgressing, ते Your, अन्ति-अच्युत-आगताः have come to the presence, O infallible one, गति-विदः knowing Your movements/intentions, तव Your, उद्गीत-मोहिताः enchanted by the loud singing (of the flute), कितव O deceiver, योषितः women, कः who, त्यजेत् would abandon, निशि at night

Anvaya: (हे) अच्युत! पतिसुतान्वयभ्रातृबान्धवान् अतिविलङ्घ्य तव उद्गीतमोहिताः गतिविदः योषितः ते अन्ति आगताः। (हे) कितव! निशि (एताः) कः त्यजेत्?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) अच्युत O infallible Lord, पतिसुतान्वयभ्रातृबान्धवान् husbands, sons, lineage, brothers, and relatives, अतिविलङ्घ्य having completely abandoned, तव उद्गीतमोहिताः enchanted by Your loud song, गतिविदः knowing Your ways, योषितः women, ते अन्ति आगताः have come into Your presence. (हे) कितव O deceiver, निशि at night, (एताः) कः त्यजेत् who would abandon such women?

Simple Meaning: O Infallible one, we have completely abandoned our husbands, sons, families, brothers, and relatives to come to You, drawn by the enchanting music of Your flute. We know Your ways and have surrendered ourselves to You. O Deceiver, what man would abandon such women who have come to him in the middle of the night?

रहसि संविदं हृच्छयोदयं प्रहसिताननं प्रेमवीक्षणम् ।

बृहदुरः श्रियो वीक्ष्य धाम ते मुहुरतिस्पृहा मुह्यते मनः ॥10.31.17॥

Word-by-word Meaning: रहसि in private/in secret, संविदम् intimate conversation/confidential talks, हृच्छय-उदयम् which awakens desire in the heart, प्रहसित-आननम् Your smiling face, प्रेम-वीक्षणम् loving glances, बृहत्-उरः Your broad chest, श्रियः of the Goddess of Fortune (Lakshmi), धाम the abode/resting place, ते Your, वीक्ष्य seeing/perceiving, मुहुः repeatedly/constantly, अतिस्पृहा with intense longing/excessive desire, मुह्यते becomes bewildered/faints, मनः the mind

Anvaya: (हे प्रिय!) रहसि हृच्छयोदयं संविदं प्रहसिताननं प्रेमवीक्षणं श्रियः धाम ते बृहदुरः च वीक्ष्य (अस्माकं) मनः मुहुः अतिस्पृहा (सत्) मुह्यते।

Literal Meaning of the Anvaya: (हे प्रिय!) O Beloved! रहसि in private, हृच्छयोदयं which awakens the desire of the heart, संविदं (Your) intimate conversation, प्रहसिताननं (Your) smiling face, प्रेमवीक्षणं (Your) loving glances, श्रियः धाम the abode of the Goddess Lakshmi, ते बृहदुरः च and Your broad chest, वीक्ष्य seeing (all these), (अस्माकं) मनः our minds, मुहुः repeatedly, अतिस्पृहा (सत्) being filled with intense longing, मुह्यते become completely bewildered.

Simple Meaning: O Beloved, when we remember Your intimate conversations in private—which ignite the fire of love in our hearts—and see Your smiling face and loving glances, our hearts are overcome. And when we look upon Your broad chest, which is the eternal resting place of the Goddess of Fortune, we are filled with such intense longing that our minds become completely lost in bewilderment.

व्रजवनौकसां व्यक्तिरङ्ग ते वृजिनहन्त्र्यलं विश्वमङ्गलम् ।

त्यज मनाक् च नस्त्वत्स्पृहात्मनां स्वजनहृद्रुजां यन्निषूदनम् ॥10.31.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्रज-वन-ओकसाम् for the dwellers of the forest of Vraja, व्यक्तिः appearance/incarnation, अङ्ग O dear one, ते Your, वृजिन-हन्त्री the destroyer of sins/suffering, अलम् completely, विश्व-मङ्गलम् the auspiciousness of the whole universe, त्यज please give, मनाक् a little/at least, च and, नः to us, त्वत्-स्पृहा-आत्मनाम् whose souls long for You, स्व-जन-हृद्-रुजाम् of the pain in the hearts of Your own people, यत् which, निषूदनम् the medicine/the destroyer

Anvaya: (हे) अङ्ग! ते व्यक्तिः व्रजवनौकसां वृजिनहन्त्री (अस्ति, तथा सा) विश्वमङ्गलम् अलम् (अस्ति)। त्वत्स्पृहात्मनां नः स्वजनहृद्रुजां यत् निषूदनं (तत्) मनाक् त्यज।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) अङ्ग O dear one, ते व्यक्तिः Your appearance, व्रजवनौकसां वृजिनहन्त्री is the destroyer of the sufferings of Vraja's forest-dwellers, (तथा सा) and it, विश्वमङ्गलम् अलम् is perfectly auspicious for the entire universe. त्वत्स्पृहात्मनां नः for us whose souls long for You, स्वजनहृद्रुजां the disease of the heart of Your own people, यत् निषूदनं which is the cure, (तत्) that, मनाक् त्यज please give even a little.

Simple Meaning: O dear Krishna, Your appearance on this earth destroys the suffering of the people of Vraja and brings auspiciousness to the entire universe. Our hearts are aching with longing for You; please grant us just a little of that medicine which cures the grief of Your own devotees.

यत्ते सुजातचरणाम्बुरुहं स्तनेषु भीताः शनैः प्रिय दधीमहि कर्कशेषु ।

तेनाटवीमटसि तद् व्यथते न किंस्वित् कूर्पादिभिर्भ्रमति धीर्भवदायुषां नः ॥10.31.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: यत् which, ते Your, सुजात-चरण-अम्बुरुहम् well-formed lotus feet, स्तनेषु on the breasts, भीताः being afraid, शनैः gently, प्रिय O beloved, दधीमहि we place, कर्कशेषु on the hard/rough, तेन with those (feet), अटवीम् the forest, अटसि You wander, तत् that, व्यथते are pained, न किंस्वित् are they not?, कूर्पादिभिः by small stones and similar things, भ्रमति wanders/is agitated, धीः the mind, भवत्-आयुषाम् of those whose life is You, नः our

Anvaya: (हे) प्रिय! ते यत् सुजातचरणाम्बुरुहं (वयं) भीताः (सत्यः) कर्कशेषु स्तनेषु शनैः दधीमहि, तेन (त्वम्) अटवीम् अटसि। तत् कूर्पादिभिः न व्यथते किंस्वित्? (इति विचिन्त्य) भवदायुषां नः धीः भ्रमति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) प्रिय O beloved, ते यत् सुजातचरणाम्बुरुहं those lotus feet of Yours which are so beautifully formed, (वयं) भीताः (सत्यः) we, being afraid (of hurting them), कर्कशेषु स्तनेषु on our hard breasts, शनैः दधीमहि place very gently, तेन with those (feet), (त्वम्) अटवीम् अटसि You wander through the forest. तत् कूर्पादिभिः न व्यथते किंस्वित् Are they not pained by small stones and pebbles?, (इति विचिन्त्य) thinking thus, भवदायुषां नः for us whose lifespan is only You, धीः भ्रमति our minds are reeling.

Simple Meaning: O Beloved, Your lotus feet are so tender that we place them upon our breasts with great care, fearing our own bodies are too harsh for them. To think that You are now wandering through the forest with those same feet, perhaps treading upon sharp stones and thorns, makes our hearts faint. Our lives are centered entirely on You, and our minds are filled with distress at the thought of Your discomfort.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “रासक्रीडायां गोपीगीतं नाम” एकत्रिंशः अध्यायः ॥31॥

Thus ends the thirty-first discourse named “The Gopis' song, (at The Lord's disappearance) during the Rasa-Play” in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
