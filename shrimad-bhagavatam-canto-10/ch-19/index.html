Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 19

The Lord swallows up a wild fire (again)

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

दावाग्निपानं नाम एकोनविंशः अध्यायः

(16 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

कीडासक्तेषु गोपेषु तद्गावो दूरचारिणीः।

स्वैरं चरन्त्यो विविशुस्तृणलोभेन गह्वरम् ॥10.19.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, क्रीडा-आसक्तेषु while being absorbed in playing, गोपेषु the cowherd boys, तत्-गावः their cows, दूर-चारिणीः wandering far away, स्वैरम् at their own will, चरन्त्यः grazing, विविशुः entered, तृण-लोभेन out of greed for grass, गह्वरम् a dense forest/thicket

Anvaya: श्रीशुकः उवाच — गोपेषु क्रीडासक्तेषु (सत्सु), तद्गावः दूरचारिणीः स्वैरं चरन्त्यः तृणलोभेन गह्वरं विविशुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, गोपेषु क्रीडासक्तेषु while the cowherd boys were absorbed in their games, तद्गावः their cows, दूरचारिणीः wandering far off, स्वैरं चरन्त्यः grazing at their own will, तृणलोभेन out of greed for grass, गह्वरं विविशुः entered a dense thicket.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: While the cowherd boys were completely preoccupied with their sporting and games, their cows wandered far away. Following their own whims and lured by the lush green grass, the animals eventually entered a dense forest.

अजा गावो महिष्यश्च निर्विशन्त्यो वनाद् वनम् ।

इषीकाटवीं निर्विविशुः क्रन्दन्त्यो दावतर्षिताः ॥10.19.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: अजाः goats, गावः cows,महिष्यः buffaloes, च and,निर्विशन्त्यः entering, वनात् from (one) forest, वनम् to (another) forest, इषीका-अटवीम् thicket of sharp grass, निर्विविशुः they entered, क्रन्दन्त्यः crying out, दाव-तर्षिताः distressed by the heat and thirst

Anvaya: अजाः गावः महिष्यः च वनात् वनं निर्विशन्त्यः (सत्यः) दावतर्षिताः क्रन्दन्त्यः इषीकाटवीं निर्विविशुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अजाः गावः महिष्यः च The goats, cows, and buffaloes, वनात् वनं निर्विशन्त्यः entering from one forest to another, दावतर्षिताः being distressed by heat and thirst, क्रन्दन्त्यः crying out, इषीकाटवीं निर्विविशुः entered a thicket of sharp grass.

Simple meaning: The goats, cows, and buffaloes wandering from one forest to another, troubled by both heat and intense thirst, crying out in distress, entered a thicket of sharp grass.

तेऽपश्यन्तः पशून् गोपाः कृष्णरामादयस्तदा ।

जातानुतापा न विदुर्विचिन्वन्तो गवां गतिम् ॥10.19.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: ते they (the boys), अपश्यन्तः not seeing, पशून् the animals/cows, गोपाः the cowherd boys, कृष्ण-राम-आदयः Krishna, Balarama, and others, तदा then, जात-अनुतापाः filled with regret, न विदुः did not know, विचिन्वन्तः searching, गवाम् of the cows, गतिम् the path/movement

Anvaya: तदा पशून् अपश्यन्तः ते कृष्णरामादयः गोपाः जातानुतापाः (सन्तः) विचिन्वन्तः (अपि) गवां गतिं न विदुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तदा Then, पशून् अपश्यन्तः not seeing the animals, ते कृष्णरामादयः गोपाः those cowherd boys led by Krishna and Balarama, जातानुतापाः becoming filled with regret, विचिन्वन्तः searching, गवां गतिं न विदुः did not know the path of the cows.

Simple meaning: Not finding the cows anywhere nearby, Krishna, Balarama, and the other cowherd boys felt deep regret for having neglected their duties while playing. Despite searching everywhere, they could not figure out which way the herd had gone.

तृणैस्तत्खुरदच्छिन्नैर्गोष्पदैरङ्कितैर्गवाम् ।

मार्गमन्वगमन् सर्वे नष्टाजीव्या विचेतसः ॥10.19.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तृणैः by the grass, तत्-खुर-दत्-छिन्नैः bitten by their teeth and cut by their hooves, गोष्पदैः by the hoofprints, अङ्कितैः marked, गवाम् of the cows, मार्गम् the path, अन्वगमन् followed, सर्वे all (of them), नष्ट-आजीव्याः lost their very source of life, विचेतसः bewildered/anxious

Anvaya: सर्वे नष्टाजीव्यः विचेतसः (गोपाः) तत्खुरदच्छिन्नैः तृणैः अङ्कितैः गवां गोष्पदैः मार्गम् अन्वगमन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वे All of them, नष्टाजीव्यः lost their very livelihood, विचेतसः being bewildered, तत्खुरदच्छिन्नैः तृणैः by the grass cut by those hooves and teeth, अङ्कितैः गवां गोष्पदैः and by the marked hoofprints of the cows, मार्गम् अन्वगमन् followed the path.

Simple meaning: The cowherd boys who had lost their source of livelihood, felt completely bewildered and began to track the herd. They followed the trail of grass that had been bitten by the cows' teeth or trampled by their hooves, carefully tracing the hoofprints marked upon the earth.

मुञ्जाटव्यां भ्रष्टमार्गं क्रन्दमानं स्वगोधनम् ।

सम्प्राप्य तृषिताः श्रान्तास्ततस्ते संन्यवर्तयन् ॥10.19.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: मुञ्जा-अटव्याम् in the Munja forest, भ्रष्ट-मार्गम् having lost their way, क्रन्दमानम् crying out, स्व-गोधनम् their own wealth of cows, सम्प्राप्य having found, तृषिताः thirsty, श्रान्ताः exhausted, ततः then, ते they (the boys), सम्-न्यवर्तयन् turned them back

Anvaya: मुञ्जाटव्यांभ्रष्टमार्गं क्रन्दमानं स्वगोधनं सम्प्राप्य, तृषिताः श्रान्ताः ते ततः (पशून्) संन्यवर्तयन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मुञ्जाटव्यां In the Munja forest, भ्रष्टमार्गं क्रन्दमानं स्वगोधनं सम्प्राप्य having found their own wealth of cows which were crying and had lost their way, तृषिताः श्रान्ताः being thirsty and tired, ते they, ततः (पशून्) संन्यवर्तयन् then turned the animals back from there.

Simple meaning: In the dense Munja forest, the boys finally located their own herd of cows, which had lost their way and were bellowing in distress. Although the boys themselves were exhausted and parched with thirst, they immediately began to round up the animals to lead them back.

ता आहूता भगवता मेघगम्भीरया गिरा ।

स्वनाम्नां निनदं श्रुत्वा प्रतिनेदुः प्रहर्षिताः ॥10.19.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताः they (the cows), आहूताः being called, भगवता by the Supreme Lord (Krishna), मेघ-गम्भीरया as deep as a rumbling cloud, गिरा with a voice, स्व-नाम्नाम् of their own names, निनदम् the sound, श्रुत्वा hearing, प्रतिनेदुः they bellowed in response, प्रहर्षिताः being very joyful

Anvaya: भगवता मेघगम्भीरया गिरा आहूताः ताः स्वनाम्नां निनदं श्रुत्वा प्रहर्षिताः (सत्यः) प्रतिनेदुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवता By the Lord, मेघगम्भीरया गिरा with a voice deep like a cloud, आहूताः being called, ताः those cows, स्वनाम्नां निनदं श्रुत्वा hearing the sound of their own names, प्रहर्षिताः being joyful, प्रतिनेदुः bellowed in response.

Simple meaning: When the Supreme Lord Krishna called out to them with a voice as deep and resonant as a rumbling rain cloud, the cows were filled with joy. Upon hearing their individual names being called, they all bellowed back in happy response.

ततः समन्ताद् वनधूमकेतुर्यदृच्छयाभूत् क्षयकृद् वनौकसाम् ।

समीरितः सारथिनोल्बणोल्मुकैर्विलेलिहानः स्थिरजङ्गमान् महान् ॥10.19.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, समन्तात् on all sides, वन-धूम-केतुः a forest fire - which has smoke as its banner, यदृच्छया by chance/suddenly, अभूत् became, क्षय-कृत् causing destruction, वन-ओकसाम् of the forest dwellers, समीरितः fanned/driven, सारथिना by its charioteer (the wind), उल्बण-उल्मुकैः with terrible sparks/firebrands, विलेलिहानः licking (with its tongues of flame), स्थिर-जङ्गमान् the stationary and the moving beings, महान् great/massive

Anvaya: ततः सारथिना समीरितः वनधूमकेतुः समन्तात् यदृच्छया अभूत्, उल्बणोल्मुकैः स्थिरजङ्गमान् विलेलिहानः महान् (सः) वनौकसाम् क्षयकृत् (अभूत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, सारथिना समीरितः driven by its charioteer (the wind), वनधूमकेतुः a forest fire, समन्तात् यदृच्छया अभूत् appeared suddenly on all sides. उल्बणोल्मुकैः With terrible sparks, स्थिरजङ्गमान् विलेलिहानः licking both moving and non-moving beings, महान् that great fire, वनौकसाम् क्षयकृत् (अभूत्) became the destroyer of the forest dwellers.

Simple meaning: Suddenly, a massive forest fire erupted on all sides, threatening everyone in the forest. Driven by its "charioteer," the wind, the fire grew fierce, sending out terrible sparks and licking at all moving and non-moving creatures with its tongues of flame. It appeared like a great force of destruction for all the inhabitants of the woods.

तमापतन्तं परितो दवाग्निं गोपाश्च गावः प्रसमीक्ष्य भीताः ।

ऊचुश्च कृष्णं सबलं प्रपन्ना यथा हरिं मृत्युभयार्दिता जनाः ॥10.19.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् that, आपतन्तम् falling upon/advancing, परितः on all sides, दव-अग्निम् forest fire, गोपाः the cowherd boys, च and, गावः the cows, प्रसमीक्ष्य seeing well, भीताः frightened, ऊचुः said/spoke, च and, कृष्णम् to Krishna, स-बलम् with Balarama, प्रपन्नाः taking shelter, यथा just as, हरिम् to Lord Hari (Vishnu), मृत्यु-भय-अर्दिताः distressed by the fear of death, जनाः people

Anvaya: परितः आपतन्तं तं दवाग्निं प्रसमीक्ष्य भीताः गोपाः गावः च प्रपन्नाः (सन्तः) सबलं कृष्णम् ऊचुः, यथा मृत्युभयार्दिताः जनाः हरिम् (ऊचुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: परितः आपतन्तं तं दवाग्निं प्रसमीक्ष्य Seeing that forest fire advancing from all sides, भीताः गोपाः गावः च the frightened cowherd boys and cows, प्रपन्नाः taking shelter, सबलं कृष्णम् ऊचुः spoke to Krishna and Balarama, यथा just as, मृत्युभयार्दिताः जनाः people distressed by the fear of death, हरिम् (ऊचुः) (speak) to Lord Hari.

Simple meaning: Seeing the fierce forest fire closing in from every direction, the cowherd boys and the cows became terrified. Seeking shelter, they cried out to Krishna and Balarama, just as people who are haunted by the fear of death surrender and pray to the Supreme Lord Hari.

कृष्ण कृष्ण महावीर हे रामामितविक्रम ।

दावाग्निना दह्यमानान् प्रपन्नांस्त्रातुमर्हथः ॥10.19.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्ण कृष्ण O Krishna, O Krishna, महा-वीर O great hero, हे राम O Balarama, अमित-विक्रम of unlimited prowess, दावाग्निना by the forest fire, दह्यमानान् those who are being burned, प्रपन्नान् those who have surrendered, त्रातुम् to save, अर्हथः You both should

Anvaya: कृष्ण कृष्ण महावीर! हे अमितविक्रम राम! दावाग्निना दह्यमानान् प्रपन्नान् (अस्मान्) त्रातुम् अर्हथः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्ण कृष्ण महावीर! O Krishna, Krishna, the great hero! हे अमितविक्रम राम! O Balarama of unlimited prowess! दावाग्निना दह्यमानान् (अस्मान्) (us) who are being burned by the forest fire, प्रपन्नान् who have surrendered, त्रातुम् अर्हथः You both should save.

Simple meaning: "O Krishna! O Krishna! O great Hero! O Balarama of unlimited strength! We are being consumed by this terrible forest fire. We have surrendered completely unto You—please, save us who are Your own!"

नूनं त्वद्बान्धवाः कृष्ण न चार्हन्त्यवसीदितुम् ।

वयं हि सर्वधर्मज्ञ त्वन्नाथास्त्वत्परायणाः ॥10.19.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: नूनम् surely, त्वत्-बान्धवाः Your friends/kinsmen, कृष्ण O Krishna, न not, च and, अर्हन्ति deserve, अवसीदितुम् to perish/suffer, वयम् we, हि indeed, सर्व-धर्म-ज्ञ O Knower of all duties, त्वत्-नाथाः having You as our Master, त्वत्-परायणाः completely devoted to You

Anvaya: कृष्ण! त्वद्बान्धवाः (वयं) नूनम् अवसीदितुं न अर्हन्ति। सर्वधर्मज्ञ! वयं हि त्वन्नाथाः त्वत्परायणाः (च स्मः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्ण! O Krishna! त्वद्बान्धवाः Your kinsmen, नूनम् अवसीदितुं न अर्हन्ति surely do not deserve to perish. सर्वधर्मज्ञ! O Knower of all duties! वयं हि त्वन्नाथाः for we have You as our Master, त्वत्परायणाः and are completely devoted to You.

Simple meaning: "O Krishna, surely Your own friends and dear ones do not deserve to perish in this way. O Knower of all righteousness, we belong to You. We have no other master but You, and we are completely devoted to You."

श्री शुक उवाच

वचो निशम्य कृपणं बन्धूनां भगवान् हरिः ।

निमीलयत मा भैष्ट लोचनानीत्यभाषत ॥10.19.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, वचः the words, निशम्य hearing, कृपणम् piteous/distressed, बन्धूनाम् of His friends, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, हरिः Hari, निमीलयत close, मा भैष्ट do not be afraid, लोचनानि your eyes, इति thus, अभाषत He spoke

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — बन्धूनां कृपणं वचः निशम्य भगवान् हरिः "मा भैष्ट, लोचनानि निमीलयत" इति अभाषत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said: बन्धूनां कृपणं वचः निशम्य Hearing the piteous words of His friends, भगवान् हरिः the Supreme Lord Hari, मा भैष्ट do not be afraid, लोचनानि निमीलयत close your eyes, इति अभाषत thus He spoke.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: Hearing the distressed cries of His dear friends, the Supreme Lord Hari told them, "Do not be afraid. Just close your eyes."

तथेति मीलिताक्षेषु भगवानग्निमुल्बणम् ।

पीत्वा मुखेन तान् कृच्छ्राद् योगाधीशो व्यमोचयत् ॥10.19.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: तथा इति "So be it", मीलित-अक्षेषु while their eyes were closed, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अग्निम् the fire, उल्बणम् terrible/fierce, पीत्वा having drunk, मुखेन with His mouth, तान् them, कृच्छ्रात् from the terrible danger, योग-अधीशः the Lord of all mystic powers, व्यमोचयत् delivered/released

Anvaya: "तथा" इति मीलिताक्षेषु (तेषु), योगाधीशः भगवान् उल्बणम् अग्निं मुखेन पीत्वा तान् कृच्छ्रात् व्यमोचयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: "तथा" इति मीलिताक्षेषु Saying "So be it," while their eyes were closed, योगाधीशः भगवान् the Supreme Lord, the Master of all yoga, उल्बणम् अग्निं मुखेन पीत्वा drinking the fierce fire with His mouth, तान् कृच्छ्रात् व्यमोचयत् delivered them from that terrible danger.

Simple meaning: Saying "So be it," the cowherd boys closed their eyes. Then, the Supreme Lord Krishna, the Master of all mystic power, simply swallowed the terrible forest fire with His mouth, instantly delivering His friends and the cows from the deadly peril.

ततश्च तेऽक्षीण्युन्मील्य पुनर्भाण्डीरमापिताः ।

निशाम्य विस्मिता आसन्नात्मानं गाश्च मोचिताः ॥10.19.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, च and, ते they (the boys), अक्षीणि their eyes, उन्मील्य opening, पुनः again, भाण्डीरम् the Bhandira tree/forest, आपिताः reached/brought to, निशाम्य seeing, विस्मिताः astonished, आसन् they were, आत्मानम् themselves, गाः the cows, च and, मोचिताः saved

Anvaya: ततः च ते अक्षीणि उन्मील्य पुनः भाण्डीरम् आपिताः, आत्मानं गाः च मोचिताः निशाम्य विस्मिताः आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः च ते And then they, अक्षीणि उन्मील्य opening their eyes, पुनः भाण्डीरम् आपिताः were again brought back to the Bhandira forest, आत्मानं गाः च मोचिताः निशाम्य seeing themselves and the cows saved, विस्मिताः आसन् were astonished.

Simple meaning: When the cowherd boys opened their eyes, they found themselves back at the Bhandira tree. They were absolutely astonished to see that they and all their cows had been completely saved from the fire and miraculously transported back to safety.

कृष्णस्य योगवीर्यं तद् योगमायानुभावितम् ।

दावाग्नेरात्मनः क्षेमं वीक्ष्य ते मेनिरेऽमरम् ॥10.19.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णस्य of Krishna, योग-वीर्यम् the mystic power, तत् that, योगमाया-अनुभावितम् manifested by His internal potency (yogamaya), दावाग्नेः from the forest fire, आत्मनः of themselves, क्षेमम् the safety, वीक्ष्य seeing, ते they (the cowherd boys), मेनिरे thought/considered (Him), अमरम् a demigod

Anvaya: योगमायानुभावितं तत् कृष्णस्य योगवीर्यं (वीक्ष्य), दावाग्नेः आत्मनः क्षेमं च वीक्ष्य ते (कृष्णम्) अमरं मेनिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: योगमायानुभावितं तत् कृष्णस्य योगवीर्यं That mystic power of Krishna manifested through Yogamaya, दावाग्नेः आत्मनः क्षेमं च वीक्ष्य and seeing their own safety from the forest fire, ते (कृष्णम्) अमरं मेनिरे they considered Krishna to be a demigod.

Simple meaning: Witnessing that extraordinary mystic power of Krishna—which was displayed through His internal potency—and seeing how they had been miraculously saved from the forest fire, the cowherd boys were struck with wonder. They began to think that their friend Krishna must be a powerful demigod.

गाः सन्निवर्त्य सायाह्ने सहरामो जनार्दनः ।

वेणुं विरणयन् गोष्ठमगाद् गोपैरभिष्टुतः ॥10.19.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: गाः the cows, सन्निवर्त्य having rounded up, सायाह्ने in the late afternoon/evening, सह-रामः along with Balarama, जनार्दनः Krishna, वेणुम् the flute, विरणयन् playing loudly, गोष्ठम् to the cow-pasture/village, अगात् went, गोपैः by the cowherd boys, अभिष्टुतः being praised

Anvaya: सायाह्ने सहरामः जनार्दनः गाः सन्निवर्त्य वेणुं विरणयन् गोपैः अभिष्टुतः (सन्) गोष्ठम् अगात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सायाह्ने In the evening, सहरामः जनार्दनः Lord Krishna along with Balarama, गाः सन्निवर्त्य having rounded up the cows, वेणुं विरणयन् playing His flute, गोपैः अभिष्टुतः being praised by the cowherd boys, गोष्ठम् अगात् went to the village.

Simple meaning: In the evening, Lord Krishna and Balarama gathered the cows together. As they headed back toward the village of Vraja, Krishna played His flute beautifully while the cowherd boys followed behind, singing His praises for the wonderful deeds He had performed.

गोपीनां परमानन्द आसीद् गोविन्ददर्शने ।

क्षणं युगशतमिव यासां येन विनाभवत् ॥10.19.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोपीनाम् of the gopis, परम-आनन्दः supreme bliss, आसीत् there was, गोविन्द-दर्शने in seeing Govinda (Krishna), क्षणम् a moment, युग-शतम् a hundred ages, इव like, यासाम् for whom, येन which (Krishna), विना without, अभवत् became

Anvaya: गोविन्ददर्शने गोपीनां परमानन्दः आसीत्, यासां येन विना क्षणम् (अपि) युगशतम् इव अभवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोविन्ददर्शने In seeing Govinda, गोपीनां परमानन्दः आसीत् there was supreme bliss for the gopis, यासां for whom, येन विना without Him, क्षणम् (अपि) even a moment, युगशतम् इव अभवत् became like a hundred ages.

Simple meaning: The gopis experienced the highest state of bliss upon seeing Govinda return. For them, every moment without Him was as long as a hundred yugas.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशम स्कन्धे दावाग्निपानं नाम एकोनविंशः अध्यायः॥19॥

Thus ends the nineteenth discourse named, 'Shree Krishna swallows up a wildfire (again),' in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
