Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 42

Krishna’s grace on the hunchback woman, breaking of the sacrificial bow, fear of Kamsa, and a description of the amphitheatre for wrestlers

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

कृष्णस्य कुब्जायां कृपा, धनुषभङ्गः, कंसस्य भयं, मल्लरङ्गोपवर्णनं नाम द्विचत्वारिंशः अध्यायः

(38 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

अथ व्रजन् राजपथेन माधवः स्त्रियं गृहीताङ्गविलेपभाजनम् ।

विलोक्य कुब्जां युवतीं वराननां यान्तीं प्रहसन् रसप्रदः ॥10.42.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अथ then, व्रजन् walking, राज-पथेन on the royal road, माधवः Krishna (the husband of the goddess of fortune), स्त्रियम् a woman, गृहीत-अङ्ग-विलेप-भाजनम् carrying a vessel of fragrant body ointments, विलोक्य seeing, कुब्जाम् a hunchbacked woman, युवतीम् young, वर-आननाम् with a beautiful face, यान्तीम् walking along, प्रहसन् smiling, रस-प्रदः the bestower of transcendental pleasure

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — अथ राजपथेन व्रजन् रसप्रदः माधवः, गृहीताङ्गविलेपभाजनं यान्तीं कुब्जां युवतीं वराननां स्त्रियं विलोक्य प्रहसन् (ताम् उवाच)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अथ then, राजपथेन व्रजन् walking on the royal road, रसप्रदः माधवः Krishna, the bestower of pleasure, गृहीताङ्गविलेपभाजनं यान्तीं who was walking along carrying a vessel of body ointments, कुब्जां युवतीं वराननां स्त्रियं a woman who was young, hunchbacked, and possessed a beautiful face, विलोक्य seeing, प्रहसन् smilingly (addressed her).

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Then, while walking down the royal road of Mathura, Lord Krishna, the bestower of transcendental pleasure, noticed a young woman walking along. She was hunchbacked but had a highly attractive and beautiful face, and she was carrying a vessel filled with fragrant body ointments. Seeing her, the Lord smiled and addressed her.

का त्वं वरोर्वेतदु हानुलेपनं कस्याङ्गने वा कथयस्व साधु नः।

देह्यावयोरङ्गविलेपमुत्तमं श्रेयस्ततस्ते न चिराद् भविष्यति ॥10.42.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: का who, त्वम् you, वर-ऊरु O woman with beautiful thighs, एतत् this, उ indeed, अनुलेपनम् ointment, कस्य for whom, अङ्गने O beautiful lady, वा or, कथयस्व please tell, साधु clearly, नः to us, देहि please give, आवयोः to the two of us (Krishna and Balarama), अङ्ग-विलेपम् body ointment, उत्तमम् excellent, श्रेयः supreme benefit, ततः from that, ते for you, न चिरात् very soon, भविष्यति will happen

Anvaya: (श्री भगवान् उवाच) — हे वरूरु! त्वं का? हे अङ्गने! एतत् अनुलेपनं कस्य वा? नः साधु कथयस्व। आवयोः उत्तमम् अङ्गविलेपं देहि, ततः ते न चिरात् श्रेयः भविष्यति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (The Supreme Lord said) — हे वरूरु O woman with beautiful thighs, त्वं का who are you, हे अङ्गने O beautiful lady, एतत् अनुलेपनं कस्य वा for whom is this ointment intended, नः साधु कथयस्व please tell us clearly, आवयोः उत्तमम् अङ्गविलेपं देहि please give the two of us this excellent body ointment, ततः ते from that action for you, न चिरात् very soon, श्रेयः भविष्यति supreme benefit will happen.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna said: O beautiful lady, who are you? For whom are you carrying this excellent ointment? Please tell us frankly. We would appreciate it if you could give this fine body paste to the two of us. By doing so, you will very soon achieve the highest spiritual benefit.

सैरन्ध्री उवाच

दास्यस्म्यहं सुन्दर कंससम्मता त्रिवक्रनामा ह्यनुलेपकर्मणि ।

मद्भावितं भोजपतेरतिप्रियं विना युवां कोऽन्यतमस्तदर्हति ॥10.42.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: सैरन्ध्री उवाच The maidservant said, दासी a maidservant, अस्मि am, अहम् I, सुन्दर O beautiful one, कंस-सम्मता respected by Kamsa, त्रिवक्र-नामा known by the name Trivakra, हि indeed, अनुलेप-कर्मणि in the work of preparing ointments, मत्-भावितम् prepared by me, भोज-पतेः of the chief of the Bhojas (Kamsa), अति-प्रियम् very dear, विना without, युवाम् you two, कः who, अन्यतमः else, तत्-अर्हति is worthy of that

Anvaya: सैरन्ध्री उवाच — हे सुन्दर! अहम् अनुलेपकर्मणि कंससम्मता दासी, हि त्रिवक्रनामा अस्मि। भोजपतेः अतिप्रियं मद्भावितं (एतत् अनुलेपनं) युवां विना अन्यतमः कः तत् अर्हति?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सैरन्ध्री उवाच The maidservant said, हे सुन्दर O beautiful one, अहम् अनुलेपकर्मणि कंससम्मता दासी I am a maidservant respected by Kamsa for my work in preparing ointments, हि त्रिवक्रनामा अस्मि indeed known by the name Trivakra. भोजपतेः अतिप्रियं मद्भावितम् (एतत् अनुलेपनं) this ointment prepared by me is very dear to Kamsa, युवां विना without you two, अन्यतमः कः who else, तत् अर्हति is worthy of that.

Simple Meaning: The maidservant replied: O beautiful one, I am a maidservant highly respected by King Kamsa for my expertise in preparing fragrant ointments. My name is Trivakra. This paste prepared by me is extremely dear to the King of the Bhojas. Aside from the two of you, who else could possibly be worthy of using it?

रूपपेशलमाधुर्यहसितालापवीक्षितैः ।

धर्षितात्मा ददौ सान्द्रमुभयोरनुलेपनम् ॥10.42.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: रूप-पेशल-माधुर्य-हसित-आलाप-वीक्षितैः by His beauty, elegant charm, sweetness, laughter, conversation, and glances, धर्षित-आत्मा her heart completely captivated, ददौ she gave, सान्द्रम् a generous amount of thick paste, उभयोः to both of them, अनुलेपनम् the ointment

Anvaya: रूपपेशलमाधुर्यहसितालापवीक्षितैः धर्षितात्मा सा उभयोः सान्द्रम् अनुलेपनं ददौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: रूपपेशलमाधुर्यहसितालापवीक्षितैः By His beauty, elegant charm, sweetness, laughter, conversation, and glances, धर्षितात्मा सा she, whose heart was completely captivated, उभयोः to both of them, सान्द्रम् अनुलेपनं ददौ gave a generous, thick quantity of the fragrant ointment.

Simple Meaning: Her heart was completely captivated by Lord Krishna's extraordinary beauty, elegant charm, sweet words, enchanting laughter, and loving glances. Overwhelmed with affection, she joyfully offered a generous, thick portion of the fragrant ointment to both Krishna and Balarama.

ततस्तावङ्गरागेण स्ववर्णेतरशोभिना ।

सम्प्राप्तपरभागेन शुशुभातेऽनुरञ्जितौ ॥10.42.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, तौ the two of them (Krishna and Balarama), अङ्ग-रागेण with the body ointment, स्व-वर्ण-इतर-शोभिना which shone beautifully in contrast to their respective complexions, सम्प्राप्त-पर-भागेन reaching a state of supreme splendor, शुशुभाते they shone brilliantly, अनुरञ्जितौ beautifully anointed

Anvaya: ततः स्ववर्णेतरशोभिना सम्प्राप्तपरभागेन अङ्गरागेण अनुरञ्जितौ तौ शुशुभाते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, स्ववर्णेतरशोभिना by that which shone beautifully in contrast to their respective complexions, सम्प्राप्तपरभागेन achieving a state of supreme splendor, अङ्गरागेण by the body ointment, अनुरञ्जितौ being beautifully anointed, तौ शुशुभाते the two of them shone brilliantly.

Simple Meaning: Then, being beautifully anointed with that fragrant body paste, which contrasted exquisitely with their respective dark and fair complexions, both Krishna and Balarama achieved a state of supreme splendor and shone with brilliant radiance.

प्रसन्नो भगवान् कुब्जां त्रिवक्रां रुचिराननाम् ।

ऋज्वीं कर्तुं मनश्चक्रे दर्शयन् दर्शने फलम् ॥10.42.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रसन्नः pleased, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, कुब्जाम् the hunchbacked woman, त्रिवक्राम् Trivakra (crooked in three places), रुचिर-आननाम् who possessed a beautiful face, ऋज्वीम् straight, कर्तुम् to make, मनः चक्रे made up His mind, दर्शयन् demonstrating, दर्शने in seeing Him, फलम् the fruit or reward

Anvaya: प्रसन्नः भगवान् दर्शने फलं दर्शयन् रुचिराननां कुब्जां त्रिवक्रां ऋज्वीं कर्तुं मनः चक्रे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रसन्नः भगवान् The pleased Supreme Lord, दर्शने फलं दर्शयन् demonstrating the true reward of beholding Him, रुचिराननां कुब्जां त्रिवक्रां who was a young hunchbacked woman with a beautiful face crooked in three places, ऋज्वीं कर्तुं मनः चक्रे made up His mind to make her straight.

Simple Meaning: Being highly pleased with her service, the Supreme Lord Krishna decided to straighten the hunched body of Trivakra, who, despite her physical deformity, possessed a beautiful face. By doing this, the Lord wished to visually demonstrate to everyone the immense, transformative reward of gaining His personal audience.

पद्भ्यामाक्रम्य प्रपदे द्व्यङ्गुल्युत्तानपाणिना ।

प्रगृह्य चुबुकेऽध्यात्ममुदनीनमदच्युतः ॥10.42.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: पद्भ्याम् with His two feet, आक्रम्य stepping upon, प्रपदे her toes, द्वि-अङ्गुलि-उत्तान-पाणिना with the fingers of His hand pointing upward and using two fingers, प्रगृह्य catching hold, चुबुके under the chin, अध्यात्मम् upward along her body, उदनीनमत् raised up, अच्युतः the infallible Lord Krishna

Anvaya: अच्युतः पद्भ्यां (तस्याः) प्रपदे आक्रम्य द्व्यङ्गुल्युत्तानपाणिना चुबुके प्रगृह्य अध्यात्मम् उदनीनमत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अच्युतः The infallible Lord Krishna, पद्भ्यां with His own two feet, तस्याः प्रपदे आक्रम्य stepping upon the front of her toes, द्व्यङ्गुल्युत्तानपाणिना with His hand pointing upward and using two fingers, चुबुके प्रगृह्य catching hold of her under the chin, अध्यात्मम् उदनीनमत् raised her body upward to make her straight.

Simple Meaning: The infallible Lord Krishna then stepped upon her toes with His own feet, placed two fingers of His upward-pointing hand under her chin, and lifted her body straight up.

सा तदर्जुसमानङ्गी बृहच्छ्रोणिपयोधरा ।

मुकुन्दस्पर्शनात् सद्यो बभूव प्रमदोत्तमा ॥10.42.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she, तत्-ऋजु-समान-अङ्गी with a body straight and perfectly proportioned, बृहत्-श्रोणि-पयोधरा possessing large hips and breasts, मुकुन्द-स्पर्शनात् from the touch of Mukunda (Krishna), सद्यः instantly, बभूव became, प्रमदा-उत्तमा a most beautiful woman

Anvaya: सा मुकुन्दस्पर्शनात् सद्यः बृहच्छ्रोणिपयोधरा ऋजुसमानङ्गी प्रमदोत्तमा बभूव।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सा She, मुकुन्दस्पर्शनात् सद्यः from the touch of Mukunda instantly, बृहच्छ्रोणिपयोधरा possessing large hips and breasts, ऋजुसमानङ्गी with a body that was straight and perfectly proportioned, प्रमदोत्तमा बभूव became a most beautiful woman.

Simple Meaning: By the mere touch of Lord Mukunda, she was instantly transformed. Her physical deformities vanished, leaving her with a perfectly straight, well-proportioned body, beautiful breasts, and broad hips. She became an exceptionally stunning and elegant woman.

ततो रूपगुणौदार्यसम्पन्ना प्राह केशवम् ।

उत्तरीयान्तमाकृष्य स्मयन्ती जातहृच्छया ॥10.42.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, रूप-गुण-औदार्य-सम्पन्ना endowed with beauty, good qualities, and generosity, प्राह spoke, केशवम् to Kesava (Krishna), उत्तरीय-अन्तम् the end of His upper garment, आकृष्य pulling, स्मयन्ती smiling, जात-हृच्छया her heart filled with Cupid's desire

Anvaya: ततः रूपगुणौदार्यसम्पन्ना जातहृच्छया सा स्मयन्ती केशवम् उत्तरीयान्तम् आकृष्य प्राह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, रूपगुणौदार्यसम्पन्ना endowed with extraordinary beauty, good qualities, and generosity, जातहृच्छया सा she, whose heart was now filled with desire, स्मयन्ती smilingly, केशवम् उत्तरीयान्तम् आकृष्य pulling the end of Keshava's upper garment, प्राह spoke to Him.

Simple Meaning: Then, fully endowed with exquisite physical beauty, graceful qualities, and charm, her heart became filled with romantic desire for Lord Krishna. Smiling sweetly, she pulled the edge of Kesava's upper garment and addressed Him.

एहि वीर गृहं यामो न त्वां त्यक्तुमिहोत्सहे ।

त्वयोन्मथितचित्तायाः प्रसीद पुरुषर्षभ ॥10.42.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: एहि please come, वीर O hero, गृहम् to my house, यामः let us go, न not, त्वाम् you, त्यक्तुम् to leave, इह here, उत्सहे I can bear, त्वया by you, उन्मथित-चित्तायाः whose heart is completely agitated, प्रसीद please be merciful, पुरुष-ऋषभ O foremost of men

Anvaya: वीर! एहि, गृहं यामः। इह त्वां त्यक्तुम् (अहं) न उत्सहे। पुरुषर्षभ! त्वया उन्मथितचित्तायाः (मम उपरि) प्रसीद।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वीर O hero, एहि please come, गृहं यामः let us go to my house, इह त्वां त्यक्तुम् (अहं) न उत्सहे I cannot bear to leave you here behind, पुरुषर्षभ O foremost of men, त्वया उन्मथितचित्तायाः (मम उपरि) प्रसीद please show mercy upon me, whose heart has been completely agitated by you.

Simple Meaning: She said: O heroic one, please come along with me to my home. I simply cannot bear to leave you here. O best among men, my heart has been completely captivated and stirred by your charm, so please show favor and be merciful to me.

एवं स्त्रिया याच्यमानः कृष्णो रामस्य पश्यतः ।

मुखं वीक्ष्यानुगानां च प्रहसंस्तामुवाच ह ॥10.42.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this manner, स्त्रिया by the woman, याच्यमानः being begged, कृष्णः Lord Krishna, रामस्य of Balarama, पश्यतः looking on, मुखम् the faces, वीक्ष्य glancing at, अनुगानाम् of His companions, च and, प्रहसन् laughing, ताम् to her, उवाच ह spoke

Anvaya: एवं स्त्रिया याच्यमानः कृष्णः, पश्यतः रामस्य अनुगानां च मुखं वीक्ष्य प्रहसन् ताम् उवाच ह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं स्त्रिया याच्यमानः कृष्णः in this manner being begged by the woman, Lord Krishna, पश्यतः रामस्य of Balarama who was looking on, अनुगानां च मुखं वीक्ष्य and glancing at the faces of His companions, प्रहसन् laughing, ताम् उवाच ह spoke to her.

Simple Meaning: Being entreated by the woman in this way, Lord Krishna looked at Balarama, who was watching the scene, and glanced at the faces of His cowherd friends. Laughing, He then replied to her.

एष्यामि ते गृहं सुभ्रु पुंसामाधिविकर्शनम् ।

साधितार्थोऽगृहाणां नः पान्थानां त्वं परायणम् ॥10.42.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: एष्यामि I will come, ते to your, गृहम् house, सुभ्रु O woman with beautiful eyebrows, पुंसाम् of men, आधि-विकर्शनम् relieving the mental distress, साधित-अर्थः having accomplished my purpose, अगृहाणाम् who have no permanent home, नः for us, पान्थानाम् who are mere travelers, त्वम् you, परायणम् the ultimate shelter

Anvaya: हे सुभ्रु! पुंसाम् आधिविकर्शनं ते गृहं साधितार्थः (अहम्) एष्यामि। अगृहाणां पान्थानां नः त्वं परायणम् (असि)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हे सुभ्रु O woman with beautiful eyebrows, पुंसाम् आधिविकर्शनं which relieves the mental distress of men, ते गृहं your house, साधितार्थः (अहम्) I, having accomplished my purpose here, एष्यामि will come. अगृहाणां पान्थानां नः For us who have no permanent home and are mere travelers, त्वं परायणम् you are the ultimate shelter.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna said: O lady with beautiful eyebrows, I will certainly visit your house who can relieve the anxieties of men later, after I have accomplished my purpose here in Mathura. For travelers like us who have no fixed home, a generous person like you is the ultimate shelter.

विसृज्य माध्व्या वाण्या तां व्रजन् मार्गे वणिक्पथैः ।

नानोपायनताम्बूलस्रग्गन्धैः साग्रजोऽर्चितः ॥10.42.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: विसृज्य bidding farewell, माध्व्या with sweet, वाण्या speech, ताम् to her, व्रजन् walking, मार्गे on the road, वणिक्-पथैः by the merchants along the marketplace, नाना-उपायन-ताम्बूल-स्रग्-गन्धैः with various gifts, betel nut, flower garlands, and fragrant substances, स-अग्रजः along with His elder brother Balarama, अर्चितः was worshiped

Anvaya: तां माध्व्या वाण्या विसृज्य, मार्गे व्रजन् सग्रजः (माधवः) वणिक्पथैः नानोपायनताम्बूलस्रग्गन्धैः अर्चितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तां माध्व्या वाण्या विसृज्य Bidding her farewell with sweet, pleasing words, मार्गे व्रजन् while walking along the road, सग्रजः (माधवः) Krishna along with His elder brother Balarama, वणिक्पथैः by the merchants in the marketplace, नानोपायनताम्बूलस्रग्गन्धैः अर्चितः was worshiped with various sorts of gifts, betel nut, flower garlands, and fragrant ointments.

Simple Meaning: After bidding Trivakra farewell with these sweet and comforting words, Lord Krishna continued walking down the road with His elder brother Balarama. As they passed through the marketplace, the local merchants joyfully worshiped them by offering all kinds of respectful gifts, betel nut, beautiful flower garlands, and aromatic substances.

तद्दर्शनस्मरक्षोभादात्मानं नाविदन् स्त्रियः ।

विस्रस्तवास:कबरवलयालेख्यमूर्तयः ॥10.42.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-दर्शन-स्मर-क्षोभात् from the mental agitation caused by Cupid upon seeing Him, आत्मानम् themselves, न अविदन् did not notice, स्त्रियः the women, विस्रस्त-वासः-कबर-वलयाः whose garments, braids, and bangles had become loosened, आलेख्य-मूर्तयः like painted forms or statues

Anvaya: तद्दर्शनस्मरक्षोभात् स्त्रियः आत्मानं न अविदन्, (ताः) विस्रस्तवासःकबरवलयाः आलेख्यमूर्तयः (इव बभूवुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तद्दर्शनस्मरक्षोभात् From the agitation of desire brought about by seeing Him, स्त्रियः आत्मानं न अविदन् the women did not even notice their own selves. (ताः) विस्रस्तवासःकबरवलयाः They, whose clothes, hair braids, and bracelets had slipped loose, आलेख्यमूर्तयः (इव बभूवुः) became just like painted statues.

Simple Meaning: Upon beholding His captivating beauty, the women of the city experienced such intense romantic agitation that they completely lost awareness of their own bodies. Their garments slipped, their hair braids loosened, and their bangles slid down. Frozen in ecstasy, they stood perfectly still, appearing just like painted statues.

ततः पौरान् पृच्छमानो धनुषः स्थानमच्युतः ।

तस्मिन् प्रविष्टो ददृषे धनुरैन्द्रमिवाद्भुतम् ॥10.42.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, पौरान् from the citizens, पृच्छमानः inquiring, धनुषः of the sacrificial bow, स्थानम् the place, अच्युतः the infallible Lord Krishna, तस्मिन् inside that place, प्रविष्टः having entered, ददृषे saw, धनुः the bow, ऐन्द्रम् इव like the bow of Indra (a rainbow), अद्भुतम् wonderful

Anvaya: ततः पौरान् धनुषः स्थानं पृच्छमानः अच्युतः, तस्मिन् प्रविष्टः ऐन्द्रम् इव अद्भुतं धनुः ददृषे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, पौरान् धनुषः स्थानं पृच्छमानः inquiring about the location of the bow from the citizens, अच्युतः the infallible Lord Krishna, तस्मिन् प्रविष्टः and having entered that place, ऐन्द्रम् इव अद्भुतं धनुः ददृषे saw that wonderful bow, which was like the magnificent rainbow of Indra.

Simple Meaning: Then, inquiring from the local citizens, the infallible Lord Krishna found the location where the sacrificial bow was kept. Upon entering the hall, He beheld the wonderful bow, which was as remarkably large and brilliant as Indra's rainbow.

पुरुषैर्बहुभिर्गुप्तमर्चितं परमर्द्धिमत् ।

वार्यमाणो नृभिः कृष्णः प्रसह्य धनुराददे ॥10.42.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुरुषैः by men, बहुभिः by many, गुप्तम् guarded, अर्चितम् worshiped, परम-ऋद्धिमत् highly ornamented with great opulence, वार्यमाणः being forbidden, नृभिः by the guards, कृष्णः Lord Krishna, प्रसह्य forcefully, धनुः the bow, आददे took up

Anvaya: बहुभिः पुरुषैः गुप्तम् अर्चितं परमर्द्धिमत् धनुः, नृभिः वार्यमाणः (अपि) कृष्णः प्रसह्य आददे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बहुभिः पुरुषैः गुप्तम् Guarded by many men, अर्चितं परमर्द्धिमत् धनुः the bow which was regularly worshiped and highly ornamented with opulence, नृभिः वार्यमाणः (अपि) even though being forbidden by those guards, कृष्णः प्रसह्य आददे Lord Krishna forcefully took it up.

Simple Meaning: The sacrificial bow was heavily guarded by numerous men, highly revered, and lavishly decorated with great opulence. Even though the guards explicitly forbade Him from touching it, Lord Krishna walked right up and forcefully picked it up.

करेण वामेन सलीलमुद्धृतं सज्यं च कृत्वा निमिषेण पश्यताम् ।

नृणां विकृष्य प्रबभञ्ज मध्यतो यथेक्षुदण्डं मदकर्युरुक्रमः ॥10.42.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: करेण with His hand, वामेन left, सलीलम् effortlessly (as if in play), उद्धृतम् lifted up, सज्यम् strung, च and, कृत्वा having made, निमिषेण in the blinking of an eye, पश्यताम् while they were watching, नृणाम् the men, विकृष्य pulling, प्रबभञ्ज broke it, मध्यतः in the middle, यथा just as, इक्षु-दण्डम् a stick of sugarcane, मद-करी a furious elephant, उरुक्रमः the powerful one

Anvaya: उरुक्रमः वामेन करेण सलीलम् उद्धृतं, सज्यं कृत्वा च, नृणां पश्यतां निमिषेण विकृष्य मध्यतः प्रबभञ्ज, यथा मदकर्युरुक्रमः इक्षुदण्डम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: उरुक्रमः The powerful Krishna, वामेन करेण सलीलम् उद्धृतं lifting (the bow) easily with the left hand, सज्यं कृत्वा च and drawing the string across it, नृणां पश्यतां while the guards were still looking, निमिषेण विकृष्य pulling it in the blink of the eye, मध्यतः प्रबभञ्ज broke (the bow) in the middle, यथा मदकर्युरुक्रमः इक्षुदण्डम् just as a furious and strong elephant breaks a stalk of sugarcane.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna effortlessly lifted the massive bow with His left hand. While the guards and onlookers watched in absolute amazement, He strung it and pulled the bowstring back with such immense force that the bow snapped right in the middle in the blink of an eye, just as a furious elephant breaks a stalk of sugarcane.

धनुषो भज्यमानस्य शब्दः खं रोदसी दिशः ।

पूरयामास यं श्रुत्वा कंसस्त्रासमुपागमत् ॥10.42.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: धनुषः of the bow, भज्यमानस्य which was being broken, शब्दः the sound, खम् the sky, रोदसी the earth and heaven, दिशः all directions, पूरयामास filled, यम् which, श्रुत्वा hearing, कंसः King Kamsa, त्रासम् terror, उपागमत् experienced

Anvaya: भज्यमानस्य धनुषः शब्दः खं रोदसी दिशः पूरयामास, यं श्रुत्वा कंसः त्रासम् उपागमत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भज्यमानस्य धनुषः शब्दः The sound of the bow being broken, खं रोदसी दिशः पूरयामास completely filled the sky, the earth and heaven, and all directions, यं श्रुत्वा hearing which, कंसः त्रासम् उपागमत् King Kamsa experienced terror.

Simple Meaning: The deafening crash of the sacrificial bow snapping apart resonated everywhere, completely filling the earth, the sky, and all directions. Upon hearing this terrifying explosion, King Kamsa was struck with deep fear.

तद्रक्षिणः सानुचराः कुपिता आततायिनः ।

ग्रहीतुकामा आवव्रुर्गृह्यतां बध्यतामिति ॥10.42.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्-रक्षिणः its guards, स-अनुचराः along with their followers, कुपिताः angered, आततायिनः raising their weapons to attack, ग्रहीतु-कामाः desiring to capture, आवव्रुः surrounded Them, गृह्यताम् catch Him!, बध्यताम् bind Him!, इति thus shouting

Anvaya: कुपिताः आततायिनः तद्रक्षिणः सानुचराः "गृह्यतां बध्यताम्" इति ग्रहीतुकामाः (तौ) आवव्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कुपिताः आततायिनः The angered, weapon-wielding, तद्रक्षिणः सानुचराः guards of the bow along with their attendants, "गृह्यतां बध्यताम्" इति "Seize Him! Bind Him!" thus shouting, ग्रहीतुकामाः desiring to capture, (तौ) आवव्रुः surrounded Them.

Simple Meaning: Enraged by the destruction of the bow, the guards and their attendants raised their weapons to attack. Desiring to capture Krishna and Balarama, they surrounded Them from all sides, shouting, "Seize Him! Bind Him!"

अथ तान् दुरभिप्रायान् विलोक्य बलकेशवौ ।

क्रुद्धौ धन्वन आदाय शकले तांश्च जघ्नतुः ॥10.42.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, तान् them, दुरभिप्रायान् having evil intentions, विलोक्य seeing, बल-केशवौ Balarama and Kesava (Krishna), क्रुद्धौ becoming angry, धन्वनः of the bow, आदाय taking up, शकले the two pieces, तान् positions / them, च and, जघ्नतुः struck down

Anvaya: अथ बलकेशवौ तान् दुरभिप्रायान् विलोक्य क्रुद्धौ (सन्तौ) धन्वनः शकले आदाय तान् च जघ्नतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, बलकेशवौ Balarama and Kesava, तान् दुरभिप्रायान् विलोक्य seeing them harboring evil intentions, क्रुद्धौ सन्तौ becoming angry, धन्वनः शकले आदाय taking up the two broken pieces of the bow, तान् च जघ्नतुः struck them all down.

Simple Meaning: Seeing that the guards were approaching with malicious and murderous intent, Balarama and Krishna became angry. Picking up the two broken halves of the massive bow, They used them as weapons and struck down the attacking guards.

बलं च कंसप्रहितं हत्वा शालामुखात्ततः ।

निष्क्रम्य चेरतुर्हृष्टौ निरीक्ष्य पुरसम्पदः ॥10.42.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: बलम् the military force, च and, कंस-प्रहितम् sent by Kamsa, हत्वा having killed, शाला-मुखात् from the main entrance of the bow-hall, ततः then, निष्क्रम्य coming out, चेरतुः walked about, हृष्टौ joyfully, निरीक्ष्य observing, पुर-सम्पदः the opulence of the city

Anvaya: कंसप्रहितं बलं च हत्वा, ततः शालामुखात् निष्क्रम्य हृष्टौ पुरसम्पदः निरीक्ष्य चेरतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कंसप्रहितं बलं च हत्वा Having also killed the military reinforcement sent by Kamsa, ततः शालामुखात् निष्क्रम्य then coming out from the main entrance of the sacrificial hall, हृष्टौ joyfully, पुरसम्पदः निरीक्ष्य observing the opulence and beauty of the city, चेरतुः They walked about.

Simple Meaning: After also destroying the additional military forces dispatched by King Kamsa, Balarama and Krishna stepped out through the main gates of the sacrificial hall. Feeling completely relaxed and joyful, They continued strolling through Mathura, admiring the magnificent opulence of the city.

तयोस्तदद्भुतं वीर्यं निशाम्य पुरवासिनः ।

तेजः प्रागल्भ्यं रूपं च मेनिरे विबुधोतमौ ॥10.42.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: तयोः of the two of Them, तत् that, अद्भुतम् wonderful, वीर्यम् prowess, निशाम्य observing, पुर-वासिनः the residents of the city, तेजः Their brilliance, प्रागल्भ्यम् Their boldness, रूपम् Their beauty, च and, मेनिरे considered Them, विबुध-उत्तमौ the two foremost among the demigods

Anvaya: तयोः तत् अद्भुतं वीर्यं तेजः प्रागल्भ्यं रूपं च निशाम्य, पुरवासिनः (तौ) विबुधोत्तमौ मेनिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तयोः तत् अद्भुतं वीर्यं तेजः प्रागल्भ्यं रूपं च निशाम्य Observing Their wonderful prowess, brilliance, boldness, and exquisite beauty, पुरवासिनः the residents of the city, तौ विबुधोत्तमौ मेनिरे considered the two of Them to be the foremost among the demigods.

Simple Meaning: Witnessing Their astonishing prowess, radiant splendor, fearless boldness, and captivating beauty, the citizens of Mathura concluded that these two extraordinary youths must be the highest among the celestial demigods descended to earth.

तयोर्विचरतोः स्वैर्मादित्योऽस्तमुपेयिवान् ।

कृष्णरामौ वृतौ गोपैः पुराच्छकटमीयतुः ॥10.42.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: तयोः while the two of Them, विचरतोः were wandering about, स्वैरम् at Their own free will, आदित्यः the sun, अस्तम् उपेयिवान् reached the western mountain, कृष्ण-रामौ Krishna and Balarama, वृतौ surrounded, गोपैः by the cowherd boys, पुरात् from the city, शकटम् to Their camp of wagons, ईयतुः returned

Anvaya: तयोः स्वैरं विचरतोः (सतोः) आदित्यः अस्तम् उपेयिवान्, गोपैः वृतौ कृष्णरामौ पुरात् शकटम् ईयतुः।

Simple Meaning: While Krishna and Balarama were wandering freely through the city streets at Their own pleasure, the sun began to set. Surrounded by Their cowherd friends, the two brothers then left the city and returned to Their camp where the baggage wagons were stationed.

गोप्यो मुकुन्दविगमे विरहातुरा या आशासताशिष ऋता मधुपुर्यभूवन् ।

सम्पश्यतां पुरुषभूषणगात्रलक्ष्मीं हित्वेतरान् नु भजतश्चकमेऽयनं श्रीः ॥10.42.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः the gopis, मुकुन्द-विगमे at the departure of Mukunda (Krishna), विरह-आतुराः distressed by separation, याः who, आशासत desired, आशिषः blessings / predictions, ऋताः true, मधु-पुरी Mathura, अभूवन् became, सम्पश्यताम् for those who were gazing upon, पुरुष-भूषण-गात्र-लक्ष्मीम् the beauty of the limbs of the ornament of manhood (Krishna), हित्वा leaving aside, इतरान् others, नु certainly, भजतः who worships Him, चकमे desired, अयनम् the exclusive shelter, श्रीः the goddess of fortune

Anvaya: मुकुन्दविगमे विरहातुराः गोप्यः याः आशिषः आशासत, पुरुषभूषणगात्रलक्ष्मीं सम्पश्यतां मधुपुरी (ताः) ऋताः अभूवन्। इतरान् भजतः हित्वा श्रीः (कृष्णस्य) अयनं नु चकमे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मुकुन्दविगमे विरहातुराः गोप्यः याः आशिषः आशासत The gopis who, distressed by separation at the departure of Mukunda, spoke of those various desires and predictions, पुरुषभूषणगात्रलक्ष्मीं सम्पश्यतां मधुपुरी for those in Mathura who are fully gazing upon the beauty of the limbs of the ornament of all men, (ताः) ऋताः अभूवन् those have become true; इतरान् भजतः हित्वा Leaving aside all others who serve her, श्रीः (कृष्णस्य) अयनं नु चकमे Lakshmi, the goddess of fortune, certainly desired Him who is her exclusive shelter.

Simple Meaning: The statements and predictions made by the gopis of Vrindavan, when they were distressed by separation during Mukunda's departure, have now proven completely true. The residents of Mathura are now experiencing the highest fortune by gazing directly upon the exquisite beauty of Krishna, who is the ornament of all mankind. Indeed, this is the very beauty desired by Goddess Lakshmi herself, who abandoned all others who serve her, to serve Him exclusively as her ultimate shelter.

अवनिक्ताङ्घ्रियुगलौ भुक्त्वा क्षीरोपसेवनम् ।

ऊषतुस्तां सुखं रात्रिं ज्ञात्वा कंसचिकीर्षितम् ॥10.42.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: अवनिक्त-अङ्घ्री-युगलौ having washed Their pairs of feet, भुक्त्वा having eaten, क्षीर-उपसेवनम् rice boiled in milk (kheer) or food with milk preparations, ऊषतुः They passed, ताम् that, सुखम् comfortably, रात्रिम् night, ज्ञात्वा knowing well, कंस-चिकीर्षितम् what Kamsa intended to do

Anvaya: अवनिक्ताङ्घ्रियुगलौ क्षीरोपसेवनं भुक्त्वा, कंसचिकीर्षितं ज्ञात्वा (अपि) तां रात्रिं सुखम् ऊषतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अवनिक्ताङ्घ्रियुगलौ Having washed Their pairs of feet, क्षीरोपसेवनं भुक्त्वा and having eaten food prepared with milk, कंसचिकीर्षितं ज्ञात्वा (अपि) even though fully knowing what Kamsa intended to do, तां रात्रिं सुखम् ऊषतुः They passed that night comfortably.

Simple Meaning: After washing Their feet and enjoying a meal of rice cooked with milk, Krishna and Balarama went to rest. Even though They were completely aware of King Kamsa's malicious plans against Them for the following day, They spent that night in absolute comfort and peace.

कंसस्तु धनुषो भङ्गं रक्षिणां स्वबलस्य च ।

वधं निशम्य गोविन्दरामविक्रीडितं परम् ॥10.42.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: कंसः King Kamsa, तु indeed, धनुषः of the bow, भङ्गम् the breaking, रक्षिणाम् of the guards, स्व-बलस्य of his own military force, च and, वधम् the killing, निशम्य hearing, गोविन्द-राम-विक्रीडितम् the playful pastimes of Govinda (Krishna) and Rama (Balarama), परम् supreme / extraordinary

Anvaya: कंसः तु धनुषः भङ्गं रक्षिणां स्वबलस्य च वधं परं गोविन्दरामविक्रीडितं (च) निशम्य (भीरुः बभूव)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कंसः तु King Kamsa indeed, धनुषः भङ्गं the breaking of the bow, रक्षिणां स्वबलस्य च वधं and the killing of the guards and his own military force, परं गोविन्दरामविक्रीडितं च and all this as the extraordinary, effortless play of Govinda and Rama, निशम्य hearing (became deeply terrified).

Simple Meaning: When King Kamsa heard about the shattering of the sacrificial bow, the slaughter of his guards, and the destruction of the additional military forces he had dispatched—all accomplished as mere effortless play by Govinda and Balarama—he was overcome with deep dread.

दीर्घप्रजागरो भीतो दुर्निमित्तानि दुर्मतिः।

बहून्यचष्टोभयथा मृत्योर्दौत्यकराणि च ॥10.42.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: दीर्घ-प्रजागरः remaining awake for a long time (insomnia), भीतः terrified, दुर्निमित्तानि bad omens, दुर्मतिः the evil-minded Kamsa, बहूनि many, अचष्ट saw, उभयथा of both kinds (in sleep and while awake), मृत्योः of death, दौत्य-कराणि acting as messengers, च and

Anvaya: भीतः दुर्मतिः (कंसः) दीर्घप्रजागरः (सन्), मृत्योः दौत्यकराणि उभयथा बहूनि दुर्निमित्तानि च अचष्ट।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भीतः दुर्मतिः कंसः The terrified, evil-minded Kamsa, दीर्घप्रजागरः (सन्) remaining awake for a long time through sleeplessness, मृत्योः दौत्यकराणि acting as messengers of death, उभयथा in both states (while awake and dreaming), बहूनि दुर्निमित्तानि च अचष्ट saw many bad omens.

Simple Meaning: Filled with terror, the wicked King Kamsa lay awake for most of the night, unable to sleep. Both while awake and when he finally dozed off, he witnessed numerous ominous portents and nightmares that acted as unmistakable messengers of his impending death.

अदर्शनं स्वशिरसः प्रतिरूपे च सत्यपि ।

असत्यपि द्वितीये च द्वैरूप्यं ज्योतिषां तथा ॥10.42.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: अदर्शनम् non-seeing (not seeing), स्व-शिरसः of one's own head, प्रतिरूपे in a reflection, च and, सत्यपि even though existing, असत्यपि even though not existing, द्वितीये a second (shadow / reflection), च and, द्वैरूप्यम् double vision / seeing two, ज्योतिषाम् of the luminaries (sun, moon, and stars), तथा also

Anvaya: प्रतिरूपे सत्यपि स्वशिरसः अदर्शनं, द्वितीये असत्यपि च (द्वितीयादर्शनं), तथा ज्योतिषां द्वैरूप्यम् (अचष्ट)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रतिरूपे सत्यपि Even though a reflection was present, स्वशिरसः अदर्शनं the non-seeing of his own head, द्वितीये असत्यपि च and even though a second shadow or companion reflection should not exist (seeing one), तथा ज्योतिषां द्वैरूप्यम् as well as seeing double images of the celestial luminaries, अचष्ट he observed.

Simple Meaning: While awake, Kamsa looked into a mirror or water and could not see the reflection of his own head, though the rest of his body was visible. He also saw a double shadow where there should only be one, and when he looked up at the sky, the sun, moon, and stars appeared split in two.

छिद्रप्रतीतिश्छायायां प्राणघोषानुपश्रुतिः ।

स्वर्णप्रतीतिर्वृक्षेषु स्वपदानामदर्शनम् ॥10.42.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: छिद्र-प्रतीतिः the appearance of holes, छायायाम् in one's shadow, प्राण-घोष-अनुपश्रुतिः not hearing the internal sound of one's life air (when plugging the ears), स्वर्ण-प्रतीतिः the appearance of gold, वृक्षेषु on the trees, स्व-पदानाम् of one's own footprints, अदर्शनम् the non-seeing (not seeing)

Anvaya: छायायां छिद्रप्रतीतिः, प्राणघोषानुपश्रुतिः, वृक्षेषु स्वर्णप्रतीतिः, स्वपदानामदर्शनम् (च अचष्ट)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: छायायां छिद्रप्रतीतिः The appearance of holes in his shadow, प्राणघोषानुपश्रुतिः the non-hearing of the internal sound of his own life-breath, वृक्षेषु स्वर्णप्रतीतिः the perception of a golden hue on the leaves of trees, स्वपदानामदर्शनम् (च) and the non-seeing of his own footprints in dust or mud, (अचष्ट) he observed.

Simple Meaning: Kamsa also observed other terrifying waking omens: he saw holes in his own shadow, he could no longer hear the internal vibrating sound of life-force when covering his ears, the leaves on the trees appeared to be made of bright gold, and his feet left no footprints when walking upon loose soil.

स्वप्ने प्रेतपरिष्वङ्गः खरयानं विषादनम् ।

यायान्नलदमाल्येकस्तैलाभ्यक्तो दिगम्बरः ॥10.42.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्वप्ने in dreams, प्रेत-परिष्वङ्गः embracing a ghost or dead body, खर-यानम् riding on a donkey, विष-अदनम् eating poison, यायात् one goes, नलद-माली wearing a garland of oleander flowers, एकः alone, तैल-अभ्यक्तः smeared with oil, दिगम्बरः naked

Anvaya: स्वप्ने प्रेतपरिष्वङ्गः, खरयानं, विषादनं, (तथा) तैलाभ्यक्तः दिगम्बरः नलदमाली एकः यायात् (इति अचष्ट)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्वप्ने In dreams, प्रेतपरिष्वङ्गः embracing a corpse or ghost, खरयानं riding on a donkey, विषादनं eating poison, तथा तैलाभ्यक्तः दिगम्बरः नलदमाली एकः यायात् as well as wandering about alone smeared with oil, naked, and wearing a garland of oleander flowers, (इति अचष्ट) he saw.

Simple Meaning: In his dreams, Kamsa experienced more terrifying omens: he embraced ghosts and corpses, rode upon a donkey, and swallowed deadly poison. He also saw himself wandering completely alone, stripped naked, smeared with oil from head to toe, and wearing a garland of funeral flowers.

अन्यानि चेत्थं भूतानि स्वप्नजागरितानि च ।

पश्यन् मरणसन्त्रस्तो निद्रां लेभे न चिन्तया ॥10.42.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्यानि other, च and, इत्थम् भूतानि of this kind, स्वप्न-जागरितानि occurrences in both dream and waking states, च and, पश्यन् seeing, मरण-सन्त्रस्तः deeply terrified of death, निद्राम् sleep, लेभे न did not obtain, चिन्तया due to anxiety

Anvaya: इत्थंभूतानि अन्यानि च स्वप्नजागरितानि (दुर्निमित्तानि) पश्यन्, मरणसन्त्रस्तः (कंसः) चिन्तया निद्रां न लेभे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इत्थं भूतानि अन्यानि च स्वप्नजागरितानि (दुर्निमित्तानि) पश्यन् Seeing other such bad omens of this kind in both his dreams and waking state, मरणसन्त्रस्तः (कंसः) King Kamsa, deeply terrified of death, चिन्तया due to overwhelming anxiety, निद्रां न लेभे did not obtain sleep.

Simple Meaning: Beholding these and many other such ominous portents both while awake and while dreaming, King Kamsa became paralyzed with the fear of death. Consumed by constant, agonizing anxiety, he was completely unable to catch even a moment of sleep.

व्युष्टायां निशि कौरव्य सूर्ये चाद्भ्यः समुत्थिते ।

कारयामास वै कंसो मल्लक्रीडामहोत्सवम् ॥10.42.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्युष्टायाम् when it had passed, निशि the night, कौरव्य O descendant of Kuru (Parikshit), सूर्ये when the sun, च and, अद्भिः from the water / horizon, समुत्थिते had arisen, कारयामास caused to be performed, वै indeed, कंसः King Kamsa, मल्ल-क्रीडा-महोत्सवम् the great festival of wrestling matches

Anvaya: कौरव्य! निशि व्युष्टायां सूर्ये च अद्भ्यः समुत्थिते (सति), कंसः वै मल्लक्रीडामहोत्सवं कारयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कौरव्य O King Parikshit, descendant of Kuru! निशि व्युष्टायां when the night had finally ended, सूर्ये च अद्भ्यः समुत्थिते (सति) and when the sun had risen out of the morning waters, कंसः वै King Kamsa indeed, मल्लक्रीडामहोत्सवं कारयामास caused the grand wrestling festival to begin.

Simple Meaning: O descendant of Kuru (King Parikshit), as the agonizing night finally came to an end and the sun rose over the eastern horizon, King Kamsa gave the order to commence the grand wrestling festival he had meticulously arranged.

आनर्चुः पुरुषा रङ्गं तूर्यभेर्यश्च जघ्निरे ।

मञ्चाश्चालङ्कृताः स्रग्भिः पताकाचैलतोरणैः ॥10.42.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: आनर्चुः worshipped, पुरुषाः the men (assigned to the task), रङ्गम् the wrestling arena, तूर्य-भेर्यः च and the trumpets and kettle-drums, जघ्निरे were sounded, मञ्चाः च and the high viewing platforms / galleries, अलङ्कृताः were decorated, स्रग्भिः with flower garlands, पताका-चैल-तोरणैः with flags, ribbons, and archways

Anvaya: पुरुषाः रङ्गम् आनर्चुः, तूर्यभेर्यः च जघ्निरे, मञ्चाः च स्रग्भिः पताकाचैलतोरणैः अलङ्कृताः (अभवन्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पुरुषाः रङ्गम् आनर्चुः The designated men worshipped the arena, तूर्यभेर्यः च जघ्निरे and the musical trumpets and kettle-drums were loudly beaten, मञ्चाः च स्रग्भिः पताकाचैलतोरणैः अलङ्कृताः (अभवन्) and the tiered viewing platforms were beautifully adorned with garlands, flags, colorful fabrics, and ornamental arches.

Simple Meaning: The officials in charge ritually consecrated and worshipped the wrestling arena, while musicians struck up a loud, festive din on kettle-drums and trumpets. The surrounding viewing galleries were beautifully decorated with fresh flower garlands, fluttering flags, colorful textiles, and magnificent ceremonial arches.

तेषु पौरा जानपदा ब्रह्मक्षत्रपुरोगमाः ।

यथोपजोषं विविशू राजानश्च कृतासनाः ॥10.42.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: तेषु upon those (platforms), पौराः the city-dwellers, जानपदाः the inhabitants of the countryside, ब्रह्म-क्षत्र-पुरोगमाः headed by the Brahmanas and Kshatriyas, यथा उपजोषम् according to their comfort / pleasure, विविशुः entered and took their places, राजानः च and the kings, कृत-आसनाः who were assigned specific seats

Anvaya: ब्रह्मक्षत्रपुरोगमाः ते पौराः जानपदाः च तेषु यथा उपजोषं विविशुः, राजानः च कृतासनाः (अभवन्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ब्रह्मक्षत्रपुरोगमाः ते पौराः जानपदाः च Those citizens of the city and inhabitants of the rural districts, headed by the Brahmanas and Kshatriyas, तेषु यथा उपजोषं विविशुः entered and took their places upon those galleries according to their comfort and preference; राजानः च कृतासनाः (अभवन्) and the visiting kings took their designated, specially prepared seats.

Simple Meaning: The citizens of Mathura and the people arriving from the surrounding country districts—led by the prominent Brahmanas and Kshatriyas—entered the arena and took their places comfortably in the galleries. Meanwhile, the visiting royal guests took their places in the specially reserved, luxurious seating areas assigned to them.

कंसः परिवृतोऽमात्यै राजमञ्च उपाविशत् ।

मन्डलेश्वर मध्यस्थो हृदयेन विदूयता ॥10.42.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: कंसः King Kamsa, परिवृतः surrounded, अमात्यैः by his ministers, राज-मञ्चे on the royal dais / pavilion, उपाविशत् sat down, मण्डल-ईश्वर-मध्य-स्थः situated in the middle of the subordinate rulers (district kings), हृदयेन with a heart, विदूयता burning with anxiety / deeply distressed

Anvaya: अमात्यैः परिवृतः कंसः मन्डलेश्वरमध्यस्थः (सन्) विदूयता हृदयेन राजमञ्चे उपाविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अमात्यैः परिवृतः कंसः King Kamsa, surrounded by his ministers, मन्डलेश्वरमध्यस्थः (सन्) sitting in the midst of the rulers of various provinces, विदूयता हृदयेन with a deeply anxious and burning heart, राजमञ्चे उपाविशत् sat down upon the royal viewing platform.

Simple Meaning: Surrounded by his ministers and seated right in the center of all the provincial kings, King Kamsa took his place on the elevated royal dais. Yet, despite all this grand display of power, his heart was burning and trembling with intense, unspoken anxiety.

वाद्यमानेषु तूर्येषु मल्लतालोत्तरेषु च ।

मल्लाः स्वलङ्कृता दृप्ताः सोपाध्यायाः समाविशन् ॥10.42.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: वाद्यमानेषु while being sounded, तूर्येषु the musical instruments / trumpets, मल्ल-ताल-उत्तरेषु च and especially the specific rhythms meant for wrestlers (clapping and thumping beats), मल्लाः the wrestlers, सु-अलङ्कृताः beautifully adorned, दृप्ताः arrogant / proud, स-उपाध्यायाः accompanied by their coaches / teachers, समाविशन् entered the arena

Anvaya: तूर्येषु मल्लतालोत्तरेषु च वाद्यमानेषु (सत्सु), स्वलङ्कृताः दृप्ताः मल्लाः सोपाध्यायाः समाविशन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तूर्येषु मल्लतालोत्तरेषु च वाद्यमानेषु (सत्सु) While the musical instruments and the rhythmic wrestling beats were being loudly played, स्वलङ्कृताः दृप्ताः मल्लाः the beautifully adorned, arrogant wrestlers, सोपाध्यायाः समाविशन् entered the assembly along with their trainers.

Simple Meaning: As the musical instruments resounded alongside the intense, clapping rhythms specific to wrestling matches, the proudly arrogant wrestlers—splendidly adorned and accompanied by their coaches—strode confidently into the arena.

चाणूरो मुष्टिकः कूटः शलस्तोशल एव च ।

त आसेदुरुपस्थानं वल्गुवाद्यप्रहर्षिताः ॥10.42.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: चाणूरः Chanura, मुष्टिकः Mushtika, कूटः Kuta, शलः Shala, तोशलः Toshala, एव च and also, ते they, आसेदुः approached / took their places, उपस्थानम् the wrestling arena / stage, वल्गु-वाद्य-प्रहर्षिताः highly stimulated by the enchanting music

Anvaya: चाणूरः मुष्टिकः कूटः शलः तोशलः एव च ते वल्गुवाद्यप्रहर्षिताः (सन्तः) उपस्थानम् आसेदुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: चाणूरः मुष्टिकः कूटः शलः तोशलः एव च Chanura, Mushtika, Kuta, Shala, and Toshala, ते वल्गुवाद्यप्रहर्षिताः सन्तः they, being highly stimulated and energized by the pleasant, rhythmic music of the arena drums, उपस्थानम् आसेदुः approached and stepped onto the wrestling stage.

Simple Meaning: Kamsa’s champion fighters—Chanura, Mushtika, Kuta, Shala, and Toshala—stepped onto the wrestling floor. Energized and highly motivated by the vibrant, driving rhythms of the musical instruments, they proudly took their places on the main stage.

नन्दगोपादयो गोपा भोजराजसमाहुताः ।

निवेदितोपायनास्ते एकस्मिन् मञ्च आविशन् ॥10.42.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्द-गोप-आदयः headed by Nanda Gopa, गोपाः the cowherds, भोज-राज-समाहुताः being invited by the King of the Bhojas (Kamsa), निवेदित-उपायनाः having presented their gifts / offerings, ते they, एकस्मिन् on one particular, मञ्चे viewing platform / gallery, आविशन् entered and sat down

Anvaya: भोजराजसमाहुताः नन्दगोपादयो ते गोपाः निवेदितोपायनाः (सन्तः) एकस्मिन् मञ्चे आविशन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भोजराजसमाहुताः Being summoned and invited by Kamsa, the King of the Bhojas, नन्दगोपादयो ते गोपाः those cowherds headed by Nanda Baba, निवेदितोपायनाः (सन्तः) having formally presented their royal taxes and gifts, एकस्मिन् मञ्चे आविशन् sat down together upon one of the viewing platforms.

Simple Meaning: Having been invited to the festival by King Kamsa, Nanda and the cowherds first presented the royal gifts and dairy tributes they had brought from Vraja. Afterward, they went to the galleries and took their seats together on one of the designated viewing platforms to watch the matches.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “कृष्णस्य कुब्जायां कृपा, धनुषभङ्गः, कंसस्य भयं, मल्लरङ्गोपवर्णनं” नाम द्विचत्वारिंशः अध्यायः ॥42॥

Thus ends the forty-second discourse named “Krishna’s grace on the hunchback woman, breaking of the sacrificial bow, fear of Kamsa, and a description of the amphitheatre for wrestlers”, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.

॥ सर्वं श्रीकृष्णार्पणमस्तु ॥
