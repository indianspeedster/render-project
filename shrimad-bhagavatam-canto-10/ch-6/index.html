श्री शुक उवाच

नन्दः पथि वचः शौरेर्न मृषेति विचिन्तयन् ।

हरिं जगाम शरणमुत्पातागमशङ्कितः ॥10.6.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Sri Shuka said, नन्दः Nanda, पथि on the way, वचः words, शौरेः of Shouri, न not, मृषा false, इति thus, विचिन्तयन् considering, हरिम् to Hari, जगाम went, शरणम् for shelter, उत्पात-आगम-शङ्कितः fearing the arrival of disturbances

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच – नन्दः पथि शौरेः वचः न मृषा इति विचिन्तयन्, उत्पातागमशङ्कितः हरिं शरणं जगाम।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Sri Shuka said: नन्दः पथि Nanda, on the way, शौरेः वचः न मृषा इति विचिन्तयन् considering that the words of Shouri were not false, उत्पातागमशङ्कितः fearing the coming of disturbances, हरिं शरणं जगाम surrendered to Hari.

Simple meaning: Sri Shuka said: While traveling, Nanda reflected that Shouri’s words could not be untrue. Fearing impending calamities, he sought refuge in Hari.

कंसेन प्रहिता घोरा पूतना बालघातिनी ।

शिशूंश्चचार निघ्नन्ती पुरग्रामव्र​जादिषु ॥10.6.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: कंसेन by Kamsa, प्रहिता sent, घोरा terrible, पूतना Putana, बालघातिनी killer of infants, शिशून् infants, च and, अचार wandered, निघ्नन्ती killing, पुर-ग्राम-व्र​ज-आदिषु in cities, villages, cowherd settlements and other places

Anvaya: कंसेन प्रहिता घोरा बालघातिनी पूतना, पुरग्रामव्र​जादिषु शिशून् निघ्नन्ती अचार।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कंसेन प्रहिता Sent by Kamsa, घोरा बालघातिनी पूतना the terrible infant-killer Putana, पुरग्रामव्र​जादिषु in cities, villages, and cowherd settlements, शिशून् निघ्नन्ती अचार wandered about killing infants.

Simple meaning: The dreadful killer of infants, Putana, dispatched by Kamsa, roamed through towns, villages, and cowherd communities, slaying young children.

न यत्र श्रवणादीनि रक्षोघ्नानि स्वकर्मसु ।

कुर्वन्ति सात्वतां भर्तुर्यातुधान्यश्च तत्र हि ॥10.6.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, यत्र where, श्रवण-आदीनि hearing and other acts (such as chanting and remembering), रक्षः-घ्नानि destructive to rakshasas, स्व-कर्मसु in their prescribed duties, कुर्वन्ति perform, सात्वतानाम् of the devotees, भर्तुः of the Lord (protector of the Satvatas), यातुधान्यः rakshasas, च and, तत्र there, हि indeed

Anvaya: यत्र स्वकर्मसु रक्षोघ्नानि सात्वतानां भर्तुः श्रवणादीनि न कुर्वन्ति, तत्र हि यातुधान्यः (भवन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र स्वकर्मसु Where, in their prescribed duties, रक्षोघ्नानि which result in the destruction of the rakshasas, सात्वतानां भर्तुः श्रवणादीनि the acts of hearing etc of (the names) of the Protector of the devotees, न कुर्वन्ति do not perform, तत्र हि यातुधान्यः (भवन्ति) there indeed rakshasas prevail.

Simple meaning: Where devotees fail to perform the protective practices such as hearing and chanting about the Lord in the course of their duties, there the rakshasa forces gain influence.

सा खेचर्येकदोपेत्य पूतना नन्दगोकुलम् ।

योषित्वा माययाऽऽत्मानं प्राविशत् कामचारिणी ॥४॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she, खे-चरी one who moves in the sky, एकदा once, उपेत्य having come, पूतना Putana, नन्द-गोकुलम् to Nanda Gokula, योषित्वा assuming the form of a woman, मायया by illusion, आत्मानम् herself, प्राविशत् entered, काम-चारिणी moving according to her desire

Anvaya: कामचारिणी खेचरी सा पूतना एकदा नन्दगोकुलम् उपेत्य, मायया आत्मानं योषित्वा प्राविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कामचारिणी खेचरी सा पूतना She, who roamed according to her desire, the one who wandered in the sky, Putana, एकदा नन्दगोकुलम् उपेत्य once came to Nanda Gokula, मायया आत्मानं योषित्वा and by her illusion assuming the form of a woman, प्राविशत् entered.

Simple meaning: One day, the sky-roaming Putana came to Nanda Gokula. Using her magical power, she disguised herself as a beautiful woman and freely entered the settlement.

तां केशबन्धव्यतिषक्तमल्लिकां बृहन्नितम्बस्तनकृच्छ्रमध्यमाम् ।

सुवाससं कम्पितकर्णभूषणत्विषोल्लसत्कुन्तलमण्डिताननाम् ॥10.6.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताम् her, केश-बन्ध-व्यतिषक्त-मल्लिकाम् with jasmine flowers intertwined in her braid,, बृहत्-नितम्ब-स्तन-कृच्छ्र-मध्यमाम् with heavy hips, full breasts and slender waist, सु-वाससम् well-dressed in fine garments, कम्पित-कर्ण-भूषण-त्विषा-उल्लसत-कुन्तल-मण्डित-आननाम् with shining earrings which were swinging and curly locks of hair adorning her face

व्यतिषक्त व्यतिषक्त p. p. 1 Mutually connected or related, linked or joined together. Src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: केशबन्धव्यतिषक्तमल्लिकां बृहन्नितम्बस्तनकृच्छ्रमध्यमां सुवाससं कम्पितकर्णभूषणत्विषोल्लसत्कुन्तलमण्डिताननां ताम् (ददृशुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: केशबन्धव्यतिषक्तमल्लिकां The one with jasmine flowers intertwined in her braided hair, बृहन्नितम्बस्तनकृच्छ्रमध्यमां with large hips and breasts and a slender waist, सुवाससं well dressed, कम्पितकर्णभूषणत्विषोल्लसत्कुन्तलमण्डिताननां with face adorned with curly locks shining with the splendor of moving earrings, ताम् (ददृशुः) her, they saw.

Simple meaning: They beheld her beautifully adorned — jasmine woven into her hair, full-hipped and full-bosomed with a slender waist, dressed in fine garments, her face framed by curling locks that shimmered with the glow of her swinging earrings.

वल्गुस्मितापाङ्गविसर्गवीक्षितैर्मनो हरन्तीं वनितां व्रजौकसाम् ।

अमंसताम्भोजकरेण रूपिणीं गोप्यः श्रियं द्रष्टुमिवागतां पतिम् ॥10.6.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: वल्गु-स्मित-अपाङ्ग-विसर्ग-वीक्षितैः with charming smiles and casting side glances, मनः the minds, हरन्ती stealing, वनिताम् woman, व्रजौकसाम् of the residents of Vraja, अमंसत thought, अम्भोज-करेण holding a lotus in her hand, रूपिणी beautifully formed, गोप्यः the cowherd women, श्रियम् Lakshmi (the goddess of fortune), द्रष्टुम् to see, इव as if, आगताम् come, पतिम् her husband

श्रियम् - श्री, ईकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, पाक्षिकनदीसंज्ञकः, द्वितीयैकवचनम् src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: वल्गुस्मितापाङ्गविसर्गवीक्षितैः व्रजौकसां मनः हरन्तीम्, अम्भोजकरेण रूपिणीं वनितां गोप्यः अमंसत – पतिं द्रष्टुं श्रियम् आगताम् इव ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वल्गुस्मितापाङ्गविसर्गवीक्षितैः With her sweet smile and captivating sidelong glances, व्रजौकसां मनः हरन्तीम् enchanting the hearts of the people of Vraja, अम्भोजकरेण रूपिणीं वनितां holding a lotus in her hand, the beautiful lady, गोप्यः अमंसत the cowherd women thought, पतिं द्रष्टुम् to see her husband, श्रियम् आगताम् इव Goddess of Fortune - Lakshmi had come, as if.

Simple meaning: The cowherd women thought that this woman, who was stealing the minds of the residents of Vraja with her charming smiles and sidelong glances, who was beautifully formed and holding a lotus in her hand, was Goddess of Fortune Lakshmi who had come to see her husband.

बालग्रहस्तत्र विचिन्वती शिशून् यदृच्छया नन्दगृहेऽसदन्तकम् ।

बालं प्रतिच्छन्ननिजोरुतेजसं ददर्श तल्पेऽग्निमिवाहितं भसि ॥10.6.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: बाल-ग्रहः the one who seized children, तत्र there, विचिन्वती searching for, शिशून् infants, यदृच्छया by chance, नन्द-गृहे in the house of Nanda, असत्-अन्तकम् the destroyer of the wicked, बालम् the child, प्रतिच्छन्न concealed, निज-उरु-तेजसम् great splendor of his own, ददर्श saw, तल्पे on the bed, अग्निम् fire, इव like, आहितम् covered, भसि in ashes

Anvaya: तत्र बालग्रहः शिशून् विचिन्वती यदृच्छया नन्दगृहे तल्पे – भसि आहितम् अग्निम् इव – प्रतिच्छन्ननिजोरुतेजसम् असदन्तकं बालं ददर्श।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र बालग्रहः There, the one who caught hold of children, शिशून् विचिन्वती while searching for infants, यदृच्छया by chance, नन्दगृहे तल्पे in the house of Nanda, lying on the bed – भसि आहितम् अग्निम् इव like fire covered with ashes – प्रतिच्छन्ननिजोरुतेजसम् असदन्तकं बालं who was the destroyer of the wicked, whose great splendor was concealed, that child, ददर्श saw.

Simple meaning: While roaming about in search of infants, the rakshasi happened to enter Nanda’s house. There she saw the child, the destroyer of the wicked, lying on a bed—his immense divine brilliance hidden, like blazing fire covered by ashes.

विबुध्य तां बालकमारिकाग्रहं चराचरात्माऽऽस निमीलितेक्षणः ।

अनन्तमारोपयदङ्कमन्तकं यथोरगं सुप्तमबुद्धिरज्जुधीः ॥10.6.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: विबुध्य recognizing, ताम् her, बालक-मारिका-ग्रहम् the one who kills children, चर-अचर-आत्मा the soul of all moving and non-moving beings, आस was, निमीलित-ईक्षणः with eyes closed, अनन्तम् the infinite Lord, आरोपयत् placed upon, अङ्कम् her lap, अन्तकम् death personified, यथा just as, उरगम् a serpent, सुप्तम् sleeping, अबुद्धिः an unintelligent person, रज्जु-धीः the one who is thinking it to be a rope

Anvaya: चराचरात्मा अनन्तः तां बालकमारिकाग्रहं विबुध्य निमीलितेक्षणः आस। अबुद्धिः – रज्जुधीः सुप्तम् उरगं यथा – अन्तकम् अङ्कम् आरोपयत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: चराचरात्मा अनन्तः The soul of all moving and non-moving beings, the Infinite Lord, तां बालकमारिकाग्रहं विबुध्य recognizing her as a child-killing rakshasi, निमीलितेक्षणः आस remained with His eyes closed. अबुद्धिः She, the foolish one, रज्जुधीः सुप्तम् उरगं यथा as a person who thinks a sleeping snake to be a rope, अन्तकम् अङ्कम् आरोपयत placed death itself on her lap.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord, the indwelling soul of all beings, understood that she was a rakshasi who slew infants, He kept His eyes closed. In her ignorance, she lifted onto her lap the very personification of death—just as a fool might mistake a sleeping serpent for a rope and pick it up.

तां तीक्ष्णचित्तामतिवामचेष्टितां वीक्ष्यान्तरा कोशपरिच्छदासिवत् ।

वरस्त्रियं तत्प्रभया च धर्षिते निरीक्षमाणे जननी ह्यतिष्ठताम् ॥10.6.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताम् her, तीक्ष्ण-चित्ताम् having a sharp and cruel mind, अति-वाम-चेष्टिताम् with very crooked or wicked behavior, वीक्ष्य seeing, अन्तरा-कोश-परिच्छद-असिवत् like a sword covered in a sheath, वर-स्त्रियम् a beautiful woman, तत्-प्रभया by her splendor, च and, धर्षिते overpowered or dazzled, निरीक्षमाणे while observing, जननी the two mothers, हि indeed, अतिष्ठताम् stood still

Anvaya: अन्तराकोशपरिच्छदासिवत् तां तीक्ष्णचित्ताम् अतिवामचेष्टितां वरस्त्रियं तत्प्रभया हि धर्षिते निरीक्षमाणे जननी अतिष्ठताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्तराकोशपरिच्छदासिवत् तां Like a sword hidden in a sheath, तीक्ष्णचित्ताम् अतिवामचेष्टितां sharp-minded and extremely wicked in behavior, वरस्त्रियं the beautiful woman, तत्प्रभया हि धर्षिते being overpowered by her splendor, निरीक्षमाणे जननी अतिष्ठताम् the two mothers, seeing her, simply stood watching.

Simple meaning: Yashoda and Rohini saw her—a cruel and wicked being concealed within the form of a lovely woman, like a sharp sword hidden in its sheath. Dazzled by her beauty and radiance, they stood there watching.

तस्मिन् स्तनं दुर्जरवीर्यमुल्बणं घोराङ्कमादाय शिशोर्ददावथ ।

गाढं कराभ्यां भगवान् प्रपीड्य तत् प्राणैः समं रोषसम्न्वितोऽपिबत् ॥10.6.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मिन् there, स्तनम् breast, दुर्जर-वीर्यम् containing deadly, intolerable poison, उल्बणम् very powerful, घोरा the terrible one, अङ्कम् on lap, आदाय taking, शिशोः to the child, ददौ gave, अथ then, गाढम् firmly, कराभ्याम् with both hands, भगवान् the Lord, प्रपीड्य pressing, तत् that, प्राणैः with her life airs, समम् along with, रोष-समन्वितः filled with anger, अपिबत् sucked or drank

Anvaya: तस्मिन् घोरा अङ्कम् आदाय उल्बणं दुर्जरवीर्यं स्तनं शिशोः ददौ। अथ भगवान् रोषसमन्वितः कराभ्यां तत् गाढं प्रपीड्य प्राणैः समम् अपिबत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्मिन् There, घोरा she, the terrible one, अङ्कम् आदाय placing Him on her lap, उल्बणं दुर्जरवीर्यं स्तनं breast filled with deadly and powerful poison, शिशोः ददौ gave to the child. अथ भगवान् Then, The Lord, रोषसमन्वितः filled with anger, कराभ्यां तत् गाढं प्रपीड्य firmly pressed it with both hands, प्राणैः समम् अपिबत् sucked it, along with her very life airs.

Simple meaning: Placing the child on her lap, she offered her breast smeared with deadly poison. But the Supreme Lord, angered, grasped it tightly with both hands and sucked—not only the milk, but her very life breath along with it.

सा मुञ्च मुञ्चालमिति प्रभाषिणी निष्पीड्यमानाखिलजीवमर्मणी ।

विवृत्य नेत्रे चरणौ भुजौ मुहुः प्रस्विन्नगात्रा क्षिपती रुरोद ह ॥10.6.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she, मुञ्च मुञ्च release release, अलम् enough, इति thus, प्रभाषिणी speaking, निष्पीड्यमाना being squeezed, अखिल-जीव-मर्मणी in all vital spots, विवृत्य opening, नेत्रे eyes, चरणौ feet, भुजौ arms, मुहुः repeatedly, प्रस्विन्न-गात्रा with perspiring body, क्षिपती throwing about, रुरोद cried, ह indeed

Anvaya: सा “मुञ्च मुञ्च अलम्” इति प्रभाषिणी अखिलजीवमर्मणी निष्पीड्यमाना नेत्रे विवृत्य चरणौ भुजौ मुहुः क्षिपती प्रस्विन्नगात्रा रुरोद ह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सा मुञ्च मुञ्च अलम् इति प्रभाषिणी She speaking "Release me, release me, enough!", अखिलजीवमर्मणी निष्पीड्यमाना being squeezed in all vital spots, नेत्रे विवृत्य opening her eyes, चरणौ भुजौ मुहुः क्षिपती repeatedly throwing her feet and arms about, प्रस्विन्नगात्रा with perspiring body, रुरोद ह cried out indeed.

Simple meaning: She, being squeezed in all her vital spots, cried out "Release me, release me, enough!" Opening her eyes wide, repeatedly flailing her feet and arms about, her body covered in perspiration, she wailed in pain.

तस्याः स्वनेनातिगभीररंहसा साद्रिर्मही द्यौश्च चचाल सग्रहा ।

रसा दिशश्च प्रतिनेदिरे जनाः पेतुः क्षितौ वज्रनिपातशङ्कया ॥10.6.12॥

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्याः अतिगभीररंहसा स्वनेन By her cry of extremely deep and forceful sound, साद्रिः मही द्यौः च सग्रहा चचाल the earth with its mountains, heaven with its planets trembled, रसाः दिशः च प्रतिनेदिरे the nether regions and directions resounded, जनाः वज्रनिपातशङ्कया क्षितौ पेतुः people fell to the ground with fear of a thunderbolt falling.

Simple meaning: By her extremely deep and forceful cry, the earth with its mountains and heaven with its planets trembled, the nether regions and all directions resounded with the sound, and people fell to the ground in fear, thinking a thunderbolt was striking.

Breaking down the name Keshava (केशव):

  • का = Brahma (the creator)

  • ईश = Isha/Vishnu (the preserver)

  • व = Rudra/Shiva (the destroyer)

So Keshava means "one who is Brahma, Isha (Vishnu), and Shiva" - the one who contains all three aspects of the cosmic functions.

The commentary explains that at the time of cosmic dissolution (pralaya), these three apparent forms merge back into their original nature as the one Supreme Self (Paramatman). The three "forms" or "bodies" (upadhi-rupa-murti-traya) are just temporary manifestations, while the underlying reality is one.

Explanation of all the names of the Lord in the shloka:

1. अजः (Aja) - "The Unborn"

  • अ (a) = not, ज (ja) = born

  • The eternal one who has no birth, beyond the cycle of creation and destruction

  • Protects the feet (foundation/support)

2. मणिमान् (Maniman) - "The one who possesses Kaustubha Mani"

  • The one who possesses Kaustubha Mani

  • Protects the knees

3. यज्ञः (Yajna) - "Sacrifice/Ritual"

  • The Lord who is the embodiment of all sacrifices

  • All yajnas are ultimately offered to Him and are manifestations of Him

  • Protects the thighs

4. अच्युतः (Acyuta) - "The Infallible/Imperishable"

  • अ (a) = not, च्युत (cyuta) = fallen/moved

  • One who never falls from His supreme position

  • One who never abandons His devotees

  • Protects the waist and sides

5. हयास्यः (Hayasya) - "Horse-Faced"

  • हय (haya) = horse, आस्य (asya) = face

  • Refers to the Hayagriva incarnation with a horse's head

  • This form recovered the Vedas from rakshsas and represents supreme knowledge

  • Protects the belly

6. केशवः (Keshava) - Already discussed!

  • का (Brahma) + ईश (Isha) + व (Rudra/Shiva)

  • The one who is all three - creator, preserver, destroyer

  • Protects the heart

7. ईशः (Isha) - "Lord/Controller"

  • The supreme controller and master

  • One who has complete sovereignty

  • Protects the chest

8. इनः (Ina) - "Lord/Powerful"

This is from the Ashtadhyayi commentary explaining श्वेतद्वीप (Shwetadwipa):

Breaking it down:

श्वेतद्वीप (Shwetadwipa) - masculine, karmadhaaraya samaasa

Two meanings:

  1. वैकुण्ठाख्यविष्णुधाम्नि - In Vaikuntha, the abode of Vishnu

  2. क्षीरोधेः उत्तरस्थे द्वीपभेदे - A particular island situated north of the ocean of milk (Kshirodadhi)

The quote from Mahabharata, Shanti Parva:

"क्षीरोदधेः उत्तरतः श्वेतद्वीपो महाप्रभः । एकान्तिनः ते पुरुषाः श्वेतद्वीपनिवासिनः"

Translation: "North of the ocean of milk is the greatly effulgent Shvetadvipa. The beings dwelling in Shvetadvipa are ekantins (exclusive devotees of the Supreme)."

So श्वेतद्वीपपतिः (Shvetadvipapati) means "the Lord of Shvetadvipa" - referring to Vishnu who resides in this sacred white island, which is:

  • Either Vaikuntha itself

  • Or a special divine realm north of the cosmic ocean of milk

  • Inhabited by pure, exclusive devotees (ekantins)

This is a beautiful name emphasizing the Lord as the master of the realm of pure devotion!

Anvaya: कृष्णनिर्भुक्तसपद्याहतपाप्मनः दह्यमानस्य देहस्य धूमः च अगुरुसौरभः उत्थितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णनिर्भुक्तसपद्याहतपाप्मनः Of her whose sins were destroyed immediately upon being drunk by Krishna, दह्यमानस्य देहस्य धूमः च as her body was being burned, the smoke, अगुरुसौरभः उत्थितः arose fragrant like aguru.

Simple meaning: As the body was being burned, the smoke that arose was fragrant like aloe wood, because her sins had been destroyed the moment Krishna drank from her breast.

पूतना लोकबालघ्नी राक्षसी रुधिराशना ।

जिघांसयापि हरये स्तनं दत्त्वाऽऽप सद्गतिम् ॥10.6.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: पूतना Putana, लोक-बाल-घ्नी killer of children in the world, राक्षसी rakshasi, रुधिर-अशना blood eating, जिघांसया with intent to kill, अपि even, हरये to Hari, स्तनम् breast, दत्त्वा having given, आप obtained, सत्-गतिम् ultimate destination

Anvaya: लोकबालघ्नी रुधिराशना राक्षसी पूतना जिघांसया अपि हरये स्तनं दत्त्वा सद्गतिम् आप।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: लोकबालघ्नी रुधिराशना राक्षसी पूतना Putana, the rakshasi who killed children in the world and consumed blood, जिघांसया अपि even with intent to kill, हरये स्तनं दत्त्वा having given her breast to Hari, सद्गतिम् आप obtained the ultimate destination.

Simple meaning: Putana, the rakshasi who killed children throughout the world and drank blood, even though she gave her breast to Lord Hari with the intention to kill Him, still obtained the ultimate destination (liberation).

किं पुनः श्रद्धया भक्त्या कृष्णाय परमात्मने ।

यच्छन् प्रियतमं किं नु रक्तास्तन्मातरो यथा ॥10.6.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: किम् what, पुनः then, श्रद्धया with faith, भक्त्या with devotion, कृष्णाय to Krishna, परमात्मने to the Supreme Soul, यच्छन् giving, प्रियतमम् most dear, किम् what, नु indeed, रक्ताः attached, तत् those, मातरः mothers, यथा as

Anvaya: परमात्मने कृष्णाय श्रद्धया भक्त्या प्रियतमं यच्छन् किं पुनः, यथा रक्ताः तत् मातरः किं नु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: परमात्मने कृष्णाय To Krishna the Supreme Soul, श्रद्धया भक्त्या with faith and devotion, प्रियतमं यच्छन् giving what is most dear, किं पुनः what then (will be the result), यथा रक्ताः तत् मातरः as those affectionate mothers, किं नु what indeed.

Simple meaning: If even Putana attained such a destination, then what to speak of those who give their most beloved things to Krishna, the Supreme Soul, with faith and devotion? What will be the result of those mothers who have genuine affection?

पदभ्यां भक्तहृदिस्थाभ्यां वन्द्याभ्यां लोकवन्दितः ।

अङ्गं यस्याः समाक्रम्य भगवानपिबत् स्तनम् ॥10.6.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: पदाभ्याम् by the feet, भक्त-हृदि-स्थाभ्याम् situated in the hearts of devotees, वन्द्याभ्याम् worshipable, लोक-वन्दितः worshiped by the worlds, अङ्गम् body, यस्याः whose, समाक्रम्य having climbed upon, भगवान् the Lord, अपिबत् drank, स्तनम् breast

Anvaya: लोकवन्दितः भगवान् भक्तहृदिस्थाभ्यां वन्द्याभ्यां पदाभ्यां यस्याः अङ्गं समाक्रम्य स्तनम् अपिबत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: लोकवन्दितः भगवान् The Lord worshiped by all the worlds, भक्तहृदिस्थाभ्यां वन्द्याभ्यां पदाभ्याम् with His feet that are situated in the hearts of devotees and are worshipable, यस्याः अङ्गं समाक्रम्य having climbed upon whose body, स्तनम् अपिबत् drank from her breast.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord, who is worshiped by all the worlds, whose feet are enshrined in the hearts of devotees and are worthy of worship—He climbed upon her body and drank from her breast.

यातुधान्यपि सा स्वर्गमवाप जननीगतिम् ।

कृष्णभुक्तस्तनक्षीराः किमु गावो नु मातरः ॥10.6.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: यातुधानी rakshasi, अपि even, सा she, स्वर्गम् heaven, अवाप obtained, जननी-गतिम् status of a mother, कृष्ण-भुक्त-स्तन-क्षीराः whose breast milk was drunk by Krishna, किम् what, उ indeed, गावः cows, नु indeed, मातरः mothers

Anvaya: यातुधानी अपि सा कृष्णभुक्तस्तनक्षीराः जननीगतिं स्वर्गम् अवाप, गावः मातरः किम् उ नु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यातुधानी अपि सा Even though she was a rakshasi, कृष्णभुक्तस्तनक्षीराः whose breast milk was drunk by Krishna, जननीगतिं स्वर्गम् अवाप obtained the status of a mother and heaven, गावः मातरः किम् उ नु what then indeed of the cows and mothers.

Simple meaning: Even though she was a wicked rakshasi, because her breast milk was drunk by Krishna, she attained the status of a mother and obtained heaven. Then what to speak of the cows and mothers who nurse Him with genuine love?

पयांसि यासामपिबत् पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतान्यलम् ।

भगवान् देवकीपुत्रः कैवल्याद्याखिलप्रदः ॥10.6.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: पयांसि milk, यासाम् whose, अपिबत् drank, पुत्र-स्नेह-स्नुतानि flowing from motherly affection, अलम् fully, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, देवकी-पुत्रः son of Devaki, कैवल्य-आदि-अखिल-प्रदः giver of liberation and all other goals

कैवल्य (नपुंसकलिङ्गः) मोक्षः src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: कैवल्याद्याखिलप्रदः भगवान् देवकीपुत्रः यासां पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतानि पयांसि अलम् अपिबत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कैवल्याद्याखिलप्रदः The giver of liberation and all other goals, भगवान् देवकीपुत्रः the Supreme Lord, son of Devaki, यासां पुत्रस्नेहस्नुतानि पयांसि अलम् अपिबत् fully drank the milk of those which flowed from motherly affection.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord, the son of Devaki, who bestows liberation and all other desired goals, fully drank the milk of those mothers which flowed abundantly from their pure maternal affection.

तासामविरतं कृष्णे कुर्वतीनां सुतेक्षणम् ।

न पुनः कल्पते राजन् संसारोऽज्ञानसम्भवः ॥10.6.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: तासाम् of them, अविरतम् unceasingly, कृष्णे on Krishna, कुर्वतीनाम् of those doing, सुत-ईक्षणम् gazing at their son, न not, पुनः again, कल्पते is possible, राजन् O king, संसारः material existence, अज्ञान-सम्भवः born from ignorance

Anvaya: राजन्! कृष्णे अविरतं सुतेक्षणं कुर्वतीनां तासाम् अज्ञानसम्भवः संसारः पुनः न कल्पते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O king, कृष्णे अविरतं सुतेक्षणं कुर्वतीनां तासाम् For those mothers who unceasingly gaze upon Krishna as their son, अज्ञानसम्भवः संसारः पुनः न कल्पते material existence born from ignorance is no longer possible again.

Simple meaning: O king! For those mothers who continuously gaze upon Krishna as their son, rebirth in the material world, which is born from ignorance, is no longer possible.

कटधूमस्य सौरभ्यमवधाय व्रजौकसः ।

किमिदं कुत एवेति वदन्तो व्रजमाययुः ॥10.6.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: कट-धूमस्य of the smoke from the body, सौरभ्यम् fragrance, अवधाय having perceived, व्रज-ओकसः residents of Vraja, किम् what, इदम् this, कुतः from where, एव indeed, इति thus, वदन्तः speaking, व्रजम् to Vraja, आययुः came

Anvaya: व्रजौकसः कटधूमस्य सौरभ्यम् अवधाय किम् इदं कुतः एव इति वदन्तः व्रजम् आययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: व्रजौकसः The residents of Vraja, कटधूमस्य सौरभ्यम् अवधाय having perceived the fragrance of the smoke from the body, किम् इदं कुतः एव इति वदन्तः saying "What is this, from where is this indeed?", व्रजम् आययुः came to Vraja.

Simple meaning: The residents of Vraja, having perceived the fragrance of the smoke from the burning body, were wondering "What is this? Where is this fragrance coming from?" and thus came to Vraja.

ते तत्र वर्णितं गोपैः पूतनागमनादिकम् ।

श्रुत्वा तन्निधनं स्वस्ति शिशोश्चासन् सुविस्मिताः ॥10.6.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: ते they, तत्र there, वर्णितम् narrated, गोपैः by the cowherds, पूतना-आगमन-आदिकम् Putana's arrival and so on, श्रुत्वा having heard, तत्-निधनम् her death, स्वस्ति well being, शिशोः of the child, च and, आसन् were, सु-विस्मिताः very astonished

Anvaya: ते तत्र गोपैः वर्णितं पूतनागमनादिकं तन्निधनं शिशोः स्वस्ति च श्रुत्वा सुविस्मिताः आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते तत्र They there, गोपैः वर्णितं narrated by the cowherds, पूतनागमनादिकं Putana's arrival and so on, तन्निधनं her death, शिशोः स्वस्ति च and the well being of the child, श्रुत्वा having heard, सुविस्मिताः आसन् were greatly astonished.

Simple meaning: Having heard from the cowherds the narration of Putana's arrival, her death, and the child's safety, they were utterly astonished.

नन्दः स्वपुत्रमादाय प्रेत्यागतमुदारधीः ।

मूर्ध्न्युपाघ्राय परमां मुदं लेभे कुरूद्वह ॥10.6.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्दः Nanda, स्व-पुत्रम् own son, आदाय taking, प्रेत्य having returned, आगतम् come, उदार-धीः noble minded, मूर्ध्नि on the head, उपाघ्राय smelling, परमाम् supreme, मुदम् joy, लेभे obtained, कुरू-उद्वह O perpetuator of the Kurus

Anvaya: कुरूद्वह! उदारधीः नन्दः प्रेत्य आगतं स्वपुत्रम् आदाय मूर्ध्नि उपाघ्राय परमां मुदं लेभे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कुरूद्वह O perpetuator of the Kurus!, उदारधीः नन्दः The noble minded Nanda, प्रेत्य आगतं स्वपुत्रम् आदाय having taken up his son who had returned from death, मूर्ध्नि उपाघ्राय smelling his head, परमां मुदं लेभे obtained supreme joy.

Simple meaning: O perpetuator of the Kurus! The noble minded Nanda took up his son who had survived death, smelled his head affectionately, and experienced supreme joy.

य एतत् पूतनामोक्षं कृष्णस्यार्भकमद्भुतम् ।

शृणुयाच्छ्रद्धया मर्त्यो गोविन्दे लभते रतिम् ॥10.6.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: यः who, एतत् this, पूतना-मोक्षम् liberation of Putana, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, आर्भकम् childhood pastimes, अद्भुतम् wonderful, शृणुयात् may hear, श्रद्धया with faith, मर्त्यः mortal, गोविन्दे in Govinda, लभते obtains, रतिम् loving attachment

Anvaya: यः मर्त्यः एतत् कृष्णस्य अद्भुतम् आर्भकं पूतनामोक्षं श्रद्धया शृणुयात्, गोविन्दे रतिं लभते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः मर्त्यः Which mortal person, एतत् कृष्णस्य अद्भुतम् आर्भकं पूतनामोक्षं this wonderful childhood pastime of Krishna regarding Putana's liberation, श्रद्धया शृणुयात् may hear with faith, गोविन्दे रतिं लभते obtains loving attachment to Govinda.

Simple meaning: The mortal person who hears with faith this wonderful childhood pastime of Krishna regarding the liberation of Putana, obtains loving attachment to Govinda.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमे स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे

पूतनामोक्षं नाम षष्ठः अध्यायः ॥6॥

Thus ends the sixth discourse titled ‘Deliverance of Putana’, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
