Chapter 40

Akrura’s eulogy of the Lord

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

अक्रूरस्तुतिर्नाम चत्वारिंशः अध्यायः

(30 Shlokas)

अक्रूर उवाच

नतोऽस्म्यहं त्वाखिलहेतुहेतुं नारायणं पूरुषमाद्यमव्ययम् ।

यन्नाभिजातादरविन्दकोशाद् ब्रह्माऽऽविरासीद् यत एष लोकः ॥10.40.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: अक्रूरः उवाच Akrura said, नतः bowed/prostrated, अस्मि am, अहम् I, त्वा (त्वाम्) to You, अखिल-हेतु-हेतुम् the cause of all causes, नारायणम् Narayana, पूरुषम् the Supreme Person, आद्यम् the original, अव्ययम् infallible/imperishable, यत्-नाभि-जातात् born from whose navel, अरविन्द-कोशात् from the whorl of the lotus, ब्रह्मा Brahma, आविरासीत् appeared, यतः from whom, एषः this, लोकः universe (came to be)

Anvaya: अक्रूरः उवाच — अहम् अखिलहेतुहेतुम् आद्यम् अव्ययम् पूरुषं नारायणं त्वा नतः अस्मि; यन्नाभिजातात् अरविन्दकोशात् ब्रह्मा आविरासीत् यतः एषः लोकः (अभवत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अक्रूरः उवाच Akrura said, अहम् I, अखिलहेतुहेतुम् who are the cause of all causes, आद्यम् the original, अव्ययम् the imperishable, पूरुषं नारायणं Supreme Person Narayana, त्वा नतः अस्मि bow down to You, यन्नाभिजातात् from whose navel-born, अरविन्दकोशात् lotus-whorl, ब्रह्मा आविरासीत् Lord Brahma appeared, यतः (च) and from whom, एषः लोकः this entire universe (was created).

Simple Meaning: Akrura said: I bow down to You, the Supreme Person Narayana, the original and imperishable Being who is the cause of all causes. From the lotus blooming from Your navel, Lord Brahma appeared, and from him, this entire universe was brought into existence.

भूस्तोयमग्निः पवनः खमादिर्महानजादिर्मन इन्द्रियाणि ।

सर्वेन्दियार्था विबुधाश्च सर्वे ये हेतवस्ते जगतोऽङ्गभूताः ॥10.40.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: भूः earth, तोयम् water, अग्निः fire, पवनः air, खम् ether, आदिः the ego (ahankara), महान् the mahat-tattva (total material energy), अजा-आदिः the unmanifest material nature (prakriti) and others, मनः the mind, इन्द्रियाणि the senses, सर्व-इन्द्रिय-अर्थाः all the objects of the senses, विबुधाः the demigods, च and, सर्वे all, ये who, हेतवः causes, ते Your, जगतः of the universe, अङ्ग-भूताः are born of Your body (Your limbs/parts)

Anvaya: भूः तोयम् अग्निः पवनः खम् आदिः महान् अजादिः मनः इन्द्रियाणि सर्वेन्दियार्थाः सर्वे विबुधाः च ये जगतः हेतवः (सन्ति), (ते) सर्वे ते अङ्गभूताः (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भूः Earth, तोयम् water, अग्निः fire, पवनः air, खम् ether, आदिः false ego, महान् the total material energy, अजादिः the unmanifest and so on, मनः the mind, इन्द्रियाणि the senses, सर्वेन्दियार्थाः all sense objects, च and सर्वे विबुधाः all the demigods—ये those who जगतः हेतवः are the causes of the universe, (ते) सर्वे they all, ते अङ्गभूताः are parts/emanations of Your divine form.

Simple Meaning: Earth, water, fire, air, and ether; the ego, the total material energy, and the unmanifested nature; the mind, the senses, the objects of the senses, and all the presiding deities—all these factors, which combine to cause the creation of this universe, are simply parts and parcels of Your own divine form.

नैते स्वरूपं विदुरात्मनस्ते ह्यजादयोऽनात्मतया गृहीताः ।

अजोऽनुबद्धः स गुणैरजाया गुणात् परं वेद न ते स्वरूपम् ॥10.40.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, एते these (the elements and demigods), स्वरूपम् the true nature, विदुः know, आत्मनः of the Self, ते Your, हि indeed, अज-आदयः Brahma and others, अनात्मतया because of being non-Self (materially covered), गृहीताः possessed/influenced; अजः the unborn (Brahma), अनुबद्धः bound, सः he, गुणैः by the modes/qualities, अजायाः of the material nature (Prakriti), गुणात् than the modes, परम् beyond, वेद knows, न not, ते Your, स्वरूपम् true nature

Anvaya: एते अजादयः हि अनात्मतया गृहीताः ते आत्मनः स्वरूपं न विदुः। सः अजः अजायाः गुणैः अनुबद्धः (अस्ति), (अतः) गुणात् परं ते स्वरूपं न वेद।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एते अजादयः These (elements and) Brahma and others, हि indeed, अनात्मतया गृहीताः being influenced by material identity, ते आत्मनः स्वरूपं Your own true nature, न विदुः do not know. सः अजः That Brahma, अजायाः गुणैः by the modes of material nature, अनुबद्धः is bound; (अतः) therefore, गुणात् परं (that which is) beyond the modes, ते स्वरूपं Your true form, न वेद he does not know.

Simple Meaning: Because they are covered by material identification, the elements and even the demigods like Brahma cannot know Your true identity. Brahma himself is bound by the modes of material nature; therefore, he cannot perceive Your transcendental form, which exists entirely beyond those modes.

त्वां योगिनो यजन्त्यद्धा महापुरुषमीश्वरम् ।

साध्यात्मं साधिभूतं च साधिदैवं च साधवः ॥10.40.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वाम् You, योगिनः yogis, यजन्ति worship, अद्धा truly, महापुरुषम् the Supreme Person, ईश्वरम् the Controller, स-अध्यात्मम् along with the self, स-अधिभूतम् along with the material elements, च and, स-अधिदैवम् along with the presiding deities, च and, साधवः saintly persons

Anvaya: साधवः योगिनः महापुरुषम् ईश्वरं त्वां साध्यात्मं साधिभूतं च साधिदैवं च अद्धा यजन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: साधवः Saintly persons, योगिनः yogis, महापुरुषम् the Supreme Person, ईश्वरं the Controller, त्वां You, साध्यात्मं along with the self, साधिभूतं along with the material elements, च and, साधिदैवं along with the presiding deities, च and, अद्धा truly, यजन्ति worship.

Simple Meaning: Saintly yogis worship You, the Supreme Person and Controller, who are present along with the three levels of existence: the individual self, the material elements, and the presiding deities.

त्रय्या च विद्यया केचित् त्वां वै वैतानिका द्विजाः ।

यजन्ते विततैर्यज्ञैर्नानारूपामराख्यया ॥10.40.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्रय्या of the three Vedas, च and, विद्यया by the knowledge, केचित् some, त्वाम् You, वै indeed, वैतानिकाः followers of the Vedic ritual prescriptions, द्विजाः brahmanas, यजन्ते worship, विततैः elaborate, यज्ञैः with sacrifices, नाना-रूप-अमर-आख्यया through the names of various deities who are Your forms

Anvaya: केचित् वैतानिकाः द्विजाः च त्रय्या विद्यया नानारूपामराख्यया विततैः यज्ञैः त्वां वै यजन्ते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: केचित् Some, वैतानिकाः द्विजाः ritualistic brahmanas, च and, त्रय्या विद्यया by the knowledge of the three Vedas, नानारूपामराख्यया under the names of various demigods who are Your diverse forms, विततैः यज्ञैः with elaborate sacrifices, त्वां You, वै indeed, यजन्ते worship.

Simple Meaning: Some brahmanas, who strictly follow the ritualistic injunctions of the three Vedas, worship You by performing elaborate sacrifices. In these rituals, they address You through the names of various demigods, who are actually different manifestations of Your own divine form.

एके त्वाखिलकर्माणि संन्यस्योपशमं गताः ।

ज्ञानिनो ज्ञानयज्ञेन यजन्ति ज्ञानविग्रहम् ॥10.40.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: एके some, त्वा to You, अखिल-कर्माणि all their activities unto You, संन्यस्य renouncing/offering, उपशमम् peacefulness/freedom from material desire, गताः attained, ज्ञानिनः followers of the path of knowledge, ज्ञान-यज्ञेन by the sacrifice of knowledge, यजन्ति worship, ज्ञान-विग्रहम् the embodiment of knowledge

Anvaya: एके ज्ञानिनः अखिलकर्माणि त्वा संन्यस्य उपशमं गताः (सन्तः) ज्ञानयज्ञेन ज्ञानविग्रहं यजन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एके ज्ञानिनः Some seekers of knowledge, अखिलकर्माणि all activities, त्वा संन्यस्य having offered unto You, उपशमं गताः having attained peace, ज्ञानयज्ञेन by the sacrifice of knowledge, ज्ञानविग्रहं the embodiment of knowledge, यजन्ति worship.

Simple Meaning: Some seekers of knowledge offer all their activities unto You and, having attained a state of peacefulness, worship You, the personification of knowledge, through the sacrifice of transcendental knowledge.

अन्ये च संस्कृतात्मानो विधिनाभिहितेन ते ।

यजन्ति त्वन्मयास्त्वां वै बहुमूर्त्येकमूर्तिकम् ॥10.40.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्ये others, च and, संस्कृत-आत्मानः those whose minds are purified, विधिना by the rules, अभिहितेन as prescribed, ते by You, यजन्ति worship, त्वत्-मयाः absorbed in You, त्वाम् You, वै indeed, बहु-मूर्ति-एक-मूर्तिकम् who is the one single form appearing as many forms

Anvaya: अन्ये च संस्कृतात्मानः ते अभिहितेन विधिना त्वन्मयाः (सन्तः) बहुमूर्त्येकमूर्तिकं त्वां वै यजन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अन्ये च And others, संस्कृतात्मानः those with purified hearts, ते अभिहितेन विधिना according to the prescribed rules by You, त्वन्मयाः being absorbed in You, बहुमूर्त्येकमूर्तिकं the one form who exists as many forms, त्वां You, वै indeed, यजन्ति worship.

Simple Meaning: Still others, whose hearts have been purified, follow the rules of worship prescribed by You in the Vaishnava Tantras. Completely absorbed in You, they worship Your one single form as it manifests in many different ways.

त्वामेवान्ये शिवोक्तेन मार्गेण शिवरूपिणम् ।

बह्वाचार्यविभेदेन भगवन् समुपासते ॥10.40.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वाम् You, एव alone, अन्ये others, शिव-उक्तेन spoken by Lord Shiva, मार्गेण by the path, शिव-रूपिणम् in the form of Lord Shiva, बहु-आचार्य-विभेदेन according to the various different presentations of many acharyas, भगवन् O Supreme Lord, समुपासते they worship

Anvaya: भगवन्! अन्ये शिवोक्तेन मार्गेण बह्वाचार्यविभेदेन शिवरूपिणं त्वाम् एव समुपासते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवन् O Supreme Lord, अन्ये others, शिवोक्तेन मार्गेण by the path described by Lord Shiva, बह्वाचार्यविभेदेन through the various divisions of many teachers, शिवरूपिणं who has the form of Shiva, त्वाम् You, एव alone, समुपासते worship.

Simple Meaning: O Supreme Lord, there are others who follow the path described by Lord Shiva. According to the different methods taught by various teachers, they worship You alone in Your form as Lord Shiva.

सर्व एव यजन्ति त्वां सर्वदेवमयेश्वरम् ।

येऽप्यन्यदेवताभक्ता यद्यप्यन्यधियः प्रभो ॥10.40.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: सर्वे all, एव indeed, यजन्ति worship, त्वाम् You, सर्व-देवमय-ईश्वरम् the Lord who comprises all demigods, ये who, अपि also, अन्य-देवता-भक्ताः devotees of other deities, यद्यपि although, अन्य-धियः whose minds are directed elsewhere, प्रभो O Lord

Anvaya: प्रभो! ये अन्यदेवताभक्ताः (सन्ति), यद्यपि (ते) अन्यधियः, (तथापि ते) सर्वे एव सर्वदेवमयेश्वरं त्वां यजन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रभो O Lord, ये those who, अन्यदेवताभक्ताः are devotees of other demigods, यद्यपि अन्यधियः although whose minds are directed toward others, (तथापि ते) nevertheless they, सर्वे all, एव indeed, सर्वदेवमयेश्वरं the Lord who contains all deities, त्वां You, यजन्ति worship.

Simple Meaning: O Lord, even those who are devotees of other demigods and whose minds are fixed on them are actually worshipping You alone, because You are the Supreme Lord who comprises all the demigods.

यथाद्रिप्रभवा नद्यः पर्जन्यापूरिताः प्रभो ।

विशन्ति सर्वतः सिन्धुं तद्वत्त्वां गतयोऽन्ततः ॥10.40.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: यथा as, अद्रि-प्रभवाः born from the mountains, नद्यः rivers, पर्जन्य-आपूरिताः filled by the rain, प्रभो O Lord, विशन्ति enter, सर्वतः from all sides, सिन्धुम् the ocean, तद्वत् in the same way, त्वाम् You, गतयः all paths/destinations, अन्ततः in the end

Anvaya: प्रभो! यथा पर्जन्यापूरिताः अद्रिप्रभवाः नद्यः सर्वतः सिन्धुं विशन्ति, तद्वत् अन्ततः (सर्वाः) गतयः त्वाम् (विशन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रभो O Lord, यथा just as, पर्जन्यापूरिताः अद्रिप्रभवाः नद्यः rivers filled by rain, arising from the mountains, सर्वतः from all directions, सिन्धुं विशन्ति enter the ocean, तद्वत् in that same way, अन्ततः ultimately, (सर्वाः) गतयः all the different paths, त्वाम् (विशन्ति) reach You.

Simple Meaning: O Lord, just as rivers that arise from the mountains and are filled by the rain flow from all sides to enter the ocean, similarly all the various paths of worship eventually lead to You alone in the end.

सत्त्वं रजस्तम इति भवतः प्रकृतेर्गुणाः ।

तेषु हि प्राकृताः प्रोता आब्रह्मस्थावरादयः ॥10.40.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: सत्त्वम् goodness, रजः passion, तमः ignorance, इति thus, भवतः Your, प्रकृतेः of the material nature, गुणाः the modes, तेषु in them, हि indeed, प्राकृताः those products of material nature, प्रोताः are woven/intertwined, आ-ब्रह्म-स्थावर-आदयः from Brahma down to the non-moving entities and so on

Anvaya: सत्त्वं रजः तमः इति भवतः प्रकृतेः गुणाः (सन्ति), आब्रह्मस्थावरादयः प्राकृताः तेषु हि प्रोताः (सन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सत्त्वं रजः तमः इति Goodness, passion, and ignorance, भवतः Your, प्रकृतेः गुणाः are the modes of material nature, आब्रह्मस्थावरादयः from Lord Brahma down to the stationary living beings, प्राकृताः those born of nature, तेषु हि in them indeed, प्रोताः are woven.

Simple Meaning: Goodness, passion, and ignorance are the three modes of Your material nature. All created beings, from the exalted Lord Brahma down to the stationary plants and stones, are inextricably woven into these modes.

तुभ्यं नमस्तेऽस्त्वविषक्तदृष्टये सर्वात्मने सर्वधियां च साक्षिणे ।

गुणप्रवाहोऽयमविद्यया कृतः प्रवर्तते देवनृतिर्यगात्मसु ॥10.40.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: तुभ्यम् unto You, नमः obeisances, ते unto You, अस्तु let there be, अविषक्त-दृष्टये whose vision is unattached, सर्व-आत्मने the soul of all, सर्व-धियाम् of all consciousness/intelligences, च and, साक्षिणे to the witness, गुण-प्रवाहः the flow of the modes of nature, अयम् this, अविद्यया by ignorance, कृतः created, प्रवर्तते operates, देव-नृ-तिर्यग्-आत्मसु among the bodies of demigods, humans, and animals

Anvaya: अविषक्तदृष्टये सर्वात्मने सर्वधियां साक्षिणे च तुभ्यं ते नमः अस्तु। अयम् अविद्यया कृतः गुणप्रवाहः देवनृतिर्यगात्मसु प्रवर्तते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अविषक्तदृष्टये To the one whose vision is unattached, सर्वात्मने to the soul of all beings, सर्वधियां साक्षिणे to the witness of all intelligences, च and, तुभ्यं ते unto You, नमः अस्तु let there be obeisances. अयम् This, अविद्यया कृतः created by ignorance, गुणप्रवाहः flow of the material modes, देवनृतिर्यगात्मसु among the forms of demigods, humans, and animals, प्रवर्तते operates.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances unto You, whose vision is ever unattached, who are the Soul of all souls and the witness of everyone's intelligence. This flow of the material modes, generated by ignorance (Avidya), continues to operate among the various forms of demigods, humans, and animals.

अग्निर्मुखं तेऽवनिरङ्घ्रिरीक्षणं सूर्यो नभो नाभिरथो दिशः श्रुतिः ।

द्यौः कं सुरेन्द्रास्तव बाहवोऽर्णवाः कुक्षिर्मरुत् प्राणबलं प्रकल्पितम् ॥10.40.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: अग्निः fire, मुखम् mouth, ते Your, अवनिः earth, अङ्घ्रिः feet, ईक्षणम् eye, सूर्यः the sun, नभः the sky, नाभिः navel, अथो and also, दिशः the directions, श्रुतिः sense of hearing, द्यौः the heavenly planets, कम् head, सुर-इन्द्राः the leaders of the demigods, तव Your, बाहवः arms, अर्णवाः the oceans, कुक्षिः abdomen, मरुत् the wind, प्राण-बलम् vital strength and physical power, प्रकल्पितम् is conceived

Anvaya: अग्निः ते मुखम्, अवनिः अङ्घ्रिः, सूर्यः ईक्षणं नभः नाभिः, अथः दिशः श्रुतिः, द्यौः कं, सुरेन्द्राः तव बाहवः, अर्णवाः कुक्षिः, मरुत् (तव) प्राणबलं प्रकल्पितम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अग्निः ते मुखम् Fire is Your mouth, अवनिः अङ्घ्रिः earth is the feet, सूर्यः ईक्षणं the sun is the eye, नभः नाभिः the sky is the navel, अथः and also, दिशः श्रुतिः the directions are the hearing, द्यौः कं the heaven is the head, सुरेन्द्राः तव बाहवः the demigod leaders are Your arms, अर्णवाः कुक्षिः the oceans are the abdomen, मरुत् (तव) प्राणबलं the wind is Your vital strength, प्रकल्पितम् is conceived as.

Simple Meaning: Fire is Your mouth, the earth Your feet, and the sun Your eye. The sky is Your navel, and the directions are Your sense of hearing. The heavenly planets are Your head, the demigods are Your arms, and the oceans are Your abdomen. The wind is conceived as Your vital strength and physical power.

रोमाणि वृक्षौषधयः शिरोरुहा मेघाः परस्यास्थिनखानि तेऽद्रयः ।

निमेषणं रात्र्यहनी प्रजापतिर्मेढर्स्तु वृष्टिस्तव वीर्यमिष्यते ॥10.40.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: रोमाणि the hairs on Your body, वृक्ष-ओषधयः the trees and plants, शिरोरुहाः the hair on Your head, मेघाः the clouds, परस्य of the Supreme, अस्थि-नखानि the bones and nails, ते Your, अद्रयः the mountains, निमेषणम् the blinking of Your eyes, रात्रि-अहनी night and day, प्रजापतिः the progenitor of living beings, मेढ्रः the genitals, तु and, वृष्टिः the rain, तव Your, वीर्यम् semen, इष्यते is considered

Anvaya: वृक्षौषधयः ते रोमाणि, मेघाः शिरोरुहाः, अद्रयः परस्य ते अस्थिनखानि, रात्र्यहनी निमेषणं, प्रजापतिः मेढ्रः, वृष्टिः तु तव वीर्यम् इष्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वृक्षौषधयः ते रोमाणि Trees and plants are Your body hairs, मेघाः शिरोरुहाः the clouds are the hair on the head, अद्रयः the mountains, परस्य ते अस्थिनखानि of You the Supreme, are the bones and nails, रात्र्यहनी निमेषणं night and day are the blinking of the eyes, प्रजापतिः मेढ्रः the Prajapati is the genitals, वृष्टिः तु तव वीर्यम् इष्यते rain, indeed, is considered Your semen.

Simple Meaning: The trees and plants are the hairs on Your body, and the clouds are the hair on Your head. The mountains are Your bones and nails, and the passage of day and night is the blinking of Your eyes. The progenitor of all beings is Your genitals, and the rain is considered Your semen.

त्वय्यव्ययात्मन् पुरुषे प्रकल्पिता लोकाः सपाला बहुजीवसङ्कुलाः ।

यथा जले सञ्जिहते जलौकसोऽप्युदुम्बरे वा मशका मनोमये ॥10.40.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वयि in You, अव्यय-आत्मन् O inexhaustible Supreme Soul, पुरुषे the Supreme Person, प्रकल्पिताः conceived, लोकाः the worlds, स-पालाः along with their guardians, बहु-जीव-सङ्कुलाः crowded with many living entities, यथा as, जले in water, सञ्जिहते move about, जल-ओकसः aquatic animals, अपि also, उदुम्बरे in the fruit of the Udumbara tree, वा or, मशकाः tiny gnats, मनो-मये within the mind-born (universal form)

Anvaya: अव्ययात्मन्! मनोमये पुरुषे त्वयि सपालाः बहुजीवसङ्कुलाः लोकाः प्रकल्पिताः (सन्ति), यथा जले जलौकसः उदुम्बरे मशकाः वा (सञ्जिहते), (तथा ते त्वयि) सञ्जिहते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अव्ययात्मन् O Inexhaustible Soul, मनोमये within the universal form, पुरुषे the Supreme Person, त्वयि in You, सपालाः with their rulers, बहुजीवसङ्कुलाः crowded with many beings, लोकाः the worlds, प्रकल्पिताः are situated, यथा just as, जले in water, जलौकसः aquatic creatures, उदुम्बरे in an Udumbara fig, मशकाः tiny insects, वा or, (सञ्जिहते) move about, (तथा) so (they) move within You.

Simple Meaning: O inexhaustible Supreme Soul, all the worlds with their presiding guardians and teeming multitudes of living entities are situated within You, the Supreme Person. Just as aquatic creatures move about in water or tiny gnats within an Udumbara fruit, all these universes move within Your vast universal form.

यानि यानीह रूपाणि क्रीडनार्थं बिभर्षि हि ।

तैरामृष्टशुचो लोका मुदा गायन्ति ते यशः ॥10.40.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: यानि यानि whichever, इह in this world, रूपाणि forms, क्रीडन-अर्थम् for the sake of Your pastimes, बिभर्षि You assume, हि indeed, तैः by them, आमृष्ट-शुचः having their sorrows wiped away, लोकाः the people, मुदा with joy, गायन्ति sing, ते Your, यशः glories

Anvaya: इह क्रीडनार्थं यानि यानि रूपाणि (त्वं) बिभर्षि हि, तैः आमृष्टशुचः लोकाः मुदा ते यशः गायन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इह In this world, क्रीडनार्थं for the purpose of pastimes, यानि यानि whichever, रूपाणि forms, (त्वं) बिभर्षि You assume, हि indeed, तैः by those forms, आमृष्टशुचः having their grief removed, लोकाः the people, मुदा with happiness, ते Your, यशः fame, गायन्ति sing.

Simple Meaning: Whatever forms You assume in this world for the sake of performing Your pastimes, those forms take away the sorrows of the people, who then joyfully sing Your glories.

नमः कारणमत्स्याय प्रलयाब्धिचराय च ।

हयशीर्ष्णे नमस्तुभ्यं मधुकैटभमृत्यवे ॥10.40.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, कारण-मत्स्याय to the cause of Matsya avatara, प्रलय-अब्धि-चराय who moves about in the ocean of dissolution, च and, हय-शीर्ष्णे to the horse-headed incarnation (of Hayagriva), नमः obeisances, तुभ्यम् unto You, मधु-कैटभ-मृत्यवे to the killer of the asuras Madhu and Kaitabha

Anvaya: कारणमत्स्याय प्रलयाब्धिचराय च नमः, मधुकैटभमृत्यवे हयशीर्ष्णे तुभ्यं नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कारणमत्स्याय To the Fish incarnation who is the cause, प्रलयाब्धिचराय who moves in the ocean of dissolution, च and, नमः obeisances, मधुकैटभमृत्यवे to the death of Madhu and Kaitabha, हयशीर्ष्णे who has the head of a horse, तुभ्यं unto You, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances to You, who appeared as the Fish incarnation to move within the ocean of universal dissolution. I also offer my obeisances to You in Your horse-headed form as Hayagriva, the slayer of the asuras Madhu and Kaitabha.

अकूपाराय बृहते नमो मन्दरधारिणे ।

क्षित्युद्धारविहाराय नमः सूकरमूर्तये ॥10.40.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: अकूपाराय to the tortoise, बृहते the great, नमः obeisances, मन्दर-धारिणे to the holder of Mount Mandara, क्षिति-उद्धार-विहाराय whose pastime is the lifting of the earth, नमः obeisances, सूकर-मूर्तये to the form of the boar

Anvaya: मन्दरधारिणे बृहते अकूपाराय नमः, क्षित्युद्धारविहाराय सूकरमूर्तये नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मन्दरधारिणे To the holder of Mount Mandara, बृहते the great, अकूपाराय tortoise, नमः obeisances, क्षित्युद्धारविहाराय whose pastime is the lifting of the earth, सूकरमूर्तये to the boar form, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances to You, the great tortoise who held up Mount Mandara, and to You, the Lord who assumed the form of a boar and whose pastime is to lift the earth from the depths of the ocean.

नमस्तेऽद्भुतसिंहाय साधुलोकभयापह ।

वामनाय नमस्तुभ्यं क्रान्तत्रिभुवनाय च ॥10.40.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमस्ते obeisances unto You, अद्भुत-सिंहाय to the wonderful lion, साधु-लोक-भय-अपह O remover of the fear of the saintly people, वामनाय to the dwarf, नमः obeisances, तुभ्यम् unto You, क्रान्त-त्रि-भुवनाय to the one who stepped over the three worlds, च and

Anvaya: साधुलोकभयापह! अद्भुतसिंहाय ते नमः, क्रान्तत्रिभुवनाय वामनाय तुभ्यं च नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: साधुलोकभयापह O remover of the fear of the saintly, अद्भुतसिंहाय to the wonderful lion, ते unto You, नमः obeisances, क्रान्तत्रिभुवनाय who stepped over the three worlds, वामनाय to the dwarf incarnation, तुभ्यं unto You, च and, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: O remover of the fear of the saintly, I offer my obeisances unto You, who appeared as the wonderful lion-man (Narasimha avatara). I also offer my obeisances unto You, who appeared as the dwarf Vamana and stepped across all three worlds.

नमो भृगूणां पतये दृप्तक्षत्रवनच्छिदे ।

नमस्ते रघुवर्याय रावणान्तकराय च ॥10.40.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, भृगूणाम् of the Bhrigus, पतये to the lord, दृप्त-क्षत्र-वन-च्छिदे to the cutter of the forest of the arrogant kshatriyas, नमः obeisances, ते unto You, रघु-वर्याय to the best of the Raghu dynasty, रावण-अन्त-कराय to the one who ended Ravana, च and

Anvaya: दृप्तक्षत्रवनच्छिदे भृगूणां पतये नमः, रावणान्तकराय रघुवर्याय च ते नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: दृप्तक्षत्रवनच्छिदे The cutter of the forest of arrogant kshatriyas, भृगूणां पतये to the lord of the Bhrigus (Parashurama), नमः obeisances, रावणान्तकराय to the finisher of Ravana, रघुवर्याय to the best of the Raghus (Rama), च and, ते unto You, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances to You, the lord of the Bhrigus, who cut down the forest of the arrogant Kshatriyas. I also offer my obeisances to You, the greatest of the Raghu dynasty, who brought an end to Ravana.

नमस्ते वासुदेवाय नमः सङ्कर्षणाय च ।

प्रद्युम्नायानिरुद्धाय सात्वतां पतये नमः ॥10.40.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमस्ते obeisances unto You, वासुदेवाय to the son of Vasudeva (Lord Krishna), नमः obeisances, सङ्कर्षणाय to Lord Sankarshana, च and, प्रद्युम्नाय to Lord Pradyumna, अनिरुद्धाय to Lord Aniruddha, सात्वताम् of the Satvatas (devotees), पतये to the Lord/Chief, नमः obeisances

Anvaya: वासुदेवाय नमस्ते, सङ्कर्षणाय च नमः, प्रद्युम्नाय अनिरुद्धाय सात्वतां पतये नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वासुदेवाय To Vasudeva, नमस्ते obeisances unto You, सङ्कर्षणाय to Sankarshana, च and, नमः obeisances, प्रद्युम्नाय to Pradyumna, अनिरुद्धाय to Aniruddha, सात्वतां पतये to the Lord of the Yadus/devotees, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances unto You, Lord Vasudeva, and to Lord Sankarshana. I offer my obeisances to Lord Pradyumna and Lord Aniruddha, and to You, the Lord of all Your devotees.

नमो बुद्धाय शुद्धाय दैत्यदानवमोहिने ।

म्लेच्छप्रायक्षत्रहन्त्रे नमस्ते कल्किरूपिणे ॥10.40.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, बुद्धाय to Lord Buddha, शुद्धाय to the pure one, दैत्य-दानव-मोहिने to the deluder of the Daityas and Danavas (the sons of Diti and Danu), म्लेच्छ-प्राय-क्षत्र-हन्त्रे to the killer of the royal order who have become like barbarians, नमस्ते obeisances unto You, कल्कि-रूपिणे to the one who has the form of Kalki

Anvaya: शुद्धाय दैत्यदानवमोहिने बुद्धाय नमः, म्लेच्छप्रायक्षत्रहन्त्रे कल्किरूपिणे ते नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: शुद्धाय To the pure one, दैत्यदानवमोहिने to the deluder of the daityas and danavas, बुद्धाय to Buddha, नमः obeisances, म्लेच्छप्रायक्षत्रहन्त्रे to the slayer of the ruling class who are mostly barbarians, कल्किरूपिणे to the one in the form of Kalki, ते unto You, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances to Lord Buddha, the pure one who bewildered the Daityas and Danavas. I also offer my obeisances unto You in Your form as Kalki, the slayer of the pseudo-kings who have degenerated into barbarians.

Note: Lord Buddha mentioned here is not Gautama Buddha. This Buddha avatara happened at the time of the destruction of the three cities by Shiva. This is described in detail in Shiva Purana.

भगवञ्जीवलोकोऽयं मोहितस्तव मायया ।

अहं ममेत्यसद्ग्राहो भ्राम्यते कर्मवर्त्मसु ॥10.40.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवन् O Supreme Lord, जीव-लोकः the world of living entities, अयम् this, मोहितः deluded, तव Your, मायया by the illusory energy, अहम् मम इति "I and mine," असत्-ग्राहः by the false perception, भ्राम्यते is made to wander, कर्म-वर्त्मसु on the paths of fruitive activities

Anvaya: भगवन्! तव मायया मोहितः अयं जीवलोकः "अहं मम" इति असद्ग्राहो (सन्) कर्मवर्त्मसु भ्राम्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवन् O Supreme Lord, तव मायया by Your illusory energy, मोहितः deluded, अयं जीवलोकः this world of living beings, "अहं मम" इति "I and mine" thus, असद्ग्राहः with a false grasp of reality, कर्मवर्त्मसु on the paths of action/karma, भ्राम्यते is caused to wander.

Simple Meaning: O Supreme Lord, this world of living entities is deluded by Your illusory energy (Maya). Possessed by the false notions of "I" and "mine," the living beings are forced to wander aimlessly along the various paths of fruitive work.

अहं चात्मात्मजागारदारार्थस्वजनादिषु ।

भ्रमामि स्वप्नकल्पेषु मूढः सत्यधिया विभो ॥10.40.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहम् I, च and, आत्मा-आत्मज-आगार-दार-अर्थ-स्वजन-आदिषु regarding the body, children, home, wife, wealth, kinsmen and so on, भ्रमामि I wander, स्वप्न-कल्पेषु in things that are like a dream, मूढः a fool, सत्य-धिया with the thought that they are real, विभो O Almighty Lord

Anvaya: विभो! मूढः अहं च स्वप्नकल्पेषु आत्मात्मजागारदारार्थस्वजनादिषु सत्यधिया भ्रमामि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विभो O Almighty Lord, मूढः अहं a fool like me, च and, स्वप्नकल्पेषु in things which are no more real than dreams, आत्मात्मजागारदारार्थस्वजनादिषु regarding the body, children, home, wife, wealth, and relatives, सत्यधिया with the notion of reality, भ्रमामि I am wandering.

Simple Meaning: O Almighty Lord, I am a fool wandering in this world, thinking that things as temporary as dreams—such as my body, children, home, spouse, wealth, and relatives—are permanent and real.

अनित्यानात्मदुःखेषु विपर्ययमतिर्ह्यहम् ।

द्वन्द्वारामस्तमोविष्टो न जाने त्वाऽऽत्मनः प्रियम् ॥10.40.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: अनित्य-अनात्म-दुःखेषु in things that are temporary, not the self, and full of misery, विपर्यय-मतिः having a perverted/mistaken intellect, हि indeed, अहम् I, द्वन्द्व-आरामः taking pleasure in dualities (like likes and dislikes), तमः-आविष्टः overwhelmed by ignorance/darkness, न जाने I do not know, त्वा (त्वाम्) You, आत्मनः of the soul, प्रियम् the dear-most

Anvaya: हि अहम् अनित्यानात्मदुःखेषु विपर्ययमतिः द्वन्द्वारामः तमोविष्टः (सन्) आत्मनः प्रियं त्वा न जाने।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: हि Indeed, अहम् I, अनित्यानात्मदुःखेषु in things that are non-eternal, not the self, and painful, विपर्ययमतिः having a mistaken understanding, द्वन्द्वारामः rejoicing in dualities, तमोविष्टः possessed by ignorance, आत्मनः प्रियं the beloved of the soul, त्वा You, न जाने do not know.

Simple Meaning: Indeed, I have a mistaken intellect that seeks reality in the temporary, the self in the non-self, and happiness in what is actually miserable. Delighting in the dualities of life and blinded by ignorance, I have failed to recognize You, the true beloved of my soul.

यथाबुधो जलं हित्वा प्रतिच्छन्नं तदुद्भवैः ।

अभ्येति मृगतृष्णां वै तद्वत्त्वाहं पराङ्मुखः ॥10.40.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: यथा as, अबुधः a foolish person, जलम् water, हित्वा leaving/ignoring, प्रतिच्छन्नम् covered, तत्-उद्भवैः by things produced from that (water, like weeds/moss), अभ्येति goes towards, मृग-तृष्णाम् a mirage, वै indeed, तद्वत् in that same way, त्वा (त्वाम्) from You, अहम् I, पराङ्मुखः looking away/turned away

Anvaya: यथा अबुधः तदुद्भवैः प्रतिच्छन्नं जलं हित्वा मृगतृष्णां वै अभ्येति, तद्वत् अहं त्वा पराङ्मुखः (अस्मि)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यथा Just as, अबुधः a fool, तदुद्भवैः प्रतिच्छन्नं covered by what grows from it (like moss), जलं हित्वा leaving behind the water, मृगतृष्णां वै अभ्येति goes indeed toward a mirage, तद्वत् in the same way, अहं I, त्वा पराङ्मुखः am turned away from You.

Simple Meaning: Just as a foolish person might ignore a pool of water because it is covered by the weeds growing out of it and instead run toward a mirage, I have turned my back on You, seeking satisfaction in the illusory world.

नोत्सहेऽहं कृपणधीः कामकर्महतं मनः ।

रोद्धुं प्रमाथिभिश्चाक्षैर्ह्रियमाणमितस्ततः ॥10.40.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: न उत्सहे I am not able/do not have the power, अहम् I, कृपण-धीः having a bewildered or weak intelligence, काम-कर्म-हतम् struck/corrupted by lusty desires and fruitive activities, मनः the mind, रोद्धुम् to restrain, प्रमाथिभिः by the agitating, च and, अक्षैः by the senses, ह्रियमाणम् being dragged, इतः ततः here and there

Anvaya: कृपणधीः अहं कामकर्महतं प्रमाथिभिः अक्षैः च इतस्ततः ह्रियमाणं मनः रोद्धुं न उत्सहे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृपणधीः Having a weak intelligence, अहं I, कामकर्महतं (my mind) which is ruined by desires and karma, प्रमाथिभिः अक्षैः by the agitating senses, च and, इतस्ततः here and there, ह्रियमाणं being dragged, मनः the mind, रोद्धुं to restrain, न उत्सहे do not have the strength.

Simple Meaning: My intelligence is weak and my mind has been corrupted by material desires and the reactions of my past actions. I find myself unable to restrain this mind, which is being violently dragged here and there by the agitating senses.

सोऽहं तवाङ्घ्र्युपगतोऽस्म्यसतां दुरापं तच्चाप्यहं भवदनुग्रह ईश मन्ये ।

पुंसो भवेद् यर्हि संसरणापवर्गस्त्वय्यब्जनाभ सदुपासनया मतिः स्यात् ॥10.40.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः अहम् that same I, तव Your, अङ्घ्रि-उपगतः अस्मि have approached the feet, असताम् for those who are not self-controlled, दुरापम् difficult to attain, तत् च अपि and that very fact, अहम् I, भवत्-अनुग्रहः Your mercy, ईश O Lord, मन्ये I consider, पुंसः for a person, भवेत् may happen, यर्हि when, संसरण-अपवर्गः the end of the cycle of birth and death, त्वयि in You, अब्ज-नाभ O Lotus-navelled Lord, सत्-उपासनया through the association of saintly devotees, मतिः स्यात् consciousness/devotion develops

Anvaya: ईश! सः अहम् असतां दुरापं तव अङ्घ्र्युपगतः अस्मि, तत् च अपि अहं भवदनुग्रहं मन्ये। अब्जनाभ! यर्हि पुंसः संसरणापवर्गः भवेत्, (तर्हि) सदुपासनया त्वयि मतिः स्यात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ईश O Lord, सः अहम् that I, असतां दुरापं difficult to reach for the wicked, तव अङ्घ्र्युपगतः अस्मि have come to Your feet, तत् च अपि and even that, अहं भवदनुग्रहं मन्ये I consider Your grace. अब्जनाभ O Lotus-navelled One, यर्हि पुंसः when for a man, संसरणापवर्गः भवेत् the end of worldly wandering occurs, (तर्हि) then, सदुपासनया by service to the saintly, त्वयि मतिः devotion in You, स्यात् arises.

Simple Meaning: O Lord, I have finally come to Your feet, which are so difficult for the worldly-minded to reach. I consider this opportunity to be Your direct mercy. O Lotus-navelled Lord, when the time comes for a person's wandering in the cycle of birth and death to end, they develop devotion to You through the association and service of saintly souls.

नमो विज्ञानमात्राय सर्वप्रत्ययहेतवे ।

पुरुषेशप्रधानाय ब्रह्मणेऽनन्तशक्तये ॥10.40.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, विज्ञान-मात्राय to the embodiment of pure consciousness, सर्व-प्रत्यय-हेतवे to the source of all forms of knowledge/perception, पुरुष-ईश-प्रधानाय to the Lord of the individual soul (purusha) and of material nature (pradhana), ब्रह्मणे to the Absolute Truth (Brahman), अनन्त-शक्तये to the possessor of infinite potencies

Anvaya: विज्ञानमात्राय सर्वप्रत्ययहेतवे पुरुषेशप्रधानाय अनन्तशक्तये ब्रह्मणे नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विज्ञानमात्राय To the one who is pure consciousness alone, सर्वप्रत्ययहेतवे the cause of all perception, पुरुषेशप्रधानाय the master of both the individual soul and material nature, अनन्तशक्तये who possesses unlimited power, ब्रह्मणे to the Supreme Brahman, नमः obeisances.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances to the Supreme Absolute Truth (Brahman), who is the embodiment of pure consciousness and the source of all perception. He is the master of both the individual soul and material nature, and He possesses infinite, unlimited potencies.

नमस्ते वासुदेवाय सर्वभूतक्षयाय च ।

हृषीकेश नमस्तुभ्यं प्रपन्नं पाहि मां प्रभो ॥10.40.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमस्ते obeisances unto You, वासुदेवाय to the son of Vasudeva, सर्व-भूत-क्षयाय to the abode of all living beings, च and, हृषीकेश O Lord of the senses, नमः obeisances, तुभ्यम् unto You, प्रपन्नम् who am surrendered, पाहि protect, माम् me, प्रभो O Lord

क्षय   क्षयः [क्षि-अच्] 1 A house, residence, abode;  src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

—2 Loss, decline, waste, wane, decay, diminution;  src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

—3 Destruction, end, termination;  src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: वासुदेवाय सर्वभूतक्षयाय च नमस्ते। हृषीकेश! तुभ्यं नमः, प्रभो! प्रपन्नं मां पाहि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वासुदेवाय To Vasudeva, सर्वभूतक्षयाय the shelter of all beings, च and, नमस्ते obeisances unto You. हृषीकेश O Master of the senses, तुभ्यं नमः obeisances to You, प्रभो O Lord, प्रपन्नं मां me who has surrendered, पाहि protect.

Simple Meaning: I offer my obeisances unto You, Lord Vasudeva, the ultimate abode and shelter of all living beings. O Hrishikesha, Master of the senses, I offer my bows to You. O Lord, I have surrendered unto You; please protect me.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “अक्रूरस्तुतिर्नाम” चत्वारिंशः अध्यायः ॥40॥

Thus ends the fortieth discourse named “Akrura's Eulogy (of the Lord)” in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
