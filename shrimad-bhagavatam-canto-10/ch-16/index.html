The deliverance of Kaliya

श्री शुक उवाच

विलोक्य दूषितां कृष्णां कृष्णः कृष्णाहिना विभुः ।

तस्या विशुद्धिमन्विच्छन् सर्पं तमुदवासयत् ॥10.16.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Sri Shuka said, विलोक्य observing, दूषिताम् contaminated, कृष्णाम् the river Yamuna, कृष्णः Lord Krishna, कृष्ण-अहिना by the black serpent Kaliya, विभुः the Almighty Lord, तस्याः her (the river's), विशुद्धिम् purification, अन्विच्छन् desiring, सर्पम् serpent, तम् that, उदवासयत् expelled/drove away

Anvaya: विभुः कृष्णः कृष्णाहिना दूषितां कृष्णां विलोक्य तस्याः विशुद्धिम् अन्विच्छन् तं सर्पम् उदवासयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विभुः कृष्णः The all-powerful Lord Krishna, कृष्णाहिना दूषितां contaminated by the black serpent, कृष्णां विलोक्य seeing the Yamuna river, तस्याः विशुद्धिम् अन्विच्छन् desiring her purification, तं सर्पम् उदवासयत् expelled that serpent.

Simple meaning: Sri Shuka said: The almighty Lord Krishna, seeing that the river Yamuna had been polluted by the black serpent Kaliya, desired to purify her and therefore made up His mind to drive the serpent out.

राजा उवाच

कथमन्तर्जलेऽगाधे न्यगृह्णाद् भगवानहिम् ।

स वै बहुयुगावासं यथाऽऽसीद् विप्र कथ्यताम् ॥10.16.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: राजा उवाच the King said, कथम् how, अन्तर्-जले within the water, अगाधे deep/unfathomable, न्यगृह्णात् subdued/arrested, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अहिम् the serpent, सः he (the serpent), वै indeed, बहु-युग-आवासम् residing for many ages, यथा how, आसीत् was, विप्र O learned Brahmana, कथ्यताम् please tell

Anvaya: विप्र! भगवान् अगाधे अन्तर्जले अहिं कथं न्यगृह्णात्? सः (अहिः) यथा बहुयुगावासः आसीत् (तत्) वै कथ्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विप्र O learned one! भगवान् the Lord, अगाधे अन्तर्जले in the unfathomable deep water, अहिं the serpent, कथं न्यगृह्णात् how subdued? सः that serpent, यथा how, बहुयुगावासः resident for many ages, आसीत् was, कथ्यताम् please be told.

Simple meaning: King Parikshit asked: O learned Brahmana, how did the Supreme Lord subdue that serpent within the unfathomable depths of the water? Please tell me how that serpent had come to make that lake his home for so many ages.

ब्रह्मन् भगवतस्तस्य भूम्नः स्वच्छन्दवर्तिनः ।

गोपालोदारचरितं कस्तृप्येतामृतं जुषन् ॥10.16.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: ब्रह्मन् O Brahmana (Shuka), भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, तस्य of Him, भूम्नः of the Unlimited/Almighty, स्वच्छन्द-वर्तिनः who acts according to His own independent will, गोपाल-उदार-चरितम् the noble activities as a cowherd boy, कः who, तृप्येत could be satiated/satisfied, अमृतम् nectar, जुषन् partaking of/relishing

Anvaya: ब्रह्मन्! स्वच्छन्दवर्तिनः भूम्नः तस्य भगवतः अमृतं गोपालोदारचरितं जुषन् कः तृप्येत?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ब्रह्मन् O Brahmana! स्वच्छन्दवर्तिनः Of the independent, भूम्नः of the infinite, तस्य भगवतः of that Supreme Lord, अमृतं nectar-like, गोपालोदारचरितं noble cowherd pastimes, जुषन् relishing, कः तृप्येत who could be satiated?

Simple meaning: O Brahmana, who could ever feel satiated while relishing the nectar of the noble pastimes performed by the unlimited Supreme Lord, who acts according to His own sweet will and appeared as a simple cowherd boy?

श्री शुक उवाच

कालिन्द्यां कालियस्यासीध्रदः कश्चिद् विषाग्निना ।

श्रप्यमाणपया यस्मिन् पतन्त्युपरिगाः खगाः ॥10.16.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Sri Shuka said, कालिन्द्याम् in the Yamuna, कालियस्य of Kaliya, आसीत् there was, ह्रदः a lake, कश्चिद् a certain, विष-अग्निना by the fire of poison, श्रप्यमाण-पयाः whose water was boiling/cooking, यस्मिन् in which, पतन्ति fall down, उपरि-गाः traveling above, खगाः birds

Anvaya: कालिन्द्यां कश्चिद् ह्रदः आसीत् यस्मिन् कालियस्य विषाग्निना श्रप्यमाणपयाः (सन्) उपरिगाः खगाः पतन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कालिन्द्यां In the Yamuna, कश्चिद् ह्रदः आसीत् there was a certain lake, यस्मिन् in which, कालियस्य विषाग्निना by the fire of poison of Kaliya, श्रप्यमाणपयाः the water being boiled, उपरिगाः खगाः the birds flying above, पतन्ति fall down.

Simple meaning: Sri Shuka said: Within the river Yamuna, there was a pool inhabited by the serpent Kaliya. The water of this pool was constantly boiling from the intense heat of the serpent's fire-like poison, causing any birds flying overhead to fall dead into it.

विप्रुष्मता विषोदोर्मिमारुतेनाभिमर्शिताः ।

म्रियन्ते तीरगा यस्य प्राणिनः स्थिरजङ्गमाः ॥10.16.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: विप्रुष्मता carrying droplets of water, विष-उद-ऊर्मि-मारुतेन by the wind blowing over the waves of poisonous water, अभिमर्शिताः touched/affected, म्रियन्ते die, तीरगाः staying on the shore, यस्य of which (lake), प्राणिनः living entities, स्थिर-जङ्गमाः stationary (plants) and moving (animals)

Anvaya: यस्य विप्रुष्मता विषोदोर्मिमारुतेन अभिमर्शिताः तीरगाः स्थिरजङ्गमाः प्राणिनः म्रियन्ते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यस्य Of which (lake), विप्रुष्मता carrying droplets, विषोदोर्मिमारुतेन by the wind from the poisonous waves, अभिमर्शिताः touched, तीरगाः those on the shore, स्थिरजङ्गमाः stationary and moving, प्राणिनः living beings, म्रियन्ते die.

Simple meaning: The wind blowing over the waves of that lake carried poisonous droplets; merely by being touched by that toxic breeze, all living beings on the shore—whether moving animals or stationary plants and trees—would perish.

तं चण्डवेगविषवीर्यमवेक्ष्य तेन दुष्टां नदीं च खलसंयमनावतारः ।

कृष्णः कदम्बमधिरुह्य ततोऽतितुङ्गमास्फोट्य गाढरशनो न्यपतद् विषोदे ॥10.16.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् that (lake), चण्ड-वेग-विष-वीर्यम् having terribly forceful poisonous power, अवेक्ष्य observing, तेन by that (serpent), दुष्टाम् contaminated, नदीम् the river, च and, खल-संयमन-अवतारः whose incarnation is for the restraint of the wicked, कृष्णः Lord Krishna, कदम्बम् a Kadamba tree, अधिरुह्य having climbed, ततः from there, अति-तुङ्गम् very high, आस्फोट्य slapping His arms (in a challenge), गाढ-रशनः tightly tying His belt, न्यपतत् He jumped/dived, विष-उदे into the poisonous water

Anvaya: खलसंयमनावतारः कृष्णः तं चण्डवेगविषवीर्यं तेन दुष्टां नदीं च अवेक्ष्य ततः अतितुङ्गं कदम्बम् अधिरुह्य गाढरशनः (सन्) आस्फोट्य विषोदे न्यपतत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: खलसंयमनावतारः कृष्णः He whose incarnation is to curb the wicked, Krishna, तं चण्डवेगविषवीर्यं that (lake) of fierce poisonous power, तेन by him (the serpent), दुष्टां contaminated, नदीं च and the river, अवेक्ष्य seeing, ततः then, अतितुङ्गं कदम्बम् very high Kadamba tree, अधिरुह्य having climbed, गाढरशनः with belt tightly tied, आस्फोट्य slapping His arms, विषोदे न्यपतत् jumped into the poisonous water.

Simple meaning: Observing the fierce poisonous power of the lake and how the river had been ruined by the serpent, Lord Krishna—who had descended specifically to subdue the wicked—tightened His belt, climbed to the top of a very high Kadamba tree, slapped His arms like a wrestler, and dove headlong into the poisonous water.

सर्पह्रदः पुरुषसारनिपातवेगसंक्षोभितोरगविषोच्छ्वसिताम्बुराशिः ।

पर्यक्प्लुतो विषकषायविभीषणोर्मिर्धावन् धनुः शतमनन्तबलस्य किं तत् ॥10.16.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: सर्प-ह्रदः the serpent's lake, पुरुष-सार-निपात-वेग-संक्षोभित-उरग-विष-उच्छ्वसित-अम्बु-राशिः the mass of water agitated by the force of the falling of the Supreme Person and by the heavy breathing of the poisonous snakes, पर्यक्-प्लुतः overflowing on all sides, विष-कषाय-विभीषण-ऊमिः with fearsome waves foul with poison, धावन् running/extending, धनुः शतम् a hundred bow-lengths, अनन्त-बलस्य for Him of infinite strength, किम् what, तत् that

Anvaya: पुरुषसारनिपातवेगसंक्षोभितोरगविषोच्छ्वसिताम्बुराशिः विषकषायविभीषणोर्मिः सर्पह्रदः पर्यक्प्लुतः (सन्) धनुः शतं धावन् (आसीत्); अनन्तबलस्य (कृष्णस्य) तत् किम्?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पुरुषसारनिपातवेगसंक्षोभितोरगविषोच्छ्वसिताम्बुराशिः The mass of water agitated by the forceful fall of the Supreme Person and the poisonous breathing of the snakes, विषकषायविभीषणोर्मिः having fearsome waves contaminated with poison, सर्पह्रदः the serpent's lake, पर्यक्प्लुतः overflowing everywhere, धनुः शतं धावन् extending for four hundred cubits (a hundred bows), अनन्तबलस्य for the one of infinite strength, तत् किम् what was that (task)?

Simple meaning: When the Supreme Person jumped in, the mass of water—already agitated by the snakes' toxic breathing—surged with fearsome, poison-filled waves and overflowed the banks for a distance of four hundred cubits. Yet, for Lord Krishna, who possesses infinite strength, such a feat was not at all difficult.

तस्य ह्रदे विहरतो भुजदण्डघूर्णवार्घोषमङ्ग वरवारणविक्रमस्य ।

आश्रुत्य तत् स्वसदनाभिभवं निरीक्ष्य चक्षुःश्रवाः समसरत्तदमृष्यमाणः ॥10.16.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य His (Krishna's), ह्रदे in the lake, विहरतः sporting/playing, भुज-दण्ड-घूर्ण-वारि-घोषम् the sound of the water stirred by His mighty staff-like arms, अङ्ग O dear one (Parikshit), वर-वारण-विक्रमस्य having the prowess of a mighty elephant, आश्रुत्य hearing, तत् that, स्व-सदन-अभिभवम् the insult/attack to his own home, निरीक्ष्य seeing/observing, चक्षुःश्रवाः the serpent (one who hears through eyes), समसरत् came forward/attacked, तत् that (insult), अमृष्यमाणः unable to tolerate

Anvaya: अङ्ग! वरवारणविक्रमस्य तस्य ह्रदे विहरतः भुजदण्डघूर्णवार्घोषम् आश्रुत्य स्वसदनाभिभवं तत् निरीक्ष्य चक्षुःश्रवाः (कालियः) अमृष्यमाणः (सन्) समसरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग O dear one! वरवारणविक्रमस्य Of one with the prowess of a great elephant, तस्य of Him, ह्रदे विहरतः playing in the lake, भुजदण्डघूर्णवार्घोषम् the sound of water swirled by staff-like arms, आश्रुत्य hearing, स्वसदनाभिभवं the invasion of his home, तत् निरीक्ष्य seeing that, चक्षुःश्रवाः the serpent, अमृष्यमाणः not being able to tolerate, समसरत् came forward.

Simple meaning: O King, as Krishna moved in the water with the power of a mighty elephant, His staff-like arms stirred up a deafening roar. Hearing that sound and seeing his home being thus invaded, the serpent Kaliya—who could not tolerate such an insult—came forward to attack.

तं प्रेक्षणीयसुकुमारघनावदातं श्रीवत्सपीतवसनं स्मितसुन्दरास्यम् ।

क्रीडन्तमप्रतिभयं कमलोदराङ्घ्रिं सन्दश्य मर्मसु रुषा भुजया चछाद ॥10.16.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him (Krishna), प्रेक्षणीय-सुकुमार-घन-अवदातम् beautiful to behold, delicate, and dark-hued like a cloud, श्रीवत्स-पीत-वसनम् marked with Shrivatsa and wearing yellow garments, स्मित-सुन्दर-आस्यम् with a face made beautiful by a smile, क्रीडन्तम् playing, अप्रतिभयम् without fear, कमल-उदर-अङ्घ्रिम् whose feet are like the interior of a lotus, सन्दश्य biting, मर्मसु in the vital parts, रुषा with anger, भुजया with his coils (body), चछाद he enveloped/covered

Anvaya: प्रेक्षणीयसुकुमारघनावदातं श्रीवत्सपीतवसनं स्मितसुन्दरास्यम् अप्रतिभयं क्रीडन्तं कमलोदराङ्घ्रिं तं रुषा मर्मसु सन्दश्य (सः) भुजया चछाद।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रेक्षणीयसुकुमारघनावदातं Beautiful, delicate, and dark like a cloud, श्रीवत्सपीतवसनं having the Shrivatsa mark and yellow clothes, स्मितसुन्दरास्यम् with a beautiful smiling face, अप्रतिभयं fearless, क्रीडन्तं playing, कमलोदराङ्घ्रिं with feet tender like the insides of a lotus, तं Him, रुषा with rage, मर्मसु in vital points, सन्दश्य biting, भुजया with his body, चछाद covered/encircled.

Simple meaning: Seeing Lord Krishna—who looked exquisitely beautiful and delicate with His dark cloud-like complexion, His yellow silk garments, and a lovely smile on His face—playing fearlessly in the water, the enraged serpent bit Him in His vital parts and completely enveloped Him within his massive coils.

तं नागभोगपरिवीतमदृष्टचेष्टमालोक्य तत्प्रियसखाः पशुपा भृशार्ताः ।

कृष्णेऽर्पितात्मसुहृदर्थकलत्रकामा दुःखानुशोकभयमूढधियो निपेतुः ॥10.16.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him (Krishna), नाग-भोग-परिवीतम् encircled by the coils of the serpent, अदृष्ट-चेष्टम् whose movement was not seen (motionless), आलोक्य seeing, तत्-प्रिय-सखाः His dear friends, पशु-पाः the cowherd boys, भृश-आर्ताः greatly distressed, कृष्णे unto Krishna, अर्पित-आत्म-सुहृद-अर्थ-कलत्र-कामाः who had dedicated their very selves, friends, wealth, wives, and desires, दुःख-अनुशोक-भय-मूढ-धियः their minds bewildered by sorrow, grief, and fear, निपेतुः fell down

Anvaya: नागभोगपरिवीतम् अदृष्टचेष्टं तम् आलोक्य कृष्णे अर्पितात्मसुहृदर्थकलत्रकामाः तत्प्रियसखाः पशुपाः भृशार्ताः दुःखानुशोकभयमूढधियः (सन्तः) निपेतुः ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नागभोगपरिवीतम् Encircled by the serpent's body, अदृष्टचेष्टं तम् आलोक्य seeing Him motionless, कृष्णे अर्पितात्मसुहृदर्थकलत्रकामाः those who had offered their souls, friends, riches, wives, and desires to Krishna, तत्प्रियसखाः His dear friends, पशुपाः the cowherds, भृशार्ताः deeply pained, दुःखानुशोकभयमूढधियः with intelligence bewildered by agony, lamentation, and fear, निपेतुः fell to the ground.

Simple meaning: Seeing Krishna motionless and trapped in the serpent's coils, His dear cowherd friends—who had dedicated their lives, families, and every possession to Him—were overcome with intense pain. Their minds became completely paralyzed by grief and terror, and they collapsed to the ground.

गावो वृषा वत्सतर्यः क्रन्दमानाः सुदुःखिताः।

कृष्णे न्यस्तेक्षणा भीता रुदत्य इव तस्थिरे ॥10.16.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: गावः the cows, वृषाः the bulls, वत्सतर्यः the female cows that have not borne a calf yet, क्रन्दमानाः lowing piteously, सु-दुःखिताः greatly distressed, कृष्णे on Krishna, न्यस्त-ईक्षणाः having fixed their vision, भीताः afraid, रुदत्यः weeping, इव as if, तस्थिरे stood

Anvaya: गावः वृषाः वत्सतर्यः च सुदुःखिताः क्रन्दमानाः भीताः कृष्णे न्यस्तेक्षणाः रुदत्यः इव तस्थिरे।

Simple meaning: Seeing His people of Gokula—the women, children, and elders—so utterly distressed on His account and having no other refuge but Him, Lord Krishna decided to end their suffering. Imitating the behavior of a mortal for a short while by remaining trapped, He suddenly rose up, breaking free from the serpent's constricting coils.

Simple meaning: As Krishna expanded His body, the serpent’s coils were stretched to the point of intense pain, forcing him to release the Lord. Enraged, the serpent raised his many hoods and stood hissing, his nostrils steaming like vessels of poison and his eyes staring unblinkingly like burning coals as he glared at Krishna.

तं जिह्वया द्विशिखया परिलेलिहानं द्वे सृक्किणी ह्यतिकरालविषाग्निदृष्टिम् ।

Simple meaning: The serpent licked the corners of his mouth with his forked tongue, his eyes burning with a terrifying, poisonous fire. Krishna began to move playfully around him, just as Garuda circles a snake. Meanwhile, the serpent also kept circling, waiting for the perfect moment to strike.

एवं परिभ्रमहतौजसमुन्नतांसमानम्य तत्पृथुशिरः स्वधिरूढ आद्यः ।

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, परिभ्रम-हत-ओजसम् whose strength was exhausted by circling, उन्नत-अंसम् whose shoulders were raised, आनम्य bending down, तत्-पृथु-शिरः those broad heads, स्व-अधिरूढः climbed upon, आद्यः the original Lord, तत्-मूर्ध-रत्न-निकर-स्पर्श-अति-ताम्र-पाद-अम्बुजः whose lotus feet became very red from contact with the many jewels on those heads, अखिल-कला-आदि-गुरुः the original master of all arts, ननर्त danced

Anvaya: एवं परिभ्रमहतौजसम् उन्नतांसम् आनम्य तत्पृथुशिरः स्वधिरूढः अखिलकलादिगुरुः आद्यः तन्मूर्धरत्ननिकरस्पर्शातिताम्रपादाम्बुजः (सन्) ननर्त।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवम् In this way, परिभ्रमहतौजसम् (the serpent) whose strength was diminished by constant circling, उन्नतांसम् having his shoulders raised, आनम्य bending down, तत्पृथुशिरः those broad heads, स्वधिरूढः having climbed upon, अखिलकलादिगुरुः the original master of all arts, आद्यः the original Lord, तन्मूर्धरत्ननिकरस्पर्शातिताम्रपादाम्बुजः his lotus feet becoming very red by the touch of the collection of jewels on those heads, ननर्त danced.

Simple meaning: In this way, the original Lord, the master of all arts, waited until the serpent was exhausted from circling. He then forced down the serpent's raised heads and climbed upon them. As He began to dance, His lotus feet took on a deep red glow from the brilliant jewels sparkling on the serpent’s many hoods.

तं नर्तुमुद्यतमवेक्ष्य तदा तदीयगन्धर्वसिद्धसुरचारणदेववध्वः ।

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him (Krishna), नर्तुम् to dance, उद्यतम् prepared, अवेक्ष्य seeing, तदा then, तदीय-गन्धर्व-सिद्ध-सुर-चारण-देव-वध्वः His (the Lord's) devotees among the Gandharvas, Siddhas, Suras, Caranas, demigods and the apsaras, प्रीत्या with affection, मृदङ्ग-पणव-आनक-वाद्य-गीत-पुष्प-उपहार-नुतिभिः with the playing of drums (mrdangas, panavas, and anakas), song, offerings of flowers, and prayers, सहसा immediately, उपसेदुः arrived

Anvaya: तदा तं नर्तुम् उद्यतम् अवेक्ष्य तदीयगन्धर्वसिद्धसुरचारणदेववध्वः प्रीत्या मृदङ्गपणवानकवाद्यगीतपुष्पोपहारनुतिभिः सहसा उपसेदुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तदा Then, तं Him, नर्तुम् उद्यतम् अवेक्ष्य seeing Him prepared to dance, तदीयगन्धर्वसिद्धसुरचारणदेववध्वः the Gandharvas, Siddhas, Suras, Caranas, demigods and the apsaras, प्रीत्या with affection, मृदङ्गपणवानकवाद्यगीतपुष्पोपहारनुतिभिः with the music of mrdanga, panava, and anaka drums, and with songs, flower offerings, and prayers, सहसा उपसेदुः immediately approached (Him).

Simple meaning: Seeing that the Lord was preparing to dance, His devotees—the Gandharvas, Siddhas, Suras, Caranas, demigods and the apsaras —immediately arrived. Filled with love and joy, they began to accompany Him by playing various drums like the mrdanga, panava, and anaka, while singing, offering flowers, and reciting prayers.

यद् यच्छिरो न नमतेऽङ्ग शतैकशीर्ष्णस्तत्तन् ममर्द खरदण्डधरोऽङ्घ्रिपातैः ।

Anvaya: अङ्ग! खरदण्डधरः शतैकशीर्ष्णः यत् यत् शिरः न नमते तत् तत् अङ्घ्रिपातैः ममर्द। क्षीणायुषः भ्रमतः नागः आस्यतः नस्तः च उल्बणम् असृङ् वमन् परमकश्मलम् आप।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग O King, खरदण्डधरः He who carries a harsh rod of punishment, शतैकशीर्ष्णः of the one-hundred-headed (serpent), यत् यत् शिरः whichever head, न नमते did not bow, तत् तत् each of those, अङ्घ्रिपातैः with the striking of His feet, ममर्द He crushed. क्षीणायुषः His life span being exhausted, भ्रमतः नागः the reeling serpent, आस्यतः नस्तः च from his mouths and from his nostrils, उल्बणम् profusely, असृङ् वमन् vomiting blood, परमकश्मलम् आप attained extreme agony.

Simple meaning: O King! Krishna, acting as the supreme punisher, used His feet to crush whichever of Kaliya's one hundred heads refused to bow down. With his life force nearly spent and his body reeling from the impact, the serpent began to vomit blood profusely from his mouths and nostrils, eventually falling into a state of extreme agony and unconsciousness.

तस्याक्षिभिर्गरलमुद्वमतः शिरस्सु यद् यत् समुन्न्मति निःश्वसतो रुषोच्चैः ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: रुषा With anger, उच्चैः निःश्वसतः of him who was breathing heavily, अक्षिभिः from the eyes, गरलम् उद्वमतः vomiting poison, तस्य his, शिरस्सु among the heads, यद् यत् समुन्नमति whichever rose up, नृत्यन् dancing, पुराणः पुमान् the Original Personality, पदा अनुनमयन् bending it down with His foot, इह here, पुष्पैः प्रपूजितः इव as if worshipped with flowers, दमयाम्बभूव He subdued.

Simple meaning: As Kaliya breathed heavily in fury and spat poison from his eyes, whichever of his heads he tried to raise, the Original Personality, Lord Krishna, would simply force it down with His foot while dancing. To the onlookers, it appeared as if the Lord was being worshipped with flowers (the red blood on the serpent's heads) as He masterfully subdued the Great Snake.

तच्चित्रताण्डवविरुग्णफणातपत्रो रक्तं मुखैरुरु वमन् नृप भग्नगात्रः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, तत्चित्रताण्डवविरुग्णफणातपत्रः he whose umbrella-like hoods were broken by that amazing Tandava dance, मुखैः through the mouths, उरु profusely, रक्तं blood, वमन् vomiting, भग्नगात्रः having his body broken, चराचरगुरुं the guru of all moving and non-moving entities, पुराणं पुरुषं the ancient Supreme Person, तम् अरणं that shelter, नारायणं स्मृत्वा Lord Narayana, remembering, मनसा जगाम in the mind, he approached/surrendered.

Simple meaning: O King! With his umbrella-like hoods shattered by Krishna’s extraordinary dance and his body broken, Kaliya began to vomit blood profusely from his many mouths. Realizing his helplessness, he turned his mind toward the ancient Supreme Person, Lord Narayana—the master of all living and non-living beings—and surrendered to Him as his only shelter.

कृष्णस्य गर्भजगतोऽतिभरावसन्नं पार्ष्णिप्रहार परिरुग्णफणातपत्रम् ।

दृष्ट्वाहिमाद्यमुपसेदुरमुष्य पत्न्य आर्ताः श्लथद्वसनभूषणकेशबन्धाः ॥10.16.31॥

Anvaya: गर्भजगतः कृष्णस्य अतिभरावसन्नं पार्ष्णिप्रहारपरिरुग्णफणातपत्रम् अहिं दृष्ट्वा आर्ताः श्लथद्वसनभूषणकेशबन्धाः अमुष्य पत्न्यः आद्यम् उपसेदुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गर्भजगतः Of Him who carries the universe in His abdomen, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, अतिभरावसन्नं overcome by the extreme weight, पार्ष्णिप्रहारपरिरुग्णफणातपत्रम् whose umbrella-like hoods were crushed by the striking of the heels, अहिं दृष्ट्वा seeing the serpent, आर्ताः distressed, श्लथद्वसनभूषणकेशबन्धाः their clothes, ornaments, and hair-ties becoming loosened, अमुष्य पत्न्यः his (the serpent's) wives, आद्यम् उपसेदुः approached the Original Lord.

Simple meaning: When the wives of the Kaliya serpent saw that their husband was being crushed by the massive weight of Lord Krishna—who carries the entire universe within Himself—and that his umbrella-like hoods were shattered by the strikes of the Lord’s heels, they became deeply distressed. With their clothing, jewelry, and hair falling into disarray out of anxiety, they approached the Supreme Lord to plead for their husband's life.

तास्तं सुविग्नमनसोऽथ पुरस्कृतार्भाः कायं निधाय भुवि भूतपतिं प्रणेमुः ।

Anvaya: अथ सुविग्नमनसः ताः साध्व्यः पुरस्कृतार्भाः कायं भुवि निधाय भूतपतिं प्रणेमुः। शमलस्य भर्तुः मोक्षेप्सवः कृताञ्जलिपुटाः (ताः) शरणदं शरणं प्रपन्नाः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, सुविग्नमनसः ताः those with greatly agitated minds, साध्व्यः chaste ladies, पुरस्कृतार्भाः having placed their children in front, कायं their bodies, भुवि निधाय on the ground, having laid, भूतपतिं प्रणेमुः unto the Lord of all creatures, bowed down. शमलस्य भर्तुः Of the sinful husband, मोक्षेप्सवः desiring the release, कृताञ्जलिपुटाः with joined palms, शरणदं the giver of shelter, शरणं shelter, प्रपन्नाः they took.

Simple meaning: Then, with their minds deeply agitated, those chaste ladies placed their children before them and prostrated themselves on the ground before Lord Krishna, the Master of all living beings. Desiring the release of their sinful husband, they joined their palms in prayer and took full shelter of the Lord, who is the only true giver of refuge.

नागपत्न्यः ऊचुः

न्याय्यो हि दण्डः कृतकिल्बिषेऽस्मिंस्तवावतारः खलनिग्रहाय ।

रिपोः सुतानामपि तुल्यदृष्टेर्धत्से दमं फलमेवानुशंसन् ॥10.16.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: नाग-पत्न्यः ऊचुः the wives of the serpent said, न्याय्यः just/fitting, हि indeed, दण्डः punishment, कृत-किल्बिषे who has committed sins, अस्मिन् in this (Kaliya), तव Your, अवतारः incarnation, खल-निग्रहाय for the subduing of the envious/wicked, रिपोः of an enemy, सुतानाम् of sons, अपि also, तुल्य-दृष्टेः of You who have equal vision, धत्से You bestow, दमम् punishment, फलम् the result (correction), एव certainly, अनुशंसन् considering

Anvaya: नागपत्न्यः ऊचुः — कृतकिल्बिषे अस्मिन् दण्डः न्याय्यः हि। तव अवतारः खलनिग्रहाय (अस्ति)। रिपोः सुतानाम् अपि तुल्यदृष्टेः (तव) फलम् एव अनुशंसन् दमं धत्से।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नागपत्न्यः ऊचुः The wives of the serpent said, कृतकिल्बिषे अस्मिन् toward this one who has committed sins, दण्डः न्याय्यः हि punishment is just, indeed. तव अवतारः Your incarnation, खलनिग्रहाय for the subduing of the wicked. रिपोः Of an enemy, सुतानाम् अपि even, of sons, तुल्यदृष्टेः of You who possess equal vision, फलम् एव अनुशंसन् the ultimate benefit, only, intending/considering, दमं धत्से You award punishment.

Simple meaning: The wives of Kaliya said: This punishment of our husband is certainly just, for he has committed great sins. Indeed, Your very incarnation is for the purpose of subduing the wicked. Since You look upon enemies and Your own sons with an equal eye, whenever You award punishment, You do so only considering the ultimate benefit of the living being.

अनुग्रहोऽयं भवतः कृतो हि नो दण्डोऽसतां ते खलु कल्मषापहः ।

यद् दन्दशूकत्वममुष्य देहिनः क्रोधोऽपि तेऽनुग्रह एव सम्मतः ॥10.16.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: अनुग्रहः favor/mercy, अयम् this, भवतः by You, कृतः done, हि indeed, नः to us, दण्डः punishment, असताम् of the wicked, ते Your, खलु certainly, कल्मष-अपहः that which removes sins, यद् because, दन्दशूकत्वम् the state of being a serpent (envious by nature), अमुष्य of this, देहिनः embodied living being, क्रोधः anger, अपि even, ते Your, अनुग्रहः mercy, एव indeed, सम्मतः considered

Anvaya: भवतः नः अयम् अनुग्रहः कृतः हि। असतां ते दण्डः खलु कल्मषापहः। अमुष्य देहिनः यद् दन्दशूकत्वम् (तस्मात्) ते क्रोधः अपि अनुग्रहः एव सम्मतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भवतः By You, नः toward us, अयम् this (punishment), अनुग्रहः mercy, कृतः performed, हि indeed. असतां Of the wicked, ते दण्डः You (giving) punishment, खलु certainly, कल्मषापहः (is) the destroyer of sins. अमुष्य देहिनः Of this embodied being (the serpent), यद् since, दन्दशूकत्वम् the condition of a serpent, ते क्रोधः अपि Even Your anger, अनुग्रहः एव सम्मतः is regarded as mercy only.

Simple meaning: What You have done is actually a great favor to us. The punishment You award to the wicked is certainly meant to cleanse them of their sins. Since this embodied soul has taken the form of a serpent—a creature naturally filled with envy—even Your anger toward him is considered to be Your causal mercy.

तपः सुतप्तं किमनेन पूर्वं निरस्तमानेन च मानदेन ।

धर्मोऽथ वा सर्वजनानुकम्पया यतो भवांस्तुष्यति सर्वजीवः ॥10.16.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: तपः austerity, सु-तप्तम् well-performed, किम् whether, अनेन by him (Kaliya), पूर्वम् in a previous life, निरस्त-मानेन without pride, च and, मान-देन giving respect to others, धर्मः religious duties, अथवा or, सर्व-जन-अनुकम्पया with compassion for all living beings, यतः by which, भवान् You, तुष्यति are satisfied, सर्व-जीवः the life-force of all

Anvaya: पूर्वं निरस्तमानेन मानदेन अनेन च (सता) किं तपः सुतप्तम्? अथवा सर्वजनानुकम्पया धर्मः (कृतः), यतः सर्वजीवः भवान् तुष्यति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पूर्वं Previously, अनेन By him, निरस्तमानेन being free from pride, मानदेन च and being respectful to others, किं whether, तपः austerity, सुतप्तम् well-performed? अथवा Or, सर्वजनानुकम्पया with compassion for all people, धर्मः (performed) religious duty, यतः because of which, सर्वजीवः भवान् तुष्यति the soul of all beings, You, are pleased.

Simple meaning: What kind of great austerities must Kaliya have performed in his previous life? Was he free from pride and respectful to others, or did he perform religious duties with deep compassion for all living entities? We wonder this because You, the ultimate life-force of all that exists, are now so clearly pleased with him.

कस्यानुभावोऽस्य न देव विद्महे तवाङ्घ्रिरेणुस्पर्शाधिकारः।

यद्वाञ्छया श्रीर्ललनाऽऽचरत्तपो विहाय कामान् सुचिरं धृतव्रता ॥10.16.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: कस्य of what (pious work), अनुभावः the result, अस्य for this (Kaliya), न not, देव O Lord, विद्महे we know, तव-अङ्घ्रि-रेणु-स्पर्श-अधिकारः the qualification to touch the dust of Your lotus feet, यत्-वाञ्छया by the desire for which, श्रीः the Goddess of Fortune, ललना the best of women, आचरत् performed, तपः austerity, विहाय giving up, कामान् material desires, सु-चिरम् for a very long time, धृत-व्रता maintaining her vows

Anvaya: देव! अस्य तवाङ्घ्रिरेणुस्पर्शाधिकारः कस्य (पुण्यस्य) अनुभावः (इति) न विद्महे। यद्वाञ्छया ललना श्रीः कामान् विहाय सुचिरं धृतव्रता (सती) तपः आचरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देव O Lord!, अस्य For this one, तवाङ्घ्रिरेणुस्पर्शाधिकारः the right to touch the dust of Your feet, कस्य अनुभावः result of what, न विद्महे we do not know. यद्वाञ्छया Desiring which, ललना श्रीः the goddess Lakshmi, the foremost of women, कामान् विहाय abandoning all other desires, सुचिरं धृतव्रता for a very long time, being fixed in her vow, तपः आचरत् performed austerities.

Simple meaning: O Lord! We do not know what this Kaliya has done to deserve the privilege of having the dust of Your lotus feet touch his heads. Even the Goddess of Fortune, the best of all women, gave up all other desires and strictly observed severe austerities for a long time just to attain that very same dust.

न नाकपृष्ठं न च सार्वभौमं न पारमेष्ठ्यं न रसाधिपत्यम् ।

न योगसिद्धीरपुनर्भवं वा वाञ्छन्ति यत्पादरजः प्रपन्नाः ॥10.16.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, नाक-पृष्ठम् the heavenly planets, न not, च and, सार्वभौमम् sovereignty over the entire earth, न not, पारमेष्ठ्यम् the position of Lord Brahma, न not, रसा-आधिपत्यम् lordship over the subterranean worlds, न not, योग-सिद्धीः the mystic perfections of yoga, अपुनः-भवम् the state of no more birth (liberation), वा or, वाञ्छन्ति they desire, यत्-पाद-रजः the dust of whose lotus feet, प्रपन्नाः those who have surrendered

Anvaya: यत्पादरजः प्रपन्नाः नाकपृष्ठं न वाञ्छन्ति, न च सार्वभौमं, न पारमेष्ठ्यं, न रसाधिपत्यं, न योगसिद्धीः, अपुनर्भवं वा (न वाञ्छन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्पादरजः Of whose feet the dust, प्रपन्नाः those who have surrendered to, नाकपृष्ठं the heavenly kingdom, न वाञ्छन्ति do not desire, न च सार्वभौमं and neither (desire to) rule over the earth, न पारमेष्ठ्यं not the post of Brahma, न रसाधिपत्यं not dominion over the lower planets, न योगसिद्धीः not mystic powers, अपुनर्भवं वा or even the end of rebirth (liberation), न वाञ्छन्ति they do not desire.

Simple meaning: Those who have fully surrendered to the dust of Your lotus feet do not desire anything else—neither the heavenly planets, nor sovereignty over the earth, nor the position of Lord Brahma, nor lordship over the lower regions. They do not even seek the perfections of yoga or the state of liberation from birth and death.

तदेष नाथाप दुरापमन्यैस्तमोजनिः क्रोधवशोऽप्यहीशः ।

संसारचक्रे भ्रमतः शरीरिणो यदिच्छतः स्याद् विभवः समक्षः ॥10.16.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, एषः this, नाथ O Lord, आप has attained, दुरापम् difficult to attain, अन्यैः by others, तमः-जनिः born in the mode of ignorance, क्रोध-वशः under the control of anger, अपि although, अहीशः the king of serpents (Kaliya), संसार-चक्रे in the wheel of material existence, भ्रमतः for one who is wandering, शरीरिणः for an embodied living being, यत् that (dust), इच्छतः desiring, स्यात् becomes, विभवः all-opulence/attainment, समक्षः manifest before one's very eyes

Anvaya: नाथ! तमोजनिः क्रोधवशः अपि एषः अहीशः अन्यैः दुरापं तत् आप, यत् इच्छतः संसारचक्रे भ्रमतः शरीरिणः समक्षः विभवः स्यात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नाथ O Lord, तमोजनिः born of the mode of ignorance, क्रोधवशः under the sway of anger, अपि even though, एषः अहीशः this king of snakes, अन्यैः by others, दुरापं difficult to achieve, तत् आप that (mercy/dust of Your feet), has attained, यत् इच्छतः by the one desiring that mercy, संसारचक्रे भ्रमतः wandering in the cycle of birth and death, शरीरिणः for an embodied soul, समक्षः विभवः स्यात् all the opulences become manifest before the eyes.

Simple meaning: O Lord, although this king of serpents was born in the mode of ignorance and is habitually controlled by anger, he has attained Your mercy which is nearly impossible for others to achieve. For an embodied soul wandering through the cycle of birth and death, desiring Your mercy, all the opulences manifest before him.

नमस्तुभ्यं भगवते पुरुषाय महात्मने ।

भूतावासाय भूताय पराय परमात्मने ॥10.16.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, तुभ्यम् unto You, भगवते the Supreme Personality of Godhead, पुरुषाय the Supreme Person, महा-आत्मने the Great Soul, भूत-आवासाय the shelter of all living beings, भूताय who exists in all time (past, present, and future), पराय the Transcendental, परम-आत्मने the Supreme Soul

Anvaya: भगवते पुरुषाय महात्मने भूतावासाय भूताय पराय परमात्मने तुभ्यं नमः।

Simple meaning: We offer our respectful obeisances unto You, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. You are the Great Soul and the Supreme Person, the transcendental shelter of all living beings, and the eternal Supersoul who exists within and beyond everything in this universe.

ज्ञानविज्ञाननिधये ब्रह्मणेऽनन्तशक्तये ।

अगुणायाविकाराय नमस्तेऽप्राकृताय च ॥10.16.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: ज्ञान-विज्ञान-निधये unto the ocean of knowledge and realized knowledge, ब्रह्मणे unto the Absolute Truth, अन्-अन्त-शक्तये unto Him of unlimited potencies, अ-गुणाय who is without material qualities, अ-विकाराय who is without change, नमस्ते obeisances unto You, अ-प्राकृताय who is non-material/transcendental, च and

Simple meaning: We offer our obeisances unto You, who are the ocean of all knowledge and realization. You are the Supreme Absolute Truth, possessing unlimited potencies. Though You are the source of everything, You remain free from material qualities, unchanged by time, and entirely transcendental to this physical world.

कालाय कालनाभाय कालावयवसाक्षिणे ।

Anvaya: कालाय कालनाभाय कालावयवसाक्षिणे विश्वाय तदुपद्रष्ट्रे तत्कर्त्रे विश्वहेतवे (तुभ्यं नमः)।

Simple meaning: We bow down to You, who are Time itself and the central pivot around which time turns. You are the witness of every passing moment and season. You are the entire universe, yet also its creator, its supreme overseer, and the ultimate cause of all existence.

भूतमात्रेन्द्रियप्राणमनोबुद्ध्याशयात्मने ।

त्रिगुणेनाभिमानेन गूढस्वात्मानुभूतये ॥10.16.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: भूत-मात्र-इन्द्रिय-प्राण-मनः-बुद्धि-आशय-आत्मने unto Him who is the ultimate soul of the physical elements, the subtle objects of perception, the senses, the vital air, the mind, the intelligence, and the consciousness, त्रि-गुणेन by the three modes of material nature, अभिमानेन by the false ego, गूढ-स्व-आत्मा-अनुभूतये unto Him whose own spiritual perception is concealed

Anvaya: भूतमात्रेन्द्रियप्राणमनोबुद्ध्याशयात्मने त्रिगुणेन अभिमानेन गूढस्वात्मानुभूतये (तुभ्यं नमः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भूतमात्रेन्द्रियप्राणमनोबुद्ध्याशयात्मने Unto the Self of the elements, the objects of the senses, the senses, the life breath, the mind, the intellect, and the heart, त्रिगुणेन by the three modes, अभिमानेन by false ego, गूढस्वात्मानुभूतये unto Him whose personal realization is hidden, (unto You, our obeisances).

Simple meaning: We offer our obeisances to You, who are the ultimate soul of the physical elements, the subtle objects of perception, the senses, the vital air, the mind, the intelligence, and the consciousness. Though You are the source of everything, Your true identity remains hidden from those whose perception is covered by the three modes of nature and the influence of false ego.

नमोऽनन्ताय सूक्ष्माय कूटस्थाय विपश्चिते ।

नानावादानुरोधाय वाच्यवाचकशक्तये ॥10.16.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, अनन्ताय unto the infinite, सूक्ष्माय unto the subtle, कूटस्थाय unto the unchanging/fixed center, विपश्चिते unto the all-knowing/omniscient, नाना-वाद-अनुरोधाय who admits various philosophies and viewpoints, वाच्य-वाचक-शक्तये unto Him who possesses the powers of both the expressed meaning and the expressing word

Anvaya: अनन्ताय सूक्ष्माय कूटस्थाय विपश्चिते नानावादानुरोधाय वाच्यवाचकशक्तये (तुभ्यं) नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अनन्ताय Unto the unlimited, सूक्ष्माय unto the subtle, कूटस्थाय unto the immutable, विपश्चिते unto the omniscient, नानावादानुरोधाय who accommodates diverse theories, वाच्यवाचकशक्तये who is the power of the word and its meaning, (unto You, our obeisances).

Simple meaning: We offer our obeisances unto You, the infinite and subtle Absolute. You are the unchanging witness and the all-knowing Lord. You are the source of all diverse philosophical perspectives, and You possess the dual power of the word and the meaning it expresses.

नमः प्रमाणमूलाय कवये शास्त्रयोनये ।

प्रवृत्ताय निवृत्ताय निगमाय नमो नमः ॥10.16.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, प्रमाण-मूलाय unto the source of all authoritative evidence, कवये unto the enlightened one/the omniscient, शास्त्र-योनये unto the source of the revealed scriptures, प्रवृत्ताय unto the path of worldly action, निवृत्ताय unto the path of renunciation/cessation, निगमाय unto the source of the Vedas, नमः नमः repeated obeisances

Anvaya: प्रमाणमूलाय कवये शास्त्रयोनये नमः। प्रवृत्ताय निवृत्ताय निगमाय नमो नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रमाणमूलाय Unto the origin of all knowledge, कवये unto the wise, शास्त्रयोनये unto the source of all scriptures, नमः obeisances. प्रवृत्ताय Unto the path of action, निवृत्ताय unto the path of withdrawal, निगमाय unto the Vedic revelation, नमो नमः obeisances and again obeisances.

Simple meaning: We offer our respectful obeisances unto You, who are the ultimate source of all authoritative evidence and the original wise seer. You are the source of all sacred scriptures and the master of both the path of worldly engagement and the path of spiritual renunciation. We bow down again and again to You, the essence of all Vedic knowledge.

नमः कृष्णाय रामाय वसुदेवसुताय च ।

प्रद्युम्नायानिरुद्धाय सात्वतां पतये नमः ॥10.16.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, कृष्णाय unto Lord Krishna, रामाय unto Lord Rama (Balarama), वसुदेव-सुताय the son of Vasudeva, च and, प्रद्युम्नाय unto Pradyumna, अनिरुद्धाय unto Aniruddha, सात्वताम् of the Yadus (devotees), पतये unto the Lord/Master, नमः obeisances

Anvaya: कृष्णाय रामाय वसुदेवसुताय च नमः। प्रद्युम्नाय अनिरुद्धाय सात्वतां पतये नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णाय Unto Krishna, रामाय unto Rama, वसुदेवसुताय the son of Vasudeva, च and, नमः obeisances. प्रद्युम्नाय Unto Pradyumna, अनिरुद्धाय unto Aniruddha, सात्वतां of the Satvatas (Yadavas), पतये unto the Master, नमः obeisances.

Simple meaning: We offer our respectful obeisances unto Krishna and Balarama, the sons of Vasudeva. We also offer our obeisances unto Pradyumna and Aniruddha, and unto the Supreme Lord of all the devotees.

नमो गुणप्रदीपाय गुणात्मच्छादनाय च ।

गुणवृत्त्युपलक्ष्याय गुणद्रष्ट्रे स्वसंविदे ॥10.16.46॥

Word-by-word meaning: नमः obeisances, गुण-प्रदीपाय unto Him who makes the material modes manifest (the source of their light), गुण-आत्म-च्छादनाय unto Him who hides His own nature with those same modes, च and, गुण-वृत्ति-उपलक्ष्याय who is perceived through the functions of the modes (as their witness), गुण-द्रष्ट्रे to the observer of the modes, स्व-संविदे to Him who is self-cognizant (knowledge itself)

Anvaya: गुणप्रदीपाय गुणात्मच्छादनाय च नमः। गुणवृत्त्युपलक्ष्याय गुणद्रष्ट्रे स्वसंविदे (तुभ्यं) नमः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गुणप्रदीपाय Unto the one who illumines the material qualities, गुणात्मच्छादनाय who covers His own self by the qualities, च and, नमः obeisances. गुणवृत्त्युपलक्ष्याय Unto Him who is discernible through the activities of the modes, गुणद्रष्ट्रे unto the witness of the modes, स्वसंविदे unto the one who is self-luminous knowledge, (unto You, our obeisances).

Simple meaning: We offer our obeisances to You, who provide the light by which the material modes of nature function, yet who also use those very modes to veil Your true identity from the world. You are the silent observer of all material activities and can be known only through the functions of those modes. We bow to You, who are self-luminous and the very essence of pure consciousness.

अव्याकृतविहाराय सर्वव्याकृतसिद्धये ।

हृषीकेश नमस्तेऽस्तु मुनये मौनशीलिने ॥10.16.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: अव्याकृत-विहाराय unto Him whose pastimes are beyond the manifest material world (unmanifest/transcendental), सर्व-व्याकृत-सिद्धये unto the source of perfection for all manifest things, हृषीकेश O Master of the senses, नमस्ते obeisances unto You, अस्तु let there be, मुनये unto the great Sage, मौन-शीलिने who is absorbed in silent meditation (as the witness)

Anvaya: अव्याकृतविहाराय सर्वव्याकृतसिद्धये हृषीकेश मौनशीलिने मुनये (तुभ्यं) नमस्ते अस्तु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अव्याकृतविहाराय Unto Him whose play is in the unmanifest, सर्वव्याकृतसिद्धये unto the source of all manifest accomplishments, हृषीकेश O Lord of the senses, मौनशीलिने who is inherently silent, मुनये unto the Sage, नमस्ते obeisances, अस्तु let there be.

Simple meaning: O Hrishikesha, Lord of the senses, we offer our obeisances unto You. Your pastimes are transcendental to the material world, yet You are the ultimate cause of all that is manifest. We bow to You, the great Sage, who remains eternally silent as the internal witness of all.

परावरगतिज्ञाय सर्वाध्यक्षाय ते नमः ।

अविश्वाय च विश्वाय तद्द्रष्ट्रेऽस्य च हेतवे ॥10.16.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: पर-अवर-गति-ज्ञाय unto Him who knows the destination (or movements) of all things high and low, सर्व-अध्यक्षाय unto the supreme overseer of all, ते unto You, नमः obeisances, अविश्वाय who is distinct from the universe (transcendental), च and, विश्वाय who is the universe (the immanent cause), तद्-द्रष्ट्रे the witness of it, अस्य of this (universe), च and, हेतवे the ultimate cause

Anvaya: परावरगतिज्ञाय सर्वाध्यक्षाय ते नमः। अविश्वाय विश्वाय च तद्द्रष्ट्रे अस्य हेतवे च (तुभ्यं नमः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: परावरगतिज्ञाय Unto the knower of the destination of both the superior and the inferior, सर्वाध्यक्षाय unto the supervisor of all, ते unto You, नमः obeisances. अविश्वाय Unto the one who is not the universe, विश्वाय unto the universe, च and, तद्द्रष्ट्रे unto the witness of that, अस्य of this, हेतवे unto the cause, च and, (unto You, our obeisances).

Simple meaning: We offer our obeisances unto You, the Supreme Overseer who knows the path and destiny of every being, whether high or low. Though You are the very essence of this universe and its ultimate cause, You remain distinct from it as its eternal witness.

त्वं ह्यस्य जन्मस्थितिसंयमान् प्रभो गुणैरनीहोऽकृत कालशक्तिधृक् ।

तत्तत्स्वभावान् प्रतिबोधयन् सतः समीक्षयामोघविहार ईहसे ॥10.16.49॥

Word-by-word meaning: त्वम् You, हि indeed, अस्य of this (universe), जन्म-स्थिति-संयमान् creation, maintenance, and destruction, प्रभो O Lord, गुणैः by the modes of nature, अनीहः desireless/unaffected, अकृत have performed, काल-शक्ति-धृक् the wielder of the power of time, तत्-तत्-स्वभावान् the respective natures (of the living beings), प्रतिबोधयन् awakening, सतः who are existing, समीक्षया by Your glance, अ-मोघ-विहारः whose play is never-failing, ईहसे You act

Anvaya: प्रभो! कालशक्तिधृक् अनीहः त्वं हि गुणैः अस्य जन्मस्थितिसंयमान् अकृत। अमोघविहारः (त्वं) समीक्षया सतः तत्तत्स्वभावान् प्रतिबोधयन् ईहसे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रभो O Lord!, कालशक्तिधृक् Wielder of the power of time, अनीहः being without personal desire, त्वं हि You indeed, गुणैः through the modes of nature, अस्य of this (world), जन्मस्थितिसंयमान् creation, maintenance, and destruction, अकृत have done. अमोघविहारः You whose pastimes are infallible, समीक्षया by Your glance, सतः of those existing, तत्तत्स्वभावान् their various respective natures, प्रतिबोधयन् awakening, ईहसे You carry out Your activities.

Simple meaning: O Almighty Lord, although You are personally desireless and unaffected, You wield the power of time to orchestrate the creation, maintenance, and destruction of this universe through the modes of nature. With a mere glance, You awaken the latent natures of all living beings, and through Your infallible pastimes, You carry out Your divine will.

तस्यैव तेऽमूस्तनवस्त्रिलोक्यां शान्ता अशान्ता उत मूढयोनय: ।

शान्ता: प्रियास्ते ह्यधुनावितुं सतां स्थातुश्च ते धर्मपरीप्सयेहत: ॥10.16.50॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य of Him, एव certainly, ते Your, अमूः these, तनवः bodies/forms, त्रि-लोक्याम् within the three worlds, शान्ताः peaceful (in the mode of goodness), अशान्ताः restless (in the mode of passion), उत or, मूढ-योनयः born of ignorant species (in the mode of ignorance), शान्ताः those who are peaceful, प्रियाः dear, ते to You, हि indeed, अधुना now, अवितुम् to protect, सताम् the virtuous, स्थातुः of the maintainer, च and, ते Your, धर्म-परीप्सया with the desire to preserve dharma, ईहतः of one who is acting

Anvaya: त्रिलोक्याम् अमूः शान्ताः अशान्ताः उत मूढयोनयः तस्य ते एव तनवः। (तथापि) शान्ताः ते प्रियाः हि। अधुना सताम् अवितुं धर्मपरीप्सया च स्थातुः ईहतः ते (अयम् अवतारः अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: त्रिलोक्याम् In the three worlds, अमूः these, शान्ताः peaceful forms, अशान्ताः restless forms, उत or, मूढयोनयः those of ignorant births, तस्य ते एव are certainly Your, तनवः bodies. शान्ताः The peaceful ones, ते प्रियाः are dear to You, हि indeed. अधुना Now, सताम् अवितुं to protect the saintly, धर्मपरीप्सया च and with the desire to preserve righteousness, स्थातुः as the maintainer, ईहतः ते of You who are acting (this is the purpose).

Simple meaning: All varieties of living beings in the three worlds—whether they are peaceful (in goodness), restless (in passion), or ignorant (in dullness)—are ultimately Your different forms. However, those in the mode of goodness are especially dear to You. That is why You have now appeared: to act as the maintainer of the universe, to protect the virtuous, and to restore the principles of dharma.

अपराधः सकृद्भर्त्रा सोढव्यः स्वप्रजाकृतः ।

Anvaya: भगवन्! अनुगृह्णीष्व, पन्नगः प्राणान् त्यजति। साधुशोच्यानां नः स्त्रीणां प्राणः पतिः प्रदीयताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवन् O Lord!, अनुगृह्णीष्व Have mercy. पन्नगः The serpent, प्राणान् त्यजति is abandoning his life. साधुशोच्यानां Who are worthy of pity by the virtuous, नः स्त्रीणां for us women, प्राणः who is our life, पतिः our husband, प्रदीयताम् may be given back.

Simple meaning: O Supreme Lord, please be merciful! This serpent is now at the point of death, giving up his very life. We, his wives, are in a most pitiable state. Please, grant us the life of our husband, for to a wife, the husband is her very life-breath.

Simple meaning: Please command us, Your humble maidservants, and tell us what we should do. We know that anyone who executes Your order with full faith is surely liberated from all fear.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सकलत्रसुहृत्पुत्रः Along with his wives, friends, and sons, (Kaliya) अब्धेः द्वीपं to the island of the ocean, जगाम ह went. तदा एव At that very moment, क्रीडामानुषरूपिणः who assumed a playful human form, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, अनुग्रहात्  by the mercy, यमुना the Yamuna, सामृतजला having nectar-like water, निर्विषा अभवत् became free from poison.

Simple meaning: Accompanied by his wives, friends, and children, Kaliya went to the island in the ocean. At that very moment, by the mercy of the Supreme Lord, who was performing pastimes in a human-like form, the Yamuna's waters became free from poison and turned as sweet as nectar.
