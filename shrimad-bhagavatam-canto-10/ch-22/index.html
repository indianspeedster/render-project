Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 22

The Lord steals the garments of cowherd maids

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

गोपिकावस्त्रापहारः नाम  द्वाविंशः अध्यायः

(38 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

हेमन्ते प्रथमे मासि नन्दव्रजकुमारिकाः ।

चेरुर्हविष्यं भुञ्जानाः कात्यायन्यर्चनव्रतम् ॥10.22.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, हेमन्ते During the season of Hemanta (early winter), प्रथमे मासि in the first month (Margashirsha), नन्द-व्रज-कुमारिकाः the young girls of Nanda’s Vraja, चेरुः performed, हविष्यम् pure food (suitable for sacrifice), भुञ्जानाः eating, कात्यायनी-अर्चन-व्रतम् the vow of worshipping Goddess Katyayani

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — हेमन्ते प्रथमे मासि नन्दव्रजकुमारिकाः हविष्यं भुञ्जानाः कात्यायन्यर्चनव्रतं चेरुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, हेमन्ते during the pre-winter season, प्रथमे मासि in the first month, नन्दव्रजकुमारिकाः the young maidens of the village of Nanda, हविष्यं भुञ्जानाः eating only unspiced, sacrificial food, कात्यायन्यर्चनव्रतं the religious vow of worshipping Goddess Katyayani, चेरुः observed.

Simple meaning: Sri Shuka said: During the first month of the Hemanta season (the month of Margashirsha), the young marriageable girls of Vraja observed a special vow to worship Goddess Katyayani. Throughout the month, they restricted their diet to simple havishya (sanctified food during fast) to maintain their purity.

आप्लुत्याम्भसि कालिन्द्या जलान्ते चोदितेऽरुणे ।

कृत्वा प्रतिकृतिं देवीमानर्चुर्नृप सैकतीम् ॥10.22.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: आप्लुत्य having bathed, अम्भसि in the water, कालिन्द्याः of the river Yamuna (Kalindi), जल-अन्ते at the edge of the water, चोदिते arise, अरुणे when the sun (Aruna, the dawn), कृत्वा having made, प्रतिकृतिम् an image/deity, देवीम् the Goddess, आनर्चुः they worshipped, नृप O King (Parikshit), सैकतीम् made of sand

Anvaya: नृप! अरुणे चोदिते कालिन्द्याः अम्भसि आप्लुत्य जलान्ते सैकतीं देवीप्रतिकृतिं कृत्वा (ताः) आनर्चुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King!, अरुणे चोदिते When the dawn had arisen, कालिन्द्याः अम्भसि आप्लुत्य having bathed in the water of the Yamuna, जलान्ते at the water’s edge, सैकतीं देवीप्रतिकृतिं कृत्वा having fashioned a deity of the Goddess out of sand, आनर्चुः they worshipped (her).

Simple meaning: O King! Every morning at the break of dawn, the young girls would bathe in the waters of the Yamuna. Afterwards, right on the riverbank, they would craft a deity of Goddess Katyayani out of the river sand and perform their daily worship.

गन्धैर्माल्यैः सुरभिभिर्बलिभिर्धूपदीपकैः ।

उच्चावचैश्चोपहारैः प्रवालफलतण्डुलैः ॥10.22.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: गन्धैः with sandalwood paste/fragrances, माल्यैः with flower garlands, सुरभिभिः with fragrant (items), बलिभिः with offerings, धूप-दीपकैः with incense and lamps, उच्चावचैः of various kinds, च and, उपहारैः with presentations, प्रवाल-फल-तण्डुलैः with new sprouts, fruits, and grains of rice

Anvaya: (ताः) गन्धैः सुरभिभिः माल्यैः बलिभिः धूपदीपकैः प्रवालफलतण्डुलैः उच्चावचैः उपहारैः च (देवीं आनर्चुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ताः) गन्धैः सुरभिभिः माल्यैः (They) with sandalwood paste, fragrant garlands, बलिभिः धूपदीपकैः offerings, incense and lamps, प्रवालफलतण्डुलैः उच्चावचैः उपहारैः च and with various types of presentations such as new sprouts, fruits, and rice grains, (worshipped the Goddess).

Simple meaning: They worshipped the Goddess by offering her fragrant sandalwood paste, fresh flower garlands, and various other items including incense, lamps, tender sprouts, fruits, and grains of uncooked rice.

कात्यायनि महामाये महायोगिन्यधीश्वरि ।

नन्दगोपसुतं देवि पतिं मे कुरु ते नमः ।

इति मन्त्रं जपन्त्यस्ताः पूजां चक्रुः कुमारिकाः ॥10.22.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: कात्यायनि O Katyayani, महा-माये O possessor of great external energy, महा-योगिनि O possessor of great mystic power, अधीश्वरि O supreme controller, नन्द-गोप-सुतम् the son of the cowherd Nanda, देवि O Goddess, पतिम् husband, मे for me, कुरु please make, ते unto you, नमः my obeisances, इति thus, मन्त्रम् the mantra, जपन्त्यः chanting, ताः those, पूजाम् worship, चक्रुः performed, कुमारिकाः the young girls

Anvaya: कात्यायनि! महामाये! महायोगिनि! अधीश्वरि! देवि! नन्दगोपसुतं मे पतिं कुरु, ते नमः — इति मन्त्रं जपन्त्यः ताः कुमारिकाः पूजां चक्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कात्यायनि O Katyayani, महामाये O great Maya, महायोगिनि O great Yogini, अधीश्वरि O supreme controller, देवि O Goddess, नन्दगोपसुतं the son of Nanda Gopa, मे पतिं कुरु make my husband, ते नमः obeisances unto you — इति मन्त्रं जपन्त्यः chanting this mantra, ताः कुमारिकाः those young girls, पूजां चक्रुः performed their worship.

Simple meaning: Chanting the prayer, "O Goddess Katyayani, O great potency of the Lord, O possessor of great mystic power and supreme controller, please make the son of Nanda my husband. I offer my obeisances unto you," the young girls performed their daily worship.

एवं मासं व्रतं चेरुः कुमार्यः कृष्णचेतसः ।

भद्रकालीं समानर्चुर्भूयान्नन्दसुतः पतिः ॥10.22.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, मासम् for a month, व्रतम् the vow, चेरुः they observed, कुमार्यः the young girls, कृष्ण-चेतसः whose minds were absorbed in Krishna, भद्रकालीम् Goddess Bhadrakali, समानर्चुः they thoroughly worshipped, भूयात् may he become, नन्द-सुतः the son of Nanda, पतिः (my) husband

Anvaya: एवं कृष्णचेतसः कुमार्यः मासं व्रतं चेरुः। 'नन्दसुतः पतिः भूयात्' (इति प्रार्थयन्त्यः ताः) भद्रकालीं समानर्चुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं In this way, कृष्णचेतसः those whose consciousness was fixed on Krishna, कुमार्यः the young girls, मासं for a month, व्रतं चेरुः observed the vow. 'नन्दसुतः पतिः भूयात्' "May the son of Nanda be my husband," (praying thus), भद्रकालीं समानर्चुः they worshipped Goddess Bhadrakali.

Simple meaning: For an entire month, the young girls of Vraja, whose hearts were completely absorbed in Krishna, strictly followed their vow. They devotedly worshipped Goddess Bhadrakali with the single-minded prayer: "May the son of Nanda become my husband."

उषस्युत्थाय गोत्रैः स्वैरन्योन्याबद्धबाहवः ।

कृष्णमुच्चैर्जगुर्यान्त्यः कालिन्द्यां स्नातुमन्वहम् ॥10.22.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: उषसि at dawn, उत्थाय having risen, गोत्रैः by their names, स्वैः their own, अन्योन्य-आबद्ध-बाहवः with their arms locked with one another, कृष्णम् about Krishna, उच्चैः loudly, जगुः they sang, यान्त्यः while going, कालिन्द्याम् in the Yamuna, स्नातुम् to bathe, अन्वहम् every day

Anvaya: (ताः) अन्वहम् उषसि उत्थाय स्वैः गोत्रैः अन्योन्याबद्धबाहवः (सत्यः) स्नातुं कालिन्द्यां यान्त्यः कृष्णम् उच्चैः जगुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ताः) They, अन्वहम् every day, उषसि उत्थाय having risen at dawn, स्वैः गोत्रैः by their own names (calling out to each other), अन्योन्याबद्धबाहवः with their arms entwined with one another, स्नातुं कालिन्द्यां यान्त्यः while going to the Yamuna to bathe, कृष्णम् उच्चैः जगुः sang loudly about Krishna.

Simple meaning: Every day throughout that month, the young girls would wake up at dawn and call out to one another by name. Holding hands and locking arms, they would walk toward the Yamuna river to take their morning bath, all the while loudly singing the glories of Krishna.

नद्यां कदाचिदागत्य तीरे निक्षिप्य पूर्ववत् ।

वासांसि कृष्णं गायन्त्यो विजह्रुः सलिले मुदा ॥10.22.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: नद्याम् at the river, कदाचित् once, आगत्य having arrived, तीरे on the bank, निक्षिप्य placing/throwing down, पूर्व-वत् as before, वासांसि their clothing, कृष्णम् about Krishna, गायन्त्यः singing, विजह्रुः they sported/played, सलिले in the water, मुदा with joy

Anvaya: कदाचित् (ताः) नद्याम् आगत्य पूर्ववत् वासांसि तीरे निक्षिप्य कृष्णं गायन्त्यः मुदा सलिले विजह्रुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कदाचित् Once, (ताः) they, नद्याम् आगत्य having arrived at the river, पूर्ववत् as they had done before, वासांसि तीरे निक्षिप्य having placed their clothes on the bank, कृष्णं गायन्त्यः singing about Krishna, मुदा with joy, सलिले विजह्रुः sported in the water.

Simple meaning: One day, upon arriving at the river, the girls followed their usual routine by leaving their clothes on the bank. As they entered the water to play and splash about joyfully, they continued to loudly sing the glories of Krishna.

भगवांस्तदभिप्रेत्य कृष्णो योगेश्वरेश्वरः ।

वयस्यैरावृतस्तत्र गतस्तत्कर्मसिद्धये ॥10.22.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् the Supreme Lord, तत्-अभिप्रेत्य understanding their intent, कृष्णः Krishna, योगेश्वर-ईश्वरः the Lord of all masters of mystic power, वयस्यैः by His companions (the cowherd boys), आवृतः surrounded, तत्र there, गतः went, तत्-कर्म-सिद्धये to grant the success of their activity (the vow)

Anvaya: तत् अभिप्रेत्य वयस्यैः आवृतः योगेश्वरेश्वरः भगवान् कृष्णः तत्कर्मसिद्धये तत्र गतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् अभिप्रेत्य Perceiving that (their desire), वयस्यैः आवृतः surrounded by His friends, योगेश्वरेश्वरः the Lord of the lords of yoga, भगवान् कृष्णः the Supreme Lord Krishna, तत्कर्मसिद्धये for the sake of granting the perfection of their ritual, तत्र गतः went there.

Simple meaning: Lord Krishna, the supreme master of all mystic power, clearly understood the girls' inner desire. To grant them the fruit of their month-long vow, He went to the riverbank surrounded by His cowherd friends.

तासां वासांस्युपादाय नीपमारुह्य सत्वरः ।

हसद्भिः प्रहसन् बालैः परिहासमुवाच ह ॥10.22.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: तासाम् of them (the gopis), वासांसि the clothes, उपादाय taking away, नीपम् a Neepa tree (Kadamba), आरुह्य climbing, स-त्वरः quickly, हसद्भिः with the laughing, प्रहसन् laughing loudly, बालैः with the boys, परिहासम् joking words, उवाच spoke, ह indeed

Anvaya: (कृष्णः) तासां वासांसि उपादाय सत्वरः नीपम् आरुह्य हसद्भिः बालैः (सह) प्रहसन् परिहासम् उवाच ह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (Krishna) तासां वासांसि उपादाय having taken their clothes, सत्वरः quickly, नीपम् आरुह्य having climbed a Kadamba tree, हसद्भिः बालैः with the laughing boys, प्रहसन् laughing loudly, परिहासम् उवाच ह spoke joking words.

Simple meaning: Quickly taking the girls' clothes, Krishna climbed a nearby Kadamba tree. While laughing loudly along with His companions, He began to speak to the young girls with playful, joking words.

अत्रागत्याबलाः कामं स्वं स्वं वासः प्रगृह्यताम् ।

सत्यं ब्रवाणि नो नर्म यद् यूयं व्रत कर्शिताः ॥10.22.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: अत्र here, आगत्य having come, अबलाः O girls, कामम् according to your desire, स्वम् स्वम् each your own, वासः clothing, प्रगृह्यताम् please take, सत्यम् the truth, ब्रवाणि I am speaking, नो not, नर्म a joke, यद् because, यूयम् you all, व्रत-कर्शिताः are emaciated/weakened by the vow

Anvaya: हे अबलाः! अत्र आगत्य कामं स्वं स्वं वासः प्रगृह्यताम्। (अहं) सत्यं ब्रवाणि, नर्म नो, यत् यूयं व्रतकर्शिताः (स्थ)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अबलाः O girls!, अत्र आगत्य Having come here, कामं as you wish, स्वं स्वं वासः each your own clothing, प्रगृह्यताम् please take. (अहं) सत्यं ब्रवाणि I am speaking the truth, नर्म नो it is not a joke, यत् because, यूयं you all, व्रतकर्शिताः have been thinned by your vow.

Simple meaning: "O girls, please come here and take back your clothes as you like. I am telling you the truth and not joking, especially since you all have become so thin and tired from strictly following your month-long vow."

न मयोदितपूर्वं वा अनृतं तदिमे विदुः ।

एकैकशः प्रतीच्छध्वं सहैवोत सुमध्यमाः॥10.22.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: न never, मया by me, उदित-पूर्वम् spoken before, वै indeed, अनृतम् a lie, तत् that, इमे these (boys), विदुः know, एक-एकशः one by one, प्रतीच्छध्वम् you should take back, सह-एव all together, उत or, सु-मध्यमाः O slender-waisted ones

Anvaya: मया पूर्वम् अनृतं न उदितं वै, तत् इमे (बालकाः) विदुः। हे सुमध्यमाः! (यूयम्) एकैकशः उत सह एव (वासांसि) प्रतीच्छध्वम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मया By me, पूर्वम् previously, अनृतं न उदितं वै a lie, has indeed never been spoken, तत् that, इमे विदुः these boys know. हे सुमध्यमाः O slender-waisted girls, यूयम् you all, एकैकशः one by one, उत सह एव or all together, प्रतीच्छध्वम् take (your clothes) back.

Simple meaning: "I have never told a lie in my life, and these boys here can testify to that. Therefore, O slender-waisted girls, you may come and take your clothes back either one by one or all together, as you prefer."

तस्य तत् क्ष्वेलितं दृष्ट्वा गोप्यः प्रेमपरिप्लुताः ।

व्रीडिताः प्रेक्ष्य चान्योन्यं जातहासा न निर्ययुः ॥10.22.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य His (Krishna's), तत् that, क्ष्वेलितम् joking/playful act, दृष्ट्वा having seen, गोप्यः the gopis, प्रेम-परिप्लुताः overwhelmed with love, व्रीडिताः embarrassed/shameful, प्रेक्ष्य looking, च and, अन्योन्यम् at one another, जात-हासाः with smiles appearing, न never, निर्ययुः they came out (of the water)

Anvaya: तस्य तत् क्ष्वेलितं दृष्ट्वा प्रेमपरिप्लुताः गोप्यः व्रीडिताः (सत्यः) अन्योन्यं प्रेक्ष्य च जातहासाः (अभवन्, किन्तु ताः) न निर्ययुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तस्य His (Krishna's), तत् क्ष्वेलितं that playful act, दृष्ट्वा having seen, प्रेमपरिप्लुताः overwhelmed with love, गोप्यः the gopis, व्रीडिताः being embarrassed, अन्योन्यं प्रेक्ष्य looking at one another, च and, जातहासाः smiles appeared on them, (but they) न निर्ययुः did not come out.

Simple meaning: Seeing Krishna’s playful prank, the gopis were overwhelmed with love for Him. Although they felt embarrassed and glanced at one another with shy smiles, they could not bring themselves to come out of the water.

एवं ब्रुवति गोविन्दे नर्मणाऽऽक्षिप्तचेतसः।

आकण्ठमग्नाः शीतोदे वेपमानास्तमब्रुवन् ॥10.22.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, ब्रुवति while speaking, गोविन्दे Govinda (Krishna), नर्मणा by the joke, आक्षिप्त-चेतसः whose minds were captivated/disturbed, आकण्ठ-मग्नाः submerged up to the neck, शीत-उदे in the cold water, वेपमानाः shivering/trembling, तम् to Him, अब्रुवन् they spoke

Anvaya: गोविन्दे एवं ब्रुवति (सति) नर्मणा आक्षिप्तचेतसः शीतोदे आकण्ठमग्नाः वेपमानाः (ताः) तम् अब्रुवन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोविन्दे एवं ब्रुवति While Govinda was speaking thus, नर्मणा आक्षिप्तचेतसः their minds being captivated by His joking, शीतोदे आकण्ठमग्नाः submerged up to their necks in the cold water, वेपमानाः and shivering, (they) तम् अब्रुवन् spoke to Him.

Simple meaning: While Govinda spoke to them in this playful way, the girls felt their hearts completely captivated by His humor. Yet, standing submerged up to their necks in the freezing water and shivering with cold, they addressed Him as follows.

मानयं भोः कृथास्त्वां तु नन्दगोपसुतं प्रियम् ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भोः O Krishna, अनयं मा कृथाः do not do this injustice, त्वां तु indeed You, नन्दगोपसुतं the son of Nanda Gopa, प्रियं beloved, च and, व्रजश्लाघ्यं the glory of Vraja, जानीमः we know. अङ्ग O dear one, वासांसि देहि give the clothes, (वयं) वेपिताः we are shivering.

Simple meaning: "O Krishna, please do not be so unfair to us! We know You are the beloved son of Nanda Gopa and the pride of all Vraja. O dear one, we are shivering here in the cold water; please give us back our clothes."

श्यामसुन्दर ते दास्यः करवाम तवोदितम् ।

Anvaya: श्यामसुन्दर! (वयं) ते दास्यः (सन्ति), तव उदितं करवाम। (हे) धर्मज्ञ! वासांसि देहि, नो चेत् (वयं) राज्ञे ब्रुवामहे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्यामसुन्दर O Shyamasundara, (वयं) ते दास्यः (सन्ति) we are Your maidservants, तव उदितं करवाम (and) we will do what You have said. (हे) धर्मज्ञ O Knower of Dharma!, वासांसि देहि give (us our) clothes, नो चेत् otherwise, (वयं) राज्ञे ब्रुवामहे we shall tell the King.

Simple meaning: O Shyamasundara! We are Your maidservants and we are ready to do whatever You ask of us. O Knower of Virtue! Please give us back our clothes. If You do not, we will be forced to complain to the King about Your behavior.

श्री भगवान् उवाच

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच The Supreme Lord said, यदि भवत्यः मे दास्यः (सन्ति) if you all are My maidservants, मया उक्तं वा करिष्यथ and will do what has been said by Me, (तर्हि) then, शुचिस्मिताः O girls of pure smiles!, अत्र आगत्य Having come here, स्ववासांसि प्रतीच्छन्तु please take back your own clothes.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord said: If you are truly My maidservants and are prepared to do as I say, then, O girls of pure smiles, come here onto the bank and take your clothes back for yourselves.

ततो जलाशयात् सर्वा दारिकाः शीतवेपिताः ।

पाणिभ्यां योनिमाच्छाद्य प्रोत्तेरुः शीतकर्शिताः ॥10.22.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, जलाशयात् from the water (the lake), सर्वाः all, दारिकाः the young girls, शीत-वेपिताः trembling with cold, पाणिभ्याम् with both hands, योनिम् their private parts, आच्छाद्य covering, प्रोत्तेरुः came out (of the water), शीत-कर्शिताः distressed by the cold

Anvaya: ततः शीतवेपिताः शीतकर्शिताः सर्वाः दारिकाः पाणिभ्यां योनिम् आच्छाद्य जलाशयात् प्रोत्तेरुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Then, शीतवेपिताः trembling with cold, शीतकर्शिताः (and) distressed by the cold, सर्वाः दारिकाः all the young girls, पाणिभ्यां with both hands, योनिम् आच्छाद्य covering their private parts, जलाशयात् from the water, प्रोत्तेरुः came out.

Simple meaning: Then, all the young girls, shivering and distressed by the freezing water, came out of the lake, covering their nakedness with their hands.

भगवानाह ता वीक्ष्य शुद्धभावप्रसादितः ।

स्कन्धे निधाय वासांसि प्रीतः प्रोवाच सस्मितम् ॥10.22.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् the Supreme Lord, आह said, ताः them (the gopis), वीक्ष्य seeing, शुद्ध-भाव-प्रसादितः pleased by their pure intentions/devotion, स्कन्धे on His shoulder, निधाय having placed, वासांसि the clothes, प्रीतः satisfied/affectionate, प्रोवाच spoke, स-स्मितम् with a smile

Anvaya: ताः वीक्ष्य शुद्धभावप्रसादितः भगवान् वासांसि स्कन्धे निधाय प्रीतः (सन्) सस्मितं प्रोवाच।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ताः वीक्ष्य Seeing them, शुद्धभावप्रसादितः being pleased by their pure devotion, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, वासांसि स्कन्धे निधाय having placed the clothes on His shoulder, प्रीतः (सन्) being affectionate, सस्मितं प्रोवाच spoke with a smile.

Simple meaning: Seeing the girls, the Supreme Lord became very pleased by their pure-hearted devotion. Placing their clothes on His shoulder, He looked at them with great affection and, smiling, spoke the following words.

यूयं विवस्त्र यदपो धृतव्रता व्यगाहतैतत्तदु देवहेलनम् ।

Word-by-word meaning: यूयम् you all, विवस्त्राः naked, यत् because, अपः in the water, धृत-व्रताः performing a vow, व्यगाहत entered, एतत् this, तत् indeed, उ certainly, देव-हेलनम् an offense to the gods, बद्ध्वा अञ्जलिम् joining the palms (in prayer), मूर्ध्नि on the head, अपनुत्तये for the purpose of atonement, अंहसः the sin, कृत्वा having performed, नमः obeisances, अधः down/bowing, वसनम् your garments, प्रगृह्यताम् please take back

Anvaya: धृतव्रताः यूयं यत् विवस्त्राः अपः व्यगाहत, एतत् तत् उ देवहेलनम्। अंहसः अपनुत्तये मूर्ध्नि अञ्जलिं बद्ध्वा नमः कृत्वा अधः वसनं प्रगृह्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: धृतव्रताः You who are performing a vow, यूयं यत् because you, विवस्त्राः naked, अपः व्यगाहत entered the water, एतत् तत् उ this is indeed, देवहेलनम् an offense to the gods. अंहसः अपनुत्तये For the purpose of atonement of the sin, मूर्ध्नि अञ्जलिं बद्ध्वा joining your palms on your heads, नमः कृत्वा and performing obeisances, अधः (bowing) down, वसनं प्रगृह्यताम् please take back your garments.

Simple meaning: You are all performing a sacred vow, yet you entered the water naked; this is certainly an offense against the gods. To atone for this sin, you should join your palms in prayer above your heads, offer your respectful obeisances by bowing down, and then take back your clothes.

इत्यच्युतेनाभिहिता व्रजाबला मत्वा विवस्त्राप्लवनं व्रतच्युतिम् ।

तत्पूर्तिकामास्तदशेषकर्मणां साक्षात्कृतं नेमुरवद्यमृग् यतः ॥10.22.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, अच्युतेन by the infallible Lord (Achyuta), अभिहिताः addressed, व्रज-अबलाः the women of Vraja, मत्वा considering, विवस्त्र-आप्लवनम् bathing naked, व्रत-च्युतिम् a flaw in their vow, तत्-पूर्ति-कामाः desiring the completion of that (vow), तत्-अशेष-कर्मणाम् of all activities, साक्षात्-कृतम् the bestower of fruits, नेमुः they offered obeisances, अवद्य-मृक् the cleanser of all sins, यतः because (He is)

Anvaya: इति अच्युतेन अभिहिताः व्रजाबलाः विवस्त्राप्लवनं व्रतच्युतिं मत्वा तत्पूर्तिकामाः (सत्यः), यतः (सः) अशेषकर्मणां साक्षात्कृतम् अवद्यमृक् (अस्ति), (ततः तम्) नेमुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति Thus, अच्युतेन अभिहिताः addressed by Achyuta, व्रजाबलाः the women of Vraja, विवस्त्राप्लवनं व्रतच्युतिं मत्वा considering bathing naked a flaw in their vow, तत्पूर्तिकामाः (सत्यः) desiring the completion of that vow, यतः because (He is), अशेषकर्मणां साक्षात्कृतम् the bestower of fruits of all activities, अवद्यमृक् (अस्ति) and the cleanser of sins, (ततः तम्) नेमुः they offered obeisances to Him.

Simple meaning: Having been addressed in this way by Lord Achyuta, the young girls of Vraja realized that bathing naked had been a flaw in their vow. Desiring to successfully complete their penance, they offered their obeisances to Him, for He is the the bestower of fruits of all actions and the one who removes all sinful actions.

तास्तथावनता दृष्ट्वा भगवान् देवकीसुतः ।

Word-by-word meaning: ताः them (the gopis), तथा in that way, अवनताः bowed down, दृष्ट्वा seeing, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, देवकी-सुतः the son of Devaki, वासांसि the clothes, ताभ्यः to them, प्रायच्छत् gave back, करुणः merciful, तेन by that (action), तोषितः satisfied

Anvaya: देवकीसुतः भगवान् ताः तथा अवनताः दृष्ट्वा तोषितः (सन्) करुणः (सन्) ताभ्यः वासांसि प्रायच्छत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देवकीसुतः भगवान् The Supreme Lord, the son of Devaki, ताः तथा अवनताः दृष्ट्वा seeing them bowed down in that way, तोषितः (सन्) being satisfied, करुणः (सन्) and being merciful, ताभ्यः वासांसि प्रायच्छत् gave the clothes back to them.

Simple meaning: Seeing the girls bow down in that manner, the Supreme Lord, the son of Devaki, felt satisfied with their surrender. Moved by deep compassion, He gave them back their clothes.

दृढं प्रलब्धास्त्रपया च हापिताः प्रस्तोभिताः क्रीडनवच्च कारिताः ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ताः) They, दृढं प्रलब्धाः were greatly teased, त्रपया हापिताः च and deprived of their modesty, प्रस्तोभिताः mocked, क्रीडनवत् कारिताः च and made to act like playthings, वस्त्राणि च एव अपहृतानि and their clothes were indeed taken away, तथापि nevertheless, प्रियसङ्गनिर्वृताः being happy by the association of their beloved, ताः अमुं न अभ्यसूयन् they did not feel any resentment toward Him.

Simple meaning: Although the girls had been greatly teased, mocked, and deprived of their modesty—treated like mere playthings and having their clothes taken away—they did not feel the slightest bit of resentment toward Krishna. They were simply overjoyed to be in the company of their beloved.

परिधाय स्ववासांसि प्रेष्ठसङ्गमसज्जिताः ।

Word-by-word meaning: परिधाय having put on, स्व-वासांसि their own clothes, प्रेष्ठ-सङ्गम-सज्जिताः captivated by the association of their most beloved, गृहीत-चित्ताः whose hearts were stolen, नो चेलुः they did not move, तस्मिन् upon Him, लज्जायित-ईक्षणाः with shy glances

Anvaya: स्ववासांसि परिधाय प्रेष्ठसङ्गमसज्जिताः गृहीतचित्ताः ताः तस्मिन् लज्जायितेक्षणाः (सत्यः) नो चेलुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: स्ववासांसि परिधाय Having put on their own clothes, प्रेष्ठसङ्गमसज्जिताः being captivated by the company of their most beloved, गृहीतचित्ताः their hearts being stolen, ताः they, तस्मिन् लज्जायितेक्षणाः (सत्यः) with shy glances fixed upon Him, नो चेलुः did not move.

Simple meaning: After putting on their clothes, the girls remained enchanted by the company of their most beloved Krishna. Their hearts had been completely stolen by Him, and they stood there motionless, their eyes fixed upon Him with shy and modest glances.

तासां विज्ञाय भगवान् स्वपादस्पर्शकाम्यया ।

Simple meaning: Lord Damodara, understanding the deep resolve of the girls who had strictly followed their vow with the sole desire to touch His lotus feet, spoke to them as follows.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे) नृप O King, तस्याः उपवने in the grove of that river, पशून् कामं चारयन्तः pasturing the animals at will, क्षुधार्ताः (गोपाः) the cowherd boys, distressed by hunger, कृष्णरामौ उपागम्य approaching Krishna and Rama, इदम् अब्रुवन् spoke this.

Simple meaning: O King, while the cows were grazing freely in the nearby groves of the Yamuna, the cowherd boys began to feel the pangs of hunger. They approached Krishna and Balarama and spoke the following words.

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमे स्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे गोपिकावस्त्रापहारः नाम द्वाविंशः अध्यायः ॥22॥

Thus ends the twenty-second discourse named, 'The Lord steals away the garments of the cowherd maids', in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.

Canto 10, Chapter 22 of the Srimad Bhagavatam is famously known as the "Krishna Steals the Gopis’ Clothes" (Vastra-harana-lila). This chapter carries a deep spiritual significance beyond its literal narration. Here’s the essence of the chapter and its purport:

Summary of the Chapter

The young gopis of Vrindavan, deeply in love with Krishna, worship Goddess Katyayani to obtain Him as their husband. As part of their vrata (vow), they bathe daily in the Yamuna at dawn, praying with devotion. One day, Krishna, along with His friends, mischievously steals their garments and climbs a tree. When the gopis plead with Him to return their clothes, Krishna playfully insists that they come out of the water and individually retrieve their garments. Though embarrassed, the gopis comply, surrendering fully to Krishna. Pleased with their devotion and surrender, Krishna assures them that their desire to have Him as their husband will be fulfilled.
