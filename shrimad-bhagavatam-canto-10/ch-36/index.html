श्री शुक उवाच

अथ तर्ह्यागतो गोष्ठमरिष्टो वृषभासुरः ।

महीं महाककुत्कायः कम्पयन् खुरविक्षताम् ॥10.36.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अथ then, तर्हि at that time, आगतः arrived, गोष्ठम् at the cowherd village, अरिष्टः Arishta, वृषभ-असुरः the asura in the form of a bull, महीम् the earth, महा-ककुत्-कायः having a body with a massive hump, कम्पयन् making tremble, खुर-विक्षताम् torn up by his hooves

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — अथ तर्हि महाककुत्कायः वृषभासुरः अरिष्टः खुरविक्षतां महीं कम्पयन् गोष्ठम् आगतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अथ तर्हि Then at that time, महाककुत्कायः possessing a body with a giant hump, वृषभासुरः अरिष्टः the bull asura - Arishta, खुरविक्षतां महीं कम्पयन् making the earth tremble as it was torn by his hooves, गोष्ठम् आगतः arrived at the cow pasture.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Then, at that time, an asura Arishta, in the form of a bull with a massive hump and a giant body, arrived at the cowherd village. He made the earth tremble as he tore the ground apart with his hooves.

रम्भमाणः खरतरं पदा च विलिखन् महीम् ।

उद्यम्य पुच्छं वप्राणि विषाणाग्रेण चोद्धरन् ॥10.36.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: रम्भमाणः bellowing, खरतरम् very harshly, पदा with his feet, च and, विलिखन् scratching, महीम् the earth, उद्यम्य raising high, पुच्छम् his tail, वप्राणि the embankments, विषाण-अग्रेण with the tips of his horns, च and, उद्धरन् tearing up

Anvaya: खरतरं रम्भमाणः पदा महीं विलिखन् पुच्छम् उद्यम्य विषाणाग्रेण वप्राणि च उद्धरन् (सः आगतः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: खरतरं रम्भमाणः Bellowing very harshly, पदा महीं विलिखन् scratching the earth with his feet, पुच्छम् उद्यम्य raising his tail high, विषाणाग्रेण वप्राणि च उद्धरन् and tearing up the embankments with the tips of his horns (he arrived).

Simple Meaning: Bellowing very harshly, he scratched the earth with his hooves. With his tail raised high, he began tearing up the embankments and hillsides with the tips of his horns.

किञ्चित् किञ्चिच्छकृन्मुञ्चन् मूत्रयन् स्तब्धलोचनः ।

यस्य निर्ह्रादितेनाङ्ग निष्ठुरेण गवां नृणाम् ॥10.36.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: किञ्चित् किञ्चित् little by little, शकृत् dung, मुञ्चन् discharging, मूत्रयन् urinating, स्तब्ध-लोचनः with eyes fixed and staring, यस्य whose, निर्ह्रादितेन by the bellowing, अङ्ग O King (Parikshit), निष्ठुरेण harsh, गवाम् of the cows, नृणाम् of the men

Anvaya: अङ्ग! (सः) किञ्चित् किञ्चित् शकृत् मुञ्चन् मूत्रयन् स्तब्धलोचनः (आसीत्), यस्य निष्ठुरेण निर्ह्रादितेन गवां नृणाम् …

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग O King, किञ्चित् किञ्चित् शकृत् मुञ्चन् मूत्रयन् passing stool and urine little by little, स्तब्धलोचनः with staring eyes, यस्य निष्ठुरेण निर्ह्रादितेन by whose harsh bellowing, गवां नृणाम् of the cows and the men …

Simple Meaning: O King, discharging little bits of dung and urine with eyes fixed in a stare, the asura terrified the cows and the men with his harsh, thunderous bellowing…. (contd. In next shloka)

पतन्त्यकालतो गर्भाः स्रवन्ति स्म भयेन वै ।

निर्विशन्ति घना यस्य ककुद्यचलशङ्कया ॥10.36.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: पतन्ति fell, अ-कालतः prematurely, गर्भाः the embryos, स्रवन्ति स्म miscarried, भयेन out of fear, वै indeed, निर्विशन्ति entered, घनाः the clouds, यस्य whose, ककुदि into the hump, अचल-शङ्कया thinking it to be a mountain.

Anvaya: (यस्य निर्ह्रादितेन) भयेन वै गर्भाः अकालतः पतन्ति स्रवन्ति स्म, घनाः यस्य ककुदि अचलशङ्कया निर्विशन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (By his bellowing) भयेन वै indeed out of fear, गर्भाः अकालतः पतन्ति स्रवन्ति स्म the embryos were discharged prematurely and miscarried, घनाः clouds, यस्य ककुदि into his hump, अचलशङ्कया निर्विशन्ति entered thinking it to be a mountain.

Simple Meaning: Out of sheer terror of his roar, the embryos of the cows and women miscarried. He was so massive that clouds would drape around his hump, mistaking it for a great mountain.

तं तीक्ष्णशृङ्गमुद्वीक्ष्य गोप्यो गोपाश्च तत्रसुः ।

पशवो दुद्रुवुर्भीता राजन् संत्यज्य गोकुलम् ॥10.36.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, तीक्ष्ण-शृङ्गम् having sharp horns, उद्वीक्ष्य seeing, गोप्यः the gopis, गोपाः च and the cowherd men, तत्रसुः became terrified, पशवः the animals, दुद्रुवुः ran away, भीताः frightened, राजन् O King, संत्यज्य leaving, गोकुलम् the village of Gokula

Anvaya: राजन्! तं तीक्ष्णशृङ्गम् उद्वीक्ष्य गोप्यः गोपाः च तत्रसुः, पशवः भीताः गोकुलं संत्यज्य दुद्रुवुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King, तं तीक्ष्णशृङ्गम् उद्वीक्ष्य seeing him with sharp horns, गोप्यः गोपाः च तत्रसुः the gopis and cowherd men became terrified, पशवः भीताः the frightened animals, गोकुलं संत्यज्य दुद्रुवुः ran away leaving Gokula.

Simple Meaning: O King, seeing that asura with his sharp horns, the gopis and cowherd men were struck with fear. The terrified domestic animals fled from the village of Gokula.

कृष्ण कृष्णेति ते सर्वे गोविन्दं शरणं ययुः ।

भगवानपि तद् वीक्ष्य गोकुलं भयविद्रुतम् ॥10.36.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्ण कृष्ण इति "Krishna, Krishna!" thus, ते they, सर्वे all, गोविन्दम् Govinda, शरणम् for shelter, ययुः approached, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, अपि also, तत् that, वीक्ष्य seeing, गोकुलम् the village of Gokula, भय-विद्रुतम् fleeing in fear

Anvaya: ते सर्वे “कृष्ण कृष्ण” इति गोविन्दं शरणं ययुः, भगवान् अपि तत् गोकुलं भयविद्रुतं वीक्ष्य (तत्र आजगाम)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते सर्वे All of them, कृष्ण कृष्ण इति crying "Krishna, Krishna!", गोविन्दं शरणं ययुः approached Govinda for shelter, भगवान् अपि the Supreme Lord also, तत् गोकुलं भयविद्रुतं वीक्ष्य seeing that Gokula fleeing in fear (arrived there).

Simple Meaning: Crying out "Krishna, Krishna!", they all ran to Govinda for protection. The Supreme Lord, seeing the entire village of Gokula fleeing in terror, stepped forward to address the situation.

मा भैष्टेति गिराऽऽश्वास्य वृषासुरमुपाह्वयत् ।

गोपालैः पशुभिर्मन्द त्रासितैः किमसत्तम ॥10.36.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: मा don't, भैष्ट be afraid, इति thus, गिरा with His words, आश्वास्य comforting, वृष-असुरम् the bull asura, उपाह्वयत् He called out, गोपालैः with the cowherd men, पशुभिः with the animals, मन्द O fool, त्रासितैः who are frightened, किम् what (is the use), असत्तम O most wicked one

Anvaya: (भगवान्) “मा भैष्ट” इति गिरा आश्वास्य वृषासुरम् उपाह्वयत् — मन्द! असत्तम! त्रासितैः गोपालैः पशुभिः किम्?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (The Lord) “मा भैष्ट” इति गिरा आश्वास्य comforting them with the words "do not be afraid", वृषासुरम् उपाह्वयत् called out to the bull asura— मन्द! असत्तम! O dull-headed and most wicked one, त्रासितैः गोपालैः पशुभिः किम् what is the point of frightening the cowherd men and animals?

Simple Meaning: Comforting the people with the words "do not be afraid," the Lord called out to the bull asura: "You fool! O most wicked one, what is the use of terrifying the cowherds and their animals?"

बलदर्पहाहं दुष्टानां त्वद्विधानां दुरात्मनां ।

इत्यास्फोट्याच्युतोऽरिष्टं तलशब्देन कोपयन् ॥10.36.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: बल-दर्प-हा destroyer of the pride of strength, अहम् I, दुष्टानाम् of the wicked, त्वत्-विधानाम् of those like you, दुरात्मनाम् evil-minded, इति thus, आस्फोट्य challenging by slapping His arms, अच्युतः the infallible Lord, अरिष्टम् Arishta, तल-शब्दम् with the sound of His palms, कोपयन् angerying him

Anvaya: अहं त्वद्विधानां दुरात्मनां दुष्टानां बलदर्पहा (अस्मि), इति आस्फोट्य अच्युतः तलशब्देन अरिष्टं कोपयन् (तस्थौ)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहं I, त्वद्विधानां दुरात्मनां दुष्टानां of wicked and evil-minded ones like you, बलदर्पहा am the destroyer of the pride of strength, इति आस्फोट्य thus challenging by slapping His arms, अच्युतः Lord Achyuta, तलशब्देन with the sound of His palms, अरिष्टं (confronted) Arishta, कोपयन् provoking …

Simple Meaning: "I am the punisher of wicked, evil-minded fools like you who are proud of their strength!" Saying this, Lord Achyuta challenged the asura by slapping His arms and provoked Arishta further with the loud sound of His palms.

सख्युरंसे भुजाभोगं प्रसार्यावस्थितो हरिः ।

सोऽप्येवं कोपितोऽरिष्टः खुरेणावनिमुल्लिखन् ।

उद्यत्पुच्छभ्रमन्मेघः क्रुद्धः कृष्णमुपाद्रवत् ॥10.36.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: सख्युः of a friend, अंसे on the shoulder, भुजा-भोगम् His mighty arm, प्रसार्य placing, अवस्थितः standing, हरिः Lord Hari, सः that, अपि even, एवम् in this way, कोपितः angered, अरिष्टः Arishta, खुरेण with his hoof, अवनिम् the ground, उल्लिखन् scraping, उद्यत्-पुच्छ-भ्रमन्-मेघः whose tail was raised and tossing the clouds, क्रुद्धः furious, कृष्णम् toward Krishna, उपाद्रवत् charged

Anvaya: सख्युः अंसे भुजाभोगं प्रसार्य अवस्थितः हरिः, एवं कोपितः सः अरिष्टः अपि खुरेण अवनिम् उल्लिखन् उद्यत्पुच्छभ्रमन्मेघः क्रुद्धः कृष्णम् उपाद्रवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सख्युः अंसे भुजाभोगं प्रसार्य अवस्थितः हरिः Lord Hari standing with His mighty arm resting on a friend's shoulder, एवं कोपितः सः अरिष्टः अपि that Arishta who was thus angered, खुरेण अवनिम् उल्लिखन् scraping the ground with his hoof, उद्यत्पुच्छभ्रमन्मेघः with his tail raised and tossing the clouds, क्रुद्धः कृष्णम् उपाद्रवत् charged furiously toward Krishna.

Simple Meaning: Lord Hari stood casually with His mighty arm resting on a friend's shoulder. Thus provoked, the furious Arishta scraped the ground with his hooves and, with his tail raised so high it seemed to toss the clouds, charged headlong at Krishna.

अग्रन्यस्तविषाणाग्रः स्तब्धासृग्लोचनोऽच्युतम् ।

कटाक्षिप्याद्रवत्तूर्णमिन्द्रमुक्तोऽशनिर्यथा ॥10.36.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: अग्र-न्यस्त-विषाण-अग्रः pointing the tips of his horns forward, स्तब्ध-असृक्-लोचनः with fixed and bloodshot eyes, अच्युतम् at the infallible Lord Krishna, कटाक्षिप्य glaring from the corner of his eyes, अद्रवत् he charged, तूर्णम् swiftly, इन्द्र-मुक्तः thrown by Indra, अशनिः a thunderbolt, यथा like

Anvaya: अग्रन्यस्तविषाणाग्रः स्तब्धासृग्लोचनः अच्युतं कटाक्षिप्य इन्द्रमुक्तः अशनिः यथा तूर्णम् अद्रवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अग्रन्यस्तविषाणाग्रः Pointing the tips of his horns straight ahead, स्तब्धासृग्लोचनः with eyes fixed and bloodshot, अच्युतं कटाक्षिप्य glaring at Lord Achyuta from the corners of his eyes, इन्द्रमुक्तः अशनिः यथा like a thunderbolt hurled by Indra, तूर्णम् अद्रवत् he charged swiftly.

Simple Meaning: Pointing the tips of his horns straight ahead and fixing his bloodshot eyes on the Lord, Arishta glared at Krishna from the corners of his eyes and charged at Him swiftly, like a thunderbolt hurled by Indra.

गृहीत्वा शृङ्गयोस्तं वा अष्टादश पदानि सः ।

प्रत्यपोवाह भगवान् गज: प्रतिगजं यथा ॥10.36.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: गृहीत्वा seizing, शृङ्गयोः by the two horns, तम् him, वा indeed, अष्टादश eighteen, पदानि steps, सः He, प्रत्यपोवाह pushed him back, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, गजः an elephant, प्रतिगजम् a rival elephant, यथा just as

Anvaya: सः भगवान् तं शृङ्गयोः गृहीत्वा गजः प्रतिगजं यथा अष्टादश पदानि प्रत्यपोवाह।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः भगवान् That Supreme Lord, तं शृङ्गयोः गृहीत्वा seizing him by his two horns, गजः प्रतिगजं यथा just as an elephant (pushes) a rival elephant, अष्टादश पदानि प्रत्यपोवाह pushed him back eighteen steps.

Simple Meaning: Seizing the asura by his two horns, the Supreme Lord pushed him back eighteen steps, just as a powerful elephant repels a rival elephant.

सोऽपविद्धो भगवता पुनरुत्थाय सत्वरः ।

आपतत् स्विन्नसर्वाङ्गो निःश्वसन् क्रोधमूर्छितः ॥10.36.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he, अपविद्धः thrown back, भगवता by the Supreme Lord, पुनः again, उत्थाय getting up, सत्वरः very quickly, आपतत् charged, स्विन्न-सर्वाङ्गः his whole body covered in sweat, निःश्वसन् breathing hard, क्रोध-मूर्छितः senseless with anger

Anvaya: भगवता अपविद्धः सः सत्वरः पुनः उत्थाय स्विन्नसर्वाङ्गः निःश्वसन् क्रोधमूर्छितः आपतत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवता अपविद्धः Thrown back by the Supreme Lord, सः सत्वरः पुनः उत्थाय he quickly getting up again, स्विन्नसर्वाङ्गः with his entire body perspiring, निःश्वसन् breathing heavily, क्रोधमूर्छितः senseless with rage, आपतत् charged.

Simple Meaning: Although thrown back by the Supreme Lord, the asura quickly got up and charged again. His entire body was covered in sweat and he breathed heavily, driven senseless by his extreme rage.

तमापतन्तं स निगृह्य शृङ्गयोः पदा समाक्रम्य निपात्य भूतले ।

निष्पपीडयामास यथाऽऽर्द्रमम्बरं कृत्त्वा विषाणेन जघान सोऽपतत् ॥10.36.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, आपतन्तम् charging, सः He (Krishna), निगृह्य seizing, शृङ्गयोः by the horns, पदा with His foot, समाक्रम्य treading upon him, निपात्य throwing him down, भूतले on the ground, निष्पपीडयामास He wrung him out, यथा as, आर्द्रम् a wet, अम्बरम् cloth, कृत्त्वा having done so, विषाणेन with the horn, जघान He struck, सः he (the asura), अपतत् fell

Anvaya: सः आपतन्तं तं शृङ्गयोः निगृह्य पदा समाक्रम्य भूतले निपात्य आर्द्रम् अम्बरं यथा निष्पपीडयामास, विषाणेन जघान (च) सः अपतत्।

Word-by-word meaning: अक्रूरः उवाच Akrura said, राजन् O King, मनीषितम् design/thought, सध्र्यक् perfect/well-conceived, तव your, स्व-अवद्य-मार्जनम् clearing away your own misfortunes (or perceived threats), सिद्धि-असिध्योः in success and failure, समम् equal/equanimous, कुर्यात् one should act, दैवम् destiny/the divine, हि indeed, फल-साधनम् the accomplisher of results

Anvaya: अक्रूरः उवाच — राजन्! तव स्वावद्यमार्जनं मनीषितं सध्र्यक्, (तथापि) सिद्ध्यसिध्योः समं कुर्यात्, हि दैवं फलसाधनम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अक्रूरः उवाच Akrura said — राजन्! तव स्वावद्यमार्जनं मनीषितं सध्र्यक् O King! Your design to clear away your own perceived threats is well-conceived, (तथापि) सिद्ध्यसिध्योः समं कुर्यात् (nevertheless) one should remain equal in both success and failure, हि दैवं फलसाधनम् because indeed, it is destiny that ultimately brings about the result.

Simple Meaning: Akrura replied: "O King, your plan to eliminate what you see as your misfortune is certainly well-thought-out. However, one should maintain balance whether a plan succeeds or fails, for ultimately, it is destiny—not just effort—that determines the final outcome."

Insights from the Context: Akrura's response is a masterclass in diplomatic caution. While he appears to agree with Kamsa by calling the plan "well-conceived" (सध्र्यक्), he subtly reminds the tyrant that human effort is subservient to Providence (दैवम्). He remains "equanimous" because he knows that while Kamsa is planning a murder, the Divine is planning a deliverance.

मनोरथान् करोत्युच्चैर्जनो दैवहतानपि ।

Word-by-word meaning: मनोरथान् desires/plans, करोति makes, उच्चैः great/ambitious, जनः a person, दैव-हतान् even if struck down by destiny, अपि even, युज्यते becomes joined/affected, हर्ष-शोकाभ्याम् by joy and grief, तथापि nonetheless, आज्ञाम् command, करोमि I am carrying out, ते your

Anvaya: जनः दैवहतान् अपि मनोरथान् उच्चैः करोति, (तथा) हर्षशोकाभ्यां युज्यते; तथापि (अहं) ते आज्ञां करोमि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जनः दैवहतान् अपि मनोरथान् उच्चैः करोति A person makes grand plans even if they are already frustrated by destiny; (तथा) हर्षशोकाभ्यां युज्यते (and thus) he is subjected to the swings of joy and grief; तथापि (अहं) ते आज्ञां करोमि nevertheless, I shall carry out your command.

Simple Meaning: "People continue to build ambitious castles in the air even when destiny has already ruled against them; consequently, they find themselves caught in the grip of either elation or sorrow when the results arrive. Nevertheless, as your subject, I will execute the order you have given me."

Contextual Significance: This verse concludes the dialogue between Kamsa and Akrura.

  • The Irony: Akrura is speaking a profound truth to Kamsa's face. Kamsa thinks his "grand plan" (manoratha) will secure his life, but Akrura knows that destiny (daiva) has already decided Kamsa’s end.

  • The Duty: Despite knowing Kamsa’s evil intentions, Akrura agrees to go. He isn't being a "yes-man"; he is acting as the instrument of Providence. He knows that by "obeying" Kamsa and going to Vraja, he will actually be the one to bring Krishna to Mathura to fulfill the prophecy.

  • The Philosophical Note: The verse highlights the human tendency to ignore the inevitable power of fate, leading to emotional suffering (harsha-shoka).

प्रविवेश गृहं कंसस्तथाक्रूरः स्वमालयम् ॥10.36.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एवम् in this way, आदिश्य having instructed, च and, अक्रूरम् Akrura, मन्त्रिणः his ministers, च and, विसृज्य dismissing, सः he (Kamsa), प्रविवेश entered, गृहम् his palace, कंसः Kamsa, तथा and likewise, अक्रूरः Akrura, स्व-आलयम् his own residence

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — सः कंसः एवम् अक्रूरम् आदिश्य मन्त्रिणः च विसृज्य गृहं प्रविवेश, तथा अक्रूरः (अपि) स्वमालयम् (प्रविवेश)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said — सः कंसः एवम् अक्रूरम् आदिश्य that Kamsa, having thus instructed Akrura, मन्त्रिणः च विसृज्य and having dismissed his ministers, गृहं प्रविवेश entered his palace; तथा अक्रूरः (अपि) स्वमालयम् and Akrura likewise (entered) his own home.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: After giving these instructions to Akrura and dismissing his ministers, Kamsa retired to his palace. Akrura also returned to his own residence.

Commentary: This verse marks the end of Chapter 36 of the 10th Canto. It serves as a transition:

  • Kamsa's State: Having "set the trap," Kamsa goes inside, perhaps feeling a false sense of security that his clever plan will save him.

  • Akrura's State: Akrura leaves with a very different heart. While Kamsa thinks he has sent a messenger to fetch his "death," Akrura is overwhelmed with the realization that he is finally going to see Lord Krishna.

  • The Calm before the Storm: The stage is now set for one of the most emotional chapters in the Bhagavatam—Akrura’s journey to Vrindavana.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “अरिष्टवधः अक्रूर संप्रेषणं च नाम” षट्त्रिंशः अध्यायः ॥36॥

Thus ends the thirty-sixth discourse named “Krishna slays Arishta. Kamsa sends Akrura to Vraja to bring Krishna and Balarama to Mathura” in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
