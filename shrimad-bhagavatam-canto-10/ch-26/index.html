Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 26

Nanda’s conversation with the cowherds about Shri Krishna's glory

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

नन्दगोपानां श्रीकृष्णकीर्तिविषये संवादः नाम षड्विंशः अध्यायः

(25 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

एवं विधानि कर्माणि गोपाः कृष्णस्य वीक्ष्य ते ।

अतद्वीर्यविदः प्रोचुः समभ्येत्य सुविस्मिताः ॥10.26.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, एवम् विधानि of this kind, कर्माणि activities/deeds, गोपाः the cowherds, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, वीक्ष्य observing, ते they, अ-तत्-वीर्य-विदः not knowing His (true) power, प्रोचुः spoke, समभ्येत्य coming together, सु-विस्मिताः greatly amazed

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच— ते गोपाः कृष्णस्य एवं विधानि कर्माणि वीक्ष्य सुविस्मिताः (सन्तः) अतद्वीर्यविदः समभ्येत्य प्रोचुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— ते गोपाः Those cowherds, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, एवं विधानि of such a kind, कर्माणि deeds, वीक्ष्य having seen, सुविस्मिताः being greatly astonished, अतद्वीर्यविदः not knowing the full extent of His power, समभ्येत्य coming together, प्रोचुः spoke.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: Having witnessed such extraordinary deeds performed by Krishna, the cowherds were struck with wonder. Because they did not fully understand the true nature of His divine power, they gathered together and spoke as follows.

बालकस्य यदेतानि कर्माण्यत्यद्भुतानि वै ।

कथमर्हत्यसौ जन्म ग्राम्येष्वात्मजुगुप्सितम् ॥10.26.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: बालकस्य of a child, यत् since, एतानि these, कर्माणि deeds, अति-अद्भुतानि most wonderful, वै indeed, कथम् how, अर्हति deserves/is it possible, असौ He, जन्म birth, ग्राम्येषु among common village folk, आत्म-जुगुप्सितम् which is unworthy of Him

Anvaya: यत् बालकस्य एतानि कर्माणि वै अत्यद्भुतानि, (तस्मात्) असौ ग्राम्येषु आत्मजुगुप्सितं जन्म कथम् अर्हति?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत् Since, बालकस्य of (this) child, एतानि कर्माणि these deeds, वै indeed, अत्यद्भुतानि are most wonderful, (तस्मात्) therefore, असौ He, ग्राम्येषु among simple villagers, आत्मजुगुप्सितं a birth that seems unworthy of Him, कथम् अर्हति how can He take?

Simple meaning: "Since this boy's activities are so incredibly wonderful, how is it possible for Him to have taken birth among us simple forest-dwellers—a birth that seems completely beneath His exalted nature?"

यः सप्तहायनो बालः करेणैकेन लीलया ।

कथं बिभ्रद्गिरिवरं पुष्करं गजराडिव ॥10.26.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: यः who, सप्त-हायनः seven years old, बालः a boy, करेण hand, एकेन with one, लीलया effortlessly (as if in play), कथम् how, बिभ्रत् held, गिरि-वरम् the best of mountains (Govardhana), पुष्करम् a lotus flower, गज-राट्-इव like a kingly elephant

हायन   हायनः —नं A year. Src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: यः सप्तहायनः बालः एकेन करेण गिरिवरं गजराट् पुष्करम् इव कथं लीलया बिभ्रत्?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः Who, सप्तहायनः बालः a seven-year-old boy, एकेन करेण with one hand, गिरिवरं the great mountain, गजराट् a king elephant, पुष्करम् इव like a lotus flower, कथं how, लीलया effortlessly, बिभ्रत् held?

Simple meaning: "How could this seven-year-old boy hold up the great Govardhana Mountain with only one hand, as effortlessly as a mighty elephant carries a lotus flower in its trunk?"

तोकेनामीलिताक्षेण पूतनाया महौजसः ।

पीतः स्तनः सह प्राणैः कालेनेव वयस्तनोः ॥10.26.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तोकेन by the infant, अमीलित-अक्षेण with eyes partially closed, पूतनायाः of Putana, मह-ओजसः who possessed great power, पीतः sucked, स्तनः the breast, सह along with, प्राणैः her life air, कालेन by Time, इव just as, वयः the life span/youth, तनोः of a body

Anvaya: अमीलिताक्षेण तोकेन महौजसः पूतनायाः स्तनः प्राणैः सह पीतः – कालेन इव तनोः वयः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अमीलिताक्षेण With eyes half-closed, तोकेन by the infant, महौजसः of the very powerful, पूतनायाः Putana’s, स्तनः breast, प्राणैः सह along with her life, पीतः was sucked, कालेन इव just as, by Time, तनोः of a body, वयः the life span (is consumed).

Simple meaning: "Even as a tiny infant with His eyes half-closed, He sucked the breast of the powerful rakshasi Putana and took her life along with her milk. He did this just as inescapable Time gradually and silently consumes the life span of a living being."

हिन्वतोऽधः शयानस्य मास्यस्य चरणावुदक् ।

अनोऽपतद् विपर्यस्तं रुदतः प्रपदाहतम् ॥10.26.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: हिन्वतः of Him who was tossing (His limbs), अधः beneath (the cart), शयानस्य lying down, मास्यस्य of one who was only a few months old, चरणौ the two feet, उदक् upwards, अनः the cart, अपतत् fell, विपर्यस्तम् overturned, रुदतः of Him who was crying, प्रपद-आहतम् struck by the tips of His toes

Anvaya: अधः शयानस्य उदक् चरणौ हिन्वतः रुदतः मास्यस्य प्रपदाहतं विपर्यस्तम् अनः अपतत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अधः शयानस्य Of Him lying underneath, उदक् upwards, चरणौ हिन्वतः tossing His feet, रुदतः crying, मास्यस्य of the one-month-old (infant), प्रपदाहतं struck by the tip of His foot, विपर्यस्तम् overturned, अनः the cart, अपतत् fell.

Simple meaning: "When He was only a few months old, while lying under a handcart, He began to cry and toss His legs upward. Just by the touch of His tiny toes, that heavy cart was struck and completely overturned."

एकहायन आसीनो ह्रियमाणो विहायसा ।

दैत्येन यस्तृणावर्तमहन् कण्ठग्रहातुरम् ॥10.26.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: एक-हायनः one year old, आसीनः sitting, ह्रियमाणः being carried away, विहायसा through the sky, दैत्येन by the daitya, यः who, तृणावर्तम् Trinavarta, अहन् killed, कण्ठ-ग्रह-आतुरम् (the daitya) who became distressed by the grip on his throat

Anvaya: यः एकहायनः आसीनः (सन्) दैत्येन विहायसा ह्रियमाणः कण्ठग्रहातुरं तृणावर्तम् अहन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः Who, एकहायनः one year old, आसीनः sitting, दैत्येन by the daitya, विहायसा through the sky, ह्रियमाणः being carried away, कण्ठग्रहातुरं distressed by the seizing of his throat, तृणावर्तम् Trinavarta, अहन् killed.

Simple meaning: "When He was only one year old, while He was sitting and being carried high into the sky by the whirlwind daitya Trinavarta, He seized the daitya’s throat. Krishna throttled him so tightly that the daitya, overwhelmed with pain, was killed."

क्वचिद्धैयंगवस्तैन्ये मात्रा बद्ध उलूखले ।

गच्छन्नर्जुयोर्मध्ये बाहुभ्यां तावपातयत् ॥10.26.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्वचित् once, हैयंगव-स्तैन्ये for the theft of fresh butter, मात्रा by His mother (Yashoda), बद्धः tied, उलूखले to a wooden grinding mortar, गच्छन् walking, अर्जुनयोः मध्ये between the two Arjuna trees, बाहुभ्यां with His two arms, तौ those two (trees), अपातयत् caused to fall

Anvaya: क्वचित् हैयंगवस्तैन्ये मात्रा उलूखले बद्धः (सः) अर्जुनयोः मध्ये गच्छन् बाहुभ्यां तौ अपातयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: क्वचित् हैयंगवस्तैन्ये Once, on account of stealing fresh butter, मात्रा by His mother, उलूखले to a mortar, बद्धः bound, अर्जुनयोः मध्ये between two Arjuna trees, गच्छन् going, बाहुभ्यां by His two arms, तौ those two, अपातयत् threw down.

Simple meaning: "And once, when His mother tied Him to a wooden mortar as punishment for stealing butter, He dragged that mortar between two tall Arjuna trees. Simply by pulling with His arms, He caused those massive trees to come crashing down."

वने सञ्चारयन् वत्सान् सरामो बालकैर्वृतः ।

हन्तुकामं बकं दोर्भ्यां मुखतोऽरिमपाटयत् ॥10.26.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: वने in the forest, सञ्चारयन् tending/herding, वत्सान् the calves, स-रामः along with Balarama, बालकैः by the (cowherd) boys, वृतः surrounded, हन्तु-कामम् desiring to kill Him, बकम् Bakasura , दोर्भ्याम् with His two arms, मुखतः from the mouth/beak, अरिम् the enemy, अपाटयत् tore apart

Anvaya: सरामः बालकैर्वृतः वने वत्सान् सञ्चारयन् (सः) हन्तुकामम् अरिं बकं दोर्भ्यां मुखतः अपाटयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सरामः Along with Balarama, बालकैर्वृतः surrounded by the boys, वने in the forest, वत्सान् the calves, सञ्चारयन् tending, (सः) He (Krishna), हन्तुकामम् अरिं बकं Baka, the enemy who desired to kill, दोर्भ्यां with His two arms, मुखतः from the beak, अपाटयत् tore apart.

Simple meaning: "While tending the calves in the forest with Balarama and the other boys, He encountered the enemy Bakasura, who came with the intent to kill Him. Krishna simply seized the giant crane by the beak with His two arms and tore him apart."

वत्सेषु वत्सरूपेण प्रविशन्तं जिघांसया ।

हत्वा न्यपातयत्तेन कपित्थानि च लीलया ॥10.26.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: वत्सेषु among the calves, वत्स-रूपेण in the form of a calf, प्रविशन्तम् entering, जिघांसया with the desire to kill, हत्वा having killed, न्यपातयत् threw down, तेन with him (the rakshasa's body), कपित्थानि Kapittha fruits (wood-apples), च and, लीलया effortlessly

Anvaya: जिघांसया वत्सेषु वत्सरूपेण प्रविशन्तं (वत्सासुरं) हत्वा तेन कपित्थानि च लीलया न्यपातयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: जिघांसया With the intent to kill, वत्सेषु among the calves, वत्सरूपेण in the form of a calf, प्रविशन्तं who had entered, (वत्सासुरं) Vatsasura, हत्वा having killed, तेन with his body, कपित्थानि the Kapittha fruits, च and, लीलया effortlessly, न्यपातयत् knocked down.

Simple meaning: "When Vatsasura entered the herd in the form of a calf with the intent to kill Krishna, the Lord caught him, killed him, and then tossed his body into the trees, effortlessly knocking down many Kapittha fruits in the process."

हत्वा रासभदैतेयं तद्बन्धूंश्च बलान्वितः ।

चक्रे तालवनं क्षेमं परिपक्वफलान्वितम् ॥10.26.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: हत्वा having killed, रासभ-दैतेयम् the daitya in the form of an ass (Dhenukasura), तत्-बन्धून् च and his kinsmen, बल-अन्वितः along with Balarama, चक्रे made, ताल-वनम् the Talavana forest, क्षेमम् safe, परिपक्व-फल-अन्वितम् full of ripe fruits

Anvaya: (सः) बलान्वितः रासभदैतेयं तद्बन्धून् च हत्वा परिपक्वफलान्वितं तालवनं क्षेमं चक्रे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Krishna), बलान्वितः along with Balarama, रासभदैतेयं the ass-demon, तद्बन्धून् च and his relatives, हत्वा having killed, परिपक्वफलान्वितं which was full of ripe fruits, तालवनं the Talavana forest, क्षेमं safe, चक्रे made.

Simple meaning: "Accompanied by Balarama, He killed the daitya Dhenukasura (who appeared as a donkey) along with all his companions. In doing so, He made the Talavana forest safe for everyone to enjoy its abundance of ripe palm fruits."

प्रलम्बं घातयित्वोग्रं बलेन बलशालिना ।

अमोचयद् व्रजपशून् गोपांश्चारण्यवह्नितः ॥10.26.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रलम्बम् Pralambasura, घातयित्वा causing to be killed, उग्रम् the formidable/terrible, बलेन by Balarama, बल-शालिना who is powerfully built/mighty, अमोचयत् He delivered, व्रज-पशून् the animals of Vraja, गोपान् the cowherd boys, च and, अरण्य-वह्नितः from the forest fire

Anvaya: बलशालिना बलेन उग्रं प्रलम्बं घातयित्वा व्रजपशून् गोपान् च अरण्यवह्नितः अमोचयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बलशालिना बलेन By the mighty Balarama, उग्रं प्रलम्बं the terrible Pralamba, घातयित्वा having caused to be killed, व्रजपशून् गोपान् च the livestock of Vraja and the cowherds, अरण्यवह्नितः from the forest fire, अमोचयत् He (Krishna) saved.

Simple meaning: "After causing the formidable Pralambasura to be killed by the mighty Balarama, Krishna then saved the cowherd boys and the animals of Vraja from a terrifying forest fire."

आशीविषतमाहीन्द्रं दमित्वा विमदं ह्रदात् ।

प्रसह्योद्वास्य यमुनां चक्रेऽसौ निर्विषोदकाम् ॥10.26.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: आशीविष-तमम् the most poisonous, अहि-इन्द्रम् the king of snakes (Kaliya), दमित्वा after subduing, विमदम् devoid of pride, ह्रदात् from the lake, प्रसह्य by force, उद्वास्य expelling, यमुनाम् the Yamuna river, चक्रे made, असौ He (Krishna), निर्विष-उदकाम् whose waters are free from poison

Anvaya: असौ आशीविषतम् अहीन्द्रं दमित्वा विमदं (कृत्वा) ह्रदात् प्रसह्य उद्वास्य यमुनां निर्विषोदकां चक्रे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: असौ He (Krishna), आशीविषतम् the most deadly poisonous, अहीन्द्रं king of serpents, दमित्वा having subdued, विमदं (कृत्वा) and stripped of his pride, ह्रदात् from the lake, प्रसह्य by force, उद्वास्य having expelled, यमुनां the Yamuna, निर्विषोदकां free from poisonous water, चक्रे made.

Simple meaning: "He subdued the king of serpents, Kaliya, who was the most venomous of all snakes. After crushing his pride and forcibly expelling him from the lake, Krishna made the waters of the Yamuna pure and free from poison once again."

दुस्त्यजश्चानुरागोऽस्मिन् सर्वेषां नो व्रजौकसाम् ।

नन्द ते तनयेऽस्मासु तस्याप्यौत्पत्तिकः कथम् ॥10.26.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: दुस्त्यजः difficult to abandon, च and, अनुरागः attachment/affection, अस्मिन् for Him, सर्वेषाम् of all, नः our, व्रज-ओकसाम् residents of Vraja, नन्द O Nanda, ते your, तनये for the son, अस्मासु toward us, तस्य His, अपि also, औत्पत्तिकः natural/innate, कथम् how?

Anvaya: नन्द! अस्मिन् ते तनये नः सर्वेषां व्रजौकसाम् अनुरागः दुस्त्यजः। अस्मासु तस्य अपि (अनुरागः) कथम् औत्पत्तिकः?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्द O Nanda, अस्मिन् for Him, ते तनये for your son, नः of us, सर्वेषां of all, व्रजौकसाम् the residents of Vraja, अनुरागः affection, दुस्त्यजः is impossible to give up. अस्मासु For us, तस्य His (affection), अपि also, कथम् how, औत्पत्तिकः is so natural?

Simple meaning: "O Nanda, we find it impossible to give up our deep affection for your son. And how is it that He, too, has such a natural, spontaneous love for us? This mutual bond is truly extraordinary."

क्व सप्तहायनो बालः क्व महाद्रिविधारणम् ।

ततो नो जायते शङ्का व्रजनाथ तवात्मजे ॥10.26.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्व where (on one hand), सप्त-हायनः seven years old, बालः a boy, क्व where (on the other hand), महा-अद्रि-विधारणम् the supporting of a great mountain, ततः therefore, नः our, जायते arises, शङ्का doubt/wonder, व्रज-नाथ O Master of Vraja (Nanda), तव your, आत्मजे regarding the son

Anvaya: सप्तहायनः बालः क्व? महाद्रिविधारणं क्व? व्रजनाथ! ततः तव आत्मजे नः शङ्का जायते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सप्तहायनः बालः क्व Where a seven-year-old boy?, महाद्रिविधारणं क्व Where the holding of a great mountain? व्रजनाथ O Master of Vraja, ततः therefore, तव आत्मजे regarding your son, नः our, शङ्का doubt, जायते arises.

Simple meaning: "Where is the comparison between a small seven-year-old boy and the lifting of a massive mountain? O Nanda, Lord of Vraja, because of this vast difference, we are filled with wonder and doubt regarding the true nature of your son."

नन्द उवाच

श्रूयतां मे वचो गोपा व्येतु शङ्का च वोऽर्भके ।

एनं कुमारमुद्दिश्य गर्गो मे यदुवाच ह ॥10.26.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: नन्दः उवाच Nanda said, श्रूयताम् please hear, मे my, वचः words, गोपाः O cowherds, व्येतु let it disappear, शङ्का doubt, च and, वः your, अर्भके regarding the child, एनम् this, कुमारम् child, उद्दिश्य concerning, गर्गः Garga Muni, मे to me, यत् what, उवाच spoke, ह indeed

Anvaya: नन्द उवाच— हे गोपाः! मे वचः श्रूयताम्। अर्भके वः शङ्का च व्येतु। एनं कुमारम् उद्दिश्य गर्गः मे यत् उवाच (तत् शृणु)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्द उवाच Nanda said— हे गोपाः O cowherds, मे वचः my words, श्रूयताम् please hear. अर्भके Regarding the child, वः शङ्का your doubt, च and, व्येतु let it go away. एनं कुमारम् concerning this child, उद्दिश्य referring to, गर्गः Garga Muni, मे to me, यत् what, उवाच spoke, (hear that).

Simple meaning: Nanda Maharaja replied: "O cowherds, please listen to my words so that all your doubts regarding this child may be removed. I will tell you exactly what the great sage Garga Muni told me concerning this boy."

वर्णास्त्रयः किलास्यासन् गृह्णतोऽनुयुगं तनूः ।

शुक्लो रक्तस्तथा पीत इदानीं कृष्णतां गतः ॥10.26.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: वर्णाः colors, त्रयः three, किल indeed, अस्य of Him, आसन् were, गृह्णतः accepting, अनु-युगम् in each age (Yuga), तनूः bodies, शुक्लः white, रक्तः red, तथा and, पीतः yellow, इदानीम् now, कृष्णताम् blackness/the color Krishna, गतः has attained

Anvaya: अनुयुगं तनूः गृह्णतः अस्य शुक्लः रक्तः तथा पीतः (इति) त्रयः वर्णाः किल आसन्, इदानीं कृष्णतां गतः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अनुयुगं In every age, तनूः bodies, गृह्णतः while accepting, अस्य of Him, शुक्लः white, रक्तः red, तथा and, पीतः yellow, (इति) thus, त्रयः वर्णाः three colors, किल आसन् indeed existed, इदानीं now, कृष्णतां the color black/dark, गतः He has attained.

Simple meaning: "Garga Muni told me: 'This child has previously appeared in every Yuga (age) by accepting different bodies. In those times, He appeared in three different colors—white, red, and yellow. Now, He has appeared in this dark (Krishna) complexion.'"

प्रागयं वसुदेवस्य क्वचिज्जातस्तवात्मजः ।

वासुदेव इति श्रीमानभिज्ञाः सम्प्रचक्षते ॥10.26.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्राक् previously, अयम् this, वसुदेवस्य of Vasudeva, क्वचित् somewhere/at some time, जातः was born, तव your, आत्मजः son, वासुदेवः Vasudeva, इति thus, श्रीमान् the possessor of all opulence, अभिज्ञाः those who are wise/learned, सम्प्रचक्षते they say

Anvaya: तव अयम् आत्मजः प्राक् क्वचित् वसुदेवस्य जातः (आसीत्), (अत एव) श्रीमान् इति अभिज्ञाः वासुदेवः सम्प्रचक्षते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तव अयम् आत्मजः Your, this son, प्राक् previously, क्वचित् at some time, वसुदेवस्य जातः was born of Vasudeva, (अत एव) therefore, अभिज्ञाः the wise people, श्रीमान् वासुदेवः as 'Vasudeva, the glorious one', इति सम्प्रचक्षते thus declare.

Simple meaning: "Garga Muni further told me: 'This son of yours was previously born as the son of Vasudeva. Therefore, those who are wise and learned in the truth refer to this beautiful and opulent child as Vāsudeva.'"

बहूनि सन्ति नामानि रूपाणि च सुतस्य ते ।

गुणकर्मानुरूपाणि तान्यहं वेद नो जनाः ॥10.26.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: बहूनि many, सन्ति there are, नामानि names, रूपाणि forms, च and, सुतस्य of the son, ते your, गुण-कर्म-अनुरूपाणि according to His qualities and activities, तानि those, अहम् I (Garga Muni), वेद know, नो not, जनाः ordinary people

Anvaya: ते सुतस्य गुणकर्मानुरूपाणि बहूनि नामानि रूपाणि च सन्ति। तानि अहं वेद, जनाः नो (विदुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ते सुतस्य Of your son, गुणकर्मानुरूपाणि according to His transcendental qualities and activities, बहूनि नामानि रूपाणि च many names and forms, सन्ति exist. तानि Those, अहं वेद I (Garga Muni) know, जनाः नो ordinary people do not (know).

Simple meaning: "Garga Muni said: 'Your son has many names and many forms, all corresponding to His various qualities and pastimes. I am aware of them, but ordinary people do not know them.'

एषः वः श्रेय आधास्यद् गोपगोकुलनन्दनः ।

अनेन सर्वदुर्गाणि यूयमञ्जस्तरिष्यथ ॥10.26.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: एषः this (child), वः for all of you, श्रेयः supreme fortune/well-being, आधास्यत् will bring/bestow, गोप-गोकुल-नन्दनः the delighter of the cowherds and the community of Gokula, अनेन by Him, सर्व-दुर्गाणि all difficulties/calamities, यूयम् you all, अञ्जः easily, तरिष्यथ will cross over

Anvaya: एषः गोपगोकुलनन्दनः वः श्रेयः आधास्यत्। अनेन यूयं सर्वदुर्गाणि अञ्जः तरिष्यथ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एषः गोपगोकुलनन्दनः This delighter of the cowherds and Gokula, वः for you, श्रेयः supreme auspiciousness, आधास्यत् will provide. अनेन Through Him, यूयं you all, सर्वदुर्गाणि all great dangers, अञ्जः तरिष्यथ easily, shall cross.

Simple meaning: "Garga Muni continued: 'This boy, who brings such joy to the cowherds and all of Gokula, will bestow upon you the highest fortune. By His grace, you will easily cross over all types of obstacles and calamities.'

पुरानेन व्रजपते साधवो दस्युपीडिताः ।

अराजके रक्ष्यमाणा जिग्युर्दस्यून् समेधिताः ॥10.26.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: पुरा in the past, अनेन by Him, व्रज-पते O Master of Vraja, साधवः saintly people, दस्यु-पीडिताः harassed by thieves/criminals, अ-राजके during a time of anarchy (no king), रक्ष्यमाणाः being protected, जिग्युः they conquered, दस्यून् the thieves, समेधिताः having been strengthened

Anvaya: व्रजपते! पुरा अराजके दस्युपीडिताः साधवः अनेन रक्ष्यमाणाः (तथा) समेधिताः (सन्तः) दस्यून् जिग्युः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: व्रजपते O Master of Vraja (Nanda), पुरा अराजके In ancient times, when there was no king, दस्युपीडिताः harassed by bandits, साधवः saintly people, अनेन रक्ष्यमाणाः by Him, being protected, (तथा) and, समेधिताः being empowered, दस्यून् जिग्युः conquered the bandits.

Simple meaning: ‘O Nanda, whenever there was a state of anarchy and saintly people were being harassed by bandits, this very boy protected them. By His protection and the strength He provided, those saintly people were able to overcome their enemies.’

य एतस्मिन् महाभागाः प्रीतिं कुर्वन्ति मानवाः ।

नारयोऽभिभवन्त्येतान् विष्णुपक्षानिवासुराः ॥10.26.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: ये those who, एतस्मिन् for Him (Krishna), महा-भागाः very fortunate, प्रीतिम् love/affection, कुर्वन्ति perform/have, मानवाः human beings, न not, अरयः enemies, अभिभवन्ति overcome/defeat, एतान् them, विष्णु-पक्षान् those on the side of Vishnu, इव like, असुराः the asuras

Anvaya: ये महाभागाः मानवाः एतस्मिन् प्रीतिं कुर्वन्ति, एतान् अरयः न अभिभवन्ति, यथा विष्णुपक्षान् असुराः (न अभिभवन्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ये महाभागाः मानवाः Those highly fortunate human beings, एतस्मिन् प्रीतिं कुर्वन्ति develop love for Him, एतान् them, अरयः न अभिभवन्ति enemies cannot defeat, यथा just as, विष्णुपक्षान् those on the side of Vishnu, असुराः the asuras(cannot defeat).

Simple meaning: "Garga Muni said: 'Those highly fortunate people who develop deep love and affection for this child can never be overcome by their enemies. Just as the asuras can never defeat those who are protected by Lord Vishnu, no one can conquer those who are devoted to Krishna.'

तस्मान्नन्द कुमारोऽयं नारायणसमो गुणैः ।

श्रिया कीर्त्यानुभावेन तत्कर्मसु न विस्मयः ॥10.26.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मात् therefore, नन्द O Nanda, कुमारः boy, अयम् this, नारायण-समः equal to Narayana (the Supreme Lord), गुणैः in qualities, श्रिया in opulence/beauty, कीर्त्या in fame, अनुभावेन in influence/power, तत्-कर्मसु in His activities, न not, विस्मयः wonder/surprise

Anvaya: नन्द! तस्मात् अयं कुमारः गुणैः श्रिया कीर्त्या अनुभावेन (च) नारायणसमः (अस्ति), (अतः) तत्कर्मसु विस्मयः न (कर्तव्यः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नन्द O Nanda, तस्मात् Therefore, अयं कुमारः this boy, गुणैः in qualities, श्रिया in beauty/wealth, कीर्त्या in fame, अनुभावेन in power, नारायणसमः is equal to Narayana. (अतः) Therefore, तत्कर्मसु in His deeds, विस्मयः wonder, न (कर्तव्यः) should not be felt.

Simple meaning: "Garga Muni concluded: 'Therefore, O Nanda, this child of yours is equal to Lord Narayana Himself in terms of His qualities, His beauty, His fame, and His incredible influence. You should not be surprised at any of the miraculous feats He performs.'

इति अद्धा मां समादिश्य गर्गे स्वगृहं गते ।

मन्ये नारायणस्यांशं कृष्णमक्लिष्टकारिणम् ॥10.26.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, अद्धा plainly/directly, माम् to me, समादिश्य after instructing, गर्गे Garga Muni, स्व-गृहम् to his own home, गते having gone, मन्ये I consider/think, नारायणस्य of Lord Narayana, अंशम् a part/expansion (or possessing the same potency), कृष्णम् Krishna, अक्लिष्ट-कारिणम् one who performs tasks without any effort (literally "one who acts without fatigue")

Anvaya: गर्गे इति अद्धा मां समादिश्य स्वगृहं गते (सति), अक्लिष्टकारिणं कृष्णं नारायणस्य अंशं मन्ये।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गर्गे इति अद्धा मां समादिश्य When Garga Muni, having thus plainly instructed me, स्वगृहं गते went to his own home, अक्लिष्टकारिणं कृष्णं Krishna, who performs these effortless deeds, नारायणस्य अंशं an expansion (or equivalent) of Narayana, मन्ये I consider.

Simple Meaning: “After Garga Muni spoke these truths to me and returned to his home, I have come to realize that Krishna is no ordinary child. Because He performs such incredible feats without the slightest effort, I believe He is a direct expansion of Lord Narayana Himself."

इति नन्द वचः श्रुत्वा गर्गगीतं व्रजौकसः ।

दृष्टश्रुतानुभावास्ते कृष्णस्यामिततेजसः ।

मुदिता नन्दमानर्चुः कृष्णं च गतविस्मयाः ॥10.26.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, नन्द-वचः the words of Nanda, श्रुत्वा having heard, गर्ग-गीतम् the words sung (spoken) by Garga Muni, व्रज-ओकसः the residents of Vraja, दृष्ट-श्रुत-अनुभावाः having seen and heard the power, ते they, कृष्णस्य of Krishna, अमित-तेजसः of unlimited potency, मुदिताः being joyful, नन्दम् Nanda, आनर्चुः worshipped/honored, कृष्णम् Krishna, च and, गत-विस्मयाः their wonder (confusion) having disappeared

Anvaya: इति नन्दवचः गर्गगीतं च श्रुत्वा, अमिततेजसः कृष्णस्य दृष्टश्रुतानुभावाः ते व्रजौकसः मुदिताः गतविस्मयाः (सन्तः) नन्दं कृष्णं च आनर्चुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इति नन्दवचः गर्गगीतं च श्रुत्वा Having thus heard the words of Nanda and the speech of Garga Muni, अमिततेजसः कृष्णस्य of the power of Krishna of unlimited potency, दृष्टश्रुतानुभावाः ते व्रजौकसः those residents of Vraja—who had personally seen and heard —मुदिताः गतविस्मयाः (सन्तः) became joyful and their confusion having disappeared, नन्दं कृष्णं च आनर्चुः they honored both Nanda and Krishna.

Simple Meaning: After hearing Nanda relate the prophecies of Garga Muni, the residents of Vraja felt all their doubts and bewilderment vanish. Having both seen Krishna's miracles with their own eyes and now heard the explanation of His divine origin, they were filled with immense joy. They honored Nanda for being the father of such a child and worshipped Krishna with deep affection.

देवे वर्षति यज्ञविप्लवरुषा वज्राश्मपर्षानिलैः

सीदत्पालपशुस्त्रि आत्मशरणं दृष्ट्वानुकम्प्युत्स्मयन् ।

उत्पाट्यैककरेण शैलमबलो लीलोच्छिलीन्ध्रं यथा

बिभ्रद् गोष्ठमपान्महेन्द्रमदभित् प्रीयान्न इन्द्रो गवाम् ॥10.26.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: देवे the demigod (Indra), वर्षति when he sent rain, यज्ञ-विप्लव-रुषा out of anger due to the disruption of his sacrifice, वज्र-अश्म-पर्ष-अनिलैः with thunderbolts, hailstones, heavy rain, and fierce winds, सीदत्-पाल-पशु-स्त्रि the cowherds, animals, and women being distressed, आत्म-शरणम् having taken shelter of Him (Krishna) alone, दृष्ट्वा seeing (them), अनुकम्पी being compassionate, उत्स्मयन् smiling, उत्पाट्य picking up, एक-करेण with one hand, शैलम् the mountain (Govardhan), अबलः a small child (or seemingly without effort), लीला-उच्छिलीन्ध्रम् like a mushroom playfully (picked up), यथा just as, बिभ्रत् holding, गोष्ठम् the cowherd community, अपात् protected, महेन्द्र-मद-भित् the destroyer of the pride of great Indra, प्रीयात् may He be pleased, नः with us, इन्द्रः the Lord, गवाम् of the cows (Govinda)

Anvaya: यज्ञविप्लवरुषा देवे वज्राश्मपर्षानिलैः वर्षति (सति), सीदत्पालपशुस्त्रि आत्मशरणं दृष्ट्वा अनुकम्पी (कृष्णः) उत्स्मयन् अबलः यथा लीलोच्छिलीन्ध्रम् उत्पाट्य एककरेण शैलं बिभ्रत् गोष्ठम् अपात्, सः महेन्द्रमदभित् गवाम् इन्द्रः नः प्रीयात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यज्ञविप्लवरुषा देवे वज्राश्मपर्षानिलैः वर्षति (सति) When Indra, angry because his sacrifice was frustrated, sent forth rain accompanied by thunderbolts, hail, and wind, सीदत्पालपशुस्त्रि आत्मशरणं दृष्ट्वा seeing the cowherds, animals, and women in distress and having no other shelter but Himself, अनुकम्पी (कृष्णः) उत्स्मयन् the compassionate Krishna, smiling, अबलः यथा लीलोच्छिलीन्ध्रम् उत्पाट्य just like a child picking up a mushroom in play, एककरेण शैलं बिभ्रत् गोष्ठम् अपात् holding the mountain with one hand, protected the cowherd community. सः महेन्द्रमदभित् गवाम् इन्द्रः नः प्रीयात् May that Lord of the cows, the destroyer of Indra's false pride, be pleased with us.

Simple Meaning: When Indra's pride was hurt by the cancellation of his sacrifice, he unleashed a devastating storm of hail and thunder upon Vraja. Seeing the residents and animals shivering and helpless with no one to turn to but Him, the compassionate Krishna smiled and effortlessly lifted Govardhan Hill with one hand, just as a child might pick up a mushroom. By holding the mountain aloft, He shielded the entire community and crushed Indra's arrogance. May that Supreme Lord of the cows, Govinda, be pleased with us.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “नन्दगोपानां श्रीकृष्णकीर्तिविषये संवादः नाम” षड्विंशः अध्यायः ॥26॥
