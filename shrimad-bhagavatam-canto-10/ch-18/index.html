Shrimad Bhagwatam - Book 1

Canto 10

Chapter 18

The Lord slays Pralamba

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

प्रलम्बवधो नाम अष्टादशः अध्यायः

(32 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

अथ कृष्णः परिवृतो ज्ञातिभिर्मुदितात्मभिः ।

अनुगीयमानो न्यविशद् व्रजं गोकुलमण्डितम् ॥10.18.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, कृष्णः Krishna, परिवृतः surrounded, ज्ञातिभिः by His relatives, मुदित-आत्मभिः whose minds/souls were joyful, अनुगीयमानः being sung about, न्यविशत् entered, व्रजम् Vraja, गोकुल-मण्डितम् decorated by the cows and cowherds

Anvaya: अथ मुदितात्मभिः ज्ञातिभिः परिवृतः अनुगीयमानः कृष्णः गोकुलमण्डितं व्रजं न्यविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, मुदितात्मभिः ज्ञातिभिः परिवृतः surrounded by His joyful relatives, अनुगीयमानः being praised in song, कृष्णः Krishna, गोकुलमण्डितं व्रजं the village of Vraja which is the graced by the herds of cows, न्यविशत् entered.

Simple meaning: Then, surrounded by His joyful relatives and being glorified by their songs, Lord Krishna entered the village of Vraja, which was beautifully adorned by the community of cows and cowherds.

व्रजे विक्रीडतोरेवं गोपालच्छद्ममायया ।

ग्रीष्मो नामर्तुरभवन्नातिप्रेयाञ्छरीरिणाम् ॥10.18.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: व्रजे in Vraja, विक्रीडतोः of the two (Krishna and Balarama) who were playing, एवम् in this way, गोपाल-छद्म-मायया by the deceptive appearance of being cowherd boys, ग्रीष्मः summer, नाम named, ऋतुः the season, अभवत् arrived/became manifest, न अति-प्रेयान् not very dear/pleasant, शरीरिणाम् for embodied living beings

Anvaya: एवं गोपालच्छद्ममायया व्रजे विक्रीडतोः शरीरिणां न अतिप्रेयान् ग्रीष्मः नाम ऋतुः अभवत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं In this way, गोपालच्छद्ममायया through the illusory appearance of cowherd boys, व्रजे विक्रीडतोः while the two of them were playing in Vraja, शरीरिणां न अतिप्रेयान् not very pleasant for embodied beings, ग्रीष्मः नाम ऋतुः the season named summer, अभवत् arrived.

Simple meaning: While Krishna and Balarama were playing in Vraja in the guise of ordinary cowherd boys, the summer season arrived. This season is generally not very pleasant for living entities due to its intense heat.

स च वृन्दावनगुणैर्वसन्त इव लक्षितः ।

यत्रास्ते भगवान् साक्षाद् रामेण सह केशवः ॥10.18.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः that (summer), च and, वृन्दावन-गुणैः by the special qualities of Vrindavana, वसन्तः spring, इव like, लक्षितः perceived/appeared, यत्र where, आस्ते stays, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, साक्षात् personally, रामेण सह along with Balarama, केशवः Krishna

Anvaya: यत्र रामेण सह साक्षात् भगवान् केशवः आस्ते सः च वृन्दावनगुणैः वसन्तः इव लक्षितः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र रामेण सह Where along with Balarama, साक्षात् भगवान् केशवः the Supreme Lord Keshava Himself, आस्ते resides, सः च that (summer season) also, वृन्दावनगुणैः by the qualities of Vrindavana, वसन्तः इव लक्षितः appeared just like spring.

Simple meaning: Because the Supreme Lord Krishna, was personally residing there along with Balarama, the intense heat of summer was transformed. Due to the transcendental qualities of Vrindavana, the summer season actually felt as pleasant and refreshing as spring.

यत्र निर्झरनिर्ह्रादनिवृत्तस्वनझिल्लिकम् ।

शश्वत्तच्छीकरर्जीषद्रुममण्डलमण्डितम् ॥10.18.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: यत्र where, निर्झर-निर्ह्राद-निवृत्त-स्वन-झिल्लिकम् where the sound of the crickets (झिल्लिक) was silenced (निवृत्त) (स्वन) by the booming sound (निर्ह्राद) of the waterfalls (निर्झर), शश्वत्-तत्-शीकर-ऋजीष-द्रुम-मण्डल-मण्डितम् constantly (शश्वत्) decorated (मण्डितम्) by groups of trees (द्रुम-मण्डल) moistened by the cool spray of water (शीकर-ऋजीष) of those (waterfalls)

Anvaya: यत्र निर्झरनिर्ह्रादनिवृत्तस्वनझिल्लिकं शश्वत्तच्छीकरर्जीषद्रुममण्डलमण्डितम् (अभवत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र Where, निर्झरनिर्ह्रादनिवृत्तस्वनझिल्लिकं the noise of the crickets was drowned out by the resounding sound of the waterfalls, शश्वत्तच्छीकरर्जीषद्रुममण्डलमण्डितम् and the area was constantly adorned by clusters of trees kept fresh by the fine spray of those falls.

Simple meaning: In that forest, the loud buzzing of crickets was completely drowned out by the crashing sound of waterfalls. The entire area was beautifully adorned with groves of trees that remained lush and cool, being constantly sprayed by the mist from the falling water.

सरित्सरःप्रस्रवणोर्मिवायुना कह्लारकञ्जोत्पलरेणुहारिणा ।

न विद्यते यत्र वनौकसां दवो निदाघवह्न्यर्कभवोऽतिशाद्वले ॥10.18.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: सरित्-सरः-प्रस्रवण-ऊर्मि-वायुना by the breeze from the waves of the rivers, lakes, and waterfalls, कह्लार-कञ्ज-उत्पल-रेणु-हारिणा carrying the pollen of the white, pink, and blue lotuses, न not, विद्यते is felt/exists, यत्र where, वन-ओकसाम् for the inhabitants of the forest, दवः the distress/heat, निदाघ-वह्नि-अर्क-भवः born from the summer heat and the sun, अति-शाद्वले in that place of lush green grass

Anvaya: यत्र कह्लारकञ्जोत्पलरेणुहारिणा सरित्सरःप्रस्रवणोर्मिवायुना अतिशाद्वले वनौकसां निदाघवह्न्यर्कभवः दवः न विद्यते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र Where, कह्लारकञ्जोत्पलरेणुहारिणा carrying the pollen of various lotuses, सरित्सरःप्रस्रवणोर्मिवायुना by the breeze coming from the waves of the rivers, lakes, and waterfalls, अतिशाद्वले in that land of lush green grass, वनौकसां for the forest dwellers, निदाघवह्न्यर्कभवः दवः the scorching distress caused by the summer sun and fire, न विद्यते does not exist.

Simple meaning: In that forest, which was covered in lush green grass, the inhabitants never felt the scorching heat of the summer sun. This was because a cooling breeze, moistened by the waves of rivers, lakes, and waterfalls, blew throughout the woods, carrying the sweet fragrance and pollen of many kinds of lotuses.

अगाधतोयह्रदिनीतटोर्मिभिर्द्रवत्पुरीष्याः पुलिनैः समन्ततः ।

न यत्र चण्डांशुकरा विषोल्बणा भुवो रसं शाद्वलितं च गृह्णते ॥10.18.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: अगाध-तोय-ह्रदिनी-तट-ऊर्मिभिः by the waves touching the banks of the rivers containing very deep water, द्रवत्-पुरीष्याः with soil that is kept moist, पुलिनैः by the sandy beaches, समन्ततः on all sides, न not, यत्र where, चण्ड-अंशु-कराः the rays of the sun (the fierce-rayed one), विष-उल्बणाः as fierce as poison, भुवः of the earth, रसम् the moisture, शाद्वलितम् the green grass, च and, गृह्णते take away/dry up

Anvaya: यत्र अगाधतोयह्रदिनीतटोर्मिभिः समन्ततः द्रवत्पुरीष्याः पुलिनैः चण्डांशुकराः विषोल्बणाः भुवः रसं शाद्वलितं च न गृह्णते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र Where, अगाधतोयह्रदिनीतटोर्मिभिः because of the waves from the deep-water rivers hitting the banks, समन्ततः द्रवत्पुरीष्याः पुलिनैः and the sandy banks being moist on all sides, चण्डांशुकराः विषोल्बणाः the fierce rays of the sun which are like poison, भुवः रसं the moisture of the earth, शाद्वलितं च and the green grass, न गृह्णते do not snatch away.

Simple meaning: In Vrindavana, the rivers are very deep, and their waves constantly dampen the shore. Because the sandy banks are kept perpetually moist by this water, the fierce rays of the summer sun—even though they are as burning as poison—cannot dry up the earth’s moisture or wither the lush green grass.

वनं कुसुमितं श्रीमन्नदच्चित्रमृगद्विजम् ।

गायन्मयूरभ्रमरं कूजत्कोकिलसारसम् ॥10.18.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: वनम् the forest, कुसुमितम् full of flowers, श्रीमत् beautiful/opulent, नदत्-चित्र-मृग-द्विजम् where various (चित्र) animals (मृग) and birds (द्विज) were making sounds (नदत्), गायत्-मयूर-भ्रमरम् where the peacocks (मयूर) and bees (भ्रमर) were singing (गायत्), कूजत्-कोकिल-सारसम् where the cuckoos (कोकिल) and cranes (सारस) were cooing/calling (कूजत्)

Anvaya: (तद्) वनं कुसुमितं श्रीमत् नदच्चित्रमृगद्विजं गायन्मयूरभ्रमरं कूजत्कोकिलसारसम् (आसीत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (तद्) वनं That forest, कुसुमितं was filled with flowers, श्रीमत् beautiful, नदच्चित्रमृगद्विजं with various animals and birds resounding, गायन्मयूरभ्रमरं with peacocks and bees singing, कूजत्कोकिलसारसम् and with cuckoos and cranes chirping.

Simple meaning: That forest of Vrindavana was filled with blooming flowers and looked most beautiful. It resounded with the diverse sounds of various animals and birds; peacocks and bees sang joyfully, while cuckoos and cranes filled the air with their sweet calls.

क्रीडिष्यमाणस्तत् कृष्णो भगवान् बलसंयुतः ।

वेणुं विरणयन् गोपैर्गोधनैः संवृतोऽविशत् ॥10.18.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्रीडिष्यमाणः desiring to play, तत् that (forest), कृष्णः Krishna, भगवान् Lord, बल-संयुतः along with Balarama, वेणुम् His flute, विरणयन् vibrating/playing loudly, गोपैः by the cowherd boys, गो-धनैः by the wealth of cows, संवृतः surrounded, अविशत् entered

Anvaya: अथ बलसंयुतः भगवान् कृष्णः क्रीडिष्यमाणः वेणुं विरणयन् गोपैः गोधनैः च संवृतः तत् वनम् अविशत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, बलसंयुतः accompanied by Balarama, भगवान् कृष्णः Lord Krishna, क्रीडिष्यमाणः desiring to play, वेणुं विरणयन् playing His flute, गोपैः गोधनैः च संवृतः surrounded by the cowherd boys and the wealth of cows, तत् वनम् अविशत् entered that forest.

Simple meaning: Then, Lord Krishna, accompanied by Balarama and surrounded by His cowherd boyfriends and their many herds of cows desiring to play, entered the forest.

प्रवालबर्हस्तबकस्रग्धातुकृतभूषणाः ।

रामकृष्णादयो गोपा ननृतुर्युयुधुर्जगुः ॥10.18.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रवाल-बर्ह-स्तबक-स्रग्-धातु-कृत-भूषणाः decorated with young sprouts, peacock feathers, flower bouquets, garlands, and mineral pigments, राम-कृष्ण-आदयः Balarama, Krishna, and the others, गोपाः the cowherd boys, ननृतुः danced, युयुधुः wrestled/fought, जगुः sang

Anvaya: प्रवालबर्हस्तबकस्रग्धातुकृतभूषणाः रामकृष्णादयः गोपाः ननृतुः युयुधुः जगुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रवालबर्हस्तबकस्रग्धातुकृतभूषणाः Having made their ornaments from sprouts, peacock feathers, bunches of flowers, garlands, and colorful minerals, रामकृष्णादयः गोपाः the cowherd boys headed by Balarama and Krishna, ननृतुः danced, युयुधुः wrestled, जगुः sang.

Simple meaning: The cowherd boys, led by Balarama and Krishna, decorated themselves with fresh sprouts, peacock feathers, bouquets, flower garlands, and colorful forest minerals. Thus adorned, they danced, wrestled, and sang together.

कृष्णस्य नृत्यतः केचिज्जगुः केचिदवादयन् ।

वेणुपाणितलैः शृङ्गैः प्रशशंसुरथापरे ॥10.18.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृष्णस्य of Krishna, नृत्यतः who was dancing, केचित् some (boys), जगुः sang, केचित् some, अवादयन् played (instruments), वेणु-पाणि-तलैः with flutes and the palms of their hands, शृङ्गैः with horns, प्रशशंसुः offered praise, अथ and, अपरे others

Anvaya: कृष्णस्य नृत्यतः केचित् जगुः केचित् वेणुपाणितलैः शृङ्गैः अवादयन् अथ अपरे प्रशशंसुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: कृष्णस्य नृत्यतः While Krishna was dancing, केचित् जगुः some sang, केचित् वेणुपाणितलैः शृङ्गैः अवादयन् some played music with flutes, their palms, and horns, अथ अपरे प्रशशंसुः and others offered words of praise.

Simple meaning: While Krishna danced, some of the boys sang and others played music using their flutes, horns, and by clapping their hands. Still others offered Him words of encouragement and praise.

गोपजातिप्रतिच्छन्नौ देवा गोपालरूपिणः ।

ईडिरे कृष्णरामौ च नटा इव नटं नृप ॥10.18.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप-जाति-प्रतिच्छन्नौ disguised in the community of cowherds, देवाः demigods, गोपाल-रूपिणः appearing as cowherd boys, ईडिरे they worshipped/offered praise, कृष्ण-रामौ to Krishna and Balarama, च and, नटाः actors, इव like, नटम् a (master) actor, नृप O King (Parikshit)

Anvaya: नृप! नटम् इव नटाः गोपालरूपिणः देवाः गोपजातिप्रतिच्छन्नौ कृष्णरामौ ईडिरे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, नटम् इव नटाः Like actors praising a master actor, गोपालरूपिणः देवाः the demigods appearing in the forms of cowherds, गोपजातिप्रतिच्छन्नौ disguised within the cowherd community, कृष्णरामौ ईडिरे offered praise to Krishna and Balarama.

Simple meaning: O King Parikshit! Just as professional actors honor a master performer, these demigods appearing in the forms of cowherds offered their worship and praise to Krishna and Balarama who were also disguised within the cowherd community.

भ्रामणैर्लङ्घनैः क्षेपैरास्फोटनविकर्षणैः ।

चिक्रीडतुर्नियुद्धेन काकपक्षधरौ क्वचित् ॥10.18.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: भ्रामणैः by whirling each other around, लङ्घनैः by leaping, क्षेपैः by throwing, आस्फोटन-विकर्षणैः by slapping their own arms and pulling each other, चिक्रीडतुः the two of them played, नियुद्धेन by wrestling, काक-पक्ष-धरौ wearing side-locks of hair (like crow wings), क्वचित् sometimes

Anvaya: काकपक्षधरौ (तौ) क्वचित् भ्रामणैः लङ्घनैः क्षेपैः आस्फोटनविकर्षणैः नियुद्धेन च चिक्रीडतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: काकपक्षधरौ Wearing their hair in side-locks, तौ those two (Krishna and Balarama), क्वचित् sometimes, भ्रामणैः लङ्घनैः क्षेपैः by whirling, leaping, and throwing, आस्फोटनविकर्षणैः by slapping their arms and pulling, नियुद्धेन च चिक्रीडतुः and by wrestling, they played.

Simple meaning: Krishna and Balarama, their hair styled in youthful side-locks, played together by whirling each other around, leaping, throwing one another, slapping their arms in challenge, and pulling each other as they engaged in wrestling matches.

क्वचिन्नृत्यत्सु चान्येषु गायकौ वादकौ स्वयम् ।

शशंसतुर्महाराज साधु साध्विति वादिनौ ॥10.18.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्वचित् sometimes, नृत्यत्सु while dancing, च and, अन्येषु others, गायकौ the two singers, वादकौ the two instrumentalists, स्वयम् themselves, शशंसतुः they both praised, महाराज O Great King (Parikshit), साधु साधु "Excellent! Excellent!", इति thus, वादिनौ saying

Anvaya: महाराज! क्वचित् अन्येषु नृत्यत्सु स्वयं गायकौ वादकौ च साधु साधु इति वादिनौ शशंसतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महाराज O Great King, क्वचित् अन्येषु नृत्यत्सु sometimes while others were dancing, स्वयं गायकौ वादकौ च themselves acting as the singers and instrumentalists, साधु साधु इति वादिनौ saying "Well done! Well done!", शशंसतुः they both offered praise.

Simple meaning: O King! Sometimes while the other cowherd boys danced, Krishna and Balarama would personally act as the singers and musicians. While performing this music, They would encourage Their friends by shouting, "Bravo! Well done!"

क्वचिद् बिल्वैः क्वचित् कुम्भैः क्व चामलकमुष्टिभिः ।

अस्पृश्यनेत्रबन्धाद्यैः क्वचिन्मृगखगेहया ॥10.18.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्वचित् sometimes, बिल्वैः with Bilva fruits, क्वचित् sometimes, कुम्भैः with Kumbha (Kapittha) fruits, क्व and sometimes, आमलक-मुष्टिभिः with handfuls of amalaka fruits, अस्पृश्य-नेत्र-बन्ध-आद्यैः by games such as touching the "untouchable" (blindman’s buff) or tying the eyes, क्वचित् sometimes, मृग-खग-ईहया by imitating the movements of animals and birds

Anvaya: क्वचित् बिल्वैः क्वचित् कुम्भैः क्व च आमलकमुष्टिभिः अस्पृश्यनेत्रबन्धाद्यैः क्वचित् मृगखगेहया (चिक्रीडतुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: क्वचित् बिल्वैः Sometimes with Bilva fruits, क्वचित् कुम्भैः sometimes with Kumbha fruits, क्व च आमलकमुष्टिभिः and sometimes with handfuls of amalaka fruits, अस्पृश्यनेत्रबन्धाद्यैः by games like blindman's buff and others, क्वचित् मृगखगेहया sometimes by mimicking the activities of deer and birds, (they played).

Simple meaning: Sometimes they played with Bilva or Kumbha fruits, and sometimes with handfuls of Amalaka fruits. They also played games like blindman's buff, or spent their time mimicking the graceful movements of the forest animals and birds.

क्वचिच्च दर्दुरप्लावैर्विविधैरुपहासकैः ।

कदाचित् स्पन्दोलिकया कर्हिचिन्नृपचेष्टया ॥10.18.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्वचित् sometimes, च and, दर्दुर-प्लावैः with frog-leaps, विविधैः with various, उपहासकैः with jests/mocking antics, कदाचित् sometimes, स्पन्दोलिकया with swinging/on a swing, कर्हिचित् sometimes, नृप-चेष्टया by acting like kings

Anvaya: क्वचित् च दर्दुरप्लावैः विविधैः उपहासकैः कदाचित् स्पन्दोलिकया कर्हिचित् नृपचेष्टया (चिक्रीडतुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: क्वचित् च And sometimes, दर्दुरप्लावैः with frog-leaps, विविधैः उपहासकैः with various joking antics, कदाचित् स्पन्दोलिकया sometimes with swinging on the vines, कर्हिचित् नृपचेष्टया sometimes by playing the role of kings, (they played).

Simple meaning: Sometimes they would hop like frogs or engage in various funny antics to make each other laugh. At other times, they would play on swings or act out the roles of kings and subjects among themselves.

एवं तौ लोकसिद्धाभिः कीडाभिश्चेरतुर्वने ।

नद्यद्रिद्रोणिकुञ्जेषु काननेषु सरस्सु च ॥10.18.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् in this way, तौ those two (Krishna and Balarama), लोक-सिद्धाभिः with games well-known in the world, क्रीडाभिः with sporting activities, चेरतुः wandered/played, वने in the forest, नदी-अद्रि-द्रोणि-कुञ्जेषु in the rivers, mountains, valleys, and bowers, काननेषु in the groves, सरस्सु in the lakes, च and

Anvaya: एवं तौ लोकसिद्धाभिः क्रीडाभिः वने नद्यद्रिद्रोणिकुञ्जेषु काननेषु सरस्सु च चेरतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: एवं In this way, तौ those two, लोकसिद्धाभिः क्रीडाभिः with games popular among the common people, वने in the forest, नद्यद्रिद्रोणिकुञ्जेषु among the rivers, mountains, valleys, and bushes, काननेषु सरस्सु च and in the gardens and lakes, चेरतुः wandered and played.

Simple meaning: In this manner, Krishna and Balarama traversed the forest, engaging in many well-known games. They played amidst the rivers, mountains, valleys, and flower-filled bowers, as well as in the various groves and lakes of Vrindavana.

पशूंश्चारयतोर्गोपैस्तद्वने रामकृष्णयोः ।

गोपरूपी प्रलम्बोऽगादसुरस्तज्जिहीर्षया ॥10.18.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: पशून् the animals (cows), चारयतोः while tending, गोपैः with the cowherd boys, तत्-वने in that forest, राम-कृष्णयोः of Balarama and Krishna, गोप-रूपी disguised as a cowherd boy, प्रलम्बः Pralamba, अगात् came, असुरः the asura, तत्-जिहीर्षया with the desire to kidnap them

Anvaya: तत् वने गोपैः पशून् चारयतोः रामकृष्णयोः तज्जिहीर्षया प्रलम्बः नाम असुरः गोपरूपी अगात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् वने In that forest, गोपैः पशून् चारयतोः रामकृष्णयोः while the two of them, Balarama and Krishna, were tending the cows with the cowherd boys, तज्जिहीर्षया with the intent to kidnap them, प्रलम्बः नाम असुरः an asura named Pralamba, गोपरूपी disguised as a cowherd, अगात् arrived.

Simple meaning: While Krishna and Balarama were tending the cows in the forest along with the other cowherd boys, an asura named Pralamba arrived there. Disguising himself as a cowherd boy, he came with the intent of kidnapping them.

तं विद्वानपि दाशार्हो भगवान् सर्वदर्शनः ।

अन्वमोदत तत्सख्यं वधं तस्य विचिन्तयन् ॥10.18.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (Pralambasura), विद्वान् knowing, अपि although, दाशार्हः the descendant of Dasharha (Krishna), भगवान् Lord, सर्व-दर्शनः who sees everything/all-knowing, अन्वमोदत accepted, तत्-सख्यम् his friendship, वधम् the killing, तस्य of him, विचिन्तयन् considering/planning

Anvaya: सर्वदर्शनः भगवान् दाशार्हः तं विद्वान् अपि तस्य वधं विचिन्तयन् तत्सख्यम् अन्वमोदत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सर्वदर्शनः The all-seeing, भगवान् दाशार्हः Supreme Lord Krishna, descendant of Dasharha, तं विद्वान् अपि although knowing him (to be an asura), तस्य वधं विचिन्तयन् thinking of his death, तत्सख्यम् अन्वमोदत accepted his friendship.

Simple meaning: Although, Lord Krishna, is all-knowing and understood perfectly that the newcomer was an asura, He nonetheless accepted him as a friend. While doing so, He began to plan the asura’s execution.

तत्रोपाहूय गोपालान् कृष्णः प्राह विहारवित् ।

हे गोपा विहरिष्यामो द्वन्द्वीभूय यथायथम् ॥10.18.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there, उपाहूय calling together, गोपालान् the cowherd boys, कृष्णः Krishna, प्राह said, विहार-वित् who knows how to play (the expert at sporting), हे गोपाः O cowherd boys, विहरिष्यामीः let us play/we shall sport, द्वन्द्वी-भूय by dividing into pairs, यथा-यथम् appropriately

Anvaya: तत्र विहारवित् कृष्णः गोपालान् उपाहूय प्राह—हे गोपाः! यथायथं द्वन्द्वीभूय विहरिष्यामीः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र There, विहारवित् कृष्णः Krishna, the expert in play, गोपालान् उपाहूय having called the cowherd boys, प्राह said— हे गोपाः O cowherd boys! यथायथं द्वन्द्वीभूय having divided into appropriate pairs, विहरिष्यामीः we shall play.

Simple meaning: Being an expert in all types of sporting activities, Lord Krishna called all the cowherd boys together and suggested, "O friends, let's have some fun! We should divide ourselves into two teams and play according to our pairs."

तत्र चक्रुः परिवृढौ गोपा रामजनार्दनौ ।

कृष्णसंघट्टिनः केचिदासन् रामस्य चापरे ॥10.18.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत्र there, चक्रुः they made, परिवृढौ the two leaders, गोपाः the cowherd boys, राम-जनार्दनौ Balarama and Krishna (Janardana), कृष्ण-संघट्टिनः on Krishna’s side, केचित् some, आसन् were, रामस्य of Balarama, च and, अपरे others

Anvaya: तत्र गोपाः रामजनार्दनौ परिवृढौ चक्रुः। केचित् कृष्णसंघट्टिनः आसन् अपरे च रामस्य (संघट्टिनः आसन्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत्र There, गोपाः the cowherd boys, रामजनार्दनौ परिवृढौ चक्रुः made Balarama and Krishna the two leaders (captains). केचित् Some, कृष्णसंघट्टिनः आसन् were on Krishna’s team, अपरे च and others, रामस्य (आसन्) were on Balarama's team.

Simple meaning: The cowherd boys then appointed Balarama and Krishna as the captains of the two teams. Some of the boys joined Krishna's side, while the others joined Balarama's side.

आचेरुर्विविधाः क्रीडा वाह्यवाहकलक्षणाः ।

यत्रारोहन्ति जेतारो वहन्ति च पराजिताः ॥10.18.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: आचेरुः they performed, विविधाः various, क्रीडाः games, वाह्य-वाहक-लक्षणाः characterized by the carrier and the carried, यत्र in which, आरोहन्ति mount/ride, जेतारः the winners, वहन्ति carry, च and, पराजिताः the defeated

Anvaya: (ते) वाह्यवाहकलक्षणाः विविधाः क्रीडाः आचेरुः यत्र जेतारः आरोहन्ति पराजिताः च वहन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ते) They, वाह्यवाहकलक्षणाः विविधाः क्रीडाः various games marked by being the carrier or the carried, आचेरुः performed, यत्र in which, जेतारः आरोहन्ति the winners ride, पराजिताः च वहन्ति and the defeated carry (them).

Simple meaning: They began playing various games based on the "carrier and the carried" rule. According to the stakes, the winners would climb onto the backs of the losers, and the defeated boys would have to carry them.

वहन्तो वाह्यमानश्च चारयन्तश्च गोधनम् ।

भाण्डीरकं नाम वटं जग्मुः कृष्णपुरोगमाः ॥10.18.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: वहन्तः those carrying, वाह्यमानाः those being carried, च and, चारयन्तः while tending/herding, च and, गो-धनम् the cows (the wealth of cattle), भाण्डीरकं नाम named Bhandiraka, वटम् the banyan tree, जग्मुः they went, कृष्ण-पुरोगमाः with Krishna in the lead

Anvaya: वहन्तः वाह्यमानाः च गोधनं चारयन्तः च कृष्णपुरोगमाः भाण्डीरकं नाम वटं जग्मुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: वहन्तः वाह्यमानाः च Carrying and being carried, गोधनं चारयन्तः च and while tending the cows, कृष्णपुरोगमाः with Krishna at their head, भाण्डीरकं नाम वटं जग्मुः they went to the banyan tree named Bhandiraka.

Simple meaning: Carrying one another and being carried in turn, and all the while herding their cows, the boys followed Krishna to a famous banyan tree known as Bhandiraka.

रामसङ्घट्टिनो यर्हि श्रीदामवृषभादयः ।

क्रीडायां जयिनस्तांस्तानूहुः कृष्णादयो नृप ॥10.18.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: राम-सङ्घट्टिनः on Balarama's side, यर्हि when, श्रीदाम-वृषभ-आदयः Shridama, Vrishabha, and others, क्रीडायाम् in the game, जयिनः (became) the winners, तान् तान् each of them, ऊहुः carried, कृष्ण-आदयः Krishna and His teammates, नृप O King

Anvaya: नृप यर्हि क्रीडायां रामसङ्घट्टिनः श्रीदामवृषभादयः जयिनः (अभवन्), (तर्हि) कृष्णादयः तान् तान् ऊहुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King!, यर्हि When, क्रीडायां in the game, रामसङ्घट्टिनः those on Balarama’s side, श्रीदामवृषभादयः such as Shridama and Vrishabha, जयिनः (अभवन्) became the winners, (तर्हि) then, कृष्णादयः Krishna and the others on His team, तान् तान् ऊहुः carried each of them (on their backs).

Simple meaning: O King! When Balarama's team—including boys like Shridama and Vrishabha—emerged victorious in the game, Krishna and the boys on His side had to carry the winners on their backs.

उवाह कृष्णो भगवान् श्रीदामानं पराजितः।

वृषभं भद्रसेनस्तु प्रलम्बो रोहिणीसुतम् ॥10.18.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: उवाह carried, कृष्णः Krishna, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, श्रीदामानम् Shridama, पराजितः having been defeated, वृषभम् Vrishabha, भद्रसेनः Bhadrasena, तु indeed, प्रलम्बः the Pralamba, रोहिणी-सुतम् the son of Rohini (Balarama)

Anvaya: पराजितः भगवान् कृष्णः श्रीदामानम् उवाह, भद्रसेनः तु वृषभम् (उवाह), प्रलम्बः रोहिणीसुतम् (उवाह)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पराजितः Having been defeated, भगवान् कृष्णः the Supreme Lord Krishna, श्रीदामानम् उवाह carried Shridama, भद्रसेनः तु and Bhadrasena, वृषभम् (उवाह) carried Vrishabha, प्रलम्बः रोहिणीसुतम् (उवाह) and Pralamba carried Balarama, the son of Rohini.

Simple meaning: Having lost the game, the Supreme Lord Krishna carried Shridama on His back, while Bhadrasena carried Vrishabha. The Pralamba, also being on the losing side, carried Balarama, the son of Rohini.

अविषह्यं मन्यमानः कृष्णं दानवपुङ्गवः ।

वहन् द्रुततरं प्रागादवरोहणतः परम् ॥10.18.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: अविषह्यम् invincible/unbearable, मन्यमानः considering, कृष्णम् Krishna, दानव-पुङ्गवः the powerful asura (Pralamba), वहन् carrying, द्रुत-तरम् more quickly, प्रागात् went ahead, अवरोहणतः (the place of) dismounting, परम् beyond

Anvaya: दानवपुङ्गवः कृष्णम् अविषह्यं मन्यमानः (रामं) वहन् अवरोहणतः परं द्रुततरं प्रागात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: दानवपुङ्गवः The powerful asura Pralamba, कृष्णम् अविषह्यं मन्यमानः considering Krishna to be invincible/unbeatable, (रामं) वहन् while carrying Balarama, अवरोहणतः परं beyond the designated dismounting point, द्रुततरं प्रागात् went ahead very rapidly.

Simple meaning: Thinking Krishna to be too powerful to overcome, the asura Pralamba decided to target Balarama instead. While carrying Balarama on his back, he didn't stop at the agreed-upon finish line but instead rushed past it at great speed.

तमुद्वहन् धरणिधरेन्द्रगौरवं महासुरो विगतरयो निजं वपुः।

स आस्थितः पुरटपरिच्छदो बभौ तडिद्द्युमानुडुपतिवाडिवाम्बुदः ॥10.18.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him (Balarama), उद्वहन् carrying, धरणि-धर-इन्द्र-गौरवम् having the weight of the king of mountains (Mount Sumeru), महा-असुरः the great asura, विगत-रयः his speed being slowed, निजम् his own, वपुः body, सः he, आस्थितः assumed, पुरट-परिच्छदः wearing golden ornaments, बभौ shone, तडिद्-द्युमान् possessing the brilliance of lightning, उडु-पति-वाट् carrying the Lord of the stars (the Moon), इव like, अम्बुदः a cloud

Anvaya: धरणिधरेन्द्रगौरवं तम् उद्वहन् विगतरयः सः महासुरः निजं वपुः आस्थितः। पुरटपरिच्छदः (सः) तडिद्द्युमान् उडुपतिवाट् अम्बुदः इव बभौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: धरणिधरेन्द्रगौरवं तम् उद्वहन् Carrying Him (Balarama), who had the weight of the king of mountains, विगतरयः with his speed slowed down, सः महासुरः that great asura, निजं वपुः आस्थितः assumed his original body. पुरटपरिच्छदः (सः) Decorated with golden ornaments, (he) तडिद्द्युमान् like a brilliant lightning-filled, उडुपतिवाट् अम्बुदः इव cloud carrying the moon, बभौ shone.

Simple meaning: As Balarama made Himself as heavy as the king of mountains, the great asura Pralamba found his speed slowed down. He then assumed his massive original form, adorned with golden ornaments. In that state, the dark-bodied asura looked like a massive cloud carrying the brilliant moon (Balarama) and streaked with the light of golden jewelry that resembled lightning.

निरीक्ष्य तद्वपुरलमम्बरे चरत् प्रदीप्तदृग् भ्रुकुटितटोग्रदंष्ट्रकम् ।

ज्वलच्छिखं कटककिरीटकुण्डलत्विषाद्भुतं हलधर ईषदत्रसत् ॥10.18.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: निरीक्ष्य observing, तत्-वपुः that body, अलम् greatly, अम्बरे in the sky, चरत् moving, प्रदीप्त-दृक् with glowing eyes, भ्रुकुटि-तट-उग्र-दंष्ट्रकम् with furrowed eyebrows and fierce teeth, ज्वलत्-शिखम् with flaming hair, कटक-किरीट-कुण्डल-त्विषा-अद्भुतम् wonderful/terrifying with the glow of bracelets, crown, and earrings, हल-धरः the wielder of the plow (Balarama), ईषत् slightly, अत्रसत् became bewildered/startled

Anvaya: अम्बरे चरत् प्रदीप्तदृग् भ्रुकुटितटोग्रदंष्ट्रकं ज्वलच्छिखं कटककिरीटकुण्डलत्विषा अद्भुतं तत् वपुः निरीक्ष्य हलधरः ईषत् अत्रसत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अम्बरे चरत् Moving through the sky, प्रदीप्तदृग् with blazing eyes, भ्रुकुटितटोग्रदंष्ट्रकं with a face fierce due to arched eyebrows and sharp teeth, ज्वलच्छिखं with hair like fire, कटककिरीटकुण्डलत्विषा अद्भुतं and made extraordinary by the radiance of his armlets, crown, and earrings— तत् वपुः निरीक्ष्य seeing that form, हलधरः Balarama, ईषत् अत्रसत् was momentarily startled.

Simple meaning: Lord Balarama saw the asura’s true form moving through the sky. With eyes like fire, a face terrifying with its scowl and sharp teeth, and hair that looked like flames, the asura shone with the eerie light of his golden ornaments. Seeing this sudden, monstrous transformation, Balarama felt a momentary surge of surprise.

अथागतस्मृतिरभयो रिपुं बलो विहायसार्थमिव हरन्तमात्मनः।

रुषाहनच्छिरसि दृढेन मुष्टिना सुराधिपो गिरिमिव वज्ररंहसा ॥10.18.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, आगत-स्मृतिः regaining His composure (remembering His true power), अभयः fearless, रिपुम् the enemy, बलः Balarama, विहायसा through the sky, अर्थम् इव as if a treasure, हरन्तम् carrying away, आत्मनः Himself, रुषा with anger, अहनत् struck, शिरसि on the head, दृढेन मुष्टिना with a firm fist, सुर-अधिपः the king of the gods (Indra), गिरिम् a mountain, इव like, वज्र-रंहसा with the force of a thunderbolt

Anvaya: अथ आगतस्मृतिः अभयः बलः विहायसा अर्थम् इव आत्मनः हरन्तं रिपुं रुषा दृढेन मुष्टिना शिरसि अहनत्, सुराधिपः वज्ररंहसा गिरिम् इव।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, आगतस्मृतिः having regained His memory (of His divinity), अभयः बलः the fearless Balarama, विहायसा through the sky, अर्थम् इव as if a treasure, आत्मनः हरन्तं who was carrying Him away, रिपुं that enemy, रुषा with anger, दृढेन मुष्टिना with a hard fist, शिरसि अहनत् struck on the head, सुराधिपः just as the King of Heaven (Indra), वज्ररंहसा with the speed/force of the thunderbolt, गिरिम् इव (strikes) a mountain.

Simple meaning: Then, quickly regaining His composure and remembering His true nature, the fearless Balarama became furious. As the asura carried Him through the sky as if stealing a treasure, Balarama struck the enemy's head with His powerful fist. The blow was as devastating as when Indra, the king of the gods, strikes a mountain with the full force of his thunderbolt.

स आहतः सपदि विशीर्णमस्तको मुखाद् वमन् रुधिरमपस्मृतोऽसुरः ।

महारवं व्यसुरपतत् समीरयन् गिरिर्यथा मघवत आयुधाहतः ॥10.18.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (the asura), आहतः being struck, सपदि immediately, विशीर्ण-मस्तकः having his head shattered, मुखात् from the mouth, वमन् vomiting, रुधिरम् blood, अपस्मृतः unconscious/lost awareness, असुरः the asura, महा-रवम् a great screaming sound, व्यसुः lifeless, अपतत् fell, समीरयन् emitting, गिरिः a mountain, यथा as, मघवतः of Indra, आयुध-आहतः struck by the weapon (the thunderbolt)

Anvaya: आहतः सः असुरः सपदि विशीर्णमस्तकः मुखात् रुधिरं वमन् अपस्मृतः महारवं समीरयन् व्यसुः (सन्) अपतत्, मघवतः आयुधाहतः गिरिः यथा।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आहतः Being struck, सः असुरः that asura, सपदि immediately, विशीर्णमस्तकः with his head shattered, मुखात् रुधिरं वमन् vomiting blood from his mouth, अपस्मृतः losing consciousness, महारवं समीरयन् emitting a terrifying scream, व्यसुः lifeless, अपतत् fell down, मघवतः आयुधाहतः गिरिः यथा just like a mountain struck by the weapon of Indra.

Simple meaning: Struck by Balarama's fist, the asura's head immediately shattered. Vomiting blood and losing all consciousness, he let out a terrible scream and fell to the ground lifeless, just like a massive mountain toppled by Indra’s thunderbolt.

दृष्ट्वा प्रलम्बं निहतं बलेन बलशालिना ।

गोपाः सुविस्मिता आसन् साधु साध्विति वादिनः॥10.18.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्ट्वा seeing, प्रलम्बम् Pralamba, निहतम् killed, बलेन by Balarama, बल-शालिना (who is) the possessor of great strength, गोपाः the cowherd boys, सु-विस्मिताः very amazed, आसन् were, साधु साधु "Excellent! Excellent!", इति thus, वादिनः saying

Anvaya: बलशालिना बलेन निहतं प्रलम्बं दृष्ट्वा गोपाः सुविस्मिताः (सन्तः) साधु साधु इति वादिनः आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बलशालिना बलेन By the powerful Balarama, निहतं प्रलम्बं दृष्ट्वा seeing Pralamba killed, गोपाः the cowherd boys, सुविस्मिताः being very amazed, साधु साधु इति वादिनः आसन् were saying "Well done! Well done!"

Simple meaning: Seeing that the powerful Balarama had slain the asura Pralamba, the cowherd boys were struck with wonder. They rushed toward Balarama, praising Him over and over again by shouting, "Bravo! Well done!"

आशिषोऽभिगृणन्तस्तं प्रशशंसुस्तदर्हणम् ।

प्रेत्यागतमिवालिङ्ग्य प्रेमविह्वलचेतसः ॥10.18.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: आशिषः blessings, अभिगृणन्तः offering/chanting, तम् Him (Balarama), प्रशशंसुः they praised, तत्-अर्हणम् who was worthy of that (praise/honor), प्रेत्य from death (having departed), आगतम् इव as if returned, आलिङ्ग्य embracing, प्रेम-विह्वल-चेतसः their hearts overwhelmed with love

Anvaya: प्रेमविह्वलचेतसः (गोपाः) प्रेत्य आगतम् इव तम् आलिङ्ग्य आशिषः अभिगृणन्तः तत् अर्हणं प्रशशंसुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रेमविह्वलचेतसः With hearts overwhelmed by love, (the cowherd boys), प्रेत्य आगतम् इव तम् as if He had returned from the dead, आलिङ्ग्य they embraced Him, आशिषः अभिगृणन्तः and chanting blessings, तत् अर्हणं प्रशशंसुः praised Him who was worthy of it .

Simple meaning: Their hearts overflowing with pure affection, the cowherd boys embraced Him as if He had returned from the dead. They chanted auspicious blessings upon Him and, offered their praises to Balarama, who truly deserved such honor.

पापे प्रलम्बे निहते देवाः परमनिर्वृताः ।

अभ्यवर्षन् बलं माल्यैः शशंसुः साधु साध्विति ॥10.18.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: पापे the sinful, प्रलम्बे Pralamba, निहते having been killed, देवाः the demigods, परम-निर्वृताः extremely satisfied/happy, अभ्यवर्षन् showered, बलम् Balarama, माल्यैः with flower garlands, शशंसुः they praised, साधु साधु "Excellent! Excellent!", इति thus

Anvaya: पापे प्रलम्बे निहते (सति) परमनिर्वृताः देवाः माल्यैः बलम् अभ्यवर्षन् साधु साधु इति शशंसुः च।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: पापे प्रलम्बे निहते Once the sinful Pralamba was killed, परमनिर्वृताः देवाः the extremely gratified demigods, माल्यैः बलम् अभ्यवर्षन् showered Balarama with flower garlands, साधु साधु इति शशंसुः च and praised Him, saying "Well done! Well done!"

Simple meaning: When the sinful asura Pralamba was killed, the demigods in the heavens felt a sense of great relief and joy. They showered Lord Balarama with celestial flower garlands and offered their heartfelt praises, exclaiming, "Bravo! Well done!"

श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे प्रलम्बवधो नाम अष्टादशः अध्यायः ॥18॥

Thus ends the eighteenth discourse named 'The Lord slays Pralamba,' in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
