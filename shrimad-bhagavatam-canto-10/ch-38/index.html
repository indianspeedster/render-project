Chapter 38

Akrura’s arrival in Vraja

श्रीमद्भागवतमहापुराणम्

दशमः स्कन्धः

अक्रूरागमनं नाम अष्टात्रिंशः अध्यायः

(43 Shlokas)

श्री शुक उवाच

अक्रूरोऽपि च तां रात्रिं मधुपुर्यां महामतिः ।

उषित्वा रथमास्थाय प्रययौ नन्दगोकुलम् ॥10.38.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, अक्रूरः Akrura, अपि also, च and, ताम् that, रात्रिम् night, मधुपुर्याम् in the city of Mathura, महामतिः the high-minded one, उषित्वा having stayed, रथम् chariot, आस्थाय mounting, प्रययौ set out, नन्द-गोकुलम् for the cowherd village of Nanda

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच — महामतिः अक्रूरः अपि च तां रात्रिं मधुपुर्याम् उषित्वा रथम् आस्थाय नन्दगोकुलं प्रययौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, महामतिः अक्रूरः the great-souled Akrura, अपि च and also, तां रात्रिं that night, मधुपुर्याम् उषित्वा having stayed in Mathura, रथम् आस्थाय mounting his chariot, नन्दगोकुलं प्रययौ departed for the cowherd village of Nanda.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: After staying that night in the city of Mathura, the high-minded Akrura mounted his chariot and set out for the cowherd village of Nanda.

गच्छन् पथि महाभागो भगवत्यम्बुजेक्षणे ।

भक्तिं परामुपगत एवमेतदचिन्तयत् ॥10.38.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: गच्छन् while going, पथि on the road, महाभागः the greatly fortunate Akrura, भगवति for the Supreme Lord, अम्बुज-ईक्षणे whose eyes are like lotus petals, भक्तिम् devotion, पराम् transcendental, उपगतः attained, एवम् in this way, एतत् this, अचिन्तयत् thought

Anvaya: महाभागः पथि गच्छन् अम्बुजेक्षणे भगवति परां भक्तिम् उपगतः (सन्) एवम् एतत् अचिन्तयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महाभागः The most fortunate Akrura, पथि गच्छन् while traveling on the road, अम्बुजेक्षणे भगवति for the lotus-eyed Supreme Lord, परां भक्तिम् उपगतः having attained supreme devotion, एवम् एतत् अचिन्तयत् thought in this manner.

Simple Meaning: While traveling on the road, the greatly fortunate Akrura was overwhelmed with supreme devotion for the lotus-eyed Supreme Lord. In that state of transcendental love, he began to think as follows.

किं मयाऽऽचरितं भद्रं किं तप्तं परमं तपः ।

किं वाथाप्यर्हते दत्तं यद् द्रक्ष्याम्यद्य केशवम् ॥10.38.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: किम् what, मया by me, आचरितम् performed, भद्रम् auspicious deed, किम् what, तप्तम् performed, परमम् supreme, तपः austerity, किम् वा or what, अथ अपि moreover, अर्हते to a deserving person, दत्तम् given in charity, यत् that, द्रक्ष्यामि I shall see, अद्य today, केशवम् Lord Keshava

Anvaya: मया किं भद्रम् आचरितम्? किं परमं तपः तप्तम्? अथ अपि अर्हते किं दत्तम्? यत् अद्य केशवं द्रक्ष्यामि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मया किं भद्रम् आचरितम् What auspicious deed has been performed by me? किं परमं तपः तप्तम् What supreme austerity has been undergone? अथ अपि अर्हते किं दत्तम् Moreover, what has been given to a deserving person, यत् that, अद्य केशवं द्रक्ष्यामि today I shall see Lord Keshava.

Simple Meaning: Akrura thought: What pious deeds have I performed? What great austerities have I observed, or what significant charity have I given to worthy recipients, that today I shall be able to see Lord Keshava?

ममैतद् दुर्लभं मन्य उत्तमश्लोकदर्शनम् ।

विषयात्मनो यथा ब्रह्मकीर्तनं शूद्र जन्मनः ॥10.37.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: मम for me, एतत् this, दुर्लभम् difficult to achieve, मन्ये I think, उत्तम-श्लोक-दर्शनम् the sight of the Lord who is praised by choice verses, विषय-आत्मनः for one whose mind is absorbed in sense objects, यथा just as, ब्रह्म-कीर्तनम् the chanting of the Vedas, शूद्र-जन्मनः for one born as a Shudra

Anvaya: विषयात्मनः मम एतत् उत्तमश्लोकदर्शनं (तथा) दुर्लभं मन्ये, यथा शूद्रजन्मनः ब्रह्मकीर्तनम् ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विषयात्मनः मम For me, whose mind is attached to sense objects, एतत् उत्तमश्लोकदर्शनं this vision of the Supreme Lord, दुर्लभं मन्ये I consider very difficult to achieve, यथा just as, शूद्रजन्मनः for a person born as a Shudra, ब्रह्मकीर्तनम् the chanting of the Vedic mantras.

Simple Meaning: I consider the opportunity to see the Supreme Lord, who is glorified by the most exquisite verses, to be very difficult for a person like me who is absorbed in worldly sense objects. It is as rare as the chanting of Vedic hymns is for one who is not qualified by birth and training to do so.

मैवं ममाधमस्यापि स्यादेवाच्युतदर्शनम् ।

ह्रियमाणः कालनद्या क्वचित्तरति कश्चन ॥10.37.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: मा एवम् not so, मम for me, अधमस्य for the fallen one, अपि even, स्यात् it may be, एव indeed, अच्युत-दर्शनम् the sight of the infallible Lord, ह्रियमाणः being swept away, काल-नद्या by the river of time, क्वचित् sometimes, तरति crosses over, कश्चन someone

Anvaya: अधमस्य मम अपि अच्युतदर्शनं स्यात् एव; मा एवम् (मन्यस्व), (यतः) कालनद्या ह्रियमाणः कश्चन क्वचित् तरति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अधमस्य मम अपि For even a fallen person like me, अच्युतदर्शनं स्यात् एव the sight of Lord Achyuta may indeed be possible; मा एवम् (do not think) Not so, (यतः) because, कालनद्या ह्रियमाणः being carried away by the river of time, कश्चन someone, क्वचित् तरति occasionally reaches the shore (crosses over).

Simple Meaning: But enough of such thoughts! Even for a fallen soul like me, the sight of the infallible Lord is surely possible. Just as someone being swept away by a swift river of time might occasionally find their way to the shore, any soul may by chance be delivered from material existence by the Lord's mercy.

ममाद्यामङ्गलं नष्टं फलवांश्चैव मे भवः ।

यन्नमस्ये भगवतो योगिध्येयाङ्घ्रिपङ्कजम् ॥10.38.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: मम my, अद्य today, अमङ्गलम् inauspiciousness, नष्टम् destroyed, फलवान् fruitful, च and, एव indeed, मे my, भवः birth/existence, यत् because, नमस्ये I shall offer obeisances, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, योगि-ध्येय-अङ्घ्रि-पङ्कजम् to the lotus feet meditated upon by great yogis

Anvaya: अद्य मम अमङ्गलं नष्टम्, मे भवः च फलवान् एव (जातः), यत् भगवतः योगिध्येयाङ्घ्रिपङ्कजं नमस्ये।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अद्य Today, मम अमङ्गलं नष्टम् my inauspiciousness is destroyed, मे भवः च and my birth, फलवान् एव (जातः) has indeed become fruitful, यत् because, भगवतः of the Supreme Lord, योगिध्येयाङ्घ्रिपङ्कजं the lotus feet which are the object of meditation for yogis, नमस्ये I shall prostrate before.

Simple Meaning: Today, all my inauspiciousness has been destroyed and my life has finally become fruitful, because I am about to offer my respects to the lotus feet of the Supreme Lord—the very same feet that the greatest yogis constantly meditate upon.

कंसो बताद्याकृत मेऽत्यनुग्रहं द्रक्ष्येऽङ्घ्रिपद्मं प्रहितोऽमुना हरेः ।

कृतवतारस्य दुरत्ययं तमः पूर्वेऽतरन् यन्नखमण्डलत्विषा ॥10.38.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: कंसः Kamsa, बत oh, अद्य today, अकृत has done, मे unto me, अति-अनुग्रहम् a great favor, द्रक्ष्ये I shall see, अङ्घ्रि-पद्मम् the lotus feet, प्रहितः sent, अमुना by him, हरेः of Lord Hari, कृत-अवतारस्य who has descended (to this world), दुरत्ययम् insurmountable, तमः the darkness (of ignorance), पूर्वे the predecessors, अतरन् crossed over, यत्-नख-मण्डल-त्विषा by the light from the glow of whose nails

Anvaya: बत! अद्य कंसः मे अत्यनुग्रहम् अकृत, (यतः) अमुना प्रहितः (अहं) कृतावतारस्य हरेः अङ्घ्रिपद्मं द्रक्ष्ये; यन्नखमण्डलत्विषा पूर्वे दुरत्ययं तमः अतरन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: बत! Oh!, अद्य Today, कंसः मे अत्यनुग्रहम् अकृत Kamsa has done me a great favor, (यतः) because, अमुना प्रहितः sent by him, कृतावतारस्य हरेः of Lord Hari who has taken an avatar, अङ्घ्रिपद्मं द्रक्ष्ये I shall see the lotus feet; यन्नखमण्डलत्विषा by the light of the circles of whose toenails, पूर्वे the ancients, दुरत्ययं तमः अतरन् crossed over the insurmountable darkness of material existence.

Simple Meaning: Oh, today Kamsa has actually done me a great favor! Being sent by him, I shall now see the lotus feet of Lord Hari, who has descended into this world. It is by the effulgence radiating from the nails of those very feet that the great sages of the past crossed over the insurmountable darkness of ignorance.

यदर्चितं ब्रह्मभवादिभिः सुरैः श्रिया च देव्या मुनिभिः ससात्वतैः ।

गोचारणायानुचरैश्चरद्वने यद् गोपिकानां कुचकुङ्कुमाङ्कितम् ॥10.37.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: यत् which (feet), अर्चितम् worshipped, ब्रह्म-भव-आदिभिः by Brahma, Shiva, and others, सुरैः by the demigods, श्रिया by the Goddess of Fortune, च and, देव्या the goddess, मुनिभिः by the sages, स-सात्वतैः along with the great devotees of the Lord, गो-चारणाय for the tending of cows, अनुचरैः with His companions, चरत् walking, वने in the forest, यत् which, गोपिकानाम् of the gopis, कुच-कुङ्कुम-अङ्कितम् marked by the kumkuma from their breasts

Anvaya: यत् (अङ्घ्रिपद्मं) ब्रह्मभवादिभिः सुरैः श्रिया देव्या मुनिभिः ससात्वतैः च अर्चितम्, (यत्) अनुचरैः सह गोचारणाय वने चरत्, यत् (च) गोपिकानां कुचकुङ्कुमाङ्कितम् (तत् अहं द्रक्ष्यामि)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत् Which lotus feet, ब्रह्मभवादिभिः सुरैः by the demigods headed by Brahma and Shiva, श्रिया देव्या by Goddess Lakshmi, मुनिभिः ससात्वतैः च and by the sages and the Lord's devotees, अर्चितम् are worshipped; यत् which, अनुचरैः सह together with His companions, गोचारणाय वने चरत् wander in the forest for the herding of the cows; यत् च and which, गोपिकानां कुचकुङ्कुमाङ्कितम् are smeared with the kumkuma from the breasts of the gopis; (that I shall see).

Simple Meaning: I shall see those lotus feet which are worshipped by the demigods like Brahma and Shiva, by the Goddess of Fortune, and by the great sages and devotees. Those same feet wander through the forest in the company of the cowherd boys while they tend the cows, and they are marked by the kumkuma from the breasts of the gopis.

द्रक्ष्यामि नूनं सुकपोलनासिकं स्मितावलोकारुणकञ्जलोचनम् ।

मुखं मुकुन्दस्य गुडालकावृतं प्रदक्षिणं मे प्रचरन्ति वै मृगाः ॥10.38.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: द्रक्ष्यामि I shall see, नूनम् surely, सु-कपोल-नासिकम् with beautiful cheeks and nose, स्मित-अवलोक-अरुण-कञ्ज-लोचनम् with eyes like red lotuses and a smiling glance, मुखम् the face, मुकुन्दस्य of Mukunda, गुड-अलक-आवृतम् framed by curling locks of hair, प्रदक्षिणम् clockwise (auspiciously), मे for me, प्रचरन्ति are moving, वै indeed, मृगाः the deer

Anvaya: नूनं (अहं) सुकपोलनासिकं स्मितावलोकारुणकञ्जलोचनं गुडालकावृतं मुकुन्दस्य मुखं द्रक्ष्यामि, (यतः) मृगाः मे प्रदक्षिणं वै प्रचरन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नूनं Surely, अहं I, सुकपोलनासिकं with a beautiful nose and cheeks, स्मितावलोकारुणकञ्जलोचनं with eyes like red lotuses and a smiling glance, गुडालकावृतं surrounded by curling locks, मुकुन्दस्य मुखं the face of Mukunda, द्रक्ष्यामि shall see; यतः because, मृगाः the deer, मे प्रदक्षिणं वै प्रचरन्ति are indeed passing me on my right side.

Simple Meaning: I shall surely behold the face of Lord Mukunda, which is adorned with beautiful cheeks and a shapely nose, eyes like blossoming red lotuses, and a gentle, smiling glance. His face is framed by exquisite curling locks of hair. I am certain of this because the deer are auspiciously passing me on my right as I travel.

Note: According to the science of omens this is considered to be a propitious omen as is borne out by the following verse: प्रदक्षिणगताः श्रेष्ठा यत्रायां मृगपक्षिणः।

अप्यद्य विष्णोर्मनुजत्वमीयुषो भारावताराय भुवो निजेच्छया ।

लावण्यधाम्नो भवितोपलम्भनं मह्यं न न स्यात् फलमञ्जसा दृशः ॥10.38.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: अपि certainly, अद्य today, विष्णोः of Lord Vishnu, मनुजत्वम् the form of a human being, ईयुषः who has assumed, भार-अवताराय to remove the burden, भुवः of the earth, निज-इच्छया by His own will, लावण्य-धाम्नः the reservoir of all beauty, भविता will be, उपलम्भनम् the perception/sight, मह्यम् for me, न न स्यात् it cannot but be (it will definitely happen), फलम् the fruit, अञ्जसा easily, दृशः of my eyes

Anvaya: निजेच्छया भुवः भारावताराय मनुजत्वम् ईयुषः लावण्यधाम्नः विष्णोः उपलम्भनम् अद्य मह्यं भविता; (अतः) अञ्जसा दृशः फलं न न स्यात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: निजेच्छया By His own will, भुवः भारावताराय for the removal of the earth's burden, मनुजत्वम् ईयुषः who has assumed a human-like form, लावण्यधाम्नः the abode of beauty, विष्णोः of Lord Vishnu, उपलम्भनम् the sight, अद्य मह्यं भविता today will be attained by me; अतः therefore, अञ्जसा easily, दृशः फलं the fruit of my eyes, न न स्यात् will certainly not fail to manifest.

Simple Meaning: Today I will surely obtain the sight of Lord Vishnu, the reservoir of all beauty, who has assumed a human form by His own sweet will to relieve the earth of its burden. Having attained His vision, the purpose of my eyes will finally be realized.

य ईक्षिताहंरहितोऽप्यसत्सतोः स्वतेजसापास्ततमोभिदाभ्रमः ।

स्वमाययाऽऽत्मन् रचितैस्तदीक्षया प्राणाक्षधीभिः सदनेष्वभीयते ॥10.30.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: यः who, ईक्षिता the witness, अहम्-रहितः devoid of false ego, अपि although, असत्-सतोः of the cause and effect (material world), स्व-तेजसा by His own spiritual power, अपास्त-तमः-भिदा-भ्रमः who has dispelled the darkness of the delusion of duality, स्व-मायया by His own internal potency, आत्मन् in Himself, रचितैः which are manifested, तत्-ईक्षया by His glance, प्राण-अक्ष-धीभिः through the vital airs, senses, and intelligence, सदनेषु within the bodies (houses), अभीयते is perceived

Anvaya: यः अह्रंरहितः अपि असत्सतोः ईक्षिता, स्वतेजसा अपास्ततमोभिदाभ्रमः (अस्ति), (यः) स्वमायया आत्मन् रचितैः तदीक्षया प्राणाक्षधीभिः सदनेषु अभीयते।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः Who, अह्रंरहितः अपि although free from ego, असत्सतोः ईक्षिता is the witness of the manifest and unmanifest, स्वतेजसा by His own brilliance, अपास्ततमोभिदाभ्रमः has cast aside the darkness of the delusion of duality; यः who, स्वमायया आत्मन् रचितैः manifested within Himself by His own Maya, तदीक्षया by His glance, प्राणाक्षधीभिः through the life-breath, senses, and intellect, सदनेषु within the bodies of living beings, अभीयते is known to exist.

Simple Meaning: Although He is the detached witness of all cause and effect and is free from the false ego of a material being, He dispels the darkness of duality by His own spiritual effulgence. By His mere glance, He activates the life-force, senses, and intelligence of all living beings, and thus He is perceived as the indwelling soul within the "houses" of their bodies.

यस्याखिलामीवहभिः सुमङ्गलैर्वाचो विमिश्रा गुणकर्मजन्मभिः ।

प्राणन्ति शुम्भन्ति पुनन्ति वै जगद् यास्तद्विरक्ताः शवशोभना मताः ॥10.38.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: यस्य whose, अखिल-अमीव-हभिः destroying all sins, सु-मङ्गलैः most auspicious, वाचः words, विमिश्राः mixed with/describing, गुण-कर्म-जन्मभिः the qualities, activities, and births, प्राणन्ति give life, शुम्भन्ति beautify, पुनन्ति purify, वै indeed, जगत् the universe, याः which (words), तत्-विरक्ताः devoid of those (the Lord's glories), शव-शोभनाः like the decoration of a corpse, मताः are considered

Anvaya: यस्य अखिलामीवहभिः सुमङ्गलैः गुणकर्मजन्मभिः विमिश्राः वाचः जगत् वै प्राणन्ति शुम्भन्ति पुनन्ति; याः तद्विरक्ताः (ताः) शवशोभनाः मताः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यस्य Whose, अखिलामीवहभिः destroying all sins, सुमङ्गलैः most auspicious, गुण-कर्म-जन्मभिः qualities, activities, and births, विमिश्राः वाचः words describing (mixed with), जगत् वै प्राणन्ति give life to the universe indeed, शुम्भन्ति beautify it, पुनन्ति and purify it; याः which, तद्विरक्ताः are devoid of those glories, ताः those, शवशोभनाः like the decoration of a dead body, मताः are considered.

Simple Meaning: Words that describe the most auspicious births, activities, and qualities of the Supreme Lord—which destroy all sins—give life to the entire universe, beautifying and purifying it. On the other hand, any words that are devoid of the Lord's glories are considered to be nothing more than the useless decoration of a corpse.

स चावतीर्णः किल सात्वतान्वये स्वसेतुपालामरवर्यशर्मकृत् ।

यशो वितन्वन् व्रज आस्त ईश्वरो गायन्ति देवा यदशेषमङ्गलम् ॥10.38.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः He, च and, अवतीर्णः has descended, किल indeed, सात्वत-अन्वये in the dynasty of the Satvatas (Yadus), स्व-सेतु-पाल-अमर-वर्य-शर्म-कृत् bringing happiness to the best of the demigods who protect His laws, यशः fame, वितन्वन् spreading, व्रजे in Vraja, आस्ते resides, ईश्वरः the Supreme Lord, गायन्ति sing, देवाः the demigods, यत् whose, अशेष-मङ्गलम् unlimitedly auspicious

Anvaya: सः ईश्वरः च किल सात्वतान्वये अवतीर्णः, स्वसेतुपालामरवर्यशर्मकृत् यशः वितन्वन् व्रजे आस्ते, देवाः यत् अशेषमङ्गलं गायन्ति ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः ईश्वरः च And that Supreme Lord, किल indeed, सात्वतान्वये अवतीर्णः has descended in the Yadu dynasty, स्वसेतुपालामरवर्यशर्मकृत् bringing joy to the great demigods who protect His religious principles, यशः वितन्वन् spreading His fame, व्रजे आस्ते stays in Vraja, देवाः यत् अशेषमङ्गलं गायन्ति whose unlimitedly auspicious glories the demigods sing.

Simple Meaning: That same Supreme Lord has now descended into the Yadu dynasty to bring happiness to the virtuous demigods who uphold His laws. He is currently residing in Vraja, spreading His divine fame, which the demigods celebrate as the source of all-pervading auspiciousness.

तं त्वद्य नूनं महतां गतिं गुरुं त्रैलोक्यकान्तं दृशिमन्महोत्सवम् ।

रूपं दधानं श्रिय ईप्सितास्पदं द्रक्ष्ये ममासन्नुषसः सुदर्शनाः ॥10.38.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् Him, तु indeed, अद्य today, नूनम् surely, महताम् of the great souls, गतिम् the goal, गुरुम् the spiritual master, त्रैलोक्य-कान्तम् the beloved of the three worlds, दृशि-मत्-महोत्सवम् a great festival for the eyes of those who possess them, रूपम् form, दधानम् manifesting, श्रियः of the Goddess of Fortune, ईप्सित-आस्पदम् the desired abode, द्रक्ष्ये I shall see, मम my, आसन् have been, उषसः from the dawn, सु-दर्शनाः auspicious to behold

Anvaya: महतां गतिं गुरुं त्रैलोक्यकान्तं दृशिमन्महोत्सवं श्रियः ईप्सितास्पदं रूपं दधानं तं तु अद्य नूनं द्रक्ष्ये; मम उषसः सुदर्शनाः आसन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महतां गतिं The goal of the great souls, गुरुं the universal teacher, त्रैलोक्यकान्तं the beloved of the three worlds, दृशिमन्महोत्सवं a grand festival for the eyes, श्रियः ईप्सितास्पदं the coveted abode of Goddess Lakshmi, रूपं दधानं manifesting a form, तं तु Him indeed, अद्य नूनं द्रक्ष्ये today I shall surely see; मम उषसः Since morning, for me, सुदर्शनाः आसन् it has been auspicious.

Simple Meaning: Today I shall surely behold the Supreme Lord, who is the ultimate goal and guru of all great souls, the beloved of the three worlds, and a grand festival for the eyes of all who see Him. He manifests a form that is the very abode of beauty desired by the Goddess of Fortune. Indeed, since the dawning of this day, it has been auspicious for me.

अथावरूढः सपदीशयो रथात् प्रधानपुंसोश्चरणं स्वलब्धये ।

धिया धृतं योगिभिरप्यहं ध्रुवं नमस्य आभ्यां च सखीन् वनौकसः ॥10.38.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, अवरूढः descending, सपदि immediately, ईशयोः of the two Lords, रथात् from the chariot, प्रधान-पुंसोः of the two Supreme Personalities, चरणम् the feet, स्व-लब्धये for my own attainment, धिया with the mind, धृतम् held, योगिभिः by yogis, अपि even, अहम् I, ध्रुवम् certainly, नमस्ये I shall offer obeisances, आभ्याम् to these two, च and, सखीन् to the friends, वन-ओकसः the forest dwellers

Anvaya: अथ (अहं) रथात् सपदि अवरूढः (सन्), योगिभिः धिया धृतम् अपि प्रधानपुंसोः ईशयोः चरणं स्वलब्धये ध्रुवं नमस्ये; आभ्यां (तथा) च वनौकसः सखीन् (च नमस्ये)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, अहं रथात् सपदि अवरूढः I, having immediately descended from the chariot, योगिभिः धिया धृतम् अपि which is held in the meditation of yogis, प्रधानपुंसोः ईशयोः चरणं the feet of the two Supreme Personalities, स्वलब्धये for my own self-attainment, ध्रुवं नमस्ये shall certainly offer obeisances; तथा च and also, आभ्यां वनौकसः सखीन् to the forest dwellers friends of these two.

Simple Meaning: Then, immediately descending from my chariot, I shall prostrate myself before the feet of the two Supreme Personalities, Krishna and Balarama. Their feet, which are the ultimate goal of life and are meditated upon by great yogis, shall be my refuge. I shall also offer my respects to all their friends, the residents of the forest.

अप्यङ्घ्रिमूले पतितस्य मे विभुः शिरस्यधास्यन्निजहस्तपङ्कजम् ।

दत्ताभयं कालभुजङ्गरंहसा प्रोद्वेजितानां शरणैषिणां नृणाम् ॥10.38.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: अपि whether, अङ्घ्रि-मूले at the base of His feet, पतितस्य of one who has fallen, मे my, विभुः the Almighty Lord, शिरसि on the head, अधास्यत् will place, निज-हस्त-पङ्कजम् His own lotus hand, दत्त-अभयम् which grants fearlessness, काल-भुजङ्ग-रंहसा by the speed of the serpent of time, प्रोद्वेजितानाम् for those who are greatly terrified, शरण-ऐषिणाम् who are seeking shelter, नृणाम् of men

Anvaya: अपि विभुः अङ्घ्रिमूले पतितस्य मे शिरसि निजहस्तपङ्कजं अधास्यत्, यत् कालभुजङ्गरंहसा प्रोद्वेजितानां शरणैषिणां नृणां दत्ताभयम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अपि विभुः Will the Almighty Lord, अङ्घ्रिमूले पतितस्य मे on my head as I lie prostrate at the base of His feet, शिरसि निजहस्तपङ्कजं अधास्यत् place His own lotus hand, यत् which, कालभुजङ्गरंहसा प्रोद्वेजितानां for those terrified by the swiftness of the serpent of time, शरणैषिणां नृणां for men seeking shelter, दत्ताभयम् grants fearlessness.

Simple Meaning: Will the Almighty Lord place His own lotus hand upon my head as I fall at His feet? That hand which grants fearlessness to all human beings who, terrified by the swift movement of the serpent of time, seek His ultimate shelter.

समर्हणं यत्र निधाय कौशिकस्तथा बलिश्चाप जगत्त्रयेन्द्रताम् ।

यद् वा विहारे व्रजयोषितां श्रमं स्पर्शेन सौगन्धिकगन्ध्यपानुदत् ॥10.38.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: समर्हणम् worshipful offering, यत्र upon which, निधाय having placed, कौशिकः Indra (the descendant of Kushika), तथा and also, बलिः Bali Maharaja, आप attained, जगत्-त्रय-इन्द्रताम् the lordship over the three worlds, यत् which, वा or, विहारे during the sporting pastimes, व्रज-योषिताम् of the women of Vraja, श्रमम् the fatigue, स्पर्शेन by the touch, सौगन्धिक-गन्धि fragrant like a Saugandhika lotus, अपानुदत् removed

Anvaya: यत्र समर्हणं निधाय कौशिकः तथा बलिः जगत्त्रयेन्द्रतां आप, यत् वा विहारे सौगन्धिकगन्धि स्पर्शेन व्रजयोषितां श्रमं अपानुदत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यत्र समर्हणं निधाय Placing worshipful offerings upon which (lotus hand), कौशिकः तथा बलिः Indra and Bali Maharaja, जगत्त्रयेन्द्रतां आप attained the sovereignty of the three worlds, यत् वा and which, विहारे during pastimes, सौगन्धिकगन्धि having the fragrance of a lotus, स्पर्शेन by its touch, व्रजयोषितां श्रमं अपानुदत् removed the fatigue of the women of Vraja.

Simple Meaning: By placing their offerings upon that hand, Indra and Bali Maharaja both attained the lordship of the three worlds. That same hand, which possesses the fragrance of a lotus, removes the weariness of the women of Vraja through its divine touch during His sporting pastimes.

न मय्युपैष्यत्यरिबुद्धिमच्युतः कंसस्य दूतः प्रहितोऽपि विश्वदृक् ।

योऽन्तर्बहिश्चेतस एतदीहितं क्षेत्रज्ञ ईक्षत्यमलेन चक्षुषा ॥10.38.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, मयि towards me, उपैष्यति will entertain, अरि-बुद्धिम् the attitude of an enemy, अच्युतः the infallible Lord, कंसस्य of Kamsa, दूतः a messenger, प्रहितः sent, अपि although, विश्व-दृक् the seer of everything, यः who, अन्तः-बहिः within and without, चेतसः of the mind, एतत् this, ईहितम् activity, क्षेत्र-ज्ञः the knower of the field, ईक्षति sees, अमलेन with spotless, चक्षुषा eye

Anvaya: अच्युतः मयि अरिबुद्धिं न उपैष्यति, कंसस्य दूतः प्रहितः अपि विश्वदृक्, यः क्षेत्रज्ञः अमलेन चक्षुषा चेतसः एतत् अन्तर्बहिः ईहितम् ईक्षति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अच्युतः मयि अरिबुद्धिं न उपैष्यति The infallible Lord will not maintain the idea that I am an enemy, कंसस्य दूतः प्रहितः अपि though I have been sent as a messenger of Kamsa, विश्वदृक् He is the witness of the universe, यः क्षेत्रज्ञः who, as the knower of the field, अमलेन चक्षुषा with a faultless eye, चेतसः एतत् अन्तर्बहिः ईहितम् ईक्षति sees these internal and external activities of the mind.

Simple Meaning: The infallible Lord will not consider me an enemy even though I come as Kamsa's messenger. Being the witness of the entire universe and the knower of all hearts, He sees everything within and without. With His pure vision, He perfectly understands the true intentions and activities of my mind.

अप्यङ्घ्रिमूलेऽवहितं कृताञ्जलिं मामीक्षिता सस्मितमार्द्रया दृशा ।

सपद्यपध्वस्तसमस्तकिल्बिषो वोढा मुदं वीतविशङ्क ऊर्जिताम् ॥10.38.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: अपि whether, अङ्घ्रि-मूले at the base of His feet, अवहितम् fallen/prostrate, कृत-अञ्जलिम् with joined palms, माम् me, ईक्षिता He will look, स-स्मितम् with a smile, आर्द्रया moistened (with affection), दृशा with a glance, सपदि immediately, अपध्वस्त-समस्त-किल्बिषः having all sins destroyed, वोढा I shall attain, मुदम् joy, वीत-विशङ्कः free from all doubt, ऊर्जिताम् intense

Anvaya: अपि अङ्घ्रिमूले अवहितं कृताञ्जलिं मां सस्मितं आर्द्रया दृशा ईक्षिता, सपदि अपध्वस्तसमस्तकिल्बिषः वीतविशङ्कः ऊर्जितां मुदं वोढा।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अपि अङ्घ्रिमूले अवहितं कृताञ्जलिं मां Also when He looks at me, prostrate at the base of His feet with palms joined in prayer, सस्मितं आर्द्रया दृशा ईक्षिता with a smiling and affectionate glance, सपदि अपध्वस्तसमस्तकिल्बिषः immediately having all my sins destroyed, वीतविशङ्कः being free from doubt, ऊर्जितां मुदं वोढा I shall carry (experience) intense joy.

Simple Meaning: When He looks upon me with a smile and a glance moistened with affection as I lie prostrate at His feet with joined palms, by that look, all my sins will be immediately destroyed; free from all misgivings, I shall experience the most intense and sublime happiness.

Note: In the shloka, Akrura is describing how, by surrendering to the Lord, falling at His feet, and offering their prayers (कृताञ्जलिं), they receive the Lord's compassionate glance (सस्मितमार्द्रया दृशा). This glance purifies them instantly (सपदि अपध्वस्तसमस्तकिल्बिषः), removing all their sins. As a result, the devotee receives bliss, which is carried by the Lord (वोढा मुदम्), and they are free from doubt (वीतविशङ्क) and filled with divine strength (ऊर्जिताम्).

सुहृत्तमं ज्ञातिमनन्यदैवतं दोर्भ्यां बृहद्भ्यां परिरप्स्यतेऽथ माम् ।

आत्मा हि तीर्थीक्रियते तदैव मे बन्धश्च कर्मात्मक उच्छ्वसित्यतः ॥10.38.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: सुहृत्-तमम् the best of friends, ज्ञातिम् a relative, अनन्य-दैवतम् who has no other Deity but Him, दोर्भ्याम् with arms, बृहद्भ्याम् long and mighty, परिरप्स्यते He will embrace, अथ then, माम् me, आत्मा indeed the soul (body and mind), हि certainly, तीर्थी-क्रियते becomes purified as a holy place, तदा एव at that very moment, मे my, बन्धः bondage, कर्म-आत्मकः consisting of fruitive reactions, उच्छ्वसिति becomes loosened, अतः thereafter

Anvaya: अथ सुहृत्तमं ज्ञातिम् अनन्यदैवतं मां बृहद्भ्यां दोर्भ्यां परिरप्स्यते, तदा एव मे आत्मा हि तीर्थीक्रियते, अतः कर्मात्मकः बन्धः च उच्छ्वसिति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ सुहृत्तमं ज्ञातिम् अनन्यदैवतं मां Then me, who is His best friend, relative, and has no other God, बृहद्भ्यां दोर्भ्यां परिरप्स्यते He will embrace with His two long arms, तदा एव at that very moment, मे आत्मा हि तीर्थीक्रियते indeed my soul and body will become as pure as a holy place, अतः and then, कर्मात्मकः बन्धः च the bondage of fruitive actions, उच्छ्वसिति will be loosened.

Simple Meaning: Then, He will embrace me—His dear friend and kinsman who knows no other God—with His long, mighty arms. At that very moment, my entire being will be purified like a holy place of pilgrimage, and the tight knots of my past fruitive actions will finally be loosened.

तदा वयं जन्मभृतो महीयसा नैवादृतो यो धिगमुष्य जन्म तत् ॥10.38.21॥

Simple Meaning: When the Lord of glorious fame addresses me as "My dear Akrura" while I am bowing before Him with joined palms, having felt the touch of His divine body, my life will finally be successful. Indeed, shame on the birth of anyone who is not recognized or respected by the Supreme Lord.

जगद्धेतू - जगत्पती - dvivachana - dvititiya vibhakti;

Anvaya: आद्यौ प्रधानपुरुषौ जगद्धेतू जगत्पती बलकेशवौ जगत्यर्थे स्वांशेन अवतीर्णौ (तौ अक्रूरः ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आद्यौ प्रधानपुरुषौ The two original and Supreme Persons, जगद्धेतू the causes of the universe, जगत्पती the lords of the universe, बलकेशवौ Balarama and Krishna, जगत्यर्थे for the sake of the world, स्वांशेन अवतीर्णौ as Their own portions (and original selves) who have descended—(Akrura saw them).

Simple Meaning: He saw Balarama and Krishna, the two original Supreme Personalities and the Lords of the universe. They, who are the very cause of creation, had descended into this world for its welfare, appearing in Their own divine forms.

दिशो वितिमिरा राजन् कुर्वाणौ प्रभया स्वया ।

यथा मारकतः शैलो रौप्यश्च कनकाचितौ ॥10.38.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: दिशः the directions, वि-तिमिराः free from darkness, राजन् O King (Parikshit), कुर्वाणौ making, प्रभया by the effulgence, स्वया Their own, यथा just as, मारकतः of emerald, शैलः a mountain, रौप्यः of silver, च and, कनक-आचितौ covered with gold

Anvaya: राजन्! स्वया प्रभया दिशः वितिमिराः कुर्वाणौ, (तौ) कनकाचितौ मारकतः रौप्यः च शैलः यथा (तथा रेजतुः)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King, स्वया प्रभया by Their own effulgence, दिशः वितिमिराः कुर्वाणौ making all directions free from darkness, (तौ) they two, कनकाचितौ covered with gold, मारकतः an emerald, रौप्यः च and a silver, शैलः mountain, यथा just as (were shining thus).

Simple Meaning: O King, by Their own divine effulgence, They were dispelling the darkness in all directions. They appeared just like two magnificent mountains—one of emerald (Krishna) and one of silver (Balarama)—both adorned with gold.

रथात्तूर्णमवप्लुत्य सोऽक्रूरः स्नेहविह्वलः ।

पपात चरणोपान्ते दण्डवद् राम कृष्णयोः ॥10.38.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: रथात् from the chariot, तूर्णम् quickly, अवप्लुत्य jumping down, सः अक्रूरः that Akrura, स्नेह-विह्वलः overwhelmed with affection, पपात fell down, चरण-उपान्ते at the proximity of Their feet, दण्ड-वत् like a staff (prostrating fully), राम-कृष्णयोः of Balarama and Krishna

Anvaya: सः अक्रूरः स्नेहविह्वलः रथात् तूर्णम् अवप्लुत्य रामकृष्णयोः चरणोपान्ते दण्डवत् पपात।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः अक्रूरः That Akrura, स्नेहविह्वलः overwhelmed with love, रथात् तूर्णम् अवप्लुत्य quickly jumping down from the chariot, रामकृष्णयोः चरणोपान्ते at the feet of Balarama and Krishna, दण्डवत् पपात fell down like a staff.

Simple Meaning: Overwhelmed by deep affection, Akrura quickly jumped down from his chariot and prostrated himself like a staff at the feet of Balarama and Krishna, offering his full obeisances.

भगवद्दर्शनाह्लादबाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणः ।

पुलकाचिताङ्ग औत्कण्ठ्यात् स्वाख्याने नाशकन् नृप ॥10.38.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवत्-दर्शन-आह्लाद-बाष्प-पर्याकुल-ईक्षणः whose eyes were overwhelmed by tears of joy born from seeing the Supreme Lord, पुलक-आचित-अङ्गः whose limbs were covered with eruptions of ecstasy (hairs standing on end), औत्कण्ठ्यात् due to intense longing/eagerness, स्व-आख्याने in announcing his own name, न not, अशकन् was able, नृप O King

Anvaya: नृप! भगवद्दर्शनाह्लादबाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणः पुलकाचिताङ्गः औत्कण्ठ्यात् स्वाख्याने न अशकन्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: नृप O King, भगवद्दर्शनाह्लादबाष्पपर्याकुलेक्षणः his eyes being clouded by tears of joy from seeing the Lord, पुलकाचिताङ्गः his body covered with horripilation, औत्कण्ठ्यात् and due to intense eagerness, स्वाख्याने न अशकन् he was not able to speak his own name.

Simple Meaning: O King, Akrura’s eyes were filled with tears of joy from seeing the Lord, and his body was so overwhelmed with ecstatic trembling, that because of his intense emotion, he could not even find the words to announce his own name.

Contextual Note: In Vedic etiquette, one typically introduces oneself when prostrating before a superior or the Divine. Akrura, despite being a mature minister and an elder relative, is so completely "lost" in the experience of Darshana (divine vision) that his physical senses momentarily fail to perform mundane tasks like speaking.

This verse beautifully captures the transition from Akrura the Messenger to Akrura the Devotee.

भगवांस्तमभिप्रेत्य रथाङ्गाङ्कितपाणिना ।

परिरेभेऽभ्युपाकृष्य प्रीतः प्रणतवत्सलः ॥10.38.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: भगवान् the Supreme Lord (Krishna), तम् him (Akrura), अभिप्रेत्य understanding (his heart/intent), रथ-अङ्ग-अङ्कित-पाणिना with His hand which is marked with the sign of the chariot wheel (the Sudarshana chakra), परिरेभे embraced, अभ्युपाकृष्य drawing him close, प्रीतः being pleased, प्रणत-वत्सलः He who is very kind to those who have surrendered

Anvaya: प्रणतवत्सलः भगवान् तम् अभिप्रेत्य प्रीतः (सन्) रथाङ्गाङ्कितपाणिना अभ्युपाकृष्य परिरेभे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: प्रणतवत्सलः भगवान् The Supreme Lord, who is compassionate toward those who surrender, तम् अभिप्रेत्य understanding him (Akrura's identity and devotion), प्रीतः (सन्) being pleased, रथाङ्गाङ्कितपाणिना with His hand marked with the sign of the disc, अभ्युपाकृष्य pulling him toward Himself, परिरेभे embraced him.

Simple Meaning: Lord Krishna, who is always affectionately disposed toward those who surrender to Him, understood Akrura's heart. Being greatly pleased, the Lord drew Akrura close with His hand—which bears the auspicious mark of the chariot wheel—and warmly embraced him.

संकर्षणश्च प्रणतमुपगुह्य महामनाः ।

गृहीत्वा पाणिना पाणी अनयत् सानुजो गृहम् ॥10.38.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: संकर्षणः Lord Balarama (Sankarshana), च and, प्रणतम् the prostrated one (Akrura), उपगुह्य embracing, महा-मनाः the high-minded/magnanimous one, गृहीत्वा having taken, पाणिना with His hand, पाणी both (Akrura’s) hands, अनयत् led, स-अनुजः along with His younger brother (Krishna), गृहम् to the house

Anvaya: महामनाः संकर्षणः च प्रणतम् उपगुह्य पाणिना पाणी गृहीत्वा सानुजः गृहम् अनयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: महामनाः संकर्षणः च And the magnanimous Sankarshana, प्रणतम् उपगुह्य embracing the bowed-down Akrura, पाणिना पाणी गृहीत्वा taking both of his hands in His own hand, सानुजः along with His younger brother, गृहम् अनयत् led him to the house.

Simple Meaning: The magnanimous Lord Balarama also embraced Akrura as he bowed in surrender. Then, taking both of Akrura's hands in His own, Balarama, accompanied by His younger brother Krishna, led him toward Their home.

पृष्ट्वाथ स्वागतं तस्मै निवेद्य च वरासनम् ।

प्रक्षाल्य विधिवत् पादौ मधुपर्कार्हणमाहरत् ॥10.38.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: पृष्ट्वा having asked, अथ then, स्वागतम् welcome, तस्मै to him (Akrura), निवेद्य having offered, च and, वर-आसनम् an excellent seat, प्रक्षाल्य having washed, विधिवत् according to scriptural injunctions, पादौ the two feet, मधुपर्क-अर्हणम् the worshipful offering of Madhuparka, आहरत् He brought

Anvaya: अथ तस्मै स्वागतं पृष्ट्वा वरासनं निवेद्य च, विधिवत् पादौ प्रक्षाल्य मधुपर्कार्हणम् आहरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अथ Then, तस्मै स्वागतं पृष्ट्वा having asked him if his journey was welcome (safe), वरासनं निवेद्य च and having offered him a distinguished seat, विधिवत् पादौ प्रक्षाल्य having washed his feet according to rite, मधुपर्कार्हणम् आहरत् He brought the worshipful offering of honey-mixture.

Simple Meaning: Then, after inquiring about the welfare of his journey and offering him an honored seat, the Lord washed Akrura’s feet according to traditional rites and presented him with the respectful offering of Madhuparka (a mixture of honey, curd, and ghee).

निवेद्य गां चातिथये संवाद्य श्रान्तमादृतः ।

अन्नं बहुगुणं मेध्यं श्रद्धयोपाहरद् विभुः ॥10.38.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: निवेद्य having offered, गाम् a cow, च and, अतिथये to the guest, संवाद्य having massaged (the feet), श्रान्तम् who was fatigued, आदृतः with great respect, अन्नम् food, बहु-गुणम् possessing many flavors/qualities, मेध्यम् pure/sanctified, श्रद्धया with faith, उपाहरत् brought/served, विभुः the Almighty (Krishna)

Anvaya: विभुः आदृतः अतिथये गां निवेद्य, श्रान्तं (तं) संवाद्य, श्रद्धया बहुगुणं मेध्यं च अन्नम् उपाहरत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: विभुः The Almighty Lord, आदृतः with great respect, अतिथये गां निवेद्य having offered a cow to the guest, श्रान्तं (तं) संवाद्य having massaged him who was tired (from the journey), श्रद्धया with faith/sincerity, बहुगुणं मेध्यं च अन्नम् many-flavored and pure food, उपाहरत् served.

Simple Meaning: The Almighty Lord, showing great respect to His guest, offered him a cow (as per tradition) and massaged his feet to relieve the fatigue of his journey. Then, with great sincerity, He served Akrura a sanctified feast consisting of many delicious and wholesome varieties.

तस्मै भुक्तवते प्रीत्या रामः परमधर्मवित् ।

मुखवासैर्गन्धमाल्यैः परां प्रीतिं व्यधात् पुनः ॥10.38.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मै to him (Akrura), भुक्तवते who had finished eating, प्रीत्या with affection, रामः Balarama, परम-धर्म-वित् the knower of the highest religious principles, मुख-वासैः with mouth-fresheners (betel nut, spices), गन्ध-माल्यैः with fragrant substances and flower garlands, पराम् supreme, प्रीतिम् satisfaction/delight, व्यधात् provided, पुनः again

Anvaya: परमधर्मवित् रामः भुक्तवते तस्मै प्रीत्या मुखवासैः गन्धमाल्यैः च पुनः परां प्रीतिं व्यधात्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: परमधर्मवित् रामः Balarama, the knower of the highest dharma, भुक्तवते तस्मै to him (Akrura) who had eaten, प्रीत्या with affection, मुखवासैः with mouth-perfuming spices, गन्धमाल्यैः च and with fragrances and garlands, पुनः परां प्रीतिं व्यधात् again provided supreme satisfaction.

Simple Meaning: After Akrura had finished his meal, Lord Balarama, who perfectly understood the highest principles of hospitality and duty, affectionately offered him aromatic spices to refresh his mouth, along with fragrant pastes and flower garlands, giving him the greatest pleasure.

पप्रच्छ सत्कृतं नन्दः कथं स्थ निरनुग्रहे ।

कंसे जीवति दाशार्ह सौनपाला इवावयः ॥10.38.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: पप्रच्छ asked, सत्कृतम् who had been well-honored, नन्दः Nanda Maharaja, कथम् how, स्थ you all are (faring), निरनुग्रहे in the one who is devoid of mercy, कंसे Kamsa, जीवति as long as (he) lives, दाशार्ह O descendant of Dasharha (Akrura), सौन-पालाः (under) a butcher-protector, इव like, अवयः sheep

Anvaya: (ततः) सत्कृतं (तं) नन्दः पप्रच्छ—दाशार्ह! निरनुग्रहे कंसे जीवति (सति), सौनपालाः अवयः इव (यूयं) कथं स्थ?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (ततः) Then, सत्कृतं (तं) to him (Akrura) who had been honored, नन्दः पप्रच्छ Nanda asked—दाशार्ह O Akrura, निरनुग्रहे कंसे जीवति while the merciless Kamsa is alive, सौनपालाः अवयः इव like sheep kept by a butcher, (यूयं) कथं स्थ how are you all (faring)?

Simple Meaning: After Akrura had been properly honored, Nanda asked him: "O descendant of Dasharha, while the merciless Kamsa is alive, how are you all managing to survive? You are like a flock of sheep being 'protected' by a butcher."

योऽवधीत् स्वस्वसुस्तोकान् क्रोशन्त्या असुतृप् खलः ।

किं नु स्वित्तत्प्रजानां वः कुशलं विमृशामहे ॥10.38.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: यः who, अवधीत् killed, स्व-स्वसुः of his own sister, तोकान् the infants, क्रोशन्त्याः while she was crying, असु-तृप् satisfying his own life-breaths (selfish), खलः the wicked one, किम् नु स्वित् what then, तत्-प्रजानाम् of his subjects, वः of you all, कुशलम् welfare, विमृशामहे can we consider/imagine

Anvaya: यः खलः असुतृप् क्रोशन्त्याः स्वस्वसुः तोकान् अवधीत्, (तस्य) तत्प्रजानां वः कुशलं किं नु स्वित् विमृशामहे?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः खलः That wicked person, असुतृप् who only cares for his own life, क्रोशन्त्याः स्वस्वसुः of his own crying sister, तोकान् the infants, अवधीत् killed; (तस्य) तत्प्रजानां of his subjects, वः (and) of you all, कुशलं किं नु स्वित् विमृशामहे how can we even imagine any welfare?

Simple Meaning: That wicked and selfish man (Kamsa) even killed the infants of his own sister while she was piteously crying. Since he is so cruel, how can we possibly imagine that his subjects, or you all, are experiencing any real welfare or peace?

इत्थं सूनृतया वाचा नन्देन सुसभाजितः ।

अक्रूरः परिपृष्टेन जहावध्वपरिश्रमम् ॥10.38.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: इत्थम् in this way, सूनृतया with truthful and pleasing, वाचा by words, नन्देन by Nanda Maharaja, सु-सभाजितः well-honored, अक्रूरः Akrura, परिपृष्टेन by the inquiries (about his welfare), जहौ gave up/forgot, अध्व-परिश्रमम् the fatigue of the road

Anvaya: इत्थं नन्देन सूनृतया वाचा सुसभाजितः परिपृष्टेन च अक्रूरः अध्वपरिश्रमं जहौ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: इत्थं In this way, नन्देन by Nanda, सूनृतया वाचा with kind and truthful words, सुसभाजितः being well-honored, परिपृष्टेन च and being questioned (about his welfare), अक्रूरः Akrura, अध्वपरिश्रमं जहौ forgot the exhaustion of his journey.

Simple Meaning: Having been thus honored by Nanda with kind, sincere words and thoughtful inquiries, Akrura completely forgot the physical exhaustion of his long journey.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “अक्रूरागमनं नाम अष्टात्रिंशः अध्यायः” ॥38॥

Thus ends the thirty-eighth discourse named “Akrura's arrival (in Vraja)”, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
