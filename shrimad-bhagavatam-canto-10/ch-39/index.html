दशमः स्कन्धः

श्री शुक उवाच

सुखोपविष्टः पर्यङ्के रामकृष्णोरुमानितः ।

लेभे मनोरथान् सर्वान् पथि यान् स चकार ह ॥10.39.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, सुख-उपविष्टः comfortably seated, पर्यङ्के on a couch, राम-कृष्ण-उरु-मानितः highly honored by Balarama and Krishna, लेभे he obtained, मनोरथान् the desires of the mind, सर्वान् all, पथि on the road, यान् which, सः he (Akrura), चकार ह had made (conceived)

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच—रामकृष्णोरुमानितः (सः अक्रूरः) पर्यङ्के सुखोपविष्टः (सन्), पथि यान् सर्वान् मनोरथान् चकार ह, (तान्) लेभे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— रामकृष्णोरुमानितः (सः अक्रूरः) Being highly honored by Balarama and Krishna, (Akrura), पर्यङ्के सुखोपविष्टः (सन्) while comfortably seated on a couch, पथि यान् सर्वान् मनोरथान् चकार ह mental desires which he had conceived while on the road, (तान्) लेभे obtained all those.

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Deeply honored by Balarama and Krishna, Akrura sat comfortably on a couch. There, he saw every one of the heart's desires he had meditated upon during his journey finally come to fruition.

किमलभ्यं भगवति प्रसन्ने श्रीनिकेतने ।

तथापि तत्परा राजन्न हि वाञ्छन्ति किन्ञ्चन ॥10.39.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: किम् what, अ-लभ्यम् unattainable, भगवति in the Supreme Lord, प्रसन्ने when satisfied, श्री-निकेतने the abode of the goddess of fortune, तथापि nevertheless, तत्-पराः those dedicated to Him, राजन् O King (Parikshit), न not, हि indeed, वाञ्छन्ति desire, किञ्चन anything

Anvaya: राजन्! श्रीनिकेतने भगवति प्रसन्ने (सति) किम् अलभ्यम्? तथापि तत्पराः (भक्ताः) किञ्चन न हि वाञ्छन्ति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King! श्रीनिकेतने भगवति प्रसन्ने (सति) When the Supreme Lord, who is the abode of Lakshmi, is pleased, किम् अलभ्यम् what is unattainable? तथापि Even then, तत्पराः (भक्ताः) those devotees who are dedicated to Him, किञ्चन न हि वाञ्छन्ति do not desire anything at all.

Simple Meaning: O King, when the Supreme Lord—the very abode of the Goddess of Fortune—is pleased, what can possibly be unattainable? Yet, those who are single-mindedly devoted to Him never desire anything for themselves.

सायंतनाशनं कृत्वा भगवान् देवकीसुतः ।

सुहृत्सु वृत्तं कंसस्य पप्रच्छान्यच्चिकीर्षितम् ॥10.39.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: सायंतन-अशनम् evening meal, कृत्वा having taken, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, देवकी-सुतः the son of Devaki, सुहृत्सु regarding their well-wishers, वृत्तम् the conduct (treatment), कंसस्य of Kamsa, पप्रच्छ inquired, अन्यत् other, चिकीर्षितम् what he intended to do

Anvaya: देवकीसुतः भगवान् सायंतनाशनं कृत्वा, सुहृत्सु कंसस्य वृत्तं अन्यत् चिकीर्षितं च पप्रच्छ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: देवकीसुतः भगवान् The Supreme Lord, the son of Devaki, सायंतनाशनं कृत्वा having finished His evening meal, सुहृत्सु कंसस्य वृत्तं (inquired about) Kamsa's treatment toward their relatives and friends, अन्यत् चिकीर्षितं च पप्रच्छ and asked about what else he (Kamsa) intended to do.

Simple Meaning: After finishing His evening meal, the Supreme Lord, Krishna, the son of Devaki, asked Akrura about how Kamsa was treating their friends and relatives, and what further plans he was plotting.

श्री भगवान् उवाच

तात सौम्यागतः कच्चित् स्वागतं भद्रमस्तु वः ।

अपि स्वज्ञातिबन्धूनामनमीवमनामयम् ॥10.39.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री भगवान् उवाच the Supreme Lord said, तात O dear uncle (fatherly figure), सौम्य O gentle one, आगतः have arrived, कच्चित् whether, स्वागतम् welcome, भद्रम् good fortune, अस्तु let there be, वः unto you, अपि whether, स्व-ज्ञाति-बन्धूनाम् of our own kinsmen and relatives, अनमीवम् health (freedom from mental distress), अनामयम् wellness (freedom from physical illness)

Anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच—सौम्य तात! कच्चित् आगतः? वः स्वागतम्। भद्रम् अस्तु। अपि स्वज्ञातिबन्धूनाम् अनमीवम् अनामयम् (अस्ति)?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच The Supreme Lord said— सौम्य तात! O gentle uncle! कच्चित् आगतः? Have you arrived safely? वः स्वागतम् You are most welcome. भद्रम् अस्तु Let there be all good fortune unto you. अपि स्वज्ञातिबन्धूनाम् अनमीवम् अनामयम् (अस्ति)? Are our own kinsmen and relatives free from mental distress and physical ailment?

Simple Meaning: The Supreme Lord said: O gentle uncle, welcome! I hope your journey was safe. May all good fortune be yours. Tell me, are our relatives and kinsmen healthy and doing well in both mind and body?

किं नु नः कुशलं पृच्छे एधमाने कुलामये ।

कंसे मातुलनाम्न्यङ्ग स्वानां नस्तत्प्रजासु च ॥10.39.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: किम् what, नु indeed, नः our, कुशलम् welfare, पृच्छे I should ask, एधमाने while he is prospering, कुल-आमये a disease to the family, कंसे Kamsa, मातुल-नाम्नि in the name of a maternal uncle, अङ्ग O dear one (Uncle Akrura), स्वानाम् of our kinsmen, नः our, तत्-प्रजासु of his subjects, च and

Anvaya: अङ्ग! कुलामये मातुलनाम्नि कंसे एधमाने (सति), नः स्वानां च तत्प्रजासु च कुशलं किं नु पृच्छे?

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अङ्ग! O dear Uncle Akrura! कुलामये (In the person of) a disease to the family, मातुलनाम्नि कंसे who bears the name of a maternal uncle, एधमाने (सति) while he is prospering, नः स्वानां च for our kinsmen, तत्प्रजासु च and for his subjects, कुशलं किं नु पृच्छे? what welfare can I possibly ask about?

Simple Meaning: O dear Uncle Akrura, while Kamsa—who is a literal disease to his own family despite the name "maternal uncle"—continues to thrive, how can I even ask about the welfare of our kinsmen or his subjects?

अहो अस्मदभूद् भूरि पित्रोर्वृजिनमार्ययोः।

यद्धेतोः पुत्रमरणं यद्धेतोर्बन्धनं तयोः ॥10.39.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: अहो alas, अस्मत् because of us, अभूत् there was, भूरि intense, पित्रोः for the parents, वृजिनम् suffering, आर्ययोः the noble ones, यत्-हेतोः because of whom (Krishna and Balarama), पुत्र-मरणम् the death of their sons, यत्-हेतोः because of whom, बन्धनम् imprisonment, तयोः of the two of them

Anvaya: अहो! आर्ययोः पित्रोः अस्मत् भूरि वृजिनम् अभूत्, यद्धेतोः (तयोः) पुत्रमरणं यद्धेतोः तयोः बन्धनं (जातम्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अहो! Alas! आर्ययोः पित्रोः For our noble parents, अस्मत् because of us, भूरि वृजिनम् अभूत् there has been great suffering, यद्धेतोः because of whom, (तयोः) पुत्रमरणं the death of their other sons occurred, यद्धेतोः because of whom, तयोः बन्धनं (जातम्) the imprisonment of those two (Devaki and Vasudeva) took place.

Simple Meaning: Alas! Our noble parents have suffered so much because of us. It was on our account that they had to endure the agonizing death of their sons and were subjected to long years of imprisonment.

दिष्ट्याद्य दर्शनं स्वानां मह्यं वः सौम्य काङ्क्षितम् ।

सञ्जातं वर्ण्यतां तात तवागमनकारणम् ॥10.39.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: दिष्ट्या by good fortune, अद्य today, दर्शनम् the sight, स्वानाम् of my own relatives, मह्यम् for me, वः of you, सौम्य O gentle one, काङ्क्षितम् desired, सञ्जातम् has occurred, वर्ण्यताम् please describe, तात O dear uncle, तव your, आगमन-कारणम् the reason for coming

Anvaya: सौम्य! अद्य मह्यं काङ्क्षितं वः स्वानां दर्शनं दिष्ट्या सञ्जातम्। तात! तव आगमनकारणं वर्ण्यताम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सौम्य! O gentle one! अद्य today, मह्यं काङ्क्षितं desired by me, वः स्वानां दर्शनं the sight of you, my own relative, दिष्ट्या सञ्जातम् has occurred by good fortune. तात! O dear uncle! तव आगमनकारणं your reason for coming, वर्ण्यताम् please be described.

Simple Meaning: O gentle one, by great good fortune, I am finally able to see you—a desire I have long cherished. My dear uncle, please tell me the reason for your visit.

श्री शुक उवाच

पृष्टो भगवता वर्णयामास माधवः।

वैरानुबन्धं यदुषु वसुदेववधोद्यमम् ॥10.38.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said, पृष्टः being asked, भगवता by the Supreme Lord, वर्णयामास he described, माधवः Akrura (descendant of Madhu), वैर-अनुबन्धम् the continuous enmity, यदुषु toward the Yadus, वसुदेव-वध-उद्यमम् the attempt to kill Vasudeva

Anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच—भगवता पृष्टः माधवः, यदुषु वैरानुबन्धं वसुदेववधोद्यमं (च) वर्णयामास।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुकः उवाच Shri Shuka said— भगवता पृष्टः being questioned by the Supreme Lord, माधवः Akrura, यदुषु वैरानुबन्धं the ongoing hostility toward the Yadus, वसुदेववधोद्यमं (च) and the attempt to murder Vasudeva, वर्णयामास described (fully).

Simple Meaning: Shri Shuka said: Upon being questioned by the Supreme Lord, Akrura explained everything—Kamsa's relentless hatred toward the Yadu dynasty and his specific plot to have Vasudeva killed.

यत्संदेशो यदर्थं वा दूतः संप्रेषितः स्वयम् ।

यदुक्तं नारदेनास्य स्वजन्मानकदुन्दुभेः ॥10.39.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: यत्-संदेशः what the message was, यत्-अर्थम् for what purpose, वा or, दूतः as a messenger, संप्रेषितः sent, स्वयम् himself (Akrura), यत्-उक्तम् what was said, नारदेन by Narada, अस्य regarding Him (Krishna), स्व-जन्म-आनकदुन्दुभेः the birth of Himself from Vasudeva (Anakadundubhi)

Anvaya: (सः अक्रूरः) यत्संदेशः यदर्थं वा स्वयं दूतः संप्रेषितः, नारदेन अस्य स्वजन्मानकदुन्दुभेः यदुक्तं (तत् सर्वं वर्णयामास)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः अक्रूरः) He (Akrura), यत्संदेशः what the message was, यदर्थं वा or for what purpose, स्वयं दूतः संप्रेषितः he himself was sent as a messenger, नारदेन by Narada, अस्य regarding Him (Krishna), स्वजन्मानकदुन्दुभेः regarding His own birth from Vasudeva, यदुक्तं (तत् सर्वं वर्णयामास) what was said (described all of that).

Simple Meaning: Akrura explained the nature of the message he carried and the purpose for which he was sent. He also revealed what Narada Muni had told Kamsa regarding Krishna's birth as the son of Vasudeva.

श्रुत्वाक्रूरवचः कृष्णो बलश्च परवीरहा ।

प्रहस्य नन्दं पितरं राज्ञाऽऽदिष्टं विजज्ञतुः ॥10.39.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रुत्वा having heard, अक्रूर-वचः the words of Akrura, कृष्णः Krishna, बलः Balarama, च and, पर-वीर-हा the destroyer of enemy heroes, प्रहस्य laughing, नन्दम् to Nanda, पितरम् the father, राज्ञा by the king (Kamsa), आदिष्टम् what was commanded, विजज्ञतुः they informed

Anvaya: अक्रूरवचः श्रुत्वा परवीरहा कृष्णः बलः च प्रहस्य, पितरं नन्दं राज्ञा आदिष्टं विजज्ञतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अक्रूरवचः श्रुत्वा Having heard the words of Akrura, परवीरहा कृष्णः बलः च Krishna and Balarama, the destroyers of enemy heroes, प्रहस्य laughing, पितरं नन्दं (to their) father Nanda, राज्ञा आदिष्टं what had been commanded by the King, विजज्ञतुः informed (him).

Simple Meaning: After listening to Akrura's words, Krishna and Balarama, the slayers of enemy warriors, simply laughed. They then informed their father, Nanda Maharaja, about the orders the King had issued.

गोपान् समादिशत् सोऽपि गृह्यतां सर्वगोरसः।

उपायनानि गृह्णीध्वम् युज्यन्तां शकटानि च ॥10.39.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोपान् the cowherd men, समादिशत् ordered, सः he (Nanda), अपि also, गृह्यताम् let it be collected, सर्व-गो-रसः all the produce of the cows (milk products), उपायनानि gifts (presentations), गृह्णीध्वम् please take, युज्यन्ताम् let them be prepared, शकटानि the wagons, च and

Anvaya: सः (नन्दः) अपि गोपान् समादिशत्—"सर्वगोरसः गृह्यताम्, उपायनानि गृह्णीध्वम्। शकटानि च युज्यन्ताम्।"

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सः (नन्दः) अपि He (Nanda) also, गोपान् समादिशत् ordered the cowherds— "सर्वगोरसः गृह्यताम् let all the dairy products be collected. उपायनानि गृह्णीध्वम् Take the gifts (for the king), शकटानि च युज्यन्ताम् and let the wagons be readied."

Simple Meaning: Nanda then issued orders to the cowherds: "Collect all the available milk products, gather various gifts for the King, and prepare the wagons for our journey."

यास्यामः श्वो मधुपुरीं दास्यामो नृपते रसान् ।

द्रक्ष्यामः सुमहत् पर्व यान्ति जानपदाः किल ।

एवमाघोषयत् क्षत्त्रा नन्दगोपः स्वगोकुले ॥10.39.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: यास्यामः we shall go, श्वः tomorrow, मधु-पुरीम् to Mathura (the city of Madhu), दास्यामः we shall give, नृपते to the king, रसान् the delicious products (of the cows), द्रक्ष्यामः we shall see, सु-महत् a very great, पर्व festival, यान्ति are going, जानपदाः the people of the countryside, किल indeed, एवम् in this way, आघोषयत् caused to be announced, क्षत्त्रा by the civic officer (messenger), नन्द-गोपः Nanda Gopa, स्व-गोकुले in his own Gokula

Anvaya: "श्वः मधुपुरीं यास्यामः, नृपते रसान् दास्यामः, सुमहत् पर्व द्रक्ष्यामः, जानपदाः (तत्र) यान्ति किल" – एवं नन्दगोपः क्षत्त्रा स्वगोकुले आघोषयत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: "श्वः मधुपुरीं यास्यामः Tomorrow we shall go to Mathura, नृपते रसान् दास्यामः we shall give the dairy products to the king, सुमहत् पर्व द्रक्ष्यामः we shall see the great festival, जानपदाः (तत्र) यान्ति किल indeed, the country folk are all going there" – एवं in this way, नन्दगोपः Nanda Gopa, क्षत्त्रा through a messenger, स्वगोकुले आघोषयत् had it announced in his own Gokula.

Simple Meaning: "Tomorrow we will head to Mathura to present our dairy offerings to the King and witness the great festival that everyone is talking about." This was the proclamation Nanda had his messenger announce throughout the village of Gokula.

गोप्यस्तास्तदुपश्रुत्य बभूवुर्व्यथिता भृशम् ।

रामकृष्णौ पुरीं नेतुमक्रूरं व्रजमागतम् ॥10.39.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः the gopis, ताः they, तत्-उपश्रुत्य hearing this, बभूवुः became, व्यथिताः distressed, भृशम् greatly, राम-कृष्णौ Balarama and Krishna, पुरीम् to the city, नेतुम् to take away, अक्रूरम् Akrura, व्रजम् to Vraja, आगतम् having come

Anvaya: ताः गोप्यः, रामकृष्णौ पुरीं नेतुम् अक्रूरं व्रजम् आगतम् (इति) तदुपश्रुत्य भृशं व्यथिताः बभूवुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ताः गोप्यः Those gopis, रामकृष्णौ पुरीं नेतुम् to take Balarama and Krishna to the city, अक्रूरं व्रजम् आगतम् (इति) that Akrura had come to Vraja, तदुपश्रुत्य hearing that, भृशं व्यथिताः बभूवुः became greatly distressed.

Simple Meaning: When the gopis heard that Akrura had arrived in Vraja specifically to take Krishna and Balarama away to the city of Mathura, they became overwhelmed with deep distress.

शोकापहानि नर्माणि प्रोद्दामचरितानि च ॥10.39.17॥

Simple Meaning: The gopis were lost in thought, remembering Krishna's graceful way of walking, His charming gestures, and His affectionate smiles and looks. They recalled His playful words that could drive away any sadness, and His awe-inspiring, heroic deeds, falling deeper into a state of divine absorption.

चिन्तन्त्यो मुकुन्दस्य भीता विरहकातराः ।

Word-by-word meaning: चिन्तन्त्यः thinking of, मुकुन्दस्य of Mukunda (Krishna), भीताः frightened, विरह-कातराः distressed by the prospect of separation, समेताः gathering together, सङ्घशः in groups, प्रोचुः they spoke, अश्रु-मुख्यः their faces covered in tears, अच्युत-आशयाः whose minds were absorbed in Achyuta (the infallible Lord)

Anvaya: मुकुन्दस्य चिन्तन्त्यः, भीताः, विरहकातराः, अच्युताशयाः, अश्रुमुख्यः (ताः गोप्यः) सङ्घशः समेताः प्रोचुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: मुकुन्दस्य चिन्तन्त्यः Thinking of Mukunda, भीताः frightened, विरहकातराः distressed by the thought of separation, अच्युताशयाः whose consciousness was fixed on Achyuta, अश्रुमुख्यः (ताः गोप्यः) those gopis whose faces were covered in tears, सङ्घशः समेताः gathering in groups, प्रोचुः spoke.

Simple Meaning: Thinking of Mukunda and terrified by the prospect of being separated from Him, the gopis—their hearts entirely absorbed in Krishna and their faces bathed in tears—gathered together in groups and began to speak.

गोप्य ऊचुः

अहो विधातस्तव न क्वचिद् दया संयोज्य मैत्र्या प्रणयेन देहिनः ।

तांश्चाकृतार्थान् वियुनङ्क्ष्यपार्थकं विक्रीडितं तेऽर्भकचेष्टितं यथा ॥10.39.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: गोप्यः ऊचुः the gopis said, अहो oh, विधातः O creator (Brahma/Providence), तव your, न not, क्वचित् anywhere, दया mercy, संयोज्य having joined, मैत्र्या with friendship, प्रणयेन with affection, देहिनः living beings, तान् them, च and, अकृत-अर्थान् without having fulfilled their desires, वियुनङ्क्षि you separate, अपार्थकम् uselessly, विक्रीडितम् play, ते your, अर्भक-चेष्टितम् the activity of a child, यथा like

Anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः—अहो विधातः! तव क्वचित् दया न (अस्ति)। देहिनः मैत्र्या प्रणयेन (च) संयोज्य, तान् च अकृतार्थान् अपार्थकं वियुनङ्क्षि। ते विक्रीडितम् अर्भकचेष्टितं यथा (अस्ति)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: गोप्यः ऊचुः The gopis said— अहो विधातः! Oh Creator! तव क्वचित् दया न (अस्ति) You have no mercy at all. देहिनः मैत्र्या प्रणयेन (च) संयोज्य Having joined living beings in friendship and affection, तान् च अकृतार्थान् and before they can fulfill their desires, अपार्थकं वियुनङ्क्षि you separate them pointlessly. ते विक्रीडितम् Your play, अर्भकचेष्टितं यथा (अस्ति) is just like the whimsical actions of a child.

Simple Meaning: The gopis said: O Creator, you truly have no mercy. First, you bring people together in bonds of deep friendship and love, and then, before they can find fulfillment, you pointlessly tear them apart. Your dealings are like the unpredictable play of a child, who builds something only to destroy it for no reason.

यस्त्वं प्रदर्श्यासितकुन्तलावृतं मुकुन्दवक्त्रं सुकपोलमुन्नसम् ।

शोकापनोदस्मितलेशसुन्दरं करोषि पारोक्ष्यमसाधु ते कृतम् ॥10.39.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: यः who, त्वम् you, प्रदर्श्य having shown, असित-कुन्तल-आवृतम् covered by dark locks of hair, मुकुन्द-वक्त्रम् the face of Mukunda, सु-कपोलम् with beautiful cheeks, उत्-नसम् with a prominent nose, शोक-अपनोद-स्मित-लेश-सुन्दरम् beautiful with a faint smile that dispels all grief, करोषि you make, पारोक्ष्यम् invisible (out of sight), असाधु poorly/unfairly, ते by you, कृतम् has been done

Anvaya: यः त्वं सुकपोलम् उन्नसम् असितकुन्तलावृतं शोकापनोदस्मितलेशसुन्दरं मुकुन्दवक्त्रं प्रदर्श्य (पुनः) पारोक्ष्यं करोषि, ते (इदं) कृतम् असाधु।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: यः त्वं You who, सुकपोलम् उन्नसम् (His face) with beautiful cheeks and a shapely nose, असितकुन्तलावृतं framed by dark curly hair, शोकापनोदस्मितलेशसुन्दरं and made lovely by a gentle smile that wipes away all sorrow, मुकुन्दवक्त्रं प्रदर्श्य having shown the face of Mukunda, (पुनः) पारोक्ष्यं करोषि (now) make it hidden from our view; ते (इदं) कृतम् असाधु this act done by you is very cruel/unfair.

Simple Meaning: After showing us Mukunda’s beautiful face—with its fine nose, lovely cheeks, and dark curls, all made radiant by a gentle smile that heals our every sorrow—you are now taking Him away from our sight. This act of yours, O Creator, is truly cruel and unfair.

क्रूरस्त्वमक्रूरसमाख्यया स्म नश्चक्षुर्हि दत्तं हरसे बताज्ञवत् ।

Anvaya: (हे विधातः!) त्वम् अक्रूरसमाख्यया क्रूरः (असि), येन (चक्षुषा) वयं मधुद्विषः एकदेशे त्वदीयं अखिलसर्गसौष्ठवम् अद्राक्ष्म, दत्तं नः तत् चक्षुः हि अज्ञवत् हरसे। बत!

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (हे विधातः!) (O Creator!) त्वम् अक्रूरसमाख्यया क्रूरः (असि) You are truly cruel, though you come under the name "Akrura" (not cruel). येन (चक्षुषा) By those eyes (you gave us), वयं मधुद्विषः एकदेशे त्वदीयं अखिलसर्गसौष्ठवम् अद्राक्ष्म through which we saw the entire perfection of your creation in one single place—the form of Krishna, दत्तं नः तत् चक्षुः हि अज्ञवत् हरसे you are now, like a fool, taking those very eyes away from us. बत! Alas!

Simple Meaning: O Creator, you are truly heartless! You have come here in the form of this man named Akrura ("The Not-Cruel One"), but your actions are anything but kind. You gave us eyes only so we could behold the absolute perfection of your entire creation in the person of Krishna. Now, like someone who doesn't understand the value of what they've given, you are cruelly snatching those eyes back. What a tragedy!

Alas, He is always looking for someone new and won't even glance at us, even though He is the cause of our current misery. We gave up everything—our homes, families, children, and husbands—just to serve Him, and yet He leaves us like this.

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw that Supreme Person), चारु who was beautiful, प्रसन्नवदनं with a cheerful face, चारुहासनिरीक्षणं with a charming smile and look, सुभ्रून्नसं with lovely eyebrows and a high nose, चारुकर्णं with beautiful ears, सुकपोलारुणाधरम् and with attractive cheeks and reddish lips.

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw the Lord, whose face was radiantly cheerful and exceptionally beautiful. His smile and glances were captivating; He had perfectly shaped eyebrows, a prominent, well-formed nose, and beautiful ears. His cheeks were lovely, complemented by the natural reddish hue of His lips.

प्रलम्बपीवरभुजं तुङ्गांसोरःस्थलश्रियम् ।

कम्बुकण्ठं निम्ननाभिं वलिमत्पल्लवोदरम् ॥10.39.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रलम्ब-पीवर-भुजम् possessing long and stout arms, तुङ्ग-अंस-उरःस्थल-श्रियम् with the beauty of high shoulders and a broad chest, कम्बु-कण्ठम् having a neck shaped like a conch shell, निम्न-नाभिम् with a deep navel, वलिमत्-पल्लव-उदरम् having an abdomen with skin-folds (valis) as delicate as a leaf

Anvaya: (सः) प्रलम्बपीवरभुजं तुङ्गांसोरःस्थलश्रियं कम्बुकण्ठं निम्ननाभिं वलिमत्पल्लवोदरं (पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord), प्रलम्बपीवरभुजं who had long, powerful arms, तुङ्गांसोरःस्थलश्रियं the beauty of whose high shoulders and broad chest was magnificent, कम्बुकण्ठं whose neck was (shapely) like a conch, निम्ननाभिं who had a deep navel, वलिमत्पल्लवोदरम् and whose abdomen, marked with three folds, was as tender as a young leaf.

Simple Meaning: Akrura beheld the Lord's majestic form: He had long, sturdy arms and a broad, beautiful chest with high shoulders. His neck was elegantly shaped like a conch shell, His navel was deep, and His waist was adorned with delicate folds, appearing as soft as a new leaf.

बृहत्कटितटश्रोणि करभोरुद्वयान्वितम् ।

चारुजानुयुगं चारुजङ्घायुगलसंयुतम् ॥10.39.49॥

Word-by-word meaning: बृहत्-कटि-तट-श्रोणि-करभ-ऊरु-द्वय-अन्वितम् with broad hips and a majestic waist, possessed of two thighs shaped like the trunk of an elephant, चारु-जानु-युगम् with a pair of beautiful knees, चारु-जङ्घा-युगल-संयुतम् endowed with a pair of charming calves

Anvaya: (सः) बृहत्कटितटश्रोणिकरभोरुद्वयान्वितं चारुजानुयुगं चारुजङ्घायुगलसंयुतं (पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord), बृहत्कटितटश्रोणिकरभोरुद्वयान्वितं who had a broad waist and majestic hips, and possessed two thighs as graceful as an elephant's trunk, चारुजानुयुगं with a pair of beautiful knees, चारुजङ्घायुगलसंयुतम् and a pair of charming calves.

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw that the Lord had broad, majestic hips and a powerful waist. His thighs were gracefully tapered like the trunk of an elephant, and His knees and calves were perfectly formed and beautiful to behold.

तुङ्गगुल्फारुणनखव्रातदीधितिभिर्वृतम् ।

नवाङ्गुल्यङ्गुष्ठदलैर्विलसत्पादपङ्कजम् ॥10.39.50॥

Word-by-word meaning: तुङ्ग-गुल्फ-अरुण-नख-व्रात-दीधितिभिः with the glow from the collection of reddish nails and high ankles, वृतम् surrounded/adorned, नव-अङ्गुलि-अङ्गुष्ठ-दलैः with petals in the form of fresh fingers and toes, विलसत्-पाद-पङ्कजम् possessing resplendent lotus-like feet

Anvaya: (सः) तुङ्गगुल्फारुणनखव्रातदीधितिभिः वृतं नवाङ्गुल्यङ्गुष्ठदलैः विलसत्पादपङ्कजं (पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord), तुङ्गगुल्फारुणनखव्रातदीधितिभिः वृतं whose feet were surrounded by the effulgence of His high ankles and the collection of His reddish nails, नवाङ्गुल्यङ्गुष्ठदलैः विलसत्पादपङ्कजम् and whose lotus-feet were resplendent with toes and thumbs that appeared like fresh petals.

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw the Lord’s magnificent lotus-feet. They were framed by high, shapely ankles and a brilliant glow emanating from His reddish nails. His toes appeared like the tender, fresh petals of a lotus flower, making His feet look exquisitely beautiful and radiant.

सुमहार्हमणिव्रातकिरीटकटकाङ्गदैः ।

कटिसूत्रब्रह्मसूत्रहारनूपुरकुण्डलैः ॥10.39.51॥

Word-by-word meaning: सु-महार्ह-मणि-व्रात-किरीट-कटक-अङ्गदैः with a crown, bracelets, and armlets set with highly valuable jewels, कटिसूत्र-ब्रह्मसूत्र-हार-नूपुर-कुण्डलैः with a waist-belt, sacred thread, necklaces, anklets, and earrings

Anvaya: (सः) सुमहार्हमणिव्रातकिरीटकटकाङ्गदैः कटिसूत्रब्रह्मसूत्रहारनूपुरकुण्डलैः (च युतं पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord who was adorned), सुमहार्हमणिव्रातकिरीटकटकाङ्गदैः with a crown, bangles, and armlets encrusted with a multitude of extremely precious gems, कटिसूत्रब्रह्मसूत्रहारनूपुरकुण्डलैः (च) and with a golden waist-band, a sacred thread, necklaces, anklets, and earrings.

Simple Meaning: The Lord was adorned with a crown, bracelets, and armlets, all set with priceless, shimmering jewels. He also wore a golden waist-belt, a sacred thread, exquisite necklaces, tinkling anklets, and beautiful earrings that framed His face.

भ्राजमानं पद्मकरं शङ्खचक्रगदाधरम् ।

श्रीवत्सवक्षसं भ्राजत्कौस्तुभं वनमालिनम् ॥10.39.52॥

Word-by-word meaning: भ्राजमानम् resplendent/shining, पद्म-करम् holding a lotus in His hand, शङ्ख-चक्र-गदा-धरम् bearing the conch, disc, and club, श्रीवत्स-वक्षसम् having the Shrivatsa mark on His chest, भ्राजत्-कौस्तुभम् wearing the glowing Kaustubha gem, वन-मालिनम् adorned with a forest-flower garland (Vanamala)

Anvaya: (सः) भ्राजमानं पद्मकरं शङ्खचक्रगदाधरं श्रीवत्सवक्षसं भ्राजत्कौस्तुभं वनमालिनं (पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord), भ्राजमानं who was effulgent, पद्मकरं holding a lotus in one hand, शङ्खचक्रगदाधरं bearing the conch shell, Sudarshana disc, and mace, श्रीवत्सवक्षसं with the mark of Shrivatsa on His chest, भ्राजत्कौस्तुभं wearing the brilliantly shining Kaustubha jewel, वनमालिनम् and adorned with a garland of fresh forest flowers.

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw the Lord looking absolutely radiant. He held a lotus, a conch, a disc, and a mace in His four hands. On His chest was the sacred Shrivatsa mark and the glowing Kaustubha gem, and He was draped in a beautiful garland of fragrant forest flowers.

सुनन्दनन्दप्रमुखैः पार्षदैः सनकादिभिः ।

सुरेशैर्ब्रह्मरुद्राद्यैर्नवभिश्च द्विजोत्तमैः ॥10.30.53॥

Word-by-word meaning: सुनन्द-नन्द-प्रमुखैः led by Sunanda and Nanda, पार्षदैः by His personal attendants, सनक-आदिभिः by Sanaka and the other Kumaras, सुर-ईशैः by the lords of the demigods, ब्रह्म-रुद्र-आद्यैः beginning with Brahma and Rudra (Shiva), नवभिः by the nine, च and, द्विज-उत्तमैः by the best of the twice-born (the great sages)

Anvaya: (सः) सुनन्दनन्दप्रमुखैः पार्षदैः, सनकादिभिः, ब्रह्मरुद्राद्यैः सुरेशैः, नवभिः द्विजोत्तमैः च (सेवितं पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord served), सुनन्दनन्दप्रमुखैः पार्षदैः by His personal attendants headed by Sunanda and Nanda, सनकादिभिः by the Kumaras starting with Sanaka, ब्रह्मरुद्राद्यैः सुरेशैः by the chiefs of the gods such as Brahma and Shiva, नवभिः द्विजोत्तमैः च and by the nine most exalted brahmanas (sages like Marichi).

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw the Lord being served and revered from all sides: by His personal attendants like Sunanda and Nanda; by the great sages headed by Sanaka; by the masters of the universe, Brahma and Shiva; and by the nine ancient and exalted sages.

प्रह्लादनारदवसुप्रमुखैर्भागवतोतमैः ।

स्तूयमानं पृथग्भावैर्वचोभिरमलात्मभिः ॥10.39.54॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रह्लाद-नारद-वसु-प्रमुखैः led by Prahlada, Narada, and Uparichara Vasu, भागवत-उत्तमैः by the most exalted devotees of the Lord, स्तूयमानम् being praised/glorified, पृथग्-भावैः with various distinct emotional moods (bhavas), वचोभिः with words, अमल-आत्मभिः by those whose hearts/souls are pure

Anvaya: (सः) प्रह्लादनारदवसुप्रमुखैः अमलात्मभिः भागवतोत्तमैः पृथग्भावैः वचोभिः (च) स्तूयमानं (पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord), प्रह्लादनारदवसुप्रमुखैः by the greatest of the Lord's devotees, headed by Prahlada, Narada, and Vasu, अमलात्मभिः who are pure-hearted, पृथग्भावैः वचोभिः (च) with prayers and words expressing their various unique transcendental sentiments, स्तूयमानम् being glorified.

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw the Supreme Lord being glorified by His most exalted, pure-hearted devotees like Prahlada, Narada, and Uparichara Vasu. Each of them was offering beautiful prayers, expressing their own unique and profound devotion through their words.

श्रिया पुष्ट्या गिरा कान्त्या कीर्त्या तुष्ट्येलयोर्जया ।

विद्ययाविद्यया शक्त्या मायया च निषेवितम् ॥10.39.55॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रिया by Shri (the goddess of fortune), पुष्ट्या by Pushti (strength/abundance), गिरा by Gira (speech/wisdom), कान्त्या by Kanti (beauty), कीर्त्या by Kirti (fame), तुष्ट्या by Tushti (contentment), इलया by Ila (the earth), ऊर्जया by Urja (vitality/power), विद्यया by Vidya (internal knowledge), अविद्यया by Avidya (the external/limiting energy - which binds the jiva to the mundane existence), शक्त्या by Shakti (potency), मायया by Maya (the creative illusion), च and, निषेवितम् served/worshipped

Anvaya: (सः) श्रिया, पुष्ट्या, गिरा, कान्त्या, कीर्त्या, तुष्ट्या, इलया, ऊर्जया, विद्यया, अविद्यया, शक्त्या, मायया च निषेवितं (पुरुषं ददर्श)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (सः) He (Akrura) (saw the Lord), श्रिया by Shri (Fortune), पुष्ट्या by Pushti (Nourishment), गिरा by Gira (Speech), कान्त्या by Kanti (Beauty), कीर्त्या by Kirti (Fame), तुष्ट्या by Tushti (Satisfaction), इलया by Ila (Earth), ऊर्जया by Urja (Energy), विद्यया by Vidya (Knowledge), अविद्यया by Avidya (Ignorance/External power - which binds the jiva to the mundane existence), शक्त्या by Shakti (Potency), मायया च and by Maya (Illusion), निषेवितम् being served.

Simple Meaning: Akrura saw the Supreme Lord being attended to by His divine energies and personified qualities: Shri, Pushti, Gira, Kanti, Kirti, and Tushti. He was served by Earth and Power, by the internal and external energies of knowledge and illusion, and by all His various potencies.

विलोक्य सुभृशं प्रीतो भक्त्या परमया युतः ।

हृष्यत्तनूरुहो भावपरिक्लिन्नात्मलोचनः ॥10.39.56॥

Word-by-word meaning: विलोक्य having seen (this vision), सु-भृशम् very much/greatly, प्रीतः pleased/delighted, भक्त्या with devotion, परमया supreme/transcendental, युतः endowed with, हृष्यत्-तनूरुहः (his) hair standing on end (due to ecstasy), भाव-परिक्लिन्न-आत्म-लोचनः with his heart and eyes overflowing with emotion (tears)

Anvaya: (अक्रूरः) (तं) विलोक्य सुभृशं प्रीतः, परमया भक्त्या युतः, हृष्यत्तनूरुहः, भावपरिक्लिन्नात्मलोचनः (अभवत्)।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: (तं) विलोक्य Having seen Him, सुभृशं प्रीतः (Akrura became) extremely delighted; परमया भक्त्या युतः endowed with supreme devotion, हृष्यत्तनूरुहः his body hair stood on end in ecstasy, भावपरिक्लिन्नात्मलोचनः and both his heart and eyes were moistened by the intensity of his transcendental emotion.

Simple Meaning: Upon witnessing this magnificent vision, Akrura was overwhelmed with joy. Filled with supreme devotion, his hair stood on end, and his heart melted with love, causing his eyes to overflow with tears of ecstasy.

गिरा गद्गदयास्तौषीत् सत्त्वमालम्ब्य सात्वतः ।

प्रणम्य मूर्ध्नावहितः कृताञ्जलिपुटः शनैः ॥10.39.57॥

Word-by-word meaning: गिरा with a voice, गद्गदया choked/faltering (with emotion), अस्तौषीत् began to offer prayers, सत्त्वम् composure/courage, आलम्ब्य regaining/taking hold of, सात्वतः the devotee (Akrura - belonging to the Satvata clan), प्रणम्य having bowed down, मूर्ध्ना with his head, अवहितः attentive/focused, कृत-अञ्जलि-पुटः with palms joined in prayer, शनैः slowly

Anvaya: सात्वतः मूर्ध्ना प्रणम्य, सत्त्वम् आलम्ब्य, अवहितः कृताञ्जलिपुटः (च भूत्वा), गद्गदया गिरा शनैः अस्तौषीत्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: सात्वतः The devotee (Akrura), मूर्ध्ना प्रणम्य having prostrated with his head, सत्त्वम् आलम्ब्य regaining his composure, अवहितः focused, कृताञ्जलिपुटः (च भूत्वा) and with palms joined, गद्गदया गिरा with a faltering voice, शनैः अस्तौषीत् slowly began to offer prayers.

Simple Meaning: Akrura, the great devotee, prostrated himself before the Lord. Regaining his composure and focusing his mind, he joined his palms in reverence and, with a voice trembling with deep emotion, began to offer his prayers slowly and soulfully.

इति श्रीमद्भागवते महापुराणे पारमहंस्यां संहितायां दशमस्कन्धे पूर्वार्धे “अक्रूरप्रतियाने नाम​” एकोनचत्वारिंशः अध्यायः ॥39॥

Thus ends the thirty-ninth discourse forming part of the story of “Akrura’s return to Mathura (along with Krishna and Balarama)”, in the first half of Book Ten of the great and glorious Bhagavata-Purana.
