श्री शुक उवाच

ततश्च पौगण्डवयः श्रितौ व्रजे बभूवतुस्तौ पशुपालसम्मतौ ।

गाश्चारयन्तौ सखिभिः समं पदैर्वृन्दावनं पुण्यमतीव चक्रतुः ॥10.15.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then/thereafter, च and, पौगण्ड-वयः the age of pauganda (6–10 years), श्रितौ having entered/attained, व्रजे in Vraja, बभूवतुः they became, तौ those two (Krishna and Balarama), पशु-पाल-सम्मतौ entrusted/accepted by the cowherd men (as fit for tending cows), गाः cows, चारयन्तौ while grazing/herding, सखिभिः with friends, समम् together, पदैः by their footsteps/presence, वृन्दावनम् Vrindavana, पुण्यम् sacred/holy, अतीव exceedingly, चक्रतुः they made

Anvaya: ततः च व्रजे पौगण्डवयः श्रितौ तौ पशुपालसम्मतौ बभूवतुः। सखिभिः समं गाः चारयन्तौ पदैः वृन्दावनम् अतीव पुण्यं चक्रतुः।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ततः Thereafter, च and, व्रजे in Vraja, पौगण्डवयः the pauganda age, श्रितौ having entered, तौ those two, पशुपालसम्मतौ accepted by the cowherd men (for herding cows), बभूवतुः they became. सखिभिः समं Together with friends, गाः चारयन्तौ while grazing cows, पदैः by their footsteps, वृन्दावनम् Vrindavana, अतीव पुण्यं exceedingly sacred, चक्रतुः they made.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: Thereafter, those two — Krishna and Balarama — having entered the pauganda age while living in Vraja, were entrusted by the cowherd elders with the tending of the cows. Herding the cows together with their friends, they made Vrindavana exceedingly holy by the touch of their feet.

Note: In Padma Purana

शुक्लाष्ट्मी कार्तिकस्य स्मृता गोपाष्ट्मी बुधैः । तत् दिनात् वासुदेवोऽभूत् गोपः पुर्वं तु वत्सपः ॥

The eighth day of the bright half of Kartika is remembered by the wise as Gopashtmi. From that day onwards, Krishna (the Son of Vasudeva) passed as a cowherd; before this however, He was a (mere) keeper of calves.

तन्माधवो वेणुमुदीरयन् वृतो गोपैर्गृणद्भिः स्वयशो बलान्वितः।

पशून् पुरस्कृत्य पशव्यमाविशद् विहर्तुकामः कुसुमाकरं वनम् ॥10.15.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् then/therefore, माधवः Madhava (Krishna), वेणुम् the flute, उदीरयन् sounding/vibrating, वृतः surrounded, गोपैः by the cowherd boys, गृणद्भिः who were singing, स्व-यशः His own glories, बल-अन्वितः accompanied by Balarama, पशून् the animals/cows, पुरस्कृत्य placing in front, पशव्यम् providing pasture for the cows, आविशत् entered, विहर्तु-कामः desiring to enjoy/play, कुसुम-आकरम् a mine of flowers/spring-like (कुसुम - flowers, आकरम् - a mine/source), वनम् the forest

Anvaya: तत् बलान्वितः माधवः वेणुम् उदीरयन् स्वयशः गृणद्भिः गोपैः वृतः पशून् पुरस्कृत्य विहर्तुकामः पशव्यं कुसुमाकरं वनम् आविशत् ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: तत् Then, बलान्वितः accompanied by Balarama, माधवः Madhava, वेणुम् the flute, उदीरयन् sounding, स्वयशः गृणद्भिः singing His own glories, गोपैः वृतः surrounded by the cowherd boys, पशून् पुरस्कृत्य placing the animals in front, विहर्तुकामः desiring to play, पशव्यं providing pasture, कुसुमाकरं वनम् the forest which was filled with flowers, आविशत् entered.

Simple meaning: Then, accompanied by Balarama and surrounded by the cowherd boys who were chanting His glories, Lord Madhava vibrated His flute. Placing the cows in front of Him, He entered the forest of Vrindavana—which was full of lush pasture and blooming with spring flowers—desiring to engage in playful pastimes.

तन्मञ्जुघोषालिमृगद्विजाकुलं महन्मनःप्रख्यपयःसरस्वता ।

वातेन जुष्टं शतपत्रगन्धिना निरीक्ष्य रन्तुं भगवान् मनो दधे ॥10.15.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that (forest), मञ्जु-घोष-अलि-मृग-द्विज-आकुलम् filled with the sweet vibrations of bees, animals, and birds, महत्-मनः-प्रख्य-पयः-सरस्वता with lakes containing water as clear as the minds of great souls, वातेन by the breeze, जुष्टम् served/touched, शत-पत्र-गन्धिना carrying the fragrance of lotuses, निरीक्ष्य observing, रन्तुम् to enjoy/play, भगवान् the Supreme Lord, मनः दधे fixed His mind

Anvaya: भगवान् मञ्जुघोषालिमृगद्विजाकुलं महन्मनःप्रख्यपयःसरस्वता शतपत्रगन्धिना वातेन जुष्टं तत् निरीक्ष्य रन्तुं मनः दधे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: भगवान् The Supreme Lord, मञ्जुघोषालिमृगद्विजाकुलं filled with the sweet sounds of bees, animals, and birds, महन्मनःप्रख्यपयःसरस्वता with lakes having water clear like the minds of great souls, शतपत्रगन्धिना carrying the fragrance of lotuses, वातेन by the breeze, जुष्टं served, तत् that (forest), निरीक्ष्य observing, रन्तुं to play, मनः दधे fixed His mind.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord saw that the forest was filled with the sweet sounds of humming bees, animals, and birds. Its lakes were filled with water as clear as the minds of great saintly persons, and a breeze carrying the fragrance of hundred-petaled lotuses blew through the trees. Observing this, He made up His mind to enjoy playing there.

स तत्र तत्रारुणपल्लवश्रिया फलप्रसूनोरुभरेण पादयोः ।

स्पृशच्छिखान् वीक्ष्य वनस्पतीन् मुदा स्मयन्निवाहाग्रजमादिपूरुषः ॥10.15.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः He, तत्र तत्र here and there, अरुण-पल्लव-श्रिया with the beauty of reddish soft leaves, फल-प्रसून-उरु-भरेण with the great burden of fruits and flowers, पादयोः at His feet, touching, शिखान् their tips, वीक्ष्य observing, वनस्पतीन् the trees, मुदा with joy, स्मयन् smiling, इव as if, आह addressed, अग्रजम् His elder brother (Balarama), आदि-पूरुषः the original Supreme Person

Anvaya: आदिपूरुषः सः तत्र तत्र अरुणपल्लवश्रिया फलप्रसूनोरुभरेण शिखान् पादयोः स्पृशत् वनस्पतीन् वीक्ष्य मुदा स्मयन् इव अग्रजम् आह ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आदिपूरुषः The original Supreme Person, सः He, तत्र तत्र here and there, अरुणपल्लवश्रिया with the beauty of reddish soft leaves, फलप्रसूनोरुभरेण with the great burden of fruits and flowers, शिखान् पादयोः स्पृशत् touching His feet with their tips, वनस्पतीन् वीक्ष्य observing the trees, मुदा स्मयन् इव as if smiling with joy, अग्रजम् आह addressed His elder brother.

Simple meaning: The original Supreme Person, Krishna, saw that the trees in the forest were bending down to touch His feet with their tips, burdened as they were by the great weight of their flowers, fruits, and beautiful reddish twigs. Observing this, He smiled and addressed His elder brother, Balarama.

Note: The words मुदा स्मयन् इव indicate that Krishna was in a joking mood. He knew that the trees were actually bowing down to worship Him. But in the following verse, Krishna, speaking in a friendly, lighthearted mood, addresses, Balarama and says that the trees are bowing down to him.

श्री भगवान् उवाच

अहो अमी देववरामरार्चितं पादाम्बुजं ते सुमनःफलार्हणम् ।

नमन्त्युपादाय शिखाभिरात्मनस्तमोऽपहत्यै तरुजन्म यत्कृतम् ॥10.15.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री भगवान् उवाच The blessed Lord said, अहो oh, अमी these (trees), देववर O best of gods!, अमर-अर्चितम् worshiped by the gods, पाद-अम्बुजम् lotus feet, ते Your, सुमनः-फल-अर्हणम् with the offerings of flowers and fruits, नमन्ति they are bowing down, उपादाय taking/holding, शिखाभिः with their heads (treetops), आत्मनः of themselves, तमः-अपहत्यै for the destruction of the ignorance/darkness, तरु-जन्म tree-birth, यत् which, कृतम् was done

Anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच – अहो देववर! अमी अमरार्चितं ते पादाम्बुजं सुमनःफलार्हणम् उपादाय आत्मनः यत् तरुजन्म कृतं तमोऽपहत्यै शिखाभिः नमन्ति ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री भगवान् उवाच The blessed Lord said, अहो Oh, देववर O best of gods!,अमी These (trees), अमरार्चितं worshiped by the gods, ते Your, पादाम्बुजं lotus feet, सुमनःफलार्हणम् offerings of flowers and fruits, उपादाय taking, आत्मनः of themselves, यत् which, तरुजन्म tree-birth, कृतं was obtained, तमोऽपहत्यै for the destruction of ignorance, शिखाभिः with their heads/tips, नमन्ति they are bowing down.

Simple meaning: The Supreme Lord said: Oh, best of gods! These trees are bowing their heads to Your lotus feet, which are worshiped even by the gods. Offering You their flowers and fruits, they are prostrating themselves to destroy the darkness of their ignorance, which caused them to be born in the forms of trees.

एतेऽलिनस्तव यशोऽखिललोकतीर्थं गायन्त आदिपुरुषानुपदं भजन्ते ।

प्रायो अमी मुनिगणा भवदीयमुख्या गूढं वनेऽपि न जहत्यनघात्मदैवम् ॥10.15.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: एते these, अलिनः bees, तव Your, यशः glories, अखिल-लोक-तीर्थम् which are the purifying for all the worlds, गायन्तः singing, आदि-पुरुष O Primeval Person!, अनुपदम् at every step/following, भजन्ते are worshiping, प्रायः certainly, अमी these, मुनि-गणाः groups of sages, भवदीय-मुख्याः who consider You as their worshipable Lord, गूढम् disguised/secretly, वने in the forest, अपि even, न जहति do not abandon, अनघ O sinless One!, आत्म-दैवम् their worshipable Deity

Anvaya: आदिपुरुष! एते अलिनः अखिललोकतीर्थं तव यशः गायन्तः अनुपदं भजन्ते। अनघ! प्रायः अमी भवदीयमुख्याः मुनिगणाः वने गूढम् अपि आत्मदैवं न जहति।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: आदिपुरुष O Primeval Person!, एते अलिनः These bees, अखिललोकतीर्थं which are the purifying for all worlds, तव यशः Your, glories, गायन्तः singing, अनुपदम् at every step, भजन्ते are worshiping. अनघ O sinless one!, प्रायः Certainly, अमी these, भवदीयमुख्याः who consider You their Lord, मुनिगणाः groups of sages, वने in the forest, गूढम् अपि even in disguise, आत्मदैवं their worshipable Deity, न जहति do not abandon.

Simple meaning: O Primeval Person! These bees are singing Your glories, which purify all the worlds, and are following You at every step to worship You. O sinless one! It appears that these are actually great sages, Your most devoted followers, who even in disguise in the forest, refuse to abandon their worshipable Deity.

नृत्यन्त्यमी शिखिन ईड्य मुदा हरिण्यः कुर्वन्ति गोप्य इव ते प्रियमीक्षणेन ।

सूक्तैश्च कोकिलगणा गृहमागताय धन्या वनौकस इयान् हि सतां निसर्गः ॥10.15.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: नृत्यन्ति are dancing, अमी these, शिखिनः peacocks, ईड्य O worshipable one, मुदा with joy, हरिण्यः the female deer, कुर्वन्ति perform/do, गोप्यः the gopis, इव like, ते to You, प्रियम् pleasure, ईक्षणेन by their glances, सूक्तैः with sweet notes, च and, कोकिल-गणाः the groups of cuckoos, गृह-आगताय to Him who has arrived at their home, धन्याः blessed, वन-ओकसः the residents of the forest, इयान् such, हि indeed, सताम् of saintly persons, निसर्गः the nature

Anvaya: ईड्य! अमी शिखिनः मुदा नृत्यन्ति। हरिण्यः गोप्यः इव ईक्षणेन, कोकिलगणाः सूक्तैः च गृहमागताय ते प्रियं कुर्वन्ति। वनौकसः धन्याः। सतां निसर्गः हि इयान्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: ईड्य O worshipable one!, अमी शिखिनः These, peacocks, मुदा नृत्यन्ति are dancing with joy. हरिण्यः The deer, गोप्यः इव ईक्षणेन like the gopis, by their glances, कोकिलगणाः च सूक्तैः and the groups of cuckoos, with sweet notes, गृहमागताय ते प्रियं कुर्वन्ति to You who has arrived at their home are pleasing You. वनौकसः धन्याः Blessed are the residents of the forest. सतां निसर्गः हि इयान् Indeed, such is the nature of saintly persons.

Simple meaning: O worshipable brother, these peacocks are dancing with joy to welcome You. The deer are pleasing You with their affectionate glances, just as the gopis do, and the cuckoos are greeting You with sweet notes, pleasing You who have arrived at their home. These forest residents are truly blessed, for such is the nature of saintly souls to welcome others with love.

धन्येयमद्य धरणी तृणवीरुधस्त्वत्पादस्पृशो द्रुमलताः करजाभिमृष्टाः ।

नद्योऽद्रयः खगमृगाः सदयावलोकैर्गोप्योऽन्तरेण भुजयोरपि यत्स्पृहा श्रीः ॥10.15.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: धन्या blessed, इयम् this, अद्य today, धरणी the earth, तृण-वीरुधः the grasses and bushes, त्वत्-पाद-स्पृशः because of touching Your feet, द्रुम-लताः the trees and creepers, करज-अभिमृष्टाः touched by Your fingernails, नद्यः the rivers, अद्रयः the mountains, खग-मृगाः the birds and animals, सदय-अवलोकैः by Your merciful glances, गोप्यः the cowherd girls, अन्तरेण between, भुजयोः Your arms, अपि also, यत् for which, स्पृहा desires, श्रीः the goddess of fortune.

Anvaya: अद्य इयं धरणी धन्या। त्वत्पादस्पृशः तृणवीरुधः (धन्याः)। करजाभिमृष्टाः द्रुमलताः (धन्याः)। सदयावलोकैः नद्यः अद्रयः खगमृगाः (धन्याः)। भुजयोः अन्तरेण गोप्यः च (धन्याः) यत् श्रीः अपि स्पृहा।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: अद्य इयं धरणी धन्या Today this earth is blessed. त्वत्पादस्पृशः तृणवीरुधः Touching Your feet, the grasses and bushes (are blessed). करजाभिमृष्टाः द्रुमलताः Touched by Your fingernails, the trees and creepers (are blessed). सदयावलोकैः नद्यः अद्रयः खगमृगाः By Your merciful glances, the rivers, the mountains, the birds and animals (are blessed). भुजयोः अन्तरेण By the embrace of Your arms, गोप्यः च and the gopis (are blessed), यत् which, श्रीः अपि स्पृहा even the goddess of fortune desires.

Simple meaning: Today this earth has become blessed. The grasses and bushes are blessed by the touch of Your feet, and the trees and creepers because Your fingernails have touched them. The rivers, mountains, birds, and animals are all blessed by Your merciful glances. Most of all, the gopis are blessed to be embraced by Your arms—an association that even the goddess of fortune herself desires.

श्री शुक उवाच

एवं वृन्दावनं श्रीमत् कृष्णः प्रीतमनाः पशून् ।

रेमे सञ्चारयन्नद्रेः सरिद्रोधस्सु सानुगः ॥10.15.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, एवम् in this way, वृन्दावनम् Vrindavana, श्रीमत् beautiful/opulent, कृष्णः Krishna, प्रीत-मनाः with a satisfied mind, पशून् the cows/animals, रेमे enjoyed, सञ्चारयन् herding/leading, अद्रेः of the mountain (Govardhana), सरित्-रोधस्सु on the banks of the rivers, स-अनुगः along with His followers

Anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच – एवं सानुगः प्रीतमनाः कृष्णः श्रीमत् वृन्दावनम् अद्रेः सरिद्रोधस्सु पशून् सञ्चारयन् रेमे।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: श्री शुक उवाच Shri Shuka said, एवं In this way, सानुगः along with His companions, प्रीतमनाः with a satisfied mind, कृष्णः Krishna, श्रीमत् वृन्दावनम् beautiful, Vrindavana, अद्रेः सरिद्रोधस्सु on the banks of the rivers of the mountain, पशून् सञ्चारयन् herding the cows, रेमे enjoyed.

Simple meaning: Shri Shuka said: In this manner, Krishna, His mind filled with satisfaction, enjoyed the beauty of Vrindavana. Together with His companions, He herded the cows along the banks of the rivers and the ridges of Govardhana Hill.

क्वचिद् गायति गायत्सु मदान्धालिष्वनुव्रतैः ।

अन्वमंसत तद् राजन् गोविन्दानुग्रहेक्षितम् ।

पीत्वा विषं परेतस्य पुनरुत्थानमात्मनः ॥10.15.52॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्वमंसत they understood/concluded, तत् that, राजन् O King, गोविन्द-अनुग्रह-ईक्षितम् due to the merciful glance of Govinda, पीत्वा having drunk, विषम् poison, परेतस्य of one who had died, पुनः-उत्थानम् rising again, आत्मनः of themselves

Anvaya: राजन्! विषं पीत्वा परेतस्य आत्मनः तत् पुनरुत्थानं गोविन्दानुग्रहेक्षितम् (इति) ते अन्वमंसत।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: राजन् O King! विषं पीत्वा Having drunk poison, परेतस्य of (themselves who were) dead, आत्मनः of their own selves, तत् that, पुनरुत्थानं rising again, गोविन्दानुग्रहेक्षितम् was due to the merciful glance of Govinda, (इति) thus, ते अन्वमंसत they understood.

Simple meaning: O King! The cowherd boys realized that even though they had died from drinking the poison, they had come back to life solely because of the merciful glance of Lord Govinda.
