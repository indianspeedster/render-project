रामाय रामभद्राय रामचन्द्राय वेधसे।

Kalidasa's Ramayana - Raghuvamsha - Sarga 12

Rama kills Ravana

(104 Shlokas)

निर्विष्टविषयस्नेहः स दशान्तमुपेयिवान् ।

आसीदासन्ननिर्वाणः प्रदीपार्चिरिवोषसि॥12.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: निर्विष्ट-विषय-स्नेहः who had enjoyed the pleasures of the senses, सः he (Dasharatha), दशा-अन्तम् the end of his reign/life, उपेयिवान् having approached, आसीत् was, आसन्न-निर्वाणः whose end is near, प्रदीप-अर्चिः the flame of a lamp, इव like, उषसि at dawn

निर्विष्ट   निर्विष्ट p. p. 1 Enjoyed, attained, experienced. Src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: निर्विष्टविषयस्नेहः दशान्तम् उपेयिवान् सः – उषसि आसन्ननिर्वाणः प्रदीपार्चिः इव – आसीत्।

Simple meaning: He (Dasharatha) who had enjoyed all pleasures (arising from the objects) of senses (.i e. worldly pleasures), who had reached the declining years of life, with his absolution drawing near, was like a flame of a lamp at dawn which was about to be extinguished, with its oil consumed, and which has reached to the very end of the wick.

तं कर्णमूलमागत्य रामे श्रीर्न्यस्यतामिति ।

कैकेयीशङ्कयेवाह पलितच्छद्मना जरा॥12.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, कर्ण-मूलम् to the root of the ear, आगत्य having come, रामे in Rama, श्रीः prosperity/royal fortune, न्यस्यताम् let it be placed, इति thus, कैकेयी-शङ्कया as if from the fear of Kaikeyi, इव like, आह said, पलित-छद्मना under the guise of grey hair, जरा old age

Anvaya: जरा कैकेयीशङ्कया इव पलितच्छद्मना कर्णमूलम् आगत्य "रामे श्रीः न्यस्यताम्" इति तम् आह।

Simple meaning: Old age, as if from the fear of Kaikeyi, under the guise of grey hair, having come to the root of his ear, said to him,  "let the royal fortune be placed in Rama".

सा पौरान्पौरकान्तस्य रामस्याभ्युदयश्रुतिः ।

प्रत्येकं ह्लादयांचक्रे कुल्येवोद्यानपादपान्॥12.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा that, पौरान् the citizens, पौर-कान्तस्य beloved of the citizens, रामस्य of Rama, अभ्युदय-श्रुतिः the news of the coronation/prosperity, प्रत्येकम् each one, ह्लादयांचक्रे delighted, कुल्या an irrigation channel, इव like, उद्यान-पादपान् the trees of a garden

Anvaya: सा पौरकान्तस्य रामस्य अभ्युदयश्रुतिः – कुल्या उद्यानपादपान् इव – पौरान् प्रत्येकम् ह्लादयांचक्रे।

Simple meaning: That news of the coronation of Rama who was the beloved of the citizens, delighted each and every of them - like an irrigation channel waters each and every tree of a garden.

तस्याभिषेकसंभारं कल्पितं क्रूरनिश्चया ।

दूषयामास कैकेयी शोकोष्णैः पार्थिवाश्रुभिः॥12.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य his (Rama's), अभिषेक-संभारम् the preparations for consecration, कल्पितम् arranged, क्रूर-निश्चया of cruel resolve, दूषयामास spoiled/polluted/defiled, कैकेयी Kaikeyi, शोक-उष्णैः hot with grief, पार्थिव-अश्रुभिः with the king's tears

Anvaya: क्रूरनिश्चया कैकेयी कल्पितं तस्य अभिषेकसंभारं शोकोष्णैः पार्थिवाश्रुभिः दूषयामास।

Simple meaning: Kaikeyi, of cruel resolve, defiled the arranged preparations for his consecration with the king's tears hot with grief.

Note: Tears of sorrow are hot. Tears of joy are cool.

सा किलाश्वासिता चण्डी भर्त्रा तत्संश्रुतौ वरौ ।

उद्ववामेन्द्रसिक्ता भूर्बिलमग्नाविवोरगौ॥12.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she, किल indeed, आश्वासिता consoled/assured, चण्डी the fierce one, भर्त्रा by her husband, तत्-संश्रुतौ related to that/promised earlier, वरौ two boons, उद्ववाम vomited forth, इन्द्र-सिक्ता sprinkled by Indra (Indra is the god of rains), भूः earth, बिल-मग्नौ hidden in a hole, इव like, उरगौ two serpents

Anvaya: चण्डी सा किल, भर्त्रा आश्वासिता तत्संश्रुतौ वरौ – इन्द्रसिक्ता भूः बिलमग्नौ उरगौ इव – उद्ववाम।

Simple meaning: That fierce one indeed, consoled by her husband, asked for (lit. vomited forth) the two boons which had been promised to her- like the earth which soaked with rains by Indra throws forth a pair of serpents hidden in the anthills.

तयोश्चतुर्दशैकेन रामं प्राव्राजयत्समाः ।

द्वितीयेन सुतस्यैच्छद्वैधव्यैकफलां श्रियम्॥12.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: तयोः of those two (boons), चतुर्दश fourteen, एकेन with one (boon), रामम् Rama, प्राव्राजयत् sent to exile, समाः years, द्वितीयेन with the second (boon), सुतस्य for her son, ऐच्छत् desired, वैधव्य-एक-फलाम् having widowhood as its only fruit, श्रियम् royal fortune

Anvaya: तयोः एकेन रामं चतुर्दश समाः प्राव्राजयत्। द्वितीयेन सुतस्य वैधव्यैकफलां श्रियम् ऐच्छत्।

Simple meaning: With the first one of those two, she sent Rama to exile for fourteen years. With the second, she desired for her son the royal fortune – having widowhood as its only fruit.

पित्रा दत्तां रुदन् रामः प्राङ्महीं प्रत्यपद्यत ।

पश्चाद्वनाय गच्छेति तदाज्ञां मुदितोऽग्रहीत्॥12.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: पित्रा by the father, दत्ताम् given, रुदन् weeping (not very eagerly), रामः Rama, प्राक् first/before, महीम् the earth (kingdom), प्रत्यपद्यत accepted, पश्चात् afterwards, वनाय for the forest, गच्छ go, इति thus, तत्-आज्ञाम् that command, मुदितः delighted, अग्रहीत् accepted

Anvaya: रामः पित्रा दत्तां महीं प्राक् रुदन् प्रत्यपद्यत। पश्चात् "वनाय गच्छ" इति तदाज्ञां मुदितः अग्रहीत्।

Simple meaning: Earlier Rama accepted the kingdom given by his father, not very eagerly. Later, he accepted that command "go to the forest", with great delight.

दधतो मङ्गलक्षौमे वसानस्य च वल्कले ।

ददृशुर्विस्मितास्तस्य मुखरागं समं जनाः॥12.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: दधतः wearing, मङ्गल-क्षौमे in auspicious silk garments, वसानस्य wearing, च and, वल्कले in bark garments, ददृशुः saw, विस्मिताः amazed, तस्य his, मुख-रागम् facial glow/complexion, समम् same/equal, जनाः people

Anvaya: मङ्गलक्षौमे दधतः च वल्कले वसानस्य तस्य समं मुखरागं जनाः विस्मिताः ददृशुः।

Simple meaning: The people saw with amazement that the glow on his face remained unchanged whether wearing auspicious silk garments or bark garments.

स सीतालक्ष्मणसखः सत्याद् गुरुमलोपयन् ।

विवेश दण्डकारण्यं प्रत्येकं च सतां मनः॥12.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he, सीता-लक्ष्मण-सखः with Sita and Lakshmana as companions, सत्यात् from truth, गुरुम् the father, अ-लोपयन् not causing to transgress/not violating, विवेश entered, दण्डक-अरण्यम् the Dandaka forest, प्रत्येकम् each one, च and, सताम् of the good people, मनः mind/heart

Anvaya: सः सत्यात् गुरुम् अलोपयन् सीतालक्ष्मणसखः दण्डकारण्यं च सतां प्रत्येकं मनः विवेश।

Simple meaning: He, not causing the father to transgress from truth, with Sita and Lakshmana as companions, entered the Dandaka forest and the minds of every good person - endeared himself to everyone.

राजापि तद्वियोगार्तः स्मृत्वा शापं स्वकर्मजम् ।

शरीरत्यागमात्रेण शुद्धिलाभमन्यत॥12.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: राजा the king, अपि also, तत्-वियोग-आर्तः pained by separation from him, स्मृत्वा having remembered, शापम् curse, स्व-कर्म-जम् born from his own deed, शरीर-त्याग-मात्रेण by merely giving up the body, शुद्धि-लाभम् attainment of purification, अन्यत thought/considered

Anvaya: तद्वियोगार्तः राजा अपि स्वकर्मजं शापं स्मृत्वा शरीरत्यागमात्रेण शुद्धिलाभम् अमन्यत।

Simple meaning: The king also, distressed by separation from him, having remembered the curse born from his own deed, thought that the attainment of purification for his sins would come by only giving up his life.

विप्रोषितकुमारं तद्राज्यमस्तमितेश्वरम् ।

रन्ध्रान्वेषणदक्षाणां द्विषामामिषततां ययौ॥12.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: विप्रोषित-कुमारम् whose prince was sent away, तत्-राज्यम् that kingdom, अस्तमित-ईश्वरम् whose lord had set/died, रन्ध्र-अन्वेषण-दक्षाणाम् skilled in searching for weak points, द्विषाम् of enemies, आमिषताम् the state of being bait/prey, ययौ went/attained

Anvaya: विप्रोषितकुमारम् अस्तमितेश्वरं तद्राज्यं रन्ध्रान्वेषणदक्षाणां द्विषाम् आमिषतां ययौ।

Simple meaning: That kingdom whose prince was sent away and whose lord had died became the prey of enemies skilled in searching for weak points.

अथानाथाः प्रकृतयो मातृबन्धुनिवासिनम् ।

मौलैरानाययामासुर्भरतं स्तम्भिताश्रुभिः॥12.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, अनाथाः without a protector, प्रकृतयः the ministers/subjects/citizens, मातृ-बन्धु-निवासिनम् residing at his maternal uncle's place, मौलैः with messengers, आनाययामासुः caused to be brought, भरतम् Bharata, स्तम्भित-अश्रुभिः with restrained tears

Anvaya: अथ अनाथाः प्रकृतयः मातृबन्धुनिवासिनं भरतं स्तम्भिताश्रुभिः मौलैः आनाययामासुः।

Simple meaning: Then the ministers without a protector, keeping their tears in check, caused Bharata who was residing at his maternal uncle's place to be brought through messengers.

श्रुत्वा तथाविधं मृत्युं कैकेयीतनयः पितुः ।

मातुर्न केवलं स्वस्याः श्रियोऽप्यासीत्पराङ्मुखः॥12.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रुत्वा having heard, तथा-विधम् such, मृत्युम् death, कैकेयी-तनयः the son of Kaikeyi (Bharata), पितुः of his father, मातुः of his mother, न not, केवलम् only, स्वस्याः his own, श्रियः of royal fortune, अपि also, आसीत् was, पराङ्मुखः averse/turned away

Anvaya: पितुः तथाविधं मृत्युं श्रुत्वा कैकेयीतनयः केवलं स्वस्याः मातुः न, श्रियः अपि पराङ्मुखः आसीत्।

Simple meaning: Having heard of such a death of his father, the son of Kaikeyi turned away from not only his own mother but also from the royal fortune.

ससैन्यश्चान्वगाद्रामं दर्शितानाश्रमालयैः ।

तस्य पश्यन्ससौमित्रेरुदश्रुर्वसतिद्रुमान्॥12.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: स-सैन्यः with army, च and, अन्वगात् followed, रामम् Rama, दर्शितान् shown/pointed out, आश्रम-आलयैः by hermitage dwellers, तस्य his (Rama's), पश्यन् seeing, स-सौमित्रेः with Lakshmana, उदश्रुः shedding tears, वसति-द्रुमान् the trees of dwelling

Anvaya: ससैन्यः (भरतः) च आश्रमालयैः दर्शितान्, ससौमित्रेः तस्य वसतिद्रुमान् पश्यन् उदश्रुः (सन्) रामम् अन्वगात्।

Simple meaning: Bharata, accompanied by his army, followed Rama. Seeing the trees under which Rama along with Lakshmana had resided, pointed out by the hermitage dwellers, he became teary-eyed.

चित्रकूटवनस्थं च कथितस्वर्गतिर्गुरोः ।

लक्ष्म्या निमन्त्रयांचक्रे तमनुच्छिष्टसंपदा॥12.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: चित्रकूट-वन-स्थम् one who was residing in Chitrakuta forest (Rama), च and, कथित-स्वर्गतिः who was informed of the heavenly departure, गुरोः of the father, लक्ष्म्या with royal fortune, निमन्त्रयांचक्रे invited, तम् him, अनुच्छिष्ट-संपदा prosperity which had not been enjoyed earlier (by Bharata) (lit. uneaten)

Anvaya: चित्रकूटवनस्थं तं च गुरोः कथितस्वर्गतिः (सन्) अनुच्छिष्टसंपदा लक्ष्म्या निमन्त्रयांचक्रे।

Simple meaning: And having informed Rama who was residing in Chitrakuta forest of their father's heavenly departure, Bharata persuaded him to partake the prosperity which had not been enjoyed by him.

स हि प्रथमजे तस्मिन्नकृतश्रीपरिग्रहे ।

परिवेत्तारमात्मानं मेने स्वीकरणाद्भुवः॥12.16॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Bharata), हि indeed, प्रथमजे in the first-born, तस्मिन् in him (Rama), अकृत-श्री-परिग्रहे who had not accepted the royal fortune, परिवेत्तारम् usurper/encroacher, आत्मानम् himself, मेने considered, स्वीकरणात् from accepting, भुवः of the earth

Anvya: सः हि प्रथमजे तस्मिन् अकृतश्रीपरिग्रहे भुवः स्वीकरणात् आत्मानम् परिवेत्तारं मेने।

Simple meaning: For, he would consider himself, he thought, to be a परिवेत्ता ( one who commits the sin of marrying before his elder brother's choosing a wife ) by his accepting the earth ( i. e. kingdom ) when his elder brother had not accepted (lit. made the acceptance of) the Royal Fortune.

Note: According to Dharma Shastra, if the younger brother gets married when the elder brother is unmarried, then the younger brother is called परिवेत्ता. Bharata considered his acceptance of the Kingdom, before his elder brother as a परिवेत्ता.

तमशक्यमपाक्रष्टुं निदेशात्स्वर्गिणः पितुः ।

ययाचे पादुके पश्चात्कर्तुं राज्याधिदेवते॥12.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, अशक्यम् unable, अपाक्रष्टुम् to drag away, निदेशात् from the command, स्वर्गिणः of the departed/heavenly, पितुः of the father, ययाचे begged, पादुके sandals, पश्चात् afterwards, कर्तुम् to make, राज्य-अधिदेवते the presiding deities of the kingdom

Anvaya: स्वर्गिणः पितुः निदेशात् अपाक्रष्टुम् अशक्यं तं पश्चात् पादुके राज्याधिदेवते कर्तुं ययाचे।

Simple meaning: Unable to drag him away from the command of the departed father, afterwards he begged for the sandals to make them the presiding deities of the kingdom.

स विसृष्टस्तथेत्युक्त्वा भ्रात्रा नैवाविशत्पुरीम् ।

नन्दिग्रामगतस्तस्य राज्यं न्यासमिवाभुनक्॥12.18॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Bharata), विसृष्टः dismissed/sent back, तथा so be it, इति thus, उक्त्वा having said, भ्रात्रा by his brother, न not, एव indeed, आविशत् entered, पुरीम् the city, नन्दिग्राम-गतः having gone to Nandigrama, तस्य his (Rama's), राज्यम् kingdom, न्यासम् deposit/trust, इव like, अभुनक् protected/preserved

अभुनक् is a special verb.

विशेषः

➤ भुज पालनाभ्यवहारयोः – उभयपदी धातुः

➤ पालनम् = रक्षणम् । Protect.

➤ अभ्यवहारः = खादनम्, भोगः । Consume / enjoy.

➤ पालनार्थे परस्मैपदप्रयोगः - when used in the sense of protection, parasmaipada form is used.

➤ राज्यं भुनक्ति = protects the kingdom

उपभोगार्थे आत्मनेपदप्रयोगः - when used in the sense of enjoyment, atmanepada form is used.

➤ राज्यं भुङ्क्ते = enjoys the kingdom

➤ राज्यं न्यासमिवाभुनक् – Bharata's focus was fully on protecting the kingdom, he did not enjoy any pleasures of kingship

Anvaya: सः भ्रात्रा "तथा" इति उक्त्वा विसृष्टः (सन्), पुरीं न एव आविशत्। नन्दिग्रामगतः (सन्) तस्य राज्यं न्यासम् इव अभुनक्।

Simple meaning: Dismissed by his brother, having said "so be it", he did not enter the city. Having gone to Nandigrama, he protected Rama’s kingdom like a deposit held in trust.

दृढभक्तिरिति ज्येष्ठे राज्यतृष्णापराङ्मुखः ।

मातुः पापस्य भरतः प्रायश्चित्तमिवाकरोत्॥12.19॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृढ-भक्तिः one with firm devotion, इति thus, ज्येष्ठे to the elder brother, राज्य-तृष्णा-पराङ्मुखः one who was averse to the thirst for kingdom, मातुः of his mother, पापस्य of the sin, भरतः Bharata, प्रायश्चित्तम् atonement, इव like, अकरोत् performed

Anvaya: ज्येष्ठे दृढभक्तिः राज्यतृष्णापराङ्मुखः भरतः इति मातुः पापस्य प्रायश्चित्तम् इव अकरोत्।

Simple meaning: Bharata, who was firmly devoted to his elder brother, was averse to the thirst for kingdom. It was as if he thus (by living in Nandigrama) performed an atonement for his mother's sin.

रामोऽपि सह वैदेह्या वने वन्येन वर्तयन् ।

चचार सानुजः शान्तो वृद्धेक्ष्वाकुव्रतं युवा॥12.20॥

Word-by-word meaning: रामः Rama, अपि also, सह with, वैदेह्या Vaidehi (Sita), वने in the forest, वन्येन with forest produce, वर्तयन् maintaining/living, चचार practiced, स-अनुजः with younger brother, शान्तः calm, वृद्ध-इक्ष्वाकु-व्रतम् the practice of old Ikshvaku kings, युवा young man

Anvaya: सानुजः शान्तः रामः वैदेह्या सह वने वन्येन वर्तयन् युवा अपि वृद्धेक्ष्वाकुव्रतं चचार।

Simple meaning: Along with his younger brother and Vaidehi, Rama calmly living in the forest on forest produce, practiced the vow of the aged Ikshvaku kings even though he was young of age.

प्रभावस्तम्भितच्छायमाश्रितः स वनस्पतिम् ।

कदाचिदङ्के सीतायाः शिश्ये किंचिदिव श्रमात्॥12.21॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रभाव-स्तम्भित-छायम् whose shade was still by his power, आश्रितः having resorted to, सः he (Rama), वनस्पतिम् tree, कदाचित् once, अङ्के in the lap, सीतायाः of Sita, शिश्ये reclined/rested, किंचित् somewhat, इव as if, श्रमात् from fatigue

Anvaya: कदाचित् सः प्रभावस्तम्भितच्छायं वनस्पतिम् आश्रितः किंचित् श्रमात् इव सीतायाः अङ्के शिश्ये।

Simple meaning: Once, he, having resorted to a tree whose shade was still by his power, as if somewhat from fatigue, reclined on the lap of Sita.

ऐन्द्रः किल नखैस्तस्या विददार स्तनौ द्विजः ।

प्रियोपभोगचिह्नेषु पौरोभाग्यमिवाचरन्॥12.22॥

Word-by-word meaning: ऐन्द्रः son of Indra, किल indeed, नखैः with claws, तस्याः her (Sita's), विददार scratched/tore, स्तनौ breasts, द्विजः bird, प्रिय-उपभोग-चिह्नेषु on the marks of the beloved's enjoyment, पौर-भाग्यम् fault-finding, इव like, आचरन् behaving/acting

Anvaya: ऐन्द्रः द्विजः किल तस्याः स्तनौ प्रियोपभोगचिह्नेषु – पौरभाग्यम् इव आचरन् – नखैः विददार।

Simple meaning: Then Indra's son in the form of a crow swooped and, it is said, scratched the breasts of Sita with his claws, where the marks made by Rama in the course of their intimacy, as if to find fault with Rama.

तस्मिन्नास्थदिषीकास्त्रं रामो रामावबोधितः ।

आत्मानं मुमुचे तस्मादेकनेत्रव्ययेन सः॥12.23॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मिन् at that (bird), आस्थत् sent forth, इषीका-अस्त्रम् a weapon made of reed, रामः Rama, रामा-अवबोधितः informed by Raama (Sita), आत्मानम् himself, मुमुचे released, तस्मात् from that, एक-नेत्र-व्ययेन with the sacrifice/loss of one eye, सः he

Anvaya: रामावबोधितः रामः तस्मिन् इषीकास्त्रम् आस्थत्। सः एकनेत्रव्ययेन तस्मात् आत्मानम् मुमुचे।

Simple meaning: Rama awakened by Sita released a reed weapon at that bird. The crow redeemed himself from that offence with the sacrifice of one eye.

रामस्त्वासन्नदेशत्वाद्भरतागमनं पुनः ।

आशङ्क्योत्सुकसारङ्गां चित्रकूटस्थलीं जहौ॥12.24॥

Word-by-word meaning: रामः Rama, तु but, आसन्न-देश-त्वात् because of nearness of region, भरत-आगमनम् Bharata's coming, पुनः again, आशङ्क्य suspecting/fearing, उत्सुक-सारङ्गाम् where deer were anxious, चित्रकूट-स्थलीम् the place of Chitrakuta, जहौ abandoned

Anvaya: रामः तु आसन्नदेशत्वात् पुनः भरतागमनम् आशङ्क्य उत्सुकसारङ्गां चित्रकूटस्थलीं जहौ।

Simple meaning: But Rama, fearing Bharata's coming again because of the nearness of the region, abandoned the place of Chitrakuta where the deer were always anxious to see Rama.

प्रययावातिथेयेषु वसन्नृषिकुलेषु सः ।

दक्षिणां दिशमृक्षेषु वार्षिकेष्विव भास्करः॥12.25॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रययौ proceeded, आतिथेयेषु in hospitable, वसन् dwelling, ऋषि-कुलेषु in the hermitages of sages, सः he (Rama), दक्षिणाम् southern, दिशम् direction, ऋक्षेषु among the constellations, वार्षिकेषु rainy season ones, इव like, भास्करः the sun

Anvaya: सः आतिथेयेषु ऋषिकुलेषु – भास्करः वार्षिकेषु ऋक्षेषु इव – वसन् दक्षिणां दिशं प्रययौ।

Simple meaning: Sojourning with the hospitable families of sages on his route Rama travelled southward like the sun passing down the ten asterisms (a prominent pattern or group of stars, typically having a popular name but smaller than a constellation) coming in the rainy season (beginning with Ardra star and ending with Swati star called the rainy asterisms)

Note: The word Riksha may also be taken in the sense of rashis, the 12 constellations; and in that case the constellations would be from karka (Cancer) to makara (Capricorn). Rama, who gradually journeyed from Ayodhya southwards, is likened to the sun travelling through constellations from the northern solstice to the southern solstice (Uttarayana to Dashinayana).

बभौ तमनुगच्छन्ती विदेहाधिपतेः सुता ।

प्रतिषिद्धापि कैकेय्या लक्ष्मीरिव गुणोन्मुखी॥12.26॥

Word-by-word meaning: बभौ shone, तम् him, अनुगच्छन्ती following, विदेह-अधिपतेः of the lord of Videha, सुता daughter, प्रतिषिद्धा forbidden, अपि even though, कैकेय्या by Kaikeyi, लक्ष्मीः royal fortune, इव like, गुण-उन्मुखी inclined toward virtues

Anvaya: तम् अनुगच्छन्ती विदेहाधिपतेः सुता – कैकेय्या प्रतिषिद्धा अपि गुणोन्मुखी लक्ष्मीः इव – बभौ।

Simple meaning: Following Rama, the daughter of the lord of Videha, shone like Goddess Lakshmi , who as it were, even though forbidden by Kaikeyi, followed Rama, attracted to his virtues.

अनसूयातिसृष्टेन पुण्यगन्धेन काननम् ।

सा चकाराङ्गरागेण पुष्पोच्चलितषट्पदम्॥12.27॥

Word-by-word meaning: अनसूया-अतिसृष्टेन given by Anasuya, पुण्य-गन्धेन with sacred fragrance, काननम् forest, सा she (Sita), चकार made, अङ्ग-रागेण with body perfume/ointment, पुष्प-उच्चलित-षट्पदम् bees (being attracted by the body perfume) who had left the flowers (and followed Sita)

Anvaya: सा अनसूयातिसृष्टेन पुण्यगन्धेन अङ्गरागेण काननं पुष्पोच्चलितषट्पदं चकार।

Simple meaning: She, adorned with the sacred fragrance of body perfume given by Anasuya, made the flowers of the forest as it were, bereft of the bees on account of them following her because of being attracted by her perfume.

संध्याभ्रकपिशस्तस्य विराधो नाम राक्षसः ।

अतिष्ठन्मार्गमावृत्य रामस्येन्दोरिव ग्रहः॥12.28॥

Word-by-word meaning: संध्या-अभ्र-कपिशः brown/reddish like twilight clouds, तस्य his (Rama's), विराधः Viradha, नाम named, राक्षसः rakshasa, अतिष्ठत् stood, मार्गम् path, आवृत्य obstructing, रामस्य of Rama, इन्दोः of the moon, इव like, ग्रहः planet/eclipse

Anvaya: संध्याभ्रकपिशः विराधः नाम राक्षसः – ग्रहः इन्दोः इव – तस्य रामस्य मार्गम् आवृत्य अतिष्ठत्।

Simple meaning: A rakshasa named Viradha, brown like twilight clouds, stood obstructing the path of that Rama - like a planet eclipsing the moon.

स जहार तयोर्मध्ये मैथिलीं लोकशोषणः ।

नभोनभस्ययोर्वृष्टिमवग्रह इवान्तरे॥12.29॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Viradha), जहार carried away, तयोः of those two, मध्ये in the middle, मैथिलीम् Maithili (Sita), लोक-शोषणः tormentor of the world, नभः-नभस्ययोः between the sky and the clouds, वृष्टिम् rain, अवग्रहः obstacle/drought, इव like, अन्तरे in between

Anvaya: लोकशोषणः सः तयोः मध्ये मैथिलीम् – अवग्रहः नभोनभस्ययोः अन्तरे वृष्टिम् इव – जहार।

Simple meaning: That rakshasa, the tormentor of the world, carried away Maithili between  Rama and Lakshmana just as drought takes away rains occurring between the months of Nabha (Shravana) and Nabhasya (Bhadrapada).

Note: Nabha and Nabhasya are names of the months of Shravan and Bhadprada. The months of Shravana and Bhadrapada are the months during which rainfall is generally the strongest; and the absence of it just then makes people most anxious; hence the comparison is very appropriate.

नभोनभस्ययोः (nabho-nabhasyayoh)

तं विनिष्पिष्य काकुत्स्थौ पुरा दूषयति स्थलीम् ।

गन्धेनाशुचिना चेति वसुधायां निचख्नतुः॥12.30॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, विनिष्पिष्य having crushed/killed, काकुत्स्थौ the two descendants of Kakutstha (Rama and Lakshmana), पुरा before, दूषयति pollutes, स्थलीम् the place, गन्धेन with smell, अशुचिना impure, च and, इति thus (thinking), वसुधायाम् in the earth, निचख्नतुः buried

Anvaya: काकुत्स्थौ तं विनिष्पिष्य "पुरा अशुचिना गन्धेन स्थलीं दूषयति च" इति वसुधायां निचख्नतुः।

Simple meaning: The two descendants of Kakutstha, having crushed him, thinking "before he pollutes the place with his impure smell", buried him in the earth .

पञ्चवट्यां ततो रामः शासनात्कुम्भजन्मनः ।

अनपोढस्थितिस्तस्थौ विन्ध्याद्रिः प्रकृताविव॥12.31॥

Word-by-word meaning: पञ्चवट्याम् in Panchavati, ततः then, रामः Rama, शासनात् from the command, कुम्भ-जन्मनः of the pot-born one (Agastya), अनपोढ-स्थितिः without deviating from his inborn conduct, तस्थौ stayed, विन्ध्य-अद्रिः the Vindhya mountain, प्रकृतौ in its natural state, इव like

Anvaya: ततः रामः कुम्भजन्मनः शासनात् पञ्चवट्यां – विन्ध्याद्रिः प्रकृतौ इव – अनपोढस्थितिः तस्थौ।

Simple meaning: Then Rama, without deviating from his inborn conduct, sojourned in panchavati hermitage by order of the pitcher-born-sage Agastya, just as the mountain Vindhya sedentarily remained in its normal state under the command of the same sage.

Note: Agastya Rishi commanded Vindhya mountains to stop growing because it was obstructing Sun’s light to the south. The analogy is Agastya asked Rama to stay in Panchavati as he asked Vindhyas to stay.

रावणावरजा तत्र राघवं मदनातुरा ।

अभिपेदे निदाघार्ता व्यालीव मलयद्रुमम्॥12.32॥

Word-by-word meaning: रावण-अवरजा the younger sister of Ravana (Shurpanakha), तत्र there, राघवम् Rama, मदन-आतुरा tormented by desire, अभिपेदे approached, निदाघ-आर्ता afflicted by summer heat, व्याली a female serpent, इव like, मलय-द्रुमम् sandalwood tree

Anvaya: तत्र मदनातुरा रावणावरजा – निदाघार्ता व्याली मलयद्रुमम् इव – राघवम् अभिपेदे।

Simple meaning: There, the younger sister of Ravana, tormented by desire, approached Rama - like a female serpent afflicted by summer heat approaches a sandalwood tree.

सा सीतासंनिधावेव तं वव्रे कथितान्वया ।

अत्यारूढो हि नारीणामकालज्ञो मनोभवः॥12.33॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she, सीता-संनिधौ in the presence of Sita, एव indeed, तम् him (Rama), वव्रे chose/proposed to, कथित-अन्वया having stated her lineage, अति-आरूढः excessively mounted/ascended, हि indeed, नारीणाम् of women, अकाल-ज्ञः not knowing the proper time, मनः-भवः born of mind (Kamadeva/desire)

Anvaya: सा सीतासंनिधौ एव कथितान्वया तं वव्रे। नारीणाम् अत्यारूढः मनोभवः अकालज्ञः हि।

Simple meaning: She, having stated her lineage, proposed to him in the very presence of Sita. Indeed, unbound passion in women knows not the proper time.

कलत्रवानहं बाले कनीयांसं भजस्व मे।

इति रामो वृषस्यन्तीं वृषस्कन्धः शशास ताम्॥12.34॥

Word-by-word meaning: कलत्रवान् having a wife, अहम् I, बाले O maiden, कनीयांसम् younger brother, भजस्व serve/approach, मे my, इति thus, रामः Rama, वृषस्यन्तीम् lustful woman (cow in heat), वृष-स्कन्धः having bull-like shoulders, शशास instructed, ताम् her

Anvaya: "बाले! अहं कलत्रवान् । मे कनीयांसं भजस्व।" इति वृषस्कन्धः रामः वृषस्यन्तीं तां शशास।

Simple meaning: "O maiden! I have a wife. Approach my younger brother." Thus Rama with bull-like shoulders instructed the woman who was like a cow in heat.

ज्येष्ठाभिगमनात्पूर्वं तेनाप्यनभिनन्दिताम् ।

साऽभूद्रामाश्रया भूयो नदीवोभयकूलभाक्॥12.35॥

Word-by-word meaning: ज्येष्ठ-अभिगमनात् because of approaching the elder, पूर्वम् before/earlier, तेन by him (Lakshmana), अपि also, अन्-अभि-नन्दिताम् not welcomed/rejected, सा she, अभूत् became, राम-आश्रया taking refuge with Rama, भूयः again, नदी a river, इव like, उभय-कूल-भाक् partaking of both banks

Anvaya: पूर्वं ज्येष्ठाभिगमनात् तेन अपि अनभिनन्दितां भूयः रामाश्रया सा – उभयकूलभाक् नदी इव – अभूत्।

Simple meaning: Rejected by Lakshmana also because she had approached the elder brother first, she again took refuge with Rama. Her situation became like a river, touching both the banks in turns.

संरम्भं मैथिलीहासः क्षणसौम्यां निनाय ताम् ।

निवातस्तिमितां वेलां चन्द्रोदय इवोदधेः॥12.36॥

Word-by-word meaning: संरम्भम् wrath/fury, मैथिली-हासः the laughter of Maithili, क्षण-सौम्याम् gentle for a moment, निनाय led/brought, ताम् her, निवात-स्तिमितम् stilled by absence of wind, वेलाम् wave, चन्द्र-उदयः moonrise, इव like, उदधेः of the ocean

Anvaya: मैथिलीहासः क्षणसौम्यां तां – चन्द्रोदयः उदधेः निवातस्तिमितां वेलाम् इव – संरम्भं निनाय ।

Simple meaning: Shurpanakha who had assumed a gentle countenance for a moment, the laughter of Maithili made her angry - just as moonrise brings the wave on the ocean stilled by absence of wind.

फलमस्योपहासस्य सद्यः प्राप्स्यसि पश्य माम् ।

मृग्याः परिभवो व्याघ्र्यामित्यवेहि त्वया कृतम्॥12.37॥

Word-by-word meaning: फलम् fruit/result, अस्य of this, उपहासस्य of mockery, सद्यः immediately, प्राप्स्यसि you will obtain, पश्य look at, माम् me, मृग्याः of a deer, परिभवः insult/humiliation, व्याघ्र्याम् to a tigress, इति thus, अवेहि know, त्वया by you, कृतम् done

Anvya: "अस्य उपहासस्य फलं सद्यः प्राप्स्यसि। मां पश्य। त्वया व्याघ्र्यां मृग्याः कृतं परिभवः इति अवेहि।"

Simple meaning: "You will immediately obtain the fruit of this mockery. Look at me. Know that you have inflicted insulted me as a deer would, upon a tigress."

इत्युक्त्वा मैथिलीं भर्तुरङ्के निविशतीं भयात् ।

रूपं शूर्पणखा नाम्नः सदृशं प्रत्यपद्यत॥12.38॥

Word-by-word meaning: इति thus, उक्त्वा having said, मैथिलीम् Maithili, भर्तुः of her husband, अङ्के in the lap, निविशतीम् entering/taking refuge, भयात् from fear, रूपम् form, शूर्पणखा Shurpanakha (one whose nails were like winnowing baskets), नाम्नः of the name, सदृशम् suitable/befitting, प्रत्यपद्यत assumed

Anvaya: इति उक्त्वा भयात् भर्तुः अङ्के निविशतीं मैथिलीं शूर्पणखा नाम्नः सदृशं रूपं प्रत्यपद्यत।

Simple meaning: Having said thus to Maithili who was taking refuge in her husband's lap from fear, Shurpanakha assumed a form befitting her name.

लक्ष्मणः प्रथमं श्रुत्वा कोकिलामञ्जुवादिनीम् ।

शिवाघोरस्वनां पश्चाद्बुबुधे विकृतेति ताम्॥12.39॥

Word-by-word meaning: लक्ष्मणः Lakshmana, प्रथमम् first, श्रुत्वा having heard, कोकिला-मञ्जु-वादिनीम् speaking pleasantly like a cuckoo, शिवा-घोर-स्वनाम् having an terrible sound like a female jackal, पश्चात् afterwards, बुबुधे understood, विकृता one who can change her form, इति thus, ताम् her

Anvaya: लक्ष्मणः प्रथमं कोकिलामञ्जुवादिनीं पश्चात् शिवाघोरस्वनां तां श्रुत्वा विकृता इति बुबुधे।

Simple meaning: Lakshmana, having first heard her speaking pleasantly like a cuckoo, afterwards hearing her loud howl like a female jackal, understood that she had the ability to change her form.

पर्णशालामथ क्षिप्रं विकृष्टासिः प्रविश्य सः ।

वैरूप्यपौनरुक्त्येन भीषणां तामयोजयत्॥12.40॥

Word-by-word meaning: पर्ण-शालाम् the thatched-hut, अथ then, क्षिप्रम् quickly, विकृष्ट-असिः with drawn sword, प्रविश्य having entered, सः he (Lakshmana), वैरूप्य-पौनरुक्त्येन by repetition of deformity, भीषणाम् frightful, ताम् her, अयोजयत् made/rendered

Anvaya: अथ सः विकृष्टासिः क्षिप्रं पर्णशालां प्रविश्य भीषणां तां वैरूप्यपौनरुक्त्येन अयोजयत।

Simple meaning: Then he, with a drawn sword, quickly entering the thatched-hut, rendered her frightful by (chopping off her nose and ears) repetition of deformity.

She was already frightful and chopping off her nose and ears, rendered her even more frightful.

सा वक्रनखधारिण्या वेणुकर्कशपर्वया ।

अङ्कुशाकारयाङ्गुल्या तावतर्जयदम्बरे॥12.41॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा she, वक्र-नख-धारिण्या bearing crooked nails, वेणु-कर्कश-पर्वया with joints rough like bamboo, अङ्कुश-आकारया having the shape of a goad, अङ्गुल्या with finger, तौ those two, अतर्जयत् threatened/menaced, अम्बरे in the sky

Anvaya: सा वक्रनखधारिण्या वेणुकर्कशपर्वया अङ्कुशाकारया अङ्गुल्या अम्बरे तौ अतर्जयत्।

Simple meaning: She, with her finger bearing crooked nails, with joints rough like bamboo, shaped like a goad, threatened those two in the sky.

प्राप्य चाशु जनस्थानं खरादिभ्यस्तथाविधम् ।

रामोपक्रममाचख्यौ रक्षःपरिभवं नवम्॥12.42॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्राप्य having reached, च and, आशु quickly, जनस्थानम् Janasthana, खर-आदिभ्यः to Khara and others, तथा-विधम् such, राम-उपक्रमम् Rama's action/conduct, आचख्यौ narrated, रक्षः-परिभवम् insult to the rakshasas, नवम् new/recent

Anvaya: आशु जनस्थानं प्राप्य च खरादिभ्यः तथाविधं नवं रक्षःपरिभवं रामोपक्रमम् आचख्यौ।

Simple meaning: And having quickly reached Janasthana, she narrated to Khara and others such new insult to the rakshasas by Rama's action.

मुखावयवलूनां तां नैरृता यत्पुरो दधुः ।

रामाभियायिनां तेषां तदेवाभूदमङ्गलम्॥12.43॥

Word-by-word meaning: मुख-अवयव-लूनाम् whose facial parts were cut, ताम् her, नैरृताः the rakshasas, यत् which, पुरः in front, दधुः placed, राम-अभियायिनाम् going against Rama, तेषाम् of those, तत् that, एव indeed, अभूत् became, अमङ्गलम् inauspicious/ill omen

Anvaya: मुखावयवलूनां तां नैरृताः पुरः यत् दधुः, तत् एव तेषां रामाभियायिनाम् अमङ्गलम् अभूत्।

Simple meaning: That they placed her whose facial parts were cut in front became indeed the inauspicious omen for those rakshasas going against Rama.

उदायुधानापततस्तान्दृप्तान्प्रेक्ष्य राघवः ।

निदधे विजयाशंसां चापे सीतां च लक्ष्मणे॥12.44॥

Word-by-word meaning: उद्-आयुधान् with raised weapons, आपततः rushing/attacking, तान् those, दृप्तान् arrogant, प्रेक्ष्य having seen, राघवः Rama, निदधे placed, विजय-आशंसाम् hope of victory, चापे on the bow, सीताम् Sita, च and, लक्ष्मणे with Lakshmana

Anvaya: उदायुधान् आपततः दृप्तान् तान् प्रेक्ष्य राघवः चापे विजयाशंसां लक्ष्मणे सीतां च निदधे।

Simple meaning: Having seen those arrogant ones rushing with raised weapons, Rama placed his hope of victory on the bow and Sita with Lakshmana.

एको दाशरथिः कामं यातुधानाः सहस्रशः ।

ते तु यावन्त एवाजौ तावांश्च ददृशे स तैः॥12.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: एकः one, दाशरथिः son of Dasharatha (Rama), कामम् indeed/granted, यातुधानाः night-wanderers/rakshasas, सहस्रशः by thousands, ते those, तु but, यावन्तः as many, एव indeed, अजौ in battle, तावान् so many, च and, ददृशे was seen, सः he, तैः by them

Anvaya: कामं, दाशरथिः एकः, यातुधानाः सहस्रशः। तु अजौ ते यावन्तः एव तावान् च सः तैः ददृशे।

Simple meaning: Granted that the son of Dasharatha was one, the rakshasas by thousands. But as many as those were in battle, so many he was seen by them. (Rama was so skilled and it appeared to the rakshasas that Rama was everywhere.)

असज्जनेन काकुत्स्थः प्रयुक्तमथ दूषणम् ।

न चक्षमे शुभाचारः स दूषणमिवात्मनः॥12.46॥

Word-by-word meaning: असज्जनेन by the wicked one (Khara), काकुत्स्थः descendant of Kakutstha (Rama), प्रयुक्तम् hurled/uttered, अथ then, दूषणम् Dushana (name), न not, चक्षमे tolerated, शुभ-आचारः of good conduct, सः he, दूषणम् fault/defect, इव like, आत्मनः of himself

Anvaya: अथ शुभाचारः सः काकुत्स्थः असज्जनेन प्रयुक्तं दूषणम् – आत्मनः दूषणम् इव – न चक्षमे।

Simple meaning: That descendant of Kakutstha of righteous conduct did not tolerate Dushana sent by the wicked one (Khara) - just as he would not tolerate censure against himself.

Note: Beautiful wordplay (shlesha- alankara) on दूषणम् (dushanam) which means both (1) the rakshasa Dushana - Ravana's commander sent by Khara to attack Rama, and (2) censure/slander/defamation. The verse simultaneously conveys: Rama did not tolerate the rakshasa Dushana sent by wicked Khara in battle, just as a person of spotless character would never tolerate slander against himself by wicked people. Kalidasa uses the rakshasa’s actual name to create a beautiful wordplay about not tolerating either physical enemies or attacks on one's reputation.

तं शरैः प्रतिजग्राह खरत्रिशिरसौ च सः ।

क्रमशस्ते पुनस्तस्य चापात्सममिवोद्ययुः॥12.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him (Dushana), शरैः with arrows, प्रतिजग्राह received/countered, खर-त्रिशिरसौ Khara and Trishiras, च and, सः he (Rama), क्रमशः in succession, ते those, पुनः again, तस्य his, चापात् from the bow, समम् equally, इव as if, उद्ययुः arose/emerged

Anvaya: सः तं खरत्रिशिरसौ च शरैः प्रतिजग्राह। क्रमशः (प्रयुक्ताः अपि) तस्य चापात् ते (शराः) पुनः समम् इव उद्ययुः।

Simple meaning: He assailed him (Dushana) as well as the rakshasas Khara and Trishiras with his arrows, which though discharged from his bow in succession, appeared to be discharged as it were, at one and the same moment.

तैस्त्रयाणां शितैर्बाणैर्यथापूर्वविशुद्धिभिः ।

आयुर्देहातिगैः पीतं रुधिरं तु पतत्रिभिः॥12.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: तैः by those, त्रयाणाम् of the three, शितैः sharp, बाणैः by arrows, यथा-पूर्व-विशुद्धिभिः who were in their previous state of being clean (not bloodied), आयुः the life, देह-अतिगैः having passed through body, पीतम् drunk, रुधिरम् blood, तु indeed, पतत्रिभिः by birds

Anvaya: देहातिगैः यथापूर्वविशुद्धिभिः शितैः तैः बाणैः त्रयाणाम् आयुः (पीतम्)। रुधिरं तु पतत्रिभिः पीतम्।

Simple meaning: The sharp-pointed arrows that pierced through the bodies of those three, remaining as clean as before, drank (took) their life, but it was the birds which drank their blood.

Note: The sentiment is that Rama’s arrows were so swift that they passed through the bodies of the rakshasas and came out clean, without getting bloodied. The arrows took the lives of the rakshasas (lit. drank) but it were the birds of prey who feasted on the dead bodies later.

तस्मिन्रामशरोत्कृत्ते बले महति रक्षसाम् ।

उत्थितं ददृशेऽन्यच्च​ कबन्धेभ्यो न किंचन॥12.49॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मिन् in that, राम-शर-उत्कृत्ते cut down by Rama's arrows, बले army, महति great, रक्षसाम् of the rakshasas, उत्थितम् risen/standing, ददृशे was seen, अन्यत् other, च and, कबन्धेभ्यः from the headless trunks, न not, किंचन anything

Anvaya: तस्मिन् रामशरोत्कृत्ते महति रक्षसां बले उत्थितं कबन्धेभ्यः च अन्यत् किंचन न ददृशे।

Simple meaning: In that great army of rakshasas cut down by Rama's arrows, other than the headless trunks, nothing else was seen standing.

सा बाणवर्षिणं रामं योधयित्वा सुरद्विषाम् ।

अप्रबोधाय सुष्वाप गृध्रच्छाये वरूथिनी॥12.50॥

Word-by-word meaning: सा that, बाण-वर्षिणम् raining arrows, रामम् Rama, योधयित्वा having fought, सुर-द्विषाम् of the enemies of gods (rakshasas), अप्रबोधाय for not waking/never to wake, सुष्वाप slept well, गृध्र-छाये in the shade of vultures, वरूथिनी army

Anvaya: सा सुरद्विषां वरूथिनी बाणवर्षिणं रामं योधयित्वा गृध्रछाये अप्रबोधाय सुष्वाप।

Simple meaning: That army of the enemies of gods, having fought Rama who rained arrows, slept in the shade of vultures never to wakeup.

राघवास्त्रविदीर्णानां रावणं प्रति रक्षसाम् ।

तेषां शूर्पणखैवैका दुष्प्रवृत्तिहराऽभवत्॥12.51॥

Word-by-word meaning: राघव-अस्त्र-विदीर्णानाम् torn by Rama's weapons, रावणम् to Ravana, प्रति toward, रक्षसाम् of the rakshasas, तेषाम् of those, शूर्पणखा Shurpanakha, एव alone, एका only one, दुः-प्रवृत्ति-हरा bearer of evil news, अभवत् became

Anvaya: राघवास्त्रविदीर्णानां तेषां रक्षसाम् एका शूर्पणखा एव रावणं प्रति दुष्प्रवृत्तिहरा अभवत्।

Simple meaning: Of those rakshasas torn by Rama's weapons, Shurpanakha alone became the only bearer of evil news toward Ravana.

निग्रहात्स्वसुराप्तानां वधाच्च धनदानुजः ।

रामेण निहितं मेने पदं दशसु मूर्धसु॥12.52॥

Word-by-word meaning: निग्रहात् from the humiliation, स्वसुः of his sister, आप्तानाम् of his own friends/allies/relatives/dear ones, वधात् from the killing, च and, धनद-अनुजः the younger brother of Dhanada-Kubera (Ravana), रामेण by Rama, निहितम् placed, मेने considered, पदम् foot, दशसु on the ten, मूर्धसु heads

Anvaya: स्वसुः निग्रहात् आप्तानां वधात् च धनदानुजः रामेण दशसु मूर्धसु पदं निहितं मेने।

Simple meaning: From the humiliation of his sister and killing of his own allies, the younger brother of Kubera considered that Rama had placed his foot on his ten heads.

रक्षसा मृगरूपेण वञ्चयित्वा स राघवौ ।

जहार सीतां पक्षीन्द्रप्रयासक्षणविघ्नितः॥12.53॥

Word-by-word meaning: रक्षसा by the rakshasa, मृग-रूपेण in the form of a deer, वञ्चयित्वा having deceived, सः he (Ravana), राघवौ the two descendants of Raghu, जहार carried away, सीताम् Sita, पक्षि-इन्द्र-प्रयास-क्षण-विघ्नितः obstructed for a moment by the effort of the king of birds

Anvaya: सः मृगरूपेण रक्षसा राघवौ वञ्चयित्वा पक्षीन्द्रप्रयासक्षणविघ्नितः सीतां जहार।

Simple meaning: Having deceived the two descendants of Raghu by a rakshasa in the form of a deer, he carried away Sita, obstructed for a moment by the effort of the king of birds.

तौ सीतान्वेषिणौ गृध्रं लूनपक्षमपश्यताम् ।

प्राणैर्दशरथप्रीतेरनृणं कण्ठवर्तिभिः॥12.54॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, सीता-अन्वेषिणौ searching for Sita, गृध्रम् the vulture (Jatayu), लून-पक्षम् with severed wings, अपश्यताम् saw, प्राणैः with life, दशरथ-प्रीतेः of friendship with Dasharatha, अनृणम् free from debt, कण्ठ-वर्तिभिः dwelling in the throat

Anvaya: सीतान्वेषिणौ तौ लूनपक्षं कण्ठवर्तिभिः प्राणैः दशरथप्रीतेः अनृणं गृध्रम् अपश्यताम्।

Simple meaning: Those two searching for Sita saw the vulture with severed wings, who was holding his life breath in his throat and who was freed from the debt of friendship with Dasharatha (in trying to defend Sita).

Note: Jatayu was Dasharatha’s friend. He saw Sita being carried away by Ravana and gave him a fight. Ravana cut off his wings and Jatayu held on for his life till he could tell Rama and Lakshmana of Sita’s abduction. By doing this, Jatayu considered himself free of the debt of friendship of Dasharatha.

स रावणहृतां ताभ्यां वचसाचष्ट मैथिलीम् ।

आत्मनः सुमहत्कर्म व्रणैरावेद्य संस्थितः॥12.55॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Jatayu), रावण-हृताम् carried away by Ravana, ताभ्याम् to those two, वचसा by words, आचष्ट told, मैथिलीम् Maithili (Sita), आत्मनः his own, सु-महत् very great, कर्म deed, व्रणैः by wounds, आवेद्य having revealed, संस्थितः departed/died

Anvaya: सः ताभ्यां रावणहृतां मैथिलीं वचसा आचष्ट। आत्मनः सुमहत् कर्म व्रणैः आवेद्य संस्थितः।

Simple meaning: He told those two by words about Maithili being carried away by Ravana. Having revealed his own very great deed by his wounds, he departed. (He did not tell anything about his own valor, instead he let his wounds do the talking.)

तयोस्तस्मिन्नवीभूतपितृव्यापत्तिशोकयोः ।

पितरीवाग्निसंस्कारात्परा ववृतिरे क्रियाः॥12.56॥

Word-by-word meaning: तयोः of those two, तस्मिन् in him, न​वीभूत-पितृ-व्यापत्ति-शोकयोः the grief of loss of father which was renewed, पितरि in a father, इव like, अग्नि-संस्कारात् from the fire ritual/cremation, पराः subsequent, ववृतिरे took place, क्रियाः rites

Anvaya: न​वीभूतपितृव्यापत्तिशोकयोः तयोः तस्मिन् पितरि इव अग्निसंस्कारात् पराः क्रियाः ववृतिरे।

Simple meaning: Those two, whose grief over the loss of father was renewed, performed all the last rites for Jatayu like for itself

वधनिर्धूतशापस्य कबन्धस्योपदेशतः ।

मुमूर्च्छ सख्यं रामस्य समानव्यसने हरौ॥12.57॥

Word-by-word meaning: वध-निर्धूत-शापस्य whose curse was shaken off by death, कबन्धस्य of Kabandha, उपदेशतः from the instruction, मुमूर्च्छ took concrete form/sprouted, सख्यम् friendship, रामस्य of Rama, समान-व्यसने having similar calamity, हरौ with Hari (Sugriva)

Anvaya: वधनिर्धूतशापस्य कबन्धस्य उपदेशतः समानव्यसने हरौ रामस्य सख्यं मुमूर्च्छ।

Simple meaning: From the instruction of Kabandha whose curse was shaken off by death, the friendship of Rama with Sugriva who had similar calamity took concrete form.

स हत्वा वालिनं वीरस्तत्पदे चिरकाङ्क्षिते ।

धातोः स्थान इवादेशं सुग्रीवं संन्यवेशयत्॥12.58॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Rama), हत्वा having killed, वालिनम् Vali, वीरः the hero, तत्-पदे in that position, चिर-काङ्क्षिते long desired, धातोः of the root word, स्थाने in place, इव like, आदेशम् substitute, सुग्रीवम् Sugriva, संन्यवेशयत् established

Anvaya: वीरः सः वालिनं हत्वा चिरकाङ्क्षिते तत्पदे सुग्रीवं – धातोः स्थाने आदेशम् इव – संन्यवेशयत्।

Simple meaning: The hero Rama, having killed Vali, established Sugriva firmly on the throne of Vali's kingdom which had been desired for a long time, just as in grammar (vyakarana-shastra) one root (dhatu) is established in place of another root through the rule of shabda-adesha (substitution).

Note: In grammatical forms like lit, lut, lrut etc., through the rule "अस्तेर्भूः”, भू is the substitute of the verb अस् 'to be' " the root 'भू' is substituted in place of 'अस्' (to be). And 'भू' performs the function of that original root.

Just as in grammar, a substitute (आदेशः) is established by removing the original form (स्थानी), similarly, having killed Vali, Rama established Sugriva in his place, and thus he (Sugriva) performed all the duties (kingdom/rule) of the original occupant (Vali).

इतस्ततश्च वैदेहीमन्वेष्टुं भर्तृचोदिताः ।

कपयश्चेरुरार्तस्य रामस्येव मनोरथाः॥12.59॥

Word-by-word meaning: इतस्ततः here and there, च and, वैदेहीम् Vaidehi (Sita), अन्वेष्टुम् to search for, भर्तृ-चोदिताः impelled by their lord, कपयः monkeys, चेरुः wandered, आर्तस्य of the distressed, रामस्य of Rama, इव like, मनोरथाः desires/thoughts

Anvaya: भर्तृचोदिताः कपयः वैदेहीम् अन्वेष्टुम् – आर्तस्य रामस्य मनोरथाः इव – इतस्ततः च चेरुः।

Simple meaning: Impelled by their lord, the monkeys wandered here and there to search for Vaidehi - like the thoughts of the distressed Rama pining for Sita.

प्रवृत्तावुपलब्धायां तस्याः संपातिदर्शनात् ।

मारुतिः सागरं तीर्णः संसारमिव निर्ममः॥12.60॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रवृत्तौ news/information, उपलब्धायाम् having been obtained, तस्याः of her (Sita), संपाति-दर्शनात् from the sight/meeting with Sampati, मारुतिः son of Marut/wind (Hanuman), सागरम् the ocean, तीर्णः crossed, संसारम् the worldly existence, इव like, निर्ममः one without attachment

Anvaya: संपातिदर्शनात् तस्याः प्रवृत्तौ उपलब्धायां मारुतिः सागरं – निर्ममः संसारम् इव – तीर्णः।

Simple meaning: Having obtained news of her from meeting with Sampati, the son of the wind crossed the ocean - like one without attachment crosses worldly existence.

दृष्टा विचिन्वता तेन लङ्कायां राक्षसीवृता ।

जानकी विषवल्लीभिः परितेव महौषधिः॥12.69॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्टा seen, विचिन्वता searching, तेन by him (Hanuman), लङ्कायाम् in Lanka, राक्षसी-वृता surrounded by rakshasis, जानकी Janaki (Sita), विष-वल्लीभिः by poisonous creepers, परिता surrounded, इव like, महा-ओषधिः great medicinal herb

Anvaya: लङ्कायां विचिन्वता तेन राक्षसीवृता जानकी – विषवल्लीभिः परिता महौषधिः इव – दृष्टा ।

Simple meaning: She was seen by him searching in Lanka, Janaki surrounded by rakshasis - like a great medicinal herb surrounded by poisonous creepers.

तस्यै भर्तुरभिज्ञानमङ्गुलीयं ददौ कपिः ।

प्रत्युद्गतमिवानुष्णैस्तदानन्दाश्रुबिन्दुभिः॥12.62॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्यै to her, भर्तुः of her husband, अभिज्ञानम् token of recognition, अङ्गुलीयम् ring, ददौ gave, कपिः the monkey (Hanuman), प्रत्युद्गतम् stepping forward in welcome, इव as if, अनुष्णैः not hot/cool, तत्-आनन्द-अश्रु-बिन्दुभिः by her tears of joy from that

Anvaya: कपिः तस्यै भर्तुः अभिज्ञानम् अङ्गुलीयं ददौ। अनुष्णैः तदानन्दाश्रुबिन्दुभिः प्रत्युद्गतम् इव।

Simple meaning: The monkey gave to her the ring, the token of recognition from her husband. It was, as it were, the cool tears of joy stepped forward to welcome the ring.

Sita’s eyes were filled with tears of joy on receiving Rama’s token ring.

Note: Tears of sorrow are hot. Tears of joy are cool. शोकोष्णैः पार्थिवाश्रुभिः दूषयामास (12.4)

निर्वाप्य प्रियसंदेशैः सीतामक्षवधोद्धतः ।

स ददाह पुरीं लङ्कां क्षणसोढारिनिग्रहः॥12.63॥

Word-by-word meaning: निर्वाप्य having cooled/soothed, प्रिय-संदेशैः with the beloved’s messages, सीताम् Sita, अक्ष-वध-उद्धतः emboldened by the killing of Aksha, सः he (Hanuman), ददाह burnt, पुरीम् city, लङ्काम् Lanka, क्षण-सोढ-अरि-निग्रहः having endured the restraint by enemies for a moment

Anvaya: प्रियसंदेशैः सीतां निर्वाप्य अक्षवधोद्धतः क्षणसोढारिनिग्रहः सः लङ्कां पुरीं ददाह।

Simple meaning: Having soothed Sita with her beloved’s messages, emboldened by the killing of Aksha, having endured the restraint by enemies for a moment, he burnt the city of Lanka.

प्रत्यभिज्ञानरत्नं च रामायादर्शयत्कृती ।

हृदयं स्वयमायातं वैदेह्या इव मूर्तिमत्॥12.64॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रत्यभिज्ञान-रत्नम् the jewel of recognition, च and, रामाय to Rama, अदर्शयत् showed, कृती the skillful one (Hanuman), हृदयम् heart, स्वयम् itself, आयातम् come, वैदेह्याः of Vaidehi, इव like, मूर्तिमत् embodied/having form

Anvaya: कृती – स्वयम् आयातं मूर्तिमत् वैदेह्याः हृदयम् इव – प्रत्यभिज्ञानरत्नं रामाय अदर्शयत् च।

Simple meaning: The one who had accomplished his task, showed the Sita’s jewel of recognition to Rama, who felt as if her heart itself in embodied form had come to him.

स प्राप हृदयन्यस्तमणिस्पर्शनिमीलितः ।

अपयोधरसंसर्गां प्रियालिङ्गननिर्वृतिम्॥12.65॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Rama), प्राप obtained, हृदय-न्यस्त-मणि-स्पर्श-निमीलितः with eyes closed by touching the jewel placed on his heart, अ-पयोधर-संसर्गाम् without contact with her breasts, प्रिया-आलिङ्गन-निर्वृतिम् the bliss of embracing his beloved

Anvaya: सः हृदयन्यस्तमणिस्पर्शनिमीलितः अपयोधरसंसर्गां प्रियालिङ्गननिर्वृतिं प्राप।

Simple meaning: He, with eyes closed by touching the jewel placed on his heart, obtained the bliss of embracing his beloved without contact with her breasts.

श्रुत्वा रामः प्रियोदन्तं मेने तत्सङ्गमोत्सुकः ।

महार्णवपरिक्षेपं लङ्कायाः परिखालघुम्॥12.66॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रुत्वा having heard, रामः Rama, प्रिया-उदन्तम् news of his beloved, मेने considered, तत्-सङ्गम-उत्सुकः eager for union with her, महा-अर्णव-परिक्षेपम् the surrounding by the great ocean, लङ्कायाः of Lanka, परिखा-लघुम् as small as a moat

Anvaya: प्रियोदन्तं श्रुत्वा तत्सङ्गमोत्सुकः रामः लङ्कायाः महार्णवपरिक्षेपं परिखालघुं मेने।

Simple meaning: Having heard news of his beloved, Rama eager for union with her considered the surrounding of Lanka by the great ocean as small as a moat.

स प्रतस्थेऽरिनाशाय हरिसैन्यैरनुद्रुतः ।

न केवलं भुवः पृष्ठे व्योम्नि संबाधवर्तिभिः॥12.67॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Rama), प्रतस्थे set forth, अरि-नाशाय for the destruction of the enemy, हरि-सैन्यैः by monkey armies, अनुद्रुतः followed, न not, केवलम् only, भुवः of the earth, पृष्ठे on the back/surface, व्योम्नि in the sky, संबाध-वर्तिभिः moving in a crowded manner

Anvaya: न केवलं भुवः पृष्ठे, (अपि तु) व्योम्नि संबाधवर्तिभिः हरिसैन्यैः अनुद्रुतः सः अरिनाशाय प्रतस्थे।

Simple meaning: He set forth for the destruction of the enemy, followed by monkey armies moving in a crowded manner not only on the surface of the earth but also in the sky.

निविष्टमुदधेः कूले तं प्रपाद विभीषणः ।

स्नेहाद्राक्षसलक्ष्म्येव बुद्धिमाविश्य चोदितः॥12.68॥

Word-by-word meaning: निविष्टम् encamped, उदधेः of the ocean, कूले on the shore, तम् him (Rama), प्रपाद approached/took refuge, विभीषणः Vibhishana, स्नेहात् from affection, राक्षस-लक्ष्म्या by the Fortune of the rakshasas, इव as if, बुद्धिम् intelligence, आविश्य having entered, चोदितः impelled

Anvaya: उदधेः कूले निविष्टं तं  विभीषणः – राक्षसलक्ष्म्या स्नेहात् बुद्धिम् आविश्य चोदितः इव – प्रपाद।

Simple meaning: Vibhishana approached Rama who was encamped on the shore of the ocean -  as if urged on out of affection by the Fortune of Rakshasa who first inspired him with wisdom.

तस्मै निशाचरैश्वर्यं प्रतिशुश्राव राघवः ।

काले खलु समार​ब्धाः फलं बध्नन्ति नीतयः॥12.69॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मै to him (Vibhishana), निशाचर-ऐश्वर्यम् lordship over night-wanderers/rakshasas, प्रतिशुश्राव promised, राघवः Rama, काले at the proper time, खलु indeed, समारब्धाः undertaken, फलम् fruit, बध्नन्ति bear/bind, नीतयः policies/strategies/political expedients

Anvaya: राघवः तस्मै निशाचरैश्वर्यं प्रतिशुश्राव। काले समारब्धाः नीतयः फलं बध्नन्ति खलु।

Simple meaning: Rama promised to him lordship over night-wanderers. Indeed, strategies undertaken at the proper time bear fruit.

स सेतुं बन्धयामास प्लवगैर्लवणाम्भसि ।

रसातलादिवोन्मग्नं शेषं स्वप्नाय शार्ङ्गिणः॥12.70॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Rama), सेतुम् bridge, बन्धयामास caused to be built, प्लवगैः by the monkeys, लवण-अम्भसि in the salt water, रसातलात् from the netherworld, इव as if, उन्मग्नम् emerged/raised, शेषम् Shesha (the serpent), स्वप्नाय for the sleep, शार्ङ्गिणः of the wielder of Sharnga bow (Vishnu)

Anvaya: सः प्लवगैः लवणाम्भसि – शार्ङ्गिणः स्वप्नाय रसातलात् उन्मग्नं शेषम् इव – सेतुं बन्धयामास ।

Simple meaning: He caused a bridge to be built by the monkeys in the salt water - a bridge that appeared like Shesha that had risen from the netherworld to become the bed of Vishnu, the wielder of Sharnga bow.

तेनोत्तीर्य पथा लङ्कां रोधयामास पिङ्गलैः ।

द्वितीयं हेमप्राकारं कुर्वद्भिरिव वानरैः॥12.71॥

Word-by-word meaning: तेन by that, उत्तीर्य having crossed, पथा by the path, लङ्काम् Lanka, रोधयामास besieged, पिङ्गलैः with tawny/golden ones, द्वितीयम् second, हेम-प्राकारम् golden rampart, कुर्वद्भिः making, इव as if, वानरैः by monkeys

Anvaya: तेन पथा उत्तीर्य – द्वितीयं हेमप्राकारम् इव कुर्वद्भिः – पिङ्गलैः वानरैः लङ्कां रोधयामास।

Simple meaning: Having crossed by that path, he besieged Lanka with the tawny monkeys - as if making a second golden rampart.

रणः प्रववृते तत्र भीमः प्लवगरक्षसाम् ।

दिग्विजृम्भितकाकुत्स्थपौलस्त्यजयघोषणः॥12.72॥

Word-by-word meaning: रणः battle, प्रववृते commenced, तत्र there, भीमः terrible, प्लवग-रक्षसाम् of monkeys and rakshasas, दिक्-विजृम्भित expanding through the directions, काकुत्स्थ-पौलस्त्य-जय-घोषणः proclaiming the victory of Kakutstha (Rama) and Paulastya (Ravana)

Anvaya: तत्र प्लवगरक्षसां भीमः दिग्विजृम्भितकाकुत्स्थपौलस्त्यजयघोषणः रणः प्रववृते।

Simple meaning: There commenced a terrible battle of monkeys and rakshasas, in which the proclamations of the victory of Kakutstha and Paulastya spread through all the directions.

पादपाविद्धपरिघः शिलानिष्पिष्टमुद्गरः ।

अतिशस्त्रनखन्यासः शैलरुग्णमतंगजः॥12.73॥

Word-by-word meaning: पादप-आविद्ध-परिघः whose iron clubs were struck by trees, शिला-निष्पिष्ट-मुद्गरः whose maces were crushed by rocks, अति-शस्त्र-नख-न्यासः surpassing weapons in the use of nails/claws, शैल-रुग्ण-मतङ्गजः whose elephants were wounded by mountains

Anvaya: पादपाविद्धपरिघः शिलानिष्पिष्टमुद्गरः अतिशस्त्रनखन्यासः शैलरुग्णमतङ्गजः रणः प्रववृते।

Simple meaning: The battle commenced where the iron clubs were struck by trees, where maces were crushed by rocks, where weapons were surpassed by the use of claws, and where elephants were wounded by mountains.

अथ रामशिरश्छेददर्शनोद्भ्रान्तचेतनाम् ।

सीतां मायेति शंसन्ती त्रिजटा समजीवयत्॥12.74॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, राम-शिरः-छेद-दर्शन-उद्भ्रान्त-चेतनाम् whose consciousness was lost on seeing Rama's head cut off, सीताम् Sita, माया illusion, इति thus, शंसन्ती declaring, त्रिजटा Trijata, समजीवयत् revived

Anvaya: अथ रामशिरश्छेददर्शनोद्भ्रान्तचेतनां सीतां "माया" इति शंसन्ती त्रिजटा समजीवयत्।

Simple meaning: Then Trijata revived Sita who had lost her consciousness on seeing Rama's head cut off, declaring "it is an illusion".

कामं जीवति मे नाथ इति सा विजहौ शुचम् ।

प्राङ्मत्वा सत्यमस्यान्तं जीवितास्मीति लज्जिता॥12.75॥

Word-by-word meaning: कामम् indeed/fortunately, जीवति lives, मे my, नाथः lord, इति thus, सा she, विजहौ abandoned, शुचम् grief, प्राक् before, मत्वा having thought, सत्यम् true, अस्य of this, अन्तम् end, जीविता alive, अस्मि I am, इति thus, लज्जिता ashamed

Anvaya: "कामं मे नाथः जीवति" इति सा शुचं विजहौ। प्राक् अस्य अन्तं सत्यं मत्वा "जीविता अस्मि" इति लज्जिता।

Simple meaning: "Fortunately my lord lives," thus she abandoned her grief. Having thought before that this end was true, she was ashamed thinking "I am alive".

गरुडापातविश्लिष्टमेघनादास्त्रबन्धनः ।

दाशरथ्योः क्षणक्लेशः स्वप्नवृत्त इवाभवत्॥12.76॥

Word-by-word meaning: गरुड-आपात-विश्लिष्ट loosened by Garuda's descent, मेघनाद-अस्त्र-बन्धनः the bondage of Meghanada's weapon, दाशरथ्योः of the two sons of Dasharatha, क्षण-क्लेशः momentary distress, स्वप्न-वृत्तः occurred in a dream, इव like, अभवत् became

Anvaya: गरुडापातविश्लिष्टमेघनादास्त्रबन्धनः क्षणक्लेशः दाशरथ्योः स्वप्नवृत्तः इव अभवत्।

Simple meaning: The momentary distress of the two sons of Dasharatha from the bondage of Meghanada's (Naga) weapon loosened by Garuda's descent became like something that occurred in a dream.

ततो बिभेद पौलस्त्यः शक्त्या वक्षसि लक्ष्मणम् ।

रामस्त्वनाहतोऽप्यासीद्विदीर्णहृदयः शुचा॥12.77॥

Word-by-word meaning: ततः then, बिभेद pierced, पौलस्त्यः descendant of Pulastya (Ravana), शक्त्या with a spear, वक्षसि in the chest, लक्ष्मणम् Lakshmana, रामः Rama, तु but, अनाहतः unstruck, अपि even though, आसीत् was, विदीर्ण-हृदयः one with his heart torn, शुचा by grief

Anvaya: ततः पौलस्त्यः शक्त्या लक्ष्मणं वक्षसि बिभेद। रामः तु अनाहतः अपि शुचा विदीर्णहृदयः आसीत्।

Simple meaning: Then the descendant of Pulastya pierced Lakshmana in the chest with a spear. But Rama, though unstruck, was as if it were his heart torn by grief.

स मारुतिसमानीतमहौषधिहतव्यथः ।

लङ्कास्त्रीणां पुनश्चक्रे विलापाचार्यकं शरैः॥12.78॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Lakshmana), मारुति-समानीत brought by the son of Marut (Hanuman), महा-औषधि-हत-व्यथः whose pain was removed by the great herb, लङ्का-स्त्रीणाम् of the women of Lanka, पुनः again, चक्रे made, विलाप-आचार्यकम् instruction in lamentation, शरैः with arrows

Anvaya: मारुतिसमानीतमहौषधिहतव्यथः सः पुनः शरैः लङ्कास्त्रीणां विलापाचार्यकं चक्रे।

Simple meaning: He whose pain was removed by the great herb brought by the son of the wind, again made instruction in lamentation for the women of Lanka with his arrows.

स नादं मेघनादस्य धनुश्चेन्द्रायुधप्रभम् ।

मेघस्येव शरत्कालो न किञ्चित्पर्यशेषयत्॥12.79॥

Word-by-word meaning: सः he (Lakshmana), नादम् sound, मेघनादस्य of Meghanada, धनुः bow, च and, इन्द्र-आयुध-प्रभम् having the splendor of Indra's rainbow, मेघस्य of a cloud, इव like, शरत्-कालः autumn season, न not, किञ्चित् anything, पर्यशेषयत् left/remaining

Anvaya: सः – शरत्कालः मेघस्य इव – मेघनादस्य नादं च इन्द्रायुधप्रभं धनुः न किञ्चित् पर्यशेषयत्।

Simple meaning: He allowed neither the war-cries of Meghnada or his bow resplendent as the rain-bow (weapon of Indra) to remain, as the autumnal season completely annihilates the thunders of clouds and the rain-bow.

कुम्भकर्णः कपीन्द्रेण तुल्यावस्थः स्वसुः कृतः ।

रुरोध रामं श‍ृङ्गीव टङ्कच्छिन्नमनःशिलः॥12.80॥

Word-by-word meaning: कुम्भकर्णः Kumbhakarna, कपीन्द्रेण by the lord of monkeys (Sugriva), तुल्य-अवस्थः made equal in condition, स्वसुः of his sister, कृतः made, रुरोध obstructed, रामम् Rama, शृङ्गी a horned animal, इव like, टङ्क-छिन्न-मनः-शिलः with red arsenic (Manas-shila) cut down by the stone-cutting instrument

Anvaya: कपीन्द्रेण स्वसुः तुल्यावस्थः कृतः कुम्भकर्णः – टङ्कच्छिन्नमनःशिलः शृङ्गी इव – रामं रुरोध।

Simple meaning: Reduced to a condition similar to that of his sister by the lord of the monkeys (Sugriva had cut off Kumbhakarna’s ears and nose, thereby reducing him to state similar to his sister Shurpanakha) Kumbhakarna rushed towards Rama, like a mountain with red arsenic ores which had been cut down by the stone-cutting instrument.

Kumbhakarna was bleeding profusely and he resembled a mountain which had been cut and had red-arsenic flowing down its slopes.

अकाले बोधितो भ्रात्रा प्रियस्वप्नो वृथा भवान् ।

रामेषुभिरितीवासौ दीर्घनिद्रां प्रवेशितः॥12.81॥

Word-by-word meaning: अकाले at an improper time, बोधितः awakened, भ्रात्रा by brother, प्रिय-स्वप्नः fond of sleep, वृथा in vain, भवान् you, राम-इषुभिः by Rama's arrows, इति thus, इव as if, असौ he (Kumbhakarna), दीर्घ-निद्राम् long sleep, प्रवेशितः was made to enter

Anvaya: "प्रियस्वप्नः भवान् वृथा भ्रात्रा अकाले बोधितः" इति इव असौ रामेषुभिः दीर्घनिद्रां प्रवेशितः।

Simple meaning: "You, who are fond of sleep, were awakened by your brother in vain, at an improper time," as if thus, he was made to enter long sleep by Rama's arrows.

इतराण्यपि रक्षांसि पेतुर्वानरकोटिषु ।

रजांसि समरोत्थानि तच्छोणितनदीष्विव॥12.82॥

Word-by-word meaning: इतराणि other, अपि also, रक्षांसि rakshasas, पेतुः fell, वानर-कोटिषु among monkey multitudes, रजांसि dust particles, समर-उत्थानि risen from battle, तत्-शोणित-नदीषु in the rivers of their blood, इव like

Anvaya: इतराणि रक्षांसि अपि वानरकोटिषु – समरोत्थानि रजांसि तच्छोणितनदीषु इव – पेतुः।

Simple meaning: Other rakshasas also fell among the monkey multitudes. They appeared like dust raised from the battle-field falling upon the streams of their blood.

निर्ययावथ पौलस्त्यः पुनर्युद्धाय मन्दिरात् ।

अरावणमरामं वा जगदद्येति निश्चितः॥12.83॥

Word-by-word meaning: निर्ययौ went forth, अथ then, पौलस्त्यः descendant of Pulastya (Ravana), पुनः again, युद्धाय for battle, मन्दिरात् from the palace, अ-रावणम् without Ravana, अ-रामम् without Rama, वा or, जगत् world, अद्य today, इति thus, निश्चितः determined

Anvaya: अथ पौलस्त्यः – "अद्य जगत् अरावणं वा अरामम्" इति निश्चितः – मन्दिरात् पुनः युद्धाय निर्ययौ।

Simple meaning: Then the descendant of Pulastya, determined that "today the world will be without Ravana or without Rama", went forth from the palace again for battle.

रामं पदातिमालोक्य लङ्केशं च वरूथिनम् ।

हरियुग्यं रथं तस्मै प्रजिघाय पुरंदरः॥12.84॥

Word-by-word meaning: रामम् Rama, पदातिम् on foot, आलोक्य having seen, लङ्का-ईशम् lord of Lanka, च and, वरूथिनम् on chariot, हरि-युग्यम् yoked with tawny colored horses, रथम् chariot, तस्मै to him, प्रजिघाय sent, पुरंदरः Indra

Anvaya: पदातिं रामं वरूथिनं लङ्केशं च आलोक्य पुरंदरः तस्मै हरियुग्यं रथं प्रजिघाय।

Simple meaning: Having seen Rama on foot and the lord of Lanka with a chariot, Indra sent a chariot yoked with tawny colored horses to Rama.

तमाधूतध्वजपटं व्योमगङ्गोर्मिवायुभिः ।

देवसूतभुजालम्बी जैत्रमध्यास्त राघवः॥12.85॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् that, आधूत-ध्वज-पटम् whose flag banners were waving, व्योम-गङ्गा-ऊर्मि-वायुभिः by the winds of the waves of the celestial Ganga, देव-सूत-भुज-आलम्बी supported by the arms of the gods' charioteer (Matali), जैत्रम् victorious, अध्यास्त mounted, राघवः Rama

Anvaya: देवसूतभुजालम्बी राघवः व्योमगङ्गोर्मिवायुभिः आधूतध्वजपटं तं जैत्रम् अध्यास्त।

Simple meaning: Supported by the arms of the gods' charioteer, Rama mounted that victorious chariot whose flag banners were waving by the winds of the waves of the celestial Ganga.

मातलिस्तस्य माहेन्द्रमामुमोच तनुच्छदम् ।

यत्रोत्पलदलक्लैब्यमस्त्राण्यापुः सुरद्विषाम्॥12.86॥

Word-by-word meaning: मातलिः Matali, तस्य to Rama, माहेन्द्रम् of Mahendra/Indra, आमुमोच released/put on, तनु-छ​दम् body armor, यत्र where, उत्पल-दल-क्लैब्यम् the softness of lotus petals, अस्त्राणि weapons, आपुः attained, सुर-द्विषाम् of the enemies of gods

Anvaya: मातलिः तस्य माहेन्द्रं तनुच्छदम् आमुमोच, यत्र सुरद्विषाम् अस्त्राणि उत्पलदलक्लैब्यम् आपुः।

Simple meaning: Matali put the armor of Indra on Rama, on which the weapons of the enemies of gods attained the softness of lotus petals.

अन्योन्यदर्शनप्राप्तविक्रमावसरं चिरात् ।

रामरावणयोर्युद्धं चरितार्थमिवाभवत्॥12.87॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्योन्य-दर्शन-प्राप्त-विक्रम-अवसरम् obtaining the opportunity for display of valor by seeing each other, चिरात् after a long time, राम-रावणयोः of Rama and Ravana, युद्धम् battle, चरित-अर्थम् fulfilled in its purpose, इव as if, अभवत् became

Anvaya: अन्योन्यदर्शनप्राप्तविक्रमावसरं चिरात् रामरावणयोः युद्धं चरितार्थम् इव अभवत्।

Simple meaning: The battle of Rama and Ravana, obtaining the opportunity for display of valor by seeing each other after a long time, became as if fulfilled in its purpose.

भुजमूर्धोरुबाहुल्यादेकोऽपि धनदानुजः ।

ददृशे ह्ययथापूर्वो मातृवंश इव स्थितः॥12.88॥

Word-by-word meaning: भुज-मूर्ध-ऊरु-बाहुल्यात् from the abundance of arms, heads, and thighs, एकः one, अपि even though, धनद-अनुजः younger brother of Dhanada/Kubera (Ravana), ददृशे appeared, हि indeed, अ-यथा-पूर्वः unlike before, मातृ-वंशः maternal lineage, इव like, स्थितः standing

Anvaya: अयथापूर्वः एकः अपि धनदानुजः भुजमूर्धोरुबाहुल्यात् मातृवंशः स्थितः इव ददृशे हि।

Simple meaning: Not being surrounded by relatives as before, the younger brother of Kubera, though standing alone, appeared on account of his many arms, heads and thighs, as if standing in the midst of his kinsmen by mother's side (Rakshasas).

Though Ravana was standing alone, he appeared as if he was surrounded by rakshasas on account of his many arms, heads and thighs.

जेतारं लोकपालानां स्वमुखैरर्चितेश्वरम् ।

रामस्तुलितकैलासमारातिं बह्वमन्यत॥12.89॥

Word-by-word meaning: जेतारम् conqueror, लोक-पालानाम् of the world protectors, स्व-मुखैः by his own heads, अर्चित-ईश्वरम् who worshipped Ishvara (Shiva), रामः Rama, तुलित-कैलासम् who weighed/lifted Kailasa, आरातिम् enemy, बहु highly, अमन्यत regarded

Anvaya: रामः लोकपालानां जेतारं स्वमुखैः अर्चितेश्वरं तुलितकैलासम् आरातिं बहु अमन्यत।

Simple meaning: Rama thought highly of his enemy who was the conqueror of the world protectors, who worshipped Ishvara with his own heads, and who weighed Kailasa.

तस्य स्फुरति पौलस्त्यः सीतासंगमशंसिनि ।

निचखानाधिकक्रोधः शरं सव्येतरे भुजे॥12.90॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य his (Rama's), स्फुरति throbbing/palpitating, पौलस्त्यः descendant of Pulastya (Ravana), सीता-सङ्गम-शंसिनि indicating union with Sita, निचखान buried, अधिक-क्रोधः with excessive anger, शरम् arrow, सव्य-इतरे in the one which is not left (right), भुजे arm

Anvaya: अधिकक्रोधः पौलस्त्यः स्फुरति सीतासङ्गमशंसिनि तस्य सव्येतरे भुजे शरं निचखान।

Simple meaning: The descendant of Pulastya, with excessive anger buried an arrow in Rama’s right arm that was throbbing indicating union with Sita.

Note: The throbbing of the right hand is considered auspicious in men.

As the battle was drawing to a close,  Rama’s right arm started to throb. It is an auspicious sign for men and for Rama it meant union with Sita. To that throbbing right arm, Ravana hit it with an arrow.

रावणस्यापि रामास्तो भित्त्वा हृदयमाशुगः ।

विवेश भुवमाख्यातुमुरगेभ्य इव प्रियम्॥12.91॥

Word-by-word meaning: रावणस्य of Ravana, अपि also, राम-अस्तः shot by Rama, भित्त्वा having pierced, हृदयम् heart, आशुगः swift arrow, विवेश entered, भुवम् earth, आख्यातुम् to tell, उरगेभ्यः to the serpents, इव like, प्रियम् pleasant news

Anvaya: रामास्तः आशुगः रावणस्य अपि हृदयं भित्त्वा – उरगेभ्यः प्रियम् आख्यातुम् इव – भुवं विवेश।

Simple meaning: The swift arrow shot by Rama, having pierced the heart of Ravana also, entered the earth - as if to tell pleasant news to the serpents.

वचसैव तयोर्वाक्यमस्त्रमस्त्रेण निघ्नतोः ।

अन्योन्यजयसंरम्भो ववृधे वादिनोरिव॥12.92॥

Word-by-word meaning: वचसा by speech, एव indeed, तयोः of those two, वाक्यम् speech, अस्त्रम् weapon, अस्त्रेण with weapon, निघ्नतोः striking, अन्योन्य-जय-संरम्भः eagerness for mutual victory, ववृधे increased, वादिनोः of debaters, इव like

Anvaya: तयोः वाक्यं वचसा एव अस्त्रेण अस्त्रं निघ्नतोः तयोः – वादिनोः इव – अन्योन्यजयसंरम्भः ववृधे।

Simple meaning: Each of them who met with words the strife of words of the other, and who with missiles met other's missiles, had their impetuous zeal for obtaining victory over the other, increased like that of two debaters.

विक्रमव्यतिहारेण सामान्याऽभूद्द्वयोरपि ।

जयश्रीरन्तरा वेदिर्मत्तवारणयोरिव॥12.93॥

Word-by-word meaning: विक्रम-व्यतिहारेण by the exchange of valor, सामान्या common/shared, अभूत् became, द्वयोः of the two, अपि also, जय-श्रीः the goddess of victory, अन्तरा in between, वेदिः an altar, मत्त-वारणयोः of two maddened elephants, इव like

Anvaya: जयश्रीः विक्रमव्यतिहारेण द्वयोः अपि अन्तरा – वेदिः मत्तवारणयोः इव – सामान्या अभूत्।

Simple meaning: As an altar where the two infuriated elephants are fighting is common to both, so did the  fortune of victory become common to both Rama and Ravana i. e. sometimes sided with the one, sometimes with the other, on account of the display of valour which each of them made in succession.

Note: The word vikrama-vyatihara is key — it means an exchange or alternation of heroic acts. Every time one warrior performs a feat of valor, the other matches or surpasses it, which then pushes victory back toward the first. So Jayashri (victory) is not static — she is oscillating, swaying back and forth, which is precisely why she ends up feeling "common to both" rather than belonging to either. In simple words, sometimes Rama gained dominance and sometimes Ravana.

कृतप्रतिकृतप्रीतैस्तयोर्मुक्तां सुरासुरैः ।

परस्परशरव्राताः पुष्पवृष्टिं न सेहिरे॥12.94॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृत-प्रतिकृत-प्रीतैः delighted by deed and counter-deed, तयोः of those two, मुक्ताम् released, सुर-असुरैः by gods and rakshasas, परस्पर-शर-व्राताः mutual multitudes of arrows, पुष्प-वृष्टिम् shower of flowers, न not, सेहिरे endured/allowed

Anvaya: कृतप्रतिकृतप्रीतैः सुरासुरैः मुक्तां पुष्पवृष्टिं तयोः परस्परशरव्राताः न सेहिरे।

Simple meaning: The gods and asuras delighted with the deeds and counter deeds, showered flowers upon them, which the arrows flying back and forth prevented from falling upon them (lit. did not endure).

Note: The gods and asuras watching this battle from the skies, delighted by the show of valor of Rama and Ravana, showered flowers upon the both of them. But both of them were releasing arrows at each other so furiously that it kind of created a barrier and prevented the shower of flowers from falling upon them.

अयःशङ्कुचितां रक्षः शतघ्नीमथ शत्रवे ।

हृतां वैवस्वतस्येव कूटशाल्मलिमक्षिपत्॥12.95॥

Word-by-word meaning: अयः-शङ्कु-चिताम् studded with iron spikes, रक्षः the rakshasas (Ravana), शतघ्नीम् a deadly weapon (shatghni), अथ then, शत्रवे at the enemy, हृताम् stolen, वैवस्वतस्य of Vaivasvata (Yama), इव like, कूट-शाल्मलिम् the kutashalmali, अक्षिपत् hurled

Anvaya: अथ रक्षः अयःशङ्कुचितां शतघ्नीं – वैवस्वतस्य हृतां कूटशाल्मलिम् इव – शत्रवे अक्षिपत्।

Simple meaning: Then the Rakshasa threw शतघ्नी against his enemy provided with pointed iron spikes, which he had obtained in war and was like the कूटशाल्मलि of the god of death.

राघवो रथमप्राप्तां तामाशां च सुरद्विषाम् ।

अर्धचन्द्रमुखैर्बाणैश्चिच्छेद कदलीसुखम्॥12.96॥

Word-by-word meaning: राघवः Rama, रथम् chariot, अप्राप्ताम् not reaching, ताम् that, आशाम् hope, च and, सुर-द्विषाम् of the enemies of gods, अर्ध-चन्द्र-मुखैः with crescent-shaped tips, बाणैः with arrows, चिच्छेद cut, कदली-सुखम् easily like a banana tree

Anvaya: राघवः रथम् अप्राप्तां तां सुरद्विषाम् आशां च अर्धचन्द्रमुखैः बाणैः कदलीसुखं चिच्छेद।

Simple meaning: Rama cut that weapon, which did not reach the chariot, and the hope of the enemies of gods with arrows having crescent-shaped tips, as easily as a banana tree.

अमोघं संदधे चास्मै धनुष्येकधनुर्धरः ।

ब्राह्ममस्त्रं प्रियाशोकशल्यनिष्कर्षणौषधम्॥12.97॥

Word-by-word meaning: अमोघम् unfailing, संदधे fixed, च and, अस्मै on him (Ravana), धनुषि on the bow, एक-धनुः-धरः the sole bowman/one without a rival, ब्राह्मम् of Brahma, अस्त्रम् weapon, प्रिया-शोक-शल्य-निष्कर्षण-औषधम् the medicine for extracting the thorn of grief of the beloved

Anvaya: एकधनुर्धरः धनुषि प्रियाशोकशल्यनिष्कर्षणौषधम् अमोघं ब्राह्मम् अस्त्रम् अस्मै च संदधे।

Simple meaning: And the bowman without a rival, fixed on the bow the unfailing weapon of Brahma towards Ravana, the medicine for extracting the thorn of grief of his beloved.

तद्व्योम्नि शतधा भिन्नं ददृशे दीप्तिमन्मुखम् ।

वपुर्महोरगस्येव करालफणमण्डलम्॥12.98॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, व्योम्नि in the sky, शतधा in a hundred parts, भिन्नम् split, ददृशे was seen, दीप्तिमत्-मुखम् radiant faced, वपुः body, महा-उरगस्य of a great serpent, इव like, कराल-फण-मण्डलम् with terrible expanded hoods

Anvaya: व्योम्नि शतधा भिन्नम् दीप्तिममन्मुखम् तत् – महोरगस्य करालफणमण्डलम् वपुः इव – ददृशे।

Simple meaning: That missile with its blazing points being divided into hundreds of splinters in the sky appeared like the body of the great serpent ( i. e. Shesha ) with his formidable expanded hood.

तेन मन्त्रप्रयुक्तेन निमेषार्धादपातयत् ।

स रावणशिरःपङ्क्तिमज्ञातव्रणवेदनाम्॥12.99॥

Word-by-word meaning: तेन by that, मन्त्र-प्रयुक्तेन empowered by mantra, निमेष-अर्धात् in half an eye-blink, अपातयत् struck down, सः he (Rama), रावण-शिरः-पङ्क्तिम् the row of Ravana's heads, अज्ञात-व्रण-वेदनाम् not knowing the pain of wounds

Anvaya: सः मन्त्रप्रयुक्तेन तेन निमेषार्धात् अज्ञातव्रणवेदनां रावणशिरःपङ्क्तिम् अपातयत्।

Simple meaning: He, in half an eye-blink, struck down with that weapon empowered by mantra the row of Ravana's heads which did not know the pain of being cut.

Rama cut off the heads in a jiffy that the heads did not realise the pain itself.

बालार्कप्रतिमेवाप्सु वीचिभिन्ना पतिष्यतः ।

रराज रक्षःकायस्य कण्ठच्छेदपरम्परा॥12.100॥

Word-by-word meaning: बाल-अर्क-प्रतिमा the reflection of the young sun, इव like, अप्सु in water, वीचि-भिन्ना broken by waves, पतिष्यतः falling, रराज shone, रक्षः-कायस्य of the rakshasa’s body, कण्ठ-छेद-परम्परा the succession of severed necks

Anvaya: पतिष्यतः रक्षःकायस्य कण्ठच्छेदपरम्परा – वीचिभिन्ना अप्सु बालार्कप्रतिमा इव – रराज।

Simple meaning: The succession of severed necks of the rakshasa’s body falling shone like the reflection of the young sun broken by waves in water.

Note: The bloodied falling heads of Ravana is likened to the reflection of the rising sun broken on the surface of the water by the waves.

मरुतां पश्यतां तस्य शिरांसि पतितान्यपि ।

मनो नातिविशस्वास पुनःसंधानशङ्किनाम्॥12.101॥

Word-by-word meaning: मरुताम् of the winds/gods, पश्यताम् watching, तस्य his (Ravana's), शिरांसि heads, पतितानि fallen, अपि even though, मनः mind, न not, अतिविशस्वास became completely confident, पुनः-संधान-शङ्किनाम् fearing rejoining/regeneration

Anvaya: पतितानि तस्य शिरांसि पश्यताम् अपि पुनःसंधानशङ्किनां मरुतां मनः न अतिविशस्वास।

Simple meaning: Even though his heads had fallen, the minds of the gods watching, did not become completely confident, fearing their rejoining.

अथ मदगुरुपक्षैलोकपालद्विपानामनुगतमलिवृन्दैर्गण्डभित्तीर्विहाय ।

उपनतमणिबन्धे मूर्ध्नि पौलस्त्यशत्रोः सुरभि सुरविमुक्तं पुष्पवर्षं पपात॥12.102॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, मद-गुरु-पक्षैः with heavy wings due to intoxication, लोक-पाल-द्विपानाम् of the elephants who were the guardians of the quarters, अनुगत-अलि-वृन्दैः followed by bees, गण्ड-भित्तीः the walls of temples/cheeks, विहाय leaving, उपनत-मणि-बन्धे the placing of the crown on which was soon to take place, मूर्ध्नि on the head, पौलस्त्य-शत्रोः of the enemy of Paulastya, सुरभि fragrant, सुर-विमुक्तम् released by gods, पुष्प-वर्षम् flower shower, पपात fell

Anvaya: अथ मदगुरुपक्षैः अलिवृन्दैः लोकपालद्विपानां गण्डभित्तीः विहाय अनुगतं सुरभि सुरविमुक्तं पुष्पवर्षम् उपनतमणिबन्धे पौलस्त्यशत्रोः मूर्ध्नि पपात।

Simple meaning: Then on the head of the enemy of the son of Pulastya, on whom the placing of the crown on which was soon to take place, there fell a shower of fragrant flowers poured down by gods. The fragrant flowers were followed by bees whose wings were heavy with ichor, which had left the spacious temples (lit. wall-like cheeks) of the elephants of the guardians of quarters.

Note: The ichor flowing down the cheeks of the elephants is very sweet and bees are attracted to it. So the poet is painting a picture here that when the flowers were showered on Rama’s head, on which the Royal Crown was soon to be placed, the bees, attracted by the fragrance of the flowers, left the cheeks of the elephants, and followed the fragrant flowers. And their wings were already laden with the weight of ichor on which they were previously hovering.

The elephants mentioned here are not ordinary elephants but are the eight mighty elephants of the quarters on which the earth rests.

The names of the eight male elephants are: Airavata (East), Pundarika (South-east), Vamana (South), Kumuda (South-west), Anjana (West), Pushpadanta (North-west), Sarvabhauma (North), Supratika (North-east)

यन्ता हरेः सपदि संहृतकार्मुकज्यमापृच्छ्य राघवमनुष्ठितदेवकार्यम् ।

नामाङ्करावणशराङ्कितकेतुयष्टिमूर्ध्वं रथं हरिसहस्रयुजं निनाय॥12.103॥

Word-by-word meaning: यन्ता charioteer, हरेः of Hari/Indra (Matali), सपदि immediately, संहृत-कार्मुक-ज्यम् whose bow-string was withdrawn, आपृच्छ्य having taken leave, राघवम् Rama, अनुष्ठित-देव-कार्यम् who had accomplished the gods' task, नाम-अङ्क-रावण-शर-अङ्कित-केतु-यष्टिम् whose flag staff was marked with the Ravana's arrows which had his name on it, ऊर्ध्वम् upward, रथम् chariot, हरि-सहस्र-युजम् yoked with a thousand horses, निनाय led/drove

Anvaya: हरेः यन्ता सपदि संहृतकार्मुकज्यम् अनुष्ठितदेवकार्यं राघवम् आपृच्छ्य नामाङ्करावणशराङ्कितकेतुयष्टिं हरिसहस्रयुजं रथम् ऊर्ध्वं निनाय।

Simple meaning: The charioteer of Indra, having taken leave of Rama, whose bow-string was immediately withdrawn and who had accomplished the gods' task, drove the chariot yoked with a thousand horses upward, whose flag staff was marked with Ravana’s arrows with his name imprinted on them.

रघुपतिरपि जातवेदोविशुद्धां प्रगृह्य प्रियां प्रियसुहृदि विभीषणे संगमय्य श्रियं वैरिणः ।

रविसुतसहितेन तेनानुयातः ससौमित्रिणा भुजविजितविमानरत्नाधिरूढः प्रतस्थे पुरीम्॥12.104॥

Word-by-word meaning: रघुपतिः lord of Raghus (Rama), अपि also, जात-वेदः-विशुद्धाम् purified by entering (the fire at) the altar, प्रगृह्य taking, प्रियाम् beloved, प्रिय-सुहृदि in the dear friend, विभीषणे Vibhishana, संगमय्य having united, श्रियम् prosperity/kingdom, वैरिणः of the enemy, रवि-सुत-सहितेन accompanied by the son of the sun (Sugriva), तेन by him, अनुयातः followed, स-सौमित्रिणा with the son of Sumitra, भुज-विजित-विमान-रत्न-अधिरूढः mounted on the excellent aerial vehicle won by the force of his arms (Pushpaka Vimana), प्रतस्थे set forth, पुरीम् to the city

Anvaya: रघुपतिः अपि जातवेदोविशुद्धां प्रियां प्रगृह्य, प्रियसुहृदि विभीषणे वैरिणः श्रियं संगमय्य, रविसुतसहितेन ससौमित्रिणा तेन अनुयातः, भुजविजितविमानरत्नाधिरूढः पुरीं प्रतस्थे।

Simple meaning: The lord of the Raghus also having accepted his beloved who was purified by fire and having united his dear friend Vibhishana to his enemy Ravana’s royal fortune (crowing him as the King of Lanka), took his seat in that best of celestial cars (Pushpaka Vimana) which he had acquired by the force of his arms, and set out for his capital accompanied by Vibhishana, Sugriva and the son of Sumitra.

Note: Meaning of “united Vibhishana to enemy’s Fortune" - Ravana who was the enemy of Rama, was the king of Lanka. After his death, Rama crowned Vibhishana as the King. So the poet says Rama united Vibhishana with the glory which was previously his enemy Ravana's.
