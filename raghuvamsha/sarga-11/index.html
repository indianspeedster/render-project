Kalidasa's Ramayana - Raghuvamsha - Sarga 11

Description of Rama and Sita’s wedding

(93 shlokas)

कौशिकेन स किल क्षितीश्वरो राममध्वरविघातशान्तये ।

काकपक्षधरमेत्य याचितस्तेजसां हि न वयः समीक्ष्यते ॥11.1॥

Word-by-word meaning: कौशिकेन by the descent of Kushika dynasty (Kaushika - Vishwamitra), सः he, किल indeed, क्षिती-श्वरः lord of the earth/king, रामम् Rama, अध्वर-विघात-शान्तये to stop the disturbances in the yagna, काकपक्षधरम् who still wore sidelocks of hair, एत्य having come, याचितः requested, तेजसाम् brilliance/valour, हि indeed, न not, वयः age, समीक्ष्यते is considered/judged

काकपक्ष   काक—पक्ष m. ‘crow's wing’, side-locks of hair on the temples of young men (three or five locks on each side left when the head is first shaved and allowed to remain there, especially in persons of the military caste)

काकपक्ष (पुंलिङ्गः)  शिखा

समानार्थकाः — काकपक्ष शिखण्डक src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya:  कौशिकेन एत्य सः क्षितीश्वरः अध्वरविघातशान्तये काकपक्षधरं रामं याचितः किल। हि तेजसां वयः न समीक्ष्यते।

Simple meaning: Sage Vishwamitra (Kaushika), having arrived, requested King Dasharatha to send Rama—who still had kakapaksha (the boyhood side-lock of hair)—for the purpose of protecting his sacrifice from disturbances. Indeed, for those endowed with valor/brilliance, age is not a consideration.

कृच्छ्रलब्धमपि लब्धवर्णभाक्तं दिदेश मुनये सलक्ष्मणम् ।

अप्यसुप्रणयिनां रघोः कुले न व्यहन्यत कदाचिदर्थिता ॥11.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: कृच्छ्र-लब्धम् obtained with difficulty, अपि even though, लब्ध-वर्ण-भाक् the king who was the patron of the learned, तम् him (Rama), दिदेश gave, मुनये to the sage, सलक्ष्मणम् together with Lakshmana, अपि even, असु-प्रणयिनाम् of the ones who ask for life also, रघोः of Raghu, कुले in the dynasty, न व्यहन्यत was never wasted, कदाचित् at any time, अर्थिता one who desires

लब्धवर्णभाक्तम् - लब्धाः वर्णाः  यैः ते लब्धवर्णाः -  one who have obtained the knowledge, तान् भजते - लब्धवर्णभाक् - their patron (King Dasharatha)

असु (पुंलिङ्गः,नित्यबहुवचनान्तः)  पञ्चवायवः

समानार्थकाः — असु प्राण src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

असुप्रणयिनाम् - असून्  प्रणयन्ति,  असुप्रणयिनः तेषाम्

Anvaya: लब्धवर्णभाक् (दशरथः)  कृच्छ्रलब्धम् अपि सलक्ष्मणं तं मुनये दिदेश। रघोः कुले असुप्रणयिनाम् अपि अर्थिता कदाचित् न व्यहन्यत।

Simple meaning: Dasharatha, who was a patron of the learned, sent Rama, who was obtained with great difficulty, along with Lakshmana to the sage Vishwamitra. In the dynasty of Raghu, even the ones who asked for (one’s) life were not turned away.

यावदादिशति पार्थिवस्तयोर्निर्गमाय पुरमार्गसंस्क्रियाम् ।

तावदाशु विदधे मरुत्सखैः सा सपुष्पजलवर्षिभिर्घनैः ॥11.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: यावत् while, आदिशति commands, पार्थिवः the king, तयोः of the two, निर्गमाय for departure, पुर-मार्ग-संस्क्रियाम् preparation of the city roads, तावत् meanwhile, आशु quickly, विदधे arranged, मरुत्सखैः accompanied by the winds, सा she (that preparation), स-पुष्प-जल-वर्षिभिः showering water along with flowers, घनैः by clouds

Anvaya: यावत् पार्थिवः तयोः निर्गमाय पुरमार्गसंस्क्रियाम् आदिशति, तावत् मरुत्सखैः सपुष्पजलवर्षिभिः घनैः सा (पुरमार्गसंस्क्रिया) आशु विदधे।

Simple meaning: No sooner had the king ordered preparations of the roads of the city for Rama and Lakshmana’s departure; the clouds, helped by the winds to blow away the dust, quickly assisted the preparations by raining water and flowers.

तौ निदेशकरणोद्यतौ पितुर्धन्विनौ चरणयोर्निपेततुः ।

भूपतेरपि तयोः प्रवत्स्यतोर्नम्रयोरुपरि बाष्पबिन्दवः ॥11.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, निदेश-करण-उद्यतौ eager to carry out the command, पितुः of their father, धन्विनौ bearing bows, चरणयोः at the feet, निपेततुः fell, भूपतेः of the king, अपि even, तयोः of those two, प्रवत्स्यतोः who were about to depart, नम्रयोः who were bent in humility, उपरि upon, बाष्प-बिन्दवः drops of tears

Anvaya: निदेशकरणोद्यतौ धन्विनौ तौ पितुः चरणयोः निपेततुः; भूपतेः अपि बाष्पबिन्दवः प्रवत्स्यतोः नम्रयोः तयोः उपरि (निपेततुः)।

Simple meaning: Rama and Lakshmana, ready to obey their father’s command, bowed at his feet with their bows in hand. As they bent down to depart, tears from the king’s eyes fell upon them.

तौ पितुर्नयनजेन वारिणा किंचिदुक्षितशिखण्डकावुभौ ।

धन्विनौ तमृषिमन्वगच्छतां पौरदृष्टिकृतमार्गतोरणौ ॥11.5॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, पितुः of their father, नयनजेन born from the eyes, वारिणा with water (tears), किंचित् slightly, उक्षित-शिखण्डकौ whose crests (hair) were sprinkled, उभौ both, धन्विनौ bearing bows, तम् that, ऋषिम् sage, अन्वगच्छताम् followed, पौर-दृष्टि-कृत-मार्ग-तोरणौ the two of them, whose paths were made to be (decorated) with festoons by the looks of the townspeople (the gaze of the townspeople along the path is compared to festoons along the path on which Rama and Lakshmana were going)

Anvaya: पितुः नयनजेन वारिणा किंचित् उक्षितशिखण्डकौ धन्विनौ तौ उभौ पौरदृष्टिकृतमार्गतोरणौ तम् ऋषिम् अन्वगच्छताम्।

Simple meaning: Those two bow-bearing princes, with the crests of their hair slightly moistened by the tears flowing from their father’s eyes, followed that sage, while the road became like an archway adorned by the gazes of the townspeople.

लक्ष्मणानुचरमेव राघवं नेतुमैच्छदृषिरित्यसौ नृपः ।

आशिषं प्रयुयुजे न वाहिनीं सा हि रक्षणविधौ तयोः क्षमा ॥11.6॥

Word-by-word meaning: लक्ष्मण-अनुचरम् with Lakshmana as his follower/attendant, एव only/alone, राघवम् Rama (descendant of Raghu), नेतुम् to take, ऐच्छत् desired, ऋषिः the sage, इति thus/that, असौ that, नृपः the king (Dasharatha), आशिषम् blessings, प्रयुयुजे bestowed/offered, न not, वाहिनीम् an army, सा that (army), हि indeed/for, रक्षण-विधौ in the task of protection, तयोः of those two, क्षमा capable/sufficient

आशिषम् - आशिष्,  षकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, द्वितीयैकवचनम्

Anvaya: ऋषिः लक्ष्मणानुचरम् एव राघवं नेतुम् ऐच्छत्, इति असौ नृपः वाहिनीं न, आशिषं प्रयुयुजे। हि सा (आशिषं) तयोः रक्षणविधौ क्षमा।

Simple meaning: The sage desired to take only Rama accompanied by Lakshmana as his attendant. Thus, that king did not send an army but instead bestowed blessings upon them. The blessings were themselves were indeed capable in the task of protection of those two (Rama and Lakshmana).

मातृवर्गचरणस्पृशौ मुनेस्तौ प्रपद्य पदवीं महौजसः ।

रेज​तुर्गतिवशात्प्रवर्तिनौ भास्करस्य मधुमाधवाविव ॥11.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: मातृ-वर्ग-चरण-स्पृशौ having touched the feet of the group of mothers (the three queens), मुनेः of the sage, तौ those two, प्रपद्य having followed, पदवीम् the path, महा-ओजसः of great vigor/power, रेज​तुः shone, गति-वशात् by the force/compulsion of motion, प्रवर्तिनौ moving forward/proceeding, भास्करस्य of the sun, मधु-माधवौ the months of Madhu and Madhava (Chaitra and Vaishaka - spring months - roughly March-April and April-May), इव like

Anvaya: महौजसः मुनेः पदवीं प्रपद्य मातृवर्गचरणस्पृशौ तौ – भास्करस्य गतिवशात् प्रवर्तिनौ मधुमाधवौ इव – रेज​तुः।

Simple meaning: Following the path of the sage of great power, those two, who had touched the feet of their mothers shone like months Madhu and Madhava following the course of the sun.

वीचिलोलभुजयोस्तयोर्गतं शैशवाच्चपलमप्यशोभत ।

तोयदागमे इवोद्ध्यभिद्ययोर्नामधेयसदृशं विचेष्टितम् ॥11.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: वीचि-लोल-भुजयोः whose arms were moving like waves, तयोः of those two, गतम् gait, शैशवात् due to their childhood, चपलम् restlessness/playfulness, अपि even, अशोभत shone/looked beautiful, तोयद-आगमे in the coming/arrival of monsoon, इव like, उद्ध्य-भिद्ययोः the Uddhya and Bhidya (rivers), नामधेय-सदृशम् similar to their names, विचेष्टितम् activities/movements

Anvaya: वीचिलोलभुजयोः तयोः – तोयदागमे उद्ध्यभिद्ययोः नामधेयसदृशं विचेष्टितम् इव – चपलम् अपि गतं शैशवात् अशोभत।

Simple meaning: The swinging of their arms while walking, even though restless and playful on account of their childhood, looked endearing – just like the workings of the rivers Uddhya and Bhidya befitting their names, in the advent of the monsoon season.

Note : These two rivers, whose location is unknown, are defined as: उज्झति उदकम् इति उद्ध्यः –  one that spurts water it is Uddhya River;  भिनत्ति कूलम् इति भिद्यः while the other that collapses banks with its rush is Bhidya River. These indicate the upshot mood of the boys for an adventure.

तौ बलातिबलयोः प्रभावतो विद्ययोः पथि मुनिप्रदिष्टयोः ।

मम्लतुर्न मणिकुट्टिमोचितौ मातृपार्श्वपरिवर्तिनाविव ॥11.9॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, बल-अतिबलयोः of Bala and Atibala, प्रभावतः by the power, विद्ययोः of the two sciences, पथि on the path (of the forest), मुनिप्रदिष्टयोः taught by the sage, मम्लतुः न did not become weary, मणि-कुट्टिम-उचितौ used to (walking on) jewel inlaid floors, मातृ-पार्श्व-परिवर्तिनौ being at the side of their mother, इव like

Anvaya: मणिकुट्टिमोचितौ तौ मुनिप्रदिष्टयोः बलातिबलयोः विद्ययोः प्रभावतः – मातृपार्श्वपरिवर्तिनौ इव – पथि न मम्लतुः।

Simple meaning: Used to walking on the comfort of palace floors inlaid with jewels, Rama and Lakshmana did not feel tired on the journey, by the power of teachings of Bala and Atibala imparted by the sage. They moved along as easily as children beside their mother.

पूर्ववृत्तकथितैः पुराविदः सानुजः पितृसखस्य राघवः ।

उह्यमान इव वाहनोचितः पादचारमपि न व्यभावयत् ॥11.10॥

Word-by-word meaning: पूर्व-वृत्त-कथितैः by the narrations of past events, पुरा-विदः of the knower of ancient lore, स-अनुजः with his younger brother, पितृ-सखस्य of his father’s friend, राघवः Rama, उह्यमानः being carried, इव as if, वाहन-उचितः fit for riding in a vehicle, पाद-चारम् walking on foot, अपि even, न not, व्यभावयत् regarded or felt

Anvaya: वाहनोचितः सानुजः राघवः पुराविदः पितृसखस्य पूर्ववृत्तकथितैः – उह्यमानः इव – पादचारम् अपि न व्यभावयत्।

Simple meaning: Rama along with his younger brother, accustomed to travelling in a chariot did not feel the fatigue of the journey, being borne as it were by the recitations of the old tales told by the knower of ancient lore and his father’s friend.

तौ सरांसि रसवद्भिरम्बुभिः कूजितैः श्रुतिसुखैः पतत्त्रिणः।

वायवः सुरभिपुष्परेणुभिश्छायया च जलदाः सिषेविरे ॥11.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, सरांसि the lakes, रसवद्भिः with tasty/sweet, अम्बुभिः waters, कूजितैः with cooings/chirpings, श्रुति-सुखैः pleasing to the ear, पतत्त्रिणः the birds, वायवः the winds, सुरभि-पुष्प-रेणुभिः with fragrant flower-pollen, छायया with shade, च and, जल-दाः the clouds, सिषेविरे served/attended to

Anvaya: सरांसि रसवद्भिः अम्बुभिः, पतत्त्रिणः श्रुतिसुखैः कूजितैः, वायवः सुरभिपुष्परेणुभिः, जलदाः छायया च तौ सिषेविरे।

Simple meaning: The lakes with sweet waters, the birds with ear-pleasing chirpings, the winds with fragrant flower-pollen, and the clouds with shade served/attended to those two.

नाम्भसां कमलशोभिनां तथा शाखिनां च न परिश्रमच्छिदाम् ।

दर्शनेन लघुना यथा तयोः प्रीतिमापुरुभयोस्तपस्विनः ॥11.12॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, अम्भसाम् of the waters, कमल-शोभिनाम् adorned with lotuses, तथा similarly, शाखिनाम् of the birds, च and, न not, परिश्रम-छिदाम् removing fatigue, यथा as much as, तयोः of those two, दर्शनेन by the sight, लघुना brief/momentary, उभयोः of both (Rama and Lakshmana), तपस्विनः the ascetics, प्रीतिम् joy/delight, आपुः obtained/attained

Anvaya: तपस्विनः लघुना उभयोः तयोः दर्शनेन यथा प्रीतिम् आपुः – तथा कमलशोभिनाम् अम्भसां, परिश्रमच्छिदां शाखिनां च न।

Simple meaning: The ascetics obtained not as much joy from the waters adorned with lotuses or from the birds that remove fatigue, as much joy obtained from a brief sight of those two.

स्थाणुदग्धवपुषस्तपोवनं प्राप्य दाशरथिरात्तकार्मुकः ।

विग्रहेण मदनस्य चारुणा सोऽभवत्प्रतिनिधिर्न कर्मणा ॥11.13॥

Word-by-word meaning: स्थाणु-दग्ध-वपुषः whose body was burnt by Shiva (Sthanu), तपः-वनम् the forest of penance/ascetics, प्राप्य having reached, दाशरथिः son of Dasharatha (Rama), आत्त-कार्मुकः having taken/holding the bow, विग्रहेण by form/appearance, मदनस्य of Kamadeva (god of love), चारुणा beautiful, सः he, अभवत् became, प्रतिनिधिः substitute/representative, न not, कर्मणा by action/function

Anvaya: सः आत्तकार्मुकः दाशरथिः स्थाणुदग्धवपुषः मदनस्य तपोवनं प्राप्य चारुणा विग्रहेण प्रतिनिधिः अभवत्, कर्मणा न।

Simple meaning: Having reached the forest of Kamadeva whose body was burnt by Shiva, the son of Dasharatha, holding his bow, became a representative for Kamadeva by his (Kamadeva’s) beautiful form, not by his function.

तौ सुकेतुसुतया खिलीकृते कौशिकाद्विदितशापया पथि ।

निन्यतुः स्थलनिवेशिताटनी लीलयैव धनुषी अधिज्यताम् ॥11.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, सुकेतु-सुतया by the daughter of Suketu, खिली-कृते made barren/desolate, कौशिकात् from Kaushika (Vishwamitra), विदित-शापया whose curse was known, पथि on the path, निन्यतुः passed/spent, स्थल-निवेशित-अटनी the end of the bow placed on the ground, लीलया with ease/playfully, एव indeed, धनुषी the bows, अधिज्यताम् being strung

अटनी   अटनि, इस्, f. or अटनी, f. the notched extremity of a bow. Src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: कौशिकात् विदितशापया सुकेतुसुतया खिलीकृते पथि, तौ स्थलनिवेशिताटनी लीलया एव धनुषी अधिज्यतां निन्यतुः।

Simple meaning: On the path made desolate by the daughter of Suketu (Tataka), whose curse was known from Kaushika, those two, placed the bow on the ground and strung them with ease.

ज्यानिनादमथ गृह्णती तयोः प्रादुरास बहुलक्षपाछविः ।

Anvaya: दाशरथिः तत् प्रसुप्तभुजगेन्द्रभीषणं धनुः वीक्ष्य आददे, येन वृषध्वजः विद्रुतक्रतुमृगानुसारिणं बाणम् असृजत्।

Simple meaning: Rama, son of Dasharatha, beheld that bow—fearsome like a slumbering serpent-king—and took it up. It was the very bow with which Lord Shiva, who has a bull on his banner, had once shot an arrow pursuing the sacrifice which had taken the form of a deer.

Note: The backstory of Shiva pursuing a sacrifice that had taken the form of a deer is primarily rooted in the story of Daksha’s Yajna (sacrifice), where Shiva destroys the sacrifice. The Yagna Purusha transforms himself into a deer to escape. This is the same Yajna to which Shiva was not invited and Sati immolates herself.

आततज्यमकरोत्स संसदा विस्मयस्तिमितनेत्रमीक्षितः ।

शैलसारमपि नातियत्नतः पुष्पचापमिव पेशलं स्मरः ॥11.45॥

Word-by-word meaning: आतत-ज्यम् strung (with the bowstring drawn tight), अकरोत् made, सः he, संसदा by the assembly, विस्मय-स्तिमित-नेत्रम् with eyes motionless in astonishment, ईक्षितः being watched, शैल-सारम् the essence of a mountain (mountain-solid bow), अपि even, न अति-यत्नतः without excessive effort, पुष्प-चापम् इव like the bow of flowers, पेशलम् delicate, स्मरः Manmatha

Anvaya: संसदा विस्मयस्तिमितनेत्रं ईक्षितः, सः शैलसारम् अपि न अतियत्नतः – स्मरः पेशलं पुष्पचापम् इव – आततज्यम् अकरोत्।

Simple meaning: Watched by the assembly whose eyes were fixed in astonishment, he strung the bow, even though it was mighty, without great effort, as Manmatha strings his bow of flowers.

भज्यमानमतिमात्रकर्षणात्तेन वज्रपरुषस्वनं धनुः ।

भार्गवाय दृढमन्यवे पुनः क्षत्रमुद्यतमिव न्यवेदयत् ॥11.46॥

Word-by-word meaning: भज्यमानम् being broken, अति-मात्र-कर्षणात् by excessive pulling, तेन by him, वज्र-परुष-स्वनम् having a sound harsh like thunder, धनुः the bow, भार्गवाय to the descendant of Bhrigu (Parashurama), दृढ-मन्यवे of firm anger, पुनः again, क्षत्रम् the Kshatriya race, उद्यतम् is rising, इव as if, न्यवेदयत् announced/proclaimed

Anvaya: तेन अतिमात्रकर्षणात् भज्यमानं वज्रपरुषस्वनं धनुः – दृढमन्यवे भार्गवाय क्षत्रं पुनः उद्यतम् इव – न्यवेदयत्।

Simple meaning: As he pulled it with great force, the bow broke with a sound as harsh as thunder. That terrible noise, as it were, announced to Parashurama, the fiercely wrathful descendant of Bhrigu, the rise of the Kshatriyas.

दृष्टसारमथ रुद्रकार्मुके वीर्यशुल्कमभिनन्द्य मैथिलः ।

राघवाय तनयामयोनिजां रूपिणीं श्रियमिव न्यवेदयत् ॥11.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: दृष्ट-सारम् having seen the true strength, अथ then, रुद्र-कार्मुके on the bow of Rudra (Shiva), वीर्य-शुल्कम् the bride-price of valor, अभिनन्द्य having gladly accepted, मैथिलः the king of Mithila (Janaka), राघवाय to Raghava (Rama), तनयाम् daughter, अ-योनिजाम् not born of a womb (Sita), रूपिणीम् embodied, श्रियम् Lakshmi (goddess of fortune), इव like, न्यवेदयत् offered/presented

Anvaya: अथ मैथिलः रुद्रकार्मुके दृष्टसारं वीर्यशुल्कम् अभिनन्द्य अयोनिजां तनयां रूपिणीं – श्रियम् इव – राघवाय न्यवेदयत्।

Simple meaning: Then King Janaka of Mithila, having witnessed Rama’s true strength in the bow of Shiva and joyfully accepting valor as the bride-price, offered his beautiful daughter Sita—born not of a womb—to Rama, as if it were Goddess Lakshmi herself.

मैथिलः सपदि सत्यसंगरो राघवाय तनयामयोनिजाम् ।

संनिधौ द्युतिमतस्तपोनिधेरग्निसाक्षिक इवातिसृष्टवान् ॥11.48॥

Word-by-word meaning: मैथिलः the king of Mithila (Janaka), सपदि immediately, सत्य-संगरः true to his vow, राघवाय to Raghava (Rama), तनयाम् daughter, अ-योनिजाम् not born of a womb (Sita), संनिधौ in the presence, द्युतिमतः radiant, तपोनिधेः of the one who treasure was austerity (the sage Vishwamitra), अग्नि-साक्षिकः with fire as witness, इव as if, अतिसृष्टवान् gave away formally bestowed

Anvaya: सत्यसंगरः मैथिलः सपदि – अग्निसाक्षिकः इव – द्युतिमतः तपोनिधेः संनिधौ अयोनिजां तनयां राघवाय अतिसृष्टवान्।

Simple meaning: True to his vow, King Janaka of Mithila immediately gave his daughter Sita to Rama, in the presence of the radiant sage Vishwamitra, as if it were in the presence of the sacred fire itself.

प्राहिणोच्च महितं महाद्युतिः कोसलाधिपतये पुरोधसम् ।

भृत्यभावि दुहितुः परिग्रहाद्दिश्यतां कुलमिदं निमेरिति ॥11.49॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्राहिणोत् sent, च and, महितम् honoured distinguished, महाद्युतिः the greatly resplendent one (Janaka), कोसल-अधिपतये to the lord of Kosala (Dasharatha), पुरोधसम् the chief priest emissary, भृत्य-भावि about to become a servant (i.e. seeking alliance in humility), दुहितुः of the daughter, परिग्रहात् regarding the marriage acceptance, दिश्यताम् may be granted bestowed, कुलम् this family lineage, इदम् this, निमेः of Nimi (Janaka’s ancestor), इति thus

Anvaya: महाद्युतिः (मैथिलः) महितं पुरोधसं कोसलाधिपतये प्राहिणोत् च — “निमेः इदं कुलं दुहितुः परिग्रहात् भृत्यभावि दिश्यताम्” इति ।

Simple meaning: The illustrious King Janaka sent his honoured priest to the lord of Kosala, King Dasharatha, with this message: “Be pleased to grant to this family of Nimi the condition of a servant to your dynasty by the acceptance of my daughter in marriage.”

अन्वियेष सदृशीं स च स्नुषां प्राप चैनमनुकूलवाग्द्विजः ।

सद्य एव सुकृतां हि पच्यते कल्पवृक्षफलधर्मि काङ्क्षितम् ॥11.50॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्वियेष was searching for, सदृशीम् suitable worthy, सः he (Dasharatha), च and, स्नुषाम् daughter-in-law, प्राप obtained, च and, एनम् this (message/proposal), अनुकूल-वाक् one who spoke favourably, द्विजः the Brahmin (priest), सद्यः immediately, एव indeed, सुकृताम् of the virtuous, हि for, पच्यते ripens, कल्प-वृक्ष-फल-धर्मि like the fruit of the wish-fulfilling tree, काङ्क्षितम् the desired object

अन्वियेष - अनु_इष्{कर्तरि;लिट्;प्र;एक;परस्मैपदी;तुदादिः}

Anvaya: सः (कोसलाधिपतिः) सदृशीं स्नुषाम् अन्वियेष, च अनुकूलवाक् द्विजः एनं प्राप । हि कल्पवृक्षफलधर्मि सुकृतां काङ्क्षितं सद्यः एव पच्यते ।

Simple meaning: King Dasharatha had been seeking a worthy bride for his son, and the Brahmin envoy, speaking favourably, reached Dasharatha. For the desires of the virtuous, ripen immediately, like the fruit of the wish-fulfilling tree.

तस्य कल्पितपुरस्क्रियाविधेः शुश्रुवान्वचनमग्रजन्मनः ।

उच्चचाल बलभित्सखो वशी सैन्यरेणुमुषितार्कदीधितिः ॥11.51॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य of him, कल्पित-पुरस्क्रिया-विधेः of the one who had been duly honored, शुश्रुवान् having heard, वचनम् the words, अग्र-जन्मनः of the high-born Brahmin (the priest), उच्चचाल set out rose to depart, बलभित्-सखः the friend of Indra (i.e. Dasharatha), वशी self-controlled mighty ruler, सैन्य-रेणु-मुषित-अर्क-दीधितिः whose army’s dust obscured the sun’s rays

Anvaya: बलभित्सखः वशी (दशरथः), तस्य कल्पितपुरस्क्रियाविधेः अग्रजन्मनः वचनं शुश्रुवान्, सैन्यरेणुमुषितार्कदीधितिः (सन्) उच्चचाल ।

Simple meaning: When the mighty and self-controlled King Dasharatha—friend of Indra—heard the words of the noble Brahmin, honoured him duly, and he set out. As he marched, the dust raised by his army dimmed the rays of the sun.

आससाद मिथिलां स वेष्टयन्पीडितोपवनपादपां बलैः ।

प्रीतिरोधमसहिष्ट सा पुरी स्त्रीव कान्तपरिभोगमायतम् ॥11.52॥

Word-by-word meaning: आससाद reached approached, मिथिलाम् Mithila, सः he (Dasharatha), वेष्टयन् bruising, पीडित-उपवन-पादपाम् whose garden trees were pressed/disturbed, बलैः by the forces (army), प्रीति-रोधम् impedance of love, असहिष्ट could not endure, सा that, पुरी city, स्त्री इव like a woman, कान्त-परिभोगम् the embrace enjoyment of her beloved, आयतम् come near

Anvaya: सः बलैः पीडितोपवनपादपां मिथिलां वेष्टयन् आससाद । सा पुरी – स्त्री आयतं कान्तपरिभोगं इव – प्रीतिरोधम् असहिष्ट।

Simple meaning: King Dasharatha arrived at Mithila, whose forces disturbed and bruised the trees of the pleasure-gardens. And that city bore the onslaught like a woman who bears the love of her beloved.

तौ समेत्य समये स्थितावुभौ भूपती वरुणवासवोपमौ ।

कन्यकातनयकौतुकक्रियां स्वप्रभावसदृशीं वितेनतुः ॥11.53॥

Word-by-word meaning: तौ those two, समेत्य having come together, समये स्थितौ well-versed in the knowledge of rituals, उभौ both, भूपती the two kings, वरुण-वासव-उपमौ comparable to Varuna and Indra, कन्यका-तनय-कौतुक-क्रियाम् the festive rites of the maiden and the prince (i.e. the wedding ceremony), स्व-प्रभाव-सदृशीम् befitting their own majesty, वितेनतुः performed carried out

Anvaya: समये स्थितौ, वरुणवासवोपमौ तौ उभौ भूपती समेत्य स्वप्रभावसदृशीं कन्यकातनयकौतुकक्रियां वितेनतुः।

Simple meaning: The two kings, comparable to Varuna and Indra, well-versed in the knowledge of rituals, performed the wedding festivities of the princess and the prince in a manner befitting their own splendour and greatness.

पार्थिवीमुदवहद्रघूद्वहो लक्ष्मणस्तदनुजामथोर्मिलाम् ।

यौ तयोरवरजौ वरौजसौ तौ कुशध्वजसुते सुमध्यमे ॥11.54॥

Word-by-word meaning: पार्थिवीम् daughter of the earth, उदवहत् married, रघू-उद्वहः the scion of the lineage of Raghu (Rama), लक्ष्मणः Lakshmana, तत्-अनुजाम् younger sister, अथ then, उर्मिलाम् joined together, यौ the two, तयोः of them, अवरजौ the two younger brothers, वर-ओजसौ full of heroic strength, तौ those two, कुशध्वज-सुते two daughters of Kushadhwaja, सुमध्यमे having slender waists

Anvaya: रघूद्वहः पार्थिवीम् उदवहत्, अथ लक्ष्मणः तदनुजाम् उर्मिलाम् (उदवहत्); वरौजसौ तौ अवरजौ सुमध्यमे कुशध्वजसुते (उद्वहताम्)।

Simple meaning: The scion of the Raghu’s lineage Rama married the daughter of the Earth, Sita. Lakshmana married her younger sister Urmila. The other younger two brothers married King Kushadhwaja’s daughters.

ते चतुर्थसहितास्त्रयो बभुः सूनवो नववधूपरिग्रहाः ।

सामदानविधिभेदनिग्रहाः सिद्धिमन्त इव तस्य भूपतेः ॥11.55॥

Word-by-word meaning: ते those, चतुर्थ-सहिताः along with the fourth, त्रयः three, बभुः became, सूनवः sons, नव-वधू-परिग्रहाः married to new brides, साम-दान-विधि-भेद-निग्रहाः sama (conciliation), dana-vidhi (strategy of gift), bheda (division), nigraha (force/punishment), सिद्धिमन्तः endowed with fruits of success, इव like, तस्य his, भू-पतेः of the king

Anvaya: ते चतुर्थसहिताः त्रयः सूनवः नववधूपरिग्रहाः सिद्धिमन्तः तस्य भूपतेः सामदानविधिभेदनिग्रहाः इव बभुः।

Simple meaning: Those three sons along with the fourth, married to new brides, became like the successful (methods of statecraft) - sama, dana, bheda, and nigraha , along with the fruits of success, of that king.

Note: The four sons are compared to the four expedients and their wives are compared to the siddhis of the expedients.

ता नराधिपसुता नृपात्मजैस्ते च ताभिरगमन्कृतार्थताम् ।

सोऽभवद्वरवधूसमागमः प्रत्ययप्रकृतियोगसंनिभः ॥11.56॥

Word-by-word meaning: ताः those (princesses), नर-अधिप-सुताः daughters of the kings, नृप-आत्मजैः by the princes, ते and they (the princes), च and, ताभिः with them, अगमन् attained, कृत-अर्थताम् fulfilment, सः that, अभवत् became, वर-वधू-समागमः the union of bridegroom and brides, प्रत्यय-प्रकृति-योग-सन्निभः resembling the conjunction of cause and effect (or subject and predicate in mutual relation)

Anvaya: ताः नराधिपसुताः नृपात्मजैः (सह) कृतार्थताम् अगमन्, ते च ताभिः (सह कृतार्थताम् अगमन्)। सः वरवधूसमागमः प्रत्ययप्रकृतियोगसंनिभः अभवत्।

Simple meaning: Those princesses attained fulfilment with the princes, and the princes likewise attained fulfilment with them. That union of bridegrooms and brides appeared like the harmonious conjunction of complementary principles, perfectly matched and complete.

एवमात्तरतिरात्मसंभवांस्तान्निवेश्य चतुरोऽपि तत्र सः ।

अध्वसु त्रिषु विसृष्टमैथिलः स्वां पुरीं दशरथो न्यवर्तत ॥11.57॥

Word-by-word meaning: एवम् thus, आत्त-रतिः having experienced delight, आत्म-सम्भवान् his own sons, तान् them, निवेश्य having established (settled), चतुरः अपि all four, तत्र there, सः he, अध्वसु त्रिषु after three days’ journey, विसृष्ट-मैथिलः having taken leave of the king of Mithila, स्वाम् his own, पुरीम् city, दशरथः Dasharatha, न्यवर्तत returned

Anvaya: एवम् आत्तरतिः सः दशरथः, तान् चतुरः अपि आत्मसम्भवान् तत्र निवेश्य, त्रिषु अध्वसु मैथिलविसृष्टः (सन्) स्वां पुरीं न्यवर्तत।

Simple meaning: Thus the delighted King Dasharatha, having married his four sons there (at Mithila), took leave of the king of Mithila who had accompanied him for his return journey for three days, and he returned to his own city.

तस्य जातु मरुतः प्रतीपगा वर्त्मसु ध्वजतरुप्रमाथिनः ।

चिक्लिशुर्भृशतया वरूथिनीमुत्तटा इव नदीरयाः स्थलीम् ॥11.58॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्य of him (Dasharatha), जातु once/at a certain time, मरुतः winds, प्रतीपगाः blowing contrary (from the front), वर्त्मसु on the roads, ध्वज-तरु-प्रमाथिनः shaking banners and trees violently, चिक्लिशुः afflicted, भृशतया intensely, वरूथिनीम् the army (retinue), उत्तटाः having risen banks, इव like, नदी-रयाः streams of rivers, स्थलीम् the land

प्रतीपग  प्रतीपग वि. प्रतीप-ग - विरुद्ध चलने वाला src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: जातु वर्त्मसु प्रतीपगाः ध्वजतरुप्रमाथिनः मरुतः – उत्तटाः नदीरयाः स्थलीम् इव – तस्य वरूथिनीं भृशतया चिक्लिशुः।

Simple meaning: On the way, at a certain time, adverse winds blew fiercely along the roads, violently shaking banners and trees. They sorely troubled his army, just as rivers in spate, with their banks overflowing, ravage the land.

लक्ष्यते स्म तदनन्तरं रविर्बद्धभीमपरिवेषमण्डलः ।

वैनतेयशमितस्य भोगिनो भोगवेष्टित इव च्युतो मणिः ॥11.59॥

Word-by-word meaning: लक्ष्यते स्म was seen, तदनन्तरम् immediately after that, रविः the sun, बद्ध-भीम-परिवेष-मण्डलः having a terrible halo around him, वैनतेय-शमितस्य subdued by Vainateya (Garuda), भोगिनः of the serpent, भोग-वेष्टितः encircled by coils, इव like, च्युतः fallen, मणिः jewel

Anvaya: तदनन्तरं बद्धभीमपरिवेषमण्डलः रविः – वैनतेयशमितस्य भोगिनः भोगवेष्टितः च्युतः मणिः इव – लक्ष्यते स्म।

Simple meaning: Immediately after that, the sun was seen surrounded by a dreadful halo, like a jewel fallen from a serpent’s hood that had been subdued by Garuda, still encircled by its coils.

श्येनपक्षपरिधूसरालकाः सांध्यमेघरुधिरार्द्रवाससः ।

अङ्गना इव रजस्वला दिशो नो बभूवुरवलोकनक्षमाः ॥11.60॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्येन-पक्ष-परिधूसर-अलकाः having tresses greyed like the wings of a hawk, सांध्य-मेघ-रुधिर-आर्द्र-वाससः with garments reddened like the blood-hued evening clouds, अङ्गनाः women, इव like, रजस्वलाः menstruating women, दिशः the quarters (directions), न not, बभूवुः became, अवलोकन-क्षमाः fit to be looked at

Anvaya: श्येनपक्षपरिधूसरालकाः सांध्यमेघरुधिरार्द्रवाससः दिशः – रजस्वला अङ्गना इव – अवलोकनक्षमाः न बभूवुः।

Simple meaning: The quarters of the sky, with the hawks flying and with the clouds having a reddish tinge due to the dust that was rising, looked like women in their menstrual state with locks grey like a hawk’s wings and garments reddened. The directions became unfit to behold.

भास्करश्च दिशमध्युवास यां तां श्रिताः प्रतिभयं ववासिरे ।

क्षत्रशोणितपितृक्रियोचितं चोदयन्त्य इव भार्गवं शिवाः ॥11.61॥

Word-by-word meaning: भास्करः the sun, च and, दिशम् directions, अध्युवास had risen, याम् which, ताम् that, श्रिताः situated, प्रतिभयम् fearsome, ववासिरे cried out, क्षत्र-शोणित-पितृ-क्रिया-उचितम् accustomed to worship the manes of his father with the blood of the Kshatriyas, चोदयन्त्य instigating, इव as if, भार्गवम् (Bhargava - descendent of Bhrigu Rishi) Parashurama, शिवाः female jackals (in this case)

Anvaya: भास्करः च यां दिशम् अध्युवास तां श्रिताः शिवाः –  क्षत्रशोणितपितृक्रियोचितं भार्गवं चोदयन्त्यः इव – प्रतिभयं ववासिरे।

Simple meaning: The female jackals resorting to that quarter which was occupied by the Sun set up a terrible howl inciting, as it were, Bhargava (Parashurama) who was accustomed to make offerings to his ancestors by means of the blood of the Kshatriyas.

तत्प्रतीपपवनादि वैकृतं प्रेक्ष्य शान्तिमधिकृत्य कृत्यवित् ।

अन्वयुङ्क्त गुरुमीश्वरः क्षितेः स्वन्तमित्यलघयत्स तद्व्यथाम् ॥11.62॥

Word-by-word meaning: तत् that, प्रतीप-पवन-आदि contrary wind and other (inauspicious omens), वैकृतम् disturbances/portents, प्रेक्ष्य having seen, शान्तिम् propitiatory rites, अधिकृत्य having undertaken/performed, कृत्यवित् knower of duties, अन्वयुङ्क्त engaged, गुरुम् the preceptor, ईश्वरः lord, क्षितेः of the earth (Dasharatha), सु-अन्तम् auspicious end, इति thus, अलघयत् made light/diminished, सः he, तत् that, व्यथाम् pain/distress

Anvaya: तत् प्रतीपपवनादि वैकृतम् प्रेक्ष्य कृत्यवित् क्षितेः ईश्वरः शान्तिम् अधिकृत्य गुरुम् अन्वयुङ्क्त। सः स्वन्तम् इति तत् व्यथाम् अलघयत्।

Simple meaning: Having seen those disturbances like contrary winds and other portents,  the knower of duties, the lord of the earth, engaged the preceptor to perform the propitiatory rites. The preceptor diminished his worries by saying that this will lead to an auspicious end.

तेजसः सपदि राशिरुत्थितः प्रादुरास किल वाहिनीमुखे ।

यः प्रमृज्य नयनानि सैनिकैर्लक्षणीयपुरुषाकृतिश्चिरात् ॥11.63॥

Word-by-word meaning: तेजसः of radiance/brilliance, सपदि suddenly, राशिः mass/heap, उत्थितः risen, प्रादुरास appeared, किल indeed, वाहिनी-मुखे at the front of the army, यः who, प्रमृज्य having wiped/rubbed, नयनानि eyes, सैनिकैः by the soldiers, लक्षणीय-पुरुष-आकृतिः having the form of a distinguished/marked person, चिरात् after a long time

Anvaya: सपदि उत्थितः तेजसः राशिः वाहिनीमुखे प्रादुरास किल, यः चिरात् सैनिकैः नयनानि प्रमृज्य लक्षणीयपुरुषाकृतिः (अभूत्)।

Simple meaning: Suddenly a mass of radiance arose and appeared at the front of the army indeed. The soldiers, blinded by the radiance, rubbed their eyes and were able to see the form of a distinguished person, after a long time.

पित्र्यमंशमुपवीतलक्षणं मातृकं च धनुरूर्जितं दधत् ।

यः ससोम इव घर्मदीधितिः सद्विजिह्व इव चन्दनद्रुमः ॥ 11.64॥

Word-by-word meaning: पित्र्यम् अंशम् paternal lineage, उपवीत-लक्षणम् marked by the sacred thread, मातृकम् maternal/from mother, च and, धनुः-ऊर्जितम् powerful/mighty bow, दधत् bearing/holding, यः who, स-सोमः with the moon, इव like, घर्म-दीधितिः the one with hot-rays (sun), स-द्विजिह्वः with a two-tongued (fork tongued snake), इव like, चन्दन-द्रुमः sandalwood tree

Anvaya: उपवीतलक्षणं पित्र्यम् अंशं, धनुरूर्जितं मातृकं च दधत् यः – ससोमः घर्मदीधितिः इव, चन्दनद्रुमः सद्विजिह्वः इव (स्तिथः)।

Simple meaning: Wearing sacred thread as his paternal mark, for his father was a brahmana; also wielding a mighty bow as maternal mark, for his mother was a lady from kshatriya dynasty, he who looked like the hot-rayed sun in company of cool-rayed moon, or like the sandalwood tree with a serpent, appeared before them.

येन रोषपरुषात्मनः पितुः शासने स्थितिभिदोऽपि तस्थुषा ।

वेपमानजननीशिरश्छिदा प्रागजीयत घृणा ततो मही ॥11.65॥

Word-by-word meaning: येन by whom, रोष-परुष-आत्मनः of a father who was overcome with rage, पितुः of the father, शासने in command, स्थिति-भिदः one who broke the limit of propriety of conduct, अपि yet, तस्थुषा by the one who remained firm, वेपमान-जननी-शिरः-छिदा by the one who had cut the head of his trembling mother, प्राक् previously, अजीयत had won, घृणा with detestation, ततः thence, मही earth

Anvaya: रोषपरुषात्मनः स्थितिभिदः अपि पितुः शासने तस्थुषा वेपमानजननीशिरश्छिदा येन प्राक् घृणा अजीयत, ततः मही अजीयत।

Simple meaning: On the orders of his father, who was overcome with rage and who had broken all limits of propriety, the one who remained steadfast and cut off the head of his trembling mother, first conquered the disdain of the world and then the world itself.

अक्षबीजवलयेन निर्बभौ दक्षिणश्रवणसंस्थितेन यः ।

क्षत्रियान्तकरणैकविंशतेर्व्याजपूर्वगणनामिवोद्वहन् ॥11.66॥

Word-by-word meaning: अक्ष-बीज-वलयेन with earring of rudraksha beads, निर्बभौ he shone, दक्षिण-श्रवण-संस्थितेन on the right ear, यः who, क्षत्रिय-अन्तकरण-एकविंशतेः of the twenty-one exterminations of the Kshatriyas, व्याज-पूर्व-गणनाम् on the pretext of keeping a count of, इव as if, उद्वहन् bearing/carrying

Anvaya: यः दक्षिणश्रवणसंस्थितेन अक्षबीजवलयेन – क्षत्रियान्तकरणैकविंशतेः व्याजपूर्वगणनाम् उद्वहन् इव – निर्बभौ।

Simple meaning: He shone with an earring of rudraksha beads in his right ear, as though it were keeping count of the twenty-one exterminations of the Kshatriyas.

तं पितुर्वधभवेन मन्युना राजवंशनिधनाय दीक्षितम् ।

बालसूनुरवलोक्य भार्गवं स्वां दशां च विषसाद पार्थिवः ॥11.67॥

Word-by-word meaning: तम् him, पितुः of father, वध-भवेन born from the killing, मन्युना with anger, राज-वंश-निधनाय for the destruction of the kshatriya lineage, दीक्षितम् consecrated/determined, बाल-सूनुः one who had young children (Dasharatha), अवलोक्य having seen, भार्गवम् Bhargava (Parashurama), स्वाम् his own, दशाम् condition/state, च and, विषसाद became dejected/sad, पार्थिवः the king

Anvaya: बालसूनुः पार्थिवः, पितुः वधभवेन मन्युना राजवंशनिधनाय दीक्षितं तं भार्गवं, स्वां दशां च अवलोक्य विषसाद।

Simple meaning: The king, who had young children, seeing Bhargava who had been determined for the destruction of the kshatriya lineage with anger born from his father's killing, and seeing his own condition, became dejected.

नाम राम इति तुल्यमात्मजे वर्तमानमहिते च दारुणे ।

हृद्यमस्य भयदायि चाभवद्रत्नजातमिव हारसर्पयोः ॥11.68॥

Word-by-word meaning: नाम name, रामः Rama, इति thus, तुल्यम् same/equal, आत्मजे in his son, वर्तमानम् existing/present, अहिते in the enemy, च and, दारुणे in the terrible one, हृद्यम् dear to the heart, अस्य of him, भय-दायि fear-giving, च and, अभवत् became, रत्न-जातम् a collection of gems, इव like, हार-सर्पयोः in a necklace and a serpent

हृद्यम् - हृदयस्य प्रियम्

Anvaya: आत्मजे, दारुणे अहिते च, तुल्यं वर्तमानं रामः इति नाम – हारसर्पयोः रत्नजातम् इव – अस्य हृद्यं भयदायि च अभवत्।

Simple meaning: The same name Rama, existing in his son and in the terrible enemy, became both dear to his heart and fear-giving to him - like the gem in a necklace and the gem on the hood of the serpent.

अर्घ्यमर्घ्यमिति वादिनं नृपं सोऽनवेक्ष्य भरताग्रजो यतः ।

क्षत्त्रकोपदहनार्चिषं ततः संदधे दृशमुदग्रतारकाम् ॥11.69॥

Word-by-word meaning: अर्घ्यम् अर्घ्यम् "respectful offering" "respectful offering", इति thus, वादिनम् speaking, नृपम् the king, सः he (Parashurama), अनवेक्ष्य not heeding/ignoring, भरत-अग्रजः the elder brother of Bharata (Rama), यतः from where, क्षत्त्र-कोप-दहन-अर्चिषम् a flame burning with the anger against the Kshatriyas, ततः then, संदधे fixed, दृशम् gaze, उदग्र-तारकाम् with upraised pupils

Anvaya: सः "अर्घ्यम् अर्घ्यम्" इति वादिनं नृपम् अनवेक्ष्य यतः भरताग्रजः ततः क्षत्त्रकोपदहनार्चिषम् उदग्रतारकां दृशं संदधे।

Simple meaning: Parashurama, not heeding the king who was saying "respectful offering, respectful offering", directed his gaze with upraised pupils – which looked like a flame burning with anger against the Kshatriyas – where the elder brother of Bharata was.

तेन कार्मुकनिषक्तमुष्टिना राघवो विगतभीः पुरोगतः ।

अङ्गुलीविवरचारिणं शरं कुर्वता निजगदे युयुत्सुना ॥11.70॥

Word-by-word meaning: तेन by him, कार्मुक-निषक्त-मुष्टिना with fist fixed on the bow, राघवः Rama, विगत-भीः fearless, पुरः-गतः standing in front, अङ्गुली-विवर-चारिणम् moving through the spaces of the fingers, शरम् arrow, कुर्वता making/placing, निजगदे spoke, युयुत्सुना desirous of fighting

Anvaya: कार्मुकनिषक्तमुष्टिना शरम् अङ्गुलीविवरचारिणं कुर्वता, युयुत्सुना, तेन विगतभीः (सन्) पुरोगतः राघवः निजगदे।

Simple meaning: By him (Parashurama) who had placed an arrow moving through the spaces of his fingers with his fist fixed on the bow, who was desirous of fighting, Rama was addressed to, who was standing in the front fearlessly. (Parashurama addressed Rama).

क्षत्त्रजातमपकारवैरि मे तन्निहत्य बहुशः शमं गतः ।

सुप्तसर्प इव दण्डघट्टनाद्रोषितोऽस्मि तव विक्रमश्रवात् ॥11.71॥

Word-by-word meaning: क्षत्त्र-जातम् the Kshatriya race, अपकार-वैरि hostile due to harm, मे to me, तत् that, निहत्य having killed, बहुशः many times, शमम् peace, गतः gone/attained, सुप्त-सर्पः a sleeping serpent, इव like, दण्ड-घट्टनात् from the striking of a stick, रोषितः angered, अस्मि I am, तव your, विक्रम-श्रवात् from hearing of valor

Anvaya: क्षत्त्रजातम् अपकारवैरि (आसीत्)। तत् बहुशः निहत्य शमं गतः (अस्मि)। सुप्तसर्पः दण्डघट्टनात् इव तव विक्रमश्रवात् रोषितः अस्मि।

Simple meaning: The Kshatriya race is my enemy because of the injustice that they did to me. That’s why by killing them many times, I had attained peace. But as a sleeping serpent is angered if hit by a stick, so am I angered by hearing of your valor.

Note: The Kshatriyas had killed Parashurama’s father.

मैथिलस्य धनुरन्यपार्थिवैस्त्वं किलानमितपूर्वमक्षणोः ।

तन्निशम्य भवता समर्थये वीर्यश‍ृङ्गमिव भग्नमात्मनः ॥11.72॥

Word-by-word meaning: मैथिलस्य of the king of Mithila (Janaka), धनुः bow, अन्य-पार्थिवैः by other kings, त्वम् you, किल indeed, अ-नमित-पूर्वम् unbent before, अक्षणोः bent, तत् that, निशम्य having heard, भवता by you, समर्थये I consider, वीर्य-शृङ्गम् the horn of valor, इव like, भग्नम् broken, आत्मनः of myself

नमित   नमित a. Bowed, bent down. Src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi

अक्षणोः - क्षण् , कर्तरि लङ्लकारः (परस्मैपदम्) , मध्यमपुरुषः , एकवचनम् src. Dhatupatha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: अन्यपार्थिवैः अनमितपूर्वं मैथिलस्य धनुः त्वम् अक्षणोः किल। तत् निशम्य भवता आत्मनः वीर्यशृङ्गं भग्नम् इव समर्थये।

Simple meaning: You indeed bent the bow of the king of Mithila that was unbent before by other kings. Having heard that, I consider you as the one who has broken as it were the supremacy (horn) of my valor.

अन्यदा जगति राम इत्ययं शब्द उच्चरित एव मामगात् ।

व्रीडमावहति मे स संप्रति व्यस्तवृत्तिरुदयोन्मुखे त्वयि ॥11.73॥

Word-by-word meaning: अन्यदा earlier/at other times, जगति in the world, रामः Rama, इति thus, अयम् this, शब्दः word, उच्चरितः uttered, एव alone, माम् me, अगात् reached/came to, व्रीडम् shame, आवहति brings, मे to me, सः that, संप्रति now, व्यस्त-वृत्तिः having reversed/opposite function, उदय-उन्मुखे when facing rise/ascent, त्वयि in you

Anvaya: अन्यदा जगति "रामः" इति अयं शब्दः उच्चरितः माम् एव अगात्। संप्रति त्वयि उदयोन्मुखे व्यस्तवृत्तिः सः मे व्रीडम् आवहति।

Simple meaning: Previously when the word Rama was uttered in this world, it was applicable to me alone. Now that epithet produces a feeling of shame in me since its meaning has been reversed on account of your rising into prominence.

बिभ्रतोऽस्त्रमचलेऽप्यकुण्ठितं द्वौ रुपू मम मतौ समागसौ ।

धेनुवत्सहरणाच्च हैहयस्त्वं च कीर्तिमपहर्तुमुद्यतः ॥11.74॥

Word-by-word meaning: बिभ्रतः of one bearing, अस्त्रम् weapon, अचले in the unshakable mountain, अपि even, अ-कुण्ठितम् unblunted/undiminished, द्वौ two, रिपू enemies, मम my, मतौ in opinion, सम-आगसौ equal in offense, धेनु-वत्स-हरणात् from stealing the cow’s calf, च and, हैहयः Haihaya (Kartavirya Arjuna), त्वम् you, च and, कीर्तिम् fame/glory, अपहर्तुम् to steal, उद्यतः prepared/ready

Anvaya: अचले अपि अकुण्ठितम् अस्त्रं बिभ्रतः मम मतौ समागसौ द्वौ रिपू – धेनुवत्सहरणात् हैहयः च, कीर्तिम् अपहर्तुम् उद्यतः त्वं च।

Simple meaning: I, who bear a weapon unblunted even against the immovable mountain, deem there are two equally offending enemies: Haihaya (Kartavirya Arjuna) for stealing our cow's calf, and you who are ready to rob me of my fame.

Note: King Kartavirya Arjuna, who belonged to the Haihaya dynasty, once stole the calf of the sacred cow belonging to Parashurama's father, sage Jamadagni. While Parashurama was away retrieving the calf, Kartavirya Arjuna's sons murdered Jamadagni. Enraged by his father's death, Parashurama vowed to annihilate all Kshatriyas from the earth.

Simple meaning: Raghava resembling the son of Hara (Shiva) - Kartikeya, moved with compassion seeing Bhargava who became powerless with regard to himself, and his own arrow which never went in vain when once put to the bow, spoke to him.

Simple meaning: Even though you are disrespectful, I am not inclined to strike you because you are a Brahmin. With this arrow, tell me, what shall I destroy - your power of movement or your gateway to heaven acquired by sacrifice?"

Word-by-word meaning: राजसत्वम् the quality of rajas, अवधूय having shaken off, मातृकम् maternal, पित्र्यम् paternal/ancestral, अस्मि I am, गमितः made to go, शमम् to peace, यदा when, ननु indeed, अनिन्दित-फलः of irreproachable fruit/result, मम my, त्वया by you, निग्रहः subjugation/defeat, अपि even, अयम् this, अनुग्रही-कृतः made into a favor/grace

Anvaya: यदा मातृकं राजसत्वम् अवधूय पित्र्यं शमं गमितः अस्मि, ननु (तदा) त्वया अनिन्दितफलः मम अयं निग्रहः अपि अनुग्रहीकृतः।

Simple meaning: Having shaken off the quality of rajas inherited from my maternal side, I am made to go to peace which is the quality of my paternal descent. Indeed, by you even this defeat of mine has been made into a favor with an irreproachable result.

Note: Parashurama’s mother was a Kshatriya which apparently has Rajas as a dominant quality. His father was a Brahmana who apparently have Satva as a dominant quality. Brahmanas are peace loving people.  Parashurama is saying that Rama removed his arrogance and humbled him, which bought him peace.

साधयाम्यहमविघ्नमस्तु ते देवकार्यमुपपादयिष्यतः ।

ऊचिवानिति वचः सलक्ष्मणं लक्ष्मणाग्रजमृषितिरोदधे ॥11.91॥

Word-by-word meaning: साधयामि I shall go, अहम् I, अविघ्नम् without obstacles, अस्तु ते may it be for you, देव-कार्यम् the work of the gods, उपपादयिष्यतः who will accomplish/fulfill, ऊचिवान् eloquent/well-spoken, इति thus, वचः words, स-लक्ष्मणम् along with Lakshmana, लक्ष्मण-अग्रजम् the elder brother of Lakshmana (Rama), ऋषिः the sage, तिरोदधे disappeared

Anvaya: "अहं साधयामि। देवकार्यम् उपपादयिष्यतः ते अविघ्नम् अस्तु।" सलक्ष्मणं लक्ष्मणाग्रजम् इति वचः ऊचिवान् ऋषिः तिरोदधे।

Simple meaning: "I will take your leave now. The work of the gods, which has been entrusted to you, may it be fulfilled without any obstacles." Having spoken these eloquent words to the elder brother of Lakshmana who was together with Lakshmana, the sage disappeared.

तस्मिन्गते विजयिनं परिरभ्य रामं स्नेहादमन्यत पिता पुनरेव जातम् ।

तस्याभवत्क्षणशुचः परितोषलाभः कक्षाग्निलङ्घिततरोरिव वृष्टिपातः ॥11.92॥

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मिन् he, गते having gone, विजयिनम् victorious, परिरभ्य embracing, रामम् Rama, स्नेहात् from affection, अमन्यत thought, पिता father, पुनः एव again indeed, जातम् born, तस्य his, अभवत् became, क्षण-शुचः of momentary grief, परितोष-लाभः obtaining of joy, कक्ष-अग्नि-लङ्घित-तरोः of a tree crossed over by forest fire, इव like, वृष्टि-पातः rainfall

Anvaya: तस्मिन् गते पिता स्नेहात् विजयिनं रामं परिरभ्य पुनः जातम् एव अमन्यत। क्षणशुचः तस्य परितोषलाभः – कक्षाग्निलङ्घिततरोः वृष्टिपातः इव – अभवत्।

Simple meaning: When Parashurama had gone, the father embracing victorious Rama, with affection, thought him to be born again indeed. The joy which he obtained after facing momentary grief, felt like rainfall for a tree being crossed over by forest fire.

अथ पथि गमयित्वा कॢप्तरम्योपकार्ये कतिचिदवनिपालः शर्वरीः शर्वकल्पः ।

पुरमविशदयोध्यां मैथिलीदर्शनीनां कुवलयितगवाक्षां लोचनैरङ्गनानाम् ॥11.93॥

Word-by-word meaning: अथ then, पथि on the path, गमयित्वा having passed, कॢप्त-रम्य-उपकार्ये made pleasant with arrangements, कतिचित् some/several, अवनि-पालः the king, शर्वरीः nights, शर्व-कल्पः like Shiva, पुरम् the city, अविशत् entered, अयोध्याम् Ayodhya, मैथिली-दर्शनीनाम् of those eager to see the Maithili princess (Sita), कुवलयित-गवाक्षाम् whose windows were filled with (eyes like) blue lotuses, लोचनैः with eyes, अङ्गनानाम् of the women

Anvaya: अथ शर्वकल्पः अवनिपालः कॢप्तरम्योपकार्ये पथि कतिचित् शर्वरीः गमयित्वा मैथिलीदर्शनीनाम् अङ्गनानाम् लोचनैः कुवलयितगवाक्षाम् अयोध्यां पुरम् अविशत्।

Simple meaning: Then, having passed several nights on the path made pleasant with arrangements, the king like Shiva entered the city of Ayodhya, whose windows seemed to be filled with blue lotuses-like eyes of women eager to see the Maithili princess.
