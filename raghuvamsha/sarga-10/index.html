Kalidasa's Ramayana - Raghuvamsha - Sarga 10

Birth of Rama

(86 Shlokas)

पृथिवीं शासतस्तस्य पाकशासनतेजसः ।

किंचिदूनमनूनर्द्धेः शरदामयुतं ययौ ॥10.1॥  

Word-by-word meaning: पृथिवीम् the earth, शासतः of the one ruling, तस्य his, पाकशासन-तेजसः of one whose radiance was equal to the subduer of a rakshasa named Paka - Indra, किंचित् slightly, ऊनम् less, अ-नून-ऋर्द्धेः of the one whose glory was not less (his glory was great), शरदाम् of autumns years, अयुतम् ten thousand, ययौ passed elapsed

Anvay: पृथिवीं शासतः पाकशासनतेजसः अनूनर्द्धेः तस्य किंचित् ऊनं शरदाम् अयुतं ययौ।

Simple meaning: Of him (Dasharatha), while ruling the earth, whose splendor was equal to that of Indra and whose glory was great, a little less than ten thousand autumns, passed.

न चोपलेभे पूर्वेषामृणनिर्मोक्षसाधनम् ।

सुताभिधानं स ज्योतिः सद्यः शोकतमोपहम् ॥10.2॥

Word-by-word meaning: न not, च and, उपलेभे obtained, पूर्वेषाम् of the ancestors, ऋण-निर्मोक्ष-साधनम् a means for release from debt, सुत-अभिधानम् named as a son, सः that, ज्योतिः light, सद्यः immediately, शोक-तम-उपहम् remover of grief and darkness

Anvay: सः पूर्वेषाम् ऋणनिर्मोक्षसाधनं सद्यः शोकतमोपहं सुताभिधानं ज्योतिः न च उपलेभे।

Simple meaning: He did not get a son—the radiant one who frees the ancestors from their debt and instantly dispels sorrow and darkness.

अतिष्ठत्प्रत्ययापेक्षसंततिः सः चिरं नृपः ।

प्राङ्मन्थादनभिव्यक्तरत्नोत्पत्तिरिवार्णवः ॥10.3॥

Word-by-word meaning: अतिष्ठत् remained waiting, प्रत्यय-अपेक्ष-संततिः he whose progeny waited for a cause to take birth, सः he, चिरम् for a long time, नृपः the king, प्राक् before, मन्थात् churning, अन्-अभिव्यक्त-रत्न-उत्पत्तिः unmanifested production of gems, इव like, अर्णवः ocean

Note: Lord Vishnu was waiting for a cause to take birth as Dasharatha’s child. So the word प्रत्यय-अपेक्ष-संततिः is a bahuvrihi samasa which describes Dasharatha as the one whose child is waiting for a cause to take birth.

Anvay: प्रत्ययापेक्षसंततिः सः नृपः – मन्थात् प्राक् अनभिव्यक्तरत्नोत्पत्तिः अर्णवः इव – चिरम् अतिष्ठत्।

Simple meaning: That king - whose progeny was waiting for a cause to take birth - waited for a long time, like the ocean in which the production of gems is not manifest before churning.

ऋष्यशृङ्गादयस्तस्य सन्तः सन्तानकाङ्क्षिणः।

आरेभिरे जितात्मानः पुत्रीयामिष्टिमृत्विजः ॥10.4॥

Word-by-word meaning: ऋष्यशृङ्ग-आदयः With Rishyashringa as their leader, तस्य of him (Dasharatha), सन्तः holy sages, सन्तान-काङ्क्षिणः of him who desired progeny, आरेभिरे began, जित-आत्मानः who had conquered their senses, पुत्रीयाम् for obtaining a son, इष्टिम् sacrifice, ऋत्विजः priests

Anvay: ऋष्यशृङ्गादयः ऋत्विजः जितात्मानः सन्तः तस्य सन्तानकाङ्क्षिणः पुत्रीयाम् इष्टिम् आरेभिरे।

Simple meaning: The holy sages led by Rishyashringa who had controlled their senses, acting as priests, began the sacrifice for obtaining a son for him, who desired progeny.

तस्मिन्नवसरे देवाः पौलस्त्योपप्लुता हरिम् ।

अभिजग्मुः निदाघार्ताः छायावृक्षम् इव अध्वगाः ॥10.5॥  

Word-by-word meaning: तस्मिन् at that, अवसरे time, देवाः the gods, पौलस्त्य-उपप्लुताः afflicted/harassed by Paulastya (Ravana), हरिम् Hari (Vishnu), अभिजग्मुः approached, निदाघ-आर्ताः afflicted by heat, छाया-वृक्षम् a shade-giving tree, इव like, अध्वगाः travelers

Anvay: तस्मिन् अवसरे पौलस्त्योपप्लुताः देवाः – निदाघार्ताः अध्वगाः छायावृक्षम् इव – हरिम् अभिजग्मुः।

Simple meaning: On that occasion, the gods, tormented by Ravana, went to Vishnu for refuge, just like heat-stricken travelers seeking shelter under a shade-giving tree.

ते च प्रापुरुदन्वन्तं बुबुधे चादिपुरुषः ।

अव्याक्षेपो भविष्यन्त्याः कार्यसिद्धेर्हि लक्षणम् ॥10.6॥   

Word-by-word meaning: ते they, च and, प्रापुः reached, उदन्वन्तम् the ocean, बुबुधे understood/knew (that the gods had arrived), च and, आदिपुरुषः the primeval person Lord Vishnu, अ-व्याक्षेपः absence of delay, भविष्यन्त्याः impending, कार्य-सिद्धेः of successful accomplishment of task, हि indeed, लक्षणम् sign

Anvay: ते उदन्वन्तं प्रापुः च। आदिपुरुषः बुबुधे च। हि अव्याक्षेपः भविष्यन्त्याः कार्यसिद्धेः लक्षणम् (अस्ति)।

Simple meaning: No sooner had the gods reached the ocean (where Lord Vishnu slept), Lord Vishnu realised that the gods had arrived and immediately woke up from His Yog Nidra. Absence of delay is indeed, an indication of the successful accomplishment of an impending task.

Description of Lord Vishnu: (Shlokas 7 - 14)

भोगिभोगासनासीनं ददृशुस्तं दिवौकसः ।

तत्फणामण्डलोदर्चिर्मणिद्योतितविग्रहम् ॥10.7॥

Word-by-word meaning: भोगि-भोग-आसन-आसीनम् seated on the comfortable seat of the serpent (Shesha), ददृशुः saw, तम् him, दिव-औकसः the residents of the heavens - gods, तत्-फणा-मण्डल-उदर्चिः-मणि-द्योतित-विग्रहम् whose body was radiant with the glow of the gems on the hoods of the serpent  

फणा - फणः - फणा,  आकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, आबन्तः, src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvay: दिवौकसः भोगिभोगासनासीनं तत्फणामण्डलोदर्चिर्मणिद्योतितविग्रहं तं ददृशुः।

Simple meaning: The gods beheld Vishnu comfortably reclining on Shesha, His radiant form glowing with the light of the jewels adorning Shesha’s many hoods.

श्रियः पद्मनिषण्णायाः क्षौमान्तरितमेखले ।
अङ्के निक्षिप्तचरणमास्तीर्णकरपल्लवे ॥10.8॥

Word-by-word meaning: श्रियः of Shri Lakshmi, पद्म-निषण्णायाः seated on a lotus, क्षौम-अन्तरित-मेखले covered with a waist-belt of fine silk, अङ्के on the lap, निक्षिप्त-चरणम्  who had placed his foot, आस्तीर्ण-कर-पल्लवे on the tender leaf-like hands spread out on Vishnu’s feet

Anvay: पद्मनिषण्णायाः श्रियः क्षौमान्तरितमेखले आस्तीर्णकरपल्लवे अङ्के निक्षिप्तचरणम् (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw Vishnu whose foot rested on the lap of Goddess Lakshmi, who was seated on a lotus. Her waist was draped in fine silk and her gentle, leaf-soft hands were on Vishnu’s feet.

प्रबुद्धपुण्डरीकाक्षं बालातपनिभांशुकम् ।

दिवसं शारदमिव प्रारम्भसुखदर्शनम् ॥10.9॥  

Word-by-word meaning: प्रबुद्ध-पुण्डरीक-अक्षम् whose eyes were like fully opened white lotus, बाल-आतप-निभ-अंशुकम्  whose garment had a radiance of young gentle sunlight (yellow colour), दिवसम् day, शारदम् autumnal, इव like, प्रारम्भ-सुख-दर्शनम् whose appearance gives joy to the yogis

प्रारम्भ - योगी

पदविभागः- पुल्लिङ्गः

व्युत्पत्तिः- प्रकृष्टं आरम्भो योगः यस्य

प्रयोगाः- 'दिवसं शारदमिव प्रारम्भसुखदर्शनम्'

उल्लेखाः- रघु० १०-९ src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi also. Vyakhyana of Raghuvamsha

Anvay: प्रबुद्धपुण्डरीकाक्षं बालातपनिभांशुकं प्रारम्भसुखदर्शनं – शारदं दिवसम् इव – (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw him who had his lotus-like eyes open, clothed in yellow garments, and pleasing to behold for the yogis – looking like an autumn day where the white lotuses have bloomed, the sun is mellow and is very pleasant in the beginning of the day - morning.

प्रभानुलिप्तश्रीवत्सं लक्ष्मीविभ्रमदर्पणम् ।

कौस्तुभाख्यमपां सारं बिभ्राणं बृहतोरसा ॥10.10॥  

Word-by-word meaning: प्रभा-अनुप्लिप्त-श्रीवत्सम् Shrivatsa mark was glowing with whose splendor, लक्ष्मी-विभ्रम-दर्पणम् the one which was the mirror for Lakshmi when she decorated herself, कौस्तुभ-आख्यम् named Kaustubha, अपाम् सारम् essence of all of the oceans, बिभ्राणम् wearing, बृहता broad, उरसा on the chest

अपाम् - अप्,  पकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, नित्यबहुवचनान्तः, षष्ठीबहुवचनम् src. Shabdarupa. Ashtadhyayi

Anvay: प्रभानुलिप्तश्रीवत्सं लक्ष्मीविभ्रमदर्पणं कौस्तुभाख्यम् अपां सारं बृहता उरसा बिभ्राणं (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw Vishnu wearing the Kaustubha gem on his broad chest. The gem’s splendor made the Shrivatsa mark on Vishnu glow. The gem whose reflecting surface served as a mirror for Lakshmi when she adorned herself. And the gem was the essence of all the oceans in the world.

बाहुभिर्विटपाकारैर्दिव्याभरणभूषितैः ।

आविर्भूतमपां मध्ये पारिजातमिवापरम् ॥10.11॥

Word-by-word meaning: बाहुभिः with arms, विटप-आकारैः in the form of the branches, दिव्य-आभरण-भूषितैः adorned by divine ornaments, आविः-भूतम् manifested, अपाम् of the oceans, मध्ये in the midst, पारिजातम् the Parijata tree, इव like, अपरम् another

Anvay: दिव्याभरणभूषितैः विटपाकारैः बाहुभिः – अपां मध्ये आविर्भूतम् अपरं पारिजातम् इव – (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw Vishnu adorned with divine ornaments adorned on his arms which appeared like branches. He looked like another Parijata tree manifested in the oceans’ midst.

दैत्यस्त्रीगण्डलेखानां मदरागविलोपिभिः ।

हेतिभिश्चेतनावद्भिरुदीरितजयस्वनम्  ॥10.12॥  

Word-by-word meaning: दैत्य-स्त्री-गण्ड-लेखानाम् the marks on the cheeks of the daitya women, मद-राग-विलोपिभिः by removing the intoxication and passion, हेतिभिः by weapons, चेतनावत्भिः as if conscious sentient, उदीरित-जय-स्वनम् proclaiming the sound of victory

Lord Vishnu’s five weapons are Panchajanya Conch, Sudarshan Chakra, Kaumodaki Gada, Nandaka Sword and Sharanga Bow. They are called पञ्च​-हेतयः - हेतिभिः -  हेति,  इकारान्तः, स्त्रीलिङ्गः, पाक्षिकनदीसंज्ञकः, तृतीयाबहुवचनम् also means अग्निज्वाला, आयुधम्, रवेरर्चिः according to different contexts

Anvay: दैत्यस्त्रीगण्डलेखानां मदरागविलोपिभिः चेतनावत्भिः हेतिभिः उदीरितजयस्वनम् (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw Vishnu whose sentient weapons seemed to issue a triumphant roar after wiping away the pride and passion marked on the cheeks of rakshasi women.

Means that, those weapons have caused the destruction of innumerable rakshasas causing their women to mourn for them.

मुक्तशेषविरोधेन कुलिशव्रणलक्ष्मणा ।

उपस्थितं प्राञ्जलिना विनीतेन गरुत्मता ॥10.13॥  

Word-by-word meaning: मुक्त-शेष-विरोधेन by one who is freed from the enmity with Shesha, कुलिश-व्रण-लक्ष्मणा by the one who was bearing the wound mark of thunderbolt of Indra - Vajra, उपस्थितम् present nearby, प्राञ्जलिना with folded hands, विनीतेन with humility, गरुत्मता by Garuda

कुलिश अकारान्तः, पुंलिङ्गः इन्द्रस्य वज्रायुधम् - src. Shabdapatha, Ashtadhyayi

Anvay: मुक्तशेषविरोधेन कुलिशव्रणलक्ष्मणा विनीतेन प्राञ्जलिना गरुत्मता उपस्थितम् (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw Vishnu with Garuda standing nearby - Garuda who was humble and reverent, freed from his enmity with Shesha and bearing the scar of Indra’s thunderbolt.

योगनिद्रान्तविशदैः पावनैरवलोकनैः ।

भृग्वादीननुगृह्णन्तं सौखशायनिकानृषीन् ॥10.14॥

Word-by-word meaning: योग-निद्रा-अन्त-विशदैः pleasant after waking up from Yog-Nidra, पावनैः अवलोकनैः by purifying glances, भृगु-आदीन् Bhrigu and others, अनुगृह्णन्तम् blessing, सौख-शायनिकान् (the sages) who asked if the Lord had slept peacefully, ऋषीन् sages

Anvay: योगनिद्रान्तविशदैः पावनैः अवलोकनैः सौखशायनिकान् भृग्वादीन् ऋषीन् अनुगृह्णन्तम् (विष्णुं ददृशुः)।

Simple meaning: The gods saw Vishnu whose glances were pleasant and purifying after just waking from His yogic sleep, and was blessing Bhrigu and the other sages who were asking if the Lord had slept peacefully.

प्रणिपत्य सुरास्तस्मै शमयित्रे सुरद्विषाम् ।

अथैनं तुष्टुवुः स्तुत्यमवाङ्मनसगोचरम्  ॥10.15॥

Word-by-word meaning: प्रणिपत्य bowing down prostrating, सुराः the gods, तस्मै to him, शमयित्रे to the subduer, सुर-द्विषाम् of the enemies of the gods, अथ then, एनम् him, तुष्टुवुः praised, स्तुत्यम् worthy of praise, अ-वाक्-म​नस-गोचरम् beyond the senses of speech and mind

Anvay: अथ सुराः सुरद्विषां शमयित्रे तस्मै प्रणिपत्य स्तुत्यं अवाङ्मनसगोचरम् एनं तुष्टुवुः।

Simple meaning:  The gods prostrated before Vishnu, the subduer of their enemies, and then praised Him—who was worthy of being praised and who transcends both speech and thought.

The gods praise Lord Vishnu: (Shlokas 16 - 33)

नमो विश्वसृजे पूर्वं विश्वं तदनु बिभ्रते ।

अथ विश्वस्य संहर्त्रे तुभ्यं त्रेधास्थितात्मने ॥10.16॥   

Word-by-word meaning: नमः salutation, विश्वसृजे to the creator of the universe, पूर्वम् at first, विश्वम् the universe, तदनु thereafter, बिभ्रते to the sustainer bearer, अथ then later, विश्वस्य of the universe, संहर्त्रे to the destroyer, तुभ्यम् to you, त्रेधा-स्थित-आत्मने whose nature abides in three forms

Anvay: पूर्वं विश्वसृजे, तदनु विश्वं बिभ्रते, अथ विश्वस्य संहर्त्रे, त्रेधास्थितात्मने तुभ्यं नमः।

Simple meaning: Salutations to You, whose nature abides in three forms—first as the creator of the universe, then as its sustainer, and finally as its destroyer.

रसान्तराण्येकरसं यथा दिव्यं पयोऽश्नुते ।

देशे देशे गुणेष्वेवमवस्थास्त्वमविक्रियः ॥10.17॥

Word-by-word meaning: रस-अन्तराणि different tastes/flavors, एक-रसम् of one taste, यथा just as, दिव्यम् पयः lit. water from the heavens - rain water, अश्नुते obtains, देशे देशे in different places, गुणेषु in qualities, एवम् thus, अवस्थाः states conditions, त्वम् you, अविक्रियः without change/immutable

Anvay: यथा एकरसं दिव्यं पयः देशे देशे रसान्तराणि अश्नुते, एवं त्वम् अविक्रियः गुणेषु अवस्थाः (अश्नुषे)।

Simple meaning: Just as the rain water of a single taste acquires different tastes depending on the place it is in (a river or an ocean), similarly You who are immutable, appear in many states depending on the attributes (Sattva, Rajas and Tamas).

अमेयो मितलोकस्त्वमनर्थी प्रार्थनावहः ।

अजितो जिष्णुरत्यन्तमव्यक्तो व्यक्तकारणम् ॥10.18॥

भारती (स्त्रीलिङ्गः)  सरस्वती - समानार्थकाः — ब्राह्मी भारती भाषा गिर् वाच् वाणी सरस्वती src. Kosha Ashtadhyayi

Literal meaning of the anvaya: The poet of the Puranas - Lord Vishnu, made a speech in a clear and modulated voice, such that the goddess of speech felt that her purpose in life was met.

बभौ सदशनज्योत्स्ना सा विभोर्वदनोद्गता ।

Word-by-word meaning: बभौ shone, स-दशन-ज्योत्स्ना radiance from the teeth, सा that, विभोः of the Lord, वदन-उद्गता arisen from the mouth, निर्यात-शेषा remainder of which emerged, चरणात् from the foot, गङ्गा Ganga, इव like, ऊर्ध्व-प्रवर्तिनी flowing upward

Anvaya: विभोः वदनोद्गता सा सदशनज्योत्स्ना, चरणात् निर्यातशेषा ऊर्ध्वप्रवर्तिनी गङ्गा इव बभौ।

Simple meaning: That radiance like moonlight from the Lord’s teeth, arising from His mouth, shone forth, as if the remaining waters from Ganga which, after emerging from His foot, flowed upward.

Lord Vishnu speaks:

जाने वो रक्षसाक्रान्तावनुभावपराक्रमौ ।

Word-by-word meaning: जाने I know, वः your, रक्षसा by the rakshasas, आक्रान्तौ afflicted/overwhelmed, अनुभाव-पराक्रमौ influence and valor, उभौ both, अङ्गिनाम् of embodied beings, तमसा by Tamas, इव like, गुणौ qualities, प्रथम-मध्यमौ first and middle (Sattva and Rajas)

Anvaya: वः अनुभावपराक्रमौ रक्षसा – अङ्गिनां प्रथममध्यमौ उभौ गुणौ तमसा इव – आक्रान्तौ, (अहं) जाने।

Simple meaning: I know that your influence and valor have been overwhelmed by the rakshasas, like both the first and the second qualities of Sattva and Rajas of embodied beings are dominated by Tamas.

विदितं तप्यमानं च तेन मे भुवनत्रयम् ।

अकामोपनतेनेव साधोर्हृदयमेनसा ॥10.39॥  

Word-by-word meaning: विदितम् known, तप्यमानम् being tormented/afflicted, च and, तेन by that, मे to me, भुवन-त्रयम् the three worlds, अ-काम-उपनतेन incurred unintentionally, इव like, साधोः of a virtuous person, हृदयम् heart, एनसा by sin/fault

एनस् (नपुंसकलिङ्गः)  पापम् src. Kosha. Ashtadhyayi

Anvaya: – अकामोपनतेन एनसा साधोः हृदयम् इव –  तेन तप्यमानं भुवनत्रयं च मे विदितम्।

Simple meaning: It is known to me that the three worlds are afflicted by that (rakshasa), like the heart of a virtuous person tormented by a sin committed unintentionally.

Note: When a virtuous person commits a sin unintentionally, and when he realises it, the guilt that he feels is a great suffering. Vishnu is comparing that feeling of pain of guilt to what the world is suffering due to the rakshasas.

कार्येषु चैककार्यत्वादभ्यर्थ्योऽस्मि न वज्रिणा ।

Word-by-word meaning: कार्येषु in tasks, च and, एक-कार्यत्वात् because of having a single task/unity of purpose, अभ्यर्थ्यः one to be requested/entreated, अस्मि I am, न not, वज्रिणा by Indra/the wielder of the thunderbolt, स्वयम् एव by itself alone, हि indeed, वातः wind, अग्नेः of fire, सारथ्यम् charioteership/guidance/help, प्रतिपद्यते assumes/takes up

Anvaya: कार्येषु एककार्यत्वात् अहं वज्रिणा अभ्यर्थ्यः न अस्मि; हि वातः स्वयम् एव अग्नेः सारथ्यं प्रतिपद्यते।

Simple meaning: In tasks, because of  unity of purpose, I need not be requested by Indra; indeed, the wind itself naturally assumes the role of charioteer for fire. (My purpose and Indra’s purpose is the same - to work for the protection of the world).

स्वासिधारापरिहृतः  कामं  चक्रस्य तेन मे ।

Word-by-word meaning: स्व-असि-धारा-परिहृतः one who warded off the blade of his own sword, कामम् at will/as desired, चक्रस्य of the discus, तेन by him, मे my, स्थापितः placed/established, दशमः tenth, मूर्धा head, लभ्यांशः obtainable share/rightful portion, इव like, रक्षसा by the rakshasa

Anvaya: स्वासिधारापरिहृतः दशमः मूर्धा मे चक्रस्य कामं लभ्यांशः इव तेन रक्षसा स्थापितः।

Simple meaning: Ravana might have left his tenth head without putting it to the blade of his sword, perhaps as an appropriate tribute for my disc.

Note: Ravana offered his nine heads to Shiva to propitiate him. The tenth head is left for Vishnu’s discus to severe, is the implied meaning of this shloka.

स्रष्टुर्वरातिसर्गात्तु मया तस्य दुरात्मनः ।

अत्यारूढं रिपोः सोढं चन्दनेनेव भोगिनः ॥10.42॥  

Word-by-word meaning: स्रष्टुः of the Creator, वर-अतिसर्गात् because of the granting/exceptional bestowal of boons, तु however, मया by me, तस्य of that, दुरात्मनः evil-souled/wicked-minded one, अत्यारूढम् excessively grown/intensified, रिपोः of the enemy, सोढम् endured/tolerated, चन्दनेन by sandalwood, इव like, भोगिनः of a serpent

Anvaya: तु स्रष्टुः वरातिसर्गात् तस्य दुरात्मनः रिपोः मया – भोगिनः चन्दनेन इव – अत्यारूढं सोढम्।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: However, because of the extraordinary boon granted by the Creator, I endured the excessive rise of that wicked enemy, just as sandalwood tree endures a serpent.

धातारं तपसा प्रीतं ययाचे स हि राक्षसः ।

दैवात्सर्गादवध्यत्वं मर्त्येष्वास्थापराङ्मुखः ॥10.43॥  

Word-by-word meaning: धातारम् the Creator/Brahma, तपसा by austerity, प्रीतम् pleased, ययाचे requested/begged, सः he, हि indeed, राक्षसः rakshasa, दैवात् by gods, सर्गात् from creation/among created beings, अ-वध्यत्वम् invincibility, मर्त्येषु among mortals, आस्था-पराङ्मुखः turning away from the possibility (of being killed)

Anvaya: सः राक्षसः तपसा प्रीतं धातारं, मर्त्येषु आस्थापराङ्मुखः (सन्), दैवात् सर्गात् अवध्यत्वं ययाचे हि।

Simple meaning: That rakshasa pleased Brahma through severe austerities and asked for invincibility from all the beings and gods. He underestimated the humans and turned away from the possibility of being killed by them.

Note: When he had propitiated the Creator Ravana sought immunity of death only from divine beings, like गरुडः​, गन्धर्वः, किन्नरः​, किंपुरुषः, सिद्धः, साध्यः​, विद्याधरः​, यक्षः, becoming improvident of mortals.

सोऽहं दाशरथिर्भूत्वा रणभूमेर्बलिक्षमम्  ।

करिष्यामि शरैस्तीक्ष्णैस्तच्छिरःकमलोच्चयम्  ॥10.44॥     

Word-by-word meaning: सः अहम् I myself, दाशरथिः son of Dasharatha, भूत्वा having become, रण-भूमेः in the battlefield, बलि-क्षमम् fit for sacrifice/offering, करिष्यामि I shall make, शरैः with arrows, तीक्ष्णैः sharp, तत्-शिरः-कमल-उच्चयम् his heads like a heap of lotuses

Anvaya: सः अहं दाशरथिः भूत्वा तच्छिरःकमलोच्चयं तीक्ष्णैः शरैः रणभूमेः बलिक्षमं करिष्यामि।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: I myself, becoming Dasharathi, shall make the heap of his heads as an offering of lotuses to the battlefield with sharp arrows.

अचिराद्यज्वभिर्भागं कल्पितं विधिवत्पुनः  ।

मायाविभिरनालीढमादास्यध्वे निशाचरैः ॥10.45॥      

Word-by-word meaning: अचिरात् soon/before long, यज्वभिः by the sacrificers, भागम् share/portion, कल्पितम् assigned/apportioned, विधिवत् according to rule/properly, पुनः again, मायाविभिः possessed of magical powers,अन्-आलीढम् untouched/unharmed, आदास्यध्वे you shall obtain/receive, निशाचरैः by the night-roaming rakshasas

Anvaya: (यूयं) यज्वभिः कल्पितं, मायाविभिः निशाचरैः अनालीढं भागम् अचिरात् पुनः विधिवत् आदास्यध्वे।

Simple meaning: Before long, you shall again properly receive your allotted share from the sacrificers, untouched by the rakshasas endowed with magical powers.

वैमानिकाः पुण्यकृतस्त्यजन्तु मरुतां पथि ।

पुष्पकालोकसंक्षोभं मेघावरणतत्पराः ॥10.46॥       

Word-by-word meaning: वैमानिकाः celestial beings/sky-dwellers, पुण्य-कृतः performers of merit/virtuous ones, त्यजन्तु let them give up/abandon, मरुताम् of the winds, पथि on the path, पुष्पक-आलोक-संक्षोभम् agitation/fear caused due to seeing of the Pushpaka Vimana, मेघ-आवरण-तत्पराः hastening to take cover in the clouds

Anvaya: मरुतां पथि पुण्यकृतः वैमानिकाः मेघावरणतत्पराः पुष्पकालोकसंक्षोभं त्यजन्तु।

Simple meaning: The meritorious celestial beings who move about in the sky, who hide behind the clouds, give up your fear on seeing Ravana’s Pushpaka Vimana.

मोक्ष्यध्वे स्वर्गबन्दीनां वेणीबन्धानदूषितान् ।

शापयन्त्रितपौलस्त्यबलात्कारकचग्रहैः ॥10.47॥

Word-by-word meaning: मोक्ष्यध्वे may you liberate/free, स्वर्ग-बन्दीनाम् those bound in heaven, वेणी-बन्धान् (the women whose hair is tied), अदूषितान् untainted, शाप-यन्त्रित-पौलस्त्य-बलात्कार-कच-ग्रहैः because of the curse on Ravana, he could not forcibly untie the hair of the women (could not force himself upon the women)

Anvaya: (यूयं) शापयन्त्रितपौलस्त्यबलात्कारकचग्रहैः अदूषितान् स्वर्गबन्दीनां वेणीबन्धान् मोक्ष्यध्वे।

Simple meaning: You will untie with your own hands the braids/hair-knots of those goddesses/celestial women who have been made prisoners of heaven by Ravana, which have not been defiled by Ravana's grasping of their hair due to Nalkubara's curse.

Special Note:—An apsara (celestial nymph) named Rambha was going to meet Nalkubara, the son of Kubera, for a romantic rendezvous. Midway, Ravaaa caught her and forcibly had sexual intercourse with her. Because of this, Nalkubara cursed Ravaaa: "If you rape any woman in this manner again, your head will shatter into hundreds of pieces." Due to this fear, Ravaaa never raped any woman again. This story is found in the Mahabharata.

Summary in Simple English:

The commentary explains that:

This context helps explain why the imprisoned celestial women remain dishonored but not fully violated, and why Rama's intervention is needed to liberate them.

रावणावग्रहक्लान्तमिति वागमृतेन सः ।

Simple meaning: Like a dark cloud, soothes the crops by it rains, similarly Lord Vishnu, with his nectar-like words comforted the devas tormented by Ravana and then disappeared.

पुरुहूतप्रभृतयः सुरकार्योद्यतं सुराः ।

अग्नेः - here अग्नि{पुं;5;एक} - from the fire

Simple meaning: Then, after the Lord of earth, Dasharatha completed the yagna for the desire of progeny, a divine person appeared from the fire, causing astonishment to the priests.

अनुप्रवेशादाद्यस्य पुंसस्तेनापि दुर्वहम् ॥10.51॥

अनेन कथिता राज्ञो गुणास्तस्यान्यदुर्लभाः ।

Simple meaning: Through this episode, the king’s exceptional and rare virtues are revealed, for even the origin of the three worlds chose him as the means for bringing forth progeny.

स तेजो वैष्णवं पत्न्योर्विभेजे चरुसम्ज्ञितम् ।

Literal meaning of the anvaya: He apportioned the divine energy of Vishnu, called the charu, between his two queens, just as the sun spreads its fresh radiance over the sky and the earth.

Simple meaning: Kausalya was his honoured queen. Kaikeyi was his beloved. Dasaratha wished that his third wife Sumitra should be honoured by both those queens.

ताभिर्गर्भः प्रजाभूत्यै दध्रे देवांशसम्भवः ।

Simple meaning: After listening to the queens’ dreams, the king was delighted and humbly considered himself honored, knowing that he will be the father of the father of the Universe.

Simple meaning: Lord, though himself being one, resided in the queens’ wombs in multiple forms, as the manifold reflection of the moon in the clear waters.

Note: The quarters felt relieved and blew a sigh of relief in the form of pure and dustless winds - is the intended meaning. Each direction has its own presiding deity. That is what is meant by quarters ruled by Indra and the others.

Simple meaning: Agni and Sun, who were afflicted by the rakshasa (Ravana), shone brightly. Agni shone without the smoke and Sun shone clearly as it were, they were freed from their impurity.

दशाननकिरीटेभ्यस्तत्क्षणं राक्षसश्रियः ।

Word-by-word meaning: दश-आनन-किरीटेभ्यः from the crown of Dashanana (Ravana), तत्क्षणम् at that very moment, राक्षस-श्रियः goddess of prosperity of the rakshasas, मणि-व्याजेन under the pretext/guise of  gems, पर्यस्ताः fell down/were scattered, पृथिव्याम् on the earth, अश्रु-बिन्दवः tear-drops

Anvay: तत्क्षणं राक्षसश्रियः अश्रुबिन्दवः दशाननकिरीटेभ्यः मणिव्याजेन पृथिव्यां पर्यस्ताः।

Simple meaning: At that instant, the tears of the goddess of prosperity of the rakshasas, in the form of the gems from the crown of Ravana, fell on the earth.

This is a striking poetic image: rakshasa prosperity itself begins to weep and falls apart the moment Rama appears.

Simple meaning: Before the commencement of musical instruments that the proud father Dasharatha had arranged to celebrate the arrival of his sons, heavenly pipes and drums started to play on the arrival of Vishnu on earth.

कुमाराः कृतसंस्कारास्ते धात्रीस्तन्यपायिनः ।

Word-by-word meaning: कुमाराः princes, कृत-संस्काराः having undergone rites/properly nurtured, ते they, धात्री-स्तन्य-पायिनः drinking nurse’s milk, आनन्देन with joy, अग्रजेन with the elder brother, इव like, समम् equally, ववृधिरे grew, पितुः of the father

Simple meaning: The princes, whose purificatory rites had been performed, sucking the breast-milk of the nurses grew up along with their eldest brother called the joy of monarch. (As the boys grew, the father’s joy also grew. So the boys and joy grew together.)

Note: The joy of Dasharatha is figuratively said to be his eldest born, because it took birth the moment the Yagna Purusha gave the divine dessert, i.e., अग्रे जातम् - अग्रजम् - earlier to the birth of Rama, and it grew with the growing boys.
